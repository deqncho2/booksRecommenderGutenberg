Synopsis: In 'Life of Johnson, Volume 3: 1776-1780', Boswell continues his captivating account of the renowned English writer and lexicographer, Samuel Johnson. This volume delves into Johnson's eventful years during the American Revolution, where he engages in passionate debates on the war and its implications. As Johnson navigates through political turmoil and personal challenges, Boswell provides a vivid portrayal of his subject's intellectual prowess, wit, and complex personality. From Johnson's encounters with influential figures to his profound reflections on life, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the mind of one of history's most celebrated literary figures.

Genres: Biography, Non-fiction, Literary Criticism

Themes: Intellectualism, Politics, Personal Growth
