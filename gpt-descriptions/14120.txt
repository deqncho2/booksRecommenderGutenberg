Synopsis: 

In 'Answer to Dr. Priestley's Letters to a Philosophical Unbeliever', Turner and Hammon embark on a thought-provoking journey that challenges the foundations of religious belief. Through a series of captivating letters, they engage in a spirited debate with Dr. Priestley, a renowned philosopher and unbeliever. As the correspondence unfolds, the authors skillfully dismantle the arguments put forth by Dr. Priestley, presenting a compelling case for the existence of God and the validity of faith. With eloquence and intellectual rigor, Turner and Hammon explore the complexities of theology, reason, and morality, leaving readers captivated by their insightful and persuasive arguments.

Genres: Philosophy, Theology, Epistolary

Themes: Existence of God, Faith and Reason, Morality
