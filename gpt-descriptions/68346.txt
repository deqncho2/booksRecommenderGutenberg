Synopsis: 

In 'England under the Angevin Kings, Volume I', Kate Norgate takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous reigns of the Angevin kings in medieval England. From the rise of Henry II to the enigmatic reign of Richard the Lionheart, this meticulously researched historical account unveils the complex web of power struggles, political intrigue, and dynastic rivalries that shaped the destiny of England.

As Norgate delves into the lives of these fascinating monarchs, she brings to life the vibrant tapestry of medieval society, from the grandeur of courtly life to the struggles of the common people. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous attention to detail, she paints a vivid picture of a kingdom on the brink of transformation, torn between tradition and progress.

With a keen eye for historical context and a talent for storytelling, Norgate skillfully navigates the intricate web of alliances and conflicts that defined this era. From the infamous murder of Thomas Becket to the legendary clash between Richard the Lionheart and Saladin during the Third Crusade, this volume is a captivating exploration of power, ambition, and the enduring legacy of the Angevin kings.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Medieval history
Themes: Power struggles, Political intrigue, Dynastic rivalries, Medieval society, Tradition vs. progress
