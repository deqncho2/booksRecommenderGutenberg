Synopsis:
'Old New Zealand: Being Incidents of Native Customs and Character in the Old Times' by Frederick Edward Maning is a captivating memoir that takes readers on a journey through the rich cultural tapestry of early New Zealand. Through a series of vivid and often humorous anecdotes, the author provides a unique glimpse into the customs, traditions, and character of the indigenous Maori people during a time of significant change and interaction with European settlers. From thrilling encounters with fearsome warriors to heartwarming tales of friendship and understanding, Maning's narrative paints a vibrant picture of a bygone era, immersing readers in the captivating world of old New Zealand.

Genres:
Memoir, Historical Fiction, Cultural Studies

Themes:
Cultural identity, Intercultural communication, Tradition and change
