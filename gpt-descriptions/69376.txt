Synopsis: In Lydia Maria Child's 'Anti-slavery catechism', a thought-provoking and powerful exploration of the abolitionist movement unfolds. Through a series of questions and answers, Child challenges readers to confront the moral implications of slavery and the urgent need for its eradication. With a compelling blend of historical context and moral reasoning, this work ignites a fire within readers to join the fight against this grave injustice.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical

Themes: Abolitionism, Morality, Social justice
