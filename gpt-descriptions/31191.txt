Synopsis: In "A Letter to the Hon. Samuel A. Eliot," Franklin Dexter pens a compelling response to Eliot's apology for voting in favor of the Fugitive Slave Bill. Through a thought-provoking exchange of ideas, Dexter challenges Eliot's stance and delves into the moral implications of supporting such a controversial legislation. As the letter unfolds, readers are drawn into a passionate debate that explores the complexities of slavery, justice, and the role of government in upholding human rights.

Genres: Historical, Political, Epistolary

Themes: Slavery, Morality, Justice, Government, Human Rights
