Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume XVII, Virginia Narratives', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a captivating collection of firsthand accounts from former slaves in Virginia. These narratives offer a unique and intimate glimpse into the lives of those who endured the horrors of slavery, sharing their personal stories of resilience, struggle, and triumph. From tales of escape and resistance to reflections on family, community, and the pursuit of freedom, this volume paints a vivid picture of the complex and deeply human experiences of enslaved individuals in Virginia. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes: Slavery, Resistance, Freedom, Family, Community
