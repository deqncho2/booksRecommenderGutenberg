Synopsis: In 'Toni, the Little Woodcarver' by Johanna Spyri, readers are transported to a quaint Swiss village where they meet Toni, a young boy with an extraordinary talent for woodcarving. As Toni's skill grows, he catches the attention of a renowned artist who offers him an opportunity that could change his life forever. However, Toni soon finds himself torn between pursuing his dreams and staying true to his roots. This heartwarming tale explores the power of passion, the importance of staying connected to one's heritage, and the choices we must make to find our true path in life.

Genres: Children's Literature, Coming-of-Age, Fiction

Themes: Pursuing one's passion, Cultural identity, Choices and consequences
