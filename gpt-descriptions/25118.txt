Synopsis: In "When Grandmamma Was New: The Story of a Virginia Childhood," Marion Harland takes us on a captivating journey through her own childhood in Virginia during the mid-19th century. Through vivid storytelling, she paints a nostalgic picture of a bygone era, filled with charming anecdotes, family traditions, and the timeless wisdom passed down from generation to generation. From the joys of exploring the countryside to the challenges of growing up in a changing world, Harland's memoir offers a heartfelt glimpse into a simpler time and the enduring bonds of family.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Family, Tradition, Nostalgia, Growth, Resilience
