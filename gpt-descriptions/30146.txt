Synopsis: In 'What She Could' by Susan Warner, a young woman named Mary embarks on a journey of self-discovery and resilience as she navigates the challenges of love, loss, and societal expectations. Set in the 19th century, Mary's determination to defy conventions and pursue her dreams leads her on a path filled with unexpected twists and turns. As she faces adversity and confronts her own limitations, Mary learns the power of inner strength and the importance of staying true to oneself.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Self-discovery, Resilience, Love, Societal Expectations
