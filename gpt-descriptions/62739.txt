Synopsis: In "King Leopold's Soliloquy: A Defense of His Congo Rule," Mark Twain takes on the role of King Leopold II of Belgium as he delivers a passionate and controversial defense of his rule over the Congo Free State. Through a series of thought-provoking monologues, Twain explores the motivations, justifications, and consequences of Leopold's actions, shedding light on the dark realities of colonialism and the exploitation of Africa. With his signature wit and satire, Twain challenges the reader to question the ethics and morality of imperialism, leaving them with a deeper understanding of the complex issues surrounding power, greed, and human rights.

Genres: Historical fiction, Satire, Political commentary

Themes: Colonialism, Imperialism, Morality, Human rights
