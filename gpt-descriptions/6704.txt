Synopsis: In 'A Domestic Problem: Work and Culture in the Household' by Abby Morton Diaz, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the complexities of domestic life. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the lives of two women from different social classes who find themselves entangled in a web of secrets, desires, and societal expectations. As they navigate the challenges of their respective roles, they are forced to confront the limitations imposed upon them by a rigid and patriarchal society. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this thought-provoking novel explores the hidden struggles and sacrifices made within the walls of the home.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Women's Literature, Social Commentary

Themes: Gender roles, Class divide, Societal expectations, Women's agency
