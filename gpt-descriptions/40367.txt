Synopsis: In "The Decoration of Houses," Edith Wharton and Ogden Codman take readers on a captivating journey through the world of interior design and architecture. Through their expert guidance, they explore the principles of good taste, functionality, and harmony in home decoration. From the grandest mansions to the humblest cottages, the authors delve into the importance of proportion, color, and the thoughtful arrangement of furniture and objects. With a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of historical styles, Wharton and Codman provide invaluable insights into creating beautiful and livable spaces. This timeless guide is a must-read for anyone seeking to transform their home into a haven of elegance and refinement.

Genres: Non-fiction, Interior Design, Architecture

Themes: Aesthetics, Functionality, Proportion, Harmony
