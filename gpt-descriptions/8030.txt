Synopsis: In the Book of Amos, a shepherd turned prophet delivers a powerful message of divine judgment and social justice to the people of Israel. Through vivid imagery and poetic language, Amos warns of impending doom and calls for repentance, challenging the nation's leaders and exposing their corruption. As he confronts the wealthy and powerful, Amos reveals the consequences of their actions and the importance of true worship. This thought-provoking book offers a timeless reflection on the relationship between faith, morality, and societal responsibility.

Genres: Religious literature, Prophetic literature, Poetry

Themes: Divine judgment, Social justice, Repentance, Corruption, True worship
