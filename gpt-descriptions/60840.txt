Synopsis: In 'The Woman Movement' by Ellen Key, a young woman named Emma finds herself caught between societal expectations and her own desires for independence. As she becomes involved in the burgeoning women's movement of the late 19th century, Emma must navigate the challenges of love, family, and personal growth. With a cast of compelling characters and a backdrop of social change, this novel explores the complexities of gender roles and the pursuit of equality in a rapidly evolving world.

Genres: Historical fiction, Women's literature, Coming-of-age

Themes: Feminism, Identity, Social change, Love and relationships
