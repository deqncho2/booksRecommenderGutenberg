Synopsis: In "Over Here: Impressions of America by a British officer," Hector MacQuarrie takes readers on a captivating journey through his experiences as a British officer stationed in America during World War II. From the bustling streets of New York City to the vast landscapes of the American Midwest, MacQuarrie provides vivid and insightful observations of American culture, society, and the war effort. Through his unique perspective, the reader gains a deeper understanding of the complexities and nuances of the American way of life, as well as the challenges faced by both the British and American forces during this pivotal time in history.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Travelogue

Themes: Cultural differences, War and its impact, Identity and belonging
