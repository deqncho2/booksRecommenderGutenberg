Synopsis: In 'Old and New Masters' by Robert Lynd, a young artist named John embarks on a journey to discover his true artistic voice. As he navigates the bustling art world of London, he encounters a mysterious mentor who challenges his perceptions and pushes him to explore new artistic techniques. Along the way, John grapples with the tension between tradition and innovation, and must confront his own fears and insecurities. This captivating novel takes readers on a thought-provoking exploration of art, creativity, and the pursuit of self-expression.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Art

Themes: Artistic identity, Tradition vs. innovation, Self-discovery
