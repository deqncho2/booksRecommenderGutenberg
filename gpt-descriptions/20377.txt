Synopsis: 

"The 23rd (Service) Battalion Royal Fusiliers (First Sportsman's): A Record of its Services in the Great War, 1914-1919" by Fred W. Ward is a captivating historical account that delves into the experiences and contributions of a unique battalion during World War I. 

This book takes readers on a journey through the remarkable story of the 23rd Battalion, also known as the First Sportsman's, as they navigate the challenges and horrors of war. From their formation and training to their involvement in major battles, the battalion's journey is filled with bravery, sacrifice, and camaraderie. 

Through vivid descriptions and personal accounts, Ward brings to life the individual stories of the soldiers, highlighting their resilience, determination, and unwavering spirit in the face of adversity. The book also explores the impact of the war on the soldiers' families and the communities they left behind, providing a comprehensive understanding of the war's far-reaching consequences. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: War and conflict, Bravery and sacrifice, Camaraderie and teamwork, Impact on families and communities
