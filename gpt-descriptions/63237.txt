Synopsis: 
In "Early Printed Books," E. Gordon Duff takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of books that were printed before the advent of the printing press. From the meticulous craftsmanship of scribes to the revolutionary impact of Gutenberg's invention, Duff explores the historical, cultural, and technological significance of these early printed books. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, he unveils the secrets behind their creation, preservation, and dissemination, shedding light on the profound influence they had on society. This book is a treasure trove of knowledge for book lovers, history enthusiasts, and anyone curious about the origins of the written word.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Printing revolution, Cultural impact, Technological advancements
