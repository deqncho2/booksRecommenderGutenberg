Synopsis: 
In the second volume of the 11th edition of the Encyclopaedia Britannica, readers are taken on a captivating journey from "Apollodorus" to "Aral." This section delves into a wide range of fascinating topics, including ancient Greek mythology, historical figures, geographical locations, and scientific discoveries. From the mythical tales of gods and heroes to the exploration of distant lands and the wonders of the natural world, this volume offers a wealth of knowledge and captivating stories that will leave readers eager to explore further.

Genres: 
Reference, Encyclopedia, Non-fiction

Themes: 
Mythology, History, Geography, Science
