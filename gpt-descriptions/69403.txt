Synopsis: 

In 'Ornithological biography, Vol. 3 (of 5): An account of the habits of the birds of the United States of America,' John James Audubon takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse avian world of America. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Audubon delves into the fascinating lives and behaviors of various bird species, providing a comprehensive account of their habits, habitats, and interactions. From the majestic Bald Eagle to the elusive Carolina Parakeet, Audubon's meticulous observations and passionate storytelling bring these feathered creatures to life, offering readers a deeper understanding and appreciation for the avian wonders that inhabit the United States.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural history, Biography

Themes: Ornithology, Biodiversity, Environmental conservation
