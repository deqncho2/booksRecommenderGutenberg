Synopsis: In "Rudyard Kipling" by John Palmer, delve into the captivating life of one of the most celebrated writers of the 19th century. This enthralling biography takes readers on a journey through Kipling's early years in India, his struggles as a young journalist, and his eventual rise to literary fame. Uncover the inspirations behind his iconic works such as "The Jungle Book" and "Kim," and explore the complex relationship between Kipling's personal experiences and his writing. Palmer's meticulous research and vivid storytelling bring to life the fascinating world of Rudyard Kipling, offering a fresh perspective on the man behind the timeless tales.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Colonialism, Identity, Artistic Inspiration
