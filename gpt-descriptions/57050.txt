Synopsis: In "Stavrogin's Confession and The Plan of The Life of a Great Sinner," Fyodor Dostoyevsky delves into the complex psyche of Nikolai Stavrogin, a mysterious and enigmatic character. Through a series of confessions and revelations, Stavrogin unveils the depths of his inner turmoil, exploring the darkest corners of his soul. As the reader is drawn into Stavrogin's confessions, they are confronted with the haunting question of what it truly means to be a great sinner. With its gripping narrative and psychological depth, this work offers a profound exploration of human nature and the complexities of morality.

Genres: Psychological fiction, Philosophical fiction, Russian literature

Themes: Sin and redemption, Morality, Guilt and remorse, Human nature, Existentialism
