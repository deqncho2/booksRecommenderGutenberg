Synopsis: 
In 'Tutt and Mr. Tutt' by Arthur Cheney Train, the reader is introduced to Ephraim Tutt, a witty and unconventional lawyer who takes on a variety of intriguing cases. From defending a man accused of murder to navigating the complexities of a high-stakes divorce, Tutt's unorthodox methods and sharp intellect make for captivating courtroom drama. As the cases unfold, the reader is drawn into Tutt's world of legal maneuvering, unexpected twists, and thought-provoking moral dilemmas. With each case, Tutt challenges the status quo and fights for justice in his own unique way, leaving the reader eagerly turning the pages to see what he will do next.

Genres: Legal fiction, Mystery, Drama

Themes: Justice, Morality, Legal ethics
