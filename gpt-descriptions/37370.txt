Synopsis: In 'The Relations of Science and Religion: The Morse Lecture, 1880', Henry Calderwood delves into the intriguing and often contentious relationship between science and religion. Through a thought-provoking exploration, Calderwood examines the ways in which these two seemingly disparate realms intersect and influence one another. With a keen eye for detail and a balanced perspective, he navigates the complexities of this age-old debate, shedding light on the potential harmony and discord that can arise when science and religion collide. This captivating lecture offers a fresh perspective on the ongoing dialogue between these two fundamental aspects of human understanding.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Science

Themes: Science and religion, Conflict and harmony, Epistemology, Philosophy of science
