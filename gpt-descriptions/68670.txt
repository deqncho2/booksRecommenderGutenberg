Synopsis: In 'The Micro-organisms of the Soil', Edward J. Russell takes readers on a captivating journey into the hidden world beneath our feet. Drawing on his extensive research at the Rothamsted Experimental Station, Russell unveils the astonishing diversity and complexity of micro-organisms that inhabit the soil. From bacteria and fungi to protozoa and nematodes, he explores their intricate interactions and the vital roles they play in sustaining life on Earth. With vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, Russell sheds light on the mysterious and often overlooked realm of soil micro-organisms, revealing their profound impact on agriculture, ecology, and human health.

Genres: Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Biodiversity, Ecology, Agriculture, Microbiology
