Synopsis: In 'The Fall of the Great Republic (1886-88)' by Coverdale, Henry Standish, a gripping tale unfolds as the once-mighty Republic faces its darkest hour. Set against the backdrop of political intrigue, power struggles, and societal upheaval, this historical novel takes readers on a thrilling journey through a tumultuous period in the nation's history. As the Republic teeters on the brink of collapse, a cast of unforgettable characters must navigate treacherous waters, making choices that will shape the destiny of an entire nation. With its richly detailed narrative and unexpected twists, 'The Fall of the Great Republic (1886-88)' is a captivating exploration of the human spirit and the fragility of power.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Drama, Thriller

Themes: Power and Corruption, Loyalty and Betrayal, Societal Change
