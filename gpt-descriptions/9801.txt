Synopsis: 
"The Habitant and Other French-Canadian Poems" by William Henry Drummond is a captivating collection of poems that takes readers on a journey through the vibrant and rich culture of French-Canadian life. From the bustling streets of Montreal to the serene landscapes of rural Quebec, Drummond's verses beautifully capture the essence of the people, their traditions, and their struggles. Through his heartfelt and often humorous poetry, the author explores themes of love, family, identity, and the enduring spirit of the French-Canadian people.

Genres: Poetry, Cultural Literature

Themes: French-Canadian culture, Love, Family, Identity, Resilience
