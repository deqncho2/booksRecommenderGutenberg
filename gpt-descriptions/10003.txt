Synopsis: In "My First Years as a Frenchwoman, 1876-1879," Mary King Waddington takes us on a captivating journey through her experiences as an American woman living in France during a transformative period. From her encounters with influential figures to her observations of French society, Waddington's memoir offers a unique perspective on the cultural, political, and social dynamics of late 19th-century France. As she navigates the complexities of her new life, readers are drawn into a world filled with intrigue, romance, and the challenges of adapting to a foreign land.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Fiction, Cultural Studies

Themes: Identity, Cultural assimilation, Gender roles, Political upheaval, Love and relationships
