Synopsis: 

In the first volume of the New York Times Current History series, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the early stages of the European War, from its inception to March 1915. This meticulously compiled collection of articles from the renowned New York Times provides a comprehensive and insightful account of the events that unfolded during this tumultuous period. From the political maneuverings of world leaders to the harrowing tales of soldiers on the front lines, this volume offers a gripping narrative that brings the war to life. With its detailed index, readers can easily navigate through the wealth of information and gain a deeper understanding of the complexities and consequences of this global conflict.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War journalism, News compilation

Themes: World War I, International politics, Military strategy, Human experiences in war
