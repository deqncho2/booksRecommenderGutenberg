Synopsis: In "Social-Democracy and Woman Suffrage," Clara Zetkin delivers a powerful paper to a conference of women belonging to the Social-Democratic Party. With eloquence and conviction, she explores the intersection of social democracy and women's suffrage, shedding light on the challenges faced by women in their fight for political equality. Zetkin's thought-provoking arguments and impassioned rhetoric captivate the audience, leaving them inspired and eager to take action.

Genres: Political discourse, Feminist literature, Historical document

Themes: Women's rights, Social democracy, Political equality
