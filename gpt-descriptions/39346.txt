Synopsis: In "Baltimore and the Nineteenth of April, 1861: A Study of the War," George William Brown takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events that unfolded in Baltimore during the early days of the American Civil War. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Brown delves into the city's divided loyalties, the clash between Union and Confederate sympathizers, and the dramatic events that occurred on the nineteenth of April, 1861. This gripping study sheds light on a pivotal moment in history, revealing the complex dynamics and intense emotions that shaped the course of the war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American history

Themes: Civil War, Loyalty, Conflict, Historical memory
