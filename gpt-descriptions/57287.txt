Synopsis: 
"The Diary of a French Private: War-Imprisonment, 1914-1915" by Gaston Riou is a gripping firsthand account of a French soldier's experiences during World War I. Through the pages of his diary, Riou takes us on a harrowing journey from the front lines of battle to his subsequent capture and imprisonment by the enemy. With vivid and poignant descriptions, he shares the physical and emotional toll of war, the camaraderie among soldiers, and the resilience needed to survive in captivity. As the diary unfolds, readers are immersed in the daily struggles, hopes, and fears of a young soldier caught in the midst of one of history's deadliest conflicts.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature
Themes: War, Survival, Camaraderie, Resilience
