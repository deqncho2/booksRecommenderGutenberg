Synopsis: 
'Enough Rope: Poems' by Dorothy Parker is a collection of witty and poignant poems that delve into the complexities of love, relationships, and the human condition. With her sharp and satirical voice, Parker explores themes of heartbreak, disillusionment, and the struggles of women in a male-dominated society. Through her clever wordplay and biting humor, she offers a unique perspective on the highs and lows of life, leaving readers both entertained and introspective.

Genres: Poetry, Satire, Humor

Themes: Love, Relationships, Feminism, Society, Human Emotions
