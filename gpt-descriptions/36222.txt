Synopsis: 

'Essays in the Study of Folk-Songs' takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of traditional folk songs. Written by Martinengo-Cesaresco, Evelyn Lilian Hazeldine Carrington, contessa, this collection of essays delves into the origins, meanings, and cultural significance of folk songs from various regions. From the haunting melodies of the Scottish Highlands to the lively tunes of Italy, the author explores the rich tapestry of human emotions and experiences woven into these timeless songs. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, the book uncovers hidden stories, explores the symbolism behind the lyrics, and sheds light on the social and historical contexts in which these songs emerged. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Music, Cultural Studies

Themes: Folklore, Tradition, Cultural Identity, Musicology
