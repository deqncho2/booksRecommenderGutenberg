Synopsis: 

In "Thomas Andrews, Shipbuilder" by Bullock, Shan F., readers are taken on a captivating journey through the life of one of history's most renowned shipbuilders. Set against the backdrop of the early 20th century, this biographical work delves into the fascinating world of shipbuilding and the remarkable achievements of Thomas Andrews. From his humble beginnings to his pivotal role in the creation of the iconic RMS Titanic, Andrews' story is one of ambition, innovation, and tragedy. As the narrative unfolds, readers are immersed in the intricate details of ship design, the challenges faced by Andrews and his team, and the profound impact of their work on the world. 

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Ambition, Innovation, Tragedy
