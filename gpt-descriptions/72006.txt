Synopsis: In 'The Useful Trees of Northern Nigeria', Hugh Vandervaes Lely takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse landscape of northern Nigeria, uncovering the hidden treasures of its trees. From the towering baobabs to the delicate shea trees, Lely explores the remarkable uses and cultural significance of these botanical wonders. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, he reveals the invaluable contributions these trees make to the lives of the local communities, offering a fascinating glimpse into the intricate relationship between nature and human existence.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature Writing, Cultural Exploration

Themes: Biodiversity, Sustainability, Cultural Heritage
