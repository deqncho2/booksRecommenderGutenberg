Synopsis: In "Twice-told Tales," Nathaniel Hawthorne weaves a collection of captivating short stories that delve into the dark recesses of the human psyche. From mysterious curses and haunted houses to forbidden love and supernatural encounters, each tale presents a unique and thought-provoking exploration of the human condition. With his masterful storytelling and richly atmospheric prose, Hawthorne invites readers into a world where reality and imagination intertwine, leaving them spellbound until the very last page.

Genres: Gothic fiction, Dark romanticism, Short stories

Themes: Guilt and sin, The nature of evil, The power of the imagination, The consequences of secrets, The supernatural and the unknown
