Synopsis: 

In 'The Fair Play Settlers of the West Branch Valley, 1769-1784: A Study of Frontier Ethnography' by George D. Wolf, readers are transported to the untamed wilderness of the West Branch Valley during the late 18th century. This captivating work delves into the lives of the Fair Play settlers, a group of pioneers who established their own system of self-governance in the face of lawlessness and uncertainty. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Wolf uncovers the challenges, triumphs, and conflicts that shaped the lives of these resilient settlers, offering a fascinating glimpse into the complexities of frontier life.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Ethnography, Frontier history

Themes: Frontier justice, Self-governance, Survival in the wilderness, Cultural adaptation
