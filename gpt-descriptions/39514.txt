Synopsis: 
"The Doctor in History, Literature, Folk-Lore, Etc." takes readers on a captivating journey through time and across various cultures, exploring the enduring presence of the enigmatic figure known as the Doctor. From ancient myths and legends to classic literature and modern-day folklore, this book delves into the multifaceted representations of the Doctor, uncovering hidden connections and shedding light on the mysterious nature of this iconic character. Through a collection of intriguing anecdotes and insightful analysis, readers will discover the Doctor's profound impact on human imagination and the enduring fascination that surrounds this timeless figure.

Genres: 
Literary analysis, Cultural studies, Folklore

Themes: 
Time travel, Heroic archetypes, Mythology, Imagination
