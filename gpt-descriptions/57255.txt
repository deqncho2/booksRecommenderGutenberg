Synopsis: In "A History of the Trial of Castner Hanway and Others, for Treason, at Philadelphia in November, 1851," the author delves into a gripping courtroom drama that unfolds during a tumultuous period in American history. Set against the backdrop of the heated debate over slavery, the trial explores the boundaries of loyalty, justice, and the fight for freedom. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author brings to life the events surrounding this historic trial, leaving readers captivated by the twists and turns of the legal proceedings and the profound implications they hold for the nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Legal drama, Political history

Themes: Slavery, Treason, Justice, Loyalty, Freedom
