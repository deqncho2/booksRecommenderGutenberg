Synopsis: In 'Main Currents in Nineteenth Century Literature - 4. Naturalism in England', Georg Brandes takes us on a captivating journey through the literary landscape of 19th century England. From the gritty streets of London to the idyllic countryside, Brandes explores the rise of naturalism and its impact on English literature. Through insightful analysis and engaging anecdotes, he delves into the works of influential authors such as Thomas Hardy, George Gissing, and George Moore, revealing the raw and unfiltered portrayal of human nature that characterized this literary movement. As we navigate the pages of this enlightening book, we witness the clash between tradition and progress, the exploration of social issues, and the unflinching examination of the human condition. 

Genres: Literary criticism, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Naturalism, Social realism, Human nature, Social issues
