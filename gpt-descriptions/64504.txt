Synopsis: In "Working With the Hands: Being a Sequel to 'Up from Slavery,'" Booker T. Washington takes readers on a captivating journey through his experiences in industrial training at Tuskegee. As he delves into the world of vocational education, Washington shares his encounters with students, teachers, and the challenges they face in a rapidly changing society. Through his insightful anecdotes and reflections, he offers a unique perspective on the power of practical skills and the transformative potential of education. This sequel to his renowned autobiography provides a compelling narrative that will leave readers inspired and eager to explore the possibilities of working with their own hands.

Genres: Autobiography, Education, History

Themes: Vocational training, Self-reliance, Racial uplift, Education as a means of empowerment
