Synopsis: In the fourth volume of "The History of Antiquity," Max Duncker takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient world, exploring the rise and fall of great empires, the clash of civilizations, and the remarkable individuals who shaped history. From the grandeur of ancient Egypt to the conquests of Alexander the Great, Duncker weaves together a tapestry of political intrigue, cultural exchange, and epic battles. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the dramatic events and larger-than-life characters that shaped the ancient world.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Ancient History

Themes: Power and politics, Cultural exchange, Rise and fall of empires, Individual agency, Historical significance
