Synopsis: In 'Measure for Measure: The Works of William Shakespeare [Cambridge Edition] [9 vols.],' Shakespeare takes us on a captivating journey through the complex world of Vienna, where a strict deputy ruler enforces the law with unwavering severity. When a young man is sentenced to death for a seemingly minor offense, his sister, a virtuous nun, pleads for his life. What unfolds is a gripping tale of power, corruption, and the blurred lines between justice and mercy. As the characters navigate their way through moral dilemmas and unexpected twists, Shakespeare masterfully explores the depths of human nature and the consequences of our actions.

Genres: Drama, Tragedy, Comedy

Themes: Justice and mercy, Morality and ethics, Abuse of power, Sexual morality, Hypocrisy
