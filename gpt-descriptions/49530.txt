Synopsis: In "The Battle of Gettysburg, 1863" by Samuel Adams Drake, readers are transported to the heart of one of the most pivotal moments in American history. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Drake brings to life the harrowing three-day battle that took place in the small town of Gettysburg during the American Civil War. As the Union and Confederate forces clash in a battle of epic proportions, the fate of a nation hangs in the balance. With a cast of unforgettable characters and gripping accounts of bravery and sacrifice, this book offers a captivating exploration of the events that unfolded on those hallowed grounds.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: War and its consequences, Leadership and strategy, Sacrifice and heroism
