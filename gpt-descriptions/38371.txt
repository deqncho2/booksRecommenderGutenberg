Synopsis: In "Exempting the Churches: An Argument for the Abolition of This Unjust and Unconstitutional Practice," James F. Morton presents a thought-provoking exploration of a controversial topic that challenges the status quo. With meticulous research and compelling arguments, Morton delves into the historical, legal, and ethical aspects surrounding the exemption of churches from taxes. As he unveils the hidden consequences and inequalities resulting from this practice, readers are invited to question long-held beliefs and consider the implications of a more equitable society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, Social Critique

Themes: Taxation, Separation of Church and State, Social Justice
