Synopsis: In 'Catharine de Bora: Social and Domestic Scenes in the Home of Luther', John G. Morris takes readers on a captivating journey into the life of Catharine de Bora, the wife of the renowned theologian Martin Luther. Through vivid descriptions and intimate portrayals, Morris unveils the hidden world behind the Reformation, showcasing the strength, resilience, and influence of a woman who played a pivotal role in shaping history. From the bustling streets of Wittenberg to the private chambers of the Luthers' home, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the daily struggles, triumphs, and love that defined Catharine's life.

Genres: Historical biography, Non-fiction, Historical fiction

Themes: Women's empowerment, Reformation, Marriage and partnership, Religious devotion
