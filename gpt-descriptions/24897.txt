Synopsis: In 'The Great White Tribe in Filipinia', Paul T. Gilbert takes readers on a captivating journey through the Philippines, exploring the lives and adventures of American expatriates in the early 20th century. Through vivid storytelling and rich historical detail, Gilbert paints a vivid picture of the clash between cultures, the allure of the exotic, and the complexities of colonialism. From the bustling streets of Manila to the remote islands of the archipelago, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into a forgotten era and the extraordinary individuals who shaped it.

Genres: Historical fiction, Adventure, Travelogue

Themes: Colonialism, Cultural clash, Exoticism, Identity
