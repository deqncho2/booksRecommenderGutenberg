Synopsis: 

'Forty-Five Years of Digitizing Ebooks: Project Gutenberg's Practices' takes readers on a captivating journey through the history and evolution of Project Gutenberg, the world's oldest digital library. Gregory B. Newby, an expert in digital libraries, provides an insightful exploration of the challenges, triumphs, and innovations that have shaped the organization over the past four and a half decades. From the early days of scanning books to the present era of mass digitization, this book offers a fascinating behind-the-scenes look at the tireless efforts of volunteers and the impact of their work on the accessibility of literature worldwide.

Genres: Non-fiction, Technology, History

Themes: Digital preservation, Access to knowledge, Volunteerism
