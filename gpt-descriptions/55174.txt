Synopsis: 
'Oratory Sacred and Secular; Or, The Extemporaneous Speaker: With Sketches of the Most Eminent Speakers of All Ages' by Pittenger is a captivating exploration of the art of public speaking. Delving into the lives and techniques of renowned orators throughout history, this book offers valuable insights into the power of eloquence and the impact it can have on both sacred and secular realms. From ancient philosophers to modern statesmen, Pittenger presents a collection of fascinating anecdotes and analysis that will leave readers inspired and eager to enhance their own speaking abilities.

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, Communication

Themes: Public speaking, Rhetoric, Influence, Historical figures
