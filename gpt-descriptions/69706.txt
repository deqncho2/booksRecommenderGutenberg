Synopsis: 
Step back in time to the year 1912 and immerse yourself in the captivating world of the fourth National Conservation Congress held in Indianapolis. This remarkable collection of proceedings offers a glimpse into the discussions, debates, and groundbreaking ideas that shaped the future of conservation in the United States. From the passionate speeches of influential figures to the unveiling of innovative strategies, this congress serves as a pivotal moment in the nation's history. Delve into the pages of this extraordinary document and witness the birth of a movement that would forever change the way we view and protect our natural resources.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical

Themes: Conservation, Environmentalism, Policy-making
