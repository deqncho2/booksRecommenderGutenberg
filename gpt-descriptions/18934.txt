Synopsis: In "My Lady Nicotine: A Study in Smoke," J.M. Barrie takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of tobacco and its alluring hold on the human psyche. Through a series of witty and insightful anecdotes, Barrie explores the various ways in which smoking becomes a deeply ingrained habit, affecting both the individual and society at large. From humorous encounters with fellow smokers to introspective musings on the nature of addiction, this book offers a unique and thought-provoking exploration of the complex relationship between humans and the seductive allure of nicotine.

Genres: Non-fiction, Essay, Memoir

Themes: Addiction, Human behavior, Social norms
