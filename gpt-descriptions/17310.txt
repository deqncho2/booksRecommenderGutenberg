Synopsis: 

In 'Tablets of Bahá'u'lláh Revealed after the Kitab-i-Aqdas', Bahá'u'lláh, the founder of the Bahá'í Faith, unveils a collection of profound and transformative writings that were revealed after the completion of his most important work, the Kitab-i-Aqdas. These tablets, filled with wisdom and guidance, offer a glimpse into the spiritual journey of humanity and the path towards unity and peace. Through poetic and thought-provoking language, Bahá'u'lláh explores the nature of God, the purpose of life, and the challenges faced by individuals and society. This collection serves as a testament to the enduring power of Bahá'u'lláh's teachings and their relevance in today's world.

Genres: Religious literature, Philosophy, Poetry

Themes: Spiritual enlightenment, Unity, Peace, Divine guidance
