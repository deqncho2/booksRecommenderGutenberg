Synopsis: 

In "The German Terror in Belgium: An Historical Record," Arnold Toynbee delves into the dark and haunting events that unfolded during the German occupation of Belgium in World War I. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, Toynbee paints a vivid picture of the atrocities committed by the German forces, revealing the true extent of the terror inflicted upon the Belgian people. From the destruction of towns and villages to the brutal treatment of civilians, this historical record uncovers the harrowing experiences endured by the Belgian population. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War literature, Investigative journalism

Themes: War atrocities, Occupation and resistance, Human suffering
