Synopsis: In "Nietzsche and Other Exponents of Individualism," Paul Carus delves into the fascinating world of individualism, exploring the philosophies of Friedrich Nietzsche and other influential thinkers. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Carus examines the impact of these ideas on society and the human condition, challenging conventional beliefs and offering a fresh perspective on the importance of individuality. As he navigates the complex terrain of philosophy, Carus invites readers on a captivating journey that explores the depths of human nature and the power of embracing one's unique identity.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Individualism, Human nature, Society, Identity
