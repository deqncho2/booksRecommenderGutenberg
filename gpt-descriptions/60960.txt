Synopsis: 

In an Unknown Prison Land: An account of convicts and colonists in New Caledonia with jottings out and home takes readers on a captivating journey to the remote island of New Caledonia, where convicts and colonists coexist in a harsh and mysterious land. Through the eyes of George Chetwynd Griffith, a British officer stationed on the island, we are immersed in a world of intrigue, danger, and unexpected alliances. As Griffith navigates the treacherous landscape, he uncovers secrets that could change the course of history and finds himself torn between loyalty and survival. With vivid descriptions and gripping storytelling, this book offers a unique perspective on the untold stories of those who lived in this unknown prison land.

Genres: Historical fiction, Adventure, Mystery

Themes: Survival, Loyalty, Exploration, Colonialism
