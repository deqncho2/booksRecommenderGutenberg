Synopsis: In 'Causes and Consequences' by John Jay Chapman, a young artist named Henry embarks on a journey of self-discovery and rebellion against societal norms. Set in the vibrant streets of early 20th century New York City, Henry finds himself torn between his passion for art and the expectations of his family. As he navigates through bohemian circles and encounters a cast of eccentric characters, Henry's choices lead him down a path of both triumph and heartbreak. This captivating novel explores the complexities of artistic expression, the clash between tradition and individuality, and the profound impact of personal choices.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Artistic expression, Rebellion, Identity, Society's expectations
