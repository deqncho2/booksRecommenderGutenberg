Synopsis: 

'Copy of Letters Sent to Great-Britain' is a collection of compelling and controversial correspondence written by His Excellency Thomas Hutchinson, the Hon. Andrew Oliver, and several other influential figures. These letters, which were never intended for public consumption, provide a fascinating glimpse into the political and social climate of colonial America during a time of great unrest. As the letters are unveiled, readers will be captivated by the intricate web of alliances, betrayals, and power struggles that unfold, ultimately shedding light on the events that would shape the course of history.

Genres: Historical, Political, Non-fiction

Themes: Colonial America, Power dynamics, Political intrigue
