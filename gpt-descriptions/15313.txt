Synopsis: In 'A Full Enquiry into the Nature of the Pastoral', Thomas Purney takes readers on a captivating exploration of the pastoral genre. Through a meticulous analysis of its origins, evolution, and significance, Purney unveils the hidden depths and complexities of this seemingly idyllic literary form. Delving into the works of renowned pastoral writers, he uncovers the underlying themes and motifs that have shaped this genre throughout history. With thought-provoking insights and engaging anecdotes, Purney invites readers to reconsider their understanding of pastoral literature and its enduring impact on our perception of nature, love, and the human experience.

Genres: Literary criticism, literary analysis, historical analysis

Themes: Nature, love, human experience, literary tradition
