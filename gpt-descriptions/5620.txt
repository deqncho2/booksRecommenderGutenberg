Synopsis: 
'Palaces and Courts of the Exposition: A Handbook of the Architecture, Sculpture and Mural Paintings, with Special Reference to the Symbolism' takes readers on a captivating journey through the grandeur and artistry of the Exposition. Written by Juliet Helena Lumbard James, this handbook delves into the intricate details of the architecture, sculpture, and mural paintings, unraveling their hidden symbolism and significance. From the majestic palaces to the opulent courts, the author's vivid descriptions transport readers to a world of beauty and wonder, inviting them to explore the rich tapestry of art and culture that the Exposition has to offer.

Genres: Non-fiction, Art, History
Themes: Symbolism, Architecture, Artistic interpretation
