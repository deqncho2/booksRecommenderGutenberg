Synopsis: In "Despotism and Democracy: A Study in Washington Society and Politics," Molly Elliot Seawell takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate web of power, ambition, and intrigue that defines Washington society. Set against the backdrop of the nation's capital, this gripping narrative delves into the lives of influential politicians, socialites, and power brokers, revealing the hidden truths and dark secrets that shape their actions. As the lines between despotism and democracy blur, Seawell masterfully explores the complex dynamics of power and the consequences it has on individuals and society as a whole.

Genres: Political fiction, Historical fiction, Drama

Themes: Power and corruption, Ambition, Social hierarchy, Morality, Influence of society
