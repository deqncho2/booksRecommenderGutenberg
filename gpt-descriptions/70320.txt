Synopsis: 
In "The Navy of the American Revolution: Its administration, its policy, and its achievements," Charles Oscar Paullin takes readers on a captivating journey through the often overlooked naval aspect of the American Revolution. From the daring exploits of privateers to the strategic decisions made by naval commanders, this book sheds light on the crucial role played by the navy in securing American independence. Paullin's meticulous research and engaging storytelling bring to life the thrilling battles at sea, the challenges faced by the fledgling navy, and the remarkable achievements that ultimately shaped the course of history.

Genres: 
Historical non-fiction, Military history, American history

Themes: 
Patriotism, Naval warfare, Leadership, Independence
