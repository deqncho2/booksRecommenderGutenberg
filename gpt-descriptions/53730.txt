Synopsis: In 'The Red Cross Girls in Belgium' by Margaret Vandercook, a group of brave and compassionate young women embark on a life-changing journey to war-torn Belgium during World War I. As they navigate the dangers and horrors of the battlefield, they form unbreakable bonds of friendship and discover the true meaning of courage and sacrifice. With their unwavering determination to help those in need, the Red Cross girls become beacons of hope in a time of darkness and despair.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Young Adult Fiction

Themes: Friendship, Courage, Sacrifice, Humanitarianism
