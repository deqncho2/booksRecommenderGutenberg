Synopsis: In "The New Freedom: A Call for the Emancipation of the Generous Energies of a People," Woodrow Wilson presents a compelling argument for the liberation of the American people from the constraints of big business and government corruption. Through a thought-provoking exploration of the nation's economic and political landscape, Wilson challenges readers to envision a society where individual liberties and the pursuit of happiness are at the forefront. With eloquence and passion, he advocates for a new era of freedom, one that empowers the generous energies of the people and fosters a more just and equitable society.

Genres: Political philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Freedom, Individualism, Government reform, Economic justice
