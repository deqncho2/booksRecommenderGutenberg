Synopsis: In "Woman's Work in Music," Arthur Elson takes us on a captivating journey through the lives and achievements of remarkable women who have made significant contributions to the world of music. From composers and performers to educators and patrons, Elson unveils the untold stories of these extraordinary women, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and the lasting impact they have had on the musical landscape. Through vivid anecdotes and meticulous research, Elson paints a vivid picture of the challenges faced by these women in a male-dominated industry, while also celebrating their immense talent and resilience. This inspiring exploration of the often overlooked role of women in music is a must-read for anyone passionate about the arts and the power of breaking barriers.

Genres: Non-fiction, Music History, Biography

Themes: Gender equality, Women's empowerment, Artistic expression, Overcoming adversity
