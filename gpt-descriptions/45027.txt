Synopsis: In Chaucer's Works, Volume 3, readers are transported to a world of enchanting tales and celestial wonders. The House of Fame takes us on a dreamlike journey through a magnificent palace where fame and reputation reign supreme. The Legend of Good Women introduces us to a group of virtuous ladies whose stories challenge traditional gender roles and celebrate the power of love. The Treatise on the Astrolabe unveils the secrets of the stars, guiding us through the intricacies of astronomy and the art of navigation. Lastly, The Sources of the Canterbury Tales offers a fascinating glimpse into the origins and inspirations behind Chaucer's timeless masterpiece. 

Genres: Poetry, Fiction, Non-fiction

Themes: Fame and reputation, Gender roles, Love and virtue, Astronomy and navigation, Literary inspiration
