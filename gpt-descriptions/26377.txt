Synopsis: 

In the July 1915 edition of the New York Times Current History, readers are transported to the tumultuous period of April to September 1915, during the heart of the European War. Through a collection of articles, essays, and firsthand accounts, this volume offers a gripping and comprehensive exploration of the events and developments that unfolded during this critical time. From the battlefields to the diplomatic negotiations, from the strategies of the warring nations to the impact on civilians, the reader is immersed in the complexities and consequences of the war that engulfed Europe. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War journalism, Periodical

Themes: World War I, International diplomacy, Military strategy, Impact on civilians, Global conflict
