Synopsis: In 'Historic Bubbles' by Frederic Leake, embark on a captivating journey through time as the author unravels the fascinating stories behind some of history's most intriguing bubbles. From the tulip mania in 17th century Netherlands to the dot-com bubble of the late 20th century, Leake delves into the captivating world of speculative manias and their profound impact on societies. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, this book sheds light on the human nature that drives these bubbles and the lessons we can learn from them. Prepare to be enthralled by the tales of greed, euphoria, and eventual downfall that have shaped our financial history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Economics

Themes: Speculation, Financial markets, Human psychology, Economic history
