Synopsis: In 'The Sabbath: a sermon preached in Holy Trinity Church, Hurdsfield', John Martindale Farrar delivers a thought-provoking sermon that challenges the proposed opening of the Crystal Palace on the Lord's day. With eloquence and conviction, Farrar explores the significance of the Sabbath and its importance in preserving the sanctity of worship and rest. As he delves into the moral implications of this controversial decision, the sermon raises questions about the balance between progress and tradition, and the potential consequences of disregarding sacred time.

Genres: Sermon, Religious Literature

Themes: Sabbath observance, Sanctity of worship, Moral implications, Tradition versus progress
