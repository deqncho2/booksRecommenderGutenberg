Synopsis:
In "Mrs. Mary Robinson, Written by Herself," we are introduced to the captivating life of Mary Robinson, a woman who defied societal norms and rose to fame as an actress and mistress to prominent figures of her time. Through her own words, we delve into her tumultuous relationships, scandalous affairs, and the challenges she faced as a woman in the 18th century. Alongside her story, we are also presented with the intriguing lives of the Duchesses of Gordon and Devonshire, two influential women who navigated the complexities of high society. This enthralling narrative takes us on a journey through love, ambition, and the pursuit of freedom in a world filled with constraints.

Genres: Biography, Historical Fiction, Drama

Themes: Gender roles, Love and relationships, Social status, Ambition, Freedom
