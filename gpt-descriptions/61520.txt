Synopsis: 

In 'The Tropical World: Aspects of man and nature in the equatorial regions of the globe,' Georg Hartwig takes readers on a captivating journey through the lush and vibrant equatorial regions of the world. From the dense rainforests of the Amazon to the exotic islands of Southeast Asia, Hartwig explores the unique and diverse ecosystems, as well as the fascinating cultures and traditions that thrive in these tropical paradises. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, the author delves into the intricate relationship between man and nature, shedding light on the delicate balance that exists in these extraordinary regions. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Travel, Natural History

Themes: Biodiversity, Cultural diversity, Environmental conservation, Human-nature interaction
