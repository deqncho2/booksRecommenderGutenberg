Synopsis: 

In this thought-provoking sermon delivered in 1853, Frederick T. Gray reflects on the life and legacy of the late Amos Lawrence, a prominent figure in Boston. Through his eloquent words, Gray explores the profound impact Lawrence had on society and challenges his listeners to consider the true measure of a person's worth. With poignant anecdotes and powerful insights, this extract from a sermon offers a unique perspective on the life of a remarkable individual and the lasting influence they can have on the world.

Genres: Sermon, Historical, Biographical

Themes: Legacy, Influence, Personal Worth
