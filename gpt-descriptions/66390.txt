Synopsis: In 'Imre: A Memorandum', Edward Prime-Stevenson takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Imre, a young man who finds himself torn between societal expectations and his own desires. Set in the late 19th century, Imre's story unfolds against the backdrop of a vibrant European city, where he navigates the complexities of love, identity, and self-discovery. As Imre grapples with his inner conflicts, he encounters a cast of intriguing characters who challenge his beliefs and push him to confront his deepest fears. With its richly detailed narrative and thought-provoking exploration of human nature, 'Imre: A Memorandum' offers a compelling tale of personal growth and the pursuit of authenticity.

Genres: LGBTQ+ Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Identity, Sexuality, Society's Expectations, Self-Discovery
