Synopsis: In "Psyche's Task: A discourse concerning the influence of superstition on the growth of institutions," James George Frazer delves into the intriguing relationship between superstition and the development of societal institutions. Through a captivating exploration of various cultures and historical periods, Frazer uncovers the profound impact that superstition has had on shaping the foundations of human civilization. With thought-provoking examples and meticulous research, the author invites readers on a fascinating journey to understand how deeply ingrained beliefs and rituals have influenced the growth and evolution of institutions throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Anthropology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Superstition, Institutional Development, Cultural Evolution
