Synopsis: 
"The War in the East: Japan, China, and Corea" by White, Trumbull takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous conflicts and power struggles in East Asia during a critical period in history. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the book unveils the complex relationships between Japan, China, and Corea (now known as Korea) and the devastating consequences of their clashes. As tensions rise and alliances shift, the reader is immersed in a world of political intrigue, military strategies, and cultural clashes that shape the destiny of nations. With its gripping narrative and rich historical context, this book offers a compelling exploration of a lesser-known chapter in East Asian history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War, Politics

Themes: Power struggles, Diplomacy, National identity, Cultural clashes
