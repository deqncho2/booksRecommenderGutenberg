Synopsis: 
In "Little Rivers: A Book of Essays in Profitable Idleness," Henry Van Dyke takes readers on a captivating journey through the beauty and tranquility of nature. Through a series of insightful essays, Van Dyke explores the joys of fishing, the serenity of rivers, and the profound lessons that can be learned from observing the natural world. From the peaceful solitude of casting a line into a flowing stream to the profound connections between man and nature, this collection of essays offers a delightful escape into the wonders of the great outdoors.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature Writing, Essay Collection

Themes: Nature appreciation, Reflection, Solitude, Spiritual connection with nature
