Synopsis: 
'Letters to Judd, an American Workingman' by Sinclair, Upton is a captivating collection of heartfelt letters that delve into the struggles and triumphs of an ordinary American worker named Judd. Through these letters, Judd's experiences and emotions are vividly portrayed, painting a vivid picture of the challenges faced by the working class in early 20th century America. As the letters unfold, the reader is taken on a journey of self-discovery, resilience, and the pursuit of the American Dream.

Genres: Fiction, Epistolary, Social Commentary

Themes: Social inequality, Labor rights, American Dream, Class struggle
