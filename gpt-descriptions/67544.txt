Synopsis: 
In 'Fors Clavigera (Volume 4 of 8): Letters to the workmen and labourers of Great Britain', John Ruskin, a renowned Victorian art critic and social thinker, addresses the working class directly, offering them a powerful and thought-provoking series of letters. Through his eloquent and impassioned writing, Ruskin delves into a wide range of topics, including the importance of craftsmanship, the impact of industrialization, the value of education, and the need for social justice. With a blend of personal anecdotes, philosophical musings, and practical advice, Ruskin's letters provide a unique perspective on the challenges faced by the working class and offer a call to action for a more just and equitable society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social criticism, Epistolary literature

Themes: Social justice, Craftsmanship, Industrialization, Education, Equality
