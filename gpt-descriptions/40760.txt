Synopsis:
"Fifty Years in Chains; or, the Life of an American Slave" by Charles Ball is a gripping and harrowing memoir that takes readers on a journey through the life of a former American slave. From his capture in Africa to his experiences on various plantations, Ball's narrative provides a firsthand account of the brutalities and injustices endured by enslaved individuals during the early 19th century. Through his powerful storytelling, Ball sheds light on the resilience, strength, and determination of those who fought for their freedom, leaving readers both captivated and deeply moved.

Genres:
Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Slave Narrative

Themes:
Slavery, Freedom, Resilience, Injustice, Human Rights
