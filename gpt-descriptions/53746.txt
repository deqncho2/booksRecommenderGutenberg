Synopsis: 
'Among the Head-Hunters of Formosa' takes readers on a thrilling journey through the remote and mysterious island of Formosa (modern-day Taiwan) in the early 20th century. Janet B. Montgomery McGovern, an intrepid explorer and anthropologist, recounts her extraordinary encounters with the indigenous tribes of Formosa, including the notorious head-hunters. From perilous treks through dense jungles to immersive experiences in tribal rituals, McGovern's vivid descriptions and captivating storytelling transport readers to a world few have ever witnessed. As she delves deeper into the customs, beliefs, and traditions of these fascinating tribes, McGovern's account becomes a testament to the resilience of human spirit and the power of cultural diversity.

Genres: Adventure, Travelogue, Anthropology

Themes: Exploration, Cultural diversity, Survival
