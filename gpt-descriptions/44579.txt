Synopsis: In "Slavery in Pennsylvania," Edward Raymond Turner delves into the often overlooked history of slavery in the state of Pennsylvania. Through meticulous research and analysis, Turner uncovers the complex and contradictory nature of slavery in a region known for its abolitionist movement. From the lives of enslaved individuals to the economic and social implications of slavery, this dissertation sheds light on a dark chapter in Pennsylvania's past.

Genres: Historical research, academic writing, non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, abolition, race relations, historical memory
