Synopsis: 
"The Journal to Stella" is a collection of intimate letters written by Jonathan Swift, the renowned author of "Gulliver's Travels," to his close friend Esther Johnson, whom he affectionately calls Stella. Through these letters, Swift shares his personal thoughts, experiences, and observations, providing a captivating glimpse into his life and the society of 18th-century England. As the correspondence unfolds, the reader becomes immersed in the witty banter, intellectual discussions, and heartfelt emotions exchanged between Swift and Stella, creating a compelling narrative that explores the complexities of friendship, love, and the human condition.

Genres: 
Epistolary, Autobiography, Literary Fiction

Themes: 
Friendship, Love, Society, Human Nature
