Synopsis: In 'A Little Book of Bores', Oliver Herford takes readers on a witty and satirical journey through the world of mundane and tiresome individuals. From the overly talkative neighbor to the pretentious art critic, Herford's sharp observations and clever humor shed light on the everyday nuisances that we all encounter. With a delightful blend of sarcasm and wit, this collection of short anecdotes and character sketches will leave readers chuckling and nodding in recognition.

Genres: Satire, Humor, Non-fiction

Themes: Mundanity of everyday life, Social observations
