Synopsis: 

In 'The 28th: A Record of War Service in the Australian Imperial Force, 1915-19, Vol. I: Egypt, Gallipoli, Lemnos Island, Sinai Peninsula', author Herbert Brayley Collett takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of the Australian soldiers during World War I. Through vivid and detailed accounts, Collett paints a vivid picture of the battles fought in Egypt, the Gallipoli campaign, Lemnos Island, and the Sinai Peninsula. From the camaraderie among the troops to the horrors of trench warfare, this book offers a firsthand perspective of the sacrifices made by the Australian soldiers during this tumultuous period in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War memoir

Themes: Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Trench warfare, World War I
