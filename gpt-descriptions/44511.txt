Synopsis: 

'With a Reservist in France: A Personal Account of All the Engagements in Which the 1st Division 1st Corps Took Part, viz.: Mons (Including the Retirement), the Marne, the Aisne, First Battle of Ypres, Neuve Chapelle, Festubert, and Loos' is a gripping memoir that takes readers on a thrilling journey through the intense battles and engagements of World War I. Written by F. A. Bolwell, a reservist who served in the 1st Division 1st Corps, this personal account provides a firsthand perspective of the harrowing experiences and heroic actions witnessed on the front lines.

As the narrative unfolds, the reader is transported to the battlefields of Mons, the Marne, the Aisne, First Battle of Ypres, Neuve Chapelle, Festubert, and Loos, where the author vividly describes the chaos, camaraderie, and courage displayed by the soldiers. Bolwell's account captures the relentless nature of war, the devastating impact on both soldiers and civilians, and the unwavering determination to protect one's comrades and fight for a cause.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature
Themes: War experiences, Heroism, Camaraderie, Sacrifice, Human resilience
