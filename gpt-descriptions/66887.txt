Synopsis: 

In 'The History of Trade Unionism: (Revised edition, extended to 1920)', Beatrice and Sidney Webb delve into the fascinating and often overlooked world of trade unions, tracing their origins and evolution up until the year 1920. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, the authors shed light on the struggles, triumphs, and transformative impact of trade unions on the lives of workers and society as a whole. From the early guilds and secret societies to the emergence of powerful labor movements, this revised edition offers a comprehensive exploration of the complex dynamics that shaped the history of trade unionism.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Labor Studies

Themes: Labor rights, Social justice, Workers' empowerment, Collective bargaining, Industrial relations
