Synopsis: In 'Mendelssohn and Certain Masterworks', Herbert F. Peyser takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and music of the renowned composer, Felix Mendelssohn. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Peyser delves into the creation and significance of some of Mendelssohn's most beloved masterpieces, unraveling the mysteries behind their composition and exploring the profound impact they had on the world of classical music. From the ethereal beauty of the Violin Concerto in E minor to the majestic grandeur of the Symphony No. 5, Peyser's exploration of Mendelssohn's genius is a captivating tribute to one of the greatest composers of the Romantic era.

Genres: Biography, Music History

Themes: Musical genius, Romanticism, Artistic inspiration
