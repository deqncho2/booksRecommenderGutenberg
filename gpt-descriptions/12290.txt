Synopsis: In 'The Church and Modern Life' by Washington Gladden, a renowned theologian and social reformer, the reader is taken on a thought-provoking journey that explores the intersection of religion and the challenges of modern society. Through a series of insightful essays, Gladden delves into pressing issues such as poverty, labor rights, and the role of the church in addressing these concerns. With a captivating blend of historical analysis, philosophical reflections, and practical suggestions, Gladden invites readers to critically examine the role of the church in shaping and responding to the complexities of the modern world. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Theology, Social Commentary

Themes: Religion and social justice, Church's responsibility in society, Ethics and morality in modern life
