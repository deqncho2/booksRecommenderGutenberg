Synopsis: 

In this captivating volume of the Encyclopaedia Britannica, 11th Edition, readers are transported to a world where the lives of historical figures and the intricacies of political systems intertwine. From the legendary Roman statesman Cincinnatus to the concept of cleruchy, this volume delves into the fascinating stories and concepts that have shaped civilizations throughout history. Discover the rise and fall of empires, the strategies of great leaders, and the complexities of governance in this engrossing exploration of human history.

Genres: Historical reference, Biography, Political science

Themes: Leadership, Governance, Historical events
