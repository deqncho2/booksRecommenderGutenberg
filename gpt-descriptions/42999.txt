Synopsis: 
In 'Old Boston Taverns and Tavern Clubs', Samuel Adams Drake takes us on a captivating journey through the rich history of Boston's taverns and the vibrant social scene that thrived within them. From the bustling waterfront establishments to the hidden gems tucked away in narrow alleyways, Drake unveils the fascinating stories of these iconic gathering places that shaped the city's identity. With vivid descriptions and intriguing anecdotes, he introduces us to the colorful characters, lively debates, and secret societies that frequented these taverns, offering a glimpse into the heart and soul of old Boston. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travelogue, Cultural history

Themes: Social life and customs, Urban development, Community identity
