Synopsis: In 'The Census in Moscow' by Leo Tolstoy, the reader is transported to the bustling city of Moscow, where a seemingly ordinary event, the census, sets off a chain of unexpected encounters and revelations. As the city's inhabitants are counted and their lives intersect, the narrative delves into the complexities of human nature, exploring the desires, secrets, and struggles that lie beneath the surface of society. With Tolstoy's masterful storytelling, this captivating tale takes readers on a journey through the lives of diverse characters, each with their own unique story to tell.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Realism

Themes: Human nature, Society, Identity, Morality
