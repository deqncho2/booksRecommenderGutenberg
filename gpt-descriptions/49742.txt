Synopsis: In "The Battle of April 19, 1775: in Lexington, Concord, Lincoln, Arlington, Cambridge, Somerville and Charlestown, Massachusetts," Frank Warren Coburn takes readers on a gripping journey through the events that unfolded on a fateful day in American history. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Coburn brings to life the intense clashes between British troops and American militia in multiple towns and cities across Massachusetts. From the initial skirmishes in Lexington and Concord to the dramatic confrontations in Lincoln, Arlington, Cambridge, Somerville, and Charlestown, this book offers a comprehensive account of the pivotal battles that ignited the American Revolution. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American Revolution

Themes: Patriotism, Courage, Conflict, Revolution, Historical significance
