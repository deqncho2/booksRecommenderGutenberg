Synopsis: In 'The Literary Discipline' by John Erskine, a renowned professor embarks on a daring experiment to challenge his students' perception of literature. As he delves into the minds of his pupils, he uncovers hidden desires, secret passions, and the power of storytelling. With each lesson, the boundaries between reality and fiction blur, leading to unexpected consequences that will forever change their lives. This captivating novel explores the transformative nature of literature and the profound impact it can have on individuals.

Genres: Fiction, Literary Fiction, Psychological Fiction

Themes: The power of literature, self-discovery, the blurred line between reality and fiction
