Synopsis: 

In "Narrative of the Captivity and Restoration of Mrs. Mary Rowlandson," Mary White Rowlandson recounts her harrowing experience as a captive during the violent conflict between Native Americans and English settlers in 17th century New England. Taken from her home by a Native American tribe, Mary endures unimaginable hardships and witnesses the brutal realities of war. Through her captivating narrative, she offers a unique perspective on the clash of cultures and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: Autobiography, Historical Fiction, Captivity Narrative

Themes: Survival, Cultural Conflict, Faith and Religion
