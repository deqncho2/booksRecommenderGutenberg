Synopsis: 
In "Slavery: letters and speeches," Horace Mann presents a collection of powerful and thought-provoking letters and speeches that delve into the complex and deeply troubling issue of slavery. Through his eloquent words, Mann explores the moral, social, and political implications of slavery, shedding light on the experiences of both the enslaved and the abolitionists who fought for their freedom. From heart-wrenching personal accounts to impassioned pleas for justice, this collection offers a poignant and eye-opening perspective on one of the darkest chapters in American history.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Historical, Political

Themes: 
Slavery, Morality, Social Justice, Abolition, Human Rights
