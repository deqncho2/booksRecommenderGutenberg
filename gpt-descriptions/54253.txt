Synopsis: 

'A Selected Bibliography of Virginia, 1607-1699' is a comprehensive compilation of historical documents and writings that offers a fascinating glimpse into the early years of Virginia's colonization. Written by John M. Jennings and E. G. Swem, this meticulously researched bibliography presents a treasure trove of primary sources, including letters, diaries, and official records, shedding light on the challenges, triumphs, and complexities of the Virginia colony during its formative years. From the struggles of the Jamestown settlers to the establishment of the House of Burgesses, this bibliography provides a valuable resource for anyone interested in delving into the rich history of early Virginia.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Reference

Themes: Early American colonization, Virginia history, Primary sources, Historical research
