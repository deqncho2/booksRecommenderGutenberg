Synopsis: In 'The Religion of Politics', Reverend Ezra S. Gannett delivers a powerful sermon before the Governor, Lieutenant Governor, Council, and Legislature of Massachusetts. With eloquence and conviction, Gannett explores the intricate relationship between religion and politics, delving into the moral responsibilities of those in power and the impact of their decisions on society. Through thought-provoking anecdotes and biblical references, he challenges his audience to reflect on the true essence of politics and the role it plays in shaping a just and righteous society.

Genres: Sermon, Political discourse, Religious literature

Themes: Religion and politics, Moral responsibility, Social justice
