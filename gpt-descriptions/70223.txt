Synopsis: 

"Two Colored Women with the American Expeditionary Forces" is a captivating memoir that follows the extraordinary journey of Addie W. Hunton and Kathryn M. Johnson, two African American women who served as YMCA workers during World War I. Through their vivid and heartfelt accounts, the reader is transported to the frontlines of the war, where these courageous women faced numerous challenges and triumphs. As they navigate the complexities of war, discrimination, and personal growth, their experiences shed light on the untold stories of African American women in the military during a time of immense social change.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Racism and Discrimination, Women's Empowerment, Personal Growth
