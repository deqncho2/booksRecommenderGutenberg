Synopsis: In 'A Man in the Zoo' by David Garnett, we follow the peculiar story of a man who willingly becomes an exhibit in a zoo. As the visitors observe him in his cage, they are drawn into a world of human curiosity, animal instincts, and the blurred lines between freedom and captivity. With unexpected twists and thought-provoking insights, this captivating tale explores the complexities of human nature and the consequences of our choices.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Psychological

Themes: Identity, Conformity, Freedom, Human Nature
