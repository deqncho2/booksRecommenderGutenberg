Synopsis: In "Fishing from the Earliest Times," Radcliffe, William takes us on a captivating journey through the history of fishing, revealing the fascinating and often overlooked aspects of this ancient practice. From the primitive techniques of our ancestors to the sophisticated methods of modern anglers, this book explores the evolution of fishing and its profound impact on human civilization. Delving into the cultural, social, and environmental dimensions of fishing, Radcliffe uncovers surprising stories, remarkable achievements, and even some hidden dangers lurking beneath the tranquil surface of this beloved pastime. Prepare to be hooked by the rich tapestry of tales that span across continents and centuries, as we discover the profound connection between humanity and the art of fishing.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Outdoor Recreation

Themes: Human ingenuity, Cultural significance, Environmental impact
