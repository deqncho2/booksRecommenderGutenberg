Synopsis: In the fourth volume of his captivating diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a journey through the tumultuous months of March and April, 1659-1660. As Pepys navigates the political landscape of Restoration England, we witness his encounters with influential figures, his reflections on the changing times, and his personal struggles. From secret meetings to scandalous affairs, Pepys' diary offers a fascinating glimpse into the complexities of life during this pivotal period in history.

Genres: Historical, Memoir, Diary

Themes: Politics, Personal Reflection, Social Change
