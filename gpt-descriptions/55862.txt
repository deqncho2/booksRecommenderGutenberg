Synopsis: In 'The Industrial History of England', Henry de Beltgens Gibbins takes readers on a captivating journey through the transformative era of industrialization in England. From the rise of factories and the impact of steam power to the struggles of the working class and the emergence of new social structures, Gibbins paints a vivid picture of the profound changes that swept across the nation. Through engaging anecdotes and meticulous research, he explores the key figures, inventions, and events that shaped England's industrial landscape, offering a fascinating insight into the triumphs and challenges of this pivotal period in history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History

Themes: Industrialization, Social change, Technological advancements
