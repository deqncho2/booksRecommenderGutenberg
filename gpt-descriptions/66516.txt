Synopsis: 

In 'Hawaiian Legends of Volcanoes', W. D. Westervelt takes us on a captivating journey into the rich mythology of Hawaii, exploring the mesmerizing tales surrounding the powerful volcanoes that shape the islands. From the fiery goddess Pele and her epic battles to the mysterious spirits that dwell within the molten depths, this collection of legends offers a fascinating glimpse into the ancient beliefs and traditions of the Hawaiian people. As the stories unfold, readers will be transported to a world where gods and humans intertwine, where the forces of nature hold both beauty and destruction, and where the power of the volcanoes is both feared and revered.

Genres: Mythology, Folklore, Cultural Studies

Themes: Power of nature, Gods and humans, Beliefs and traditions
