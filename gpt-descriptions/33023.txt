Synopsis: In "An Address, Delivered Before the Was-ah Ho-de-no-son-ne or New Confederacy of the Iroquois: Also, Genundewah, a Poem," Henry Rowe Schoolcraft and William H. C. Hosmer take readers on a captivating journey into the world of the Iroquois Confederacy. Through a combination of prose and poetry, they explore the rich history, culture, and traditions of the Iroquois people, shedding light on their unique way of life and their enduring legacy. This work offers a profound and enlightening perspective on the Iroquois Confederacy, inviting readers to delve into the depths of Native American history and heritage.

Genres: Historical, Cultural, Poetry

Themes: Native American culture, Tradition, Identity, Heritage
