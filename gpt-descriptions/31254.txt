Synopsis: In 'The Conservation of Races: The American Negro Academy. Occasional Papers No. 2', W.E.B. Du Bois delves into the complex and often overlooked topic of race conservation. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Du Bois explores the significance of preserving and understanding different racial identities, particularly focusing on the experiences of African Americans in America. With a keen eye for historical context and a passionate voice, Du Bois challenges conventional notions of race and offers insightful perspectives on the importance of racial diversity and equality.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Cultural studies

Themes: Race, Identity, Equality, Cultural preservation, Social justice
