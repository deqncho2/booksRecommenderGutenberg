Synopsis: 

In "Disraeli: A Study in Personality and Ideas" by Walter Sichel, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the life and mind of one of Britain's most enigmatic political figures. This insightful biography delves deep into the complexities of Benjamin Disraeli, a man who defied societal expectations and rose to become a prominent statesman and novelist in the 19th century. Sichel's meticulous research and engaging narrative style bring to life the fascinating world of politics, power struggles, and personal triumphs that shaped Disraeli's extraordinary career. From his early struggles as an outsider to his eventual rise to the highest echelons of British politics, this book offers a compelling exploration of the man behind the public persona. 

Genres: Biography, History, Political Science

Themes: Ambition, Identity, Power, Social Change
