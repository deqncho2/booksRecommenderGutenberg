Synopsis: In "What Prohibition Has Done to America" by Franklin, Fabian, the reader is taken on a captivating journey through the consequences of a controversial law that changed the course of American history. Through vivid storytelling and compelling anecdotes, the author explores the unexpected and far-reaching effects of Prohibition on society, politics, and the economy. From the rise of organized crime to the emergence of secret speakeasies, this thought-provoking work sheds light on the hidden side of a nation grappling with the consequences of its own decisions.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Commentary

Themes: Prohibition, Social Change, Crime, Politics, Cultural Shift
