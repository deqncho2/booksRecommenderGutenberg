Synopsis: In 'German Atrocities: Their Nature and Philosophy', Hillis delves into the dark depths of the German atrocities committed during World War I, uncovering the chilling nature and underlying philosophy behind these acts. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, the author sheds light on the horrors that unfolded, leaving readers captivated by the shocking revelations and eager to understand the motivations behind such brutality.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War literature, Investigative journalism

Themes: War crimes, Morality, Nationalism, Propaganda
