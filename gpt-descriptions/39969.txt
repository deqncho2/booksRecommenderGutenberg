Synopsis: In 'A Civic Biology, Presented in Problems', George W. Hunter takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of biology. Through a series of thought-provoking problems and engaging examples, Hunter explores the intricate web of life, from the smallest microorganisms to the complex ecosystems that surround us. As readers delve into the pages of this book, they will uncover the secrets of genetics, evolution, and the interconnectedness of all living things. With its accessible language and captivating storytelling, 'A Civic Biology' is sure to ignite a sense of wonder and curiosity about the natural world.

Genres: Science, Education, Non-fiction

Themes: Biology, Genetics, Evolution, Interconnectedness of Life
