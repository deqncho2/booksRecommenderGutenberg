Synopsis: 
'For the Term of His Natural Life' by Marcus Clarke is a gripping historical novel set in the early 19th century. It follows the life of Rufus Dawes, a young man wrongly convicted of a crime he did not commit and sentenced to a life of hard labor in the harsh penal colony of Van Diemen's Land. As Rufus endures unimaginable hardships and battles against the injustices of the system, he becomes entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and revenge. This epic tale of survival and redemption will keep readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

Genres: Historical fiction, Adventure, Romance

Themes: Injustice, Survival, Love and Betrayal
