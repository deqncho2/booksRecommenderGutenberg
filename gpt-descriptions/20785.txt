Synopsis: 

'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume XIII, Oklahoma Narratives' is a captivating collection of firsthand accounts that unveils the untold stories of slavery in Oklahoma. Through interviews with former slaves, this volume sheds light on the unique experiences, struggles, and triumphs of individuals who were once enslaved in this region. From tales of resilience and resistance to narratives of love and loss, these personal testimonies provide a powerful and poignant glimpse into a dark chapter of American history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Oral History

Themes: Slavery, Resistance, Resilience, Identity, Freedom
