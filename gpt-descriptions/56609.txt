Synopsis: In the second volume of 'A History of the British Army', Sir John William Fortescue delves into the captivating story of the British Army from its early beginnings to the conclusion of the Seven Years' War. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Fortescue explores the triumphs and challenges faced by the army, from the legendary battles of Marlborough to the strategic maneuvers of the Seven Years' War. This volume offers a comprehensive and enthralling account of the British Army's growth, tactics, and the influential figures who shaped its history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Warfare and strategy, Leadership and command, Evolution of military tactics, Political and social impact of war
