Synopsis: In 'The House of the Wolfings: A Tale of the House of the Wolfings and All the Kindreds of the Mark Written in Prose and in Verse', William Morris takes us on a captivating journey to a distant time and place. Set in a mythical land known as the Mark, the story follows the Wolfings, a noble and valiant tribe, as they face a formidable enemy threatening their way of life. As battles are fought and alliances are forged, the Wolfings must confront their deepest fears and make sacrifices for the survival of their people. With its rich blend of prose and verse, this epic tale immerses readers in a world of heroism, love, and the enduring power of community.

Genres: Epic, Fantasy, Poetry

Themes: Heroism, Love, Community, Sacrifice
