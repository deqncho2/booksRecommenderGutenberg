Synopsis: In "Elsie Inglis: The Woman with the Torch" by Eva Shaw McLaren, readers are transported to the early 20th century and introduced to the remarkable life of Elsie Inglis. Born into a time when women's rights were limited, Elsie defied societal expectations and became a pioneering doctor, suffragist, and humanitarian. From her struggles to gain acceptance in the medical field to her tireless efforts during World War I, Elsie's story is one of resilience, determination, and unwavering dedication to her beliefs. This captivating biography sheds light on a forgotten heroine who left an indelible mark on history.

Genres: Biography, History, Women's Studies

Themes: Gender equality, Social activism, Resilience
