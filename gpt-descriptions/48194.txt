Synopsis: 
In the Ryerson Memorial Volume, J. George Hodgins presents a captivating account of the unveiling of the Ryerson statue on Queen's birthday in 1889. Through vivid descriptions and historical anecdotes, Hodgins takes readers on a journey that explores the significance of this event and the legacy of Egerton Ryerson. From the grandeur of the Education department's grounds to the emotions evoked by the statue, this volume offers a unique glimpse into a momentous occasion that celebrates a prominent figure in Canadian history.

Genres: Historical, Biography, Commemorative

Themes: Legacy, Education, Historical Significance
