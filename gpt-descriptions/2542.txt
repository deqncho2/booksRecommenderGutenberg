Synopsis: 

'A Doll's House' by Henrik Ibsen is a gripping play that follows the life of Nora Helmer, a seemingly contented wife and mother living in 19th-century Norway. As the story unfolds, we witness the unraveling of Nora's carefully constructed facade, as she begins to question societal expectations and her own identity. With unexpected twists and turns, this thought-provoking play delves into themes of gender roles, societal norms, and the pursuit of personal freedom.

Genres: Drama, Realism

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, personal freedom
