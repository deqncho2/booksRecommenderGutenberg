Synopsis: 

In "Stirling Castle, its place in Scottish history" by Stair-Kerr, Eric, embark on a captivating journey through time as the author unveils the rich and tumultuous history of one of Scotland's most iconic landmarks. From its origins as a strategic fortress to its pivotal role in the Wars of Independence, this book delves into the fascinating stories and legends that have shaped Stirling Castle into a symbol of Scottish resilience and pride. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Stair-Kerr brings to life the battles, sieges, and royal intrigues that unfolded within the castle's ancient walls, captivating readers with every turn of the page.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Scottish history, Architecture

Themes: Power and politics, National identity, Military history
