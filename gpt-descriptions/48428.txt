Synopsis: 

'America's Munitions 1917-1918' takes readers on a captivating journey through the crucial years of World War I, exploring the remarkable efforts of the United States War Department in supplying munitions to support the war effort. This meticulously researched and engagingly written account unveils the untold stories of the men and women who worked tirelessly to produce and deliver the weapons and ammunition that played a pivotal role in shaping the outcome of the war. From the bustling factories to the treacherous supply lines, the book offers a gripping narrative that sheds light on the challenges, triumphs, and sacrifices made by those involved in the munitions industry during this critical period in history.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Military History

Themes: War effort, Industrial production, Supply chain logistics, Patriotism, Sacrifice
