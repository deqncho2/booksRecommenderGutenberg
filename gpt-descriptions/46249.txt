Synopsis: 

In 'A History of the Cambridge University Press, 1521-1921', author S. C. Roberts takes readers on a captivating journey through the centuries, unveiling the fascinating story behind one of the world's most renowned publishing houses. From its humble beginnings in the 16th century to its evolution into a global institution, this book delves into the triumphs, challenges, and pivotal moments that shaped the Cambridge University Press into what it is today. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Roberts paints a vivid picture of the individuals, innovations, and controversies that have left an indelible mark on the world of academia and publishing.

Genres: History, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Academic institutions, Publishing industry, Intellectual history
