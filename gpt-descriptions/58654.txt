Synopsis: In "The Psycho-Analytic Study of the Family," Flugel delves into the intricate dynamics of family relationships, uncovering hidden desires, conflicts, and unconscious motivations. Through the lens of psychoanalysis, he explores the complex interplay between parents and children, siblings, and the extended family, shedding light on the profound impact these relationships have on our psychological development. With captivating case studies and thought-provoking analysis, Flugel invites readers to embark on a fascinating journey into the depths of the family psyche.

Genres: Psychology, Family Studies, Psychoanalysis

Themes: Family dynamics, Unconscious motivations, Psychological development
