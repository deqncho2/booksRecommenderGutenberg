Synopsis: In 'The Early Introduction of Bogus Freemasonry in the United States of America and Texas Among Colored Masons', author C. L. Mitchell delves into the intriguing history of the introduction of counterfeit Freemasonry among African American Masons in the United States and Texas. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Mitchell uncovers the clandestine activities and deceptive practices that threatened the integrity of the Masonic brotherhood. As he unravels the web of deceit, the reader is taken on a journey of mystery and intrigue, exploring the impact of these fraudulent practices on the African American Masonic community. This thought-provoking work sheds light on a little-known aspect of American history and raises important questions about trust, loyalty, and the pursuit of truth.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, African American Studies, Secret Societies

Themes: Deception, Identity, Brotherhood, Historical Injustice
