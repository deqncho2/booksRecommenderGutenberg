Synopsis: In 'The Able McLaughlins' by Margaret Wilson, follow the captivating journey of a young Irish immigrant family as they settle in the American Midwest during the late 19th century. Filled with love, heartache, and the pursuit of the American Dream, this novel explores the trials and triumphs of the McLaughlin family as they navigate the challenges of a new land and strive to build a better future for themselves and their children. As secrets unravel and unexpected events unfold, readers will be drawn into a tale of resilience, sacrifice, and the enduring power of family bonds.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Family Saga

Themes: Immigration, Identity, Love and Loss, Resilience
