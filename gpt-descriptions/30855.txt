Synopsis: In "The Wife of Sir Isaac Harman," H.G. Wells delves into the complex dynamics of a marriage as Lady Harman navigates the expectations and limitations placed upon her by society. As she grapples with her own desires and ambitions, she finds herself torn between loyalty to her husband and the pursuit of her own happiness. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century England, this thought-provoking novel explores the sacrifices and compromises made in the name of love and societal expectations.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Social Commentary

Themes: Marriage, Gender Roles, Individuality, Society's Expectations
