Synopsis: In "Wagner" by John F. Runciman, the reader is taken on a captivating journey through the life of one of the most influential composers in history. From his humble beginnings to his rise to fame, this biography delves into the complex personality and creative genius of Richard Wagner. As the narrative unfolds, the reader is immersed in the world of music, art, and politics, witnessing the triumphs and controversies that shaped Wagner's career. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Runciman paints a vivid portrait of a man whose music continues to captivate audiences to this day.

Genres: Biography, Music, History

Themes: Artistic genius, Ambition, Controversy, Influence
