Synopsis:
In 'Sociology and Modern Social Problems', Charles A. Ellwood delves into the intricate web of society, exploring the underlying causes and consequences of various social issues that plague our modern world. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Ellwood uncovers the complex interplay between individuals, institutions, and societal structures, shedding light on the root causes of problems such as poverty, crime, and inequality. With compelling examples and insightful observations, this book challenges readers to critically examine the social fabric that surrounds them and consider the potential for positive change.

Genres:
Sociology, Social Sciences, Non-fiction

Themes:
Social problems, Inequality, Poverty, Crime, Societal structures
