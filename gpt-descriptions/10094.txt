Synopsis: In 'A Soldier of Virginia: A Tale of Colonel Washington and Braddock's Defeat', Burton Egbert Stevenson takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of the French and Indian War. Set against the backdrop of the American wilderness, the story follows a young and ambitious George Washington as he navigates the treacherous world of military strategy, political intrigue, and personal growth. As Washington faces the challenges of leading his men and confronting the formidable French forces, he must also grapple with his own doubts and fears. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, Stevenson paints a vivid picture of the harsh realities of war and the indomitable spirit of a young soldier determined to defend his homeland.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Patriotism, Leadership, Personal Growth, Courage
