Synopsis: In "Reconstruction in Philosophy," John Dewey takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the realms of philosophy, challenging traditional notions and offering a fresh perspective on the subject. With his characteristic clarity and intellectual rigor, Dewey explores the interconnectedness of philosophy with other disciplines, urging readers to reconsider their understanding of reality, knowledge, and the human experience. Through engaging examples and insightful analysis, Dewey invites readers to embark on a philosophical adventure that will reshape their understanding of the world.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Pragmatism, Reconstruction, Epistemology, Metaphysics
