Synopsis: 
In 'A Defence of Poetry and Other Essays', Percy Bysshe Shelley embarks on a captivating exploration of the power and significance of poetry. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Shelley delves into the nature of imagination, the role of the poet in society, and the transformative potential of art. With eloquent prose and profound insights, he challenges conventional notions and invites readers to reconsider the true essence of poetry and its impact on the human experience.

Genres: 
Essay, Literary Criticism, Philosophy

Themes: 
The power of imagination, The role of the poet in society, The transformative potential of art
