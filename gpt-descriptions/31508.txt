Synopsis: 

In 'Euthenics, the science of controllable environment: A plea for better living conditions as a first step toward higher human efficiency,' Ellen H. Richards presents a groundbreaking exploration of how our environment directly impacts our well-being and potential. Through a compelling blend of scientific research, personal anecdotes, and thought-provoking analysis, Richards makes a passionate plea for improving our living conditions as a means to enhance human efficiency and overall quality of life. From examining the impact of clean air and water to the importance of proper nutrition and education, this thought-provoking work challenges readers to reconsider the significance of their surroundings and the potential for positive change.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Social Sciences

Themes: Environmentalism, Human potential, Quality of life, Social reform
