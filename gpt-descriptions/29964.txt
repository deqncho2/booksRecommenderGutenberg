Synopsis: In 'Clarissa: Preface, Hints of Prefaces, and Postscript', Samuel Richardson introduces readers to the captivating world of Clarissa Harlowe, a young woman whose life is about to take a dramatic turn. Through a series of letters, Richardson sets the stage for a story filled with forbidden love, societal expectations, and the struggle for independence. As the plot unfolds, readers will be drawn into a web of secrets, desires, and the complexities of human relationships. Will Clarissa find the strength to navigate the challenges that lie ahead? Genres: Epistolary novel, Romance, Drama. Themes: Love and desire, Morality and virtue, Gender roles and societal expectations.
