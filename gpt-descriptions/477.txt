Synopsis: In 'Criminal Sociology' by Enrico Ferri, delve into the fascinating world of criminal behavior as the renowned criminologist takes you on a captivating journey through the minds of offenders. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Ferri explores the complex factors that contribute to criminality, from social and economic conditions to psychological and biological influences. With gripping case studies and thought-provoking theories, this book offers a unique perspective on the causes and consequences of crime, challenging conventional wisdom and shedding light on the darkest corners of human nature.

Genres: Non-fiction, Criminology, Sociology

Themes: Criminal behavior, Social factors, Psychological influences, Biological determinism
