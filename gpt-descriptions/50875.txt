Synopsis: 
In "The Business Library: What it is and what it does," Louise B. Krause takes readers on an intriguing journey through the world of business libraries. With a captivating blend of anecdotes, insights, and practical advice, Krause explores the hidden treasures and untapped potential of these unique institutions. From the fascinating history of business libraries to their modern-day role in supporting innovation and entrepreneurship, this book offers a captivating exploration of a lesser-known aspect of the business world. Whether you're a business professional, a student, or simply curious about the inner workings of these specialized libraries, Krause's engaging narrative will leave you with a newfound appreciation for the power of knowledge and information in the business realm.

Genres: Non-fiction, Business, Library Science

Themes: Knowledge management, Information access, Innovation in business
