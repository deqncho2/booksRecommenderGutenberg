Synopsis: In 'Greek Biology & Greek Medicine', Charles Singer takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient world, exploring the groundbreaking discoveries and revolutionary ideas that shaped the fields of biology and medicine. From the intricate theories of Hippocrates to the fascinating observations of Aristotle, Singer delves into the minds of these brilliant thinkers, revealing their innovative approaches to understanding the human body and the natural world. With vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the origins of modern biology and medicine, shedding light on the enduring legacy of ancient Greek scholars.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Science

Themes: Ancient knowledge, Scientific revolution, Human anatomy, Natural philosophy
