Synopsis: 
'A Source-Book of English Social History' takes readers on a captivating journey through the various aspects of English society, from the Middle Ages to the early 20th century. Through a collection of primary sources, author M. E. Monckton Jones provides a vivid and immersive exploration of the lives, struggles, and triumphs of ordinary people throughout history. From the bustling streets of medieval towns to the industrial revolution and the suffrage movement, this book offers a unique perspective on the social fabric of England. 

Genres: History, Social Sciences, Non-fiction

Themes: Social class, Gender roles, Economic change, Political movements
