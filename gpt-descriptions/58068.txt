Synopsis: In "Wagner as Man & Artist," Ernest Newman delves into the life and artistry of one of the most controversial and influential composers in history, Richard Wagner. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Newman explores the complex personality and creative genius of Wagner, shedding light on his tumultuous personal life, his revolutionary musical innovations, and the profound impact he had on the world of opera. From Wagner's early struggles and artistic aspirations to his controversial political beliefs and his enduring legacy, this captivating biography offers a comprehensive and thought-provoking exploration of the man behind the music.

Genres: Biography, Music, History

Themes: Artistic genius, Controversy, Influence, Personal struggles, Political beliefs
