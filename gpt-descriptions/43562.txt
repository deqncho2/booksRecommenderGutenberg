Synopsis: 

'Company G: A Record of the Services of One Company of the 157th N. Y. Vols. in the War of the Rebellion from Sept. 19, 1862, to July 10, 1865' by A. R. Barlow is a gripping historical account that follows the experiences of Company G, a group of soldiers from the 157th New York Volunteer Infantry Regiment, during the American Civil War. From their initial enlistment in 1862 to their final days in 1865, the book chronicles the trials, triumphs, and sacrifices of these brave men as they navigate the horrors of war, forge unbreakable bonds, and confront the profound impact of their service on their lives.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history

Themes: Patriotism, Brotherhood, Sacrifice, The human cost of war
