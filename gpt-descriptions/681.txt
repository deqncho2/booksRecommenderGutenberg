Synopsis: In "Creatures That Once Were Men" by Maksim Gorky, we are introduced to a group of impoverished workers living in a small Russian village. As they struggle to survive in harsh conditions, their lives become intertwined with the arrival of a mysterious stranger. This encounter leads to a series of events that challenge their beliefs, ignite their desires, and ultimately force them to confront the true nature of their existence.

Genres: Literary fiction, social realism, allegory

Themes: Poverty and class struggle, human dignity, the search for meaning, the power of hope
