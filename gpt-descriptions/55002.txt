Synopsis: 

'NetWorld! What People Are Really Doing on the Internet and What It Means to You' takes readers on a captivating journey through the hidden corners of the internet, revealing the shocking and fascinating truths about how people truly behave online. From the darkest corners of the dark web to the most popular social media platforms, author David H. Rothman uncovers the secrets, scandals, and unexpected connections that shape our digital lives. With a mix of investigative journalism and personal anecdotes, this eye-opening book explores the profound impact of the internet on society and challenges readers to question their own online behaviors.

Genres: Non-fiction, Technology, Social Sciences

Themes: Internet culture, Online behavior, Privacy, Digital identity, Social media impact
