Synopsis:
In 'Wear and Tear; Or, Hints for the Overworked', Dr. Mitchell delves into the often overlooked consequences of overworking oneself. Through a series of captivating anecdotes and insightful observations, he explores the physical, mental, and emotional toll that excessive labor can have on individuals. From the bustling streets of New York City to the serene countryside, the author takes readers on a journey to uncover the hidden dangers of an overworked society. With a blend of wit, wisdom, and practical advice, 'Wear and Tear' offers a thought-provoking exploration of the human cost of relentless work.

Genres:
Non-fiction, Self-help, Medical

Themes:
Work-life balance, Burnout, Mental health, Self-care
