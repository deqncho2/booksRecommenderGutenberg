Synopsis: 
'Toronto of Old: Collections and recollections illustrative of the early settlement and social life of the capital of Ontario' takes readers on a captivating journey through the early days of Toronto, providing a vivid portrayal of its settlement and social fabric. Through a collection of stories, anecdotes, and historical accounts, author Henry Scadding paints a rich and immersive picture of the city's past, offering glimpses into the lives of its inhabitants, their struggles, triumphs, and the unique challenges they faced. From the bustling streets and lively markets to the grand architecture and cultural events, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the vibrant tapestry of Toronto's early years.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Local history

Themes: Early settlement, Social life, Cultural heritage, Urban development
