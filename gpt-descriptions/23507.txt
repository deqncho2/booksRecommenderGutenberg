Synopsis: 
In "A Short History of Pittsburgh: 1758-1908," Samuel Harden Church takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of one of America's most iconic cities. From its humble beginnings as a strategic military outpost during the French and Indian War to its transformation into a booming industrial center, Church delves into the triumphs, struggles, and pivotal moments that shaped Pittsburgh's unique identity. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, he explores the city's pivotal role in the American Revolution, its rise as the "Steel City" during the Industrial Revolution, and the challenges it faced during times of economic downturn. Church's narrative is filled with fascinating anecdotes, colorful characters, and the indomitable spirit of a city that has constantly reinvented itself. 

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Urban development, Industrialization, American history
