Synopsis: 

In 'Four Months in Libby and the Campaign Against Atlanta', author Isaac N. Johnston takes readers on a captivating journey through his personal experiences as a prisoner of war during the American Civil War. Johnston vividly recounts his time spent in the notorious Libby Prison in Richmond, Virginia, and his subsequent involvement in the fierce campaign against Atlanta. Through his detailed and poignant narrative, Johnston offers a unique perspective on the harsh realities of war, the resilience of the human spirit, and the bonds formed in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: Resilience, Camaraderie, Survival, The Horrors of War
