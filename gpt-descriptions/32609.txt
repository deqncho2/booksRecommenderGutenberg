Synopsis: In 'The Children of the Poor', Jacob A. Riis takes readers on a gripping journey through the dark underbelly of New York City in the late 19th century. Through vivid descriptions and powerful photographs, Riis exposes the harsh realities faced by the impoverished children living in the slums. From their daily struggles for survival to their dreams of a better future, this eye-opening account sheds light on a forgotten segment of society, challenging readers to confront the social injustices of the time.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social documentary, Photojournalism

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Childhood, Urbanization
