Synopsis: In "Address to the Non-Slaveholders of the South: on the Social and Political Evils of Slavery," Tappan, Lewis delivers a powerful and thought-provoking message to those living in the South who do not own slaves. Through a compelling exploration of the social and political consequences of slavery, Tappan challenges the non-slaveholders to confront the moral implications of their society. With eloquence and conviction, he exposes the hidden truths and injustices that permeate the institution of slavery, urging his readers to take a stand against this deeply entrenched system. This address serves as a call to action, igniting a spark of awareness and empathy that has the potential to reshape the future of the South.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political discourse, Social commentary

Themes: Slavery, Morality, Social justice, Political activism
