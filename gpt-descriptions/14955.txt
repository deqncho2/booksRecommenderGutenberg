Synopsis: In 'The Kings and Queens of England, with Other Poems', Mary Ann H. T. Bigelow takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of England's monarchs, intertwining their stories with her own poetic musings. From the legendary figures of King Arthur and Queen Guinevere to the tumultuous reigns of Henry VIII and Elizabeth I, Bigelow's vivid verses bring to life the triumphs, tragedies, and scandals of these iconic rulers. Alongside these historical poems, the collection also delves into themes of love, power, loyalty, and the enduring legacy of royalty. Prepare to be enthralled by this poetic exploration of England's regal past.

Genres: Poetry, Historical Fiction

Themes: History, Royalty, Love, Power, Loyalty
