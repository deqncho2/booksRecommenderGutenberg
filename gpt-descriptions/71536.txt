Synopsis: 

In "Books and their makers during the Middle Ages: A study of the conditions of the production and distribution of literature from the fall of the Roman Empire to the close of the seventeenth century, Vol. I," George Haven Putnam takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of book production and distribution during the Middle Ages. From the decline of the Roman Empire to the seventeenth century, this volume explores the intricate processes involved in creating and disseminating literature, shedding light on the challenges, innovations, and cultural transformations that shaped the literary landscape of the time.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Literary criticism, Cultural history

Themes: Book production, Distribution of literature, Cultural transformation, Historical context
