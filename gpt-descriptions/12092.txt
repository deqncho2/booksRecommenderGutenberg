Synopsis:
"The Oxford Movement; Twelve Years, 1833-1845" by R.W. Church is a captivating historical account that delves into a transformative period in the Church of England. Set against the backdrop of the early Victorian era, this book explores the rise of a group of influential individuals who sought to revive and restore the traditions and rituals of the Anglican Church. As tensions rise and conflicts ensue, the reader is taken on a journey through the intellectual, political, and religious landscapes of the time, witnessing the profound impact of this movement on both the Church and society at large.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Religious history, Biography

Themes:
Religious revival, Tradition vs. modernity, Intellectual and theological debates, Influence of religion on society
