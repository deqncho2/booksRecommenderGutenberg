Synopsis: In 'P.'s Correspondence', Hawthorne introduces us to a mysterious character named P., who becomes entangled in a series of peculiar and unsettling events. Through a collection of letters, the reader is drawn into P.'s world, filled with enigmatic encounters, eerie coincidences, and a growing sense of unease. As the correspondence unfolds, secrets are revealed, and the line between reality and imagination becomes blurred. With each letter, the reader is left questioning the true nature of P.'s experiences and the unsettling truths that lie beneath the surface.

Genres: Mystery, Gothic Fiction, Psychological Thriller

Themes: Identity, Perception, Reality vs. Illusion, Isolation, Psychological Exploration
