Synopsis: In "The uncivilized races of men in all countries of the world; vol. 2 of 2," J. G. Wood takes readers on a captivating journey across the globe, exploring the fascinating customs, traditions, and characteristics of various uncivilized races. From remote tribes in Africa to indigenous communities in the Americas, Wood provides a comprehensive account of their physical attributes, social structures, mentalities, moral values, and religious beliefs. This enlightening volume offers a unique perspective on the diversity of human cultures, shedding light on the lesser-known aspects of our global society.

Genres: Anthropology, Travelogue, Cultural Studies

Themes: Cultural diversity, Human nature, Ethnography
