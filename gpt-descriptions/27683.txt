Synopsis: In "The World I Live In," Helen Keller takes readers on a captivating journey through her unique perspective of the world. Through her eloquent and insightful writing, Keller shares her experiences as a deaf-blind individual, revealing the extraordinary ways in which she perceives and interacts with the world around her. From her profound observations of nature to her deep understanding of human emotions, Keller's narrative offers a profound exploration of the human spirit and the power of resilience.

Genres: Autobiography, Memoir, Non-fiction

Themes: Perception, Resilience, Human connection, Nature, Disability
