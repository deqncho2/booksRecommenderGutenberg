Synopsis: In "The Apparatus Used by the Greeks and Romans in the Setting of Fractures and the Reduction of Dislocations," John Stewart Milne takes us on a captivating journey through the ancient world of medicine. Delving into the techniques and tools employed by the Greeks and Romans to treat fractures and dislocations, Milne uncovers a fascinating array of innovative and sometimes surprising methods. From the ingenious contraptions used to realign bones to the intricate knowledge of anatomy and physiology, this book offers a captivating glimpse into the ancient world of medical practice.

Genres: Medical History, Ancient History, Archaeology

Themes: Ancient Medicine, Surgical Techniques, Innovation
