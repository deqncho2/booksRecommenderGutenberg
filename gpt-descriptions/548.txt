Synopsis: In 'Project Trinity, 1945-1946', Maag and Rohrer take readers on a captivating journey into the top-secret world of the Manhattan Project. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, they unveil the untold story of the scientists, engineers, and military personnel who worked tirelessly to develop the world's first atomic bomb. As the project progresses, tensions rise, ethical dilemmas emerge, and the fate of humanity hangs in the balance. This gripping narrative sheds light on the complex web of scientific, political, and moral challenges faced by those involved in this groundbreaking endeavor.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Science, Military

Themes: Scientific discovery, Ethics, Cold War
