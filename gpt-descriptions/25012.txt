Synopsis: In 'The Case of Wagner, Nietzsche Contra Wagner, and Selected Aphorisms,' Friedrich Nietzsche delves into his complex relationship with the renowned composer Richard Wagner. Through a series of critical essays and aphorisms, Nietzsche explores his admiration for Wagner's artistic genius, while also dissecting the philosophical and moral implications of his work. As Nietzsche grapples with his own conflicting emotions towards Wagner, he delves into themes of art, morality, and the nature of genius, ultimately challenging conventional notions of cultural greatness.

Genres: Philosophy, Criticism, Biography

Themes: Art and its moral implications, Genius and its consequences, The nature of cultural greatness
