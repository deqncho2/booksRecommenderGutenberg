Synopsis: 
In "The Wild Flowers of California: Their Names, Haunts, and Habits" by Mary Elizabeth Parsons, embark on a captivating journey through the diverse landscapes of California, where an enchanting world of wild flowers awaits. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Parsons introduces readers to the hidden beauty of these delicate blooms, revealing their unique names, secret haunts, and fascinating habits. From the vibrant poppies that blanket the hillsides to the elusive orchids that thrive in the shadows, this book is a celebration of the extraordinary flora that graces the Golden State. Whether you are a seasoned botanist or simply a lover of nature, this enchanting guide will transport you to a realm where the wild flowers of California reign supreme.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Botany

Themes: Beauty of nature, Biodiversity, Environmental conservation
