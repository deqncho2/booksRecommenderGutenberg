Synopsis: 

In "Women's Bathing and Swimming Costume in the United States," Claudia Brush Kidwell takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of women's swimwear in America. From the modest and restrictive bathing costumes of the 19th century to the liberating and fashionable designs of the 20th century, this book explores the social, cultural, and technological factors that influenced the transformation of women's swimwear. Through vivid descriptions and captivating historical anecdotes, Kidwell unveils the fascinating stories behind the changing styles, materials, and attitudes towards women's bathing suits. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Fashion

Themes: Gender roles, Body image, Fashion trends, Social change
