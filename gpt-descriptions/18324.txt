Synopsis: 

In "The Third Great Plague: A Discussion of Syphilis for Everyday People," John H. Stokes delves into the fascinating and often misunderstood world of syphilis, unraveling its historical significance and its impact on society. Through a captivating blend of storytelling and scientific analysis, Stokes takes readers on a journey that explores the origins, transmission, and devastating consequences of this infamous disease. From the dark secrets of the past to the modern-day challenges in combating syphilis, this thought-provoking book sheds light on a topic that has long been shrouded in mystery and stigma. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical History, Science

Themes: Disease and Society, Historical Impact, Stigma and Misconceptions
