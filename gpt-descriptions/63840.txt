Synopsis: 

"The History of Oswestry" is a captivating and comprehensive exploration of the town's rich past, spanning from the ancient British era to the present day. Written by William Cathrall, this remarkable work delves into the topography of the borough, its ecclesiastical and civic history, and even delves into fascinating subjects such as botany, geology, statistics, angling, and biography. Additionally, the book provides intriguing sketches of the environs surrounding Oswestry, offering a broader understanding of the region's historical significance. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Local history, Biography

Themes: History, Topography, Local culture, Natural sciences
