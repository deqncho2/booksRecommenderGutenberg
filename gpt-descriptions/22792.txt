Synopsis: 

'Perils and Captivity' is a gripping collection of three true stories that recount the harrowing experiences of individuals facing unimaginable challenges. The first narrative follows the Picard family, who find themselves stranded after the shipwreck of the Medusa in 1816. Their struggle for survival in the midst of treacherous conditions will keep readers on the edge of their seats. The second account tells the tale of M. de Brisson, who endures a captivating captivity in the year 1785. Lastly, the book takes us on a riveting journey with Madame Godin as she navigates the treacherous river of the Amazons in 1770. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Adventure, Survival

Themes: Perseverance, Human resilience, Survival against all odds
