Synopsis: In 'Our Sabbaths in Danger: A Sermon, deprecating the contemplated opening of the Crystal Palace on the Lord's-Day', Daniel Moore delivers a powerful sermon that challenges the potential disruption of the sacred day of rest. As he addresses the controversial plan to open the Crystal Palace on Sundays, Moore delves into the moral implications and societal consequences that such a decision could bring. With eloquence and conviction, he explores the delicate balance between progress and the preservation of religious traditions, urging his audience to reflect on the importance of maintaining the sanctity of the Lord's-Day.

Genres: Sermon, Religious Literature

Themes: Religion and modernity, Sabbath observance, Moral dilemmas
