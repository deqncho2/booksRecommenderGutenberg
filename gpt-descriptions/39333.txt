Synopsis: In 'Curiosities of Human Nature', Samuel G. Goodrich takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricacies of human behavior and the peculiarities that make us who we are. From the bizarre habits of historical figures to the strange customs of different cultures, this book delves into the fascinating and often humorous aspects of human nature. With a blend of wit, curiosity, and insightful observations, Goodrich invites readers to explore the quirks and idiosyncrasies that make us marvel at the diversity and complexity of the human experience.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Anthropology

Themes: Human behavior, Cultural diversity, Quirks and idiosyncrasies
