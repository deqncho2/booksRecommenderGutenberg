Synopsis: In 'A Century of Negro Migration', Carter G. Woodson takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of African American migration in the United States. From the early days of slavery to the Great Migration of the 20th century, Woodson explores the factors that influenced this mass movement of people, shedding light on the struggles, triumphs, and resilience of African Americans seeking a better life. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Woodson paints a vivid picture of the challenges faced by those who migrated, as well as the profound impact their movement had on American society.

Genres: History, Sociology, African American Studies

Themes: Migration, Identity, Social Justice, Racism, Resilience
