Synopsis: In "My Experiences as an Executioner," Berry, James takes readers on a gripping journey into the dark and mysterious world of capital punishment. Through vivid and haunting accounts, the author shares his personal experiences and reflections as an executioner, delving into the moral dilemmas, psychological toll, and unexpected revelations that come with the job. As the narrative unfolds, readers are confronted with thought-provoking questions about justice, humanity, and the consequences of our actions. This captivating memoir offers a unique perspective on a controversial subject, leaving readers both captivated and unsettled.

Genres: Memoir, True Crime, Non-fiction

Themes: Capital punishment, Morality, Justice, Human nature
