Synopsis:
'Neighbors: Life Stories of the Other Half' by Jacob A. Riis is a captivating collection of real-life stories that takes readers on a journey through the gritty streets of New York City in the late 19th century. Through vivid descriptions and powerful narratives, Riis introduces us to the lives of the forgotten and marginalized individuals who make up the "other half" of society. From tenement dwellers to street vendors, Riis sheds light on the struggles, dreams, and resilience of these often overlooked neighbors. As we delve into their stories, we are confronted with the harsh realities of poverty, inequality, and the human spirit's remarkable ability to endure.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social documentary, Journalism

Themes: Poverty, Inequality, Resilience, Social justice
