Synopsis: 

The 'Simple Sabotage Field Manual' is a fascinating and unconventional guide that unveils the art of sabotage in a simple and accessible manner. Originally created during World War II by the United States Office of Strategic Services, this manual provides a unique insight into the tactics and techniques employed by saboteurs to disrupt enemy operations. From subtle acts of inefficiency to clever methods of obstruction, this manual offers a captivating glimpse into the world of sabotage and its potential impact on the enemy.

Genres: Non-fiction, Military, Espionage

Themes: Sabotage, Warfare, Strategic Thinking
