Synopsis: In "What a Young Husband Ought to Know," Stall, Sylvanus takes readers on a captivating journey through the challenges and discoveries of a young husband's life. Set in the early 20th century, this poignant and thought-provoking novel explores the intimate struggles and societal pressures faced by a newly married man. As the protagonist navigates the complexities of love, sexuality, and family planning, readers are drawn into a world of secrets, desires, and the pursuit of knowledge. With its raw emotions and compelling narrative, this book offers a powerful exploration of the human experience and the transformative power of understanding.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Love, Sexuality, Marriage, Family Planning, Social Expectations
