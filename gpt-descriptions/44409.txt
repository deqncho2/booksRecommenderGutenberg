Synopsis: 

'A Thousand Years of Jewish History: From the days of Alexander the Great to the Moslem Conquest of Spain' takes readers on an enthralling journey through the rich and complex tapestry of Jewish history. From the rise of Alexander the Great and the Hellenistic period to the momentous Moslem Conquest of Spain, Maurice H. Harris delves into the triumphs, struggles, and enduring resilience of the Jewish people. This captivating account explores the pivotal events, influential figures, and cultural transformations that shaped Jewish communities across the centuries, offering a comprehensive and enlightening perspective on their remarkable journey.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Jewish history, Cultural studies

Themes: Identity and belonging, Religious persecution and resilience, Cultural exchange and assimilation, Historical continuity and change
