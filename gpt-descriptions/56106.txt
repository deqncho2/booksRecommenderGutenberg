Synopsis: 
In "John Brown's Raid," the United States National Park Service takes us on a captivating journey through one of the most audacious and controversial events in American history. Set in the mid-19th century, this gripping account unveils the daring actions of John Brown and his band of followers as they embark on a mission that would forever change the course of the nation. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the National Park Service brings to life the events leading up to the raid, the intense battle that ensued, and the profound impact it had on the nation's conscience. 

Genres: 
Historical non-fiction, Biography, American history

Themes: 
Social justice, Abolitionism, Civil rights, Heroism, Violence and its consequences
