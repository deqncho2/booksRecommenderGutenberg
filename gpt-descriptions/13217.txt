Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume XII, Ohio Narratives', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a captivating collection of firsthand accounts from former slaves in Ohio. These narratives offer a unique perspective on the experiences of enslaved individuals, their struggles, and their triumphs as they navigated the complexities of slavery in the northern states. Through these powerful stories, readers are transported to a time when freedom was a distant dream for many, shedding light on the resilience and determination of those who fought for their liberation.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes:
Slavery, Freedom, Resilience, Identity, Social justice
