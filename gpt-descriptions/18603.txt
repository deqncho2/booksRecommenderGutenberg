Synopsis: In "What Social Classes Owe to Each Other," William Graham Sumner delves into the complex dynamics of social classes and the obligations they have towards one another. Through thought-provoking analysis and compelling arguments, Sumner challenges conventional notions of social responsibility and explores the consequences of different approaches to societal inequality. This captivating work offers a fresh perspective on the intricate relationship between social classes and the moral obligations that arise from it.

Genres: Political philosophy, Social commentary, Sociology

Themes: Social responsibility, Inequality, Moral obligations
