Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume IX, Mississippi Narratives', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a collection of powerful firsthand accounts from former slaves in Mississippi. These narratives offer a rare glimpse into the harsh realities of slavery, as individuals recount their experiences of bondage, escape, and the challenges they faced in their pursuit of freedom. From tales of resilience and resistance to heart-wrenching stories of separation and loss, this volume paints a vivid picture of the lives of those who endured the horrors of slavery. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Resilience, Resistance, Human rights
