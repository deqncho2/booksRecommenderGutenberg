Synopsis: In 'The Disputed V.C.: A Tale of the Indian Mutiny' by Frederick P. Gibbon, readers are transported to the tumultuous era of the Indian Mutiny. The story revolves around a young British officer, Captain Hugh Warrener, who finds himself caught in the midst of a fierce battle for honor, love, and justice. As Warrener's loyalty is tested and his courage pushed to its limits, he becomes entangled in a web of intrigue and deception that threatens to unravel everything he holds dear. With vivid descriptions of the Indian landscape and gripping action sequences, this historical novel takes readers on a thrilling journey through a pivotal moment in history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, War

Themes: Loyalty, Honor, Love, Justice, Betrayal
