Synopsis: 

In 'Seven and Nine years Among the Camanches and Apaches: An Autobiography', Edwin Eastman takes us on a captivating journey through his extraordinary life as a captive among the Native American tribes of the Camanches and Apaches. From his capture as a young boy to his eventual escape, Eastman's gripping narrative offers a unique and intimate perspective on the customs, traditions, and daily struggles of these indigenous peoples. As he navigates the harsh realities of life in the wilderness, Eastman's story is filled with unexpected friendships, heart-wrenching losses, and moments of profound self-discovery. This autobiography is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit and the power of cultural exchange in shaping one's identity.

Genres: Autobiography, Historical Non-fiction, Adventure

Themes: Cultural exchange, Identity, Resilience
