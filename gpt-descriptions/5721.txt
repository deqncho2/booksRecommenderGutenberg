Synopsis: 
In 'A House of Gentlefolk' by Turgenev, Ivan Sergeevich, we are introduced to a charming and idyllic country estate, where the lives of its inhabitants are about to be disrupted by the arrival of a mysterious and enigmatic stranger. As the story unfolds, secrets are revealed, passions are ignited, and the characters find themselves entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and longing. With its richly drawn characters and evocative descriptions of the Russian countryside, this novel explores the complexities of human relationships and the consequences of hidden desires.

Genres: 
Romance, Drama, Psychological Fiction

Themes: 
Love and desire, Social class and status, Secrets and deception
