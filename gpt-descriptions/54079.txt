Synopsis: In 'Sinners and Saints: A Tour Across the States and Round Them, with Three Months Among the Mormons', Phil Robinson takes readers on a captivating journey through the United States, exploring its diverse landscapes and encountering a wide range of intriguing characters. Along the way, he delves into the secretive world of the Mormons, spending three months among them and uncovering their unique beliefs and practices. From the bustling cities to the remote wilderness, Robinson's vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes paint a vivid picture of America in the late 19th century, offering a fascinating glimpse into the lives of both sinners and saints.

Genres: Travelogue, Non-fiction, History

Themes: Religion, Cultural exploration, Identity
