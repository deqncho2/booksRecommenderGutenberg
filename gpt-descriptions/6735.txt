Synopsis: 

In "Over the Border: Acadia, the Home of 'Evangeline'", Eliza B. Chase takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting landscapes of Acadia, a place that inspired the famous poem "Evangeline". Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Chase explores the rich history, culture, and natural beauty of this unique region. From the charming villages and picturesque coastlines to the resilient Acadian people and their enduring spirit, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into a world that is both familiar and foreign. As the author delves into the origins of "Evangeline" and its impact on the local community, readers will be transported to a place where love, loss, and resilience intertwine, leaving an indelible mark on the hearts of all who visit.

Genres: Travelogue, Historical Non-fiction, Literary Criticism

Themes: History and Heritage, Cultural Identity, Nature and Landscape
