Synopsis: 

In "Drawings in pen & pencil from Dürer's day to ours, with notes and appreciations," George Sheringham takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of art, exploring the evolution of drawing techniques from the Renaissance to the present day. With a keen eye for detail and a deep appreciation for the masters of the craft, Sheringham delves into the intricacies of each artwork, providing insightful notes and reflections that shed light on the artist's intentions and the historical context in which they were created. From the delicate strokes of Albrecht Dürer to the bold lines of contemporary artists, this book offers a visually stunning and intellectually stimulating exploration of the art of drawing.

Genres: Art History, Criticism, Non-fiction

Themes: Evolution of Art, Techniques and Styles, Historical Context
