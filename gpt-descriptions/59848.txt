Synopsis:
In the novel "Kangaroo" by D.H. Lawrence, an English writer named Richard Lovat Somers travels to Australia and becomes entangled in a mysterious and dangerous underground movement. Drawn to the charismatic leader known as Kangaroo, Somers finds himself torn between his own beliefs and the allure of this radical group. As he delves deeper into the movement, he begins to question his own identity and the true nature of power. Set against the backdrop of post-World War I Australia, "Kangaroo" is a gripping exploration of politics, nationalism, and the search for meaning in a rapidly changing world.

Genres:
Literary Fiction, Political Fiction, Historical Fiction

Themes:
Identity, Power, Nationalism, Ideology
