Synopsis: In 'Rhetoric and Poetry in the Renaissance: A Study of Rhetorical Terms in English Renaissance Literary Criticism', Donald Lemen Clark delves into the fascinating world of English Renaissance literary criticism, exploring the intricate relationship between rhetoric and poetry. Through an in-depth analysis of rhetorical terms, Clark uncovers the hidden layers of meaning and the artistry behind the works of renowned Renaissance writers. This captivating study offers a fresh perspective on the power of language and its impact on the literary landscape of the era.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Renaissance Studies, Language and Linguistics

Themes: Rhetoric, Poetry, Language, Artistry, Renaissance Literature
