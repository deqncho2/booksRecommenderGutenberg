Synopsis: 

'Frontier Service During the Rebellion: or, A History of Company K, First Infantry, California Volunteers' by George H. Pettis is a captivating historical account that delves into the experiences of Company K during the American Civil War. Set against the backdrop of the rebellion, the book follows the brave men of Company K as they navigate the treacherous frontier, facing not only the challenges of war but also the harsh realities of life on the western frontier. From intense battles to encounters with Native American tribes, the soldiers of Company K must rely on their courage, camaraderie, and resourcefulness to survive and fulfill their duty. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, War, Western

Themes: Bravery, Survival, Camaraderie, Frontier Life
