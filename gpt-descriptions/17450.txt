Synopsis: 

In "The Part Borne by the Dutch in the Discovery of Australia 1606-1765," Jan Ernst Heeres takes readers on a captivating journey through the lesser-known history of Australia's discovery. Unveiling the remarkable exploits of Dutch explorers, this book sheds light on the crucial role they played in uncovering the mysterious land down under. From the daring voyages of Willem Janszoon to the epic adventures of Abel Tasman, the narrative is filled with tales of courage, exploration, and the pursuit of new horizons. As the Dutch navigate treacherous waters and encounter indigenous peoples, readers will be enthralled by the untold stories that shaped Australia's early history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Exploration, Maritime history

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Cultural encounters, Colonialism
