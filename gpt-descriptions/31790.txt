Synopsis: In 'Family Limitation' by Margaret Sanger, a young woman finds herself trapped in a society that denies her control over her own body and reproductive choices. Faced with limited options and societal pressures, she embarks on a journey of self-discovery and empowerment, challenging the norms of her time. As she navigates the complexities of love, marriage, and motherhood, she becomes determined to break free from the constraints imposed upon her by society and fight for her right to make decisions about her own life.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Feminist Literature

Themes: Reproductive rights, Gender inequality, Female empowerment, Societal expectations
