Synopsis: In 'Psychological Aspects of the Problem of Atmospheric Smoke Pollution', J.E. Wallace delves into the hidden effects of smoke pollution on our minds, exploring the intricate relationship between our environment and mental well-being. Through a captivating exploration of case studies and scientific research, Wallace uncovers the surprising ways in which atmospheric smoke pollution can impact our psychological state. This thought-provoking work sheds light on a lesser-known aspect of pollution, inviting readers to reconsider the profound influence our surroundings have on our mental health.

Genres: Psychology, Environmental Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Environmental impact, Mental health, Human behavior, Pollution effects
