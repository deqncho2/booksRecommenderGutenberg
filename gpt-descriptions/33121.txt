Synopsis: In "The Battle of Gettysburg," Franklin Aretas Haskell takes us on a gripping journey through one of the most pivotal moments in American history. Through vivid and detailed accounts, Haskell brings to life the harrowing three-day battle that unfolded in the small town of Gettysburg during the American Civil War. With a keen eye for both the strategic maneuvers and the personal stories of soldiers on both sides, Haskell paints a vivid picture of the chaos, bravery, and sacrifice that defined this historic clash. As the battle rages on, readers will find themselves immersed in the intensity and uncertainty of war, eagerly turning the pages to discover the outcome of this monumental conflict.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: War and its consequences, Leadership and strategy, Human resilience and sacrifice
