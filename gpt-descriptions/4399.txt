Synopsis: 
In 'A Few Figs from Thistles', Edna St. Vincent Millay takes readers on a captivating journey through a collection of thought-provoking and daring poems. With her sharp wit and unapologetic exploration of love, sexuality, and societal expectations, Millay challenges conventional norms and invites readers to question the status quo. Through her lyrical and evocative verses, she delves into the complexities of relationships, the pursuit of personal freedom, and the power of self-expression. This collection is a testament to Millay's unique voice and her ability to capture the essence of human emotions and desires.

Genres: Poetry, Feminist Literature, Modernist Literature

Themes: Love and Relationships, Sexuality, Individuality, Feminism
