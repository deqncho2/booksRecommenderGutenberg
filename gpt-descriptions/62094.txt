Synopsis: In "The Middle Five: Indian Boys at School," Francis La Flesche takes us on a captivating journey into the lives of five Native American boys attending a government boarding school in the late 19th century. Through their experiences, we witness the clash of cultures, the challenges of assimilation, and the resilience of these young individuals as they navigate a world that seeks to erase their heritage. As friendships are formed and tested, secrets are unveiled, and dreams are pursued, the boys' stories intertwine to create a poignant and thought-provoking narrative that sheds light on the complexities of identity and the power of education.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Cultural Studies

Themes: Identity, Assimilation, Friendship, Education, Cultural Heritage
