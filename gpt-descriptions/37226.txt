Synopsis: 

In 'English Lands, Letters and Kings, vol. 3: Queen Anne and the Georges', Donald Grant Mitchell takes readers on a captivating journey through the reigns of Queen Anne and the Georges, exploring the political, cultural, and literary landscapes of England during this transformative period. From the rise of the Hanoverian dynasty to the emergence of the Enlightenment, Mitchell delves into the lives of monarchs, writers, and thinkers who shaped the nation's destiny. With vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the tumultuous world of 18th-century England.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Literary criticism

Themes: Monarchy, Enlightenment, Cultural transformation
