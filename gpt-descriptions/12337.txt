Synopsis: In "Dickens in Camp" by Bret Harte, the narrator recounts a poignant encounter with a group of rough and hardened gold miners who find solace and inspiration in the works of Charles Dickens. As they gather around a campfire, the miners share their personal stories and reveal the unexpected impact that Dickens' literature has had on their lives. This heartfelt tale explores the power of literature to transcend boundaries and bring hope to even the most unlikely of places.

Genres: Short story, Historical fiction

Themes: Literature's transformative power, Finding solace in art, Unexpected connections
