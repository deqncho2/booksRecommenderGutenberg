Synopsis: In "What the Schools Teach and Might Teach," John Franklin Bobbitt delves into the world of education, exploring the current state of schools and proposing innovative ideas for their improvement. Through thought-provoking analysis and compelling anecdotes, Bobbitt challenges traditional teaching methods and offers a fresh perspective on how education can better prepare students for the complexities of the modern world. This book is a captivating exploration of the educational system and a call to action for educators, parents, and policymakers alike.

Genres: Education, Non-fiction

Themes: Education reform, Teaching methods, Curriculum development
