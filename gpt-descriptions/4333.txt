Synopsis: 
In 'Studies from Court and Cloister: being essays, historical and literary dealing mainly with subjects relating to the XVIth and XVIIth centuries', J. M. Stone takes readers on a captivating journey through the intriguing world of the 16th and 17th centuries. From the opulent courts of monarchs to the secluded confines of cloisters, this collection of essays delves into the lives of historical figures and explores the literary works that shaped this transformative era. Stone's meticulous research and engaging storytelling bring to life the vibrant tapestry of this fascinating period, offering readers a glimpse into the political intrigues, religious upheavals, and artistic achievements that defined the Renaissance and Baroque periods.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Literary criticism, Biography

Themes: Renaissance, Baroque, History, Literature, Religion, Politics
