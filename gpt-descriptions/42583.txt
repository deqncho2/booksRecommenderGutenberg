Synopsis: 

In "A History of Banks for Savings in Great Britain and Ireland," author William Lewins takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of financial institutions. From their humble beginnings to their significant impact on society, this book delves into the intricate history of banks for savings. Lewins uncovers the stories of visionary individuals who pioneered these institutions, the challenges they faced, and the profound changes they brought about. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, the author sheds light on the evolution of banking practices, the struggles of the working class, and the transformative power of financial inclusion. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Finance

Themes: Financial institutions, Social impact, Economic history
