Synopsis: 
"The History of Mary I, Queen of England: as found in the public records, despatches of ambassadors, in original private letters, and other contemporary documents" delves into the captivating life and reign of Mary I, the first queen regnant of England. Through an extensive collection of public records, ambassador reports, private letters, and other contemporary documents, J.M. Stone paints a vivid and detailed portrait of this enigmatic historical figure. From her tumultuous childhood to her ascension to the throne and the challenges she faced during her reign, this book offers a fascinating exploration of Mary I's life, reign, and the impact she had on England and its people.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Power and politics, Religion and religious conflict, Gender and monarchy
