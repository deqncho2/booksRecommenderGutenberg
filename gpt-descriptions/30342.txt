Synopsis: In "Whitman: A Study" by John Burroughs, embark on a captivating exploration of the life and work of one of America's most influential poets, Walt Whitman. Through insightful analysis and personal anecdotes, Burroughs delves into the essence of Whitman's poetry, uncovering the profound impact it had on the literary world and society at large. As he delves into Whitman's unique style and revolutionary ideas, Burroughs invites readers to discover the man behind the words, painting a vivid portrait of a poet who dared to challenge conventions and redefine the boundaries of American literature.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Non-fiction

Themes: Poetry, Individualism, Literary Revolution
