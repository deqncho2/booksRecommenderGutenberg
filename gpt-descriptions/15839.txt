Synopsis: In "The Rebel of the School" by L. T. Meade, a spirited and independent young girl named Muriel is determined to challenge the strict rules and expectations of her all-girls boarding school. With her quick wit and daring nature, she becomes a source of both admiration and frustration for her classmates and teachers. As Muriel navigates the complexities of friendship, love, and societal norms, she discovers the power of rebellion and the importance of staying true to oneself.

Genres: Young Adult, Coming-of-Age, School Drama

Themes: Individuality, Rebellion, Friendship, Gender Roles
