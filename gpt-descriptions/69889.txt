Synopsis: 
'Anecdotes and memoirs of William Boen, a coloured man, who lived and died near Mount Holly, New Jersey' is a captivating collection of stories that offers a unique perspective on the life of William Boen, an African American man living in the 19th century. Through a series of anecdotes and personal memoirs, the book delves into the challenges, triumphs, and experiences of William Boen, shedding light on the complexities of race, identity, and the pursuit of freedom during a tumultuous time in American history.

Genres: 
Biography, Memoir, Historical

Themes: 
Race, Identity, Freedom, Resilience
