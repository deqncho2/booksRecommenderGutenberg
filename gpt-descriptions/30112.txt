Synopsis: In this captivating work, Leslie J. Newville delves into the fascinating story of the development of the phonograph at Alexander Graham Bell's Volta Laboratory. Through meticulous research and the contributions from the Museum of History and Technology, Newville uncovers the untold history behind this groundbreaking invention. From the challenges faced by Bell and his team to the innovative solutions they devised, this narrative sheds light on the remarkable journey that led to the creation of the phonograph. With intriguing anecdotes and historical insights, Newville paints a vivid picture of the trials and triumphs that shaped this iconic device.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Science history

Themes: Innovation, Invention, Technological advancements
