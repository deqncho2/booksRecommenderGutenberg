Synopsis: In "Elizabeth Hooton: First Quaker Woman Preacher (1600-1672)" by Emily Manners, embark on a captivating journey through the life of Elizabeth Hooton, a remarkable woman who defied societal norms and became the first female preacher in the Quaker movement. From her humble beginnings to her influential role in spreading the Quaker message, this biography unveils the challenges and triumphs of a trailblazing woman who fearlessly pursued her calling. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Manners brings to life the untold story of Elizabeth Hooton, shedding light on her impact and legacy that continues to inspire generations.

Genres: Biography, History, Religious Studies

Themes: Gender Equality, Religious Freedom, Social Activism
