Synopsis: 
In 'Heliodora, and Other Poems', H. D. (Hilda Doolittle) takes readers on a captivating journey through a collection of poems that explore the depths of love, loss, and the complexities of the human experience. From the enchanting tale of Heliodora, a mythical figure who embodies both beauty and tragedy, to the introspective musings on nature and the self, each poem in this collection is a lyrical masterpiece that will leave readers spellbound. With vivid imagery and evocative language, H. D. invites readers to delve into the realms of emotion and imagination, where the boundaries between reality and myth blur.

Genres: Poetry, Mythology, Symbolism

Themes: Love, Loss, Nature, Identity, Mythology
