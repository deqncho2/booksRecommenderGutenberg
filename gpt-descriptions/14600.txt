Synopsis: 
In 'Theory of Silk Weaving: A Treatise on the Construction and Application of Weaves, and the Decomposition and Calculation of Broad and Narrow, Plain, Novelty and Jacquard Silk Fabrics', Wolfensberger takes readers on a captivating journey into the intricate world of silk weaving. Through detailed explanations and practical examples, he unveils the secrets behind the construction and application of various weaves, unraveling the complexities of creating both broad and narrow, plain, novelty, and Jacquard silk fabrics. This comprehensive treatise offers a wealth of knowledge for both aspiring and experienced weavers, shedding light on the artistry and technicality that lies behind the creation of these exquisite textiles.

Genres: Non-fiction, Craft, Textile Arts

Themes: Silk weaving, Textile construction, Artistry, Technicality
