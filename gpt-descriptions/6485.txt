Synopsis:
In "Hugh Wynne, Free Quaker," we follow the captivating journey of Hugh Wynne, a young Quaker who finds himself torn between his pacifist beliefs and the call to fight for American independence during the Revolutionary War. As he navigates the complexities of war, love, and loyalty, Hugh's story takes us from the battlefields of Valley Forge to the elegant parlors of Philadelphia, where he encounters historical figures such as George Washington and Benjamin Franklin. With its rich historical backdrop and compelling characters, this novel immerses readers in a tale of personal growth, sacrifice, and the enduring power of friendship.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Patriotism, Identity, Morality, Friendship
