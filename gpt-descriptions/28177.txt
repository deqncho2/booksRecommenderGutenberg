Synopsis: 

In "Insects and Diseases: A Popular Account of the Way in Which Insects may Spread or Cause some of our Common Diseases," author Doane, Rennie Wilbur takes readers on a captivating journey into the hidden world of insects and their role in spreading diseases. From the buzzing mosquitoes to the tiny ticks, this book unveils the fascinating ways in which these creatures can impact our health. Through vivid descriptions and intriguing anecdotes, the author explores the intricate relationship between insects and diseases, shedding light on the potential dangers they pose to human well-being. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Medical

Themes: Disease transmission, Insect biology, Public health
