Synopsis: 

In 'Extinct Birds: An attempt to unite in one volume a short account of those birds which have become extinct in historical times', Lionel Walter Rothschild, Baron Rothschild takes readers on a captivating journey through the annals of history to explore the fascinating world of extinct birds. This comprehensive volume provides a concise account of various bird species that have tragically vanished from our planet, offering insights into their unique characteristics, habitats, and the circumstances that led to their extinction. From the majestic Dodo to the enigmatic Great Auk, Rothschild's meticulous research and vivid descriptions bring these extinct birds back to life, allowing readers to marvel at their beauty and ponder the consequences of human actions on the natural world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Ornithology

Themes: Extinction, Biodiversity, Human impact on the environment
