Synopsis: In "The Transformation of Early Christianity from an Eschatological to a Socialized Movement," Edwards delves into the fascinating evolution of Christianity during its early years. Through meticulous research and analysis, he uncovers the pivotal shift that occurred within the movement, from a focus on the imminent end of the world to a more socially oriented approach. This dissertation offers a captivating exploration of the factors that influenced this transformation, shedding light on the complex dynamics that shaped the early Christian community.

Genres: Historical research, Religious studies, Academic writing

Themes: Early Christianity, Eschatology, Social transformation, Community dynamics
