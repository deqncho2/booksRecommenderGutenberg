Synopsis: In 'Goldsmith's Friend Abroad Again' by Mark Twain, we follow the hilarious misadventures of a man named Goldsmith as he embarks on a journey abroad. From mistaken identities to cultural misunderstandings, Goldsmith finds himself in one comical situation after another, all while trying to navigate the unfamiliar territory of foreign lands. As he encounters eccentric characters and gets entangled in absurd scenarios, readers will be captivated by the uproarious escapades that unfold in this delightful tale.

Genres: Humor, Travelogue, Satire

Themes: Cultural differences, Identity, Miscommunication
