Synopsis: 

'Aviation in Canada, 1917-1918' takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of aviation during a pivotal period in Canadian history. This book provides a brief yet enthralling account of the remarkable work carried out by the Royal Air Force Canada, the Aviation Department of the Imperial Munitions Board, and the Canadian Aeroplanes Limited. From daring aerial missions to technological advancements, this narrative sheds light on the incredible contributions made by these organizations to the development of aviation in Canada. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Aviation history

Themes: Innovation and technological advancements, Contributions to war efforts, Impact of aviation on Canadian history
