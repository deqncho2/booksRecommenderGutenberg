Synopsis: 
'The Doings of the Fifteenth Infantry Brigade: August 1914 to March 1915' by Edward, Lord Gleichen is a gripping and detailed account of the experiences of the Fifteenth Infantry Brigade during the early months of World War I. From the chaos and uncertainty of the outbreak of war to the harsh realities of trench warfare, Lord Gleichen provides a firsthand narrative that immerses the reader in the intense and often harrowing events of this pivotal period. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, the author brings to life the bravery, camaraderie, and sacrifices made by the soldiers of the Fifteenth Infantry Brigade, offering a unique perspective on the Great War.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: War, Courage, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Human resilience
