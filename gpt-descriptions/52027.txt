Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The Witchcraft Delusion in New England', Cotton Mather and Robert Calef delve deeper into the chilling accounts of witchcraft trials that plagued the early American colonies. As the hysteria reaches its peak, the authors uncover shocking revelations and explore the complex web of accusations, confessions, and executions that gripped the region. With meticulous research and compelling narratives, this volume offers a captivating exploration of the dark forces that consumed New England during this tumultuous period.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Occult

Themes: Witchcraft, Hysteria, Justice, Religion, Colonial America
