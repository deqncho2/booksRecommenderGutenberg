Synopsis: 
The Anzac Book is a remarkable collection of writings and illustrations created by soldiers during World War I. This captivating anthology offers a unique glimpse into the lives and experiences of the Anzacs, the Australian and New Zealand Army Corps. Through poetry, stories, sketches, and photographs, the book provides a poignant and honest portrayal of the soldiers' bravery, camaraderie, and resilience amidst the horrors of war. From the trenches to the hospitals, from the battlefields to the home front, The Anzac Book is a testament to the indomitable spirit of those who served.

Genres: 
War literature, Anthology, Historical non-fiction

Themes: 
War experiences, Camaraderie, Resilience, Patriotism
