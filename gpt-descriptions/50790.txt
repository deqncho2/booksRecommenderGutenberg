Synopsis: 
'The Growth of Parliament and the War with Scotland (1216-1307)' takes readers on a captivating journey through a tumultuous period in English history. From the reign of King John to the end of the Scottish Wars of Independence, this book delves into the intricate web of political intrigue, power struggles, and military conflicts that shaped the growth of Parliament and the destiny of two nations. As the English monarchy faces internal challenges and external threats, the reader is immersed in a world of shifting alliances, strategic maneuvering, and the rise of parliamentary authority. With vivid storytelling and meticulous historical research, this book offers a gripping account of a pivotal era that laid the foundation for the modern British political system.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Military history

Themes: Power struggles, Political evolution, National identity, War and diplomacy
