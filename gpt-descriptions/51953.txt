Synopsis: In 'The Manager of the B. & A.: A Novel' by Kester, Vaughan, a young and ambitious woman named Emily finds herself thrust into the male-dominated world of business in the late 19th century. Determined to prove herself, she takes on the role of manager at the prestigious B. & A. company. As she navigates the challenges and obstacles that come her way, Emily must confront societal expectations, gender discrimination, and her own personal desires. With a cast of intriguing characters and a captivating plot, this novel explores the complexities of ambition, love, and the pursuit of success in a time when women's roles were limited.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Women's Fiction, Business Fiction

Themes: Gender discrimination, Ambition, Love, Societal expectations
