Synopsis: In "The Spaniards in Florida," George R. Fairbanks takes readers on a captivating journey through the notable settlement of the Huguenots in 1564 and the intriguing history and antiquities of St. Augustine, founded in 1565. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Fairbanks unveils the struggles, triumphs, and mysteries surrounding these early Spanish expeditions in Florida. From the clash of cultures to the exploration of uncharted territories, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of the Spaniards and the impact they had on the region.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Exploration, Archaeology

Themes: Clash of cultures, Exploration and colonization, Historical preservation
