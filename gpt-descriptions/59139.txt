Synopsis: 
'Irvin Cobb, His Book' is a collection of friendly tributes written in honor of the renowned American author, Irvin Shrewsbury Cobb. These heartfelt and humorous essays were presented at a special dinner held at the prestigious Waldorf-Astoria Hotel in New York on April 25th, 1915. The book offers a delightful glimpse into Cobb's life, personality, and literary achievements, as well as the impact he has had on the world of literature. Through anecdotes, personal stories, and admiration, this collection pays homage to a beloved writer who has captured the hearts of readers worldwide.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Anthology

Themes: Literary influence, Humor, Appreciation of an author's work
