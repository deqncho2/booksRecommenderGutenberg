Synopsis: 

In "The Postal Service of the United States in Connection with the Local History of Buffalo," authors Blossom and Hall take readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of the postal service in Buffalo, New York. From the early days of horse-drawn mail carriages to the modern era of efficient mail delivery, this book unveils the untold stories and remarkable individuals who played a pivotal role in shaping the postal service in Buffalo. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, the authors shed light on the challenges, triumphs, and innovations that transformed the city's mail system into what it is today. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Local history

Themes: Communication and connectivity, Evolution of postal services, Local community development
