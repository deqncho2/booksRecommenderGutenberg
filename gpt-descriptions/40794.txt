Synopsis: In 'Essays in Experimental Logic', John Dewey takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the realm of logic, challenging traditional notions and exploring new avenues of inquiry. Through a series of engaging essays, Dewey delves into the intricacies of experimental logic, offering fresh perspectives on the nature of knowledge, truth, and reasoning. With his characteristic clarity and intellectual rigor, Dewey invites readers to question their assumptions and embrace a more dynamic and pragmatic approach to logic. 

Genres: Philosophy, Logic, Non-fiction

Themes: Pragmatism, Epistemology, Scientific Method
