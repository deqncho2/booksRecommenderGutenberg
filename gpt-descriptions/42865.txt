Synopsis: In 'English Conferences of Ernest Renan: Rome and Christianity. Marcus Aurelius', Ernest Renan takes readers on a captivating journey through the historical and philosophical landscape of ancient Rome. Focusing on the enigmatic figure of Marcus Aurelius, Renan delves into the complex relationship between Rome and Christianity, exploring the clash of ideologies and the profound impact it had on the Roman Empire. With meticulous research and thought-provoking analysis, Renan offers a fresh perspective on this pivotal period in history, shedding light on the intricate interplay between religion, power, and human nature.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Philosophy, Religious studies

Themes: Religion and politics, Clash of ideologies, Power and authority, Human nature
