Synopsis: In "The History of the Standard Oil Company," Ida M. Tarbell delves into the captivating story of one of the most influential and controversial corporations in American history. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Tarbell uncovers the rise of Standard Oil, its ruthless business practices, and the immense power it wielded over the nation's economy. From the humble beginnings of John D. Rockefeller to the company's monopolistic control over the oil industry, Tarbell paints a vivid picture of the triumphs and dark side of this corporate giant. With a keen eye for detail and a commitment to uncovering the truth, Tarbell's work is a gripping exploration of the complex relationship between big business and society.

Genres: Biography, History, Investigative Journalism

Themes: Power and corruption, Monopoly and antitrust, Economic and social impact of corporations
