Synopsis: 

In 'Shoulder-Straps: A Novel of New York and the Army, 1862' by Morford, Henry, readers are transported to the vibrant and tumultuous world of New York City during the Civil War. The story follows the lives of two young men, John and Frank, as they navigate the complexities of love, friendship, and duty in a time of great upheaval. As they join the Union Army and face the harsh realities of war, their paths diverge, leading them on separate journeys of self-discovery and sacrifice. Against the backdrop of a divided nation, the novel explores the profound impact of war on individuals and the enduring power of human connection.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Love and Friendship, Duty and Sacrifice, Identity and Self-Discovery
