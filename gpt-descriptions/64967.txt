Synopsis: 

'Clara Barton: A Centenary Tribute to the World's Greatest Humanitarian' by Charles Sumner Young is a captivating biography that delves into the extraordinary life of Clara Barton, a remarkable woman who left an indelible mark on the world. From her founding of the American Red Cross Society to her pivotal role in amending the International Red Cross Convention of Geneva, Barton's tireless efforts in humanitarian work are brought to life in this inspiring tribute. Young also explores Barton's founding of the National First Aid Association of America, showcasing her commitment to saving lives and promoting safety. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this book paints a vivid portrait of a true trailblazer and her enduring legacy.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Humanitarianism, Leadership, Women's Empowerment
