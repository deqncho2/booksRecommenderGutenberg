Synopsis: In "My Beloved Poilus" by Agnes Warner, the author takes us on a captivating journey through her experiences as a nurse during World War I. Through her vivid and heartfelt storytelling, Warner provides a unique perspective on the war, sharing intimate moments and personal connections with the soldiers she cared for. From the trenches to the hospitals, she paints a poignant picture of the sacrifices made by both the soldiers and the medical staff. As the war unfolds, Warner's narrative explores the resilience, camaraderie, and the indomitable spirit of those who fought and those who cared for them.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: War, Sacrifice, Human Connection, Resilience
