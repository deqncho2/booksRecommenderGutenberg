Synopsis: 
In 'Traditions, Superstitions and Folk-lore: (Chiefly Lancashire and the North of England:) Their Affinity to Others in Widely-Distributed Localities; Their Eastern Origin and Mythical Significance,' Charles Hardwick delves into the rich tapestry of traditions, superstitions, and folk-lore that have shaped the cultural landscape of Lancashire and the North of England. From ancient rituals to curious beliefs, this captivating exploration uncovers the hidden connections between these local customs and their counterparts in far-flung regions. Hardwick also uncovers the Eastern origins and mythical significance behind these age-old practices, shedding light on the mysterious and enchanting world of folklore.

Genres: Folklore, Cultural Studies, History

Themes: Tradition, Superstition, Mythology, Cultural Heritage
