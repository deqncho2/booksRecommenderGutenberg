Synopsis: In 'What is Property? An Inquiry into the Principle of Right and of Government', Pierre-Joseph Proudhon challenges conventional notions of property and explores the fundamental principles of right and government. Through thought-provoking arguments and insightful analysis, Proudhon delves into the nature of property, its origins, and its impact on society. This groundbreaking work challenges readers to question the existing social and economic structures, offering a fresh perspective on the concept of property and its implications for justice and equality.

Genres: Political philosophy, Social criticism

Themes: Property rights, Government, Justice, Equality
