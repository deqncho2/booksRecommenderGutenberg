Synopsis: In "The Influence of Greek Ideas and Usages upon the Christian Church," Edwin Hatch explores the fascinating connection between ancient Greek philosophy and the development of early Christianity. Delving into the historical context, Hatch uncovers the profound impact that Greek ideas, language, and cultural practices had on shaping the beliefs and practices of the early Christian church. From the influence of Greek philosophy on Christian theology to the adoption of Greek language and rhetorical techniques, Hatch presents a thought-provoking analysis of the intricate relationship between these two influential traditions. This enlightening work offers a fresh perspective on the origins of Christianity and the complex interplay between Greek and Christian thought.

Genres: History, Religious Studies, Philosophy

Themes: Cultural Exchange, Religious Syncretism, Intellectual History
