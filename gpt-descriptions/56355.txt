Synopsis: In 'The Bride of Mission San José: A Tale of Early California' by John Augustine Cull, readers are transported to the vibrant and tumultuous world of early California. Set against the backdrop of Mission San José, this captivating tale follows the journey of a young woman named Maria, who finds herself torn between duty and desire. As she navigates the complexities of love, faith, and cultural identity, Maria's choices will have far-reaching consequences that will test her resilience and challenge the very foundations of her world.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Cultural identity, Love and sacrifice, Tradition versus personal freedom
