Synopsis: In "The History of the Negro Church," Carter G. Woodson takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and often overlooked history of African American churches. From their humble beginnings during slavery to their pivotal role in the Civil Rights Movement, Woodson explores the struggles, triumphs, and enduring legacy of these sacred institutions. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, he sheds light on the profound impact of the Negro church on the African American community, both spiritually and socially. This enlightening work unveils a powerful narrative of resilience, faith, and the pursuit of freedom.

Genres: History, African American Studies, Religious Studies

Themes: African American history, Religion, Social justice, Community empowerment
