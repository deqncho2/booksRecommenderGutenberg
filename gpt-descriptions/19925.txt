Synopsis: In 'At the Sign of the Barber's Pole: Studies In Hirsute History' by Andrews, William, embark on a fascinating journey through time as the author delves into the captivating world of hair and its significance throughout history. From ancient civilizations to modern times, this book uncovers the hidden stories behind the styles, trends, and rituals surrounding hair, revealing surprising connections and shedding light on the cultural, social, and even political implications of our follicular choices. Prepare to be enthralled by the untold tales lurking beneath the surface of our hairdos.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Hair as a symbol of identity, Cultural significance of grooming practices, Evolution of beauty standards
