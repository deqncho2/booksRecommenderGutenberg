Synopsis: 
In "The World War: A History of the Nations and Empires Involved and a Study of the Events Culminating in the Great Conflict," Marshall Logan takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex web of nations and empires that led to the outbreak of the First World War. From the political tensions and alliances to the economic rivalries and military strategies, this comprehensive account delves into the intricate factors that shaped the world's most devastating conflict. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Logan brings to life the key events and influential figures that ultimately culminated in the Great War, offering readers a deeper understanding of the global impact and lasting consequences of this monumental historical event.

Genres: History, Military History, Non-fiction

Themes: War and Conflict, Diplomacy and Alliances, Nationalism, Imperialism, Global Consequences
