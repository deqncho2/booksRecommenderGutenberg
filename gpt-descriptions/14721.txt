Synopsis: 
'Speeches & Letters of Abraham Lincoln, 1832-1865' is a captivating collection of the most influential speeches and letters by one of America's greatest leaders, Abraham Lincoln. From his early political career to his presidency during the tumultuous Civil War, this book offers a unique insight into Lincoln's thoughts, beliefs, and struggles. Through his eloquent words, Lincoln addresses issues of slavery, equality, and the preservation of the Union, providing a powerful and thought-provoking perspective on the challenges faced by a nation divided. This collection is a testament to Lincoln's remarkable ability to inspire, unite, and lead a nation through one of its darkest periods.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Leadership, Equality, Slavery, National Unity
