Synopsis: 

In 'London's Underworld' by Thomas Holmes, readers are taken on a gripping journey through the dark and mysterious underbelly of Victorian London. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, Holmes uncovers the hidden world of criminals, prostitutes, and con artists that thrived in the shadows of the bustling city. From the notorious dens of thieves to the secret societies that ruled the streets, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the dangerous and often shocking lives of those who dwelled in London's criminal underworld.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, True Crime

Themes: Crime and punishment, Social inequality, Urban life
