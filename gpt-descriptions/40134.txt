Synopsis: In 'A Moral Alphabet' by Hilaire Belloc, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the alphabet, where each letter represents a different moral lesson. From the amusing antics of mischievous children to the wisdom of age, Belloc's witty and thought-provoking verses explore the complexities of human nature and the importance of moral values. With each letter, readers are introduced to a new character or situation that challenges their understanding of right and wrong, leaving them pondering the intricacies of morality long after the final page.

Genres: Poetry, Children's Literature, Philosophy

Themes: Morality, Ethics, Human Nature
