Synopsis: In 'Types of Weltschmerz in German Poetry', Braun, Wilhelm Alfred takes readers on a captivating journey through the depths of German poetry, exploring the various manifestations of Weltschmerz. From melancholic landscapes to unrequited love, the author delves into the intricate emotions and existential dilemmas that have shaped the German poetic tradition. Through insightful analysis and poignant examples, Braun offers a unique perspective on the different types of Weltschmerz, leaving readers with a profound understanding of the human condition and the power of poetry.

Genres: Literary criticism, Poetry analysis, Cultural studies

Themes: Weltschmerz, German poetry, Melancholy, Existentialism, Love, Human emotions
