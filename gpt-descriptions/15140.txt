Synopsis: 
'Washington's Birthday: Its history, observance, spirit, and significance as related in prose and verse, with a selection from Washington's speeches and writings' is a captivating exploration of the life and legacy of George Washington. This book delves into the rich history and traditions surrounding Washington's birthday, shedding light on its observance, spirit, and profound significance. Through a collection of prose, verse, and excerpts from Washington's own speeches and writings, readers are taken on a journey that unveils the essence of this revered figure and the enduring impact he had on the nation. From his leadership during the Revolutionary War to his role as the first President of the United States, this book offers a compelling glimpse into the remarkable life of George Washington.

Genres: 
Historical, Biography, Anthology

Themes: 
Leadership, Patriotism, Legacy
