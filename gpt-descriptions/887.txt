Synopsis: In 'Intentions' by Oscar Wilde, a collection of essays and dialogues, the reader is taken on a thought-provoking journey through the mind of one of the most brilliant and controversial writers of the Victorian era. With his signature wit and charm, Wilde explores various topics such as art, literature, and society, challenging conventional wisdom and questioning the very nature of truth. Through his eloquent prose and sharp observations, he invites readers to reconsider their own beliefs and perceptions, leaving them both entertained and enlightened.

Genres: Essay, Non-fiction, Philosophy

Themes: Artistic expression, Aestheticism, Social criticism
