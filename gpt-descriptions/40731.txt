Synopsis: 

In 'Tenting on the Plains; or, General Custer in Kansas and Texas', Elizabeth Bacon Custer takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed landscapes of Kansas and Texas during the late 19th century. Through her vivid and personal accounts, she offers a unique perspective on the challenges and triumphs of life on the frontier, as well as the remarkable character of her husband, General George Armstrong Custer. From encounters with Native American tribes to the harsh realities of military life, this memoir paints a captivating picture of a tumultuous era in American history.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Adventure

Themes: Frontier life, Native American relations, Military history, Personal resilience
