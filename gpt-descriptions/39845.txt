Synopsis: 
'Early Illustrated Books: A History of the Decoration and Illustration of Books in the 15th and 16th Centuries' takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of book decoration and illustration during the Renaissance. From the intricate woodcuts of the 15th century to the vibrant and detailed engravings of the 16th century, this book explores the evolution of visual storytelling in printed works. With stunning examples and insightful analysis, Alfred W. Pollard delves into the techniques, styles, and influences that shaped these early illustrated books, shedding light on the artistic and cultural significance of this period.

Genres: Art History, Literary Criticism, Cultural History

Themes: Renaissance art, Book production, Visual storytelling, Cultural exchange
