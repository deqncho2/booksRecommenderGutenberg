Synopsis: In the final volume of his monumental work, Henry Hallam delves into the fascinating constitutional history of England from the reign of Henry VII to George II. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, Hallam uncovers the intricate web of political, legal, and social developments that shaped the nation during this transformative period. From the consolidation of royal power to the rise of parliamentary authority, this volume offers a captivating exploration of the forces that shaped England's constitutional landscape. With vivid storytelling and a wealth of historical detail, Hallam brings to life the key figures and events that defined this era, shedding light on the complex evolution of England's governance.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Constitutional development, Monarchy, Parliament, Political power, Legal system
