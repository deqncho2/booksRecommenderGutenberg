Synopsis: 
'Index Expurgatorius Anglicanus: Or, a descriptive catalogue of the principal books printed or published in England, which have been suppressed, or burnt by the common hangman, or censured, or for which the authors, printers, or publishers have been prosecuted' is a captivating exploration of the suppressed literature in England. Written by W. H. Hart, this descriptive catalogue unveils a hidden world of books that have faced censorship, burning, and prosecution. From controversial ideas to subversive literature, this work delves into the forbidden realm of literature that has been deemed dangerous by the authorities. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Literary Criticism

Themes: Censorship, Freedom of Expression, Suppression of Ideas
