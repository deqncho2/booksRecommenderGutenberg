Synopsis: In 'The Spinster: 1905' by Hichens, Robert, we are introduced to Miss Mary Charrington, a woman in her thirties who has resigned herself to a life of spinsterhood. However, her world is turned upside down when she receives an unexpected marriage proposal from a mysterious suitor. As Mary navigates the complexities of love, societal expectations, and her own desires, she must confront the choices she has made and the possibility of a different future. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century England, this captivating novel explores the power of love and the pursuit of happiness in a time when societal norms dictated a woman's fate.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: Love, Identity, Societal Expectations
