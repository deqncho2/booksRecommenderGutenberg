Synopsis: 

"The Village Labourer, 1760-1832: A Study in the Government of England before the Reform Bill" takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives of rural workers in England during a transformative period in history. Drawing on extensive research and firsthand accounts, Barbara Bradby Hammond and J. L. Hammond shed light on the struggles, aspirations, and resilience of the village labourers, providing a fascinating glimpse into the social and political landscape of the time. From the harsh realities of poverty and exploitation to the emergence of collective action and the fight for reform, this book offers a compelling narrative that will leave readers with a deeper understanding of the challenges faced by the working class in pre-Reform Bill England.

Genres: History, Social Sciences, Non-fiction

Themes: Social inequality, Rural life, Political reform, Working-class struggles, Government and governance
