Synopsis: In "Caroline the Illustrious, vol. 1," author W. H. Wilkins delves into the captivating life of Caroline, the Queen-Consort of George II and occasional Queen-Regent. This enthralling study takes readers on a journey through Caroline's remarkable experiences and the tumultuous times in which she lived. From her influential role in politics to her complex relationships and her unwavering determination, Caroline's story is one of power, intrigue, and resilience. As the first volume of a two-part series, this book sets the stage for an exploration of a remarkable woman who left an indelible mark on history.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Power and politics, Gender roles and expectations, Resilience and determination
