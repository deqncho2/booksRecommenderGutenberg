Synopsis: In 'Shakespeare's Treatment of Love & Marriage, and Other Essays', C. H. Herford delves into the captivating world of Shakespeare's plays, exploring the intricate dynamics of love and marriage. With insightful analysis and thought-provoking interpretations, Herford uncovers the complexities of Shakespeare's characters and their relationships, shedding light on the timeless themes of passion, desire, and the societal constraints that shape love. From the star-crossed lovers of Romeo and Juliet to the comedic entanglements of A Midsummer Night's Dream, this collection of essays offers a fresh perspective on Shakespeare's exploration of the human heart.

Genres: Literary criticism, Shakespearean studies, Essay collection

Themes: Love, Marriage, Passion, Desire, Societal constraints
