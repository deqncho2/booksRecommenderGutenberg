Synopsis:
In 'Japanese Girls and Women: Revised and Enlarged Edition', Alice Mabel Bacon takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives of Japanese girls and women, offering a unique and intimate glimpse into their culture, traditions, and daily experiences. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Bacon explores various aspects of Japanese society, including education, marriage, motherhood, and the evolving role of women in a rapidly changing world. From the bustling streets of Tokyo to the serene countryside, this book paints a rich and nuanced portrait of Japanese femininity, highlighting the challenges, triumphs, and resilience of these remarkable individuals.

Genres: Non-fiction, Cultural Studies, Travelogue

Themes: Gender roles, Cultural identity, Social change, Tradition and modernity
