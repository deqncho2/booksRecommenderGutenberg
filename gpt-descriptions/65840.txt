Synopsis: 

In 'Report on the Migration of Birds in the Spring and Autumn of 1885. Seventh Report: (Vol. II No. 2)', a team of dedicated naturalists embark on a fascinating journey to document the awe-inspiring phenomenon of bird migration. Led by renowned ornithologists Cordeaux, Harvie-Brown, Barrington, More, and Clarke, this report takes readers on a captivating exploration of the intricate patterns and behaviors of birds during their seasonal journeys. From the breathtaking sight of thousands of birds soaring through the sky to the meticulous observations of their habitats and feeding habits, this report offers a unique glimpse into the mysterious world of avian migration. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural history, Ornithology

Themes: Migration, Nature, Scientific observation
