Synopsis: In "The Outcasts, and Other Stories" by Maksim Gorky, readers are transported to the gritty streets of late 19th-century Russia, where they encounter a diverse cast of characters struggling against the harsh realities of poverty, oppression, and societal norms. From a young boy's journey through the slums to a woman's fight for independence, these interconnected stories delve into the depths of human resilience, hope, and the pursuit of a better life. With vivid descriptions and poignant narratives, Gorky paints a vivid picture of a society on the brink of change, where the outcasts of society find solace, strength, and unexpected connections amidst their struggles.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories, Realism

Themes: Poverty, Social Injustice, Resilience, Freedom, Human Connection
