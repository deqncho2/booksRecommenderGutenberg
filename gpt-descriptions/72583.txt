Synopsis: 
In 'The genuine works of Hippocrates, Vol. 1 (of 2)', readers are transported back in time to ancient Greece, where they will delve into the remarkable medical knowledge of the renowned physician, Hippocrates. This volume presents a collection of his authentic works, translated from the original Greek, accompanied by a preliminary discourse and insightful annotations. From exploring the intricacies of human anatomy to discussing the principles of medical ethics, Hippocrates takes readers on a captivating journey through the foundations of medicine, offering invaluable insights into the art of healing.

Genres: 
Medical literature, Historical non-fiction, Ancient Greek texts

Themes: 
Medicine and healing, Human anatomy, Medical ethics
