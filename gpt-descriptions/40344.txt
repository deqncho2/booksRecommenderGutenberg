Synopsis: 
'Poems, 1916-1918' by Francis Brett Young is a captivating collection of poetry that takes readers on a profound journey through the tumultuous years of World War I. Through evocative verses, Young explores the raw emotions, the horrors of war, and the resilience of the human spirit. From the trenches to the home front, these poems offer a poignant reflection on the impact of war on both individuals and society as a whole. With vivid imagery and lyrical language, 'Poems, 1916-1918' invites readers to delve into the depths of human experience during a time of great upheaval.

Genres: Poetry, War Literature

Themes: War, Human Emotions, Resilience
