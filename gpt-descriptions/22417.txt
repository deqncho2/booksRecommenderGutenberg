Synopsis: In 'The Etiquette of To-day', Edith B. Ordway takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricacies of modern social etiquette. From formal dinner parties to casual gatherings, she explores the evolving rules and expectations of polite society, shedding light on the delicate balance between tradition and contemporary norms. Through a series of engaging anecdotes and practical advice, Ordway offers a fresh perspective on the art of social interaction, revealing the hidden nuances that can make or break one's reputation in the eyes of others. As readers delve into this insightful exploration of modern manners, they will discover the power of etiquette in shaping personal relationships, navigating social hierarchies, and ultimately, finding their place in a rapidly changing world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Self-help, Etiquette

Themes: Social norms, Personal relationships, Adaptation to change
