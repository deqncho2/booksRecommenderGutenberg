Synopsis: In "The Friar's Daughter: A Story of the American Occupation of the Philippines," Charles Lincoln Phifer takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of the American occupation of the Philippines. Set against the backdrop of a nation grappling with its newfound independence, the story follows the life of a young woman named Maria, the daughter of a Spanish friar. As Maria navigates the complexities of her identity and the changing world around her, she becomes entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and political intrigue. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, Phifer paints a vivid picture of a nation in transition, where personal desires clash with the larger forces shaping history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Political Drama

Themes: Identity, Independence, Love and Betrayal, Political Intrigue
