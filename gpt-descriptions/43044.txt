Synopsis: 

In the second volume of 'George Eliot's Life, as Related in Her Letters and Journals,' we delve deeper into the fascinating world of one of the most celebrated Victorian novelists. Through her personal letters and journals, we witness George Eliot's journey as she navigates the complexities of love, friendship, and the pursuit of her literary ambitions. From her unconventional relationships to her struggles with self-doubt and societal expectations, this volume offers a captivating glimpse into the inner life of a remarkable woman whose words continue to resonate with readers today.

Genres: Biography, Autobiography, Letters, Journals

Themes: Love and Relationships, Ambition and Creativity, Gender and Society
