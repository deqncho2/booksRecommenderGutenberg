Synopsis: 
'Princes and Poisoners: Studies of the Court of Louis XIV' by Funck-Brentano, Frantz takes readers on a captivating journey into the intriguing world of the court of Louis XIV, the Sun King. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the author delves into the lives of the princes and poisoners who shaped this opulent and treacherous era. From scandalous affairs to political machinations, from secret alliances to deadly plots, this book uncovers the hidden truths and dark secrets that lurked behind the grandeur of Versailles. As the courtiers navigate a web of power, ambition, and betrayal, readers are transported to a time of lavish excess and dangerous intrigue.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Court intrigue

Themes: Power and politics, Betrayal and loyalty, Courtly life and etiquette
