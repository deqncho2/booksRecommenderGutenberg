Synopsis: In 'The Enemies of Books', William Blades takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of book destruction and preservation. From fire and water to insects and careless readers, Blades explores the various threats that have endangered books throughout the ages. With fascinating anecdotes and historical examples, this book sheds light on the often overlooked battle to protect and preserve the written word.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Bibliography

Themes: Book preservation, Historical preservation, Cultural heritage
