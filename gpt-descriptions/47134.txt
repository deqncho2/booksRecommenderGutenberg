Synopsis: 
Step back in time to the year 1913 and join the American Library Association as they gather for their thirty-fifth general meeting in the picturesque town of Kaaterskill, New York. In this collection of papers and proceedings, you will delve into the fascinating world of libraries and witness the discussions, debates, and groundbreaking ideas that shaped the future of the American library system. From the challenges of cataloging and classification to the importance of library education and the role of libraries in society, this captivating work offers a unique glimpse into the early days of the library profession and the passionate individuals who dedicated their lives to the pursuit of knowledge.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Library Science

Themes: Library profession, Cataloging and classification, Library education, Role of libraries in society
