Synopsis: In "The French Revolution 1789-1795" by Bertha Meriton Gardiner, embark on a captivating journey through one of the most tumultuous periods in history. This gripping account delves into the heart of the French Revolution, exploring the political upheaval, social unrest, and the rise and fall of key figures that shaped this pivotal era. From the storming of the Bastille to the Reign of Terror, the author paints a vivid picture of the revolution's impact on the lives of ordinary citizens and the profound changes it brought to the nation. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Gardiner brings to life the passion, idealism, and chaos that defined this transformative period in French history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Revolution, Power and Politics, Social Change
