Synopsis: 

In 'Salem Witchcraft and Cotton Mather: A Reply', Charles Wentworth Upham delves into the infamous Salem witch trials, offering a thought-provoking analysis and a compelling response to the influential Puritan minister, Cotton Mather. Upham explores the events that unfolded during the witch trials, examining the role of Mather and his controversial writings. With meticulous research and insightful commentary, Upham challenges Mather's perspective and sheds new light on the complex web of accusations, hysteria, and religious fervor that gripped the town of Salem. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: Witchcraft, Puritanism, Religious fanaticism, Historical interpretation
