Synopsis: 
In "Observations on the slave trade and a description of some part of the coast of Guinea, during a voyage, made in 1787, and 1788, in company with Doctor A. Sparrman and Captain Arrehenius," Carl Bernhard Wadström takes readers on a captivating journey along the coast of Guinea. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, Wadström sheds light on the brutal realities of the slave trade and provides a detailed exploration of the region's geography, culture, and people. As he navigates the treacherous waters of the Atlantic, Wadström's observations offer a unique perspective on the horrors of slavery and the resilience of those affected by it.

Genres: 
Historical non-fiction, travelogue, social commentary

Themes: 
Slavery, human rights, cultural exploration
