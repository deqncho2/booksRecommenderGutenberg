Synopsis: 
'Apollo and Marsyas, and Other Poems' by Lee-Hamilton, Eugene is a collection of captivating and thought-provoking poems that explore various aspects of human existence. From the mythical tale of Apollo and Marsyas to introspective musings on love, loss, and the human condition, this collection takes readers on a journey through the depths of emotion and the complexities of life. With vivid imagery and lyrical language, each poem offers a unique perspective and invites readers to reflect on their own experiences and perceptions of the world.

Genres: Poetry, Mythology, Philosophy

Themes: Love, Loss, Human Nature, Identity, Artistic Expression
