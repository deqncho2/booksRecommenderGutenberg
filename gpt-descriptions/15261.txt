Synopsis: In 'A Portraiture of Quakerism, Volume 2', Thomas Clarkson provides a fascinating exploration of the education, discipline, social manners, civil and political economy, religious principles, and character of the Society of Friends, commonly known as Quakers. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Clarkson delves into the unique practices and beliefs of this religious group, shedding light on their distinctive way of life and the impact it has on their interactions with society. This volume offers a captivating glimpse into the inner workings of Quakerism, revealing the profound influence it has on its adherents and the wider world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, History

Themes: Religious practices and beliefs, Social and cultural impact, Education and discipline, Civil and political economy
