Synopsis: 
'A Son of the Middle Border' by Hamlin Garland is a captivating memoir that takes readers on a journey through the author's formative years in the American Midwest. From his childhood on a farm to his experiences as a young man seeking adventure, Garland's vivid storytelling paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs of life on the frontier. As he navigates the complexities of family, love, and personal growth, readers will be drawn into the rich tapestry of the Middle Border and the indomitable spirit of its people.

Genres: Memoir, Autobiography, Historical Fiction

Themes: Coming of Age, Family, Frontier Life, Identity, Pursuit of Dreams
