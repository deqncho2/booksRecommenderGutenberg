Synopsis: In "The County: The 'Dark Continent' of American Politics," author Henry S. Gilbertson takes readers on a captivating journey into the heart of American politics, uncovering the hidden power dynamics and intricate web of corruption that exist within the county level of government. Through a series of gripping anecdotes and in-depth research, Gilbertson sheds light on the often overlooked yet influential role that county politics play in shaping the nation's political landscape. From backroom deals to power struggles, this eye-opening exploration reveals the dark underbelly of American politics that lies within the county system.

Genres: Political non-fiction, investigative journalism, social commentary

Themes: Corruption, power dynamics, local politics, influence, democracy
