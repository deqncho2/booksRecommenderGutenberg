Synopsis: In 'Mobilizing Woman-Power', Harriot Stanton Blatch takes us on a captivating journey through the early days of the women's suffrage movement. Through her vivid storytelling, she introduces us to a diverse group of courageous women who fought tirelessly for their right to vote. From the exhilarating protests to the heart-wrenching setbacks, this book offers a compelling account of the challenges and triumphs that shaped the course of history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Women's Studies

Themes: Women's rights, Activism, Social change
