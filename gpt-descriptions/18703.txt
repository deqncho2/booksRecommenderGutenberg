Synopsis: 
In 'Illustrated Catalogue of the Collections Obtained from the Indians of New Mexico in 1880', James Stevenson takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich cultural heritage of the Native American tribes of New Mexico. Through detailed descriptions and stunning illustrations, Stevenson showcases the diverse artifacts and objects collected during his expedition, shedding light on the customs, traditions, and artistic expressions of these indigenous communities. From intricately woven textiles to intricately carved pottery, this catalogue offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of the Native Americans of New Mexico in the late 19th century.

Genres: Ethnography, Anthropology, Art History

Themes: Native American culture, Material culture, Cultural preservation
