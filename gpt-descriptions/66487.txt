Synopsis: In "The year of jubilee; but not to Africans," Nathaniel S. Prime delivers a powerful discourse on the 49th anniversary of American independence. With a thought-provoking perspective, Prime explores a topic that challenges conventional beliefs and sheds light on a hidden truth. This discourse captivates readers by delving into a controversial aspect of American history, leaving them eager to uncover the revelations within.

Genres: Historical, Political, Speech

Themes: American independence, Slavery, Equality, Social justice
