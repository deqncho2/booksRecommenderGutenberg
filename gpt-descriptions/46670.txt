Synopsis: In the second book of the Chronicles of England, Scotland and Ireland, we delve into the reign of Edward the Third, who ascended to the throne after his father's resignation. This captivating historical account takes us through the tumultuous events and political intrigues that shaped Edward's rule, as he navigates the challenges of war, alliances, and the pursuit of power. From battles on foreign soil to the complexities of courtly life, this book offers a vivid portrayal of a pivotal period in English history.

Genres: Historical fiction, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and politics, War and conquest, Royal lineage and succession
