Synopsis: In 'Autobiography' by John Stuart Mill, the reader is taken on a captivating journey through the life and mind of one of the most influential thinkers of the 19th century. From his unconventional upbringing to his intellectual pursuits and political activism, Mill's autobiography offers a unique insight into the personal and philosophical development of a brilliant mind. With vivid anecdotes and introspective reflections, this work delves into the struggles, triumphs, and profound ideas that shaped Mill's life and legacy.

Genres: Autobiography, Philosophy, Political Science

Themes: Individualism, Liberty, Utilitarianism, Intellectual Growth
