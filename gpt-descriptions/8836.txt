Synopsis: In 'Weymouth New Testament in Modern Speech, Galatians', Richard Francis Weymouth presents a fresh and contemporary translation of the biblical book of Galatians. This powerful epistle, written by the apostle Paul, delves into the struggles faced by early Christians and explores the themes of faith, freedom, and the true nature of salvation. Weymouth's modern language and engaging style bring this ancient text to life, making it accessible and relatable to readers of all backgrounds.

Genres: Religious literature, Biblical translation, Christian non-fiction

Themes: Faith, Freedom, Salvation, Early Christianity
