Synopsis: 

'The Heroic Record of the British Navy: A Short History of the Naval War, 1914-1918' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous naval war that unfolded during World War I. Written by Sir Henry Howarth Bashford and Archibald Hurd, this book provides a concise yet gripping account of the British Navy's heroic efforts and pivotal role in the conflict. From the daring exploits of naval officers to the strategic battles fought on the high seas, the authors paint a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by the British Navy during this critical period in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War memoir

Themes: Heroism, Naval warfare, World War I, British history
