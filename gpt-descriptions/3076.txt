Synopsis: 
In "Ten Days That Shook the World," John Reed takes us on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the Russian Revolution. Through his firsthand account, Reed immerses readers in the heart of the revolution, capturing the intensity, chaos, and passion that defined those ten pivotal days. From the electrifying speeches of Lenin to the fierce battles on the streets of Petrograd, Reed's narrative paints a vivid picture of a nation on the brink of transformation. As he navigates the complex web of political intrigue and ideological clashes, Reed's personal experiences and encounters with key figures provide a unique perspective on this historic moment. With its raw and unfiltered portrayal of the revolution's impact on the lives of ordinary people, "Ten Days That Shook the World" is a captivating and eye-opening exploration of one of the most significant events in modern history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political journalism

Themes: Revolution, Social change, Power dynamics, Ideological conflict
