Synopsis: 
'William Wycherley [Four Plays]' is a collection of four witty and scandalous plays by the renowned English playwright, William Wycherley. In these plays, Wycherley masterfully explores the complexities of love, marriage, and social conventions in 17th-century England. From the hilarious misunderstandings and deceptions in 'The Country Wife' to the passionate and dangerous liaisons in 'The Plain Dealer,' Wycherley's plays are filled with sharp dialogue, clever wordplay, and unexpected twists. Through his vivid characters and their intricate relationships, Wycherley offers a satirical and provocative glimpse into the society of his time.

Genres: Comedy, Satire, Restoration Drama

Themes: Love and desire, Marriage and infidelity, Social conventions and hypocrisy
