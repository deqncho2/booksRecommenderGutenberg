Synopsis: 

In 'Ireland as It Is, and as It Would Be Under Home Rule', Robert John Buckley takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex political landscape of Ireland. Through vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Buckley paints a vivid picture of the current state of Ireland and offers a thought-provoking vision of what the future could hold under Home Rule. From the struggles of the Irish people to the potential impact of political change, this book delves into the heart of Ireland's identity and its aspirations for self-governance. 

Genres: Political analysis, Historical commentary, Social commentary

Themes: Irish nationalism, Self-governance, Political reform, Cultural identity
