Synopsis: 
In "Summa Theologica, Part II-II (Secunda Secundae)", Thomas Aquinas, a renowned theologian and philosopher, delves into the complexities of moral theology and ethics. This profound work explores various moral dilemmas and provides insightful arguments and explanations, challenging readers to contemplate the nature of virtue, sin, and the pursuit of a virtuous life. Through a systematic and logical approach, Aquinas presents a comprehensive analysis of human actions, their consequences, and the principles that guide ethical decision-making. This translation by the Fathers of the English Dominican Province allows readers to engage with Aquinas' timeless wisdom and thought-provoking insights.

Genres: Theology, Philosophy, Ethics
Themes: Moral theology, Virtue, Sin, Ethical decision-making
