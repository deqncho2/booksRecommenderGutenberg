Synopsis: In "Early Theories of Translation" by Flora Ross Amos, embark on a captivating journey through the history of translation as we explore the fascinating theories that shaped this art form. From ancient civilizations to the Renaissance, witness the evolution of translation and the profound impact it has had on language, culture, and communication. Delve into the minds of renowned translators and philosophers as they grapple with the complexities of bridging linguistic and cultural gaps. Discover the secrets behind the art of translation and unravel the mysteries that lie within its rich history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Linguistics

Themes: Translation theory, Language and culture, Communication
