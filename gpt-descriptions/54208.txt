Synopsis: In 'Standard Measures of United States, Great Britain and France: History and actual comparisons. With appendix on introduction of the mètre', Arthur S. C. Wurtele takes readers on a fascinating journey through the historical development and comparisons of standard measures in the United States, Great Britain, and France. From the origins of measurement systems to the challenges faced in establishing uniformity, Wurtele explores the intricate details of how these three nations shaped their respective systems. With a special focus on the introduction of the mètre, readers will uncover the intriguing stories behind the adoption of this revolutionary unit of measurement. This comprehensive study offers a captivating exploration of the evolution of standard measures and their impact on society.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Science

Themes: Measurement systems, Historical development, Uniformity, Cultural impact
