Synopsis: 

In 'Prehistoric Man', Duckworth takes readers on a captivating journey back in time to explore the lives of our ancient ancestors. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, he paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by prehistoric humans as they navigated a harsh and unforgiving world. From the discovery of fire to the development of tools and the emergence of complex social structures, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the origins of humanity and the remarkable ingenuity of our early ancestors.

Genres: Non-fiction, Anthropology, Archaeology

Themes: Human evolution, Survival, Cultural development
