Synopsis: 

In "All About Coffee," William H. Ukers takes readers on an enchanting journey through the captivating history, cultural significance, and global impact of the beloved beverage that has become an integral part of our daily lives. From its mysterious origins in ancient Ethiopia to its widespread popularity across continents, Ukers delves into the fascinating tales of coffee's discovery, cultivation, and consumption. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, he explores the intricate processes of coffee production, the rise of coffeehouses as social hubs, and the profound influence of coffee on art, literature, and commerce. This comprehensive and enlightening exploration of coffee's past, present, and future will leave readers with a newfound appreciation for the rich and complex world that resides within their morning cup.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Culinary

Themes: Cultural significance, Global impact, Social history
