Synopsis: 

'John Stuart Mill; His Life and Works: Twelve Sketches by Herbert Spencer, Henry Fawcett, Frederic Harrison, and Other Distinguished Authors' offers a captivating exploration of the life and intellectual contributions of one of the most influential thinkers of the 19th century, John Stuart Mill. Through twelve insightful sketches penned by renowned authors, readers are taken on a journey through Mill's personal experiences, philosophical ideas, and political activism. From his upbringing in a highly intellectual family to his groundbreaking works on utilitarianism and individual liberty, this book delves into the multifaceted aspects of Mill's life and legacy. Each sketch provides a unique perspective, shedding light on different facets of Mill's character and intellectual pursuits. 

Genres: Biography, Philosophy, History

Themes: Intellectualism, Utilitarianism, Individual Liberty, Political Activism
