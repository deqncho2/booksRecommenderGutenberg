Synopsis: 

In "Nuts to Crack; or Quips, Quirks, Anecdote and Facete of Oxford and Cambridge Scholars," Richard Gooch takes us on a delightful journey through the eccentricities and wit of the scholars from two of the world's most prestigious universities. From humorous anecdotes to clever quips, this collection offers a glimpse into the unique world of Oxford and Cambridge scholars, showcasing their intellectual prowess and their ability to find humor in the most unexpected places. 

Genres: Humor, Anecdotes, Academic

Themes: Wit and humor, Intellectualism, University culture
