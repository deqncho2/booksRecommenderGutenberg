Synopsis: 

'Great Zimbabwe, Mashonaland, Rhodesia: An account of two years' examination work in 1902-4 on behalf of the government of Rhodesia' takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient ruins of Great Zimbabwe. Richard Nicklin Hall, an explorer and archaeologist, shares his firsthand experiences and discoveries during his two-year examination of this mysterious site. As he delves into the history and significance of Great Zimbabwe, Hall uncovers secrets that challenge conventional beliefs and shed light on the enigmatic civilization that once thrived there. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, this book offers a fascinating exploration of a lost world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Archaeology, History

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Exploration, Cultural heritage
