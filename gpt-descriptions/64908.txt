Synopsis: In 'What Is Art?' by Leo Tolstoy, the reader embarks on a thought-provoking exploration of the nature and purpose of art. Through a series of insightful essays, Tolstoy challenges conventional notions of art and delves into its true essence, questioning its role in society and its ability to evoke genuine emotions. With a captivating blend of philosophical musings and personal anecdotes, Tolstoy invites readers to reconsider their understanding of art and its profound impact on the human experience.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Literary Criticism

Themes: The nature of art, the purpose of art, the role of art in society, the emotional power of art
