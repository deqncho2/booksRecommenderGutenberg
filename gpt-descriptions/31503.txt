Synopsis: 
In "Contemporary Russian Novelists," Serge Persky takes readers on a captivating journey through the minds and works of some of the most talented and enigmatic writers of modern Russia. From the depths of Moscow's literary underground to the vast landscapes of Siberia, Persky delves into the lives and creative processes of these novelists, revealing the struggles, triumphs, and unique perspectives that shape their captivating narratives. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the rich tapestry of contemporary Russian literature.

Genres: Literary criticism, Biography, Cultural studies

Themes: Identity, Politics, Artistic expression, Social commentary
