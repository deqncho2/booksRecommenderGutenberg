Synopsis: 

In "The Mute Stones Speak: The Story of Archaeology in Italy," author Paul Lachlan MacKendrick takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of Italian archaeology. From the ancient Etruscans to the mighty Romans, MacKendrick unveils the remarkable discoveries and groundbreaking excavations that have shaped our understanding of Italy's past. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, he brings to life the passionate archaeologists who dedicated their lives to unearthing the secrets buried beneath Italy's soil. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the tales of lost cities, magnificent artworks, and the enigmatic remnants of ancient civilizations. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Archaeology

Themes: Exploration, Cultural Heritage, Historical Preservation
