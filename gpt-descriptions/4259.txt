Synopsis: In the third volume of "Andersonville: A Story of Rebel Military Prisons," author John McElroy continues his gripping account of the infamous Confederate prison during the American Civil War. As the prisoners of Andersonville face unimaginable hardships and struggle to survive in the harsh conditions, McElroy delves deeper into the complex dynamics between the captives and their captors. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, he uncovers the untold stories of bravery, resilience, and the human spirit that emerged amidst the horrors of war. This volume offers a compelling exploration of the daily struggles, alliances, and conflicts that shaped the lives of those confined within the walls of Andersonville.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Biographical Fiction

Themes: Survival, Resilience, Human Spirit, Prison Life, War and Conflict
