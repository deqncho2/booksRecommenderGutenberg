Synopsis: 
In "The Honey-Bee: Its Natural History, Physiology and Management," Edward Bevan takes readers on a captivating journey into the intricate world of honey bees. From their fascinating natural history to the inner workings of their physiology, Bevan delves into the secrets of these remarkable creatures. With a blend of scientific knowledge and captivating storytelling, he explores the complex social structure of bee colonies, their remarkable communication methods, and the vital role they play in our ecosystem. As Bevan unravels the mysteries of the honey bee, readers will gain a newfound appreciation for these industrious insects and the delicate balance of nature they contribute to.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Science

Themes: Nature's intricacies, Ecosystem interdependence, Animal behavior
