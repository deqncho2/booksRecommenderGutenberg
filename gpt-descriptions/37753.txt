Synopsis: 

'Notes on the Mammals of Gogebic and Ontonagon Counties, Michigan, 1920: Occasional Papers of the Museum of Zoology, Number 109' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse mammal species found in the counties of Gogebic and Ontonagon in Michigan. Written by Lee R. Dice and Harley Bakwel Sherman, this meticulously researched work provides a comprehensive account of the mammals' behavior, habitats, and distribution in the region during the early 20th century. From the elusive woodland creatures to the majestic predators, the authors delve into the fascinating world of these mammals, shedding light on their ecological significance and the challenges they face in their natural environment. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural history, Zoology

Themes: Biodiversity, Conservation, Wildlife research
