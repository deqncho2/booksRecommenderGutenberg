Synopsis: 
In "An Account of the Late Improvements in Galvanism," Giovanni Aldini takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of electricity and its effects on the human body. Through a series of curious and intriguing experiments, Aldini showcases the incredible power of galvanism, captivating the attention of the Commissioners of the French National Institute and the audiences in the Anatomical Theatres of London. As the experiments unfold, readers are left in awe of the astonishing and sometimes unsettling results. This account offers a unique insight into the advancements in galvanism and the profound impact it has on both science and society.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: 
Electricity, Human Anatomy, Scientific Advancements
