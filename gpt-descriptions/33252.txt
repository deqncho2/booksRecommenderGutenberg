Synopsis: 
'Tuscan Sculpture of the Fifteenth Century: A Collection of Sixteen Pictures Reproducing Works by Donatello, the Della Robia, Mino da Fiesole, and Others, with Introduction' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich artistic heritage of 15th-century Tuscan sculpture. Through a collection of sixteen stunning pictures, the book showcases the masterpieces created by renowned sculptors such as Donatello, the Della Robia, Mino da Fiesole, and others. With an insightful introduction, the author delves into the historical and cultural context surrounding these sculptures, providing a deeper understanding of their significance and impact. This book is a visual feast that will transport readers to a time of artistic brilliance and inspire a newfound appreciation for the beauty and craftsmanship of Tuscan sculpture.

Genres: Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Renaissance art, Sculpture, Italian culture and history
