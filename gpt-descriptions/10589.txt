Synopsis: 

'Turkish Prisoners in Egypt: A Report by the Delegates of the International Committee of the Red Cross' takes readers on a gripping journey into the depths of a little-known chapter of history. Set during World War I, this eye-opening report unveils the harrowing experiences of Turkish prisoners held captive in Egypt. Through the meticulous documentation of the International Committee of the Red Cross delegates, readers are exposed to the unimaginable challenges faced by these prisoners, shedding light on the human spirit's resilience in the face of adversity. As the report delves into the conditions, struggles, and hopes of these prisoners, it uncovers a hidden world of courage, survival, and the enduring power of compassion.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, War

Themes: Human resilience, Survival, Compassion, Prisoner experiences, World War I
