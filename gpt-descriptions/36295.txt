Synopsis: In 'The Pastor's Wife' by Elizabeth von Arnim, a young woman named Emmeline marries a pastor and moves to a small village, expecting a life of piety and devotion. However, she soon discovers that her role as the pastor's wife comes with its own set of challenges and expectations. As she navigates the complexities of her new position, Emmeline finds herself questioning her own desires and ambitions, and the boundaries of love and duty. With a cast of intriguing characters and a captivating setting, this novel explores the inner world of a woman struggling to find her own voice and identity in a society that often stifles individuality.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Women's Literature

Themes: Identity, Gender Roles, Marriage, Social Expectations
