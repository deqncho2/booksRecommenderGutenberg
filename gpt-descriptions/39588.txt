Synopsis: In 'The Human Slaughter-House: Scenes from the War that is Sure to Come', Wilhelm Lamszus takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing realities of war. Set in the early 20th century, the story follows a diverse group of individuals whose lives become intertwined amidst the chaos and brutality of conflict. As the war unfolds, the characters are forced to confront their deepest fears, question their loyalties, and grapple with the profound moral dilemmas that arise in times of war. Lamszus paints a vivid and haunting portrait of the human condition in the face of violence and destruction, leaving readers captivated by the raw emotions and stark realities that unfold.

Genres: War fiction, Historical fiction, Drama

Themes: The horrors of war, Moral ambiguity, Human resilience, Loyalty and betrayal
