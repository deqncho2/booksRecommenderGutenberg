Synopsis: In "The Vagrancy Problem: The Case for Measures of Restraint for Tramps, Loafers, and Unemployables," William Harbutt Dawson delves into the controversial issue of vagrancy and proposes measures to address it. Through a comprehensive study of detention colonies and labor houses across Europe, Dawson explores the effectiveness of various approaches in dealing with the growing problem of homelessness and unemployment. This thought-provoking work sheds light on the complexities of social welfare and raises important questions about the balance between individual freedom and societal responsibility.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, History

Themes: Vagrancy, Homelessness, Social Welfare, Individual Freedom, Societal Responsibility
