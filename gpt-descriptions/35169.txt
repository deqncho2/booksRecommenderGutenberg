Synopsis: 

In the ninth volume of the renowned Encyclopaedia Britannica, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of "Electrostatics" to "Engis." This volume delves into the intricate study of electricity, exploring the principles of electrostatics, the behavior of electric charges, and the mesmerizing phenomena that arise from their interactions. From the exploration of lightning and the discovery of electric currents to the practical applications of electricity in various fields, this volume offers a comprehensive and enlightening exploration of the electrifying subject matter. As readers progress through the pages, they will encounter captivating stories, groundbreaking experiments, and the brilliant minds that have shaped our understanding of electricity. 

Genres: Reference, Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Electricity, Electrostatics, Scientific Discoveries
