Synopsis: 
In "Bell's Cathedrals: The Cathedral Church of Exeter: A Description of Its Fabric and a Brief History of the Episcopal See," Addleshaw takes readers on a captivating journey through the magnificent Exeter Cathedral. With meticulous attention to detail, the author unveils the architectural wonders and historical significance of this iconic structure. From its stunning stained glass windows to its intricate carvings, readers will be transported to a world of awe-inspiring beauty. Alongside the architectural exploration, Addleshaw delves into the rich history of the Episcopal See, shedding light on the influential figures and events that shaped the cathedral's past. This book is a must-read for history enthusiasts, architecture lovers, and anyone seeking to uncover the secrets of one of England's most remarkable cathedrals.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Architecture

Themes: Architecture, History, Religious Heritage
