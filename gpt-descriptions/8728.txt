Synopsis: In "The Winning of Canada: a Chronicle of Wolfe" by William Wood, readers are transported to the 18th century during the height of the French and Indian War. This gripping historical narrative follows the remarkable journey of General James Wolfe as he leads the British forces in their quest to capture the strategic stronghold of Quebec. Filled with vivid descriptions of battles, political intrigue, and personal sacrifices, this chronicle brings to life the heroic efforts and strategic brilliance of Wolfe and his men. As the fate of Canada hangs in the balance, readers will be captivated by the high-stakes drama and the ultimate triumph that forever changed the course of history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Military History, Biography

Themes: Leadership, Sacrifice, National Identity
