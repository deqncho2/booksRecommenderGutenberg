Synopsis: In "The Numbering of the People," George Allen, the Vicar of St. Thomas's Church in Islington, delivers a powerful sermon in conjunction with the census of 1861. With eloquence and passion, he explores the significance of the census and its implications for society. Through thought-provoking anecdotes and biblical references, Allen delves into the deeper meaning behind the act of numbering the people, shedding light on the individual's place in the grand tapestry of humanity. This sermon challenges the congregation to reflect on their own lives and consider the broader implications of their existence.

Genres: Sermon, Religious, Historical

Themes: Identity, Society, Spirituality
