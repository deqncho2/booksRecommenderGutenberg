Synopsis: 
'Political and Literary essays, 1908-1913' by Cromer, Evelyn Baring, Earl of is a collection of thought-provoking essays that delve into the political and literary landscape of the early 20th century. Through a series of insightful and eloquent writings, the author explores various topics such as imperialism, democracy, literature, and the role of the individual in society. With a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of the complexities of the time, Baring offers a captivating exploration of the political and literary world of his era.

Genres: 
Essay, Political Non-fiction, Literary Criticism

Themes: 
Imperialism, Democracy, Literature, Individualism
