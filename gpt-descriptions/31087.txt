Synopsis: 
In 'A Rebel War Clerk's Diary at the Confederate States Capital', J.B. Jones takes us on a captivating journey through the heart of the Confederate States during the American Civil War. Through the eyes of a rebel war clerk, we witness the inner workings of the government, the struggles of the soldiers, and the daily life of the citizens. As the war rages on, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and the true cost of war becomes painfully clear. With vivid descriptions and a keen eye for detail, Jones paints a vivid picture of a nation torn apart by conflict, leaving readers eager to uncover the untold stories hidden within the pages of this remarkable diary.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Memoir, War

Themes: Loyalty, Sacrifice, Survival, Political Intrigue
