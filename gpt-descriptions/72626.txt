Synopsis: In "The Witchcraft Delusion of 1692," Thomas Hutchinson takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the darkest periods in American history. Set in Salem, Massachusetts, this non-fiction account delves into the mysterious events that unfolded during the infamous Salem witch trials. Hutchinson meticulously examines the social, religious, and political factors that contributed to the mass hysteria and the subsequent persecution of innocent individuals. Through vivid storytelling and extensive research, he sheds light on the complex web of fear, suspicion, and superstition that gripped the town, leaving readers captivated by the chilling tale of witchcraft and its devastating consequences.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, True Crime

Themes: Mass hysteria, Religious fanaticism, Social and political tensions
