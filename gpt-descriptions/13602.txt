Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume IV, Georgia Narratives, Part 1', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a collection of powerful and poignant firsthand accounts from former slaves in Georgia. These narratives offer a rare glimpse into the harsh realities of slavery, as individuals share their personal experiences, struggles, and triumphs. From tales of resilience and resistance to heart-wrenching stories of separation and loss, this volume paints a vivid picture of the lives of enslaved individuals in Georgia. Through their voices, we gain a deeper understanding of the complexities and lasting impact of slavery in America.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes:
Slavery, Resistance, Resilience, Identity, Freedom
