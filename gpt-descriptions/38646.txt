Synopsis: In 'The Eldest Son' by Archibald Marshall, a young man named Richard Larchdale finds himself torn between duty and desire when he unexpectedly inherits his family's estate. As he navigates the complexities of his new role as the eldest son, Richard must confront family secrets, societal expectations, and the pursuit of true happiness. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century England, this captivating novel explores the challenges of identity, love, and the choices that shape our lives.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Duty vs. Desire, Family Secrets, Societal Expectations, Identity, Love
