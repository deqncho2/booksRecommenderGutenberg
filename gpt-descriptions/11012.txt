Synopsis: 
'The Autobiography of an Ex-Colored Man' follows the life of an unnamed protagonist who navigates the complexities of racial identity in early 20th century America. Born to a mixed-race mother and a white father, the protagonist grapples with the challenges of being both accepted and rejected by different racial communities. As he grows older, he finds himself torn between embracing his African American heritage and passing as white to gain societal advantages. Through his experiences as a musician, a lover, and an observer of racial dynamics, the protagonist offers a poignant exploration of race, identity, and the pursuit of self-discovery.

Genres: 
Autobiography, Fiction, African American Literature

Themes: 
Racial identity, Passing, Cultural assimilation, Self-discovery
