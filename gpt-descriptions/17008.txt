Synopsis: In 'A Counter-Blaste to Tobacco', King James I of England takes a bold stance against the growing popularity of tobacco, unleashing a passionate and thought-provoking argument that challenges societal norms. Through a series of compelling arguments and witty rhetoric, the king delves into the physical, social, and moral implications of tobacco use, leaving readers questioning their own habits and beliefs. 

Genres: Essay, Satire, Historical 

Themes: Health, Morality, Social Influence
