Synopsis: 

In "Isaac Watts; his life and writings, his homes and friends" by Edwin Paxton Hood, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the life and works of the renowned hymn writer, Isaac Watts. From his humble beginnings to his rise as one of the most influential figures in Christian hymnody, this book delves into the fascinating details of Watts' personal life, his relationships, and the impact of his writings on generations to come. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, Hood paints a vivid portrait of a man whose words continue to resonate with believers around the world.

Genres: Biography, History, Music

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Creativity and inspiration, Influence and legacy
