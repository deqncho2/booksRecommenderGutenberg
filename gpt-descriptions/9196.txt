Synopsis: 
"The Clockmaker; Or, the Sayings and Doings of Samuel Slick, of Slickville" is a witty and entertaining novel that follows the adventures of Samuel Slick, a charismatic and cunning clockmaker from Slickville. Through his humorous anecdotes and sharp observations, Slick provides a unique perspective on the social and political landscape of early 19th-century Nova Scotia. As he travels across the province, encountering a colorful cast of characters, Slick's clever wit and shrewd insights shed light on the complexities of human nature and the pursuit of success in a rapidly changing world.

Genres: Satire, Comedy, Historical Fiction

Themes: Social commentary, Ambition, Human nature
