Synopsis: 

In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume XV, Tennessee Narratives', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a captivating collection of firsthand accounts from former slaves in Tennessee. These narratives offer a unique and intimate glimpse into the lives of those who endured the horrors of slavery, sharing their personal stories of resilience, survival, and the pursuit of freedom. From tales of family bonds and acts of resistance to the challenges faced during Reconstruction, this volume paints a vivid picture of the complex and often overlooked experiences of enslaved individuals in Tennessee. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Oral History

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Resilience, Family, Resistance, Reconstruction
