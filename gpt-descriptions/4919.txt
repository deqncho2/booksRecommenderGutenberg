Synopsis: 
In the fourth volume of 'The Letters of Horace Walpole, Earl of Orford', readers are transported into the captivating world of 18th-century England through the intimate correspondence of Horace Walpole. This collection of letters offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of the aristocracy, filled with scandalous gossip, political intrigue, and personal reflections. From his witty observations on society to his passionate discussions on art and literature, Walpole's letters provide a vivid portrait of a bygone era, where love affairs, social rivalries, and historical events intertwine to create a captivating narrative.

Genres: 
Biography, Historical Non-fiction, Epistolary

Themes: 
Society and Culture, Politics and Power, Art and Literature
