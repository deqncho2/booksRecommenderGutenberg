Synopsis: In 'Essays on Russian Novelists', William Lyon Phelps takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of Russian literature. From the passionate characters of Dostoevsky to the enchanting landscapes of Tolstoy, Phelps delves into the depths of these renowned authors' works, exploring the complexities of human nature, the struggles of society, and the power of love and redemption. Through insightful analysis and thought-provoking interpretations, this collection of essays offers a fresh perspective on some of the greatest Russian novelists of all time.

Genres: Literary criticism, Non-fiction, Russian literature

Themes: Human nature, Society, Love and redemption
