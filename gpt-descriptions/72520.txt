Synopsis: 

'Annals of the persecution in Scotland: From the Restoration to the Revolution' by James Aikman is a gripping historical account that delves into the tumultuous period of Scottish history between the Restoration and the Revolution. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Aikman unveils the harrowing experiences of the Scottish people as they faced religious persecution and political upheaval. From secret meetings and underground networks to daring escapes and acts of defiance, this book uncovers the untold stories of those who fought for their beliefs and freedom in the face of adversity. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Religious persecution, Political turmoil, Resistance and resilience
