Synopsis: In "Chats on Old Copper and Brass," Fred W. Burgess takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of antique copper and brass artifacts. From ancient times to the present day, Burgess delves into the history, craftsmanship, and stories behind these beautiful objects, revealing the secrets they hold and the allure they possess. Through engaging conversations and anecdotes, he explores the significance of these metals in various cultures, their use in everyday life, and the artistry involved in their creation. Whether it's a delicate copper vase or a majestic brass sculpture, each piece has a tale to tell, and Burgess skillfully unveils their hidden narratives, leaving readers eager to discover more about these timeless treasures.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Material culture, Craftsmanship, Cultural significance
