Synopsis: In 'Post mortem: Essays, historical and medical', Charles MacLaurin delves into the fascinating world of death, exploring its historical and medical aspects through a collection of thought-provoking essays. From ancient burial practices to the evolution of forensic science, MacLaurin uncovers captivating stories and intriguing facts that shed light on our understanding of mortality. With a blend of meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this book takes readers on a captivating journey through the mysteries and complexities of death.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medicine

Themes: Death, Mortality, History of Medicine, Forensic Science
