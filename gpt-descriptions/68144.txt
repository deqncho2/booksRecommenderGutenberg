Synopsis: In "Thoughts upon slavery," John Wesley delves into the controversial topic of slavery, presenting a thought-provoking exploration that challenges societal norms and raises important questions about human rights and morality. Through a series of powerful arguments and compelling anecdotes, Wesley invites readers to critically examine the institution of slavery and its impact on both the enslaved and the enslavers. This thought-provoking work offers a unique perspective on a dark chapter in history, leaving readers with a renewed sense of empathy and a call to action.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Social Commentary

Themes: Slavery, Human Rights, Morality, Social Justice
