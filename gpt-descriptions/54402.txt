Synopsis: In 'A Broken Journey: Wanderings from the Hoang-Ho to the Island of Saghalien and the Upper Reaches of the Amur River', Mary Gaunt takes readers on an extraordinary adventure through the remote and captivating landscapes of China and Russia. From the bustling streets of Peking to the untamed wilderness of Saghalien, Gaunt's vivid descriptions and encounters with diverse cultures and people will transport readers to a world filled with both beauty and danger. As she navigates through treacherous rivers, encounters bandits, and witnesses the harsh realities of life in these regions, Gaunt's journey becomes not only a physical exploration but also a profound introspection into the human spirit and the resilience of the human soul.

Genres: Travelogue, Adventure, Memoir

Themes: Exploration, Cultural diversity, Resilience
