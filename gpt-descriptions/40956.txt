Synopsis: 

In Volume 15, Slice 5 of the renowned Encyclopaedia Britannica, readers are taken on a captivating journey through a diverse range of topics, from the intricate workings of joints in the human body to the influential reign of the Byzantine Emperor Justinian I. This volume delves into the fascinating world of anatomy, exploring the structure and function of joints, shedding light on their importance in human movement and mobility. Additionally, it offers a comprehensive account of the life and accomplishments of Justinian I, a pivotal figure in the history of the Byzantine Empire. With its rich and detailed entries, this volume provides a wealth of knowledge that will captivate readers and deepen their understanding of these intriguing subjects.

Genres: Reference, Non-fiction

Themes: Anatomy, History, Biography
