Synopsis: In 'The Morality of Woman, and Other Essays', Ellen Key delves into the complex and often controversial topic of women's morality. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Key challenges societal norms and explores the multifaceted nature of female morality. From examining the role of women in society to questioning traditional notions of virtue, Key offers a fresh perspective on the moral landscape of womanhood. With her insightful analysis and compelling arguments, she invites readers to reconsider their preconceived notions and engage in a profound exploration of morality and gender.

Genres: Non-fiction, Feminist literature, Social commentary

Themes: Women's rights, Gender roles, Morality, Feminism
