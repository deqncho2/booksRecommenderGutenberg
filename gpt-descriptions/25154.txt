Synopsis: 

In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume II, Arkansas Narratives, Part 4', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a captivating collection of firsthand accounts from former slaves in Arkansas. These narratives offer a unique and intimate glimpse into the lives of individuals who endured the horrors of slavery and ultimately triumphed over adversity. From tales of resilience and survival to stories of love and loss, this volume paints a vivid picture of the human spirit's indomitable strength in the face of unimaginable cruelty. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Oral History

Themes: Slavery, Resilience, Human Spirit, Survival
