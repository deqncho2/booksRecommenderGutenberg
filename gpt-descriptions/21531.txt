Synopsis: 

In 'Woodwork Joints: How They Are Set Out, How Made and Where Used,' author William Fairham takes readers on a fascinating journey into the world of woodworking, revealing the secrets behind the art of creating strong and beautiful joints. From the humble butt joint to the intricate dovetail, Fairham explores the various techniques, tools, and materials used in woodworking, providing practical guidance and expert advice along the way. With detailed illustrations and step-by-step instructions, this book is a must-read for both beginners and seasoned woodworkers alike, offering valuable insights into the craftsmanship and precision required to master the art of joinery.

Genres: Non-fiction, Crafts and Hobbies, Woodworking

Themes: Craftsmanship, Precision, Practicality
