Synopsis: 

"A Narrative of the Most Remarkable Particulars in the Life of James Albert Ukawsaw Gronniosaw, an African Prince, as Related by Himself" is a captivating autobiography that takes readers on a journey through the extraordinary life of James Albert Ukawsaw Gronniosaw. Born into African royalty, Gronniosaw's life takes an unexpected turn when he is captured and sold into slavery. From his harrowing experiences on a plantation to his eventual escape and journey to England, Gronniosaw's story is one of resilience, courage, and the pursuit of freedom. 

Genres: Autobiography, Slave Narrative, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Identity, Freedom, Resilience
