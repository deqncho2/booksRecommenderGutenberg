Synopsis:
In "Maria Antoinette: Makers of History," John S. C. Abbott takes us on a captivating journey through the life of one of history's most enigmatic figures. From her opulent upbringing in Austria to her fateful marriage to the future King of France, this biography delves into the fascinating world of Maria Antoinette. As we follow her through the glittering halls of Versailles and witness the tumultuous events that led to the French Revolution, we are drawn into a tale of power, intrigue, and tragedy. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Abbott paints a vivid portrait of a woman who became both a symbol of extravagance and a victim of circumstance. This gripping account of Maria Antoinette's life will leave readers spellbound, eager to uncover the secrets and mysteries that surrounded her until the very end.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and privilege, Revolution and social change, Personal identity and public perception
