Synopsis: In "Beethoven: The story of a little boy who was forced to practice," Tapper, Thomas takes us on a captivating journey into the life of a young Ludwig van Beethoven. Set in 18th-century Germany, this biographical novel explores the struggles and triumphs of a child prodigy who was pushed to his limits by his demanding father. From his early days as a piano virtuoso to his relentless pursuit of musical excellence, Beethoven's story is one of passion, determination, and the transformative power of music. As we delve into the world of this musical genius, we witness the sacrifices he made and the obstacles he overcame to become one of the greatest composers of all time.

Genres: Biographical Fiction, Historical Fiction, Music

Themes: Ambition, Family Dynamics, Artistic Expression, Perseverance
