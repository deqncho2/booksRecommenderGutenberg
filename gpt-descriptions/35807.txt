Synopsis: In 'Non-combatants and Others' by Macaulay, Rose, a group of individuals find themselves caught in the midst of World War II, each grappling with their own personal struggles and desires. From a young woman seeking love and adventure to a soldier questioning his duty, their lives intertwine in unexpected ways, revealing the complexities of human nature and the profound impact of war. As the war rages on, the characters must confront their own fears, make difficult choices, and ultimately discover the true meaning of courage and sacrifice.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Drama

Themes: Love and Relationships, Identity, Morality, the Effects of War
