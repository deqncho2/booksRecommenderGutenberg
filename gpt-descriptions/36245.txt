Synopsis: 
In "A History of Literary Criticism in the Renaissance: With special reference to the influence of Italy in the formation and development of modern classicism," Joel Elias Spingarn takes readers on a captivating journey through the Renaissance period, exploring the profound impact of Italy on the evolution of classicism. From the revival of ancient Greek and Roman literature to the emergence of new literary forms, Spingarn delves into the minds of influential thinkers and writers, shedding light on their contributions to the development of literary criticism. This comprehensive work offers a fascinating exploration of the Renaissance era, revealing the intricate interplay between literature, art, and intellectual thought.

Genres: Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Renaissance, Classicism, Influence, Literature, Art, Intellectual Thought
