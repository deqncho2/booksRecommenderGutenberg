Synopsis: 

'Notes of a Camp-Follower on the Western Front' is a gripping and intimate account of life on the frontlines during World War I. Written by E. W. Hornung, the author of the famous Raffles series, this book takes readers on a journey through the harrowing experiences of a camp-follower, providing a unique perspective on the war.

Through vivid and evocative descriptions, the author paints a vivid picture of the daily struggles, camaraderie, and horrors faced by those who supported the troops on the Western Front. From the chaos of the battlefield to the quiet moments of reflection, the protagonist's observations offer a compelling insight into the human spirit amidst the brutality of war.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Fiction, War Literature
Themes: War, Survival, Human Resilience, Camaraderie
