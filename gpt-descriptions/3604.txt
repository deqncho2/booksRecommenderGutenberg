Synopsis: In "Jailed for Freedom" by Doris Stevens, a group of courageous women embark on a daring journey to fight for their rights and challenge the oppressive norms of society. Set against the backdrop of the suffrage movement in the early 20th century, this gripping narrative follows the struggles, sacrifices, and triumphs of these trailblazing women as they navigate the treacherous path towards equality. With their unwavering determination and unwavering spirit, they defy societal expectations and risk everything for the cause they believe in. As their fight intensifies, the women find themselves facing unimaginable obstacles, including imprisonment and persecution. Will their unwavering spirit be enough to overcome the forces that seek to silence them? Genres: Historical Fiction, Women's Literature, Activism Themes: Gender Equality, Social Justice, Civil Rights
