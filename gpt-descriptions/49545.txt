Synopsis: In "Social Work; Essays on the Meeting Ground of Doctor and Social Worker," Richard C. Cabot delves into the complex and often overlooked relationship between doctors and social workers. Through a collection of insightful essays, Cabot explores the challenges and opportunities that arise when these two professions intersect, shedding light on the crucial role social workers play in the healthcare system. With thought-provoking anecdotes and thought-provoking analysis, this book offers a unique perspective on the collaboration between doctors and social workers, ultimately highlighting the potential for transformative change in patient care.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical, Social Sciences

Themes: Collaboration, Healthcare, Patient Care, Interdisciplinary Approach
