Synopsis: In 'The Way to Abolish Slavery' by Charles Stearns, a passionate abolitionist takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the dark corridors of slavery. Through vivid storytelling and compelling arguments, Stearns challenges the prevailing notions of his time and offers a radical solution to the deeply entrenched institution. As he delves into the complexities of human rights, morality, and the power dynamics of society, readers are left questioning their own beliefs and the role they can play in effecting change. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Justice

Themes: Abolitionism, Human Rights, Social Change
