Synopsis: 

'Broad-Sword and Single-Stick: With Chapters on Quarter-Staff, Bayonet, Cudgel, Shillalah, Walking-Stick, Umbrella and Other Weapons of Self-Defence' is a comprehensive guide that delves into the art of self-defense through various weapons. Written by Rowland George Allanson-Winn, Baron Headley, and Clive Phillipps-Wolley, this book explores the techniques and strategies behind wielding weapons such as broad-swords, single-sticks, quarter-staffs, bayonets, and even unconventional items like walking-sticks and umbrellas.

With detailed instructions and historical context, the authors provide a fascinating insight into the world of self-defense and the various weapons used throughout history. From the precise movements of a broad-sword to the agility required for single-stick fighting, this book offers a comprehensive guide for both beginners and experienced practitioners.

Genres: Non-fiction, Martial Arts, Self-Defense

Themes: Self-defense techniques, Historical weaponry, Martial arts training
