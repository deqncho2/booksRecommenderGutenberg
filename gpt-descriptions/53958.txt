Synopsis: In 'The Kallikak Family: A Study in the Heredity of Feeble-Mindedness', Henry Herbert Goddard delves into the fascinating story of the Kallikak family, tracing their lineage and exploring the hereditary factors that contribute to feeble-mindedness. Through meticulous research and compelling anecdotes, Goddard uncovers the complex interplay between genetics, environment, and social factors that shape the lives of individuals within this family. As the narrative unfolds, readers are drawn into a thought-provoking exploration of the consequences of inherited traits and the implications for society at large.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Sociology

Themes: Heredity, Mental Health, Social Impact
