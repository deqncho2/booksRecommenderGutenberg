Synopsis: 

'Memoirs of the Late War, Vol 1 (of 2): Comprising the Personal Narrative of Captain Cooke, of the 43rd Regiment Light Infantry; the History of the Campaign of 1809 in Portugal, by the Earl of Munster; and a Narrative of the Campaign of 1814 in Holland, by Lieut. T. W. D. Moodie, H. P. 21st Fusileers' is a collection of personal narratives and historical accounts from three different individuals who participated in various campaigns during the late war. 

Captain Cooke, a member of the 43rd Regiment Light Infantry, shares his personal experiences and insights into the events he witnessed during the war. The Earl of Munster provides a detailed history of the Campaign of 1809 in Portugal, offering a broader perspective on the military strategies and political context of the time. Lieutenant T. W. D. Moodie, from the 21st Fusileers, recounts his own experiences during the Campaign of 1814 in Holland, shedding light on the challenges faced by soldiers on the ground.

Genres: Historical memoir, Military history

Themes: War experiences, Military strategy, Personal narratives, Historical events
