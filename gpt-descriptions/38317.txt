Synopsis: 
'Annals of the Bodleian Library, Oxford, A.D. 1598-A.D. 1867: With a Preliminary Notice of the earlier Library founded in the Fourteenth Century' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of one of the world's most renowned libraries. From its humble beginnings in the fourteenth century to its transformation into a treasure trove of knowledge, this book unveils the fascinating stories, challenges, and triumphs that shaped the Bodleian Library over the course of nearly three centuries. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, William Dunn Macray paints a vivid picture of the library's evolution, the influential figures who contributed to its growth, and the profound impact it had on academia and the world of literature. 

Genres: History, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Library history, Intellectual heritage, Cultural preservation
