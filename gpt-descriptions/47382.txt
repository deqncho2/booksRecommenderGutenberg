Synopsis: In 'The Sounds of Spoken English: A Manual of Ear Training for English Students: (4th edition)', Walter Ripman takes readers on a captivating journey into the intricacies of spoken English. Through a combination of engaging exercises and insightful explanations, Ripman unravels the mysteries of pronunciation, intonation, and rhythm, providing readers with the tools to master the art of spoken English. From identifying subtle nuances in vowel sounds to understanding the impact of stress on meaning, this manual offers a comprehensive exploration of the sounds that shape the English language. Whether you are a beginner or an advanced learner, 'The Sounds of Spoken English' will revolutionize your understanding and command of spoken English.

Genres: Language Learning, Linguistics, Education

Themes: Pronunciation, Intonation, Rhythm, Language Acquisition
