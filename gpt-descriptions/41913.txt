Synopsis: 
In 'Travels Through North America, During the Years 1825 and 1826', Bernhard, Duke of Saxe-Weimar-Eisenach takes readers on an enthralling journey across the vast and diverse landscapes of North America. From bustling cities to untamed wilderness, the Duke's vivid descriptions and keen observations paint a captivating picture of the continent during the early 19th century. As he encounters various cultures, explores natural wonders, and delves into the political and social fabric of the young nation, the Duke's narrative offers a unique perspective on the complexities and contradictions of America's emerging identity.

Genres: Travelogue, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Exploration, Cultural Exchange, Identity, Nature
