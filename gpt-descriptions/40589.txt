Synopsis: In 'A Brief History of Wood-engraving From Its Invention', Joseph Cundall takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of wood-engraving. From its humble beginnings to its rise as a popular art form, Cundall explores the techniques, tools, and artists that have shaped this unique medium. Through vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, he unveils the intricate process behind wood-engraving and highlights its significant contributions to the world of art and printing. This concise history is a must-read for art enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone curious about the evolution of this timeless craft.

Genres: Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Evolution of Art, Printmaking Techniques, Historical Significance
