Synopsis: 

"The Great Riots of New York, 1712 to 1873" by Joel Tyler Headley takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of New York City, exploring the various riots that shaped the city's development over a span of more than a century. From the slave revolt of 1712 to the infamous Draft Riots during the Civil War, this book delves into the explosive events that shook the very foundations of the city, revealing the deep-seated tensions, social inequalities, and political struggles that ignited these uprisings. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Headley brings to life the chaos, violence, and resilience of the people who fought for their rights and challenged the status quo in the streets of New York.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Social unrest, Civil rights, Political upheaval, Urban development
