Synopsis: In 'The Panama Canal' by Frederic J. Haskin, embark on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of one of the world's most remarkable engineering feats. From the audacious vision of its inception to the arduous challenges faced during its construction, this book unveils the gripping tale of the Panama Canal. Delve into the lives of the courageous individuals who dedicated themselves to this colossal project, as they battled against nature's fury and political obstacles. As the narrative unfolds, the reader will be enthralled by the triumphs and tragedies that shaped the destiny of this iconic waterway, forever altering global trade and transportation.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Engineering

Themes: Ambition, Perseverance, Globalization
