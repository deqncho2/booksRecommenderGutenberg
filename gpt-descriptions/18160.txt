Synopsis: 
In 'In the World War', Ottokar Theobald Otto Maria, Graf Czernin von und zu Chudenitz takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of World War I. Through his unique perspective as a diplomat and politician, Czernin provides a gripping account of the political intrigues, power struggles, and behind-the-scenes negotiations that shaped the course of the war. From the grand halls of diplomacy to the trenches of the front lines, this book offers a compelling narrative that sheds light on the complex dynamics and human stories behind one of the most significant conflicts in history.

Genres: 
Historical non-fiction, memoir, political history

Themes: 
War and diplomacy, power and politics, human resilience
