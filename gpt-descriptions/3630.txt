Synopsis: In 'What to Do? Thoughts Evoked by the Census of Moscow', Tolstoy takes us on a captivating journey through the bustling streets of Moscow, where a young nobleman named Nekhlyudov finds himself questioning the purpose and meaning of his privileged existence. As he encounters various individuals from different walks of life, Nekhlyudov's worldview is challenged, leading him to embark on a profound introspection that will forever alter his life. This thought-provoking tale explores the complexities of social inequality, the search for personal fulfillment, and the moral responsibilities that come with privilege.

Genres: Fiction, Philosophical novel

Themes: Social inequality, Personal fulfillment, Moral responsibility
