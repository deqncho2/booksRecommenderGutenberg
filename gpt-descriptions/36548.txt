Synopsis: In 'A Plain Introduction to the Criticism of the New Testament, Vol. I.', Frederick Henry Ambrose Scrivener takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of biblical criticism. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, Scrivener explores the origins, authorship, and textual variations of the New Testament, shedding light on the fascinating complexities of its composition. From ancient manuscripts to early translations, this volume unveils the hidden layers of the New Testament, challenging readers to question their assumptions and delve deeper into the mysteries of this sacred text.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, Biblical Criticism

Themes: Textual analysis, Authorship, Historical context, Religious scholarship
