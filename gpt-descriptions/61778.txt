Synopsis: 

In "Lafayette in America in 1824 and 1825, Vol. 2 (of 2): Or, Journal of a Voyage to the United States," Auguste Levasseur takes us on an enthralling journey through the United States during the early 19th century. As a close companion to the renowned French general and statesman, Marquis de Lafayette, Levasseur provides a vivid and intimate account of their travels, encounters, and experiences across America. From the bustling streets of New York City to the vast landscapes of the Midwest, the reader is immersed in a captivating narrative that explores the cultural, political, and social fabric of the young nation.

Genres: Historical travelogue, Memoir, Biography

Themes: Friendship and camaraderie, Exploration and discovery, Cultural exchange, Political landscapes, Social dynamics
