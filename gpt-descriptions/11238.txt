Synopsis: In "The Fight for Conservation," Gifford Pinchot takes readers on a captivating journey through the early days of environmentalism, where he recounts his personal experiences and battles to protect America's natural resources. From his encounters with powerful industrialists to his efforts in establishing the first national forest system, Pinchot's memoir is a gripping tale of determination, political intrigue, and the fight to preserve the beauty of the American landscape.

Genres: Memoir, Environmental Nonfiction, History

Themes: Conservation, Environmentalism, Political Activism
