Synopsis: In "The Judicial Murder of Mary E. Surratt," author David Miller DeWitt delves into the gripping true story of a woman accused of conspiring to assassinate President Abraham Lincoln. Set in the aftermath of the American Civil War, this meticulously researched account explores the controversial trial and subsequent execution of Mary E. Surratt, shedding light on the complexities of justice, loyalty, and the blurred lines between guilt and innocence. DeWitt's narrative skillfully unravels the web of political intrigue and personal sacrifice, leaving readers captivated by the haunting tale of a woman caught in the crosshairs of history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Legal drama

Themes: Justice, Loyalty, Guilt and innocence
