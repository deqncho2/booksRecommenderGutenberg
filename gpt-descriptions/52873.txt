Synopsis: 
'A History of Sarawak under Its Two White Rajahs 1839-1908' is a captivating historical account that delves into the fascinating story of Sarawak, a region in Borneo, during the reign of its two British Rajahs. From the arrival of James Brooke in 1839 to the end of Charles Brooke's rule in 1908, the book explores the challenges, triumphs, and complexities faced by these unconventional rulers in their quest to establish and maintain their kingdom. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the authors shed light on the political intrigues, cultural clashes, and economic transformations that shaped Sarawak's history under the Brooke dynasty.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Adventure

Themes: Colonialism, Governance, Cultural exchange, Exploration
