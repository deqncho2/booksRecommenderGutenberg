Synopsis: In 'Sartor Resartus, and On Heroes, Hero-Worship, and the Heroic in History', Thomas Carlyle takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of an enigmatic philosopher, exploring the complexities of human existence and the power of heroism. Through a unique blend of fiction and philosophy, Carlyle delves into the struggles of the protagonist, unraveling the mysteries of his past and the profound impact of his ideas. As the narrative unfolds, readers are transported to a world where the boundaries between reality and imagination blur, leaving them questioning their own perceptions of truth and the meaning of life.

Genres: Fiction, Philosophy, Biography

Themes: Existentialism, Heroism, Identity, Truth, Philosophy of History
