Synopsis: In "John Brown: An Address at the 14th Anniversary of Storer College," Frederick Douglass delivers a powerful speech honoring the memory of the controversial abolitionist John Brown. Douglass recounts his personal experiences with Brown and explores the impact of his radical actions on the fight against slavery. Through vivid anecdotes and impassioned rhetoric, Douglass sheds light on the complexities of Brown's character and the lasting legacy he left behind.

Genres: Historical, Speech, Biography

Themes: Abolitionism, Radicalism, Legacy
