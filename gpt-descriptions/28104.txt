Synopsis: In 'The Pulpit Of The Reformation, Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 4,' three influential figures of the Reformation era, Latimer, Knox, and Welch, deliver powerful sermons that challenge the established religious order. Through their passionate and thought-provoking words, they ignite a spark of change and inspire their listeners to question the traditions and doctrines of the time. Each sermon delves into different aspects of faith, righteousness, and the role of the Church, leaving the reader captivated and eager to explore the depths of their teachings.

Genres: Religious literature, Sermons, Historical non-fiction

Themes: Reformation, Religious reform, Faith, Church authority, Righteousness
