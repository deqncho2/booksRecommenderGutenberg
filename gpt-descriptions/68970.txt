Synopsis: 

In "Passed by the Censor: The Experience of an American Newspaper Man in France," Williams Wythe takes readers on a thrilling journey through the tumultuous landscape of World War I. As an American journalist stationed in France, Wythe provides a firsthand account of the war's impact on the country and its people. From the frontlines to the bustling streets of Paris, he uncovers the truth behind the censorship imposed by the military authorities and the challenges faced by journalists in reporting the war. With vivid descriptions and gripping anecdotes, Wythe offers a unique perspective on the war and the sacrifices made by those involved.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, war journalism, memoir

Themes: War and its impact, censorship, journalism, patriotism, sacrifice
