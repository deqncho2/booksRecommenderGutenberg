Synopsis:
In "Rousseau (Volume 1 and 2)" by John Morley, delve into the captivating life of one of the most influential philosophers of the Enlightenment era, Jean-Jacques Rousseau. This two-volume biography takes readers on a journey through Rousseau's tumultuous life, from his humble beginnings in Geneva to his rise as a prominent thinker and writer in 18th-century France. Morley skillfully explores Rousseau's complex relationships, his controversial ideas on education and society, and the profound impact he had on the political and philosophical landscape of his time. As the story unfolds, readers will be captivated by the personal struggles, intellectual brilliance, and revolutionary ideas that shaped Rousseau's life and work.

Genres: Biography, Philosophy, History

Themes: Enlightenment, Social Contract, Education, Individualism, Political Philosophy
