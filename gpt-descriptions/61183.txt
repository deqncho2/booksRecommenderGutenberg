Synopsis: In 'Weeds' by Pío Baroja, a young man named Andrés Hurtado returns to his hometown after a long absence, only to find it plagued by corruption, poverty, and a sense of hopelessness. As he navigates through the decaying streets and encounters a cast of eccentric characters, Andrés becomes entangled in a web of secrets and personal struggles. With his determination to bring about change, he sets out on a journey that will test his resilience and force him to confront the dark underbelly of society.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Social Commentary

Themes: Corruption, Poverty, Social Injustice, Individualism, Redemption
