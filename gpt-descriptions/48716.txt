Synopsis: In "The History of the 36th (Ulster) Division," Cyril Falls delves into the captivating story of one of the most renowned divisions in World War I. From its formation and training in Northern Ireland to its heroic actions on the battlefields of the Western Front, Falls paints a vivid picture of the division's triumphs, struggles, and the indomitable spirit of its soldiers. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, the author brings to life the experiences of the men who fought and sacrificed for their country, shedding light on the complex dynamics of war and the lasting impact it had on the Ulster Division.

Genres: Military history, Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Patriotism, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, War's impact on society
