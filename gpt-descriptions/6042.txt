Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The Diary and Letters of Madame D'Arblay', Fanny Burney continues to captivate readers with her intimate and vivid accounts of her life as a renowned novelist and a member of the royal court. Through her diary entries and letters, she takes us on a journey filled with fascinating encounters, political intrigue, and personal triumphs and tribulations. As she navigates the complexities of her relationships, both professional and personal, Burney provides a unique glimpse into the world of 18th-century England, offering a compelling narrative that keeps readers eagerly turning the pages.

Genres: Biography, Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Personal growth, Social dynamics, Literary life, Gender roles
