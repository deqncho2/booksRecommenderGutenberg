Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume V, Indiana Narratives', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a captivating collection of firsthand accounts from former slaves in Indiana. These narratives offer a unique perspective on the institution of slavery, shedding light on the experiences, struggles, and triumphs of individuals who lived through this dark period in American history. From tales of escape and resistance to stories of resilience and community, these narratives paint a vivid picture of the lives of enslaved individuals in Indiana. 

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes:
Slavery, Freedom, Resistance, Community, Identity
