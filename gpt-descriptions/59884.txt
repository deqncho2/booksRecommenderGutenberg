Synopsis: 

'Spiritual Folk-Songs of Early America: Two Hundred and Fifty Tunes and Texts, with an Introduction and Notes' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich musical heritage of early America. This collection presents a treasure trove of two hundred and fifty spiritual folk-songs, accompanied by insightful notes and an engaging introduction that sheds light on the historical and cultural context of these timeless melodies. From haunting ballads to uplifting hymns, this book offers a glimpse into the spiritual lives of the early American settlers, capturing the essence of their faith, struggles, and triumphs through the power of music.

Genres: Music, History, Folklore

Themes: Spirituality, Cultural Heritage, Musical Traditions
