Synopsis: 
'Farthest North, Vol. I: Being the Record of a Voyage of Exploration of the Ship 'Fram' 1893-1896' takes readers on an extraordinary journey to the Arctic as Fridtjof Nansen and his crew set out to reach the North Pole. Facing treacherous conditions, extreme cold, and the constant threat of being trapped in ice, Nansen's gripping account details their harrowing experiences and the remarkable discoveries they make along the way. As they push the boundaries of human exploration, the crew's resilience and determination are put to the ultimate test, making this a captivating tale of adventure and survival.

Genres: Adventure, Exploration, Non-fiction

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Human resilience, Survival in extreme conditions
