Synopsis: In 'Critical Miscellanies (Vol. 3 of 3), Essay 6: Harriet Martineau', John Morley delves into the life and works of the remarkable Harriet Martineau, a prominent 19th-century writer and social theorist. Morley explores Martineau's unique perspective on various social issues, her fearless approach to controversial topics, and her significant contributions to the feminist movement. Through an engaging analysis of Martineau's writings and personal experiences, Morley sheds light on the enduring legacy of this influential figure.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Social Commentary

Themes: Feminism, Social Reform, Intellectual Independence
