Synopsis: In "The Raid of John Brown at Harper's Ferry as I Saw It," Samuel V. Leech provides a gripping firsthand account of one of the most daring and controversial events in American history. As a young sailor aboard the USS Niagara, Leech finds himself unexpectedly thrust into the midst of John Brown's audacious raid on the federal armory at Harper's Ferry. Through his vivid and detailed recollections, Leech takes readers on a thrilling journey, filled with suspense, danger, and the clash of ideologies. As the raid unfolds, Leech grapples with his own beliefs and loyalties, ultimately witnessing the profound impact that this pivotal moment in history has on the nation. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Adventure

Themes: Heroism, Ideological conflict, Consequences of actions
