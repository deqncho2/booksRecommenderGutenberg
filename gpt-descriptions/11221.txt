Synopsis: 
In 'The Bent Twig' by Dorothy Canfield Fisher, we follow the journey of a young woman named Helen, who is determined to break free from the constraints of her traditional upbringing. As she navigates the challenges of love, education, and societal expectations, Helen discovers her own strength and resilience. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century America, this coming-of-age story explores the complexities of family, identity, and the pursuit of personal fulfillment.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Historical

Themes: Self-discovery, Independence, Gender roles, Education
