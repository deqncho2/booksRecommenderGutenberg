Synopsis: 

In 'Fifty Birds of Town and City', authors Hines and Anastasi take readers on an enchanting journey through the urban landscape, uncovering the hidden world of birds that thrive amidst the hustle and bustle of city life. From the majestic falcons soaring above skyscrapers to the tiny sparrows nesting in the cracks of old buildings, this captivating book introduces fifty unique bird species that have adapted to urban environments in surprising ways. Through vivid descriptions and stunning photographs, the authors shed light on the fascinating behaviors, habitats, and challenges faced by these feathered inhabitants, offering a fresh perspective on the coexistence of nature and civilization.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Photography

Themes: Urban wildlife, Adaptation, Biodiversity
