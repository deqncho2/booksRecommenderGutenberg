Synopsis: In 'American Lutheranism, Volume 2: The United Lutheran Church (General Synod, General Council, United Synod in the South)', Friedrich Bente delves into the fascinating history of the United Lutheran Church, exploring the intricate dynamics between the General Synod, General Council, and United Synod in the South. From the early struggles for unity to the challenges faced during the Civil War and Reconstruction era, Bente uncovers the complex journey of these Lutheran bodies as they navigate theological differences, regional tensions, and societal changes. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Bente paints a vivid picture of the United Lutheran Church's formation, growth, and impact on American religious history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious history

Themes: Denominational unity, Theological diversity, Regionalism, Societal change
