Synopsis: In 'Myths of the Cherokee: Extract from the Nineteenth Annual Report of the Bureau of American Ethnology,' James Mooney delves into the rich and captivating world of Cherokee mythology. Through a collection of enchanting tales passed down through generations, Mooney explores the origins of the Cherokee people, their relationship with nature, and the profound wisdom embedded within their myths. From the creation of the world to the adventures of legendary heroes, these myths offer a glimpse into the spiritual beliefs and cultural heritage of the Cherokee people.

Genres: Mythology, Folklore, Anthropology

Themes: Cultural heritage, Spiritual beliefs, Nature, Heroism
