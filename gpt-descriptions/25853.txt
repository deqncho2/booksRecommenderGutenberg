Synopsis: 
In 'The Letters of Charles Dickens. Vol. 2, 1857-1870', readers are granted a fascinating glimpse into the personal and professional life of one of the most celebrated authors in history. This collection of letters showcases the wit, charm, and brilliance of Charles Dickens as he navigates the literary world, grapples with personal challenges, and engages in passionate debates on social issues of the time. From his travels across Europe to his enduring friendships with fellow writers, these letters offer a captivating portrait of a literary genius and the world that shaped him.

Genres: 
Biography, Autobiography, Correspondence

Themes: 
Literary life, Social issues, Personal struggles
