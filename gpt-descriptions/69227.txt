Synopsis: In "Sir John Everett Millais," A. L. Baldry delves into the life and artistry of one of the most celebrated British painters of the 19th century. From his early days as a prodigious child artist to his rise as a prominent figure in the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood, Millais's journey is filled with triumphs, controversies, and personal struggles. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, Baldry paints a compelling portrait of a man whose artistic vision challenged conventions and left an indelible mark on the art world.

Genres: Biography, Art History

Themes: Artistic rebellion, Creative process, Society and culture
