Synopsis: In 'A Plain and Easie Method for Preserving (by God's Blessing) Those That Are Well from the Infection of the Plague, or Any Contagious Distemper, in City, Camp, Fleet, Etc., and for Curing Such as Are Infected with It,' Thomas Willis presents a practical guide to safeguarding oneself from the deadly plague and other contagious diseases. Through a combination of medical knowledge and divine intervention, Willis offers a comprehensive approach to prevention and treatment, providing hope in the face of a devastating epidemic.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical literature, Self-help

Themes: Disease prevention, Public health, Divine intervention
