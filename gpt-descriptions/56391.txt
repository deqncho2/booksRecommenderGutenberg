Synopsis: 
Step back in time to the bustling streets of 17th century London with 'The Little London Directory of 1677: The oldest printed list of the merchants and bankers of London'. This fascinating historical document takes readers on a journey through the city's vibrant trade and commerce, providing a glimpse into the lives of the merchants and bankers who shaped London's financial landscape. From the opulent halls of the Royal Exchange to the humble shops lining the narrow alleyways, this directory offers a captivating snapshot of a bygone era. Discover the secrets of London's thriving economy, uncover hidden rivalries, and witness the birth of modern finance in this meticulously preserved piece of history.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Reference

Themes: London history, trade and commerce, social hierarchy, financial institutions
