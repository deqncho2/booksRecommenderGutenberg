Synopsis: 

In 'Bad Drains; and How to Test Them', author R. Harris Reeves delves into the murky world of sewers, drains, and sanitary fittings, uncovering the hidden dangers lurking beneath our feet. With a focus on the origin and transmission of zymotic disease, Reeves takes readers on a fascinating journey through the underbelly of urban infrastructure, revealing the alarming consequences of neglected drainage systems. Through meticulous research and practical advice, this book offers invaluable insights into the testing and ventilation of drains, shedding light on a topic often overlooked but of utmost importance to public health.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Health

Themes: Public health, Sanitation, Disease prevention
