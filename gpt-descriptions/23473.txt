Synopsis: 

In 'Lectures on Land Warfare', an anonymous author delves into the intricate art of warfare, providing a comprehensive tactical manual specifically designed for infantry officers. Through a captivating exploration of historical battles, ranging from the legendary Battle of Thermopylae in 480 B.C. to the Battle of the Sambre in November 1918, the author skillfully examines the underlying principles that shape the strategies and tactics employed in warfare. With vivid illustrations and examples drawn from military history, this manual offers invaluable insights into the complexities of land warfare, making it an indispensable resource for military enthusiasts and officers alike.

Genres: Military history, Tactical manual, Non-fiction

Themes: Warfare strategies, Tactical principles, Historical analysis
