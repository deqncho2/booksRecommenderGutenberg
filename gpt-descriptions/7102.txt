Synopsis: In Chapters 11 to 15 of "Adventures of Huckleberry Finn" by Mark Twain, Huck and Jim continue their journey down the Mississippi River, encountering a variety of colorful characters and facing unexpected challenges. From a clever disguise to a daring escape, Huck's resourcefulness and Jim's unwavering determination are put to the test. As they navigate through dangerous situations and make difficult decisions, the bond between the two grows stronger, revealing the complexities of friendship and the pursuit of freedom.

Genres: Adventure, Coming-of-age, Satire

Themes: Friendship, Freedom, Morality, Racism
