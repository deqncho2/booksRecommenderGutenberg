Synopsis: 

In 'Wars & Treaties, 1815 to 1914', Arthur Ponsonby, Baron takes readers on a captivating journey through a century of conflict and diplomacy. From the aftermath of the Napoleonic Wars to the eve of World War I, this meticulously researched book delves into the intricate web of international relations, exploring the causes, consequences, and intricacies of the major wars and treaties that shaped the world during this period. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, Ponsonby sheds light on the political maneuverings, power struggles, and diplomatic negotiations that defined this era, offering a fresh perspective on the events that led to the outbreak of the Great War. 

Genres: History, Politics, International Relations

Themes: War and Conflict, Diplomacy, Power and Politics
