Synopsis: In 'The Miracle of the Great St. Nicolas: 1920' by France Anatole, a small village in France is plagued by poverty and despair after the devastation of World War I. The villagers, desperate for hope, turn to their beloved patron saint, St. Nicolas, for a miracle. As the story unfolds, unexpected events and mysterious occurrences begin to unfold, leading the villagers to question their faith and the power of miracles. With vivid descriptions and a touch of magical realism, this captivating tale explores the transformative power of belief and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Magical Realism, Religious Fiction

Themes: Faith, Hope, Resilience, Miracles
