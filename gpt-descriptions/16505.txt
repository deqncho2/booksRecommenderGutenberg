Synopsis: In 'The Voice of the People' by Ellen Anderson Gholson Glasgow, a young woman named Laura embarks on a journey of self-discovery and rebellion against societal expectations in the post-Civil War South. As she navigates the complexities of love, family, and her own desires, Laura finds herself torn between tradition and her own voice. Set against the backdrop of a changing society, this captivating novel explores the power of individuality and the struggle for personal freedom.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Romance

Themes: Identity, Gender Roles, Social Change
