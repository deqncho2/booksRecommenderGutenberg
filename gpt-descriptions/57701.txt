Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The History of Duelling', author J. G. Millingen delves deeper into the captivating world of duels, exploring the historical context, infamous duellists, and the intricate codes of honor that governed these deadly encounters. From the aristocratic duels of Europe to the duelling culture in America, Millingen presents a fascinating account of this controversial practice, filled with gripping anecdotes and vivid descriptions. As the author uncovers the motivations behind duelling and its impact on society, readers are taken on a thrilling journey through the dark and dangerous realm of honor-bound combat.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Honor, Society, Violence, Tradition
