Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume VI, Kansas Narratives', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a collection of powerful and poignant firsthand accounts from former slaves in Kansas. These narratives offer a unique perspective on the experiences of enslaved individuals, their struggles for freedom, and their resilience in the face of adversity. From tales of escape and resistance to stories of family bonds and community support, this volume sheds light on the often overlooked history of slavery in Kansas and the remarkable individuals who fought for their freedom.

Genres:
Historical Non-fiction, Oral History, African American Studies

Themes:
Slavery, Freedom, Resistance, Family, Community
