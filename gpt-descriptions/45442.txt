Synopsis: In "A Camera Actress in the Wilds of Togoland," Meg Gehrts takes readers on a captivating journey through the West African forests as she immerses herself in the world of cinematography. As she collects films depicting native life and assumes the role of the white woman in Anglo-African cinematograph dramas, Gehrts encounters thrilling adventures, makes intriguing observations, and gains invaluable experiences. This memoir offers a unique perspective on the challenges and triumphs of a camera actress in a foreign land, providing a fascinating glimpse into the world of early filmmaking and the complexities of cultural representation.

Genres: Memoir, Adventure, Cultural Exploration

Themes: Filmmaking, Cultural Identity, Exploration of African Landscapes
