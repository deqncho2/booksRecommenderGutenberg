Synopsis: 

In "Through lands that were dark: Being a record of a year's missionary journey in Africa and Madagascar," F. H. Hawkins takes readers on an extraordinary adventure through the heart of Africa and Madagascar. This captivating memoir follows the author's experiences as a missionary, providing a vivid and immersive account of the challenges, triumphs, and encounters with diverse cultures and landscapes. From navigating treacherous terrains to forging deep connections with local communities, Hawkins' journey is filled with unexpected twists and turns that will keep readers on the edge of their seats. As the author delves into the complexities of missionary work and the transformative power of faith, readers are invited to explore the depths of human resilience and the profound impact of cultural exchange.

Genres: Memoir, Travelogue, Adventure

Themes: Missionary work, Cultural exchange, Faith, Resilience
