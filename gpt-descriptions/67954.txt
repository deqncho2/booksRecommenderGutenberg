Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The Life of Clara Barton, Founder of the American Red Cross,' author William E. Barton delves deeper into the remarkable journey of Clara Barton, a woman who defied societal norms and dedicated her life to humanitarian work. This captivating biography uncovers the untold stories of Barton's tireless efforts during the Civil War, her struggles to establish the American Red Cross, and her unwavering commitment to providing aid to those in need. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by Barton's indomitable spirit, her unwavering determination, and her unwavering belief in the power of compassion. 

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Humanitarianism, Women's Empowerment, Resilience
