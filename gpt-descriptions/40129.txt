Synopsis: In 'Missy: A Novel' by Miriam Coles Harris, readers are introduced to Missy, a young woman who finds herself caught between societal expectations and her own desires. Set in the late 19th century, Missy navigates the challenges of love, family, and personal growth in a world that often restricts women's choices. As she grapples with her own identity and the expectations placed upon her, Missy embarks on a journey of self-discovery that will test her resilience and challenge the norms of her time.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Romance

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, self-discovery, love and relationships
