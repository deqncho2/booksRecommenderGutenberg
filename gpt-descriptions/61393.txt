Synopsis: In "The Singing Church: The Hymns It Wrote and Sang," Edmund S. Lorenz takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of hymns and their profound impact on religious worship. From the humble beginnings of early Christian chants to the powerful hymns of the Reformation and beyond, Lorenz explores the stories behind these timeless songs and the individuals who composed them. Through vivid anecdotes and insightful analysis, he reveals the deep spiritual significance and cultural influence of hymns, shedding light on their ability to unite communities, inspire faith, and express the deepest emotions of the human soul. This engaging exploration of the hymns that have shaped the Christian tradition will leave readers with a newfound appreciation for the power of music in worship.

Genres: Non-fiction, Music, History

Themes: Worship, Spirituality, Cultural Influence
