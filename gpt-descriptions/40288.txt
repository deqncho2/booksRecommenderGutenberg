Synopsis: 
In 'The Modes of Ancient Greek Music', Monro delves into the fascinating world of ancient Greek music, uncovering its secrets and exploring its profound influence on the culture and society of the time. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Monro takes readers on a journey through the various modes of Greek music, shedding light on their unique characteristics and the emotions they evoked. From the hauntingly beautiful melodies of the Dorian mode to the lively and celebratory rhythms of the Lydian mode, this book offers a captivating exploration of the musical landscape of ancient Greece.

Genres: Non-fiction, Musicology, History

Themes: Ancient Greek culture, Music theory, Emotional expression
