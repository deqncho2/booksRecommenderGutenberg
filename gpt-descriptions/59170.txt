Synopsis: 

In 'Tea, Its Mystery and History' by Samuel Phillips Day, embark on a captivating journey through time and culture as the author unravels the enigmatic origins and fascinating evolution of tea. From ancient legends and mythical tales to the intricate rituals and customs surrounding this beloved beverage, Day delves into the depths of tea's rich history, leaving readers spellbound by its secrets and allure. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be transported to distant lands, encountering influential figures, and witnessing the profound impact that tea has had on societies throughout the ages. Prepare to be captivated by the mysteries and complexities that lie within a simple cup of tea.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Origins and evolution, Cultural significance, Rituals and customs
