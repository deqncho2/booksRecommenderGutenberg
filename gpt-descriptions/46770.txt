Synopsis: 
In "Neath Verdun, August-October, 1914," Maurice Genevoix takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of soldiers during the early months of World War I. Through vivid and haunting descriptions, the author transports readers to the frontlines near Verdun, where the horrors of war unfold. From the chaos and confusion of battle to the camaraderie and resilience of the soldiers, Genevoix paints a poignant and unforgettable portrait of the human spirit in the face of unimaginable adversity.

Genres: Historical fiction, War literature, Memoir

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, the resilience of the human spirit, the bonds of camaraderie in times of crisis, the brutality and futility of war.
