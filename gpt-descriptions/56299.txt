Synopsis: 
'From Convent to Conflict; Or, A Nun's Account of the Invasion of Belgium' by Marie Antoine, Sister is a gripping memoir that takes readers on a harrowing journey through the invasion of Belgium during World War I. The story follows Sister Marie Antoine, a nun who finds herself caught in the midst of the chaos and destruction as her peaceful convent is transformed into a battleground. Through her eyes, we witness the horrors of war, the resilience of the human spirit, and the power of faith in the face of adversity. As Sister Marie Antoine navigates the treacherous landscape of war, she must confront her own fears and make unimaginable sacrifices to protect those she holds dear.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Fiction, War

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and communities, Faith and spirituality, Sacrifice and resilience
