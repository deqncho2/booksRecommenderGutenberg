Synopsis: In "Hospital Transports: A memoir of the Embarkation of the Sick and Wounded from the Peninsula of Virginia in the Summer of 1862," Frederick Law Olmsted takes readers on a poignant journey through the harrowing experiences of transporting the sick and wounded during the American Civil War. Through vivid and heartfelt storytelling, Olmsted provides a unique perspective on the challenges faced by medical personnel and the resilience of those affected by the ravages of war.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: War and its impact on individuals, Human resilience and compassion
