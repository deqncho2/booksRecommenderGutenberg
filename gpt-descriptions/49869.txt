Synopsis: 

"The Ways of a Worker of a Century Ago: as Shown by the Diary of Joseph Lye, Shoemaker" takes readers on a captivating journey into the life of Joseph Lye, a shoemaker living a century ago. Through the pages of his diary, we are transported to a time when craftsmanship and hard work were the backbone of society. As we delve into Joseph's daily struggles, triumphs, and encounters, we witness the challenges and joys of a bygone era. From the bustling streets of the city to the quiet solitude of his workshop, Joseph's diary offers a unique perspective on the life of a worker in the past, revealing the intricacies of his trade and the profound impact it had on his identity and sense of purpose.

Genres: Historical fiction, Memoir

Themes: Work ethic, Craftsmanship, Social change
