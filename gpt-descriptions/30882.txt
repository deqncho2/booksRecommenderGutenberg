Synopsis: In "God and Mr. Wells: A Critical Examination of 'God the Invisible King'," Archer, William takes on the task of dissecting H.G. Wells' controversial book that challenges traditional religious beliefs. With meticulous analysis and thought-provoking arguments, Archer delves into the depths of Wells' ideas, questioning the very nature of God and the role of religion in society. As the pages turn, readers will find themselves engrossed in a captivating exploration of faith, reason, and the boundaries of human understanding.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Religious Studies

Themes: Religion, Atheism, Philosophy of God, Humanism
