Synopsis: 

In "History of the Impeachment of Andrew Johnson, President of the United States, by the House of Representatives, and his trial by the Senate for high crimes and misdemeanors in office, 1868," Edmund G. Ross delves into the gripping and controversial events surrounding the impeachment of President Andrew Johnson. With meticulous detail and a captivating narrative, Ross explores the political tensions, power struggles, and legal battles that unfolded during this pivotal moment in American history. As the nation grapples with the question of whether a president can be removed from office, readers will be drawn into the dramatic twists and turns of this extraordinary impeachment trial.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Legal drama

Themes: Abuse of power, Political division, Constitutional crisis
