Synopsis: In "Atalanta in Calydon," Algernon Charles Swinburne takes us on a captivating journey to the ancient Greek city of Calydon, where a dramatic and tragic event unfolds. The story revolves around the hunt of the Calydonian boar, a mythical creature sent by the gods as a punishment. As the city's greatest heroes and heroines gather to face this formidable challenge, their actions and desires intertwine, leading to unexpected consequences. Swinburne's poetic masterpiece explores the complexities of human nature, the clash between fate and free will, and the timeless struggle between love and pride.

Genres: Tragedy, Poetry

Themes: Fate vs. Free Will, Love and Desire, Pride and Hubris
