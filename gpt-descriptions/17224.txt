Synopsis: In 'Chance and Luck', Richard A. Proctor takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of probability and randomness. From the roll of dice to the shuffling of cards, Proctor explores the intricate balance between chance and luck that shapes our lives in unexpected ways. Through engaging anecdotes and thought-provoking examples, he delves into the mysteries of probability, revealing the hidden patterns and surprising outcomes that lie beneath the surface of our everyday experiences. This enlightening exploration of chance and luck will leave readers questioning the role of fate in their own lives and pondering the delicate balance between randomness and destiny.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Mathematics

Themes: Probability, Randomness, Fate, Patterns
