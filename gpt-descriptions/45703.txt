Synopsis:
In "Water Supply: the Present Practice of Sinking and Boring Wells: With Geological Considerations and Examples of Wells Executed," Ernest Spon takes readers on a fascinating journey into the world of water supply. Through a combination of geological insights and real-life examples, Spon explores the art and science of sinking and boring wells. From the challenges faced by engineers to the innovative techniques employed, this book offers a captivating exploration of the methods used to tap into the Earth's hidden water resources. Whether you are a curious reader or a professional in the field, Spon's work will leave you with a deeper understanding of the complex process behind water supply.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, Geology

Themes: Water supply, Engineering challenges, Geological considerations
