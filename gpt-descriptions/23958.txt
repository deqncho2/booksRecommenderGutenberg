Synopsis: In 'The Romance of Words (4th ed.)', Ernest Weekley takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history and evolution of the English language. From its humble beginnings to its global dominance, Weekley explores the intricate web of influences that have shaped English into the rich and diverse language it is today. With wit and charm, he uncovers the surprising origins of everyday words and phrases, revealing the hidden stories behind their usage. From the intriguing etymology of "serendipity" to the unexpected connections between English and other languages, this book is a delightful exploration of the romance and magic of words.

Genres: Non-fiction, Linguistics, History

Themes: Language evolution, Etymology, Cultural influences
