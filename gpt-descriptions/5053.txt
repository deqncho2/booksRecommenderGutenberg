Synopsis: In "Creditors," Strindberg delves into the complex dynamics of a love triangle, exploring the manipulative power of deception and the destructive consequences of jealousy. As the characters navigate their relationships, hidden motives and psychological games come to light, leading to a gripping climax that challenges the boundaries of trust and loyalty. In "Pariah," Strindberg presents a haunting tale of isolation and societal rejection, as a young woman finds herself trapped in a world that refuses to accept her. Through vivid imagery and intense emotions, the play delves into themes of identity, alienation, and the struggle for self-acceptance.

Genres: Drama, Psychological Thriller

Themes: Love and betrayal, Deception, Jealousy, Identity, Alienation, Self-acceptance
