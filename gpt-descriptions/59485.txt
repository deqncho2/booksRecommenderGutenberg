Synopsis: 

In 'Wintering Bees in Cellars', Phillips and Demuth take readers on a fascinating journey into the world of beekeeping during the winter months. Through vivid descriptions and practical advice, they explore the intricate process of preparing and caring for bees as they hibernate in cellars, ensuring their survival until spring. From the careful selection of healthy colonies to the meticulous maintenance of temperature and humidity, the authors delve into the art and science of wintering bees, revealing the dedication and expertise required to protect these vital pollinators.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Agriculture

Themes: Beekeeping, Winter Survival, Environmental Stewardship
