Synopsis: 

'American Masters of Sculpture: Being Brief Appreciations of Some American Sculptors and of Some Phases of Sculpture in America' takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of American sculpture, offering insightful and concise appreciations of various renowned American sculptors and exploring different aspects of sculpture in America. From the iconic works of Augustus Saint-Gaudens to the innovative creations of Isamu Noguchi, this book delves into the lives, inspirations, and artistic techniques of these master sculptors, providing a fascinating glimpse into the evolution of sculpture in America. With vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, Charles H. Caffin invites readers to discover the beauty, significance, and impact of American sculpture.

Genres: Art, Biography, History

Themes: Artistic expression, American identity, Evolution of sculpture
