Synopsis: 

In 'Manual for Noncommissioned Officers and Privates of Cavalry of the Army: of the United States 1917. To be also used by Engineer Companies (Mounted) for Cavalry Instruction and Training', the United States War Department presents a comprehensive guide to the essential skills and knowledge required for cavalry soldiers during World War I. This manual covers a wide range of topics, including horsemanship, weapons handling, scouting, and combat tactics. It provides detailed instructions and illustrations to ensure that both noncommissioned officers and privates are well-prepared for the challenges they may face on the battlefield. 

Genres: Military, Instructional, Historical

Themes: Military training, Cavalry tactics, Soldier preparedness
