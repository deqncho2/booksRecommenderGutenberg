Synopsis: 

In 'Women as World Builders: Studies in Modern Feminism', Dell, Floyd takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives and achievements of remarkable women who have shaped the world we live in today. From groundbreaking scientists and influential writers to political activists and social reformers, this book delves into the untold stories of these extraordinary women and their contributions to society. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Dell, Floyd sheds light on the struggles, triumphs, and lasting legacies of these trailblazers, inspiring readers to reconsider the role of women in history and the potential for change.

Genres: Non-fiction, Feminist literature, Biography

Themes: Feminism, Gender equality, Women's empowerment, Historical significance
