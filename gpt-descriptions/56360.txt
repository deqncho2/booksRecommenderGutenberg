Synopsis: In "How the Nations Waged War," J.M. Kennedy takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of World War I, offering a comprehensive account of the strategies, battles, and political maneuvers that shaped the conflict. From the trenches of the Western Front to the naval battles in the Atlantic, Kennedy delves into the intricacies of warfare, exploring the tactics employed by each nation involved. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, this companion volume to "How the War Began" sheds light on the lesser-known aspects of the war, revealing the immense sacrifices made by soldiers and civilians alike. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, World War I

Themes: War strategies, Battle tactics, Political maneuvering, Sacrifice, Global conflict
