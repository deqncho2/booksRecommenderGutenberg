Synopsis: 

'Hyde Park from Domesday-book to Date' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of one of London's most iconic landmarks. From its humble beginnings as a hunting ground for medieval kings to its transformation into a beloved public park, author John Ashton delves into the fascinating stories and secrets that have shaped Hyde Park over the centuries. With vivid descriptions and intriguing anecdotes, this book unveils the hidden treasures and notable events that have unfolded within its sprawling grounds. From royal scandals to political rallies, from grand celebrations to quiet moments of reflection, 'Hyde Park from Domesday-book to Date' offers a captivating exploration of a place that has witnessed the ebb and flow of history.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Urban development, Royal history, Public spaces, Cultural heritage
