Synopsis: 

In "South: The Story of Shackleton's Last Expedition, 1914-1917," Sir Ernest Shackleton takes readers on a thrilling and harrowing journey to the Antarctic. Set against the backdrop of World War I, Shackleton and his crew embark on a daring expedition to be the first to cross the continent. However, their ship, the Endurance, becomes trapped in the ice, forcing them to face unimaginable challenges and tests of endurance. As they battle the elements and struggle for survival, Shackleton's leadership and unwavering determination become the driving force behind their fight against nature's unforgiving wrath.

Genres: Adventure, Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Exploration, Perseverance, Leadership, Survival
