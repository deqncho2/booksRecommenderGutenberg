Synopsis: In "The Life and Genius of Nathaniel Hawthorne," Frank Preston Stearns delves into the captivating life and literary brilliance of one of America's most renowned authors. From Hawthorne's early struggles and personal tragedies to his profound impact on American literature, Stearns paints a vivid portrait of a complex and enigmatic figure. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, this biography offers a fascinating exploration of the man behind the timeless tales, shedding light on the inspirations, motivations, and inner workings of Hawthorne's genius.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Artistic creativity, the human condition, the power of imagination, the role of literature in society
