Synopsis: In "The Stolen Bacillus and Other Incidents," H.G. Wells takes readers on a thrilling journey through a collection of short stories that explore the unexpected consequences of scientific experiments and human curiosity. From a stolen vial of deadly bacteria to a mysterious substance that grants invisibility, each incident presents a unique and thought-provoking scenario that will keep readers on the edge of their seats. As the stories unfold, Wells delves into the moral and ethical implications of scientific advancements, raising questions about the limits of human knowledge and the potential dangers that lie within our own creations.

Genres: Science Fiction, Mystery, Thriller

Themes: Scientific curiosity, Morality, Consequences of human actions
