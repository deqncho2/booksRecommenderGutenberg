Synopsis: In the second volume of "The History of the Confederate War, Its Causes and Its Conduct," George Cary Eggleston continues his captivating narrative of the American Civil War, delving into the critical events and decisions that shaped the Confederacy. From the early battles to the struggles of the Confederate government, Eggleston provides a comprehensive account of the war's progression, offering both a historical and critical perspective. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, this volume explores the complexities of the Confederate cause and the challenges faced by its leaders, shedding light on the motivations and consequences of their actions.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Civil War history

Themes: Causes and consequences of the American Civil War, Leadership and decision-making in times of crisis, Confederate perspective on the war, Analysis of military strategies and tactics
