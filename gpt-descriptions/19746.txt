Synopsis: In 'The Colonel's Dream' by Charles W. Chesnutt, a retired Confederate colonel named French seeks to rebuild his life in the post-Civil War South. As he navigates the complexities of race, class, and power, French becomes entangled in a web of political intrigue and personal ambition. With vivid characters and a thought-provoking narrative, this novel explores the challenges of reconciliation and the pursuit of the American Dream in a divided society.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Fiction, Social Commentary

Themes: Race Relations, Social Inequality, Political Corruption, Identity, Redemption
