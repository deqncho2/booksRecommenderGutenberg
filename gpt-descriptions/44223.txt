Synopsis: 
In 'Back Home: Being the Narrative of Judge Priest and His People', we are transported to a small Southern town where Judge Priest, a beloved and eccentric figure, presides over the community with wisdom and humor. Through a series of interconnected stories, we meet a colorful cast of characters, including a mischievous young boy, a spirited widow, and a charming con artist. As the narrative unfolds, we witness the joys, sorrows, and everyday struggles of these ordinary yet extraordinary people, all while exploring the complexities of life in the post-Civil War South. With its heartwarming tales and rich Southern charm, this book offers a delightful journey into a bygone era.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Southern Literature
Themes: Community, Justice, Southern culture, Post-Civil War era
