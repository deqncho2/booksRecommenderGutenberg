Synopsis: In "Merry Tales," Mark Twain takes readers on a delightful journey through a collection of humorous and witty short stories. From a mischievous boy's clever pranks to a comical case of mistaken identity, each tale is filled with Twain's signature wit and satirical observations. With his unique storytelling style, Twain invites readers to laugh, ponder, and reflect on the absurdities of human nature and the quirks of society. 

Genres: Humor, Satire, Short Stories

Themes: Human nature, Society, Irony
