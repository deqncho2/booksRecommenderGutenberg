Synopsis: 
"The Paths of Inland Commerce; A Chronicle of Trail, Road, and Waterway" takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of transportation in America. From the early days of Native American trails to the development of bustling trade routes, this chronicle explores the fascinating evolution of inland commerce. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Archer Butler Hulbert uncovers the untold stories of the pioneers, traders, and adventurers who shaped the nation's transportation network. Discover the challenges they faced, the triumphs they achieved, and the lasting impact they left on American society.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travel literature, Adventure

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Economic development, Cultural exchange
