Synopsis: 
'Letters from Mesopotamia in 1915 and January, 1916: From Robert Palmer, who was killed in the Battle of Um El Hannah, June 21, 1916, aged 27 years' is a collection of poignant letters written by Robert Stafford Arthur Palmer during his time serving in Mesopotamia during World War I. These letters provide a unique and personal insight into the experiences, emotions, and challenges faced by soldiers on the front lines. Through his vivid descriptions and heartfelt words, Palmer takes the reader on a journey through the harsh realities of war, the camaraderie among soldiers, and the longing for home and loved ones. As the letters progress, the reader becomes deeply invested in Palmer's story, eagerly anticipating each new correspondence and hoping for his safe return.

Genres: 
Historical Non-fiction, War Literature, Memoir

Themes: 
War and its impact on individuals, Sacrifice and bravery, Longing for home and loved ones
