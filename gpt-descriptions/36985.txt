Synopsis: In 'The eBook is 40 (1971-2011)' by Marie Lebert, embark on a captivating journey through the evolution of the eBook, from its humble beginnings to its revolutionary impact on the world of literature. This meticulously researched and engagingly written account explores the technological advancements, cultural shifts, and the tireless efforts of pioneers that shaped the eBook industry. Discover the untold stories, controversies, and triumphs that have defined the digital reading experience, making this book a must-read for anyone curious about the past, present, and future of books.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Technology

Themes: Technological innovation, Cultural transformation, Publishing industry, Digital revolution
