Synopsis: In 'The Short Constitution', Russell and Wade take readers on a captivating journey through the intricate world of constitutional law. With a unique blend of historical anecdotes, legal analysis, and thought-provoking insights, this book unravels the complexities of the United States Constitution in a concise and engaging manner. From the Founding Fathers' original intentions to the modern-day interpretations, the authors shed light on the pivotal moments that have shaped the nation's legal framework. As readers delve into the pages of 'The Short Constitution', they will discover a wealth of knowledge that challenges their understanding of the Constitution and its impact on society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Law, History

Themes: Constitutional law, Legal interpretation, Historical context
