Synopsis: In "The Early Life of Abraham Lincoln," Ida M. Tarbell delves into the lesser-known aspects of one of America's most iconic figures. Through a collection of unpublished documents and reminiscences from Lincoln's early friends, Tarbell paints a vivid picture of the formative years of the future president. From his humble beginnings in a log cabin to his pursuit of education and his early experiences as a lawyer, this book uncovers the untold stories that shaped Lincoln's character and set him on the path to greatness. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Tarbell offers a fresh perspective on the man who would become one of the most influential leaders in American history.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Early life and upbringing, Education and self-improvement, Determination and resilience
