Synopsis: In 'The Trial of Henry Hetherington, on an Indictment for Blasphemy', Henry Hetherington finds himself in a courtroom, facing charges of blasphemy. As the trial unfolds, Hetherington's unyielding defense challenges societal norms and raises profound questions about freedom of speech, religious tolerance, and the power of the written word. With the courtroom as the stage, this gripping account delves into the clash between personal beliefs and the law, leaving readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Legal drama, Historical fiction

Themes: Freedom of speech, Religious tolerance, Power dynamics
