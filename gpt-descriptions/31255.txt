Synopsis: In "The Educated Negro and His Mission," W.S. Scarborough delves into the complex journey of the educated African American in post-Civil War America. Through personal anecdotes and insightful analysis, Scarborough explores the challenges and responsibilities faced by the educated Negro, shedding light on the importance of education, racial uplift, and the pursuit of equality. This thought-provoking work offers a unique perspective on the struggles and aspirations of African Americans during a pivotal period in history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Autobiography, Social Commentary

Themes: Education, Racial Uplift, Equality, Identity, Social Justice
