Synopsis: In "Huxley and education: Address at the Opening of the College Year, Columbia University, September 28, 1910," Henry Fairfield Osborn delves into the profound impact of education on society, drawing inspiration from the renowned biologist Thomas Huxley. Through thought-provoking insights and captivating anecdotes, Osborn explores the transformative power of education and its potential to shape the future. This address serves as a compelling reminder of the importance of knowledge and learning in the pursuit of progress and enlightenment.

Genres: Non-fiction, Education, Speech

Themes: Education, Progress, Enlightenment
