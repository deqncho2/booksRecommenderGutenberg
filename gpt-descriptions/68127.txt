Synopsis: In "Stories from the Iliad; Or, the siege of Troy" by Jean Lang, readers are transported to the epic world of ancient Greece, where gods and mortals clash in a legendary battle for honor and glory. This captivating retelling of the Iliad brings to life the heroic deeds of Achilles, the cunning strategies of Odysseus, and the tragic fate of Hector. From the grandeur of Mount Olympus to the blood-soaked fields of Troy, this book immerses readers in a tale of love, war, and the timeless struggle between destiny and free will.

Genres: Epic, Mythology, Historical Fiction

Themes: Heroism, Fate vs. Free Will, Honor, Love and Loss
