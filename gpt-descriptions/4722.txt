Synopsis: In "An Essay Towards a New Theory of Vision," George Berkeley takes readers on a captivating journey into the nature of perception and challenges our understanding of reality. Through thought-provoking arguments and intriguing examples, Berkeley explores the idea that our perception of the world is not based on physical objects but rather on our own mental experiences. As he delves into the intricacies of vision, Berkeley presents a revolutionary theory that will make readers question the very nature of their own senses.

Genres: Philosophy, Epistemology

Themes: Perception, Idealism, Empiricism
