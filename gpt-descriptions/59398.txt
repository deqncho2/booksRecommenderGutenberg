Synopsis: 

In 'Birds of the National Parks in Hawaii', author William W. Dunmire takes readers on a captivating journey through the lush landscapes of Hawaii's national parks, unveiling the vibrant and diverse avian inhabitants that call these parks home. From the majestic I'iwi with its striking red plumage to the elusive Pueo owl, Dunmire's vivid descriptions and stunning photographs bring these feathered creatures to life, showcasing their unique behaviors, habitats, and the challenges they face in an ever-changing environment. As readers delve into the pages of this book, they will not only gain a deeper appreciation for the beauty of Hawaii's avifauna but also discover the crucial role these birds play in the delicate ecosystems of the national parks. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Photography

Themes: Biodiversity, Conservation, Environmentalism
