Synopsis: In 'Diddie, Dumps, and Tot; Or, Plantation Child-Life', Pyrnelle, Louise Clarke takes readers on a captivating journey into the lives of three young siblings growing up on a Southern plantation. Through their adventures and misadventures, the children navigate the complexities of race, family, and friendship, while discovering the power of love and resilience. Set against the backdrop of the post-Civil War era, this heartwarming tale explores the joys and challenges of childhood in a time of great change.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Family Drama

Themes: Race Relations, Identity, Resilience, Love and Friendship
