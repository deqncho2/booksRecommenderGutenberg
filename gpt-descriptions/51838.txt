Synopsis: 
'Personal Reminiscences of the War of 1861-5: In Camp—en Bivouac—on the March—on Picket—on the Skirmish Line—on the Battlefield—and in Prison' is a captivating memoir that takes readers on a gripping journey through the American Civil War. Written by W. H. Morgan, a soldier who experienced the war firsthand, this book offers a unique and intimate perspective on the various aspects of military life during this tumultuous period. From the camaraderie in camp to the adrenaline-filled moments on the battlefield, Morgan's vivid recollections provide a compelling narrative that will leave readers eager to discover the challenges and triumphs he encountered.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Military History

Themes: War experiences, Camaraderie, Survival, Sacrifice
