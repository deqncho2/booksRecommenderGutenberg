Synopsis: 
In "An Account of Two Voyages to New-England, Made During the Years 1638, 1663," Josselyn takes readers on a captivating journey to the early days of New England colonization. Through his vivid descriptions and personal experiences, he paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by the settlers in this new land. From encounters with Native Americans to the harsh realities of survival, Josselyn's account offers a unique perspective on the early history of America.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, travelogue, memoir

Themes: Exploration and colonization, cultural encounters, survival and adaptation
