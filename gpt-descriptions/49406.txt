Synopsis: In "The Dawn of the XIXth Century in England: A social sketch of the times," John Ashton takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and transformative era of 19th-century England. From the bustling streets of London to the opulent estates of the aristocracy, Ashton paints a vivid picture of the social, cultural, and political landscape of the time. Through a series of captivating anecdotes and historical insights, he explores the lives of both the privileged and the working class, shedding light on the stark contrasts and intricate interplay between them. From the rise of industrialization to the emergence of new social movements, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into a pivotal period that shaped the course of English history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Social history, Cultural studies

Themes: Social class, Industrial revolution, Cultural change, Political upheaval
