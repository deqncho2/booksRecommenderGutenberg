Synopsis: In 'Theism and Humanism: Being the Gifford Lectures Delivered at the University of Glasgow, 1914', Arthur James Balfour explores the complex relationship between religion and humanism. Through a series of thought-provoking lectures, Balfour delves into the fundamental questions of existence, the nature of God, and the role of humanity in the universe. With a captivating blend of philosophical inquiry and intellectual discourse, this book challenges readers to reconsider their beliefs and contemplate the intricate interplay between faith and reason.

Genres: Philosophy, Theology, Non-fiction

Themes: Religion, Humanism, Existence, Faith, Reason
