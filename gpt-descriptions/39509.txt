Synopsis: In 'The Black Man, the Father of Civilization, Proven by Biblical History,' James Morris Webb delves into a thought-provoking exploration of biblical history to shed light on the often overlooked contributions of the black man to civilization. Through meticulous research and compelling arguments, Webb challenges conventional narratives and presents a compelling case for the pivotal role played by black individuals in shaping human history. This eye-opening work invites readers to reconsider long-held beliefs and discover a hidden tapestry of cultural and historical significance.

Genres: Historical, Cultural Studies, Religious Studies

Themes: African history, Cultural contributions, Historical revisionism
