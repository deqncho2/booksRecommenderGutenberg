Synopsis: 

"The Evidence in the Case: A Discussion of the Moral Responsibility for the War of 1914, as Disclosed by the Diplomatic Records of England, Germany, Russia" delves into the intricate web of diplomatic records to uncover the truth behind the moral responsibility for the devastating war that shook the world in 1914. James M. Beck presents a thought-provoking analysis that challenges conventional narratives and sheds light on the hidden motivations and actions of the major powers involved. As the evidence unfolds, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the corridors of power, where secrets, alliances, and betrayals intertwine to shape the course of history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political analysis, Diplomatic history

Themes: Moral responsibility, War and its consequences, Diplomacy and international relations
