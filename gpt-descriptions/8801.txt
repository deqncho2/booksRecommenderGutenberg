Synopsis: 
In "Drum-Taps," Walt Whitman takes readers on a poignant and immersive journey through the American Civil War. Through a collection of powerful and evocative poems, Whitman captures the raw emotions, experiences, and sacrifices of soldiers on the battlefield, as well as the impact of the war on the nation as a whole. From the camaraderie and bravery to the devastation and loss, "Drum-Taps" offers a deeply moving exploration of the human spirit in times of conflict.

Genres: Poetry, Historical Literature

Themes: War, Patriotism, Human Resilience, Loss and Grief
