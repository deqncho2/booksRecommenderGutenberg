Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume IV, Georgia Narratives, Part 4', the voices of former slaves from Georgia come alive as they share their personal stories of resilience, struggle, and triumph. These narratives provide a unique and intimate glimpse into the harsh realities of slavery, the complex relationships between slaves and their owners, and the indomitable spirit of those who fought for freedom. From tales of daring escapes to heart-wrenching accounts of family separations, this volume offers a powerful testament to the strength and resilience of the human spirit in the face of unimaginable adversity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Resilience, Family, Identity
