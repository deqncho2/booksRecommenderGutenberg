Synopsis: In 'Fables for the Times' by Henry Wallace Phillips, readers are transported to a world where animals take on human characteristics and engage in thought-provoking conversations. Through a collection of captivating fables, Phillips cleverly uses these anthropomorphic creatures to shed light on the social and political issues of his time. From the cunning fox to the wise owl, each fable presents a unique perspective on the human condition, challenging readers to reflect on their own beliefs and actions. With its imaginative storytelling and insightful allegories, 'Fables for the Times' offers a captivating exploration of society's complexities and the timeless lessons we can learn from the animal kingdom.

Genres: Fiction, Allegory, Satire

Themes: Social and political commentary, Morality, Human nature
