Synopsis: 

In "English Men of Letters: Coleridge," H. D. Traill delves into the life and literary genius of Samuel Taylor Coleridge, one of the most influential poets and thinkers of the Romantic era. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Traill paints a vivid portrait of Coleridge's tumultuous personal life, his profound philosophical ideas, and his enduring contributions to English literature. From his close friendship with William Wordsworth to his struggles with addiction and his quest for spiritual enlightenment, this biography offers a captivating exploration of the complex and enigmatic mind behind some of the most celebrated poems in the English language.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Romanticism, Nature, Imagination, Philosophy, Addiction
