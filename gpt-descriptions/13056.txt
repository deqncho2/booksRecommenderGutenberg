Synopsis: 

In Volume 10 of "The German Classics of the Nineteenth and Twentieth Centuries," readers are taken on a captivating journey through the lives of three influential figures in German history: Prince Otto Von Bismarck, Count Helmuth Von Moltke, and Ferdinand Lassalle. This collection delves into the political, military, and social landscapes of Germany during the 19th and 20th centuries, offering a fascinating exploration of the individuals who shaped the nation's destiny. From Bismarck's cunning strategies to Moltke's military brilliance and Lassalle's revolutionary ideas, this volume presents a rich tapestry of German history that will leave readers enthralled.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Politics, Military Strategy, Social Change
