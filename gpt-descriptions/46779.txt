Synopsis: 

'Pottery and Porcelain, from early times down to the Philadelphia exhibition of 1876' takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of pottery and porcelain. From ancient civilizations to the modern era, Charles Wyllys Elliott delves into the intricate techniques, exquisite designs, and cultural significance of these timeless art forms. With vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, the author explores the evolution of pottery and porcelain, highlighting the masterpieces and innovations that have shaped the industry. From delicate tea sets to grand vases, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of the artistry and craftsmanship that have made pottery and porcelain cherished treasures throughout history.

Genres: Art History, Non-fiction, Historical

Themes: Artistic evolution, Cultural significance, Craftsmanship
