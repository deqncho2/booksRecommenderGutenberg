Synopsis: In "The Ordeal of Mark Twain," Brooks, Van Wyck delves into the fascinating life of one of America's most beloved authors, Mark Twain. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, the author uncovers the trials and tribulations that Twain faced throughout his career, revealing the complex and often contradictory nature of this literary genius. From his early struggles as a journalist to his worldwide fame as the creator of iconic characters like Tom Sawyer and Huckleberry Finn, Twain's journey is a captivating exploration of creativity, fame, and the pursuit of truth.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Creativity, Fame, Identity, Truth
