Synopsis: In "Roosevelt in the Bad Lands" by Hermann Hagedorn, embark on an exhilarating journey through the untamed American West alongside a young Theodore Roosevelt. Leaving behind his privileged life in New York City, Roosevelt ventures into the rugged Badlands of North Dakota, where he faces countless challenges and discovers a profound connection with nature. As he immerses himself in the wild, Roosevelt's character is shaped, and his determination to protect the land and its inhabitants is ignited. This captivating biography delves into the transformative experiences that molded one of America's most influential presidents.

Genres: Biography, Adventure, Historical

Themes: Wilderness exploration, Self-discovery, Conservation
