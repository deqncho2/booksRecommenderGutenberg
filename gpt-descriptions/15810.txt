Synopsis: 
In "The Age of Erasmus: Lectures Delivered in the Universities of Oxford and London," P. S. Allen takes readers on a captivating journey through the Renaissance period, focusing on the influential figure of Erasmus. Through a series of engaging lectures, Allen explores the intellectual, cultural, and political landscape of the time, shedding light on Erasmus' impact and legacy. From his groundbreaking ideas on education and humanism to his clashes with religious authorities, Erasmus emerges as a complex and fascinating figure who challenged the status quo. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Allen brings this pivotal era to life, offering readers a deeper understanding of the Age of Erasmus and its lasting significance.

Genres: History, Biography, Academic

Themes: Renaissance, Humanism, Intellectual Revolution, Religious Reform
