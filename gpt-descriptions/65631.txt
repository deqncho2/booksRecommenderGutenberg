Synopsis: In 'May; vol. I' by Oliphant, Mrs. (Margaret), readers are introduced to the captivating May Churchill, a young woman with a mysterious past and an uncertain future. Set in the Victorian era, May's life takes an unexpected turn when she finds herself caught between the allure of high society and the secrets of her own identity. As she navigates the complexities of love, betrayal, and societal expectations, May's journey becomes a thrilling exploration of self-discovery and the power of personal agency.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Romance

Themes: Identity, Society and Class, Love and Betrayal
