Synopsis: In 'The Coquette, or, The History of Eliza Wharton: A Novel: Founded on Fact', we are introduced to the captivating and enigmatic Eliza Wharton, a young woman who finds herself torn between societal expectations and her own desires. Set in the late 18th century, this epistolary novel takes us on a journey through Eliza's tumultuous relationships, as she navigates the complexities of love, friendship, and the constraints placed upon women in a patriarchal society. As Eliza's choices lead her down a dangerous path, the reader is left questioning the consequences of societal pressures and the pursuit of personal happiness.

Genres: Epistolary novel, Historical fiction, Romance

Themes: Gender roles, Social expectations, Love and desire, Morality, Independence
