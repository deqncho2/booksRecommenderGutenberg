Synopsis: In 'Ragged Homes and How to Mend Them' by Bayly, Mrs. (Mary), we are introduced to a world where poverty and despair seem to be the norm. The story follows the lives of several individuals who find themselves trapped in the cycle of destitution, struggling to make ends meet and maintain their dignity. As they navigate through the harsh realities of their circumstances, they discover unexpected sources of hope and resilience that may just hold the key to mending their broken lives. This poignant tale explores the depths of human resilience and the power of compassion in the face of adversity.

Genres: Fiction, Social Realism, Drama

Themes: Poverty, Resilience, Compassion, Social Inequality
