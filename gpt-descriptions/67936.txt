Synopsis: In 'Working Life of Women in the Seventeenth Century', Alice Clark delves into the fascinating and often overlooked world of women's work during this pivotal period in history. Through meticulous research and compelling anecdotes, Clark uncovers the diverse roles and challenges faced by women from all walks of life, shedding light on their contributions to society and the barriers they encountered. From the bustling streets of London to the rural countryside, this book offers a captivating exploration of the daily lives, aspirations, and struggles of women in the seventeenth century.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Women's studies, Social history

Themes: Gender roles, Labor and work, Social inequality, Women's empowerment
