Synopsis: 
'The Light That Failed' by Rudyard Kipling is a gripping tale that follows the life of Dick Heldar, a talented artist and war correspondent. As he navigates the challenges of love, loss, and the pursuit of artistic perfection, Heldar's journey takes unexpected turns that test his resilience and unravel the depths of his character. Set against the backdrop of war and the vibrant art scene of late 19th-century London, this novel explores the complexities of human relationships and the sacrifices one must make in the pursuit of one's passion.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, War Fiction

Themes: Love and Loss, Artistic Pursuit, Identity and Self-discovery
