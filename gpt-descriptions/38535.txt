Synopsis: In "The Great Illusion: A Study of the Relation of Military Power to National Advantage," Norman Angell challenges conventional wisdom by presenting a thought-provoking analysis of the true nature of military power and its impact on national prosperity. Through a compelling exploration of historical events and economic principles, Angell unveils a surprising perspective that challenges the prevailing belief in the efficacy of military strength. This groundbreaking work offers a fresh and captivating examination of the relationship between military power and national advantage, inviting readers to question long-held assumptions and reconsider the true sources of security and prosperity.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, Economics

Themes: Military power, National advantage, Security, Prosperity, International relations
