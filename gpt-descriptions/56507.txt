Synopsis: In 'The Geographical Distribution of Animals, Volume 2', Alfred Russel Wallace takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse landscapes of our planet, exploring the intricate relationships between living and extinct species. With a keen eye for detail and a passion for unraveling the mysteries of the Earth's past, Wallace delves into the fascinating changes that have shaped the planet's surface over time. From the lush rainforests of the Amazon to the icy expanses of the Arctic, this volume offers a comprehensive study of the distribution of animals across different regions, shedding light on the remarkable adaptations and evolutionary processes that have occurred throughout history. Prepare to be enthralled by Wallace's meticulous observations and thought-provoking insights as he unveils the hidden secrets of our planet's natural history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Science

Themes: Geographical distribution, Evolution, Extinction, Earth's history
