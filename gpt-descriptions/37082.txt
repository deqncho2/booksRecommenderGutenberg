Synopsis: In "A Short View of the Laws Now Subsisting with Respect to the Powers of the East India Company," Pulteney and Stewart delve into the intricate legal framework surrounding the East India Company's ability to borrow money, incur debts, and engage in various mercantile transactions. Exploring the company's powers to purchase goods on credit, freight ships, and more, the authors shed light on the complex regulations governing the company's financial activities. This concise view offers a fascinating glimpse into the legal intricacies of one of the most influential trading companies in history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Legal analysis, Historical

Themes: Corporate power, Financial regulations, Trade and commerce
