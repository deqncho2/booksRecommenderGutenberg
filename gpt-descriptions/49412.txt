Synopsis: In 'The Women of The American Revolution, Vol. 2', Elizabeth Fries Ellet delves into the untold stories of the remarkable women who played pivotal roles during the American Revolution. From spies and soldiers to writers and activists, Ellet uncovers the hidden heroines who fought for freedom and shaped the course of history. Through vivid narratives and meticulous research, this volume sheds light on the often overlooked contributions of these extraordinary women, offering a fresh perspective on the Revolution and the indomitable spirit of the women who fought alongside their male counterparts.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Women's history

Themes: Women's empowerment, Patriotism, Historical legacy
