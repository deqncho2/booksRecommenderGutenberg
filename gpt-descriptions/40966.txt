Synopsis: In 'The Trial of Jesus from a Lawyer's Standpoint, Vol. 1 (of 2): The Hebrew Trial', Walter M. Chandler delves into the legal aspects of the trial of Jesus, examining the events leading up to his crucifixion from a lawyer's perspective. Through meticulous research and analysis, Chandler presents a compelling narrative that sheds new light on the trial, exploring the political and religious motivations behind the prosecution. As the trial unfolds, the reader is taken on a thought-provoking journey, questioning the fairness of the proceedings and the implications for justice. This captivating volume sets the stage for a deeper understanding of one of history's most significant trials.

Genres: Historical, Legal, Religious

Themes: Justice, Religion, Politics
