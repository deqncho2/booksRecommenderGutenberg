Synopsis:
In "Salomé: A Tragedy in One Act," Oscar Wilde presents a captivating and intense tale that unfolds within a single act. Set in the biblical era, the story revolves around the infamous Salomé, stepdaughter of King Herod, who becomes infatuated with the prophet Jokanaan (John the Baptist). As the narrative progresses, a web of desire, power, and manipulation is woven, leading to a shocking climax that will leave readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres:
Tragedy, Drama, Historical Fiction

Themes:
Desire, Power, Manipulation, Forbidden Love
