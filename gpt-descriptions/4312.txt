Synopsis: 
'A Life's Morning' by George Gissing is a captivating novel that follows the life of a young woman named Mary Barfoot. After the sudden death of her parents, Mary is left to navigate the challenges of Victorian society and find her place in the world. As she strives for independence and self-discovery, Mary encounters various obstacles and must make difficult choices that will shape her future. With its richly drawn characters and thought-provoking narrative, this novel explores themes of love, ambition, societal expectations, and the pursuit of personal fulfillment.

Genres: 
Realistic Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Social Commentary

Themes: 
Love and Relationships, Gender Roles, Social Class, Individualism
