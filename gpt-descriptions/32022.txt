Synopsis: In "A Problem in Greek Ethics," John Addington Symonds delves into the controversial topic of sexual inversion, exploring its historical roots and psychological implications. Addressing medical psychologists and jurists, Symonds presents a thought-provoking inquiry into this phenomenon, drawing upon ancient Greek culture and philosophy to shed light on the complexities of human sexuality. Through a meticulous examination of historical texts and personal anecdotes, Symonds challenges societal norms and invites readers to question their own understanding of sexual identity and morality.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Philosophy

Themes: Sexual inversion, Human sexuality, Ancient Greek culture, Morality, Psychological implications
