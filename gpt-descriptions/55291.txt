Synopsis: 
'Graft: A Comedy in Four Acts' by Brighouse, Harold is a delightful tale that follows the misadventures of a group of characters in a small English town. The story revolves around the ambitious and cunning Sam Grundy, who hatches a plan to secure his future by marrying into a wealthy family. However, his scheme takes an unexpected turn when he finds himself entangled in a web of hilarious misunderstandings, mistaken identities, and comical situations. As the plot unfolds, the audience is taken on a rollercoaster ride of laughter and surprises, as the characters navigate their way through love, money, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Comedy, Farce, Satire

Themes: Social climbing, Deception, Love and relationships
