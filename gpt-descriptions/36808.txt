Synopsis: In 'Crying for the Light; Or, Fifty Years Ago', the reader is transported back in time to a small Scottish village in the early 19th century. The story revolves around the lives of two young sisters, Mary and Elsie, who find themselves caught in a web of secrets, forbidden love, and societal expectations. As they navigate the challenges of their time, the sisters must confront their own desires and ambitions, while also grappling with the consequences of their choices. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this historical novel takes readers on a captivating journey through love, loss, and the pursuit of personal freedom.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Love and Desire, Family and Relationships, Societal Expectations, Personal Freedom
