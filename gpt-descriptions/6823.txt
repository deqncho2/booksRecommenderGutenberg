Synopsis: 

'Army Letters from an Officer's Wife, 1871-1888' is a captivating collection of letters written by Frances Marie Antoinette Mack Roe, offering a unique and intimate glimpse into the life of an officer's wife during the late 19th century. Through her heartfelt and vivid correspondence, Roe shares her experiences, challenges, and joys as she navigates the ever-changing landscape of military life, from the hardships of separation and the constant moves to the camaraderie and resilience of the military community. With a keen eye for detail and a genuine voice, Roe's letters paint a vivid picture of the era, capturing the essence of love, duty, and sacrifice.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Military History

Themes: Love and Relationships, Military Life, Women's Experiences, Resilience and Adaptability
