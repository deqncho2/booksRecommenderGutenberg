Synopsis: In "The Great Book-Collectors," Charles Isaac Elton and Mary Augusta Elton take readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of book collecting. From ancient manuscripts to rare first editions, this book explores the lives and passions of the individuals who dedicated their lives to amassing extraordinary libraries. Through vivid anecdotes and historical accounts, the authors shed light on the adventures, rivalries, and obsessions that drove these collectors to acquire and preserve literary treasures. Delving into the stories behind famous libraries and the eccentric characters who built them, "The Great Book-Collectors" offers a captivating glimpse into the captivating world of bibliophiles.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Biography

Themes: Book collecting, Bibliophilia, Obsession, History of books and libraries
