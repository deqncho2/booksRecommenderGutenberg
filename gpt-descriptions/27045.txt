Synopsis: 

In 'Project Gutenberg (1971-2008)' by Marie Lebert, embark on a captivating journey through the evolution of the digital age. This enthralling work delves into the fascinating history of Project Gutenberg, the world's oldest digital library, and its profound impact on the dissemination of knowledge and literature. From its humble beginnings in 1971 to its remarkable growth over the next four decades, witness the tireless efforts of dedicated individuals who revolutionized the way we access and share information. As you delve into the pages of this book, you will uncover the triumphs, challenges, and pivotal moments that shaped the digital revolution, leaving you with a newfound appreciation for the power of technology and its ability to transform the world.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Technology

Themes: Digital revolution, Information dissemination, Technological innovation
