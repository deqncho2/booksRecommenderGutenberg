Synopsis: In "Victorian Literature: Sixty Years of Books and Bookmen," Clement King Shorter takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse literary landscape of the Victorian era. From the works of Charles Dickens to the poetry of Alfred Lord Tennyson, Shorter delves into the lives and creations of the most influential writers of the time. Through anecdotes, analysis, and personal reflections, he paints a vivid picture of the literary world that shaped an entire era, offering a fascinating glimpse into the minds and motivations of these literary giants. 

Genres: Literary criticism, Biography, History

Themes: Victorian literature, Literary influence, Cultural impact
