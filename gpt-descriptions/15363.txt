Synopsis: In 'Carving and Serving' by Mary J. Lincoln, a young woman named Emily finds herself caught between societal expectations and her own desires as she navigates the world of culinary arts in the late 19th century. As she strives to break free from the confines of traditional gender roles, Emily discovers a passion for carving and serving that challenges the norms of her time. With determination and resilience, she embarks on a journey of self-discovery, facing obstacles and triumphs along the way. This captivating tale explores the complexities of identity, ambition, and the pursuit of one's true calling.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Women's Fiction

Themes: Gender Roles, Self-Discovery, Ambition, Identity
