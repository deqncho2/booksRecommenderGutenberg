Synopsis: 

"The Maryland Line in the Confederate States Army" is a captivating historical account that delves into the experiences and contributions of the Maryland soldiers who fought for the Confederacy during the American Civil War. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, author W. W. Goldsborough sheds light on the unique challenges faced by these brave men as they navigated the complexities of loyalty, identity, and sacrifice in a divided nation. From the battlefields of Antietam to the struggles within their own communities, this book offers a compelling narrative that explores the untold stories of the Maryland Line and their significant impact on the war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Loyalty, Identity, Sacrifice, Divided loyalties, Civil War history
