Synopsis: In the fourteenth volume of his renowned diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a captivating journey through the early months of 1661-62. As he navigates the bustling streets of London, Pepys encounters a myriad of intriguing characters and finds himself entangled in a web of political intrigue, scandalous affairs, and personal triumphs. From his encounters with influential figures to his observations of the city's vibrant social scene, Pepys provides a vivid and intimate account of the Restoration era. With his characteristic wit and candidness, he offers a fascinating glimpse into the daily life, ambitions, and desires of a man living in a time of great change and opportunity.

Genres: Diary, Historical Fiction, Memoir

Themes: Restoration era, Politics, Social life, Personal ambition, Scandal
