Synopsis: 

In 'The Story of the Great War, Volume 7: American Food and Ships; Palestine; Italy invaded; Great German Offensive; Americans in Picardy; Americans on the Marne; Foch's Counteroffensive,' the gripping narrative continues to unfold, chronicling the pivotal events of World War I. This volume delves into the crucial role played by the United States, as American food and ships become instrumental in the war effort. The reader is taken on a journey through the invasion of Italy, the Great German Offensive, and the heroic actions of American soldiers in Picardy and on the Marne. Finally, the narrative culminates in Foch's Counteroffensive, a turning point in the war that holds the fate of nations in the balance.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War history, Military history

Themes: World War I, American involvement, Military strategy, International alliances, Heroism, Turning points in history
