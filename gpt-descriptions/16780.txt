Synopsis: 
In "The Declaration of Independence of The United States of America," Thomas Jefferson presents a powerful and impassioned argument for the separation of the American colonies from British rule. Through eloquent prose and a compelling narrative, Jefferson explores the fundamental rights of all individuals and the inherent duty to resist oppression. This seminal document not only serves as a declaration of independence but also lays the groundwork for a new nation built on the principles of liberty, equality, and self-governance.

Genres: Political philosophy, Historical document

Themes: Freedom, Individual rights, Resistance to tyranny
