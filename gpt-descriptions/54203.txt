Synopsis: In 'The Prisoners of Mainz' by Alec Waugh, a group of diverse individuals find themselves thrown together in a German prisoner-of-war camp during World War II. As they navigate the challenges of confinement, they form unexpected bonds and confront their own personal demons. With tension, humor, and moments of profound introspection, this captivating novel explores the resilience of the human spirit and the power of friendship in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Literary Fiction

Themes: Friendship, Resilience, Identity, Survival
