Synopsis:
In 'Minimum Gauge Railways', Sir Arthur Percival Heywood takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of narrow-gauge railways. Through vivid descriptions and insightful anecdotes, Heywood explores the challenges and triumphs of designing and operating these unique railways. From the rugged terrains of remote regions to the bustling industrial centers, the author unveils the fascinating stories behind the creation and evolution of minimum gauge railways. With a blend of technical expertise and storytelling prowess, Heywood invites readers to discover the ingenuity and determination that went into building these miniature marvels of engineering. Whether you are a railway enthusiast or simply curious about the history of transportation, this book promises to transport you to a world where small trains conquer big challenges.

Genres:
Non-fiction, History, Engineering

Themes:
Innovation, Engineering feats, Transportation history
