Synopsis: In 'Some Problems of the Peace Conference', Haskins and Lord delve into the intricate challenges faced by the delegates during the historic peace conference. Through a meticulous analysis of the negotiations, they shed light on the complex issues that arose and the delicate balance that had to be struck. From territorial disputes to economic considerations, the authors provide a captivating account of the obstacles encountered and the strategies employed to achieve a lasting peace.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Diplomacy, International Relations, Conflict Resolution
