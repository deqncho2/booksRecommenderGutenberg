Synopsis: 
In "Hugh Selwyn Mauberley," Ezra Pound takes us on a poetic journey through the life and struggles of the titular character. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century Europe, the narrative unfolds through a series of vivid and introspective verses. As we delve into Mauberley's experiences, we witness his battles with societal expectations, artistic expression, and the ever-changing world around him. Pound's masterful use of language and imagery paints a captivating portrait of a man grappling with his place in a rapidly evolving society.

Genres: Poetry, Modernist literature

Themes: Identity, Artistic expression, Societal expectations, Change
