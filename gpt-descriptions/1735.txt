Synopsis: In Plato's "Sophist," a young philosopher embarks on a quest to uncover the true nature of sophistry, a deceptive form of rhetoric that challenges the very essence of knowledge and truth. As he engages in a series of intense dialogues with renowned thinkers, he must navigate through intricate arguments and cunning wordplay, all while questioning his own beliefs and understanding of reality. With each encounter, the boundaries between appearance and reality blur, leading to profound revelations and unexpected twists that will keep readers captivated until the very end.

Genres: Philosophy, Drama, Dialogue

Themes: Knowledge and ignorance, Truth and deception, Appearance and reality
