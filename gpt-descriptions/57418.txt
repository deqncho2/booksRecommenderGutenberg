Synopsis: In 'A Modern Madonna' by Caroline Abbot Stanley, a young woman named Mary finds herself torn between societal expectations and her own desires when she becomes unexpectedly pregnant. As she navigates the challenges of single motherhood in the early 20th century, Mary must confront the judgment and prejudice of those around her, while also discovering the strength and resilience within herself. This poignant and thought-provoking novel explores the complexities of motherhood, the pursuit of personal fulfillment, and the power of self-acceptance.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Women's Literature

Themes: Motherhood, Society's Expectations, Self-Discovery, Resilience
