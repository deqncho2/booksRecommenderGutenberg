Synopsis: In "Schubert and His Work," Herbert F. Peyser takes readers on a captivating journey into the life and music of one of the greatest composers of the Romantic era. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Peyser unveils the fascinating story behind Franz Schubert's genius, exploring his struggles, triumphs, and the profound impact he had on the world of classical music. From his humble beginnings to his untimely death, this book offers a compelling portrait of a musical prodigy whose compositions continue to resonate with audiences today.

Genres: Biography, Music History, Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic genius, Romanticism, Musical legacy
