Synopsis: In "The Neutral Merchant: In Relation to the Law of Contraband of War and Blockade under the Order in Council of 11th March, 1915," Francis Taylor Piggott delves into the complex world of international trade during times of war. Through a meticulous examination of legal principles and historical events, Piggott explores the challenges faced by neutral merchants caught between the conflicting interests of belligerent nations. As he navigates the intricacies of contraband and blockade laws, the reader is taken on a journey that reveals the high stakes and moral dilemmas faced by those involved in international commerce during times of conflict.

Genres: Legal analysis, Historical non-fiction

Themes: International trade, War, Legal ethics
