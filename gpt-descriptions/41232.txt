Synopsis: In 'Hungry Hearts' by Anzia Yezierska, we are introduced to the vibrant and bustling world of New York City's Lower East Side at the turn of the 20th century. The story follows the lives of several immigrant families as they navigate the challenges of assimilation, poverty, and the pursuit of the American Dream. Through the eyes of Sara Smolinsky, a young Jewish girl with a hunger for knowledge and independence, we witness the struggles and triumphs of these characters as they strive to find their place in a new land.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Immigrant Literature

Themes: Assimilation, Poverty, Identity, Gender Roles, Family Dynamics
