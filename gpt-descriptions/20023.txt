Synopsis:
In "The Letters of Queen Victoria: A Selection from Her Majesty's Correspondence between the Years 1837 and 1861. Volume 1, 1837-1843," readers are granted a rare glimpse into the personal and private world of Queen Victoria, one of the most influential monarchs in British history. Through a carefully curated selection of her letters, this volume transports us to the early years of Victoria's reign, from her ascension to the throne in 1837 to the formative period of 1843. From her passionate love for Prince Albert to her struggles with political affairs and her deep devotion to her children, these letters reveal the multifaceted nature of Victoria's character and the challenges she faced as a ruler and a woman. With each page, readers are immersed in the intimate thoughts, emotions, and experiences of a queen who left an indelible mark on the world.

Genres: Biography, Historical Non-fiction, Epistolary

Themes: Monarchy, Love and Relationships, Gender Roles, Politics, Family Dynamics
