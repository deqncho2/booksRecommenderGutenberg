Synopsis: 

In 'General History of Connecticut, from Its First Settlement Under George Fenwick to its Latest Period of Amity with Great Britain', Samuel Peters takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and tumultuous history of Connecticut. From the early struggles of the settlers to the eventual establishment of a thriving colony, Peters weaves together a narrative that is both informative and engaging. Through his meticulous research and vivid storytelling, he brings to life the key events, influential figures, and cultural dynamics that shaped the development of this remarkable state. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Colonial literature

Themes: Colonialism, Settler-Indigenous relations, Political and social development
