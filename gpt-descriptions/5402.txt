Synopsis: 
Step into the seedy underbelly of 18th-century London with the '1811 Dictionary of the Vulgar Tongue' by Francis Grose. This fascinating and scandalous dictionary unveils a hidden world of slang, obscenities, and colorful expressions used by the lower classes. From thieves and prostitutes to sailors and beggars, Grose's dictionary provides a captivating glimpse into the secret language of the streets. Delve into the pages of this illicit lexicon and discover the shocking and often hilarious words and phrases that were once considered taboo. 

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Historical, Linguistics

Themes: 
Language and communication, Social class and subcultures
