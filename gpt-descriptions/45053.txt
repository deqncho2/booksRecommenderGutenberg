Synopsis: In "The Churches and Modern Thought: An inquiry into the grounds of unbelief and an appeal for candour," Phelips, Vivian delves into the complex relationship between religion and modernity. Through a thought-provoking exploration of the grounds of unbelief, the author challenges conventional notions and invites readers to engage in a candid examination of their own beliefs. With a captivating blend of intellectual rigor and personal reflection, this book offers a fresh perspective on the intersection of faith and reason, leaving readers with a renewed sense of curiosity and a deeper understanding of the complexities of modern thought.

Genres: Philosophy, Religious Studies, Non-fiction

Themes: Religion and modernity, Unbelief and skepticism, Faith and reason, Intellectual exploration
