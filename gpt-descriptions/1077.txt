Synopsis: In 'The Mirror of Kong Ho' by Bramah, Ernest, we follow the captivating journey of Kong Ho, a Chinese man who travels to London in the early 20th century. Through his unique perspective, we witness the clash of cultures, the absurdities of Western society, and the challenges faced by an outsider trying to navigate a foreign land. As Kong Ho encounters various eccentric characters and experiences both humorous and thought-provoking situations, the story unfolds with wit, charm, and a touch of mystery. Will Kong Ho find his place in this strange new world, or will he forever remain an outsider?

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Comedy

Themes: Cultural clash, Identity, Social commentary
