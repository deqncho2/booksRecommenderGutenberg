Synopsis:
In 'Liber Amoris, Or, The New Pygmalion', Hazlitt takes us on a deeply personal and introspective journey through the tumultuous landscape of love. Through a series of passionate and often painful encounters, the narrator explores the complexities of desire, obsession, and the power dynamics inherent in romantic relationships. As he grapples with his own emotions and vulnerabilities, the boundaries between love and madness blur, leading to a profound exploration of the human heart and its capacity for both ecstasy and torment.

Genres:
Romance, Autobiography, Psychological Fiction

Themes:
Love and Desire, Obsession, Power Dynamics, Madness
