Synopsis: 

In 'Superstitions of the Highlands & Islands of Scotland: Collected Entirely from Oral Sources', John Gregorson Campbell delves into the mystical world of Scottish folklore, unveiling a treasure trove of ancient superstitions and beliefs. Drawing from oral sources, Campbell takes readers on a captivating journey through the Highlands and Islands, exploring the rich tapestry of supernatural tales, mythical creatures, and magical practices that have shaped the region's cultural heritage. From encounters with mischievous fairies to encounters with shape-shifting creatures, this collection of stories offers a fascinating glimpse into the hidden realms of Scottish folklore.

Genres: Folklore, Mythology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Supernatural beliefs, Scottish folklore, Cultural heritage
