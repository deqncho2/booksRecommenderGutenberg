Synopsis: 
In "Sculpture of the Exposition Palaces and Courts," Juliet Helena Lumbard James takes readers on a captivating journey through the art of statuary at the Panama-Pacific International Exposition in San Francisco. With vivid and descriptive notes, the author unveils the intricate details and symbolism behind each sculpture, transporting readers to a world of beauty and creativity. From the grandeur of the exposition palaces to the hidden gems in the courts, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the sculptures that adorned the event. 

Genres: Art, History, Travel

Themes: Beauty, Creativity, Symbolism
