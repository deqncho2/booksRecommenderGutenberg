Synopsis:
'The Folk-lore of Plants' takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of plants, revealing the hidden stories and beliefs that have been woven into their existence for centuries. From the mystical properties of herbs to the symbolic meanings of flowers, this book uncovers the rich tapestry of folklore surrounding the plant kingdom. Delving into ancient myths, superstitions, and cultural traditions, the author explores how plants have been revered, feared, and utilized by different civilizations throughout history. With a blend of fascinating anecdotes and botanical knowledge, 'The Folk-lore of Plants' offers a captivating exploration of the intricate relationship between humans and the natural world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Folklore, Botany

Themes: Nature, Mythology, Cultural Traditions
