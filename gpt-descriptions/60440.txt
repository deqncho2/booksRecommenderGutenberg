Synopsis: 

In 'London Labour and the London Poor, Vol. 2', Henry Mayhew delves deeper into the hidden underbelly of Victorian London, uncovering the lives and struggles of the city's most marginalized inhabitants. From street performers and beggars to prostitutes and thieves, Mayhew's vivid and unflinching descriptions paint a haunting portrait of a society on the fringes. Through his interviews and observations, he exposes the harsh realities faced by these individuals, shedding light on their daily struggles, hopes, and dreams. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Journalism

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Urban life, Human resilience
