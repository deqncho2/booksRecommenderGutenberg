Synopsis: In 'The English Husbandman (The Second Booke)', Markham takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of the Kitchin-Garden, where the art of planting strange flowers and the secrets of breeding all manner of Cattell are revealed. With detailed instructions and insightful tips, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the techniques and practices employed by English husbandmen. From the cultivation of exotic blooms to the nurturing of various livestock, readers will discover the wonders of the natural world and the skills required to master it.

Genres: Non-fiction, Agriculture, Gardening

Themes: Horticulture, Animal husbandry, Self-sufficiency
