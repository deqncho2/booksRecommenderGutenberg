Synopsis: In the 27th volume of his renowned diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a captivating journey through the eventful month of March 1663-64. From scandalous affairs to political intrigue, Pepys provides a vivid and intimate account of his personal and professional life in Restoration-era London. As he navigates the complexities of his relationships, encounters with influential figures, and the ever-present threat of the plague, Pepys offers a fascinating glimpse into the vibrant and tumultuous world of 17th-century England.

Genres: Diary, Historical Fiction, Memoir

Themes: Personal relationships, Political intrigue, Social dynamics, Historical events
