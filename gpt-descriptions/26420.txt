Synopsis: In 'The Convert' by Elizabeth Robins, a young woman named Vida Levering embarks on a journey of self-discovery and rebellion against societal norms. Set in the late 19th century, Vida finds herself torn between her desire for independence and the expectations placed upon her as a woman. As she becomes involved in the suffrage movement and encounters influential figures such as Emmeline Pankhurst, Vida's beliefs and convictions are put to the test. With secrets, political intrigue, and a touch of romance, 'The Convert' explores the complexities of gender roles and the fight for women's rights during a transformative era.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Feminist Literature, Political Drama

Themes: Gender roles, Women's suffrage, Individualism, Social expectations
