Synopsis: In "Finger Prints" by Francis Galton, a renowned scientist delves into the fascinating world of fingerprints, uncovering their hidden secrets and unveiling their remarkable significance. Through meticulous research and captivating anecdotes, Galton explores the unique patterns and individuality of fingerprints, revealing their potential applications in solving crimes and identifying individuals. As he unravels the mysteries behind these intricate marks, Galton takes readers on a captivating journey that challenges conventional wisdom and sheds light on the extraordinary nature of human identity.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Crime

Themes: Identity, Individuality, Forensic Science
