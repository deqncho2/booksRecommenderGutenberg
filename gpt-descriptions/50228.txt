Synopsis: 
'Illustrations of Shakspeare, and of Ancient Manners' by Francis Douce is a captivating exploration of the world of William Shakespeare and the customs and traditions of his time. This book delves into the fascinating characters of clowns and fools in Shakespeare's plays, providing insightful dissertations on their significance. Additionally, Douce examines the popular collection of tales called 'Gesta Romanorum' and sheds light on the English Morris dance, offering a comprehensive understanding of these cultural practices. Through detailed illustrations and engaging analysis, Douce invites readers to immerse themselves in the rich tapestry of Shakespearean literature and the intriguing customs of the past.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Cultural Studies, History

Themes: Shakespearean characters, Folklore and storytelling, Cultural traditions and customs
