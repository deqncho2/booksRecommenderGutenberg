Synopsis: In "Yorktown and the Siege of 1781," Charles E. Hatch, Jr. takes readers on a captivating journey back to the final decisive battle of the American Revolution. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Hatch brings to life the dramatic events that unfolded during the siege of Yorktown, where General George Washington and his troops faced off against the formidable British forces. As the fate of a nation hangs in the balance, readers will be enthralled by the strategic maneuvers, daring tactics, and the personal sacrifices made by both sides in this pivotal moment of history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American Revolution

Themes: Leadership, Strategy, Sacrifice, Independence, Determination
