Synopsis: In "A Sketch of how 'The Diamond Anthem' was Sung around the World: The 60th Anniversary of the Accession Day of Her Majesty Queen Victoria," Cumberland Barlow takes readers on a captivating journey across the globe as he recounts the remarkable events surrounding the performance of a special anthem in honor of Queen Victoria's 60th anniversary. From grand concert halls to remote villages, the anthem's melody unites people from different cultures and backgrounds, creating a powerful sense of unity and celebration. As the story unfolds, readers are introduced to a diverse cast of characters, each with their own unique connection to the anthem and the Queen. Through their experiences, Barlow explores themes of loyalty, national pride, and the enduring legacy of a beloved monarch.

Genres: Historical fiction, Music, Cultural exploration

Themes: Loyalty, National pride, Unity, Legacy
