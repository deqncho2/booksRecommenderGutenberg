Synopsis: 

'Charles Lamb: A Memoir' by Cornwall, Barry is a captivating biography that delves into the life of the renowned English essayist and poet, Charles Lamb. Through meticulous research and intimate anecdotes, the author paints a vivid portrait of Lamb's fascinating journey, from his humble beginnings to his literary success. This memoir offers a glimpse into Lamb's complex personality, his struggles with mental health, and his enduring friendship with fellow writer Samuel Taylor Coleridge. As the narrative unfolds, readers are transported to the vibrant literary circles of 19th-century London, where Lamb's wit and charm left an indelible mark. 

Genres: Biography, Memoir, Literary Criticism

Themes: Friendship, Mental Health, Literary Legacy
