Synopsis: 

Step back in time and explore the hidden secrets of New York City's old taverns in this captivating historical journey. 'Old Taverns of New York' by W. Harrison Bayles takes readers on a fascinating tour through the city's past, uncovering the stories and legends that have been etched into the walls of these iconic establishments. From the bustling streets of Manhattan to the quaint corners of Brooklyn, this book delves into the rich history, colorful characters, and intriguing anecdotes that make up the tapestry of New York's tavern culture. Discover the hidden speakeasies of the Prohibition era, the meeting places of famous historical figures, and the lively atmosphere that once filled these beloved establishments. With vivid descriptions and captivating storytelling, 'Old Taverns of New York' is a must-read for history enthusiasts, urban explorers, and anyone seeking to uncover the forgotten tales of the city's past.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travel, Local history

Themes: New York City history, Urban culture, Social gatherings, Prohibition era, Famous figures
