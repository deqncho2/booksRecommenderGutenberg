Synopsis: In 'The Psychology of Revolution', Gustave Le Bon delves into the fascinating realm of human behavior during times of revolution. Through a captivating exploration of historical events and psychological analysis, Le Bon uncovers the hidden forces that drive individuals to participate in revolutionary movements. From the French Revolution to the Russian Revolution, he examines the collective psychology of crowds, the power of suggestion, and the role of charismatic leaders in shaping the course of revolutions. With thought-provoking insights and compelling anecdotes, Le Bon offers a unique perspective on the complex interplay between the individual and the collective in times of social upheaval.

Genres: Psychology, History, Sociology

Themes: Revolution, Crowd Psychology, Leadership, Social Change
