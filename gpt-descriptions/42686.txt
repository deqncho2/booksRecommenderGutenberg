Synopsis: 

In 'A History of Epidemics in Britain, Volume 1 (of 2): From A.D. 664 to the Extinction of Plague', Charles Creighton takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of epidemics in Britain. From the devastating outbreaks of the bubonic plague to lesser-known diseases that plagued the nation, this comprehensive volume delves into the social, cultural, and medical aspects of these epidemics. Creighton's meticulous research and engaging storytelling bring to life the struggles, fears, and resilience of the British people as they faced these deadly diseases throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medical History

Themes: Epidemics, Public Health, Social Impact, Medical Advances
