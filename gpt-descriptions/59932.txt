Synopsis:
In 'Canada To-day and To-morrow', Arthur E. Copping takes readers on a captivating journey through the vast and diverse landscapes of Canada. From the bustling cities to the untouched wilderness, Copping explores the country's rich history, vibrant culture, and the challenges it faces in the modern era. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, he paints a vivid picture of Canada's present and envisions its future, offering thought-provoking insights into the nation's identity and its place in the world.

Genres:
Non-fiction, Travel, History

Themes:
National identity, Cultural diversity, Environmental conservation, Socio-economic challenges
