Synopsis: "White man bery unsartin": "Nigger haint got no friends, no how"; the blackest chapter in the history of the Republican Party; the men who robbed and combined to rob the freedmen of their hard earnings by Adams, F. Colburn is a gripping historical account that delves into a dark period in the Republican Party's past. The book uncovers the shocking truth behind the exploitation and theft of the hard-earned earnings of freedmen. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Adams exposes the individuals and networks responsible for these injustices, shedding light on a chapter often overlooked in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, investigative journalism, political history

Themes: Racial discrimination, political corruption, economic exploitation
