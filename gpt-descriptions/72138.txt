Synopsis: 

'Early Explorers of Plymouth Harbor, 1525-1619' takes readers on a captivating journey through the untold stories of the brave individuals who ventured into the uncharted waters of Plymouth Harbor during the 16th and 17th centuries. From intrepid explorers seeking new trade routes to settlers in search of a better life, this book unveils the remarkable tales of these early pioneers and their encounters with the unknown. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Henry F. Howe paints a vivid picture of the challenges, triumphs, and mysteries that awaited these explorers in the vast expanse of Plymouth Harbor.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Adventure, Exploration

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Colonialism, Cultural encounters, Human resilience
