Synopsis: In "Queen Victoria, her girlhood and womanhood" by Grace Greenwood, readers are transported into the fascinating life of one of the most iconic monarchs in history. From her humble beginnings as a young princess to her ascension to the throne, this captivating biography delves into the personal and political struggles that shaped Queen Victoria's remarkable journey. With vivid storytelling and rich historical detail, Greenwood paints a vivid portrait of a woman who defied societal expectations and left an indelible mark on the world.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Royalty, Feminism, Power and Politics
