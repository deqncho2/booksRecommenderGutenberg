Synopsis: 
In 'A Hermit's Wild Friends; or, Eighteen Years in the Woods', Mason Augustus Walton takes readers on a captivating journey into the secluded life of a hermit. Living alone in the wilderness for eighteen years, the hermit forms an unexpected bond with the wild creatures that surround him. From playful squirrels to wise owls, the hermit's wild friends become his companions, teaching him valuable lessons about survival, friendship, and the interconnectedness of all living beings. As the hermit's story unfolds, readers are transported into a world of solitude, adventure, and the profound beauty of nature.

Genres: Adventure, Nature Writing, Memoir

Themes: Solitude, Friendship, Nature, Survival
