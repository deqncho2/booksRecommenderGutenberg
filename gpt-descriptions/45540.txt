Synopsis: In 'The Christ Myth', Arthur Drews delves into the controversial and thought-provoking exploration of the historical existence of Jesus Christ. Drawing upon extensive research and critical analysis, Drews challenges the traditional narrative surrounding Jesus, presenting a compelling argument that challenges long-held beliefs. As he unravels the layers of myth and legend, the reader is taken on a captivating journey that challenges their understanding of religion, history, and the power of belief.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, Historical Analysis

Themes: Mythology, Historical Accuracy, Religious Belief, Skepticism
