Synopsis: In 'The Law of Hotel Life; or, the Wrongs and Rights of Host and Guest', author R. Vashon Rogers delves into the intriguing world of hotels, exploring the complex dynamics between hosts and guests. Through a series of captivating anecdotes and thought-provoking scenarios, Rogers uncovers the hidden truths and unspoken rules that govern the interactions within these temporary abodes. From the secrets kept by hotel staff to the expectations and entitlements of guests, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the often overlooked realm of hotel life.

Genres: Non-fiction, Sociology, Hospitality

Themes: Power dynamics, Hospitality industry, Social etiquette
