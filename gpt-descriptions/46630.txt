Synopsis: In 'The Supernatural in the New Testament, Possible, Credible, and Historical', Charles Adolphus Row delves into the validity of objections against Christianity as a divine revelation. Through a meticulous examination of historical evidence, Row explores the possibility and credibility of supernatural occurrences in the New Testament. With thought-provoking arguments and compelling analysis, this book challenges skeptics and believers alike to reconsider their views on the supernatural in Christianity.

Genres: Theology, Religious Studies, Historical Analysis

Themes: Supernatural, Christianity, Divine Revelation, Historical Validity
