Synopsis: 
In the aftermath of the devastating earthquake and fire that struck San Francisco in 1906, a remarkable organization emerged to provide relief to the city's shattered population. 'San Francisco Relief Survey' delves into the fascinating story of this organization, exploring their innovative methods and tireless efforts to rebuild the city and restore hope to its residents. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, this book uncovers the untold stories of heroism, resilience, and the indomitable human spirit that emerged from the ashes of one of the greatest disasters in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Disaster history, Social history

Themes: Resilience, Community, Humanitarianism, Rebuilding
