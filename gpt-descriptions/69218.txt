Synopsis: In 'The Worship of the Golden Calf: A Story of Wage-Slavery in Massachusetts' by Charles Sheldon, we are transported to the bustling industrial city of Massachusetts in the late 19th century. The story revolves around a young factory worker named John, who becomes entangled in the harsh realities of wage-slavery and the dehumanizing effects of industrialization. As John navigates the treacherous world of labor exploitation, he finds himself torn between his desire for a better life and the sacrifices he must make to survive. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this gripping tale explores the dark underbelly of capitalism and the struggle for dignity in an unforgiving society.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Social Commentary, Drama

Themes: Labor exploitation, Industrialization, Social inequality, Human dignity
