Synopsis: 

'Letters and Discussions on the Formation of Colored Regiments' by Alfred M. Green is a thought-provoking and inspiring work that explores the role of colored people in the United States during the Great Slaveholders' Rebellion. Through a series of letters and discussions, Green delves into the formation of colored regiments and the duty of the colored people in this tumultuous time. The book sheds light on the challenges, sacrifices, and contributions of the colored soldiers, as well as the broader implications of their involvement in the war. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, African American Studies

Themes: Civil War, Racism, Patriotism, Equality
