Synopsis: In 'False Dawn (The 'Forties)' by Edith Wharton, readers are transported to the vibrant and tumultuous world of New York City in the 1940s. The story revolves around the lives of Pauline and Amherst, a married couple who find themselves caught in a web of secrets, desires, and societal expectations. As they navigate the complexities of their relationship, they encounter a cast of intriguing characters, each with their own hidden agendas. With its richly detailed setting and compelling characters, 'False Dawn' delves into the depths of human nature, exploring themes of love, betrayal, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Romance

Themes: Love and Marriage, Social Expectations, Secrets and Betrayal
