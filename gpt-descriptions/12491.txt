Synopsis: In 'Twelve Types', G.K. Chesterton presents a collection of intriguing character sketches that delve into the lives of twelve diverse individuals. From a famous poet to a notorious criminal, each portrait offers a unique perspective on the complexities of human nature. With his characteristic wit and insight, Chesterton invites readers to explore the depths of these captivating personalities, leaving them pondering the mysteries of the human condition.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Philosophy

Themes: Human nature, Individuality, Society, Morality
