Synopsis:
'Letters of a Diplomat's Wife, 1883-1900' offers a captivating glimpse into the intriguing world of international diplomacy through the eyes of Mary King Waddington. As the wife of a French diplomat, Mary finds herself at the center of political and social events in Europe and beyond. Through her vivid and intimate letters, she shares her experiences, encounters with influential figures, and the challenges she faces as a woman navigating the complex world of diplomacy. From glittering balls to secret negotiations, Mary's letters provide a fascinating and personal account of a remarkable era.

Genres:
Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Biography

Themes:
Diplomacy, Politics, Gender Roles, Society, International Relations
