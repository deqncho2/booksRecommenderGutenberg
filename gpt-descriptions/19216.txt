Synopsis: In 'The History of England in Three Volumes, Vol. I., Part F.: From Charles II. to James II.', David Hume delves into a tumultuous period of English history, exploring the reigns of two contrasting monarchs. From the restoration of Charles II to the controversial rule of James II, Hume uncovers the political intrigues, religious conflicts, and power struggles that shaped this era. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, he brings to life the complex characters and events that defined this pivotal time in England's past.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Monarchy and power, Religious conflicts, Political intrigue
