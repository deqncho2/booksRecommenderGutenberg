Synopsis: 

In 'British Pomology: Or, the History, Description, Classification, and Synonymes, of the Fruits and Fruit Trees of Great Britain,' Robert Hogg takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse world of British fruits and fruit trees. From the luscious apples and pears to the exotic cherries and plums, Hogg's meticulous research and vivid descriptions bring these fruits to life, revealing their fascinating history, unique characteristics, and even their various synonyms. Whether you are a seasoned pomologist or simply a fruit enthusiast, this comprehensive guide will leave you eager to explore the bountiful orchards of Great Britain and discover the hidden treasures they hold.

Genres: Non-fiction, Reference, Horticulture

Themes: Fruit cultivation, Botany, British agricultural heritage
