Synopsis: 

In 'History of the English People, Volume II: The Charter, 1216-1307; The Parliament, 1307-1400', John Richard Green takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of English history from the signing of the Magna Carta to the rise of Parliament. This volume delves into the political, social, and cultural developments that shaped England during this transformative era. From the struggles for power and the emergence of constitutional rights to the impact of the Black Death and the Hundred Years' War, Green paints a vivid picture of a nation in flux. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, he brings to life the key figures, events, and ideas that shaped the destiny of the English people.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power struggles, Constitutional rights, Social change, Cultural transformation
