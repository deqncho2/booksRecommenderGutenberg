Synopsis: 

In "The Royal Observatory, Greenwich: A Glance at Its History and Work," E. Walter Maunder takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and groundbreaking work of one of the world's most renowned observatories. From its humble beginnings as a small observatory in the 17th century to its pivotal role in shaping our understanding of the universe, Maunder delves into the fascinating stories of the astronomers, scientists, and instruments that have made the Royal Observatory, Greenwich a beacon of astronomical discovery. With vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, this book offers a glimpse into the remarkable achievements and ongoing research that continue to unfold within the walls of this iconic institution.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Science

Themes: Astronomy, Scientific Discovery, Historical Significance
