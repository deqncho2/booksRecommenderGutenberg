Synopsis: 
Step into the whimsical world of Lewis Carroll, the enigmatic author behind the beloved Alice's Adventures in Wonderland. In this captivating biography, Moses, Belle delves into the extraordinary life of Carroll, exploring his creative genius, his complex relationships, and the inspirations that shaped his iconic stories. From his childhood fascination with puzzles and wordplay to his close friendship with Alice Liddell, the real-life inspiration for Alice, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the mind of a literary genius. Uncover the secrets behind Carroll's pseudonym, his mathematical prowess, and the mysterious photographs that still puzzle scholars today. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Moses, Belle paints a vivid portrait of a man who blurred the lines between reality and fantasy, leaving an indelible mark on children's literature and the world of imagination.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Creativity, Imagination, Identity, Childhood, Friendship
