Synopsis: 

In 'Twenty Years of Congress, Vol. 1: From Lincoln to Garfield, with a Review of the Events Which Led to the Political Revolution of 1860', James Gillespie Blaine takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous political landscape of the United States during the pivotal years from Lincoln's presidency to Garfield's assassination. Blaine, a prominent politician and historian, provides a detailed account of the events, personalities, and political maneuverings that shaped this transformative period in American history. From the Civil War and Reconstruction to the rise of the Republican Party and the challenges faced by the nation, this volume offers a comprehensive and engaging exploration of the political revolution that unfolded in the mid-19th century.

Genres: History, Political Science, Biography

Themes: American politics, Civil War, Reconstruction, Republican Party, Political revolution
