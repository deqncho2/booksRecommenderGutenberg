Synopsis: In "Woman; Her Position and Influence in Ancient Greece and Rome, and Among the Early Christians," Sir James Donaldson delves into the captivating history of women in ancient civilizations and early Christianity. Through meticulous research and analysis, he uncovers the multifaceted roles and remarkable influence that women held in these societies. From the powerful priestesses of ancient Greece to the influential women of the Roman Empire, and the early Christian women who played pivotal roles in shaping the faith, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the often overlooked contributions of women throughout history.

Genres: History, Women's Studies, Religious Studies

Themes: Gender roles, Women's empowerment, Religion and society
