Synopsis: 

In the second volume of "The Historians' History of the World," readers are taken on a captivating journey through the ancient civilizations of Israel, India, Persia, Phoenicia, and the Minor Nations of Western Asia. Delving into the rich tapestry of these diverse cultures, the book uncovers the fascinating stories of their rise and fall, their triumphs and struggles, and the profound impact they had on shaping the world we know today. From the legendary tales of ancient Israel to the enigmatic wisdom of India, from the mighty Persian Empire to the seafaring Phoenicians, and from the lesser-known but equally intriguing minor nations, this volume offers a comprehensive exploration of the ancient world's most influential civilizations.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Cultural exchange, Power and empire, Historical legacy
