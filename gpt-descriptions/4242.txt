Synopsis: 

In 'Belgium' by Joseph E. Morris, a gripping tale unfolds as a young American journalist finds himself immersed in the heart of Europe during the tumultuous years of World War I. Drawn to the captivating allure of Belgium, he becomes entangled in a web of danger, espionage, and forbidden love. As he navigates the treacherous landscape of war-torn Europe, he must confront his own demons and make choices that will shape his destiny forever. With vivid descriptions and unexpected twists, 'Belgium' takes readers on a thrilling journey through a country torn apart by conflict, where bravery and sacrifice become the ultimate test of humanity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Romance

Themes: Love and Sacrifice, Courage in the Face of Adversity, The Impact of War on Individuals and Society
