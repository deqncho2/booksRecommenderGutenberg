Synopsis: 

In 'Wellington's Army, 1809-1814', Charles Oman takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years of the Napoleonic Wars. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Oman delves into the inner workings of the British army under the leadership of the legendary Duke of Wellington. From the intense battles of Talavera and Salamanca to the grueling Peninsular Campaign, Oman paints a vivid picture of the soldiers' experiences, the strategies employed, and the challenges faced by Wellington and his men. 

Genres: Military history, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Leadership, Strategy, Warfare, Resilience
