Synopsis: In "May Fair," Michael Arlen takes readers on a captivating journey into the heart of London during the fifteenth and sixteenth years of King George the Fifth's reign. This entertaining work aims to reveal the real state of affairs in the city, exposing the follies, misadventures, and galanteries of the charming people who inhabit it. Through a series of intriguing events and reflections, Arlen offers a glimpse into the lives of the upper class, leaving readers eager to uncover the secrets and scandals that lie within the pages.

Genres: Historical fiction, Satire, Social commentary

Themes: Society and class, Morality and decadence, Human nature and desire
