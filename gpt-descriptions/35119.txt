Synopsis: 
'Trenching at Gallipoli: The personal narrative of a Newfoundlander with the ill-fated Dardanelles expedition' takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of John Gallishaw, a Newfoundlander who finds himself amidst the ill-fated Dardanelles expedition during World War I. From the treacherous trenches to the relentless battles, Gallishaw's personal narrative offers a raw and intimate account of the horrors and camaraderie that defined this infamous campaign. As he navigates the unforgiving landscape of Gallipoli, readers will be captivated by the resilience and courage displayed by Gallishaw and his fellow soldiers, as they face unimaginable challenges and forge unbreakable bonds in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, War Memoir

Themes: War, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Resilience
