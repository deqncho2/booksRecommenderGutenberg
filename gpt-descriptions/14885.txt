Synopsis: In the captivating novel 'Red Pottage' by Mary Cholmondeley, two women from different backgrounds find themselves entangled in a web of love, ambition, and societal expectations. Rachel West and Hester Gresley navigate the complexities of relationships, personal desires, and the consequences of their choices in a world where appearances are everything. As their lives intertwine, secrets are revealed, and the true nature of love and friendship is put to the test. Set against the backdrop of late Victorian England, 'Red Pottage' is a compelling tale of passion, betrayal, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: Love and desire, Social expectations, Ambition and success
