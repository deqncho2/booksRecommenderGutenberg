Synopsis: 

In "The Methods of Glass Blowing and of Working Silica in the Oxy-Gas Flame," author W. A. Shenstone takes readers on a captivating journey into the intricate world of glass blowing and working with silica in the oxy-gas flame. Through detailed explanations and step-by-step instructions, Shenstone unveils the secrets behind this ancient art form, providing a valuable resource for chemical and physical students alike. From the delicate techniques of shaping molten glass to the precise manipulation of silica in the fiery embrace of the oxy-gas flame, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the craftsmanship and scientific principles that underpin the creation of beautiful glass objects. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Craftsmanship

Themes: Glass blowing, Silica manipulation, Craftsmanship, Scientific principles
