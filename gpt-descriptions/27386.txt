Synopsis: In 'House of John Procter, Witchcraft Martyr, 1692', William P. Upham delves into the haunting events surrounding the infamous Salem Witch Trials. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Upham uncovers the dark secrets and personal struggles of John Procter, a man caught in the midst of a community consumed by fear and hysteria. As the accusations of witchcraft escalate, Procter's loyalty, integrity, and love for his family are put to the ultimate test. This gripping narrative explores the depths of human nature, the power of truth, and the devastating consequences of mass hysteria.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Drama

Themes: Mass Hysteria, Truth and Integrity, Family and Loyalty
