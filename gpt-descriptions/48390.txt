Synopsis:
In "Social England under the Regency, Vol. 1," John Ashton takes us on a captivating journey through the vibrant and scandalous world of early 19th-century England. From the lavish balls and extravagant parties of the aristocracy to the gritty streets of London, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the social fabric of the Regency era. Through vivid anecdotes and historical accounts, Ashton explores the lives of both the privileged and the working class, shedding light on the intricate web of relationships, customs, and societal norms that shaped this captivating period in English history.

Genres:
Historical Non-fiction, Social History, Biography

Themes:
Society and Class, Gender Roles, Cultural Transformation
