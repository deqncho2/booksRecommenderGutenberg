Synopsis: In "Human Nature and Conduct: An introduction to social psychology," John Dewey delves into the intricate workings of human behavior and the impact of social influences. Through a thought-provoking exploration, Dewey challenges conventional wisdom and offers a fresh perspective on the complexities of human nature. With captivating examples and insightful analysis, he uncovers the hidden forces that shape our actions and decisions, ultimately shedding light on the intricate interplay between individuality and society. This enlightening journey into social psychology will leave readers questioning their own assumptions and reevaluating their understanding of human behavior.

Genres: Psychology, Philosophy, Sociology

Themes: Human behavior, Social influences, Individuality, Society
