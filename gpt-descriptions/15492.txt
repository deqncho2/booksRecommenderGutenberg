Synopsis: In Henrik Ibsen's "A Doll's House," we are introduced to Nora, a seemingly content wife and mother who lives a comfortable life with her husband Torvald. However, as the story unfolds, we discover that Nora's seemingly perfect life is built on a foundation of secrets and lies. As she begins to question her role in society and the expectations placed upon her, Nora embarks on a journey of self-discovery that will challenge the norms of her time and have far-reaching consequences.

Genres: Drama, Realism

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, identity, deception, independence
