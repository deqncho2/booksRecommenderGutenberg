Synopsis: In 'An Inquiry into the Principles of Political Oeconomy', Sir James Steuart delves into the intricate workings of domestic policy in free nations. Exploring various aspects such as population, agriculture, trade, industry, money, coin, interest, circulation, banks, exchange, public credit, and taxes, Steuart provides a comprehensive analysis of the science behind these elements. Through his thought-provoking essay, he offers insights into the complex interplay between economic factors and political decision-making, shedding light on the principles that govern the prosperity and stability of nations.

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Political Science

Themes: Domestic policy, Economic principles, Political decision-making
