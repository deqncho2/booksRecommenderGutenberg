Synopsis:
"The Admirable Crichton" by J.M. Barrie is a captivating play that takes readers on a thrilling journey of social hierarchy and survival. The story revolves around the eccentric Lord Loam and his family, who find themselves stranded on a deserted island after a shipwreck. As they struggle to adapt to their new circumstances, the roles of the servants and the aristocracy begin to shift, leading to unexpected consequences. With wit, humor, and thought-provoking twists, Barrie explores the complexities of class, power dynamics, and the true nature of human character.

Genres:
Comedy, Drama, Satire

Themes:
Class hierarchy, Social roles, Survival, Human nature
