Synopsis: 
"The Humour and Pathos of Anglo-Indian Life: Extracts from his brother's note-book, made by Dr. Ticklemore" takes readers on a captivating journey through the unique and often comical experiences of Anglo-Indians. Dr. Ticklemore, a keen observer, compiles a collection of anecdotes, stories, and reflections that offer a glimpse into the lives of those living in British India. From humorous encounters with the local population to poignant tales of love and loss, this book paints a vivid picture of the joys and challenges faced by Anglo-Indians in a vibrant and diverse society.

Genres: 
Humor, Memoir, Fiction

Themes: 
Cultural identity, Colonialism, Love and loss
