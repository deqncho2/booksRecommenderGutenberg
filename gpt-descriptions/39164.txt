Synopsis: 

In 'The Pocket Gophers (Genus Thomomys) of Utah', Stephen David Durrant takes readers on a fascinating journey into the world of these elusive creatures. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Durrant unveils the hidden lives of pocket gophers, shedding light on their behavior, habitat, and evolutionary significance. From their intricate tunnel systems to their unique adaptations, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of these remarkable rodents. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Zoology

Themes: Animal behavior, Evolution, Ecology
