Synopsis: 

In 'How Members of Congress Are Bribed', J. Hampton Moore delves into the dark underbelly of American politics, exposing a shocking web of corruption and deceit. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, Moore uncovers the intricate methods used to bribe members of Congress, revealing a world where power and money intertwine in the most sinister ways. As the author peels back the layers of this scandalous operation, readers will be captivated by the shocking revelations and the lengths to which some individuals will go to manipulate the political landscape. 

Genres: Political thriller, investigative journalism, non-fiction

Themes: Corruption, power dynamics, political manipulation
