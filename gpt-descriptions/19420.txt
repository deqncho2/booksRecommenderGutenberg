Synopsis: 
In 'Bell's Cathedrals: The Cathedral Church of York', Arthur Clutton-Brock takes readers on a captivating journey through the magnificent York Minster. With vivid descriptions and historical anecdotes, he explores the intricate details of the cathedral's architecture and provides a glimpse into the rich history of the Archi-Episcopal See. From the awe-inspiring stained glass windows to the towering spires, this book offers a fascinating exploration of one of England's most iconic cathedrals.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Architecture

Themes: Religious heritage, Gothic architecture, English history
