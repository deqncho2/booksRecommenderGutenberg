Synopsis: In 'Essays on some unsettled Questions of Political Economy', John Stuart Mill delves into the complex and controversial realm of political economy, exploring various unsettled questions that have long intrigued scholars and policymakers. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Mill challenges conventional wisdom and offers fresh perspectives on topics such as the nature of wealth, the role of government in economic affairs, and the impact of free trade. With his characteristic intellectual rigor and eloquence, Mill invites readers on a captivating journey through the intricacies of political economy, shedding light on pressing issues that continue to shape our world today.

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Political Science

Themes: Wealth, Government intervention, Free trade, Economic policy, Social progress
