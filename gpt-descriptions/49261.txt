Synopsis: In 'The Glebe 1913/11 (Vol. 1, No. 2): Diary of a Suicide' by Wallace E. Baker, readers are taken on a haunting journey through the pages of a diary that reveals the innermost thoughts and struggles of a troubled individual. As the diary entries unfold, the reader becomes immersed in a gripping narrative that explores the depths of despair, the complexities of the human mind, and the haunting allure of self-destruction. With each turn of the page, the reader is left questioning the true nature of the protagonist's fate, and the profound impact their story will have on their own understanding of life and mortality.

Genres: Psychological fiction, Gothic fiction, Epistolary novel

Themes: Mental health, Existentialism, Morality, Isolation
