Synopsis: In 'Is Life Worth Living Without Immortality?: A Lecture Delivered Before the Independent Religious Society, Chicago', Mangasarian explores the profound question of whether life holds any value if there is no possibility of immortality. Through a thought-provoking lecture, he challenges conventional beliefs and invites readers to contemplate the meaning and purpose of existence. With eloquence and intellectual rigor, Mangasarian delves into the depths of human nature, questioning the significance of our actions and the pursuit of a fulfilling life. This captivating work offers a unique perspective on the eternal quest for immortality and its implications on our perception of life.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Immortality, Meaning of Life, Existentialism
