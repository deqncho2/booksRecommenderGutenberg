Synopsis: 

In "History of the Eighty-sixth Regiment, Illinois Volunteer Infantry, during its term of service," author John R. Kinnear takes readers on a captivating journey through the experiences of a regiment during the American Civil War. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Kinnear brings to life the trials and triumphs of the brave soldiers who fought for their beliefs and the preservation of their nation. From the initial recruitment to the final battles, this gripping account offers a unique perspective on the war, shedding light on the sacrifices made by these unsung heroes.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Patriotism, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Conflict, Resilience
