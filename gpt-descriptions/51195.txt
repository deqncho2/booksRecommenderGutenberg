Synopsis: 

'Camp, Court and Siege: A Narrative of Personal Adventure and Observation During Two Wars: 1861-1865; 1870-1871' by Hoffman, Wickham is a captivating memoir that takes readers on a thrilling journey through two significant wars in American and European history. The author, Hoffman Wickham, shares his firsthand experiences and observations as he navigates the chaos and danger of the American Civil War and the Franco-Prussian War. From the battlefields to the courtrooms, and even during sieges, Wickham's narrative provides a unique perspective on the events that shaped these tumultuous periods. 

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: War, Personal Courage, Survival, Historical Events
