Synopsis: In 'The Old Irish World', Alice Stopford Green takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient and mysterious world of Ireland. From the legendary tales of warriors and kings to the intricate social structures and religious beliefs, Green unveils the rich tapestry of Irish history. Through her vivid storytelling, she explores the origins of the Irish people, their unique customs, and the profound impact they had on the development of Western civilization. Delving into the realms of mythology, politics, and art, 'The Old Irish World' offers a fascinating glimpse into a forgotten era that continues to shape Ireland's identity today.

Genres: History, Mythology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Irish identity, Ancient civilizations, Cultural heritage
