Synopsis: 

In 'Proof-Reading: A Series of Essays for Readers and Their Employers, and for Authors and Editors', F. Horace Teall takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of proofreading. Through a collection of insightful essays, Teall explores the importance of meticulous editing and the impact it has on the written word. From uncovering common errors to discussing the art of effective communication, this book offers valuable advice for both readers and those involved in the publishing process. With engaging anecdotes and practical tips, Teall's essays shed light on the often overlooked but essential role of proofreading in ensuring clarity and precision in written works.

Genres: Non-fiction, Writing, Language

Themes: Editing and proofreading, Communication, Language precision
