Synopsis:
'Letters of Marcus Tullius Cicero' offers a captivating glimpse into the life and mind of one of ancient Rome's most influential figures. Through a collection of personal letters, Cicero reveals his thoughts, struggles, and triumphs as he navigates the treacherous political landscape of the Roman Republic. From his passionate defense of the rule of law to his poignant reflections on friendship and duty, these letters provide a fascinating insight into the complexities of Cicero's character and the turbulent times in which he lived.

Genres:
Epistolary, Historical, Political

Themes:
Politics and power, Friendship and loyalty, Ethics and morality
