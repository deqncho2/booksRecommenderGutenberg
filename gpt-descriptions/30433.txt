Synopsis: 
In 'Émile; Or, Concerning Education; Extracts', Rousseau takes us on a captivating journey through the unconventional education of a young boy named Émile. As we follow Émile's development from infancy to adulthood, Rousseau challenges traditional educational practices and offers a radical perspective on how to raise a child. Through thought-provoking anecdotes and philosophical musings, Rousseau explores the importance of nature, freedom, and self-discovery in the process of education. This extract from Rousseau's influential work provides a fascinating glimpse into his revolutionary ideas on education and child-rearing.

Genres: 
Philosophy, Education, Autobiography

Themes: 
Nature vs. Nurture, Freedom, Self-discovery, Education
