Synopsis: In "Washington and his colleagues; a chronicle of the rise and fall of federalism," Henry Jones Ford takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous political landscape of early America. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Ford delves into the lives and actions of George Washington and his fellow statesmen, revealing the intricate web of alliances, rivalries, and compromises that shaped the nation. From the birth of the federal government to its eventual decline, this gripping narrative explores the challenges faced by these influential figures and the profound impact they had on the course of American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Leadership, Power dynamics, Nation-building, Political ideologies
