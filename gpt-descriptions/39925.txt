Synopsis: In 'A Short History of English Music', Ford, Ernest takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of English music, from its humble beginnings to its grand achievements. From the enchanting melodies of medieval times to the revolutionary compositions of the Renaissance and beyond, this book delves into the lives of the brilliant composers who shaped the course of English music. With vivid descriptions and insightful anecdotes, Ford paints a vivid picture of the cultural, social, and political influences that shaped each era of English music. Whether you are a music enthusiast or simply curious about the rich tapestry of English musical heritage, this book is sure to captivate and inspire.

Genres: Non-fiction, Music History, Cultural History

Themes: Evolution of music, Cultural influences, Artistic expression
