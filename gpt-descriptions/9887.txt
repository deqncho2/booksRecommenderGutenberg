Synopsis: 

In 'Essays in War-Time: Further Studies in the Task of Social Hygiene', Havelock Ellis delves into the profound impact of war on society, exploring its effects on human behavior, relationships, and the overall well-being of individuals. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, Ellis examines the intricate interplay between war and social hygiene, shedding light on the complex dynamics that arise during times of conflict. From the psychological toll on soldiers to the societal shifts that occur in wartime, Ellis offers a compelling analysis of the multifaceted consequences of war. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, Psychology

Themes: War, Social Hygiene, Human Behavior, Relationships, Societal Impact
