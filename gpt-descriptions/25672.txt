Synopsis: In The Boyhood of Lincoln: A Tale of the Tunker Schoolmaster and the Times of Black Hawk, Hezekiah Butterworth takes readers on a captivating journey into the early life of Abraham Lincoln. Set in the rural Midwest during the early 19th century, the story follows young Lincoln as he navigates the challenges of poverty, education, and the turbulent times of the Black Hawk War. Through encounters with a wise schoolmaster and interactions with the local community, Lincoln's character is shaped, and his determination to make a difference in the world begins to emerge. This engaging tale offers a glimpse into the formative years of one of America's most beloved presidents.

Genres: Historical fiction, Coming-of-age, Biographical

Themes: Poverty and resilience, Education and self-improvement, Moral character and leadership
