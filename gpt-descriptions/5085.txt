Synopsis: In 'Characters of Shakespeare's Plays', Hazlitt delves into the fascinating world of Shakespeare's characters, exploring their complexities, motivations, and the enduring impact they have had on literature and theatre. From the tragic figures of Hamlet and Macbeth to the comedic brilliance of Falstaff and Beatrice, Hazlitt's insightful analysis brings these iconic characters to life, shedding light on their psychological depths and the timeless themes they embody. 

Genres: Literary criticism, Drama, Biography

Themes: Human nature, Tragedy, Comedy, Morality, Power, Love
