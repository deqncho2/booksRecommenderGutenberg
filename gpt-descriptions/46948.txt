Synopsis: In "The Disaster Which Eclipsed History: The Johnstown Flood," author R. K. Fox delves into the gripping account of one of the deadliest natural disasters in American history. Set in the late 19th century, this non-fiction narrative takes readers on a heart-wrenching journey through the events leading up to and following the catastrophic flood that devastated the town of Johnstown, Pennsylvania. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Fox paints a vivid picture of the human tragedy and resilience that unfolded amidst the raging waters. As the story unfolds, readers will be captivated by the harrowing tales of survival, the shocking failures of engineering, and the profound impact this disaster had on the lives of countless individuals. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Disaster

Themes: Tragedy, Resilience, Human Nature, Engineering Failures
