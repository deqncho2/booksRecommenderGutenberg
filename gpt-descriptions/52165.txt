Synopsis: In 'Transylvanian Superstitions: From: The Nineteenth Century (Vol. 18), London, July-December 1885, pp. 130-150', Gerard, E. delves into the mysterious world of Transylvanian folklore, unearthing a collection of captivating and chilling superstitions. From ancient rituals to eerie legends, this volume offers a glimpse into the supernatural beliefs that have haunted the people of Transylvania for centuries. As the author explores these tales, readers will be transported to a realm where darkness and enchantment intertwine, leaving them both fascinated and unsettled.

Genres: Non-fiction, Folklore, Supernatural

Themes: Superstition, Folk beliefs, Cultural traditions
