Synopsis: 

In 'South Africa and the Boer-British War, Volume I', Halstead and Hopkins delve into the captivating history of South Africa and its people, with a particular focus on the war of 1899 and 1900. This meticulously researched volume takes readers on a journey through the complex dynamics between the Boers and the British, exploring the events leading up to the war and the intense battles that ensued. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, the authors shed light on the political, social, and cultural factors that shaped this pivotal period in South African history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War history, Political history

Themes: Colonialism, Nationalism, Conflict and war, Cultural identity
