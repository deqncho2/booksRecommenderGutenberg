Synopsis: In 'The Code of Honor: Or, Rules for the Government of Principals and Seconds in Duelling', John Lyde Wilson delves into the intricate world of duelling, exploring the rules and principles that govern this controversial practice. Through a series of insightful guidelines, Wilson provides a comprehensive understanding of the code of honor that duellists must adhere to, shedding light on the motivations, rituals, and consequences of engaging in this deadly tradition. With a blend of historical anecdotes and practical advice, this work offers a captivating glimpse into a society where honor and reputation were valued above all else.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Legal

Themes: Honor, Reputation, Morality, Tradition
