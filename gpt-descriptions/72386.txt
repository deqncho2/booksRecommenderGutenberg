Synopsis: 
In "Annals of the early Caliphate from the death of Mahomet to the Omeyyad and Abbaside dynasties A.H. XI-LXI (A.D. 632-680) from original sources," Sir William Muir takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period following the death of the Prophet Muhammad. Drawing from original sources, Muir delves into the power struggles, political intrigue, and religious fervor that shaped the early Caliphate. From the rise of the Omeyyad and Abbaside dynasties to the challenges faced by the Muslim community, this book offers a gripping account of a pivotal era in Islamic history.

Genres: History, Biography, Religious Studies

Themes: Power struggles, Political intrigue, Religious development
