Synopsis: In 'Subjectivity' by Norman Spinrad, a groundbreaking technology allows individuals to experience the subjective realities of others, blurring the lines between identity, empathy, and manipulation. As the protagonist delves into the minds of various characters, they uncover a web of secrets and hidden agendas that challenge their own perception of truth. With each mind-bending encounter, the boundaries of subjectivity and the nature of consciousness are pushed to their limits, leading to a thrilling exploration of the human psyche.

Genres: Science Fiction, Psychological Thriller, Speculative Fiction

Themes: Identity, Empathy, Manipulation, Perception, Consciousness
