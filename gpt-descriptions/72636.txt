Synopsis: In "Social Organization: A Study of the Larger Mind," Charles Horton Cooley takes readers on a captivating exploration of the intricate web of social relationships that shape our lives. Through insightful observations and thought-provoking analysis, Cooley delves into the dynamics of human interaction, uncovering the hidden forces that influence our behavior and shape our identities. From the complexities of group dynamics to the power of social influence, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the inner workings of society and the human mind.

Genres: Sociology, Psychology, Social Sciences

Themes: Social interaction, Group dynamics, Identity formation, Social influence, Human behavior
