Synopsis: In "Lord George Bentinck: A Political Biography," Benjamin Disraeli, Earl of Beaconsfield, delves into the life and career of the enigmatic Lord George Bentinck, a prominent figure in British politics during the 19th century. From his aristocratic upbringing to his influential role in Parliament, Bentinck's story is one of ambition, intrigue, and unwavering dedication to his political ideals. Disraeli masterfully unravels the complexities of Bentinck's character, shedding light on the inner workings of a man who left an indelible mark on British politics.

Genres: Biography, Political History

Themes: Ambition, Political Ideals, Intrigue
