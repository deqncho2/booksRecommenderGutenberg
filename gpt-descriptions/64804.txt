Synopsis: 

In 'What Became of the Slaves on a Georgia Plantation?: Great Auction Sale of Slaves, at Savannah, Georgia, March 2d & 3d, 1859', Doesticks, Q. K. Philander takes us on a gripping journey to the heart of the American South during a time of great turmoil. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, the author unveils the shocking reality of a slave auction that took place in Savannah, Georgia. As the story unfolds, we witness the lives of the enslaved individuals who were bought and sold, their hopes and dreams, and the profound impact this auction had on their destinies. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, social commentary, memoir

Themes: Slavery, human rights, social injustice, resilience
