Synopsis: In 'The Oxford Reformers: John Colet, Erasmus, and Thomas More', Frederic Seebohm delves into the lives and ideas of three influential figures of the Renaissance era. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, the author explores the intellectual and spiritual journeys of John Colet, Erasmus, and Thomas More, as they navigate the complex landscape of religious reform, humanism, and political turmoil. From their early encounters at Oxford University to their individual quests for truth and justice, this captivating narrative sheds light on the profound impact these reformers had on their society and the lasting legacy they left behind.

Genres: Biography, History, Religious Studies

Themes: Religious reform, Humanism, Intellectual pursuits, Political and social change
