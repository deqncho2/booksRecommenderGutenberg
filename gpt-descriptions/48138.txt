Synopsis: In the final volume of 'The Complete Works in Philosophy, Politics and Morals of the late Dr. Benjamin Franklin,' readers are treated to a collection of thought-provoking essays and letters that delve into the depths of human nature, society, and governance. From exploring the intricacies of personal virtue to offering astute observations on the balance of power, Franklin's writings continue to captivate and challenge readers, even centuries after his passing. This volume serves as a testament to Franklin's intellectual prowess and enduring relevance, leaving readers eager to uncover the wisdom contained within its pages.

Genres: Philosophy, Political Science, Essays

Themes: Human nature, Virtue, Society, Governance, Power
