Synopsis: In 'The Panama Canal Conflict between Great Britain and the United States of America: A Study', Oppenheim delves into the fascinating historical dispute between two world powers over the construction of the Panama Canal. Through meticulous research and analysis, Oppenheim uncovers the intricate web of political maneuvering, economic interests, and strategic ambitions that led to this intense conflict. As the tension escalates, readers will be captivated by the high-stakes negotiations, secret alliances, and unexpected twists that shaped the outcome of this pivotal event in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, International relations

Themes: Imperialism, Nationalism, Diplomacy, Power struggle
