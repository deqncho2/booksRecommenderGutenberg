Synopsis: 

In 'New York Times Current History: The European War, Vol 2, No. 1, April, 1915: April-September, 1915', readers are transported to the tumultuous period of the First World War, specifically the months between April and September 1915. This volume delves into the intricate web of events, battles, and political developments that shaped the war during this crucial time. From the trenches of the Western Front to the diplomatic negotiations behind closed doors, this collection of articles from The New York Times provides a comprehensive and gripping account of the war's progression.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War journalism, Periodical

Themes: World War I, International diplomacy, Military strategy, Sociopolitical impact
