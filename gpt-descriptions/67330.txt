Synopsis: In 'A History of Magic and Experimental Science, Volume 2', Lynn Thorndike takes readers on a captivating journey through the first thirteen centuries of our era, exploring the fascinating world of magic and experimental science. From the ancient civilizations of Egypt and Greece to the rise of Christianity and the Islamic Golden Age, this volume delves into the rich tapestry of human knowledge and the quest for understanding the mysteries of the universe. Thorndike's meticulous research and engaging storytelling bring to life the remarkable individuals, groundbreaking discoveries, and profound ideas that shaped the course of history. Prepare to be enthralled by the wonders and complexities of the past as you uncover the secrets of magic and experimental science.

Genres: History, Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Human curiosity, Intellectual progress, Cultural exchange, Religious influence
