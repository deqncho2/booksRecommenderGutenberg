Synopsis: 

In "George Eliot; a Critical Study of Her Life, Writings and Philosophy," George Willis Cooke delves into the captivating world of one of the most influential Victorian novelists. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Cooke explores the life, works, and philosophical ideas of George Eliot, shedding light on the complexities of her literary genius. From her early struggles and unconventional choices to her profound exploration of human nature and morality, this critical study offers a fascinating glimpse into the mind of a remarkable writer. 

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Philosophy

Themes: Identity, Morality, Human Nature, Feminism
