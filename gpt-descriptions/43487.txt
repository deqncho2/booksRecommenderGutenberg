Synopsis: 

In 'The Depot for Prisoners of War at Norman Cross, Huntingdonshire. 1796 to 1816', Thomas Walker takes readers on a captivating journey into the little-known world of a prisoner of war camp during the late 18th and early 19th centuries. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Walker unveils the daily struggles, triumphs, and unexpected alliances that unfolded within the walls of the Norman Cross depot. From daring escape attempts to surprising acts of compassion, this book sheds light on the human spirit's resilience in the face of adversity. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Captivity, Survival, Human resilience, Interpersonal relationships
