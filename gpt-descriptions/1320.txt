Synopsis: 

In 'Criminal Psychology: A Manual for Judges, Practitioners, and Students' by Hans Gross, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the intricate world of criminal psychology. This comprehensive manual delves into the minds of criminals, exploring the motives, behaviors, and psychological factors that drive individuals to commit heinous acts. Through a combination of real-life case studies, expert analysis, and practical advice, Gross provides a fascinating insight into the inner workings of criminal minds, offering invaluable knowledge for judges, practitioners, and students alike. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Crime

Themes: Criminal behavior, Psychological profiling, Forensic psychology, Criminal justice system
