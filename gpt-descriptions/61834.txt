Synopsis: In 'The City of Dreadful Night', Rudyard Kipling takes readers on a haunting journey through a mysterious city that exists in the realm of nightmares. As the protagonist explores the dark and desolate streets, he encounters a series of enigmatic characters and experiences a sense of profound despair and isolation. Through vivid descriptions and poetic language, Kipling paints a chilling portrait of a city that seems to embody the darkest aspects of human existence. This atmospheric and thought-provoking work delves into the depths of the human psyche, exploring themes of existentialism, alienation, and the search for meaning in a seemingly indifferent world.

Genres: Gothic fiction, psychological fiction, poetry

Themes: Existentialism, alienation, despair, the human condition
