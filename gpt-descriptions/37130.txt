Synopsis: 
In 'Sheffield and its Environs 13th to the 17th century: A descriptive catalogue of land charters and other documents forming the Brooke Taylor collection,' T. Walter Hall takes readers on a captivating journey through the historical documents that shed light on the development of Sheffield and its surrounding areas. From land charters to personal letters, this descriptive catalogue unveils the fascinating stories and intricate connections that shaped the region over a span of four centuries. As readers delve into the Brooke Taylor collection, they will uncover the secrets, conflicts, and triumphs of the people who inhabited this vibrant landscape.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, archival research, local history

Themes: Local history, land ownership and development, social and economic changes, personal narratives
