Synopsis: 
'From October to Brest-Litovsk' by Leon Trotsky takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events that unfolded in Russia during the early days of the Bolshevik Revolution. As a key figure in the revolution, Trotsky provides a firsthand account of the intense negotiations and power struggles that took place during the peace talks with Germany. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Trotsky offers a unique perspective on the challenges faced by the newly formed Soviet government and the high-stakes decisions that shaped the course of history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political memoir, War literature

Themes: Revolution, Power dynamics, Diplomacy, War and peace
