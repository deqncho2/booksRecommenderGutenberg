Synopsis: In "The Russo-Japanese Conflict: Its Causes and Issues," Asakawa, Kan'ichi delves into the fascinating and often overlooked history of the Russo-Japanese War. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, the author uncovers the complex web of political, economic, and territorial factors that led to this significant conflict. From the clash of empires to the clash of cultures, this book offers a gripping account of the events that shaped the early 20th century.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, International relations

Themes: Imperialism, Nationalism, Diplomacy, Power dynamics
