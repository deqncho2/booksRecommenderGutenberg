Synopsis: In "Three Addresses to Girls at School," Wilson, J. M. delivers a series of captivating and thought-provoking speeches that challenge the traditional expectations placed upon young girls. Through his engaging anecdotes and insightful advice, Wilson encourages the girls to embrace their individuality, pursue their passions, and challenge societal norms. As he delves into topics such as education, self-expression, and the importance of character, Wilson inspires the girls to break free from the constraints of society and strive for greatness. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Self-help, Education

Themes: Individuality, Empowerment, Education, Self-expression
