Synopsis: In "Washington and His Comrades in Arms: A Chronicle of the War of Independence," George McKinnon Wrong takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of the American Revolution. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Wrong delves into the lives of George Washington and his fellow soldiers, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and the bonds that formed on the battlefield. From the early battles of Lexington and Concord to the decisive moments at Yorktown, this chronicle paints a vivid picture of the sacrifices made by these brave men and the pivotal role they played in shaping the destiny of a nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Military history

Themes: Leadership, Patriotism, Brotherhood, Sacrifice
