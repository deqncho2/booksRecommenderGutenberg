Synopsis: 
In 'The English Utilitarians, Volume 1', Stephen, Leslie takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives and ideas of some of the most influential thinkers of the 19th century. From Jeremy Bentham's radical utilitarianism to John Stuart Mill's nuanced philosophy, this book delves into the intellectual and social landscape of the time, exploring the clash between tradition and progress, individualism and social welfare. As Stephen weaves together biographical details and philosophical arguments, readers are invited to ponder the profound impact these utilitarian thinkers had on shaping modern society.

Genres: Biography, Philosophy, History

Themes: Utilitarianism, Social reform, Individualism, Progress
