Synopsis: In "The Unlit Lamp: A Study in Inter-actions," Elisabeth Sanxay Holding weaves a captivating tale of a young woman's journey towards self-discovery and independence. Set in the early 20th century, the story follows Evelyn Gower, a sheltered and obedient daughter who finds herself at a crossroads when her father's sudden death leaves her with limited options. As Evelyn navigates the complexities of love, marriage, and societal expectations, she must confront the choices that will shape her future. With a cast of intriguing characters and unexpected twists, this novel explores the delicate balance between duty and personal fulfillment.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Self-discovery, Independence, Love and Marriage, Societal Expectations
