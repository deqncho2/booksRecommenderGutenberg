Synopsis:
In 'Truth [Vérité]' by Émile Zola, we are introduced to a young woman named Thérèse Raquin, who finds herself trapped in a loveless marriage. Desperate for passion and excitement, she embarks on a dangerous affair that sets off a chain of events with devastating consequences. As secrets unravel and guilt consumes the characters, the story delves into the depths of human desire, betrayal, and the haunting power of truth.

Genres:
Realism, Psychological Fiction, Tragedy

Themes:
Passion, Betrayal, Guilt, Consequences
