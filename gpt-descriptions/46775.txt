Synopsis: In 'Tea Drinking in 18th-Century America: Its Etiquette and Equipage', Roth, Rodris takes readers on a fascinating journey into the world of tea drinking in 18th-century America. Exploring the etiquette and equipage surrounding this beloved beverage, the author delves into the social, cultural, and historical aspects of tea consumption during this time period. From the elaborate tea sets and accessories to the intricate rituals and customs, this work provides a comprehensive understanding of the significance of tea in American society. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Roth, Rodris paints a vivid picture of a time when tea was not just a drink, but a symbol of refinement, status, and social interaction.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural studies

Themes: Tea culture, Social customs, Material culture, American history
