Synopsis: 
In 'The Apology' by Xenophon, Socrates finds himself on trial for his life, accused of corrupting the youth and impiety. As he defends himself against these charges, Socrates employs his renowned Socratic method, engaging in thought-provoking dialogues with his accusers and challenging their beliefs. Through his eloquent arguments and unwavering commitment to truth, Socrates reveals the depths of his wisdom and the true nature of his character.

Genres: Philosophy, Biography

Themes: Wisdom, Justice, Integrity
