Synopsis: In 'Small Horses in Warfare' by Sir Walter Gilbey, a captivating tale unfolds as the author delves into the fascinating history of the often overlooked role of small horses in warfare. From ancient civilizations to modern conflicts, Gilbey explores the remarkable contributions these diminutive equines have made on the battlefield. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, the author sheds light on the bravery, agility, and resilience of these unsung heroes, revealing a hidden aspect of military history that will leave readers in awe.

Genres: Non-fiction, Military History, Animal Studies

Themes: Heroism, Military Strategy, Animal Welfare
