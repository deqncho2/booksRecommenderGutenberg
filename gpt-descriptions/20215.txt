Synopsis: 
In "An Onlooker in France 1917-1919," Sir William Orpen takes us on a captivating journey through his experiences as an artist and observer during the tumultuous years of World War I. Through his vivid and poignant descriptions, Orpen provides a unique perspective on the war, capturing the devastation, heroism, and resilience of the people he encounters. From the trenches to the bustling streets of Paris, Orpen's keen eye and artistic sensibility bring to life the human stories that unfolded amidst the chaos of war.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Art

Themes: War and its impact on society, Human resilience and endurance, Art as a means of expression
