Synopsis: In 'The South African Question' by Olive Schreiner, a young woman named Lyndall embarks on a journey of self-discovery in the complex and turbulent landscape of South Africa. As she navigates through societal expectations, political unrest, and personal relationships, Lyndall's quest for freedom and autonomy becomes intertwined with the larger struggle for justice and equality. With vivid descriptions and thought-provoking insights, Schreiner's novel explores the intricate web of power dynamics and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Identity, Gender Roles, Colonialism, Social Justice
