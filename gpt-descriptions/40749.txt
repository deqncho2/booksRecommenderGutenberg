Synopsis: In 'The Civil War in America: Fuller's Modern Age, August 1861' by Russell, William Howard, Sir, readers are transported to the heart of the American Civil War. Through the eyes of a seasoned journalist, the story unfolds with gripping intensity as the author provides a firsthand account of the events that took place in August 1861. From the battlefields to the political maneuverings, Russell's narrative captures the chaos, bravery, and human drama that defined this pivotal moment in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War journalism, Biography

Themes: War and conflict, Politics and power, Human resilience
