Synopsis: In 'Lectures on the Philosophy and Practice of Slavery', William A. Smith delves into the complex and controversial institution of domestic slavery in the United States. Through a series of thought-provoking lectures, Smith explores the philosophy behind slavery and the duties that masters have towards their slaves. With a critical and analytical approach, the author sheds light on the intricate dynamics of this deeply ingrained social system, challenging readers to question their own beliefs and assumptions.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Slavery, Ethics, Power dynamics, Social justice
