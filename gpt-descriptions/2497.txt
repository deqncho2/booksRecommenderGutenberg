Synopsis:
'Put Yourself in His Place' by Charles Reade is a gripping and suspenseful novel that follows the life of Henry Little, a young engineer who becomes entangled in a web of deceit and corruption. When a mysterious accident occurs at the local factory, Henry finds himself at the center of a dangerous conspiracy that threatens not only his own life but also the lives of those he holds dear. As he delves deeper into the dark underbelly of the town, Henry must navigate treacherous alliances, unexpected betrayals, and a love that could either save him or destroy him. With its intricate plot twists and morally complex characters, this thrilling tale will keep readers on the edge of their seats until the very last page.

Genres: Mystery, Suspense, Historical Fiction

Themes: Deception, Corruption, Love and Loyalty
