Synopsis: 
In the land of the lion and sun, or, modern Persia: Being experiences of life in Persia from 1866 to 1881 takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and enigmatic world of Persia during the late 19th century. Through the eyes of the author, Charles James Wills, we are immersed in a rich tapestry of cultural encounters, political intrigue, and personal reflections. From the bustling bazaars of Tehran to the majestic palaces of Isfahan, Wills paints a vivid picture of a country caught between tradition and modernity, where ancient customs clash with the winds of change. As he navigates the complexities of Persian society, Wills encounters a cast of fascinating characters, each with their own secrets and desires. With its blend of adventure, romance, and historical insight, this memoir offers a captivating glimpse into a world long gone, yet still resonating with echoes of the past.

Genres: Memoir, Travelogue, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Cultural exploration, Political dynamics, Tradition versus modernity
