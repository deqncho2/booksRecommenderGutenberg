Synopsis: In "Memoir of Roger Williams, the Founder of the State of Rhode-Island," James D. Knowles delves into the captivating life of Roger Williams, a man whose radical ideas and unwavering determination shaped the foundation of Rhode Island. From his early days as a Puritan minister to his eventual exile and establishment of a new colony, Williams's journey is one of religious freedom, political intrigue, and personal resilience. Knowles skillfully weaves together historical accounts, personal letters, and anecdotes to paint a vivid portrait of this enigmatic figure, shedding light on the lesser-known aspects of his life and the lasting impact he had on American history.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Religious freedom, Political dissent, Colonial America
