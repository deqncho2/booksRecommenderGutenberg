Synopsis: 
'Between the Twilights: Being studies of Indian women by one of themselves' takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives of Indian women, as seen through the eyes of Cornelia Sorabji. Through a series of intimate and thought-provoking narratives, Sorabji delves into the complexities of Indian society, shedding light on the struggles, triumphs, and untold stories of women from various walks of life. From the bustling streets of Mumbai to the serene villages of Rajasthan, this collection of stories offers a unique and authentic perspective on the diverse experiences of Indian women. As Sorabji uncovers the layers of their lives, she challenges societal norms and explores the themes of identity, empowerment, and the pursuit of justice.

Genres: 
Biography, Cultural Studies, Feminist Literature

Themes: 
Identity, Empowerment, Social Justice
