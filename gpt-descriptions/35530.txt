Synopsis: In "Fundamental Peace Ideas including The Westphalian Peace Treaty (1648) and The League Of Nations (1919): in connection with International Psychology and Revolutions," Arthur MacDonald delves into the fascinating world of international relations, psychology, and revolutions. Exploring the historical significance of the Westphalian Peace Treaty and the League of Nations, MacDonald uncovers the intricate connections between these pivotal events and the human psyche. Through a thought-provoking analysis, he unveils the profound impact of these peace initiatives on the global stage, shedding light on the intricate interplay between power, diplomacy, and the human desire for harmony. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Psychology

Themes: International relations, Peace treaties, Diplomacy, Human psychology, Revolutions
