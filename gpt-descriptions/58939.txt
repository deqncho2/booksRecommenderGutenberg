Synopsis: In "The Dolphin in History," Lilly and Montagu take us on a captivating journey through time, exploring the fascinating relationship between humans and dolphins. From ancient civilizations to modern-day encounters, they uncover the profound impact these intelligent creatures have had on our history, culture, and even our understanding of ourselves. Through captivating anecdotes and meticulous research, the authors reveal the deep connections between humans and dolphins, shedding light on the mysteries that surround these enigmatic creatures. Prepare to be enthralled by the captivating tales of dolphin-human interactions that span centuries and continents.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Natural History

Themes: Animal-human relationships, Cultural significance, Evolutionary biology
