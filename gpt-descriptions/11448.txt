Synopsis: 
In 'Cicero' by W. Lucas Collins, readers are transported to ancient Rome, where they follow the captivating life of one of history's most influential figures. This gripping historical novel delves into the political intrigue, power struggles, and personal triumphs of Cicero, a brilliant orator and statesman. From his humble beginnings to his rise as a prominent lawyer and politician, the story unravels the complexities of Cicero's character and the challenges he faces in a society filled with corruption and treachery. As the narrative unfolds, readers are immersed in a world of ambition, loyalty, and betrayal, where Cicero's wit and intelligence become his greatest weapons. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biographical Fiction, Political Drama

Themes: Ambition, Power, Loyalty, Betrayal, Corruption
