Synopsis: 
In 'The Man of Feeling' by Henry Mackenzie, we are introduced to Harley, a sensitive and compassionate young man who possesses an uncanny ability to empathize with others. As he navigates through the complexities of society, Harley finds himself drawn to individuals from all walks of life, from the destitute to the aristocracy. Through his encounters, he experiences a range of emotions, from love and friendship to heartbreak and despair. As the story unfolds, we witness Harley's journey of self-discovery and the profound impact he has on those around him.

Genres: 
Romance, Bildungsroman, Sentimental novel

Themes: 
Empathy, Human connection, Morality, Social class
