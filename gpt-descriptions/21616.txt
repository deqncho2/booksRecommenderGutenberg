Synopsis: In "The Ape, the Idiot & Other People" by W. C. Morrow, a series of interconnected stories takes readers on a journey through the bizarre and unexpected. From a man who transforms into an ape to a village plagued by an idiot, each tale is filled with dark humor, strange occurrences, and thought-provoking twists. As the characters navigate their peculiar circumstances, they grapple with questions of identity, morality, and the blurred lines between reality and fantasy. Prepare to be captivated by Morrow's imaginative storytelling and the peculiar world he creates.

Genres: Fiction, Fantasy, Dark Comedy

Themes: Identity, Morality, Reality vs. Fantasy
