Synopsis: 
In 'The Iron Ration: Three Years in Warring Central Europe', George Abel Schreiner takes us on a gripping journey through the tumultuous years of World War I. As a young American journalist stationed in Central Europe, Schreiner provides a firsthand account of the horrors and triumphs he witnesses on the front lines. From the trenches of the Western Front to the war-torn cities of Germany and Austria-Hungary, he paints a vivid picture of the devastating impact of war on both soldiers and civilians. Amidst the chaos, Schreiner also delves into the complexities of international politics and the human spirit's resilience in the face of unimaginable adversity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War memoir

Themes: War and its consequences, Human resilience, International politics
