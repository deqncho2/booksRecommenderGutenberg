Synopsis: In "Physics and Politics," Walter Bagehot delves into the intriguing intersection of science and politics, exploring the application of the principles of "natural selection" and "inheritance" to political society. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Bagehot uncovers the hidden dynamics that shape political systems and their evolution. With captivating insights and compelling arguments, he challenges conventional wisdom and offers a fresh perspective on the intricate relationship between science and politics.

Genres: Political science, Social commentary, Philosophy

Themes: Evolutionary theory, Political systems, Social order
