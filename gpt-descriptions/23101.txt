Synopsis: In "Three Thousand Years of Mental Healing," George Barton Cutten takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of mental healing practices. From ancient civilizations to modern times, this thought-provoking exploration delves into the fascinating evolution of mental health treatments and the individuals who shaped them. Discover the secrets behind ancient rituals, the power of faith healing, and the emergence of psychoanalysis. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, Cutten sheds light on the enduring quest for mental well-being throughout the ages.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Psychology

Themes: Mental health, Healing practices, Historical development
