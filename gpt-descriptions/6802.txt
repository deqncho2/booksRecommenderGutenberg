Synopsis: In 'White Slaves; or, the Oppression of the Worthy Poor', Louis Albert Banks takes readers on a gripping journey through the dark underbelly of society, where the lives of the deserving but destitute are tragically entangled in a web of exploitation and injustice. As the story unfolds, we follow the struggles and triumphs of a group of individuals who find themselves caught in a system that values profit over compassion. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, Banks sheds light on the harsh realities faced by the worthy poor, leaving readers questioning the true meaning of justice and the power of empathy.

Genres: Social realism, Historical fiction, Drama

Themes: Social inequality, Poverty, Exploitation, Justice, Empathy
