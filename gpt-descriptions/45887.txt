Synopsis: 

In 'Homes and Haunts of the Most Eminent British Poets, Vol. 2 (of 2)', William Howitt takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives and dwellings of some of the greatest British poets. From the enchanting landscapes of Wordsworth's Lake District to the bustling streets of London where Keats found inspiration, this volume unveils the intimate spaces that shaped the creative genius of these literary icons. With vivid descriptions and historical anecdotes, Howitt paints a vivid picture of the places where these poets lived, loved, and penned their immortal verses. 

Genres: Biography, Travel, Literary Criticism

Themes: Literary heritage, Creative inspiration, Historical significance
