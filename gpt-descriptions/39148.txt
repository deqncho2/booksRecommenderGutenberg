Synopsis: 

In 'How Justice Grew: Virginia Counties, An Abstract of Their Formation', Martha W. Hiden takes readers on a captivating journey through the historical development of Virginia counties. From the earliest settlements to the present day, Hiden delves into the intricate details of how these counties were formed, exploring the fascinating stories and events that shaped their boundaries and governance. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, the author unveils the complex tapestry of Virginia's county system, shedding light on the political, social, and cultural forces that influenced its growth. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Genealogy, Local history

Themes: County formation, Governance, Historical development
