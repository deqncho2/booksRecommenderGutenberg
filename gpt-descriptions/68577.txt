Synopsis: In "The Mother" by Pearl S. Buck, a young Chinese woman named Kwei-lan finds herself torn between tradition and her own desires as she navigates the complexities of marriage and motherhood. Set in early 20th century China, Kwei-lan's journey is filled with unexpected twists and turns that challenge her beliefs and force her to confront the limitations placed upon women in her society. As she grapples with the expectations of her family and the changing world around her, Kwei-lan must find the strength to forge her own path and redefine what it means to be a mother.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Cultural Fiction, Women's Fiction

Themes: Tradition vs. Modernity, Gender Roles, Identity, Family Dynamics
