Synopsis: 

"The Dawn of Modern Medicine" takes readers on a captivating journey through the revival of the science and art of medicine in Western Europe during the latter half of the eighteenth century and the first part of the nineteenth. Albert H. Buck delves into the fascinating historical events, breakthroughs, and individuals that shaped this transformative period in medical history. From the discovery of new treatments and surgical techniques to the emergence of medical institutions and the pioneers who challenged traditional beliefs, this book offers a riveting account of the remarkable advancements that laid the foundation for modern medicine.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Medical history

Themes: Medical advancements, Scientific revolution, Historical progress
