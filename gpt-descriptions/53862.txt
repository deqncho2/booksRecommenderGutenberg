Synopsis: 

In "Loughton, Essex: A brief account of the Manor and Parish," William Chapman Waller takes readers on a captivating journey through the history and secrets of Loughton, a charming town in Essex. From its humble beginnings as a medieval manor to its transformation into a thriving parish, Waller delves into the fascinating tales of the people, events, and landmarks that have shaped Loughton's identity over the centuries. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the author uncovers hidden stories, uncanny legends, and unexpected connections that will leave readers eager to explore this enchanting corner of England.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Local history, Travel

Themes: Local heritage, Community development, Historical preservation
