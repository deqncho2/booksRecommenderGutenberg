Synopsis: In 'A Story of the Red Cross; Glimpses of Field Work', Clara Barton takes us on a riveting journey through her experiences as a nurse during the American Civil War. From the chaos of the battlefield to the harrowing conditions of makeshift hospitals, Barton's firsthand accounts provide a compelling and emotional insight into the life-saving work of the Red Cross. With vivid descriptions and heartfelt anecdotes, this book offers a captivating glimpse into the courage, resilience, and compassion of those who dedicate their lives to helping others in times of crisis.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Humanitarianism, War, Resilience, Compassion
