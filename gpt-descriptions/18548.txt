Synopsis: In the Transactions of the American Society of Civil Engineers, vol. LXVIII, Sept. 1910, Charles M. Jacobs presents an intriguing paper titled "The New York Tunnel Extension of the Pennsylvania Railroad, The North River Division." This captivating work delves into the ambitious project of extending the Pennsylvania Railroad's tunnel system in New York City. With meticulous detail and technical expertise, Jacobs explores the challenges faced by engineers in constructing this groundbreaking transportation infrastructure. From the complexities of tunneling under the Hudson River to the innovative engineering solutions employed, this paper offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of civil engineering at the turn of the 20th century.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, History

Themes: Infrastructure development, Technological innovation, Engineering challenges
