Synopsis: In 'Studies in Civics' by J.T. McCleary, a captivating exploration of society and government unfolds through a series of thought-provoking essays. From dissecting the intricacies of democracy to examining the role of citizens in shaping their communities, McCleary delves into the fundamental principles that underpin our modern civilization. With a keen eye for detail and a knack for weaving historical anecdotes, this book offers a fresh perspective on the dynamics of power, the importance of civic engagement, and the delicate balance between individual rights and collective responsibilities.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, Social Commentary

Themes: Democracy, Citizenship, Government, Civic Engagement, Individual Rights, Collective Responsibilities
