Synopsis: 

In 'Insanity: Its Causes and Prevention', Henry Putnam Stearns delves into the mysterious realm of the human mind, exploring the enigmatic causes behind insanity and offering invaluable insights into its prevention. Through a captivating blend of scientific research, historical analysis, and personal anecdotes, Stearns takes readers on a thought-provoking journey that challenges conventional wisdom and sheds light on the complexities of mental health. From examining the impact of societal pressures to unraveling the intricate relationship between genetics and environment, this book uncovers the multifaceted factors that contribute to the onset of insanity. With a compassionate and empathetic approach, Stearns also provides practical strategies and interventions for individuals and communities to combat this pervasive issue. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Medical

Themes: Mental health, Causes of insanity, Prevention strategies
