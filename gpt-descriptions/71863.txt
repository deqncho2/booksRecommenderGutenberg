Synopsis:
In "Nigeria: Its Peoples and Its Problems," Edmund Dene Morel takes readers on a captivating journey through the complexities of Nigeria, a country rich in diversity and plagued by numerous challenges. From the vibrant cultures and traditions of its people to the deep-rooted issues that have hindered its progress, Morel delves into the heart of Nigeria's struggles and triumphs. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, he explores the historical, political, and social factors that have shaped the nation, shedding light on the intricate tapestry of Nigeria's past and present. This thought-provoking exploration offers a compelling glimpse into the multifaceted nature of Nigeria and its ongoing quest for stability and prosperity.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Politics

Themes: Cultural diversity, Colonial legacy, Political instability, Socio-economic challenges
