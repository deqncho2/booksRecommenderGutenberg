Synopsis:
In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume II, Arkansas Narratives, Part 2', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a collection of powerful and poignant firsthand accounts from former slaves in Arkansas. These narratives offer a unique and intimate glimpse into the lives of those who endured the horrors of slavery, sharing their struggles, triumphs, and the resilience of the human spirit. From tales of escape and resistance to stories of love and family, this volume paints a vivid picture of the complex and often brutal world of slavery in Arkansas. 

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes:
Slavery, Resistance, Family, Identity, Freedom
