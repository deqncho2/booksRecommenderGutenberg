Synopsis:
'Rossa's Recollections, 1838 to 1898: Childhood, boyhood, manhood; customs, habits and manners of the Irish people; Erinach and Sassenach; Catholic and protestant; Englishman and Irishman; English religion; Irish plunder; social life and prison life; the Fenian movement; Travels in Ireland, England, Scotland and America' is a memoir written by O'Donovan Rossa, Jeremiah. In this captivating account, Rossa takes us on a journey through his life, from his early years in Ireland to his travels across various countries. He delves into the customs, habits, and manners of the Irish people, exploring the complex dynamics between the Erinach and Sassenach, Catholic and Protestant, and Englishman and Irishman. Rossa also sheds light on the influence of English religion and the impact of Irish plunder. He provides vivid descriptions of social life and prison life, offering a unique perspective on the Fenian movement and its significance. Through his travels in Ireland, England, Scotland, and America, Rossa offers a rich tapestry of experiences, shedding light on the cultural, social, and political landscapes of the time.

Genres:
Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Travelogue

Themes:
Identity, Nationalism, Religion, Social Inequality, Political Activism
