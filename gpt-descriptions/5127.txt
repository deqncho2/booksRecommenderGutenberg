Synopsis: In "Shakespeare, Bacon, and the Great Unknown" by Lang, Andrew, a captivating exploration unfolds as the author delves into the mysterious question of who truly authored the works attributed to William Shakespeare. With meticulous research and thought-provoking analysis, Lang presents a compelling case that challenges the conventional narrative surrounding the Bard's identity. As he unravels the enigma, the reader is taken on a thrilling journey through history, literature, and the secrets of the Elizabethan era. This thought-provoking investigation will leave readers questioning everything they thought they knew about Shakespeare and the power of hidden truths.

Genres: Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, Historical Analysis

Themes: Authorship, Identity, Conspiracy, Literary Legacy
