Synopsis: In 'The Modern Ku Klux Klan', author Henry P. Fry delves into the secretive world of the infamous white supremacist organization, exploring its origins, ideologies, and the individuals who perpetuate its existence. Through extensive research and interviews, Fry uncovers the hidden truths behind the Klan's resurgence in the 20th century, shedding light on the motivations and tactics employed by its members. From the burning crosses to the hooded robes, this book offers a chilling and thought-provoking examination of a dark chapter in American history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Sciences

Themes: Racism, Extremism, Identity, Power dynamics
