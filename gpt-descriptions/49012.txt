Synopsis: In "The Postal Power of Congress: A Study in Constitutional Expansion," Rogers Lindsay delves into the fascinating history of the United States Postal Service and its profound impact on the nation's development. From its humble beginnings to its pivotal role in shaping communication and commerce, Lindsay explores the untold stories behind the postal system's growth and the constitutional debates it sparked. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this book uncovers the hidden power and influence of the postal service, shedding light on a crucial aspect of American history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Constitutional interpretation, Government power, Communication and commerce
