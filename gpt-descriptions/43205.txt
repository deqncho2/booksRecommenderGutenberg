Synopsis: In 'Is the Devil a Myth?' by C. F. Wimberly, a renowned theologian embarks on a thought-provoking exploration of the age-old question: does the Devil truly exist? Through a captivating blend of historical analysis, philosophical inquiry, and personal anecdotes, Wimberly challenges conventional beliefs and invites readers to reconsider their understanding of good and evil. As he delves into the origins of the Devil myth and its impact on society, the author raises profound questions about the nature of temptation, the power of human agency, and the complexities of moral responsibility. This thought-provoking journey will leave readers questioning their own beliefs and pondering the eternal battle between light and darkness.

Genres: Theology, Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Good vs. Evil, Mythology, Morality, Human Agency
