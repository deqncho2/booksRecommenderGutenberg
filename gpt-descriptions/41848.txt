Synopsis: 
'With the Judæans in the Palestine Campaign' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of World War I, as seen through the eyes of the author, J. H. Patterson. As a commander of the Zion Mule Corps, Patterson provides a firsthand account of the trials and triumphs experienced by the Judæans, a group of Jewish volunteers who played a crucial role in the Palestine Campaign. From the scorching deserts to the treacherous battlefields, this gripping narrative immerses readers in the harrowing realities of war, while also shedding light on the resilience, bravery, and unwavering spirit of the Judæans.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War memoir

Themes: World War I, Jewish identity, Courage and sacrifice
