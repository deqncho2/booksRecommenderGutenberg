Synopsis: 

'Crusoe's Island: A Ramble in the Footsteps of Alexander Selkirk: With Sketches of Adventure in California and Washoe' follows the author, J. Ross Browne, as he embarks on a journey to explore the remote island where Alexander Selkirk, the inspiration for Robinson Crusoe, was marooned. As Browne delves into the history and legends surrounding Selkirk's time on the island, he also shares his own thrilling adventures in California and Washoe. From encounters with Native Americans to treacherous landscapes, Browne's narrative takes readers on a captivating exploration of both the past and the present.

Genres: Travelogue, Adventure, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Exploration, Survival, History, Identity
