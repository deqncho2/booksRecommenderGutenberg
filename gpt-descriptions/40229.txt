Synopsis: 

In "History of Randolph-Macon College, Virginia: The Oldest Incorporated Methodist College in America," Richard Irby takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and storied past of Randolph-Macon College. From its humble beginnings as a small Methodist academy to its evolution into a prestigious institution, this book unveils the fascinating history behind one of America's oldest Methodist colleges. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Irby explores the challenges, triumphs, and transformative moments that have shaped the college over the centuries. From the influence of prominent Methodist leaders to the impact of the Civil War and the struggles for racial integration, this book offers a comprehensive and compelling account of Randolph-Macon College's enduring legacy.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Education

Themes: Methodist heritage, Higher education, Social and cultural transformation
