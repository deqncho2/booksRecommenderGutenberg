Synopsis: 
'Aspects of the Novel' by E. M. Forster is a captivating exploration of the art of novel writing. Through a series of insightful and thought-provoking essays, Forster delves into the various elements that make a novel successful, including plot, characters, and narrative style. Drawing on examples from classic literature, he offers both practical advice and profound observations on the power of storytelling. With his signature wit and wisdom, Forster takes readers on a journey through the inner workings of the novel, shedding light on its complexities and illuminating the magic that lies within its pages.

Genres: Literary criticism, non-fiction

Themes: The art of storytelling, the role of characters, the structure of a novel, the power of narrative, the relationship between author and reader.
