Synopsis: In 'Sea-Power and Other Studies', Sir Cyprian Bridge takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of naval history and strategy. From ancient times to the modern era, Bridge explores the pivotal role of sea power in shaping the course of nations and the outcome of wars. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, he delves into the triumphs and failures of naval commanders, the development of naval technology, and the strategic importance of controlling the seas. This collection of essays offers a compelling exploration of the enduring power and influence of navies throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Military

Themes: Naval warfare, Strategy, Power dynamics
