Synopsis: 
In 'Camp Life in the Woods and the Tricks of Trapping and Trap Making', author W. Hamilton Gibson takes readers on an exhilarating journey into the wilderness, where survival skills and the art of trapping become essential. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, Gibson shares his expertise on various trapping techniques and trap making, revealing the secrets of the trade. From constructing snares and deadfalls to understanding animal behavior, this book is a treasure trove of knowledge for both novice and experienced outdoorsmen. Prepare to be enthralled by the thrilling adventures and practical wisdom that await you in the pages of this captivating guide.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Outdoor and Survival, Nature

Themes: 
Wilderness survival, Trapping techniques, Nature appreciation
