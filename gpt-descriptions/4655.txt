Synopsis: In "The Seigneurs of Old Canada: A Chronicle of New World Feudalism," Munro takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of Canada's seigneurs. From the early days of French colonization to the struggles for power and influence, this chronicle unveils the intricate web of feudalism that shaped the destiny of the New World. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Munro paints a vivid picture of the seigneurs' lives, their relationships with the Indigenous peoples, and the conflicts that arose as the British Empire began to assert its dominance. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the tales of ambition, love, and betrayal that unfold against the backdrop of a rapidly changing continent.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Canadian history

Themes: Feudalism, Colonialism, Power dynamics, Cultural exchange, Identity
