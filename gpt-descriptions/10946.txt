Synopsis: 
In "Three Frenchmen in Bengal: The Commercial Ruin of the French Settlements in 1757," Samuel Charles Hill takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events that unfolded in the French settlements of Bengal during the year 1757. Through the eyes of three Frenchmen, we witness the rise of political intrigue, economic rivalry, and the impending doom that threatens to engulf the French presence in Bengal. As the story unfolds, the reader is drawn into a world of power struggles, betrayal, and the desperate fight for survival in a foreign land. Will these three Frenchmen be able to navigate the treacherous waters of colonial politics and save their settlements from ruin?

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Political Drama

Themes: Colonialism, Power Struggles, Betrayal, Survival
