Synopsis: 

In "Narrative of William W. Brown, a Fugitive Slave," William Wells Brown takes us on a gripping journey through his life as a slave and his daring escape to freedom. From his early years on a plantation in the South to his harrowing experiences as a fugitive, Brown's narrative is a powerful testament to the resilience of the human spirit and the quest for liberty. Through his vivid storytelling, he exposes the brutalities of slavery and the profound impact it has on individuals and communities. As Brown navigates the treacherous path to freedom, readers are drawn into a world of danger, suspense, and the unwavering determination to reclaim one's humanity.

Genres: Autobiography, Slave narrative, Historical non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Resilience, Injustice, Identity
