Synopsis: 

In 'Old Quebec, the city of Champlain', Emily P. Weaver takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting streets of Old Quebec, a city steeped in history and charm. From the cobblestone lanes to the towering fortifications, Weaver's vivid descriptions transport readers to a bygone era, where the echoes of French settlers and the spirit of Samuel de Champlain still linger. As the narrative unfolds, the reader becomes immersed in the rich tapestry of Quebec's past, discovering hidden gems and untold stories that bring the city to life. With each turn of the page, Weaver uncovers the secrets of Old Quebec, revealing a world of intrigue, romance, and adventure that will leave readers yearning to explore this captivating city for themselves.

Genres: Travel, History, Non-fiction

Themes: History, Culture, Exploration, Heritage
