Synopsis: In 'Sketches in the House: The Story of a Memorable Session (1893)', T. P. O'Connor takes readers on a captivating journey through the inner workings of the British Parliament during a particularly eventful session. Through a series of vivid and humorous sketches, O'Connor introduces us to a colorful cast of politicians, each with their own ambitions, rivalries, and secrets. As the session unfolds, tensions rise, alliances shift, and scandals threaten to unravel the delicate balance of power. With wit and insight, O'Connor offers a behind-the-scenes look at the political machinations and personal dramas that shape the destiny of a nation.

Genres: Political fiction, Satire, Historical fiction

Themes: Power and ambition, Political intrigue, Personal relationships, Society and class
