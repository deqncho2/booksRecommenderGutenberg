Synopsis: 
'Poems, 1914-1919' by Maurice Baring is a collection of poignant and evocative poems that capture the essence of the tumultuous years of World War I. Through his lyrical verses, Baring explores the profound impact of war on the human spirit, delving into themes of love, loss, sacrifice, and the search for meaning amidst chaos. From the trenches to the home front, these poems offer a glimpse into the emotional landscape of those who lived through one of the most devastating conflicts in history.

Genres: Poetry, War Literature

Themes: War, Love, Loss, Sacrifice, Human Spirit
