Synopsis: 

'From Capetown to Ladysmith: An Unfinished Record of the South African War' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of the South African War. Written by George Warrington Steevens, a renowned war correspondent, this book offers a firsthand account of the conflict, providing a unique perspective on the bravery, hardships, and complexities faced by both soldiers and civilians.

As Steevens travels from Capetown to Ladysmith, he encounters a diverse range of individuals, each with their own stories and struggles. From the frontlines to the heart of the war-torn country, the author vividly describes the landscapes, battles, and the impact of war on the people he encounters. With his keen observations and evocative storytelling, Steevens brings the war to life, immersing readers in the intensity and chaos of the South African War.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War journalism, Travelogue

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, Bravery and resilience in the face of adversity, The complexities of conflict and its consequences
