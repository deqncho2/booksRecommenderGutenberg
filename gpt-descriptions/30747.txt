Synopsis: 

Step back in time and explore the captivating world of George Washington's Alexandria, a bustling seaport in Virginia. In this enthralling historical account, Moore, Gay Montague takes readers on a journey through the vibrant streets and waterfront of Alexandria, where the young George Washington once walked. Discover the secrets and stories behind the city's rich history, from its role in the American Revolution to its transformation into a thriving commercial hub. With vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, this book paints a vivid picture of a bygone era, offering a glimpse into the life and times of one of America's most iconic figures.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Local History

Themes: American Revolution, Colonial America, George Washington, Seaport Life, Historical Preservation
