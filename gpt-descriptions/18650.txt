Synopsis: In 'The War and the Churches' by Joseph McCabe, a gripping exploration of the relationship between religion and war unfolds. Through meticulous research and thought-provoking analysis, McCabe delves into the complex role that churches have played in times of conflict. From the Crusades to World War I, he uncovers the motivations, actions, and consequences of religious institutions during times of war. With a keen eye for detail and a fearless approach to challenging long-held beliefs, McCabe presents a compelling narrative that will leave readers questioning the intersection of faith and violence.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Religious Studies

Themes: Religion and war, Church history, Ethics and morality
