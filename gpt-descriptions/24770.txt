Synopsis: 
In 'A Prisoner of Morro; Or, In the Hands of the Enemy' by Sinclair, Upton, we follow the gripping tale of a young American journalist who finds himself imprisoned in the notorious Morro Castle in Cuba during the Spanish-American War. As he navigates the harsh conditions and the constant threat of danger, he must rely on his wit and resourcefulness to survive. Along the way, he encounters a cast of intriguing characters, including fellow prisoners, sympathetic guards, and even a mysterious woman who may hold the key to his escape. With each twist and turn, the protagonist's determination to regain his freedom is tested, leading to a thrilling climax that will keep readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Adventure, Historical Fiction, War

Themes: Survival, Freedom, Resilience
