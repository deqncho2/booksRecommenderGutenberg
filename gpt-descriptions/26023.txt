Synopsis: In "The League of Nations and Its Problems: Three Lectures," L. Oppenheim delves into the complexities and challenges faced by the League of Nations, an international organization established after World War I. Through a series of insightful lectures, Oppenheim explores the League's formation, its role in maintaining peace, and the obstacles it encounters along the way. With a keen eye for detail and a thought-provoking analysis, Oppenheim sheds light on the inner workings of this ambitious endeavor, leaving readers captivated by the intricacies of international diplomacy.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, History

Themes: International cooperation, Peacekeeping, Diplomacy, Global governance
