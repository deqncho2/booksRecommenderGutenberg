Synopsis: In "The Saxons: A Drama of Christianity in the North," Edwin Davies Schoonmaker takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of the Saxon invasion of Britain. Set against the backdrop of the clash between paganism and Christianity, this gripping drama follows the lives of various characters as they navigate the complexities of faith, love, and power. As the Saxons and the Britons collide, alliances are tested, loyalties are questioned, and the fate of Christianity hangs in the balance. With its rich historical detail and compelling narrative, this book offers a riveting exploration of the human spirit and the enduring power of belief.

Genres: Historical fiction, Drama, Religious fiction

Themes: Clash of cultures, Faith and spirituality, Power and politics
