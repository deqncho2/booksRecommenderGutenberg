Synopsis: In "The Proper Limits of the Government's Interference with the Affairs of the East-India Company," John Dalrymple, Earl of Stair, delves into the complex relationship between the government and the East-India Company during a tumultuous period. Through a series of reflections, he explores the challenges faced by both entities and the consequences of their actions. With the backdrop of a distracted society, Stair offers thought-provoking insights into the proper boundaries of government interference and the implications for the East-India Company.

Genres: Political discourse, historical analysis, philosophical reflection

Themes: Government intervention, corporate governance, societal turmoil
