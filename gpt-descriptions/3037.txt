Synopsis: In "The Age of Big Business: A Chronicle of the Captains of Industry," Burton Jesse Hendrick takes readers on a captivating journey through the rise of American industrial giants. From the early days of the railroad and oil industries to the emergence of powerful tycoons like Andrew Carnegie and John D. Rockefeller, this book delves into the fascinating stories of these influential figures and the impact they had on shaping the nation's economy. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Hendrick explores the triumphs, controversies, and struggles faced by these captains of industry, offering a compelling narrative that sheds light on the complex dynamics of American capitalism during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Genres: History, Biography, Business

Themes: Industrialization, Capitalism, Entrepreneurship, Wealth and Power
