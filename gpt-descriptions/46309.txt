Synopsis: In the second part of his captivating memoirs, Charles Greville continues to provide an intimate and insightful account of the reign of Queen Victoria from 1837 to 1852. Through his unique perspective as a close observer and confidant of the royal family, Greville delves into the political intrigues, personal relationships, and societal changes that shaped this transformative era. From the Queen's tumultuous marriage to Prince Albert to the rise of the Chartists and the Great Exhibition of 1851, Greville's journal offers a vivid portrayal of the challenges and triumphs of Queen Victoria's reign.

Genres: Memoir, History, Biography

Themes: Monarchy, Politics, Society, Personal Relationships
