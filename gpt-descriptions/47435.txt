Synopsis: 

In "British Canals: Is their resuscitation practicable?" Edwin A. Pratt takes readers on a captivating exploration of the forgotten waterways that once played a vital role in shaping Britain's industrial landscape. With a keen eye for historical detail and a passion for preservation, Pratt delves into the challenges and possibilities of reviving these canals, offering a thought-provoking analysis of their potential impact on modern society. From the engineering marvels of the past to the potential for leisure and tourism in the present, this book presents a compelling case for the revival of these forgotten water highways.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Engineering

Themes: Industrial heritage, Conservation, Urban planning
