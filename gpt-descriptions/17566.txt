Synopsis: In 'The Shoulders of Atlas: A Novel' by Mary Eleanor Wilkins Freeman, a young woman named Ruth finds herself torn between duty and desire in a small New England town. As she navigates the complexities of love, family, and societal expectations, Ruth must confront the consequences of her choices and the limitations placed upon her by society. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this captivating novel explores the timeless struggle between personal freedom and societal constraints.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Romance

Themes: Love and Duty, Societal Expectations, Personal Freedom
