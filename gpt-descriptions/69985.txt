Synopsis: 

In 'Ilex cassine, the aboriginal North American tea: Its history, distribution, and use among the native North American Indians', Edwin M. Hale delves into the captivating world of a unique tea that has been an integral part of the indigenous North American culture for centuries. From its origins to its significance among the native tribes, this book uncovers the fascinating history, distribution, and usage of Ilex cassine, a tea that holds a special place in the hearts and traditions of the native North American Indians.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Indigenous culture, Traditional medicine, Native American history
