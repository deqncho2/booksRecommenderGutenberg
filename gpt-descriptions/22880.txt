Synopsis: 
In 'Bell's Cathedrals: A Short Account of Romsey Abbey: A Description of the Fabric and Notes on the History of the Convent of SS. Mary & Ethelfleda', Rev. Thomas Perkins takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and architectural marvels of Romsey Abbey. From its humble beginnings as a convent founded by two remarkable women, to its transformation into a magnificent place of worship, Perkins unveils the secrets and stories hidden within the abbey's walls. With vivid descriptions and insightful historical notes, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of those who lived and worshipped in Romsey Abbey throughout the centuries.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Architecture, Religious history

Themes: Women's history, Spirituality, Architectural heritage
