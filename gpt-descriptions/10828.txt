Synopsis: 
In 'Roman History, Books I-III' by Livy, readers are transported to the early days of Rome, where the city is still in its infancy and the destiny of a great empire is being shaped. Livy vividly recounts the legendary tales of Romulus and Remus, the founding of Rome, and the early struggles and triumphs of its people. From the thrilling battles against neighboring tribes to the internal conflicts within the city, Livy's narrative paints a captivating picture of the rise of Rome and the extraordinary individuals who played a pivotal role in its history.

Genres: Historical fiction, Epic, Biography

Themes: Power and ambition, Fate and destiny, Heroism and sacrifice
