Synopsis: 

In 'Negro Folk Rhymes: Wise and Otherwise: With a Study', Thomas Washington Talley delves into the rich and captivating world of African American folk rhymes. This collection of rhymes, passed down through generations, offers a glimpse into the cultural heritage and oral traditions of the African American community. Talley's meticulous study not only presents these rhymes in their original form but also provides insightful analysis and historical context, shedding light on their significance and meaning. From playful and humorous verses to poignant and thought-provoking ones, this book takes readers on a journey through the vibrant tapestry of African American folklore, offering a deeper understanding of the resilience, creativity, and wisdom embedded within these rhymes.

Genres: Folklore, Poetry, Cultural Studies
Themes: African American culture, Oral traditions, Heritage, Resilience, Wisdom
