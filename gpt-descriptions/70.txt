Synopsis: In "What Is Man? and Other Essays," Mark Twain delves into the complexities of human nature, exploring the depths of our thoughts, beliefs, and actions. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, Twain challenges conventional wisdom, questions societal norms, and offers his unique perspective on the human condition. From humorous anecdotes to profound philosophical musings, this collection invites readers to ponder the true essence of humanity and the mysteries that lie within.

Genres: Essay, Satire, Philosophy

Themes: Human nature, Morality, Society, Individualism
