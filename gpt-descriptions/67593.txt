Synopsis: In "Alkibiades, a tale of the Great Athenian War" by Charles Hamilton Bromby, readers are transported to ancient Athens during the tumultuous period of the Peloponnesian War. The story revolves around the enigmatic and charismatic figure of Alkibiades, a brilliant and ambitious Athenian general. As the war rages on, Alkibiades finds himself torn between loyalty to his city and his own personal ambitions, leading to a series of dramatic events that will shape the fate of Athens and its people.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War, Political Drama

Themes: Ambition, Loyalty, Betrayal, Power Struggles, Consequences of War
