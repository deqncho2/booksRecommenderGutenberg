Synopsis: 
In 'Trapped in 'Black Russia': Letters June-November 1915', Ruth Pierce takes us on a captivating journey through her personal letters, chronicling her experiences as an American woman trapped in war-torn Russia during the tumultuous period of June to November 1915. As she navigates the treacherous landscape of a country on the brink of revolution, Ruth's letters provide a vivid and intimate account of her struggles, fears, and unexpected encounters with both danger and compassion. Through her words, we witness the resilience and determination of a woman caught in the midst of chaos, as she grapples with the complexities of love, loss, and the pursuit of freedom.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Memoir, Epistolary

Themes: War and Conflict, Survival, Love and Relationships, Identity, Resilience
