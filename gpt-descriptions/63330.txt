Synopsis: In 'The Members of the Established Church Vindicated', Geary responds to a letter from Rev. John Alexander, addressing the controversial topic of Infant Education. Geary passionately defends the members of the established church against certain charges made by Alexander, shedding light on the importance of their role in shaping the education of young children. As the debate unfolds, Geary skillfully presents a compelling argument that challenges the assumptions and implications put forth by Alexander. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious, Education

Themes: Church, Education, Religious Authority, Controversy
