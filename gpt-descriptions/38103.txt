Synopsis: In "Trial of C. B. Reynolds For Blasphemy, at Morristown, N. J., May 1887: Defence," Robert Green Ingersoll takes us on a gripping journey through a controversial trial that challenges the boundaries of free speech and religious beliefs. Set in the late 19th century, this thought-provoking work explores the defense of a man accused of blasphemy, delving into the complexities of faith, reason, and the power of words. As Ingersoll presents his compelling arguments, the reader is left questioning the limits of religious tolerance and the importance of intellectual freedom.

Genres: Non-fiction, Legal Drama, Historical

Themes: Freedom of speech, Religious tolerance, Intellectual freedom
