Synopsis: In "Impressions of Theophrastus Such," George Eliot takes us on a captivating journey through the minds and lives of various characters, each with their own quirks and idiosyncrasies. Through a series of insightful and often humorous sketches, Eliot explores the complexities of human nature, delving into the depths of human behavior, social conventions, and the masks we wear in society. From the eccentric to the ordinary, the characters in this collection of essays offer a thought-provoking reflection on the intricacies of human existence.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Character Study

Themes: Human nature, Social conventions, Individuality, Hypocrisy
