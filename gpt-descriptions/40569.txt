Synopsis: In 'Tales of Mean Streets' by Arthur Morrison, the gritty streets of London come alive through a collection of interconnected stories that delve into the lives of the city's working-class inhabitants. From the struggles of poverty and crime to the complexities of love and loyalty, this gripping anthology takes readers on a journey through the dark underbelly of Victorian society. With vivid descriptions and unforgettable characters, Morrison paints a vivid portrait of a world where survival is a constant battle and hope can be found in the most unexpected places.

Genres: Fiction, Crime, Historical

Themes: Poverty, Crime, Love, Loyalty
