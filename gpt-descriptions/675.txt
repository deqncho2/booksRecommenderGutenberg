Synopsis: 
In "American Notes," Charles Dickens takes readers on a captivating journey across the Atlantic to explore the United States in the 19th century. Through his keen observations and witty anecdotes, Dickens provides a vivid and often humorous account of his experiences, encounters, and impressions of American society, culture, and politics. From bustling cities to the vast wilderness, from the bustling Mississippi River to the grandeur of Niagara Falls, Dickens paints a colorful picture of a rapidly changing nation. With his characteristic charm and sharp social commentary, he delves into the complexities of American life, shedding light on both its virtues and its flaws.

Genres: Travelogue, Non-fiction, Social commentary

Themes: Cultural differences, National identity, Social inequality
