Synopsis: 
'Anti-Suffrage Essays' presents a collection of thought-provoking and controversial essays that challenge the prevailing notions surrounding women's suffrage. Through a series of compelling arguments, the author delves into the complexities of the suffrage movement, exploring the potential consequences and questioning the necessity of granting women the right to vote. With a blend of historical analysis, social commentary, and philosophical insights, this collection offers a unique perspective on a pivotal moment in women's history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Commentary

Themes: Gender roles, Political rights, Feminism, Social change
