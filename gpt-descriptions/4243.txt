Synopsis: In "Sir Thomas More, or, Colloquies on the Progress and Prospects of Society," Robert Southey takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through a series of conversations with the renowned historical figure, Sir Thomas More. Set in the 16th century, the book explores various aspects of society, including politics, religion, education, and the pursuit of happiness. Through these engaging dialogues, Southey challenges conventional wisdom and offers insightful perspectives on the progress and future of society. As the conversations unfold, readers are invited to ponder the complexities of human nature and the potential for societal improvement. 

Genres: Historical fiction, philosophical fiction

Themes: Society and progress, politics and governance, religion and spirituality, education and knowledge
