Synopsis: 

In 'The Group Mind: A Sketch of the Principles of Collective Psychology: With Some Attempt to Apply Them to the Interpretation of National Life and Character', McDougall delves into the fascinating realm of collective psychology, exploring the intricate dynamics that shape the behavior and mindset of groups. Through insightful analysis and thought-provoking examples, McDougall offers a captivating exploration of how the collective mind influences national life and character. From the influence of social norms to the power of shared beliefs, this book sheds light on the hidden forces that shape our societies and the individuals within them.

Genres: Psychology, Sociology, Social Science

Themes: Collective psychology, Group dynamics, National identity, Social influence
