Synopsis: In "Browning and His Century," Helen A. Clarke delves into the life and times of the renowned poet Robert Browning, exploring the fascinating world he inhabited and the impact he had on the literary landscape of the 19th century. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Clarke unveils the complexities of Browning's personal life, his artistic evolution, and the societal changes that shaped his work. From his unconventional marriage to the influential friendships he forged, this biography offers a compelling portrait of a man who defied conventions and left an indelible mark on the world of poetry.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic expression, Love and relationships, Societal change
