Synopsis: 
Set in early 20th century Canada, "What a Young Wife Ought to Know" follows the journey of a young woman named Sophie, who finds herself navigating the complexities of marriage, motherhood, and societal expectations. As she grapples with the challenges of intimacy, contraception, and the consequences of her choices, Sophie's story unfolds with raw honesty and emotional depth. Through her experiences, the novel explores the profound impact of societal taboos and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: 
Historical fiction, Drama, Women's literature

Themes: 
Sexuality, Reproductive rights, Gender roles, Social norms
