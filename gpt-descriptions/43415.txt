Synopsis: 
'Insomnia; and Other Disorders of Sleep' by Lyman, Henry M. delves into the mysterious world of sleep disorders, exploring the haunting experiences of individuals plagued by insomnia and other nocturnal afflictions. Through a collection of captivating case studies and scientific research, the author unravels the complexities of these disorders, shedding light on the profound impact they have on the lives of those affected. From vivid nightmares to sleepwalking, the book takes readers on a fascinating journey through the enigmatic realm of sleep, offering both insight and empathy for those who suffer from these conditions.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical, Psychology

Themes: Sleep disorders, Mental health, Human experiences
