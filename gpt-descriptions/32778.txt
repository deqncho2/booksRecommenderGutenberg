Synopsis: In "A Pushcart at the Curb," Dos Passos takes us on a captivating journey through the lives of various characters in New York City. From struggling artists to ambitious businessmen, from immigrants seeking a better life to disillusioned socialites, the narrative weaves together their individual stories, hopes, and dreams against the backdrop of a rapidly changing society. As their paths intersect and diverge, the reader is immersed in a vivid portrayal of the human experience in the bustling metropolis, where ambition, love, and the pursuit of happiness collide with the harsh realities of urban life.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Modernist Literature

Themes: The American Dream, Social Inequality, Urbanization, Identity, Human Connection
