Synopsis: 

In 'A History of Southern Missouri and Northern Arkansas: Being an Account of the Early Settlements, the Civil War, the Ku-Klux, and Times of Peace', author William Monks takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of two neighboring regions. From the early settlements and the challenges faced by the pioneers, to the gripping accounts of the Civil War and the rise of the Ku-Klux Klan, Monks weaves together a compelling narrative that sheds light on the complex dynamics of this often overlooked part of America. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the author brings to life the struggles, triumphs, and conflicts that shaped the destinies of Southern Missouri and Northern Arkansas. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, American history, regional history

Themes: Settlement and colonization, Civil War and its aftermath, social and racial tensions, regional identity and pride
