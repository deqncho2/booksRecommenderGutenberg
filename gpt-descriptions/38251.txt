Synopsis: In "Oscar Wilde" by Leonard Cresswell Ingleby, delve into the captivating life of one of the most brilliant and controversial figures in literary history. This biography takes readers on a journey through the flamboyant world of Oscar Wilde, exploring his wit, charm, and the scandalous events that ultimately led to his downfall. From his rise as a celebrated playwright to his tumultuous personal life, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the mind of a man who challenged societal norms and paid a high price for his audacity.

Genres: Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic genius, Social rebellion, Consequences of societal constraints
