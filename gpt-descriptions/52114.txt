Synopsis: In 'Spanish Prisons: The Inquisition at Home and Abroad, Prisons Past and Present' by Griffiths, Arthur, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the dark and mysterious world of Spanish prisons. From the infamous Spanish Inquisition to modern-day penitentiaries, this book delves into the history, architecture, and stories behind these institutions. With vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, the author explores the secrets and scandals that have unfolded within the walls of these prisons, revealing the harsh realities faced by inmates throughout the centuries. As readers navigate through the pages, they will be engrossed by the tales of escape attempts, political prisoners, and the inner workings of the Spanish justice system. Genres: History, True Crime, Non-fiction. Themes: Incarceration, Power and Control, Human Rights.
