Synopsis: In Joseph Conrad's "Heart of Darkness," a young sailor named Marlow embarks on a treacherous journey up the Congo River in search of the enigmatic ivory trader, Kurtz. As Marlow delves deeper into the heart of Africa, he encounters the darkness that resides within the human soul, confronting the brutal realities of colonialism and the moral ambiguity of civilization. Through vivid descriptions and haunting encounters, Conrad explores the depths of human nature and the consequences of unchecked power.

Genres: Adventure, Psychological Fiction, Colonial Literature

Themes: The darkness within human nature, the destructive effects of imperialism, the struggle between civilization and savagery, the ambiguity of morality
