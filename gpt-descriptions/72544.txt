Synopsis: 

In 'Midwifery and the diseases of women', Joel Shew presents a descriptive and practical work that explores the superiority of water-treatment in various women's health issues. From menstruation disorders to pregnancy complications, Shew delves into the effectiveness of water-treatment in addressing conditions such as chlorosis, leucorrhea, prolapsus uteri, hysteria, spinal diseases, and other weaknesses specific to females. The book also covers topics like abortion, uterine hemorrhage, and the general management of childbirth and nursing. With a focus on providing practical solutions, Shew offers valuable insights into the benefits of water-treatment for women's health.

Genres: Medical literature, Women's health, Midwifery

Themes: Water-treatment, Women's health, Pregnancy complications, Menstruation disorders, Childbirth management
