Synopsis: In 'A Zola Dictionary; the Characters of the Rougon-Macquart Novels of Emile Zola,' J. G. Patterson delves into the intricate web of characters that populate Emile Zola's renowned Rougon-Macquart series. From the ambitious and ruthless to the vulnerable and tragic, Patterson provides a comprehensive guide to the diverse personalities that shape Zola's epic saga. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, this dictionary offers a fascinating exploration of the complex relationships, motivations, and destinies of the characters that captivate readers throughout Zola's novels.

Genres: Literary criticism, reference, biography

Themes: Human nature, social class, ambition, family dynamics, morality
