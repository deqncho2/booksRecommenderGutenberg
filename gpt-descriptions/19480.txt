Synopsis: In 'Ray's Daughter: A Story of Manila' by Charles King, readers are transported to the vibrant city of Manila, where they follow the journey of Ray's daughter, a young woman named Maria. Born into a life of poverty and hardship, Maria's resilience and determination lead her on a path of self-discovery and empowerment. As she navigates the complexities of love, family, and societal expectations, Maria's story intertwines with the rich tapestry of Manila's history and culture. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this captivating novel explores the depths of human emotions and the power of hope in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Romance

Themes: Poverty and Social Inequality, Identity and Self-Discovery, Love and Relationships
