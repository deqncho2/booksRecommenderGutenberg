Synopsis: In 'The Boy Nihilist: or, Young America in Russia' by Allan Arnold, a young American student finds himself immersed in the dangerous world of Russian nihilism during the late 19th century. Drawn to the radical ideas of the movement, he becomes entangled in a web of intrigue, love, and betrayal. As he navigates the treacherous landscape of revolutionary Russia, he must confront his own beliefs and question the limits of his loyalty. Will he succumb to the allure of nihilism or find a way to reconcile his ideals with the harsh realities of the world around him?

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Thriller, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Nihilism, Revolution, Ideology, Identity, Betrayal
