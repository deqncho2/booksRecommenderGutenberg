Synopsis: In "Lest We Forget": Chicago's Awful Theater Horror, Everett Marshall takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most tragic events in Chicago's history. Set in the late 19th century, this captivating non-fiction work unveils the shocking details of a devastating theater fire that claimed the lives of hundreds. As the author delves into the aftermath of the disaster, he uncovers a web of secrets, corruption, and heart-wrenching stories of survival. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Marshall paints a haunting picture of a city in mourning and the quest for justice that followed. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, True Crime

Themes: Tragedy, Loss, Justice, Corruption
