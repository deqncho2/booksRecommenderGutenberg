Synopsis: 

In "A History of Italian Painting," Frank Jewett Mather takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and vibrant world of Italian art. From the early Renaissance to the Baroque period, Mather delves into the lives and works of renowned Italian painters, revealing the fascinating stories behind their masterpieces. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, he explores the evolution of artistic techniques, the influence of religion and mythology, and the societal and cultural contexts that shaped Italian painting. From the breathtaking frescoes of Giotto to the enigmatic beauty of Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa, this book offers a comprehensive and enthralling exploration of Italy's artistic legacy.

Genres: Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Renaissance, Baroque, Artistic Techniques, Religion, Mythology, Cultural Context
