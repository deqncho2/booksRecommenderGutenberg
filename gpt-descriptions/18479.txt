Synopsis: In "The Campaigns of the British Army at Washington and New Orleans 1814-1815," Gleig, G. R. takes readers on a captivating journey through two pivotal campaigns of the British Army during the War of 1812. From the dramatic events surrounding the burning of Washington to the thrilling Battle of New Orleans, this historical account offers a vivid and detailed exploration of the military strategies, key figures, and intense battles that shaped these significant moments in American and British history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War memoir

Themes: War and conflict, Leadership and strategy, National identity, Historical significance
