Synopsis:
'Elene; Judith; Athelstan, or the Fight at Brunanburh; Byrhtnoth, or the Fight at Maldon; and the Dream of the Rood: Anglo-Saxon Poems' is a collection of four captivating Anglo-Saxon poems that transport readers to a world of heroic battles, religious devotion, and profound introspection. 

In 'Elene,' a determined queen embarks on a perilous journey to discover the true cross of Christ, facing daunting challenges and testing her faith along the way. 'Judith' tells the gripping tale of a courageous widow who takes matters into her own hands to save her people from a ruthless enemy. 'Athelstan, or the Fight at Brunanburh' recounts a historic battle that showcases the valor and resilience of warriors defending their land. 'Byrhtnoth, or the Fight at Maldon' delves into the tragic consequences of loyalty and honor in the face of overwhelming odds. Lastly, 'The Dream of the Rood' presents a thought-provoking vision in which a talking cross reveals the profound significance of Christ's crucifixion.

Genres:
Epic poetry, Historical fiction, Religious literature

Themes:
Heroism, Faith, Sacrifice, Loyalty, Redemption
