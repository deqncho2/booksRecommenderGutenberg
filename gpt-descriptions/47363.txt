Synopsis: 

In "The Life of James McNeill Whistler," Elizabeth Robins Pennell and Joseph Pennell delve into the fascinating and enigmatic life of the renowned American artist. From his early struggles and artistic development to his controversial personality and influential friendships, this biography offers a captivating exploration of Whistler's journey through the art world. Through vivid anecdotes and insightful analysis, the Pennells provide a vivid portrait of a complex and talented individual, shedding light on the motivations and inspirations behind Whistler's iconic works.

Genres: Biography, Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic development, Controversy, Friendship, Individuality
