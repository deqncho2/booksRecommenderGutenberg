Synopsis: In 'The Defendant', G.K. Chesterton takes readers on a captivating journey through a collection of essays that challenge conventional wisdom and explore the complexities of human nature. From the absurdity of modern life to the paradoxes of religion and the importance of individuality, Chesterton's witty and thought-provoking observations will leave readers questioning their own assumptions and embracing the beauty of the world around them.

Genres: Essay collection, Non-fiction, Philosophy

Themes: Individuality, Paradox, Religion, Absurdity, Human nature
