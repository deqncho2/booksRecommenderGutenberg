Synopsis: 

In 'Famous Assassinations of History from Philip of Macedon, 336 B. C., to Alexander of Servia, A. D. 1903', Johnson, Francis takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring some of the most notorious assassinations that have shaped the course of history. From the shocking demise of powerful rulers to the covert plots that forever altered the fate of nations, this book delves into the dark and intriguing world of political intrigue and power struggles. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Johnson unravels the mysteries surrounding these infamous assassinations, shedding light on the motives, conspiracies, and consequences that have left an indelible mark on the annals of history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Biography

Themes: Power and politics, Conspiracy, Historical impact
