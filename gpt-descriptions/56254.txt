Synopsis: 
In "The Flowers and Gardens of Japan," Florence Du Cane takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of Japanese flora. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Du Cane explores the intricate beauty of traditional Japanese gardens, showcasing their unique design principles and the symbolism behind each carefully chosen plant. From the serene tranquility of Zen gardens to the vibrant colors of cherry blossoms, this book unveils the captivating allure of Japan's floral landscapes, offering a glimpse into the country's rich cultural heritage and deep appreciation for nature.

Genres: Non-fiction, Travel, Art

Themes: Japanese culture, Nature appreciation, Garden design
