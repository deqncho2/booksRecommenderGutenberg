Synopsis: In "Robert Browning," G. K. Chesterton delves into the life and mind of the renowned Victorian poet, exploring the enigmatic nature of his work and the fascinating contradictions that shaped his character. Through a captivating blend of biography and literary analysis, Chesterton unravels the complexities of Browning's poetry, shedding light on the profound themes and hidden meanings that lie within. From his unconventional love story with fellow poet Elizabeth Barrett to his relentless pursuit of artistic expression, Browning's life becomes a captivating tale of passion, mystery, and artistic genius.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Poetry

Themes: Artistic expression, Love and relationships, Individuality, Victorian society
