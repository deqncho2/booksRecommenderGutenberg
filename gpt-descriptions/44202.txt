Synopsis: In "The Red Cross in Peace and War" by Clara Barton, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the life and experiences of the remarkable founder of the American Red Cross. From her tireless efforts during the Civil War to her groundbreaking humanitarian work around the world, Barton's story is one of unwavering dedication, resilience, and compassion. This gripping account sheds light on the challenges she faced, the lives she touched, and the lasting impact of her pioneering organization. 

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Humanitarianism, Courage, Resilience
