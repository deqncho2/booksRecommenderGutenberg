Synopsis: In "Paganism Surviving in Christianity," Lewis, Abram Herbert takes readers on a captivating journey through the hidden remnants of ancient pagan beliefs that have managed to survive within the framework of Christianity. From mysterious symbols and rituals to the subtle influence on religious practices, this thought-provoking exploration unveils a fascinating interplay between two seemingly opposing faiths. As the author delves into the historical and cultural context, readers are invited to question the boundaries between paganism and Christianity, ultimately challenging their own understanding of religious syncretism. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Religion

Themes: Religious syncretism, Cultural assimilation, Symbolism
