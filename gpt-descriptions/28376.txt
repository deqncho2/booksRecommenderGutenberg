Synopsis: 

'The Wide, Wide World' by Susan Warner is a captivating coming-of-age novel that follows the journey of young Ellen Montgomery. After the death of her mother, Ellen is sent to live with her strict and distant aunt in a small New England town. As she navigates the challenges of her new life, Ellen finds solace in her deep love for nature and her unwavering faith. Through her trials and triumphs, Ellen learns valuable lessons about resilience, friendship, and the power of the human spirit. 

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Historical 

Themes: Resilience, Faith, Friendship, Nature
