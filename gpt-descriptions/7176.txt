Synopsis: In "A Letter to a Hindu," Tolstoy pens a thought-provoking letter addressing the complex relationship between religion, violence, and non-violent resistance. Through a series of compelling arguments and personal anecdotes, Tolstoy challenges the reader to question the foundations of their beliefs and consider the true essence of spirituality. This powerful and introspective letter serves as a catalyst for self-reflection and a call to reevaluate the role of violence in society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Political discourse

Themes: Religion, Non-violence, Spirituality, Morality
