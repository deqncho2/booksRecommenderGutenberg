Synopsis: 
'Every Man out of His Humour' by Ben Jonson is a satirical comedy that takes place in the vibrant city of London. The story revolves around a group of eccentric characters who are all trying to escape their own self-imposed "humours" or personal quirks. As they navigate through a series of hilarious and absurd situations, their attempts to break free from their humours lead to unexpected consequences and uproarious misunderstandings. With sharp wit and biting social commentary, Jonson explores the follies and foibles of human nature in a world where everyone is desperately trying to be someone they are not.

Genres: Comedy, Satire, Drama

Themes: Identity, Self-deception, Social satire
