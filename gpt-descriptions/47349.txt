Synopsis: In 'The Wild Garden', William Robinson takes readers on a captivating journey through the transformation of groves and gardens, revealing the beauty that can be achieved by incorporating hardy exotic plants. From the depths of the Dark Ages to the present day, Robinson explores the evolution of gardening practices and inspires readers to embrace the naturalization of plants to create stunning landscapes. With vivid descriptions and practical advice, this book is a must-read for anyone seeking to enhance their own garden and bring a touch of the exotic into their lives.

Genres: Non-fiction, Gardening, History

Themes: Naturalization of plants, Garden transformation, Evolution of gardening practices
