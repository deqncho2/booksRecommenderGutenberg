Synopsis: 

In 'South Africa and the Transvaal War, Vol. 7 (of 8): The Guerilla War, from February 1901 to the Conclusion of Hostilities', Louis Creswicke takes readers on a gripping journey through the intense and unpredictable guerilla warfare that unfolded in South Africa during the early 20th century. As the conflict escalates, Creswicke vividly portrays the strategies, tactics, and challenges faced by both the British forces and the Boer guerilla fighters. With a keen eye for detail and a knack for storytelling, the author brings to life the dramatic events, daring escapes, and unexpected alliances that shaped the outcome of this brutal war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War memoir

Themes: Guerilla warfare, Colonialism, Nationalism, Resilience, Survival
