Synopsis: In 'An Enquiry into the Causes of the Frequent Executions at Tyburn', Bernard Mandeville delves into the dark underbelly of 18th-century London, exploring the reasons behind the alarming number of executions taking place at Tyburn. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Mandeville uncovers the intricate web of social, economic, and moral factors that contribute to this grim reality. As he peels back the layers of society, the reader is confronted with unsettling truths and forced to question their own assumptions about justice, morality, and the nature of humanity.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical analysis

Themes: Crime and punishment, Social inequality, Morality, Human nature
