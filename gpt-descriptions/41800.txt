Synopsis: In "The Treaty of Waitangi; or, how New Zealand became a British Colony," Thomas Lindsay Buick takes readers on a captivating journey through the historical events that led to New Zealand becoming a British colony. From the initial encounters between Māori and European explorers to the signing of the Treaty of Waitangi, Buick weaves together a compelling narrative filled with political intrigue, cultural clashes, and the struggle for sovereignty. As the story unfolds, readers will be immersed in the complexities of colonialism and the profound impact it had on the indigenous Māori people. This book is a must-read for anyone interested in understanding the fascinating history of New Zealand's colonization.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Colonial history, Indigenous studies

Themes: Colonialism, Indigenous rights, Cultural exchange, Political negotiations
