Synopsis: In 'Isopel Berners: The History of certain doings in a Staffordshire Dingle, July, 1825', George Borrow takes us on a captivating journey through the enchanting Staffordshire countryside. The story follows the adventures of the enigmatic Isopel Berners, a young woman with a mysterious past, as she encounters a diverse cast of characters and embarks on a series of unexpected escapades. Set against the backdrop of the idyllic Dingle, Borrow weaves a tale filled with intrigue, romance, and the timeless quest for self-discovery. As the narrative unfolds, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and Isopel's resilience and determination are put to the ultimate test. This enthralling work will leave readers eagerly turning the pages, eager to uncover the truth behind Isopel's past and the fate that awaits her.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Identity, Love and Relationships, Self-discovery
