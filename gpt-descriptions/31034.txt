Synopsis: 
'By-ways in Book-land: Short Essays on Literary Subjects' takes readers on a captivating journey through the vast and enchanting world of literature. In this collection of short essays, W. H. Davenport Adams delves into various literary subjects, exploring the lives of renowned authors, analyzing their works, and uncovering hidden gems of the literary world. From Shakespeare to Dickens, from ancient Greek epics to modern poetry, Adams offers insightful and thought-provoking commentary that will leave readers eager to dive deeper into the pages of their favorite books. With his engaging writing style and extensive knowledge, Adams invites readers to explore the nooks and crannies of Book-land, discovering new perspectives and gaining a deeper appreciation for the power of literature.

Genres: Literary criticism, Non-fiction, Essays

Themes: Literature appreciation, Author analysis, Literary exploration
