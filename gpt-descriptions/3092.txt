Synopsis: 

In "The Conquest of New France; A Chronicle of the Colonial Wars," George McKinnon Wrong takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous conflicts that shaped the destiny of New France. From the early clashes between the French and English settlers to the pivotal battles that ultimately led to the fall of Quebec, this chronicle offers a gripping account of the struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices of those involved in the colonial wars. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Wrong paints a vivid picture of the political intrigues, military strategies, and personal stories that unfolded during this pivotal period in North American history. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Colonial history

Themes: Conflict and conquest, Cultural clashes, Power dynamics, Exploration and colonization
