Synopsis: 
In "Three years in field hospitals of the Army of the Potomac," Anna M. Holstein takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of a Civil War nurse. Through her vivid and poignant storytelling, we witness the unimaginable horrors and triumphs that unfold within the walls of the field hospitals. From the chaos of battlefields to the heart-wrenching moments of tending to wounded soldiers, Holstein's account offers a unique perspective on the sacrifices made during this tumultuous time in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, War literature

Themes: Sacrifice, Resilience, Compassion, Humanitarianism
