Synopsis: 

In "On the Edge of the War Zone: From the Battle of the Marne to the Entrance of the Stars and Stripes," Mildred Aldrich takes us on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of World War I. As an American expatriate living in France, she provides a unique perspective on the war, recounting her experiences and observations from the front lines. From the chaos of the Battle of the Marne to the arrival of the American troops, Aldrich's vivid storytelling immerses readers in the heart-wrenching realities of war, capturing both the heroism and the devastation that unfolded during this pivotal period in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, War literature
Themes: War, Patriotism, Resilience, Humanitarianism
