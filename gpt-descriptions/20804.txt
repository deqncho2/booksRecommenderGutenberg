Synopsis: 

'Lucretia Borgia According to Original Documents and Correspondence of Her Day' by Gregorovius, Ferdinand is a captivating historical biography that delves into the life of one of history's most infamous women. Through meticulous research and analysis of original documents and correspondence, Gregorovius paints a vivid portrait of Lucretia Borgia, revealing the complexities and intrigues that surrounded her during the Renaissance era. From her controversial family background to her influential role in the political landscape, the book uncovers the secrets and scandals that have shrouded Lucretia Borgia's reputation for centuries. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be drawn into a world of power, ambition, and betrayal, where Lucretia's actions and choices shape her destiny and leave an indelible mark on history.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and Politics, Gender and Identity, Family Dynamics
