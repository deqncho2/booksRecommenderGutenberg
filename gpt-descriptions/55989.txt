Synopsis: 

In 'Celtic Folklore: Welsh and Manx', Sir John Rhys delves into the rich tapestry of Welsh and Manx folklore, unearthing captivating tales of mythical creatures, heroic figures, and ancient traditions. Volume 2 of this enchanting collection continues to transport readers to a world where dragons roam, fairies dance in moonlit glens, and ancient legends come to life. From the hauntingly beautiful Isle of Man to the rugged landscapes of Wales, this volume offers a mesmerizing exploration of Celtic folklore, filled with wonder, magic, and the enduring spirit of the Celtic people.

Genres: Folklore, Mythology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Celtic mythology, Folk traditions, Supernatural beings, Oral storytelling, Cultural heritage
