Synopsis: 
'A Concise Dictionary of Middle English from A.D. 1150 to 1580' is a comprehensive and fascinating exploration of the English language during the Middle English period. Written by Mayhew and Skeat, this dictionary takes readers on a linguistic journey through time, uncovering the meanings and origins of words used between the years 1150 and 1580. From Chaucer's Canterbury Tales to the works of Shakespeare, this dictionary provides invaluable insights into the evolution of the English language, offering a glimpse into the rich tapestry of words that shaped literature and communication during this transformative era.

Genres: 
Reference, Linguistics, History

Themes: 
Language evolution, Middle English literature, Historical linguistics
