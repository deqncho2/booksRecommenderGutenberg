Synopsis: 

In "Mother's Remedies: Over One Thousand Tried and Tested Remedies from Mothers of the United States and Canada," author Thomas Jefferson Ritter delves into the treasure trove of wisdom passed down through generations of mothers. This comprehensive collection of over one thousand remedies offers a fascinating glimpse into the homespun remedies and folk wisdom that have been relied upon by mothers across North America. From herbal concoctions to soothing balms, Ritter presents a diverse range of remedies that have stood the test of time. Whether it's a remedy for a common cold or a solution for a stubborn stain, this book is a captivating exploration of the resourcefulness and ingenuity of mothers throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Health and Wellness, Home Remedies

Themes: Traditional knowledge, Natural healing, Family and community, Women's wisdom
