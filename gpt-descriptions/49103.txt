Synopsis: In "The First Printed Translations into English of the Great Foreign Classics: A Supplement to Text-Books of English Literature," William James Harris takes readers on a captivating journey through the early translations of renowned foreign classics into English. From the works of ancient Greek philosophers to the epic tales of medieval Europe, Harris explores the challenges and triumphs of bringing these literary treasures to a new audience. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, he sheds light on the fascinating individuals who undertook these translations, their motivations, and the impact they had on English literature. This supplement serves as a captivating companion to English literature textbooks, offering a deeper understanding of the rich tapestry of global literary influences that have shaped the English canon.

Genres: Literary Criticism, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Translation, Cultural Exchange, Literary Influence
