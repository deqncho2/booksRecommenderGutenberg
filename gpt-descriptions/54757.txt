Synopsis: In 'The Sultan and His People' by Oscanyan, C., readers are transported to the exotic world of the Ottoman Empire, where a young sultan finds himself torn between duty and desire. As he navigates the treacherous waters of palace politics and encounters a mysterious group of rebels, the sultan must make difficult choices that will shape the fate of his people. With vivid descriptions, captivating characters, and unexpected twists, this tale of power, love, and sacrifice will keep readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Political Intrigue

Themes: Power and Responsibility, Love and Sacrifice, Loyalty and Betrayal
