Synopsis: In "Christianity and Ethics: A Handbook of Christian Ethics," Alexander, Archibald B. D. explores the intricate relationship between Christianity and ethical principles. This thought-provoking handbook delves into the moral dilemmas faced by Christians in various aspects of life, offering insightful guidance and perspectives. From the complexities of personal morality to the challenges of societal ethics, Alexander provides a comprehensive examination of how Christian beliefs intersect with ethical decision-making. Drawing from biblical teachings and theological insights, this book offers a compelling exploration of the moral landscape through a Christian lens.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, Philosophy

Themes: Christian ethics, Moral decision-making, Biblical teachings, Ethical dilemmas, Theological perspectives
