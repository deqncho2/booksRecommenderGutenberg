Synopsis: In 'Working Women of Japan', Sidney Lewis Gulick takes us on a captivating journey into the lives of Japanese women who defy societal norms and embrace the challenges of the modern world. Through intimate interviews and personal anecdotes, Gulick unveils the untold stories of these remarkable women, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and the profound impact they have on Japanese society. From the bustling streets of Tokyo to the serene countryside, this book offers a unique perspective on the evolving role of women in Japan, leaving readers inspired and enlightened.

Genres: Non-fiction, Cultural Studies, Women's Studies

Themes: Gender roles, Feminism, Cultural diversity, Social change
