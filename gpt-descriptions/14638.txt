Synopsis: In "The Lutherans of New York, Their Story and Their Problems" by Wenner, George Unangst, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the history and challenges faced by the Lutheran community in New York. From their humble beginnings to their struggles for acceptance and identity, this book delves into the lives of individuals who have shaped the Lutheran faith in the bustling city. As the narrative unfolds, readers will discover the unique experiences, conflicts, and triumphs that have shaped the Lutherans' presence in New York, making this a compelling exploration of faith, community, and resilience.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Religion

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Community, Identity, Resilience
