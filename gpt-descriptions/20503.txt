Synopsis: 

"The Nation's River: A report on the Potomac" takes readers on a captivating journey through the history, ecology, and significance of the Potomac River. From its humble beginnings as a pristine waterway to its transformation into a vital lifeline for the nation's capital, this report delves into the river's past, present, and future. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the United States Department of the Interior sheds light on the challenges and triumphs that have shaped the Potomac, offering a compelling narrative that will leave readers eager to explore this iconic river themselves.

Genres: Non-fiction, Environmental Science, History

Themes: Environmental conservation, Water resource management, Historical significance
