Synopsis: In 'The Donkey, the Elephant, and the Goat at a Public Meeting' by Preston Jack, a seemingly ordinary public meeting takes an unexpected turn when three unlikely characters find themselves at the center of a heated debate. As tensions rise and tempers flare, the donkey, the elephant, and the goat must navigate the complexities of politics, power, and personal beliefs. With humor and wit, this story explores the dynamics of democracy and the importance of finding common ground in a divided world.

Genres: Political satire, allegory, fable

Themes: Politics, power, democracy, compromise, unity
