Synopsis: In 'Bahaism and Its Claims: A Study of the Religion Promulgated by Baha Ullah and Abdul Baha', Samuel Graham Wilson delves into the fascinating world of Bahaism, a religion that emerged in the 19th century. Through meticulous research and analysis, Wilson explores the origins, teachings, and impact of Baha Ullah and Abdul Baha, the central figures of this faith. As he uncovers the beliefs and practices of Bahaism, Wilson raises thought-provoking questions about its validity and its potential to shape the future of religious thought.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, History

Themes: Religion, Spirituality, Comparative Religion, Faith, Religious Movements
