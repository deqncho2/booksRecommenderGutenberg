Synopsis: In 'A Colored Man Round the World', David F. Dorr takes readers on an extraordinary journey as he recounts his experiences traveling across the globe in the late 19th century. From encounters with exotic cultures and breathtaking landscapes to navigating racial prejudices and societal expectations, Dorr's memoir offers a captivating glimpse into a world on the brink of change. As he faces both adversity and triumph, the reader is left eagerly turning the pages, eager to discover what lies ahead for this intrepid traveler.

Genres: Memoir, Travelogue, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Identity, Racism, Cultural Exploration, Adventure
