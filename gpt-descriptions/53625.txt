Synopsis: In "Shakespeare and His Times," Nathan Drake takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and works of the renowned playwright. This volume delves into the biography of Shakespeare, offering insightful criticisms on his genius and writings. Drake presents a new chronology of Shakespeare's plays, shedding light on their order and development. Additionally, he explores the enigmatic object of Shakespeare's sonnets, unraveling their true meaning. Moreover, Drake provides a comprehensive history of the manners, customs, amusements, superstitions, poetry, and elegant literature of Shakespeare's era, immersing readers in the vibrant world that shaped the playwright's imagination.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Shakespeare's genius, the interpretation of his works, the cultural and historical context of his era
