Synopsis: 

In "Greece and the Allies, 1914-1922," George Frederick Abbott takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years of Greece's involvement in World War I and the subsequent Greco-Turkish War. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Abbott unveils the complex web of political alliances, military strategies, and diplomatic negotiations that shaped Greece's fate during this critical period. From the heroic battles fought on the frontlines to the high-stakes negotiations behind closed doors, this gripping account sheds light on the untold stories of courage, betrayal, and resilience that unfolded in Greece during these turbulent years.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Political history

Themes: War and conflict, National identity, Diplomacy and international relations
