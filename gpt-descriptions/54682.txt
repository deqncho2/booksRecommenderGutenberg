Synopsis: 
In 'Zuñi Folk Tales', Frank Hamilton Cushing takes readers on a captivating journey into the rich and enchanting world of the Zuñi people. Through a collection of traditional folk tales, Cushing unveils a tapestry of mythical creatures, magical encounters, and timeless wisdom. From the mischievous Coyote to the wise Spider Woman, these tales transport readers to a realm where animals speak, spirits roam, and the boundaries between the natural and supernatural blur. As the stories unfold, readers will be immersed in the vibrant culture and beliefs of the Zuñi, discovering the power of storytelling to convey profound truths about the human experience.

Genres: Folklore, Mythology, Anthropology

Themes: Cultural preservation, Spirituality, Wisdom
