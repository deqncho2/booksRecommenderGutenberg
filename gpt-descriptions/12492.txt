Synopsis: In "Recent Tendencies in Ethics: Three Lectures to Clergy Given at Cambridge," Sorley delves into the intriguing world of moral philosophy, captivating readers with his thought-provoking insights and challenging traditional notions of ethics. Through a series of engaging lectures, he explores the complexities of moral decision-making, the role of religion in shaping ethical principles, and the impact of societal changes on our understanding of right and wrong. As Sorley navigates through these captivating topics, readers are left pondering the profound implications of his ideas and questioning their own moral compass.

Genres: Philosophy, Ethics, Non-fiction

Themes: Moral philosophy, Religion and ethics, Societal changes and ethics
