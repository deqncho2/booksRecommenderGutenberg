Synopsis: 
'Four Plays of Aeschylus' is a collection of four captivating and thought-provoking plays by the ancient Greek playwright Aeschylus. In these plays, Aeschylus explores the complexities of human nature, the consequences of hubris, and the eternal struggle between fate and free will. From the gripping tale of a vengeful goddess seeking justice to the haunting story of a cursed family, each play delves into the depths of human emotions and the moral dilemmas faced by its characters. With powerful dialogues, intense conflicts, and unexpected twists, 'Four Plays of Aeschylus' takes readers on a journey through the timeless themes of justice, fate, and the power of the gods.

Genres: Tragedy, Drama

Themes: Justice, Fate, Hubris, Moral Dilemmas
