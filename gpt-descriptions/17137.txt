Synopsis: 

In "Some Mooted Questions in Reinforced Concrete Design," Edward Godfrey delves into the fascinating world of civil engineering, exploring the cutting-edge advancements and controversies surrounding reinforced concrete design. Published in 1910, this thought-provoking paper from the American Society of Civil Engineers presents a series of intriguing questions that challenge the conventional wisdom of the time. Godfrey's meticulous analysis and innovative ideas will captivate readers as he navigates through the complexities of this emerging field, shedding light on the potential of reinforced concrete and its impact on the future of construction. 

Genres: Technical, Engineering, Historical

Themes: Innovation, Controversy, Advancements in Construction
