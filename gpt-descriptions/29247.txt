Synopsis: In 'The Academic Questions, Treatise De Finibus, and Tusculan Disputations', the renowned Roman philosopher Marcus Tullius Cicero delves into the world of Greek philosophy, exploring the ideas and teachings of various Greek philosophers. Through a series of dialogues and debates, Cicero engages with these ancient thinkers, presenting their arguments and counterarguments on topics ranging from ethics and happiness to the nature of the universe. With his characteristic eloquence and wit, Cicero offers a captivating exploration of the philosophical landscape of his time, shedding light on the intellectual currents that shaped ancient Rome.

Genres: Philosophy, Classics, Non-fiction

Themes: Philosophy, Ethics, Happiness, Nature of the Universe
