Synopsis: In the fourth volume of "The Life of George Washington," John Marshall delves into the pivotal role that Washington played as the Commander in Chief of the American forces during the War of Independence. This captivating biography explores the challenges and triumphs that Washington faced as he led his country to establish its independence. From the strategic battles to the personal sacrifices, Marshall paints a vivid portrait of a remarkable leader who would go on to become the first President of the United States.

Genres: Biography, History, Military

Themes: Leadership, Independence, Patriotism
