Synopsis: 

In "India and Tibet: A history of the relations which have subsisted between the two countries from the time of Warren Hastings to 1910; with a particular account of the mission to Lhasa of 1904," Sir Francis Edward Younghusband takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex and intriguing relationship between India and Tibet. From the time of Warren Hastings to the year 1910, this historical account delves into the political, cultural, and diplomatic interactions that have shaped the dynamic between these two nations. With a particular focus on the mission to Lhasa in 1904, Younghusband provides a fascinating and detailed account of this pivotal event. 

Genres: History, Politics, Diplomacy

Themes: International relations, Cultural exchange, Diplomatic missions
