Synopsis:
'Elizabeth Montagu, the queen of the bluestockings, Volumes 1 and 2: Her correspondence from 1720 to 1761' offers a captivating glimpse into the life and mind of Elizabeth Montagu, a prominent figure in 18th-century British society. Through her extensive correspondence, we are transported to a world of intellectual salons, literary discussions, and social gatherings, where Montagu's wit, intelligence, and influence shine. As we delve into her letters, we witness her friendships with renowned writers and thinkers of the time, such as Samuel Johnson and David Garrick, and her involvement in the bluestocking movement, a group of intellectual women challenging societal norms. This collection of letters not only provides a fascinating insight into Montagu's personal and professional life but also offers a broader understanding of the cultural and intellectual landscape of the era.

Genres:
Biography, Correspondence, Historical

Themes:
Intellectualism, Gender roles, Societal norms
