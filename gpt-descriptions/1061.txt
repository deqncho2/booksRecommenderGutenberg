Synopsis: In 'Myths and Myth-Makers: Old Tales and Superstitions Interpreted by Comparative Mythology', John Fiske delves into the fascinating world of ancient myths and legends, unraveling their hidden meanings and exploring their enduring significance. Through a comparative analysis of various mythologies from around the world, Fiske uncovers the common threads that connect these tales, shedding light on the universal human experiences they reflect. From the gods and goddesses of ancient Greece to the heroic figures of Norse mythology, Fiske's exploration takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich tapestry of human imagination and belief.

Genres: Comparative Mythology, Folklore, Literary Criticism

Themes: Mythology, Symbolism, Cultural Identity
