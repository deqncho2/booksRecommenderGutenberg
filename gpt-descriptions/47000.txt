Synopsis: In "Marriage, As It Was, As It Is, and As It Should Be: A Plea for Reform," Annie Besant delves into the complex institution of marriage, exploring its historical roots, its current state, and proposing a vision for its future. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Besant challenges societal norms and advocates for a more equitable and fulfilling union between partners. With compelling arguments and insightful observations, she invites readers to question traditional notions of marriage and consider the possibilities for a more progressive and harmonious approach to this fundamental aspect of human life.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Commentary, Feminist Literature

Themes: Marriage, Gender Equality, Social Reform, Traditionalism vs. Progressivism
