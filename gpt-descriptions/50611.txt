Synopsis: In "The Pillar of Fire; or, Israel in Bondage," J. H. Ingraham takes readers on a captivating journey through ancient Egypt, where the Hebrew people find themselves enslaved under the oppressive rule of Pharaoh. Amidst the backdrop of political intrigue, forbidden love, and divine intervention, the story follows the life of a young Hebrew prince who becomes entangled in a web of secrets and power struggles. As the Hebrews yearn for freedom and struggle to maintain their faith, they must confront their own doubts and fears while facing the wrath of a vengeful deity. Will they find deliverance from their bondage, or will they succumb to the forces that seek to destroy them?

Genres: Historical Fiction, Religious Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Faith and Doubt, Oppression and Freedom, Divine Intervention, Love and Sacrifice
