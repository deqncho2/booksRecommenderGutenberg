Synopsis: In "Considerations on the Negroe cause commonly so called," Samuel Estwick pens a thought-provoking address to Lord Mansfield, the lord chief justice of the Court of King's Bench. Through a series of compelling arguments, Estwick challenges the prevailing notions surrounding the "Negroe cause" and delves into the complexities of the issue. With a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of the legal and moral implications, Estwick presents a fresh perspective that forces the reader to question their own beliefs and biases.

Genres: Non-fiction, Legal discourse, Social commentary

Themes: Racial equality, Legal justice, Moral responsibility
