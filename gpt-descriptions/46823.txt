Synopsis: 

In 'Ten Years in India, in the 16th Queen's Lancers, and Three Years in South Africa, in the Cape Corps Levies', author W. J. D. Gould takes readers on an exhilarating journey through his experiences as a soldier in two different continents. From the vibrant landscapes of India to the tumultuous landscapes of South Africa, Gould's memoir offers a captivating account of his ten years in the 16th Queen's Lancers and three years in the Cape Corps Levies. 

Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Gould provides a unique perspective on the challenges and triumphs of military life in colonial India and South Africa. From encounters with local cultures and customs to the harsh realities of warfare, the author's narrative paints a compelling picture of the soldier's life during this time period. 

Genres: Memoir, Military History, Travelogue

Themes: Colonialism, Cultural Exchange, Warfare, Identity
