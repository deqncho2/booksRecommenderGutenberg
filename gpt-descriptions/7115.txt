Synopsis: In 'Giant Hours with Poet Preachers', William L. Stidger takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives of renowned poets who also happened to be preachers. From the eloquent sermons of John Donne to the spiritual musings of Gerard Manley Hopkins, Stidger delves into the unique intersection of poetry and faith. Through vivid anecdotes and insightful analysis, he explores how these poet preachers used their words to inspire, challenge, and uplift their congregations. This book is a celebration of the power of language and the profound impact it can have on our spiritual lives.

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: Poetry, Faith, Inspiration, Language
