Synopsis: 
'The Borzoi 1920: Being a sort of record of five years' publishing' takes readers on a captivating journey through the early years of Alfred A. Knopf, Inc., a renowned publishing house. This memoir-like account offers a behind-the-scenes look at the challenges, triumphs, and literary discoveries that shaped the company's formative years. From the exhilarating pursuit of talented authors to the struggles of navigating a rapidly changing industry, this book immerses readers in the fascinating world of publishing during the 1920s.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Publishing industry, Literary discovery, Entrepreneurship, Cultural shifts
