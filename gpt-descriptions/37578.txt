Synopsis: In 'The Later Life' by Louis Couperus, we follow the captivating journey of a middle-aged man named Van der Welcke, who finds himself at a crossroads in life. As he navigates the complexities of love, family, and societal expectations, Van der Welcke is forced to confront his own desires and confront the consequences of his choices. Set against the backdrop of a changing world, this thought-provoking novel explores the universal themes of identity, self-discovery, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Literary fiction, psychological fiction, family drama

Themes: Identity, self-discovery, love, family, societal expectations
