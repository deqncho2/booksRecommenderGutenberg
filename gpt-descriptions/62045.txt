Synopsis: In this captivating biography, Chesterton, Perris, and Garnett delve into the life and mind of the renowned Russian author, Leo Tolstoy. From his aristocratic upbringing to his spiritual awakening and subsequent transformation, the authors paint a vivid portrait of a man torn between his worldly desires and his quest for meaning. Through Tolstoy's tumultuous relationships, his literary achievements, and his philosophical musings, the reader is taken on a journey that explores the complexities of human nature and the eternal struggle between passion and morality.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Philosophy

Themes: Identity, Morality, Spiritual Awakening, Love and Relationships, Art and Literature
