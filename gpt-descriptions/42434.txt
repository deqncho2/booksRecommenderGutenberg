Synopsis: 

'The Religious Persecution in France 1900-1906' by J. Napier Brodhead (Jane Napier) is a gripping historical account that delves into a tumultuous period in French history. Set between 1900 and 1906, the book uncovers a dark chapter marked by religious intolerance and persecution. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, the author unveils the struggles faced by religious minorities and the challenges they encountered in a society grappling with political and social upheaval. As the narrative unfolds, readers are drawn into a world of clandestine meetings, secret alliances, and courageous individuals who risked everything to defend their faith. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Religious studies

Themes: Religious persecution, Intolerance, Freedom of religion, Political unrest
