Synopsis: 
In "In our first year of war: messages and addresses to the Congress and the people, March 5, 1917, to January 8, 1918," Woodrow Wilson takes us on a compelling journey through the early stages of World War I. Through a collection of his speeches and messages, Wilson provides a unique insight into the challenges and decisions faced by the United States during this tumultuous period. From the delicate balance of neutrality to the eventual entry into the war, Wilson's words capture the essence of a nation grappling with its role on the global stage.

Genres: 
Historical, Political, Non-fiction

Themes: 
War and Conflict, Diplomacy, National Identity
