Synopsis: 
In 'An Artilleryman's Diary' by Jones, Jenkins Lloyd, we are taken on a gripping journey through the eyes of a young soldier during a time of war. From the chaos and destruction of the battlefield to the intimate moments of camaraderie and fear, this diary offers a raw and unfiltered account of the realities faced by those on the front lines. As the protagonist navigates the horrors of war, we witness the profound impact it has on his psyche and the sacrifices he must make to survive. With vivid descriptions and poignant reflections, this diary provides a haunting and unforgettable portrayal of the human experience in the midst of conflict.

Genres: War, Historical Fiction, Memoir

Themes: The brutality of war, camaraderie and brotherhood, the psychological toll of combat
