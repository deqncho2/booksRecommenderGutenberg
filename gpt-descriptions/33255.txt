Synopsis: 
'Within Prison Walls' is a captivating narrative that takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the state prison at Auburn, New York. Written by Thomas Mott Osborne, the story unfolds during a week of voluntary confinement, offering a unique perspective on the inner workings of the prison system. As the author immerses himself in the daily routines and interactions of both inmates and guards, he uncovers surprising truths and challenges preconceived notions about crime and punishment. With vivid descriptions and compelling anecdotes, 'Within Prison Walls' sheds light on the complexities of life behind bars and raises important questions about justice and rehabilitation.

Genres: Non-fiction, Memoir, Social Commentary

Themes: Prison life, Criminal justice, Rehabilitation, Human nature
