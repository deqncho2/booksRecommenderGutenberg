Synopsis: In "Doctor and Patient" by Silas Weir Mitchell, a renowned physician delves into the complex relationship between doctors and their patients. Through a series of captivating anecdotes and thought-provoking insights, Mitchell explores the intricate dynamics that unfold within the walls of the doctor's office. From the challenges of diagnosing mysterious ailments to the emotional toll of treating the sick, this collection of stories offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of medicine. With a blend of compassion and clinical expertise, Mitchell sheds light on the profound impact that the doctor-patient relationship can have on both parties involved.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical literature, Psychology

Themes: Doctor-patient relationship, Medical ethics, Empathy, Healing, Communication
