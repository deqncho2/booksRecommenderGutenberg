Synopsis: In "Abigail Adams and Her Times," Laura Elizabeth Howe Richards takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of one of America's most influential First Ladies. From her humble beginnings in colonial Massachusetts to her pivotal role in shaping the nation's early history, Abigail Adams emerges as a remarkable woman ahead of her time. Through her letters and personal anecdotes, Richards paints a vivid portrait of a fiercely intelligent and independent woman who navigated the complexities of marriage, motherhood, and politics with grace and determination. As readers delve into the pages of this biography, they will be enthralled by Abigail's unwavering commitment to education, her unwavering support for women's rights, and her unwavering love for her husband, John Adams. 

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Women's rights, Education, Marriage, Politics
