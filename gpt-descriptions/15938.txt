Synopsis: In 'The Yankee Tea-party; Or, Boston in 1773', Henry C. Watson takes readers on a thrilling journey back to the tumultuous times leading up to the American Revolution. Set in Boston in 1773, the story follows a group of passionate patriots as they navigate the treacherous waters of political unrest, secret societies, and the brewing conflict with the British Empire. Amidst the backdrop of the iconic Boston Tea Party, the characters find themselves entangled in a web of intrigue, danger, and the fight for freedom. With its vivid historical detail and gripping narrative, this book offers a captivating glimpse into the events that shaped the birth of a nation.

Genres: Historical fiction, Adventure, Political thriller

Themes: Revolution, Patriotism, Freedom, Loyalty, Betrayal
