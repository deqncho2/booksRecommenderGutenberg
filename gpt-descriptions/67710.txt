Synopsis: In 'Russia in 1916', Graham, Stephen takes us on a captivating journey through the vast and enigmatic landscapes of Russia during a pivotal year in its history. Through his vivid and immersive storytelling, he introduces us to a diverse cast of characters, from peasants to aristocrats, soldiers to artists, all navigating the tumultuous political and social climate of the time. As the country teeters on the brink of revolution, the reader is transported into a world of intrigue, passion, and uncertainty, where personal destinies intertwine with the fate of an entire nation.

Genres: Historical fiction, Political drama, Literary fiction

Themes: Revolution, Social inequality, Identity, Nationalism
