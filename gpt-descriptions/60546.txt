Synopsis: 
In "Thirteen years at the Russian court," Pierre Gilliard, a Swiss tutor, provides a captivating firsthand account of his time spent with the last Russian royal family, the Romanovs. From the opulent halls of the Winter Palace to the tragic events that unfolded during the Russian Revolution, Gilliard offers a unique perspective on the final years and ultimate fate of Czar Nicholas II and his family. Through his intimate observations and personal experiences, the reader is transported into the heart of the Russian court, witnessing the complexities of royal life and the profound impact of political unrest. As the story unfolds, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and the Romanovs' destiny hangs in the balance.

Genres: Biography, History, Memoir

Themes: Royalty, Revolution, Loyalty, Tragedy
