Synopsis: In "Richard II: Makers of History" by Abbott, Jacob, embark on a captivating journey through the tumultuous reign of Richard II, a young and enigmatic king of England. Set against a backdrop of political intrigue, power struggles, and the complexities of monarchy, this historical narrative delves into the life of a ruler whose actions would shape the destiny of a nation. As Richard II grapples with his own insecurities and the challenges of maintaining his authority, the reader is drawn into a world of courtly drama, betrayal, and the quest for personal and political redemption. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Drama

Themes: Power and Authority, Loyalty and Betrayal, Personal Identity and Self-Discovery
