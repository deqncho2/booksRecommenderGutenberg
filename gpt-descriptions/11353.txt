Synopsis: 

In "History of Liberia: Johns Hopkins University Studies in Historical and Political Science," J.H.T. McPherson delves into the captivating story of Liberia, a nation founded by freed African-American slaves in the early 19th century. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, McPherson uncovers the struggles, triumphs, and complexities that shaped Liberia's unique history. From the initial settlement and the challenges of establishing a new society, to the impact of colonization and the fight for independence, this book offers a fascinating exploration of a nation's journey towards self-determination. 

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Political Science, African Studies

Themes: African-American History, Colonization, Independence, Identity, Nation-building
