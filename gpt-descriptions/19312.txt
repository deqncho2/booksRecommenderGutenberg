Synopsis: 
In 'Tablets of Abdul-Baha Abbas', ʻAbdu'l-Bahá takes readers on a profound spiritual journey through a collection of his writings and letters. These tablets offer a glimpse into the wisdom, guidance, and love of ʻAbdu'l-Bahá, the son of Bahá'u'lláh, the founder of the Bahá'í Faith. Through his words, he explores various aspects of human existence, the nature of the soul, the purpose of life, and the interconnectedness of all beings. This compilation invites readers to reflect on their own spiritual path and discover the transformative power of love and unity.

Genres: Religious literature, Spiritual writings, Epistolary

Themes: Spirituality, Love, Unity, Human existence, Purpose of life
