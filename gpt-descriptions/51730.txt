Synopsis: 

In "Life of David W. Patten, the First Apostolic Martyr" by Lycurgus A. Wilson, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the life of an extraordinary man. David W. Patten, a devoted apostle, faces numerous trials and challenges as he navigates his spiritual path. From his early encounters with Joseph Smith, the founder of the Latter Day Saint movement, to his courageous acts of faith and his ultimate sacrifice, Patten's story is one of unwavering devotion and unwavering determination. As the narrative unfolds, readers are drawn into a world of faith, persecution, and the pursuit of truth, leaving them eager to discover the fate of this remarkable apostle.

Genres: Biography, Religious Literature, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Faith and Devotion, Persecution and Sacrifice, Spiritual Journeys
