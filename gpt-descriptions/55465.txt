Synopsis: In 'The Peoples of India', J.D. Anderson takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse and enchanting tapestry of India's people. From the bustling streets of Delhi to the serene backwaters of Kerala, this book delves into the lives, traditions, and customs of the various communities that make up this vibrant nation. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, Anderson paints a vivid picture of the rich cultural heritage and the incredible diversity that exists within India. From the nomadic tribes of the Himalayas to the ancient civilizations of the Indus Valley, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the people who have shaped India's history and continue to shape its future.

Genres: Non-fiction, Travel, Anthropology

Themes: Cultural diversity, Identity, Tradition, History
