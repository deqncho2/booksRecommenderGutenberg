Synopsis: 

In "The Panama Canal: A history and description of the enterprise," J. Saxon Mills takes readers on a captivating journey through the remarkable story of one of the world's greatest engineering feats. From its conception to its completion, Mills delves into the challenges, triumphs, and controversies surrounding the construction of the Panama Canal. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, he brings to life the daring visionaries, the backbreaking labor, and the political maneuvering that shaped this monumental project. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Engineering

Themes: Engineering marvels, Human perseverance, Political and economic impact
