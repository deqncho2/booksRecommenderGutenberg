Synopsis: In 'Hilda: A Story of Calcutta', Sara Jeannette Duncan takes readers on a captivating journey through the bustling streets of Calcutta, India. The story revolves around Hilda Howe, a young woman who finds herself caught between the traditions of British colonial society and the vibrant, complex world of India. As Hilda navigates the challenges of love, friendship, and cultural identity, she discovers a hidden side of Calcutta that will forever change her perspective. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, Duncan paints a vivid portrait of a city on the brink of change, and a woman on a quest for self-discovery.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Cultural Fiction

Themes: Cultural identity, Colonialism, Love and relationships, Social expectations
