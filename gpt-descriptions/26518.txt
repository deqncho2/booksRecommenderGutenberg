Synopsis: 
'Journal of an American Prisoner at Fort Malden and Quebec in the War of 1812' is a captivating firsthand account of James Reynolds' experiences as a prisoner during the War of 1812. Through his journal entries, Reynolds takes us on a gripping journey, detailing the harsh conditions, the camaraderie among fellow prisoners, and the constant uncertainty of their fate. From the initial capture at Fort Malden to the grueling journey to Quebec, Reynolds provides a unique perspective on the war and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: 
Historical Non-fiction, Memoir, War

Themes: 
Captivity, Survival, Patriotism
