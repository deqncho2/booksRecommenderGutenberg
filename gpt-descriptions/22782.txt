Synopsis: In "The Buddha: A Drama in Five Acts and Four Interludes," Carus, Paul takes us on a captivating journey through the life of Siddhartha Gautama, the historical figure who would become known as the Buddha. Set against the backdrop of ancient India, this dramatic tale explores the profound spiritual transformation of a young prince who forsakes his privileged life to seek enlightenment and liberate humanity from suffering. As Siddhartha faces various trials and encounters influential figures, the reader is drawn into a world of inner turmoil, self-discovery, and the pursuit of ultimate truth. Through vivid dialogue and poignant scenes, Carus masterfully brings to life the timeless story of the Buddha, leaving readers inspired and enlightened.

Genres: Drama, Historical Fiction, Spiritual

Themes: Enlightenment, Self-discovery, Suffering, Spiritual Awakening
