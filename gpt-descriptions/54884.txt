Synopsis: 

'The Tragedy of Fotheringay' takes readers on a captivating journey into the tumultuous life of Mary Queen of Scots, as seen through the eyes of her physician, D. Bourgoing. Based on Bourgoing's journal and unpublished manuscripts, this historical novel delves into the gripping events surrounding Mary's imprisonment at Fotheringay Castle. As secrets, political intrigue, and personal loyalties collide, the story unfolds with gripping intensity, leaving readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biographical Fiction, Political Drama

Themes: Power and Politics, Loyalty and Betrayal, Personal Sacrifice
