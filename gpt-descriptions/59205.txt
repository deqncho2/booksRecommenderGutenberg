Synopsis: 

In 'Feminism in Greek Literature from Homer to Aristotle', F. A. Wright takes us on a captivating journey through the ancient Greek world, exploring the often overlooked voices of women in their literature. From the epic poems of Homer to the philosophical musings of Aristotle, this book unveils the hidden narratives of strong and influential women who defied societal norms and challenged the patriarchal structures of their time. Through a meticulous analysis of various Greek texts, Wright sheds light on the struggles, triumphs, and complexities of these remarkable female characters, offering a fresh perspective on the rich tapestry of Greek literature.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Feminist Studies, Classical Studies

Themes: Gender roles, Women's agency, Patriarchy, Representation of women in literature
