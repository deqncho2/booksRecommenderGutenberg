Synopsis: In "Right Living as a Fine Art: A Study of Channing's Symphony as an Outline of the Ideal Life and Character," Newell Dwight Hillis delves into the profound teachings of William Ellery Channing, a prominent figure in American Unitarianism. Through an exploration of Channing's Symphony, Hillis presents a captivating analysis of the principles and practices that can guide individuals towards living a truly fulfilling and virtuous life. With thought-provoking insights and practical wisdom, this book offers a compelling roadmap for cultivating one's character and embracing the beauty of right living.

Genres: Philosophy, Self-help, Spiritual

Themes: Moral development, Personal growth, Ethical living
