Synopsis: In the fifth volume of "History of Friedrich II of Prussia," Carlyle delves into the tumultuous period of Frederick the Great's reign, exploring the complex dynamics of power, war, and diplomacy. As Frederick faces numerous challenges, including the Seven Years' War and the struggle for territorial expansion, Carlyle paints a vivid portrait of a ruler driven by ambition and a relentless pursuit of greatness. With meticulous research and captivating storytelling, this volume offers a gripping account of Frederick's triumphs and tribulations, leaving readers eager to uncover the fate of Prussia.

Genres: Historical biography, Military history, Political history

Themes: Ambition, Power struggles, War and diplomacy, Leadership
