Synopsis: 
Dubliners by James Joyce is a captivating collection of fifteen short stories that delve into the lives of ordinary people in early 20th century Dublin. From a young boy's encounter with a mysterious stranger to a woman's struggle with her unfulfilled desires, each story offers a glimpse into the complexities of human emotions and the challenges of living in a society constrained by tradition and stagnation. With vivid descriptions and subtle symbolism, Joyce paints a vivid portrait of Dublin and its inhabitants, exploring themes of paralysis, identity, and the search for meaning in a world filled with disillusionment and frustration.

Genres: 
Literary Fiction, Short Stories

Themes: 
Paralysis, Identity, Disillusionment, Frustration
