Synopsis: 

In the second volume of the New York Times Current History series, delve into the tumultuous period of April to September 1915, during the heart of the European War. This captivating collection of articles and reports from the renowned newspaper offers a vivid and comprehensive account of the events that unfolded during this critical time. From the battlefields to the diplomatic negotiations, from the shifting alliances to the impact on civilians, this volume provides a gripping narrative that brings the reader into the heart of the conflict. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War journalism, News analysis

Themes: World War I, International diplomacy, Military strategy, Humanitarian impact
