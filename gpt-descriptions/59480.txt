Synopsis: In 'The Malthusian Handbook: Designed to Induce Married People to Limit Their Families Within Their Means,' an anonymous author presents a thought-provoking guide that challenges societal norms and explores the consequences of unchecked population growth. Through a series of compelling arguments and practical advice, the author encourages married couples to consider the impact of their family size on their own well-being and the world around them. As readers delve into this controversial handbook, they will be confronted with provocative ideas that challenge their preconceived notions about family planning and the responsibilities of parenthood.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Commentary, Self-help

Themes: Population control, Family planning, Societal responsibility
