Synopsis: 
'Books Before Typography: A Primer of Information About the Invention of the Alphabet and the History of Book-Making up to the Invention of Movable Types; Typographic Technical Series for Apprentices #49' takes readers on a captivating journey through the origins of the alphabet and the fascinating history of book-making. From ancient civilizations to the invention of movable types, Frederick W. Hamilton delves into the evolution of written communication and the art of book production. This informative primer provides a comprehensive understanding of the foundations that paved the way for the modern world of typography.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Origins of written communication, Evolution of book-making, Historical significance of typography
