Synopsis: In 'Greater Greece and Greater Britain; and, George Washington, the Expander of England', Edward A. Freeman explores the fascinating connections between ancient Greece and the British Empire, drawing unexpected parallels between the two. He also delves into the role of George Washington, the first President of the United States, in shaping the expansion of England. With thought-provoking insights and historical analysis, Freeman uncovers the hidden links that connect these seemingly disparate worlds.

Genres: History, Comparative Literature, Political Science

Themes: Empire building, Historical connections, Leadership
