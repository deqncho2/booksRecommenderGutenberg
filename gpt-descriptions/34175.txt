Synopsis: 

In 'Domestic Animals: History and description of the horse, mule, cattle, sheep, swine, poultry and farm dogs; with directions for their management, breeding, crossing, rearing, feeding, and preparation for a profitable market; also their diseases and remedies. Together with full directions for the management of the dairy,' Richard Lamb Allen takes readers on a comprehensive journey through the world of domestic animals. From horses and mules to cattle, sheep, swine, poultry, and farm dogs, this book provides a detailed exploration of each animal's history, description, and management. Allen offers valuable insights into breeding, crossing, rearing, feeding, and preparing these animals for a profitable market. Furthermore, he delves into the diseases they may encounter and provides remedies to ensure their well-being. The book also includes full directions for managing a dairy, making it an indispensable guide for farmers and animal enthusiasts alike.

Genres: Non-fiction, Agriculture, Animal Husbandry

Themes: Animal management, Breeding, Farming techniques, Disease prevention, Dairy management
