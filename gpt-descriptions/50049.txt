Synopsis: In "King René d'Anjou and His Seven Queens" by Staley, Edgcumbe, embark on a captivating journey through the life of King René d'Anjou, a remarkable ruler of the 15th century. This enthralling historical account unveils the fascinating stories of the seven influential women who played pivotal roles in the king's life. From queens and mistresses to artists and poets, each queen brings her unique charm and influence to the court, shaping the destiny of a kingdom. As the narrative unfolds, secrets, betrayals, and unexpected alliances emerge, painting a vivid portrait of a king torn between love, duty, and power. With its rich historical backdrop and intricate web of relationships, this book offers a captivating glimpse into the world of medieval royalty.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Romance

Themes: Power and Politics, Love and Relationships, Gender Roles
