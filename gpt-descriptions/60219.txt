Synopsis: In "The Sexes in Science and History: An inquiry into the dogma of woman's inferiority to man," Eliza Burt Gamble delves into the long-standing belief of women's inferiority and challenges it with a compelling exploration of science and history. Through a meticulous examination of various disciplines, Gamble presents a thought-provoking argument that challenges societal norms and sheds light on the true potential of women. This captivating work invites readers to question deeply ingrained beliefs and consider the untapped contributions of women throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Feminist literature, Historical analysis

Themes: Gender equality, Women's empowerment, Societal norms, Historical bias
