Synopsis: 

In "Windows: A Book About Stained & Painted Glass," Lewis F. Day takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate world of stained and painted glass. From the awe-inspiring cathedrals of Europe to the hidden gems found in small churches and private residences, this book unveils the secrets and stories behind these mesmerizing works of art. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Day explores the historical, cultural, and artistic significance of stained and painted glass, revealing the craftsmanship and creativity that went into their creation. Whether you are an art enthusiast, a history buff, or simply curious about the beauty that lies within these windows, this book will leave you with a newfound appreciation for this timeless art form.

Genres: Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Stained Glass, Artistic Craftsmanship, Cultural Significance
