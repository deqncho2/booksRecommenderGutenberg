Synopsis: In the second volume of "The Life and Work of Susan B. Anthony," author Ida Husted Harper delves into the remarkable journey of one of the most influential figures in American history. Through a collection of public addresses, personal letters, and accounts from her contemporaries, Harper paints a vivid portrait of Susan B. Anthony's tireless efforts and unwavering dedication to the cause of women's suffrage. From her early activism to her pivotal role in the suffrage movement, this volume offers a comprehensive exploration of Anthony's life and legacy, shedding light on the challenges she faced and the triumphs she achieved. With a wealth of primary sources and firsthand accounts, readers are invited to witness the indomitable spirit of a woman who fought tirelessly for equality and social justice.

Genres: Biography, History, Women's Studies

Themes: Women's suffrage, Activism, Equality, Social justice
