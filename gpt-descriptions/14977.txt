Synopsis: In "The Red Record: Tabulated Statistics and Alleged Causes of Lynching in the United States," Ida B. Wells-Barnett delves into the dark and haunting reality of lynching in America. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, she exposes the shocking statistics and uncovers the underlying causes behind this brutal form of violence. As she fearlessly confronts the racial prejudice and systemic injustice that perpetuated lynching, Wells-Barnett's powerful words shed light on a painful chapter in American history that cannot be ignored.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Justice

Themes: Racial violence, Systemic injustice, Activism, Civil rights
