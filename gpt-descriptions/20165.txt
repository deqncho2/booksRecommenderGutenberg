Synopsis: In 'The Theory and Practice of Perspective', Storey, G. A. takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of art and perception. Through a combination of historical anecdotes, practical techniques, and philosophical musings, the author explores the fascinating interplay between the artist's eye and the viewer's mind. From the Renaissance masters to modern-day visionaries, this book delves into the secrets of perspective, revealing how it has shaped and continues to shape our understanding of the world around us. Prepare to be enthralled by the intricate dance between art and perception, as Storey, G. A. unveils the hidden depths of perspective.

Genres: Art, Non-fiction, History

Themes: Perception, Artistic Techniques, Historical Influence
