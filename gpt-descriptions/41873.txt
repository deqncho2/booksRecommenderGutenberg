Synopsis: In "The History of Prostitution: Its Extent, Causes, and Effects throughout the World," William W. Sanger delves into the intriguing and controversial topic of prostitution, exploring its historical roots, global prevalence, and the profound impact it has had on societies across time. From ancient civilizations to modern times, Sanger uncovers the complex web of factors that have contributed to the existence and evolution of this profession, shedding light on the social, economic, and cultural forces that have shaped its existence. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, Sanger offers a comprehensive examination of the causes and consequences of prostitution, challenging conventional beliefs and inviting readers to critically reflect on this enduring aspect of human history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Social norms and deviance, Gender and sexuality, Economic exploitation, Cultural perspectives, Power dynamics
