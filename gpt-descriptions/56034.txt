Synopsis: 

In 'Folklore of Scottish Lochs and Springs', James M. Mackinlay takes readers on a captivating journey through the mystical and enchanting world of Scottish folklore. From the depths of Loch Ness to the hidden springs of the Highlands, this book delves into the rich tapestry of legends, myths, and supernatural tales that have been passed down through generations.

Discover the haunting stories of water spirits, kelpies, and selkies that inhabit the lochs, and the ancient rituals and beliefs associated with these sacred bodies of water. Uncover the secrets of hidden springs said to possess healing powers and the mysterious creatures that guard them. Mackinlay's vivid descriptions and meticulous research bring these captivating tales to life, transporting readers to a realm where reality and myth intertwine.

Genres: Folklore, Mythology, Scottish History

Themes: Supernatural beings, Legends and myths, Scottish culture and traditions
