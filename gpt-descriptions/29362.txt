Synopsis: 
In 'The Goat-gland Transplantation', Sydney Blanshard Flower delves into the fascinating world of Dr. J.R. Brinkley, a controversial figure who claimed to have found a revolutionary cure for impotence. With over 600 operations performed on both men and women, Dr. Brinkley's method involved transplanting goat glands into his patients, promising them renewed vitality and sexual prowess. Flower explores the rise and fall of this extraordinary medical phenomenon, shedding light on the ethical dilemmas, societal reactions, and the ultimate impact of Dr. Brinkley's controversial practice.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical History, Biography

Themes: Medical ethics, Quackery, Sexual health, Scientific progress
