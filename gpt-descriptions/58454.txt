Synopsis: 

In "Joseph Rogers, M.D.: Reminiscences of a Workhouse Medical Officer," Joseph Rogers takes us on a captivating journey through his experiences as a medical officer in workhouses during the 19th century. From the grim conditions of the workhouses to the heart-wrenching stories of the destitute, Rogers provides a unique perspective on the struggles faced by the poor and the challenges he encountered in providing medical care. Through his vivid and poignant recollections, the reader is transported to a time when compassion and determination were essential in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical, Social Commentary

Themes: Poverty, Healthcare, Social Injustice
