Synopsis: In "The Trimmed Lamp, and Other Stories of the Four Million," Henry O. presents a collection of captivating short stories that delve into the lives of ordinary people living in New York City. From struggling artists to ambitious young women, each story offers a unique glimpse into the hopes, dreams, and challenges faced by the diverse inhabitants of the bustling metropolis. With wit, humor, and a touch of melancholy, Henry O. paints a vivid picture of the human experience in the early 20th century.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories, Realism

Themes: Urban life, Social class, Dreams and aspirations, Human connection
