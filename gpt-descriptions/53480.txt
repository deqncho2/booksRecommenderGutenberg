Synopsis: In 'The Leardo Map of the World, 1452 or 1453: In the Collections of the American Geographical Society', John Kirtland Wright takes readers on a captivating journey through time and space as he unravels the mysteries behind a remarkable map. This map, created in the 15th century by the Italian cartographer Giovanni Leardo, holds secrets that could reshape our understanding of the world. As Wright delves into the map's origins, he uncovers a web of intrigue, adventure, and hidden knowledge that spans continents and centuries. From ancient civilizations to uncharted territories, this book offers a thrilling exploration of the past and the power of maps to shape our perception of the world.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cartography, Adventure

Themes: Exploration, Knowledge, Cultural exchange
