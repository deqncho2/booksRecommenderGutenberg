Synopsis: In 'The Nature of Animal Light' by Harvey, E. Newton, a renowned biologist embarks on a captivating exploration of the mysterious phenomenon of bioluminescence in the animal kingdom. From the depths of the ocean to the darkest corners of the rainforest, the author unveils the enchanting world of creatures that emit their own light, captivating readers with tales of glowing jellyfish, fireflies, and even deep-sea anglerfish. As he delves deeper into the science behind this extraordinary ability, he uncovers surprising connections between bioluminescence and evolution, ecology, and even human health. With vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, this book takes readers on an illuminating journey through the wonders of animal light.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Nature

Themes: Bioluminescence, Evolution, Ecology, Natural Phenomena
