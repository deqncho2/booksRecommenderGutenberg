Synopsis: In 'The Education of the Child' by Ellen Key, a young girl named Anna embarks on a transformative journey of self-discovery and intellectual growth. Set in the late 19th century, Anna's unconventional upbringing challenges societal norms and traditional educational methods. As she navigates through various educational experiences, Anna encounters influential figures who shape her understanding of the world and her place in it. With a captivating blend of personal anecdotes and philosophical reflections, Key explores the profound impact of education on a child's development, emphasizing the importance of nurturing individuality and fostering a love for learning.

Genres: Fiction, Bildungsroman, Philosophy

Themes: Education, Individuality, Self-discovery, Intellectual growth
