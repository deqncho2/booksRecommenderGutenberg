Synopsis: 

In 'Studies in Modern Music, Second Series: Frederick Chopin, Antonin Dvořák, Johannes Brahms', author W. H. Hadow delves into the lives and works of three influential composers of the 19th century. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Hadow uncovers the unique musical journeys of Chopin, Dvořák, and Brahms, exploring their struggles, triumphs, and the profound impact they had on the world of classical music. From Chopin's revolutionary compositions to Dvořák's exploration of nationalistic themes, and Brahms' mastery of symphonic forms, this book offers a captivating exploration of the lives and legacies of these musical geniuses.

Genres: Biography, Music History, Music Criticism

Themes: Artistic innovation, Nationalism, Musical legacy
