Synopsis: 
'Essays Irish and American' by Yeats, John Butler is a captivating collection of thought-provoking essays that explore the cultural and literary connections between Ireland and America. From examining the influence of Irish folklore on American literature to delving into the complexities of Irish identity in the context of American society, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the interplay between these two rich and diverse cultures. Through insightful analysis and engaging storytelling, Yeats sheds light on the shared experiences, struggles, and triumphs that have shaped both Irish and American literature and identity.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, Cultural Studies

Themes: 
Cultural identity, Literary influence, Nationalism, Transatlantic connections
