Synopsis: In "Seldwyla Folks: Three Singular Tales," Keller, Gottfried takes readers on a captivating journey through the idyllic Swiss village of Seldwyla, where eccentric characters and unexpected events intertwine. In the first tale, a young artist finds himself caught between his passion for painting and the demands of society. The second story follows a cunning trickster who uses his wit to navigate the complexities of love and marriage. Lastly, a mysterious stranger arrives in Seldwyla, bringing with him a series of inexplicable events that challenge the villagers' beliefs and values. With vivid descriptions and a touch of whimsy, Keller invites readers to explore the human condition and the complexities of life in a small community.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories, Regional Literature
Themes: Identity, Love and Relationships, Social Conventions
