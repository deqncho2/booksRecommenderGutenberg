Synopsis: In "The Life of Samuel Taylor Coleridge: 1838," Gillman, James takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous life of one of the greatest English poets of all time. From Coleridge's early struggles with addiction and his profound friendship with William Wordsworth, to his literary achievements and philosophical musings, this biography delves deep into the complex mind of a brilliant yet tormented artist. With vivid descriptions and intimate anecdotes, Gillman paints a vivid portrait of Coleridge's genius and the profound impact he had on the Romantic movement.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Addiction, Friendship, Artistic Genius, Romanticism
