Synopsis: 

In 'Report of the Proceedings at the Examination of Charles G. Davis, Esq., on the Charge of Aiding and Abetting in the Rescue of a Fugitive Slave: Held in Boston, in February, 1851,' Charles G. Davis finds himself at the center of a controversial trial that shakes the foundations of justice and morality. Accused of aiding and abetting in the rescue of a fugitive slave, Davis faces a courtroom filled with tension, passion, and conflicting ideologies. As the examination unfolds, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and the true nature of freedom and justice is put on trial.

Genres: Legal drama, Historical non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Morality, Justice, Freedom
