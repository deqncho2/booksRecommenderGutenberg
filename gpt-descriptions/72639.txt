Synopsis: In "Economic Effects of the World War upon Women and Children in Great Britain," Hobbs and Andrews delve into the often overlooked consequences of war on the most vulnerable members of society. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, they explore the profound impact of the war on women and children in Great Britain, shedding light on the economic struggles they faced and the resilience they displayed in the face of adversity. This thought-provoking work uncovers a hidden chapter of history, revealing the untold stories of those who bore the brunt of war's aftermath.

Genres: Historical research, Social commentary, Gender studies

Themes: Gender inequality, Socioeconomic impact, Resilience, War's aftermath
