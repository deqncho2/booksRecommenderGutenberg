Synopsis: In "The City of Domes: a walk with an architect about the courts and palaces of the Panama-Pacific International Exposition, with a discussion of its architecture, its sculpture, its mural decorations, its coloring and its lighting, preceded by a history of its growth," John D. Barry takes readers on a captivating journey through the magnificent Panama-Pacific International Exposition. As an architect, Barry provides a detailed exploration of the exposition's stunning architecture, sculptures, mural decorations, coloring, and lighting. Alongside this visual feast, he also delves into the exposition's history, tracing its growth and development. This immersive experience offers readers a unique perspective on the grandeur and artistic marvels of the Panama-Pacific International Exposition.

Genres: Non-fiction, Architecture, History

Themes: Artistic beauty, Architectural innovation, Historical significance
