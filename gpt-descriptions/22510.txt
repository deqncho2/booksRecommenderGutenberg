Synopsis: In 'Documentary History of the Rio Grande Pueblos of New Mexico; I. Bibliographic Introduction: Papers of the School of American Archaeology, No. 13', Adolph Francis Alphonse Bandelier takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and cultural heritage of the Rio Grande Pueblos of New Mexico. Through meticulous research and analysis, Bandelier uncovers a treasure trove of documents, manuscripts, and records that shed light on the fascinating past of these indigenous communities. From ancient rituals and traditions to the impact of Spanish colonization, this documentary history offers a comprehensive and engrossing exploration of the Rio Grande Pueblos' evolution over time.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Archaeology, Anthropology

Themes: Indigenous history, Cultural heritage, Colonialism, Ethnography
