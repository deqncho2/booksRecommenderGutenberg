Synopsis: 

In 'Project Gutenberg (1971-2005)' by Marie Lebert, embark on a captivating journey through the evolution of the digital age. This enthralling work delves into the fascinating history of Project Gutenberg, a groundbreaking initiative that revolutionized the way we access and share literature. From its humble beginnings in 1971 to its remarkable growth over the next three decades, witness the tireless efforts of dedicated individuals who sought to make knowledge accessible to all. As the story unfolds, encounter the challenges, triumphs, and controversies that shaped this iconic project, ultimately leaving an indelible mark on the world of literature.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Technology

Themes: Digital revolution, Accessibility, Information sharing
