Synopsis: In "The Economic Position of Women," the Academy of Political Science in the City of New York delves into a thought-provoking exploration of the multifaceted challenges faced by women in the realm of economics. Through a comprehensive analysis of historical context, societal norms, and policy implications, this work sheds light on the complex dynamics that have shaped and continue to shape the economic position of women. From examining the gender wage gap to exploring the impact of gender roles on career advancement, this book offers a compelling and enlightening perspective on the economic struggles and triumphs of women throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, Gender Studies

Themes: Gender inequality, Economic empowerment, Societal norms, Policy implications
