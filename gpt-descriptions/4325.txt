Synopsis: In 'The New Book of Martyrs' by Georges Duhamel, a renowned surgeon finds himself caught in a web of moral dilemmas as he navigates the complex world of medicine and ethics. Set in the early 20th century, the story follows Dr. Lucien Brémont as he grapples with the consequences of his decisions, the clash between tradition and progress, and the profound impact of his work on both his patients and himself. As he faces personal and professional challenges, Dr. Brémont must confront the true meaning of sacrifice and the boundaries of human compassion.

Genres: Fiction, Medical Drama, Historical Fiction

Themes: Ethics, Morality, Progress, Sacrifice, Compassion
