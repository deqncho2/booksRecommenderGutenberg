Synopsis: 

'A New Orchard And Garden: or, The best way for planting, grafting, and to make any ground good, for a rich Orchard: Particularly in the North and generally for the whole kingdome of England' is a comprehensive guidebook written by Harward Simon and William Lawson. This enlightening work delves into the art of cultivating orchards and gardens, providing invaluable insights into the best techniques for planting, grafting, and improving soil quality. Focusing on the specific needs of the North of England, while also offering advice applicable to the entire kingdom, the authors share their expertise to help readers create thriving orchards and gardens.

Genres: Non-fiction, Gardening, Horticulture

Themes: Horticultural techniques, Soil improvement, Orchard cultivation
