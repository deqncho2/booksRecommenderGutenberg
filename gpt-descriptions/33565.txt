Synopsis: In 'The Bachelors: A Novel' by William Dana Orcutt, we are introduced to a group of young men living in New York City during the early 20th century. Each bachelor has their own unique personality, dreams, and struggles as they navigate the complexities of love, friendship, and societal expectations. As their paths intertwine, secrets are revealed, relationships are tested, and the true nature of their characters is unveiled. This captivating novel takes readers on a journey filled with romance, ambition, and the pursuit of happiness, leaving them eager to discover the fate of these intriguing bachelors.

Genres: Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-age

Themes: Love and relationships, Friendship, Identity, Ambition, Society and societal expectations
