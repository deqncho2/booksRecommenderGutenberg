Synopsis: In 'Eighteenth Century Waifs' by John Ashton, embark on a captivating journey through the lives of forgotten children in 18th-century England. From the bustling streets of London to the tranquil countryside, this book unveils the hidden stories of young orphans, beggars, and vagabonds struggling to survive in a harsh and unforgiving world. Through vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, Ashton sheds light on the hardships, adventures, and unexpected triumphs of these resilient waifs, painting a vivid picture of a bygone era.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Social Commentary, Adventure

Themes: Poverty and Survival, Resilience, Social Inequality
