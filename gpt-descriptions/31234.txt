Synopsis: In 'A History of the English Church in New Zealand', Henry Thomas Purchas takes readers on a captivating journey through the establishment and growth of the Anglican Church in New Zealand. From the early missionary efforts to the challenges faced by the Church in a rapidly changing society, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the struggles, triumphs, and enduring legacy of the English Church in this distant land. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Purchas brings to life the key figures, events, and controversies that shaped the Church's history, providing a rich tapestry of religious, cultural, and social developments in New Zealand.

Genres: History, Religious Studies, Biography

Themes: Missionary work, Colonialism, Cultural assimilation, Religious identity, Social change
