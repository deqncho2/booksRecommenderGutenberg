Synopsis: 

In "The Surrender of Napoleon," Sir Frederick Lewis Maitland takes us on a captivating journey into one of history's most significant moments - the surrender of Napoleon Bonaparte. Set aboard the H.M.S. Bellerophon, this narrative provides a detailed account of the events that unfolded between May 24th and August 8th, 1815, during Napoleon's residence on the ship. Maitland's firsthand perspective offers a unique insight into the interactions, conversations, and intrigues that took place during this crucial period. As the reader delves into the pages of this enthralling historical account, they will be transported back in time to witness the final chapter of Napoleon's extraordinary life.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Power and downfall, Historical events and figures
