Synopsis: In "The Riddle of the Rhine: Chemical Strategy in Peace and War" by Victor Lefebure, a captivating exploration of the strategic use of chemicals unfolds. Set against the backdrop of World War I, this gripping non-fiction work delves into the intricate world of chemical warfare, revealing the secrets behind the development and deployment of deadly gases. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Lefebure unveils the shocking reality of this unconventional form of warfare, shedding light on the profound impact it had on the course of history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Military

Themes: Chemical warfare, Strategy, World War I, Technological advancements
