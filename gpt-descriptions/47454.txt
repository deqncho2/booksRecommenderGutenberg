Synopsis: 
In 'Egoists, A Book of Supermen', James Huneker takes us on a captivating journey through the lives and minds of some of the most influential figures in literature and philosophy. From Stendhal to Nietzsche, and from Baudelaire to Ibsen, Huneker delves into the complexities of their characters, their creative processes, and the impact they had on the world around them. Through vivid and insightful portraits, he explores the struggles, passions, and rebellions that shaped these extraordinary individuals, revealing the essence of their genius and the profound ideas that continue to resonate today.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Philosophy

Themes: Individualism, Artistic Expression, Rebellion, Intellectualism
