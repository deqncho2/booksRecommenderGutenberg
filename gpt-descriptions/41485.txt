Synopsis: In "Makers and Romance of Alabama History," B.F. Riley takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and often overlooked history of Alabama. From the early Native American tribes to the struggles of the Civil War and the birth of the civil rights movement, Riley weaves together a tapestry of fascinating stories and characters that shaped the state's identity. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, this book offers a compelling exploration of Alabama's past, shedding light on the triumphs, tragedies, and enduring spirit of its people.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Cultural studies

Themes: History, Identity, Resilience, Cultural heritage
