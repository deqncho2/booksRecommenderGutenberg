Synopsis: 
'Under Fire: The Story of a Squad' by Henri Barbusse takes readers on a gripping journey into the heart of World War I. Set in the trenches of the Western Front, the story follows a group of soldiers from different backgrounds as they navigate the horrors and camaraderie of war. Through their eyes, we witness the relentless battles, the fear and courage, the bonds formed amidst chaos, and the profound impact of war on the human spirit. As the squad faces the harsh realities of combat, their individual stories intertwine, revealing the complex nature of humanity in the face of adversity.

Genres: War fiction, Historical fiction, Literary fiction

Themes: War and its effects on individuals and society, Brotherhood and camaraderie, The fragility of life, The dehumanizing nature of war, The search for meaning in the midst of chaos
