Synopsis: 
In 'A Great Man: A Frolic' by Arnold Bennett, the story revolves around the life of a renowned artist, Edwin Clayhanger, who finds himself caught between his passion for art and the expectations of his family. As Edwin navigates the complexities of love, ambition, and societal pressures, he must make difficult choices that will shape his future. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century England, this captivating novel explores the inner struggles of a man torn between his own desires and the responsibilities placed upon him.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Coming-of-age

Themes: Artistic expression, Family expectations, Love and relationships, Individuality
