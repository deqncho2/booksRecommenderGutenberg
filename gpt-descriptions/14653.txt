Synopsis: 
In 'The Feast of St. Friend' by Arnold Bennett, a young man named Denry Machin finds himself caught up in a whirlwind of social events and unexpected opportunities. From humble beginnings, Denry's ambition and resourcefulness lead him to navigate the upper echelons of society, where he becomes the center of attention and the object of desire for many. As he rises to prominence, Denry must confront the consequences of his actions and make difficult choices that will shape his future. With wit, charm, and a touch of mischief, 'The Feast of St. Friend' takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of ambition, social climbing, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Comedy

Themes: Ambition, Social status, Morality, Self-discovery
