Synopsis: 

'The Nation's Peril: Twelve Years' Experience in the South. Then and Now. The Ku Klux Klan, a Complete Exposition of the Order: Its Purpose, Plans, Operations, Social and Political Significance; The Nation's Salvation.' takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous years following the Civil War in the United States. Written by an anonymous author, this book provides a firsthand account of the rise and activities of the notorious Ku Klux Klan, shedding light on their hidden agenda and the impact they had on society.

Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author unveils the true purpose, plans, and operations of the Ku Klux Klan, delving into their social and political significance during a time of great upheaval. The narrative explores the nation's perilous state, both then and now, as it grapples with the aftermath of war and the challenges of reconstruction.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political commentary, Social commentary

Themes: Racism, Power and control, Social justice, Historical memory
