Synopsis: 

In "Little Folks of North America: Stories about children living in the different parts of North America," Mary Hazelton Blanchard Wade takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse lives of children across North America. From the bustling streets of New York City to the serene landscapes of the Canadian wilderness, this collection of stories introduces us to a colorful cast of characters, each with their own unique experiences and adventures. Through heartwarming tales of friendship, bravery, and discovery, readers will be transported to different corners of the continent, gaining a deeper understanding of the rich tapestry of cultures and traditions that make up North America.

Genres: Children's Literature, Adventure, Cultural Fiction

Themes: Diversity, Friendship, Exploration, Cultural Exchange
