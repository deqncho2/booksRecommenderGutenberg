Synopsis: In "Marie Antoinette and the Downfall of Royalty," Imbert de Saint-Amand takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of one of history's most iconic queens. From her opulent upbringing in Austria to her marriage to the future King of France, Marie Antoinette's story is one of glamour, intrigue, and ultimately, tragedy. As the French Revolution looms, the young queen finds herself caught in a web of political unrest, scandal, and public scrutiny. Imbert de Saint-Amand's vivid storytelling brings to life the complexities of Marie Antoinette's character, her struggles as a woman in power, and the dramatic events that led to the downfall of the French monarchy.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and privilege, Gender roles, Revolution and social change
