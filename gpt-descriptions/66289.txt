Synopsis: 
In 'The Man Who Made the World' by Richard Matheson, a brilliant scientist named Dr. Jonathan Seward makes a groundbreaking discovery that has the potential to change the world as we know it. However, as he delves deeper into his creation, he begins to question the consequences of his actions and the ethical implications of playing god. As the line between creator and creation blurs, Dr. Seward finds himself in a moral dilemma that could have far-reaching consequences.

Genres: Science Fiction, Thriller, Psychological

Themes: Morality, Responsibility, Hubris
