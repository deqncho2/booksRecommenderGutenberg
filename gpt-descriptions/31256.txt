Synopsis: In 'How the Black St. Domingo Legion Saved the Patriot Army in the Siege of Savannah, 1779: The American Negro Academy. Occasional Papers No. 5', T. G. Steward delves into a little-known chapter of American history, revealing the remarkable story of the Black St. Domingo Legion and their crucial role in the Siege of Savannah in 1779. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Steward uncovers the bravery and heroism of these African American soldiers who fought alongside the Patriot Army, ultimately altering the course of the Revolutionary War. This captivating account sheds light on a forgotten aspect of the struggle for independence and highlights the significant contributions of black soldiers during this pivotal period in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, African American history

Themes: African American contributions to the Revolutionary War, Overcoming adversity, Forgotten history
