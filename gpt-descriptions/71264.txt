Synopsis: In "The Irish Rebellion of 1916: Or, The Unbroken Tradition," Nora Connolly O'Brien takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of the Easter Rising in Ireland. Through the eyes of her father, James Connolly, a key figure in the rebellion, O'Brien paints a vivid picture of the sacrifices, bravery, and unwavering determination of those who fought for Irish independence. As the story unfolds, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and the true cost of freedom becomes clear. This gripping historical account will leave readers on the edge of their seats, eager to uncover the fate of Ireland and its people.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biographical Fiction, Political Drama

Themes: Nationalism, Sacrifice, Revolution, Family, Identity
