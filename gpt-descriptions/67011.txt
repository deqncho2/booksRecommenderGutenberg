Synopsis: In "A poem on the earthquake at Lisbon" by Biddolf, John, the reader is taken on a captivating journey through the devastating earthquake that struck Lisbon. Through vivid imagery and powerful language, the poem explores the profound impact of this natural disaster on the city and its inhabitants. As the poem unfolds, the reader is left pondering the fragility of human existence and the complex relationship between humanity and the forces of nature.

Genres: Poetry, Historical, Natural Disaster

Themes: Human fragility, Nature's power, Existentialism
