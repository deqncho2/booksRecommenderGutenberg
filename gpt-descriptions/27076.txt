Synopsis: 

In "Drug Supplies in the American Revolution," Griffenhagen, George B. takes readers on a captivating journey through the often overlooked aspect of medicine and pharmaceuticals during the American Revolution. Delving into the fascinating world of apothecaries, military hospitals, and the challenges faced by both the British and American forces, this book uncovers the crucial role that drug supplies played in shaping the outcome of the war. From the scarcity of essential medicines to the innovative methods employed to overcome these obstacles, Griffenhagen paints a vivid picture of the medical landscape of the time. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this book sheds light on a lesser-known aspect of the American Revolution, offering a fresh perspective on the war that shaped a nation.

Genres: History, Military History, Medical History

Themes: Medicine and healthcare in war, Supply chain logistics, Revolutionary War history
