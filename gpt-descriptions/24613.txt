Synopsis: In "The Negro and the Nation: A History of American Slavery and Enfranchisement," George Spring Merriam delves into the complex and often overlooked history of American slavery and the subsequent struggle for enfranchisement. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Merriam uncovers the untold stories of African Americans and their pivotal role in shaping the nation's history. From the horrors of slavery to the fight for civil rights, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of the African American experience and its profound impact on the nation.

Genres: History, Non-fiction, African American Studies

Themes: Slavery, Civil Rights, Racism, Social Justice
