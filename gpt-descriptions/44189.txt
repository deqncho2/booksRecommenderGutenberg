Synopsis: 

In "Florida: Past and Present," Samuel C. Upham takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and vibrant present of the Sunshine State. From the early Native American settlements to the arrival of European explorers, Upham delves into the fascinating past of Florida, uncovering tales of conquest, colonization, and cultural exchange. But this book is not just a historical account; it also offers a vivid portrayal of the present-day Florida, with its diverse landscapes, unique climate, and abundant natural resources. Upham's detailed observations from Sunland, located on the Manatee River in the Gulf Coast of South Florida, provide valuable insights into the region's climate, soil, and the remarkable variety of its productions. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travelogue, Natural history

Themes: Florida history, Cultural exchange, Environmental diversity
