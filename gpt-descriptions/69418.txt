Synopsis: 
In "Impressions of Ukiyo-ye, the school of the Japanese colour-print artists," Amsden, Dora takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of Ukiyo-e, the traditional Japanese art form. Through vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Amsden explores the intricate techniques and profound beauty of these mesmerizing woodblock prints. From the bustling streets of Edo to the serene landscapes of Kyoto, the reader is transported to a realm where art and life intertwine, revealing the essence of Japanese culture and society. With each turn of the page, Amsden unveils the secrets behind the creation of these masterpieces, shedding light on the artists' inspirations, their innovative use of color and composition, and the profound impact Ukiyo-e had on the art world. This book is a captivating exploration of a timeless art form that continues to captivate and inspire audiences worldwide.

Genres: Art History, Cultural Studies, Non-fiction

Themes: Japanese art, Ukiyo-e, Traditional culture, Aesthetics, Influence of art
