Synopsis: 
In 'Letters Concerning Poetical Translations: And Virgil's and Milton's Arts of Verse, &c.', William Benson takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of poetic translations. Through a series of letters, Benson explores the art of translating poetry, delving into the works of renowned poets such as Virgil and Milton. With insightful analysis and thought-provoking discussions, Benson sheds light on the intricacies of poetic expression and the challenges faced by translators. This engaging work offers a unique perspective on the power and beauty of poetry, leaving readers eager to explore the art of translation themselves.

Genres: 
Literary Criticism, Poetry, Epistolary

Themes: 
Poetic translation, Artistic expression, Challenges of translation
