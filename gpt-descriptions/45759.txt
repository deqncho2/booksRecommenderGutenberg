Synopsis: In 'London and the Kingdom - Volume 3', Reginald R. Sharpe takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of London, drawing primarily from the archives at Guildhall. From political intrigue to social upheaval, this meticulously researched volume unveils the hidden stories and forgotten events that shaped the city and its relationship with the kingdom. As the secrets of London's past are unveiled, readers will be transported to a bygone era, where power struggles, scandals, and triumphs intertwine to create a vivid tapestry of the city's history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, archival research, urban history

Themes: Power and politics, social change, historical preservation
