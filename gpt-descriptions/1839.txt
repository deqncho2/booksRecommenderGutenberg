Synopsis: In 'Other Things Being Equal' by Emma Wolf, a young woman named Rachel is torn between her duty to her family and her desire for personal freedom. Set in the late 19th century, Rachel finds herself caught in a web of societal expectations and the limitations placed upon her as a woman. As she navigates through love, family secrets, and the pursuit of her own dreams, Rachel must make difficult choices that will shape her future. This captivating novel explores the complexities of identity, love, and the struggle for independence in a time of societal constraints.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Gender roles, Family duty, Personal freedom, Love and sacrifice
