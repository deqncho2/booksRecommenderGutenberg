Synopsis: In "This Troubled World," Eleanor Roosevelt takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events that shaped the mid-20th century. From the rise of fascism to the challenges of the Great Depression, Roosevelt's unique perspective as a prominent political figure and humanitarian offers a compelling narrative that explores the complexities of power, resilience, and the pursuit of justice. Through her vivid storytelling, she sheds light on the struggles and triumphs of individuals and nations, ultimately leaving readers with a profound understanding of the human spirit and its capacity for change.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, memoir, political commentary

Themes: Power and politics, resilience, social justice, human rights
