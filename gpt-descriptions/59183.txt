Synopsis: In 'Psychology and Pedagogy of Anger' by Richardson, Roy Franklin, delve into the complex world of anger and its impact on human behavior. This thought-provoking book explores the intricate relationship between psychology and pedagogy, shedding light on the underlying causes and consequences of anger. Through insightful analysis and real-life examples, Richardson offers a fresh perspective on how anger can shape our lives and the lives of those around us. Prepare to be captivated by this compelling exploration of one of our most powerful emotions.

Genres: Psychology, Education, Self-help

Themes: Anger management, Emotional intelligence, Personal growth
