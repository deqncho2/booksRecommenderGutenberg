Synopsis: In 'The Leatherworker in Eighteenth-Century Williamsburg', author Thomas K. Ford takes readers on a fascinating journey into the world of leather craftsmanship in colonial America. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Ford explores the nature of leather and the various crafts involved in its making and usage during the 18th century. From tanning hides to creating intricate leather goods, the book provides a captivating glimpse into the lives and skills of the leatherworkers of Williamsburg. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Craftsmanship, Colonial America

Themes: Leatherworking, Craftsmanship, Colonial life, Material culture
