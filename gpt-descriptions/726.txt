Synopsis: In "A Psychological Counter-Current in Recent Fiction," William Dean Howells delves into the intricate depths of the human mind as he explores the psychological undercurrents present in contemporary literature. Through a series of insightful analyses, Howells uncovers the hidden motivations, desires, and conflicts that shape the characters and narratives of modern fiction. From the complexities of love and identity to the struggles of societal expectations, this thought-provoking work offers a captivating exploration of the human psyche and its impact on storytelling.

Genres: Literary criticism, psychology, cultural studies

Themes: Psychology, human nature, identity, societal expectations
