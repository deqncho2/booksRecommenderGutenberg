Synopsis:
In "Joseph Conrad," Hugh Walpole delves into the life of one of the most enigmatic and influential writers of the 20th century. From Conrad's early years as a sailor, navigating treacherous waters and encountering exotic cultures, to his later struggles with personal demons and the challenges of being a writer in a rapidly changing world, Walpole paints a vivid portrait of a man torn between his love for adventure and his longing for stability. Through a masterful blend of biography and literary analysis, Walpole takes us on a captivating journey through Conrad's life, revealing the fascinating inspirations behind his timeless works.

Genres:
Biography, Literary Criticism, Adventure

Themes:
Identity, Colonialism, Morality, Human Nature
