Synopsis: In 'The Internet and Languages [around the year 2000]' by Marie Lebert, embark on a captivating exploration of the intricate relationship between the internet and languages. Delve into the fascinating world of online communication, where languages evolve, adapt, and sometimes even clash. Discover how the internet has become a powerful catalyst for linguistic diversity, connecting people from all corners of the globe while simultaneously challenging traditional linguistic boundaries. Through thought-provoking anecdotes and insightful analysis, Lebert sheds light on the profound impact of the internet on our languages, offering a glimpse into a rapidly changing linguistic landscape.

Genres: Non-fiction, Linguistics, Technology

Themes: Language evolution, Online communication, Linguistic diversity, Global connectivity
