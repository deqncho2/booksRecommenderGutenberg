Synopsis: 

'Arguments before the Committee on Patents of the House of Representatives, on H. R. 11943, to Amend Title 60, Chapter 3, of the Revised Statutes of the United States Relating to Copyrights: May 2, 1906' takes readers behind the scenes of a crucial congressional hearing that could shape the future of copyright laws in the United States. Through a series of compelling arguments and discussions, the book explores the complexities and controversies surrounding copyright legislation, shedding light on the various perspectives and interests at play. This thought-provoking work provides a unique glimpse into the inner workings of the government and the ongoing battle to balance the rights of creators and the public.

Genres: Non-fiction, Legal, Government

Themes: Copyright law, Intellectual property, Legislative process
