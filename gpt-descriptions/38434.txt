Synopsis: In "Lincoln's Use of the Bible," S. Trevena Jackson delves into the profound influence that the Bible had on Abraham Lincoln, one of America's most revered presidents. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Jackson explores how Lincoln's deep understanding of biblical texts shaped his moral compass, political decisions, and leadership style. From his childhood to his presidency, this captivating exploration reveals the profound impact of the Bible on Lincoln's life and legacy.

Genres: Biography, History, Religious Studies

Themes: Religion and Politics, Leadership, Moral Guidance
