Synopsis: In 'The Arrow-Maker: A Drama in Three Acts' by Mary Austin, a small Native American community is faced with a crisis that threatens their way of life. As tensions rise and conflicts emerge, the community must navigate the complexities of tradition, identity, and the struggle for survival. Through the captivating dialogue and powerful character interactions, the play explores the profound impact of change and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Drama, Historical Fiction, Cultural

Themes: Tradition vs. Modernity, Identity, Survival, Community
