Synopsis: In 'Justice' by John Galsworthy, a young man named Robert is accused of a crime he claims he did not commit. As the trial unfolds, the reader is taken on a gripping journey through the complexities of the legal system, the pursuit of truth, and the consequences of one's actions. With unexpected twists and moral dilemmas, 'Justice' explores the lengths one will go to seek justice and the impact it has on individuals and society.

Genres: Legal drama, psychological thriller, social commentary

Themes: Justice and injustice, truth and deception, morality and ethics, the power of the legal system, the consequences of actions
