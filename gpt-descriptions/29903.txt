Synopsis: In "The Sexual Question," Auguste Forel delves into a comprehensive exploration of human sexuality, examining it from scientific, psychological, hygienic, and sociological perspectives. With a captivating blend of research, analysis, and thought-provoking insights, Forel navigates the complex terrain of human desire, attraction, and relationships. From the biological underpinnings of sexual behavior to the societal implications of sexual norms, this groundbreaking study sheds light on the multifaceted nature of human sexuality, challenging conventional beliefs and offering a fresh understanding of this fundamental aspect of human existence.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Sociology

Themes: Human sexuality, Gender roles, Social norms, Psychological aspects of desire and attraction
