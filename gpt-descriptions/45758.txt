Synopsis: In 'The I. W. W.: A Study of American Syndicalism', Paul F. Brissenden delves into the fascinating world of the Industrial Workers of the World (I.W.W.), a radical labor union that emerged in the early 20th century. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Brissenden explores the origins, ideology, and impact of this influential organization, shedding light on the struggles and triumphs of American syndicalism. From the fiery speeches of charismatic leaders to the dramatic clashes with powerful corporations, this book offers a captivating exploration of a pivotal moment in American labor history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Labor rights, Radicalism, Social justice
