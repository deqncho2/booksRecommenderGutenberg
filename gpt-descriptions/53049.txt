Synopsis: 
Instead of the Thorn: A Novel by Clara Louise Burnham is a captivating story that follows the life of a young woman named Mary, who finds herself torn between societal expectations and her own desires. Set in the late 19th century, Mary's journey takes her from the confines of her small New England town to the bustling streets of Boston, where she encounters a world filled with both opportunities and challenges. As she navigates through love, loss, and self-discovery, Mary must confront the thorny choices that lie before her and ultimately decide whether to conform to the expectations of others or forge her own path.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Identity, Independence, Societal Expectations
