Synopsis: 
Step into the hidden world of ancient Rome and uncover the secrets that lie behind the grandeur and power. In 'The Private Life of the Romans', Harold Whetstone Johnston takes us on a captivating journey through the daily lives of the Romans, revealing their intimate rituals, scandalous love affairs, and intriguing social customs. From the bustling streets of Rome to the opulent villas of the elite, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the private lives of one of history's most influential civilizations.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural studies, Anthropology

Themes: Roman society, Daily life, Social customs, Love and relationships, Power dynamics
