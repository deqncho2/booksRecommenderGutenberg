Synopsis: 

'Out of the Briars: An Autobiography and Sketch of the Twenty-ninth Regiment Connecticut Volunteers' takes readers on a captivating journey through the experiences of Alexander Herritage Newton, a soldier in the American Civil War. From the initial call to arms to the harrowing battles and the camaraderie forged in the face of adversity, Newton's memoir offers a firsthand account of the sacrifices and triumphs of the Twenty-ninth Regiment Connecticut Volunteers. With vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, the author paints a vivid picture of the realities of war and the indomitable spirit of those who fought.

Genres: Autobiography, Military History, Memoir

Themes: Patriotism, Sacrifice, Brotherhood, Resilience
