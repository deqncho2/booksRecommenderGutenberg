Synopsis: 
'Press Cuttings' by Bernard Shaw is a satirical play that takes a humorous and thought-provoking look at the suffragette movement in early 20th century England. The story revolves around a suffragette leader who finds herself in a series of absurd situations, including a potential war between England and a fictional country. As the play unfolds, Shaw cleverly explores the themes of gender equality, political satire, and the power of the media.

Genres: 
Satire, Comedy, Political Drama

Themes: 
Gender Equality, Suffragette Movement, Political Satire, Media Influence
