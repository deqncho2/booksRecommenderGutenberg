Synopsis: In 'We're Friends, Now' by Hasse, Henry, a chance encounter between two strangers leads to an unexpected friendship that transcends societal norms. As they navigate the complexities of their relationship, they discover the power of genuine connection and the transformative nature of acceptance. With each passing day, their bond grows stronger, challenging their preconceived notions and forcing them to confront their own vulnerabilities. But as their friendship deepens, they must confront the inevitable question: Can they truly be friends, or is there something more between them?

Genres: Contemporary Fiction, LGBTQ+, Romance

Themes: Friendship, Identity, Acceptance, Love
