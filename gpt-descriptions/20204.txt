Synopsis: In "The Storm-Cloud of the Nineteenth Century," John Ruskin delivers two captivating lectures that delve into the ominous atmosphere of the era. With a keen eye for detail and a powerful command of language, Ruskin explores the metaphorical storm-cloud that hangs over society, examining its causes and consequences. Through thought-provoking analysis and vivid imagery, he sheds light on the challenges and uncertainties faced by the people of the nineteenth century. This enlightening work offers a unique perspective on the turbulent times and leaves readers pondering the storm-clouds that may still linger today.

Genres: Non-fiction, Essay, Lecture

Themes: Social criticism, Cultural analysis, Historical reflection
