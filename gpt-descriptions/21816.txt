Synopsis: 
In 'The Confidence-Man: His Masquerade', a diverse group of passengers embark on a steamboat journey down the Mississippi River. As they navigate the ever-changing social dynamics and encounter a mysterious stranger, their beliefs, identities, and trust are put to the test. With each encounter, the stranger assumes a new disguise, challenging the passengers' perceptions and forcing them to question their own motives and desires. In this intricate web of deception and self-discovery, Melville explores the complexities of human nature and the fragility of trust.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Psychological novel

Themes: Identity, Deception, Trust, Human nature
