Synopsis: In 'Political Thought in England from Locke to Bentham', Harold J. Laski takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of political ideas in England during the 17th and 18th centuries. From the influential writings of John Locke to the groundbreaking theories of Jeremy Bentham, Laski explores the intellectual landscape that shaped the foundations of modern political thought. With insightful analysis and engaging storytelling, he delves into the minds of these great thinkers, unraveling their ideas and examining their impact on society. This thought-provoking exploration of political philosophy will leave readers with a deeper understanding of the ideas that have shaped the political landscape of England and beyond.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Philosophy, History

Themes: Enlightenment, Liberalism, Individualism, Social Contract, Utilitarianism
