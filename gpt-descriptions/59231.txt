Synopsis: In "Robert Curthose, Duke of Normandy," Charles W. David delves into the captivating life of a forgotten historical figure. This meticulously researched biography takes readers on a journey through the tumultuous world of medieval Europe, following the enigmatic Robert Curthose, the eldest son of William the Conqueror. From his early days as a young prince to his struggles for power and his eventual downfall, this book unveils the complex character of a man caught between his ambitions and his loyalty to his family. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, David paints a vivid portrait of a fascinating figure whose legacy has been overshadowed by his more famous siblings. 

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Power and ambition, Family dynamics, Medieval Europe
