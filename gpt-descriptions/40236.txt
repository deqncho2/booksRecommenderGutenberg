Synopsis: In the second volume of his memoirs, Benjamin Franklin takes readers on a captivating journey through his life, sharing his most intriguing essays, letters, and miscellaneous writings. Carefully selected from all his published works, this collection encompasses a wide range of topics, including familiar anecdotes, moral reflections, political insights, economic theories, and philosophical musings. With a keen eye for what is both entertaining and valuable, Franklin offers a wealth of wisdom and wit that will captivate the general reader.

Genres: Autobiography, Essay, Letter Collection

Themes: Self-reflection, Morality, Politics, Economics, Philosophy
