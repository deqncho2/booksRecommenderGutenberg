Synopsis: In 'The Elements of Child-protection' by Engel, Sigmund, a renowned child psychologist, delves into the dark and complex world of child abuse and the measures necessary to protect vulnerable children. Through a series of gripping case studies and expert analysis, Engel uncovers the shocking realities that children face and provides invaluable insights into the psychological and societal factors that contribute to their victimization. With a compassionate and thought-provoking approach, this book explores the intricate web of child-protection, shedding light on the urgent need for awareness, prevention, and intervention. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Social Issues

Themes: Child abuse, Child protection, Psychological trauma, Societal responsibility
