Synopsis: In the second volume of "The Brontë Family, with special reference to Patrick Branwell Brontë," Francis A. Leyland delves deeper into the captivating lives of the Brontë siblings. Focusing on the enigmatic figure of Patrick Branwell Brontë, the author uncovers the untold stories, secrets, and struggles that shaped the talented family. From their isolated upbringing on the Yorkshire moors to their literary successes and personal tragedies, this volume offers a fascinating exploration of the Brontës' complex dynamics and the profound impact they had on each other's lives. As the narrative unfolds, readers are drawn into a world of creativity, passion, and resilience, where the bonds of family are tested and the pursuit of artistic expression becomes a means of survival.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction
Themes: Family dynamics, Artistic expression, Personal struggles, Literary legacy
