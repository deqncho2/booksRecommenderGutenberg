Synopsis: In "Giotto and his works in Padua," John Ruskin takes readers on a captivating journey through the stunning frescoes in the Arena Chapel. Through a series of woodcuts executed for the Arundel Society, Ruskin provides an insightful and explanatory notice of Giotto's masterpieces. Delving into the details and symbolism of each artwork, Ruskin's exploration of Giotto's works in Padua is a fascinating and enlightening experience.

Genres: Art history, Criticism, Non-fiction

Themes: Renaissance art, Symbolism, Interpretation
