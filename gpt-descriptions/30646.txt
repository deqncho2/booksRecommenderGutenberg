Synopsis: In "Woman under Socialism" by August Bebel, a thought-provoking exploration of the role of women in society unfolds. Through a series of compelling anecdotes and insightful analysis, Bebel challenges traditional gender norms and advocates for the emancipation of women. From examining the impact of capitalism on women's lives to envisioning a future where gender equality is achieved, this book offers a powerful critique of the prevailing social order and presents a compelling vision for a more just society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political philosophy, Feminism

Themes: Gender equality, Socialism, Capitalism, Women's rights
