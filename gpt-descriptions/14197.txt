Synopsis: In 'The Living Present' by Gertrude Franklin Horn Atherton, a young woman named Mary Stanhope finds herself caught in a web of secrets and intrigue when she inherits a mysterious estate in the English countryside. As she delves deeper into the history of the property, Mary uncovers a hidden world of forbidden love, family secrets, and a haunting presence that refuses to be forgotten. With each revelation, Mary's own life becomes entangled in the past, forcing her to confront her own desires and the choices she must make to secure her future.

Genres: Mystery, Gothic Fiction, Historical Fiction

Themes: Love and Desire, Family Secrets, Identity and Self-Discovery
