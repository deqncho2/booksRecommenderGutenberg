Synopsis: In 'Wood and Garden: Notes and thoughts, practical and critical, of a working amateur', Gertrude Jekyll takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of gardening. With her keen observations and practical advice, Jekyll shares her experiences as a working amateur, offering a unique perspective on the art of cultivating woodlands and gardens. From the beauty of wildflowers to the intricacies of garden design, Jekyll's musings will inspire both seasoned gardeners and novices alike. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Gardening, Memoir

Themes: Nature, Beauty, Design, Practicality
