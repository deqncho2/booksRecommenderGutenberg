Synopsis: In 'The Squirrel-Cage' by Dorothy Canfield Fisher, we follow the life of Elizabeth, a young woman who finds herself trapped in the monotonous routine of domesticity. As she navigates the challenges of marriage, motherhood, and societal expectations, Elizabeth yearns for something more. When an unexpected opportunity arises, she must decide whether to break free from the confines of her squirrel-cage existence or continue to conform to societal norms. This thought-provoking novel explores the complexities of personal fulfillment and the sacrifices one must make to find true happiness.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Coming-of-age

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, personal fulfillment, self-discovery
