Synopsis: In "Woodstock: An historical sketch," Clarence Winthrop Bowen takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of Woodstock, a small town nestled in the heart of New England. From its humble beginnings as a colonial settlement to its transformation into a thriving artistic community, Bowen unveils the fascinating stories and characters that have shaped Woodstock's identity over the centuries. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the author paints a vivid picture of the town's evolution, capturing the essence of its charm and resilience. As readers delve into the pages of this historical sketch, they will be transported to a bygone era, where they will witness the triumphs and tribulations of a community that has stood the test of time.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Local history, Biography

Themes: Community resilience, Cultural evolution, Historical preservation
