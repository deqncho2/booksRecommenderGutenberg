Synopsis: 

In "Fish Populations, Following a Drought, in the Neosho and Marais des Cygnes Rivers of Kansas," James E. Deacon takes readers on a captivating journey through the aftermath of a drought and its impact on fish populations in two rivers of Kansas. As the rivers slowly recover from the devastating effects of the drought, Deacon explores the intricate web of relationships between different fish species and their habitats. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, he unveils the surprising resilience and adaptability of these aquatic creatures, shedding light on the delicate balance of nature and the potential for recovery even in the face of adversity.

Genres: Non-fiction, Environmental Science, Natural History

Themes: Environmental resilience, Ecosystem dynamics, Conservation
