Synopsis: In "Poor Folk in Spain," Cora and Jan Gordon take readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant streets of Spain, immersing them in the lives of the country's impoverished inhabitants. Through vivid descriptions and heartfelt encounters, the authors shed light on the struggles, resilience, and indomitable spirit of the Spanish poor. From the bustling markets of Madrid to the picturesque villages of Andalusia, this book offers a poignant and eye-opening exploration of a society often overlooked. As the Gordons delve into the lives of these individuals, they uncover stories of hardship, hope, and the enduring power of human connection.

Genres: Travelogue, Non-fiction, Social commentary

Themes: Poverty, Resilience, Cultural exploration
