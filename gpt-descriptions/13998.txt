Synopsis: 

In 'Ireland and the Home Rule Movement', Michael McDonnell, Sir takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of Ireland's fight for self-governance. From the early days of the movement to the heated debates in Parliament, McDonnell delves into the political, social, and cultural factors that shaped this pivotal period in Irish history. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author brings to life the key figures, events, and ideologies that defined the Home Rule Movement, offering a comprehensive and compelling account of Ireland's struggle for autonomy.

Genres: History, Politics, Biography

Themes: Nationalism, Colonialism, Political Activism
