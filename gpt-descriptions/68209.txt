Synopsis: In "George Bernard Shaw: His Plays," H.L. Mencken delves into the fascinating world of one of the most influential playwrights of the 20th century. Through insightful analysis and captivating anecdotes, Mencken explores Shaw's unique approach to theater, his biting wit, and his unapologetic critique of societal norms. From the witty banter of "Pygmalion" to the thought-provoking themes of "Major Barbara," Mencken takes readers on a journey through Shaw's diverse and thought-provoking body of work. This book is a must-read for theater enthusiasts and anyone interested in the intersection of art and social commentary.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: Social critique, Satire, Artistic expression
