Synopsis: In 'Report on the Plans of the Various Railways before the Examiners of the House of Commons, 1864, so far as relates to the Parish of Kensington', author James Broadbridge takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate world of railway planning in the mid-19th century. Focusing specifically on the Parish of Kensington, Broadbridge unveils a web of political intrigue, financial maneuvering, and conflicting interests as various railway companies vie for control over this coveted territory. As the plans unfold, unexpected alliances are formed, secrets are revealed, and the fate of Kensington hangs in the balance. This meticulously researched report offers a fascinating glimpse into the inner workings of the railway industry during a time of rapid expansion and societal change.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, political thriller, urban planning

Themes: Power struggles, urban development, economic growth, political influence
