Synopsis: In 'Schizophrenic' by Noel M. Loomis, a brilliant psychologist finds himself entangled in a web of mystery and danger when he takes on a new patient with a unique and perplexing condition. As he delves deeper into the mind of his patient, he begins to question his own sanity and the boundaries between reality and illusion. With each twist and turn, the line between the real world and the world of the mind becomes increasingly blurred, leaving the reader questioning what is truly happening.

Genres: Psychological thriller, Mystery, Suspense

Themes: Mental illness, Perception vs. reality, Identity
