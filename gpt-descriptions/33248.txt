Synopsis: In "Milton's Tercentenary," Henry A. Beers delivers a captivating address before the Modern Language Club of Yale University on the occasion of John Milton's three hundredth birthday. With eloquence and insight, Beers explores the enduring legacy of this literary giant, delving into the profound impact of Milton's works on the world of literature and beyond. Through an engaging analysis of Milton's life, writings, and influence, Beers offers a thought-provoking tribute to one of the greatest poets in history.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Biography, Speech

Themes: Legacy, Influence, Literary Analysis
