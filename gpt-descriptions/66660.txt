Synopsis: 

In "Polio: Infantile Paralysis" by Prudential Insurance Company of America, a gripping and eye-opening account unfolds, shedding light on the devastating impact of polio on individuals and communities. Through a collection of personal stories, historical context, and medical insights, this book delves into the harrowing experiences of those affected by this debilitating disease. From the initial symptoms to the long-lasting consequences, the narrative explores the resilience and determination of both patients and their loved ones in the face of adversity. As the pages turn, readers will gain a deeper understanding of the profound societal implications of polio and the tireless efforts made to combat it.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical, Historical

Themes: Resilience, Adversity, Medical advancements, Community impact
