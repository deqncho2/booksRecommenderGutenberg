Synopsis: In "An Unsocial Socialist" by Bernard Shaw, we follow the story of Sidney Trefusis, a young man who rebels against the conventions of society and embarks on a journey of self-discovery. As he navigates through the complexities of love, politics, and class struggle, Sidney's unconventional beliefs and actions challenge the norms of his time. With wit and satire, Shaw explores the clash between individualism and socialism, shedding light on the hypocrisy and absurdity of the Victorian era.

Genres: Satire, Social commentary, Political fiction

Themes: Individualism, Socialism, Love and relationships, Class struggle
