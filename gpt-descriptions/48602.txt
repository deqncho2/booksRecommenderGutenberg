Synopsis: 

In 'The Early Norman Castles of the British Isles,' author Ella S. Armitage takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history and architecture of the early Norman castles that dot the British Isles. From the imposing fortresses of William the Conqueror to the lesser-known but equally intriguing castles scattered across the landscape, Armitage delves into the stories behind their construction, the strategic importance they held, and the lives of those who inhabited them. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, the author brings to life the turbulent times of the Norman invasion and the subsequent establishment of these iconic structures. 

Genres: History, Architecture, Non-fiction

Themes: Norman Conquest, Medieval Warfare, Cultural Heritage
