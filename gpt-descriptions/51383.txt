Synopsis: In 'Cawnpore' by George Otto Trevelyan, a gripping historical account unfolds as the author delves into the tragic events that took place during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Trevelyan paints a vivid picture of the siege of Cawnpore, a pivotal moment in British colonial history. As the tension rises and the fate of the British residents hangs in the balance, readers are taken on a heart-wrenching journey that explores the complexities of loyalty, betrayal, and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Colonial history

Themes: Loyalty, Betrayal, Resilience, Colonialism, Rebellion
