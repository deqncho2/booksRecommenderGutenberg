Synopsis: 

Through the Mackenzie Basin: A Narrative of the Athabasca and Peace River Treaty Expedition of 1899 takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed wilderness of Canada's Mackenzie Basin. Written by Charles Mair, this gripping narrative follows a group of explorers as they navigate treacherous rivers, encounter indigenous communities, and face the challenges of negotiating a treaty with the First Nations people. As they venture deeper into the heart of the unknown, the expedition members must confront their own fears and prejudices, ultimately leading to profound personal transformations. 

Genres: Adventure, Historical Fiction, Travelogue

Themes: Exploration, Indigenous Culture, Identity, Transformation
