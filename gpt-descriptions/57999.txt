Synopsis: 
'The Tragedies of Seneca: Translated into English Verse, to Which Have Been Appended Comparative Analyses of the Corresponding Greek and Roman Plays, and a Mythological Index' is a collection of tragic plays by the Roman philosopher Seneca, translated into English verse. This compilation includes comparative analyses of the corresponding Greek and Roman plays, providing a unique perspective on the similarities and differences between the two cultures. Additionally, a mythological index is included, offering readers a comprehensive guide to the mythological references found throughout the plays. 

Genres: Tragedy, Poetry, Comparative Literature

Themes: Fate and Free Will, Morality, Power and Corruption
