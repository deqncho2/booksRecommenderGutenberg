Synopsis: 

"The Negro in Tennessee, 1790-1865" by C. Perry Patterson is a captivating historical account that delves into the lives and experiences of African Americans in Tennessee during a crucial period in American history. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Patterson uncovers the struggles, triumphs, and contributions of this marginalized community, shedding light on their resilience and determination in the face of adversity. From the early days of slavery to the tumultuous era of the Civil War, this book offers a comprehensive and thought-provoking exploration of the African American experience in Tennessee, providing a deeper understanding of the complex dynamics of race and identity in the United States.

Genres: History, African American Studies, Social Sciences

Themes: Slavery, Civil Rights, Identity, Resilience, Social Justice
