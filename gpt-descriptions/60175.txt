Synopsis: In "By the Good Sainte Anne: A Story of Modern Quebec," Anna Chapin Ray takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and enchanting province of Quebec. The story follows the lives of two young women, one from a wealthy English-speaking family and the other from a humble French-speaking background. As they navigate the complexities of love, family, and societal expectations, their paths intertwine in unexpected ways, leading to profound personal growth and self-discovery. Set against the backdrop of Quebec's rich cultural heritage and breathtaking landscapes, this novel explores the power of friendship, the clash of traditions, and the pursuit of happiness in a rapidly changing world.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Cultural Identity, Social Class, Friendship, Love and Relationships
