Synopsis: 
'The Book of Hallowe'en' takes readers on a captivating journey through the history, traditions, and folklore surrounding the beloved holiday. From ancient Celtic rituals to modern-day celebrations, Ruth Edna Kelley delves into the origins of Halloween, exploring its mystical and enchanting aspects. Through vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, this book unveils the hidden meanings behind Halloween symbols, customs, and superstitions, revealing a world of magic and mystery that lies just beneath the surface of this bewitching holiday.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Folklore

Themes: Halloween traditions, Celtic mythology, Supernatural folklore
