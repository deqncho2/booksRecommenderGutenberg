Synopsis: 

In 'Stories from the Iliad' by H. L. Havell, readers are transported to the ancient world of Greek mythology, where the epic tale of the Trojan War unfolds. This captivating retelling brings to life the legendary heroes and gods, as they navigate a world filled with honor, love, betrayal, and the devastating consequences of war. From the valiant Achilles to the cunning Odysseus, the reader is immersed in a world of larger-than-life characters and their extraordinary feats. As the conflict between the Greeks and Trojans escalates, the story explores the complexities of human nature and the timeless themes of fate, loyalty, and the pursuit of glory. 

Genres: Mythology, Historical Fiction, Epic Poetry
Themes: Honor, Love, Betrayal, Consequences of War, Fate, Loyalty, Glory
