Synopsis: In 'The Woman Who Did' by Grant Allen, we are introduced to Herminia Barton, a fiercely independent and unconventional woman living in Victorian England. Herminia challenges societal norms and expectations by pursuing a life of her own choosing, defying the conventions of marriage and motherhood. As she navigates through a world that seeks to confine her, Herminia's determination and resilience are put to the test. This thought-provoking novel explores themes of feminism, individualism, and the limitations imposed by society.

Genres: Fiction, Feminist Literature, Social Commentary

Themes: Feminism, Individualism, Societal Expectations
