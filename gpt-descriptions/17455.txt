Synopsis: In "The Poison Tree: A Tale of Hindu Life in Bengal" by Bankimacandra Cattopadhyaya, readers are transported to the vibrant and complex world of 19th-century Bengal. The story revolves around the lives of two brothers, one a devout Brahmin and the other a rebellious and free-spirited individual. As their paths diverge, they find themselves entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and societal expectations. Amidst the backdrop of a changing society, the novel explores the clash between tradition and modernity, the complexities of human relationships, and the search for personal identity.

Genres: Historical fiction, Romance, Cultural

Themes: Tradition vs. modernity, Love and betrayal, Identity and self-discovery
