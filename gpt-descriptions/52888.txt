Synopsis: In 'What Every Girl Should Know' by Margaret Sanger, four young women living in a Catholic reformatory in the early 20th century embark on a journey of self-discovery and rebellion against societal norms. As they navigate the challenges of adolescence, they uncover a hidden world of knowledge and empowerment that challenges their beliefs and changes their lives forever. This thought-provoking and inspiring novel explores the power of education, the importance of reproductive rights, and the resilience of young women in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Feminist Literature

Themes: Education, Reproductive Rights, Female Empowerment, Rebellion, Identity
