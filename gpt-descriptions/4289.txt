Synopsis: In "The Duchess of Berry and the Court of Charles X," Imbert de Saint-Amand takes us on a captivating journey through the opulent and scandalous world of the French royal court during the reign of Charles X. Focusing on the enigmatic figure of the Duchess of Berry, the author unveils a tale of political intrigue, forbidden love, and the struggle for power. As the Duchess navigates the treacherous waters of courtly life, her actions and alliances have far-reaching consequences that could shape the destiny of the monarchy itself. With its vivid descriptions and compelling narrative, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into a bygone era of aristocratic excess and the complex dynamics of a royal court on the brink of revolution.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political drama

Themes: Power and politics, Love and betrayal, Courtly intrigue
