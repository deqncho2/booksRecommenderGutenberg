Synopsis: 
In "The Old Roman World: The Grandeur and Failure of Its Civilization," Lord John takes readers on a captivating journey through the rise and fall of one of history's most influential civilizations. From the legendary founding of Rome to the height of its power and the eventual decline, this book offers a vivid and comprehensive exploration of the Roman Empire. Through engaging storytelling and meticulous research, Lord John delves into the political intrigues, military conquests, cultural achievements, and societal transformations that shaped the ancient world. From the awe-inspiring architecture of the Colosseum to the philosophical musings of Marcus Aurelius, readers will be enthralled by the rich tapestry of Roman history. However, amidst the grandeur and splendor, the book also uncovers the seeds of the empire's downfall, exploring the corruption, internal conflicts, and external pressures that ultimately led to its collapse. "The Old Roman World" is a captivating and thought-provoking exploration of a civilization that continues to shape our modern world.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Rise and fall of civilizations, Power and corruption, Cultural achievements, Societal transformation
