Synopsis: 
'Enoch Soames: A Memory of the Eighteen-Nineties' by Max Beerbohm is a captivating and mysterious tale set in the vibrant literary scene of 1890s London. The story follows the eccentric and enigmatic character of Enoch Soames, a struggling writer who makes a Faustian pact to travel to the future and see if his work has gained any recognition. With a mix of wit, satire, and a touch of the supernatural, this intriguing narrative takes readers on a journey through time and explores the consequences of artistic ambition.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Fantasy

Themes: Artistic ambition, Time travel, Literary culture, Identity
