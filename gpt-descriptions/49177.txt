Synopsis: 

'India under Ripon: A Private Diary' by Wilfrid Scawen Blunt takes readers on a captivating journey through the political and social landscape of India during Lord Ripon's viceroyalty. Through the lens of a private diary, the author provides a unique and intimate perspective on the events, intrigues, and challenges faced by the British administration and the Indian people during this pivotal period. As the diary unfolds, readers are immersed in a world of political maneuvering, cultural clashes, and personal reflections, shedding light on the complexities of colonial rule and the aspirations of a nation on the brink of change.

Genres: Historical fiction, Political drama, Memoir

Themes: Colonialism, Nationalism, Cultural identity, Power dynamics
