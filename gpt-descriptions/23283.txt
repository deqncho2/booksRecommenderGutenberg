Synopsis: In "The Youth of Jefferson: Or, a Chronicle of College Scrapes at Williamsburg, in Virginia, A.D. 1764," John Esten Cooke takes us on a thrilling journey through the mischievous escapades of a young Thomas Jefferson during his college years. Set in the vibrant city of Williamsburg, Virginia, this captivating tale follows Jefferson and his friends as they navigate the challenges of academia, encounter love and friendship, and find themselves entangled in a web of secrets and intrigue. As the plot unfolds, the reader is drawn into a world of youthful rebellion, intellectual curiosity, and the forging of a future leader.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Adventure

Themes: Youthful Rebellion, Intellectual Pursuit, Friendship, Love, Secrets and Intrigue
