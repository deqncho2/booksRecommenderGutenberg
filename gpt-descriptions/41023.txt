Synopsis: 

'From Workhouse to Westminster: The Life Story of Will Crooks, M.P.' by Haw, George takes readers on a captivating journey through the remarkable life of Will Crooks. Born into poverty and raised in a workhouse, Crooks defies all odds to become a Member of Parliament. This inspiring biography delves into his struggles, triumphs, and the impact he made on society, shedding light on the challenges faced by the working class during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. From his early years in the workhouse to his rise in politics, this book offers a compelling narrative that will leave readers inspired and in awe of Crooks' resilience and determination.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Political activism, Overcoming adversity
