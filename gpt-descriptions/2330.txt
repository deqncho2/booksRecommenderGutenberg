Synopsis: In 'Religions of Ancient China', Herbert Allen Giles takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich tapestry of religious beliefs and practices in ancient China. From the mystical rituals of Taoism to the philosophical teachings of Confucianism, and the enigmatic practices of Buddhism, this book unveils the fascinating world of spirituality that shaped the lives of the Chinese people for centuries. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Giles explores the origins, myths, and rituals of these ancient religions, shedding light on their profound influence on Chinese culture, society, and individual lives. Delve into the captivating world of ancient Chinese religions and discover the profound wisdom and spiritual traditions that continue to resonate today.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Religion

Themes: Ancient Chinese culture, Spirituality, Religious practices, Influence on society
