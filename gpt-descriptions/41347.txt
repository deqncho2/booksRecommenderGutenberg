Synopsis: In 'The Great Civil War in Lancashire (1642-1651)' by Broxap, Ernest, readers are transported to the tumultuous era of the English Civil War, specifically focusing on the intense conflict that unfolded in Lancashire. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous historical research, the author delves into the lives of ordinary people caught in the midst of this brutal war, highlighting their struggles, sacrifices, and unwavering determination. As the war rages on, alliances are tested, loyalties are questioned, and the very fabric of society is torn apart. With its gripping narrative and rich historical backdrop, this book offers a captivating exploration of a lesser-known aspect of the English Civil War.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Drama

Themes: Loyalty, Sacrifice, Conflict, Resilience
