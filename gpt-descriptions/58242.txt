Synopsis: 
In 'The Characters of Theophrastus: A Translation, with Introduction', Theophrastus takes us on a captivating journey through the intricacies of human nature. Through a series of vivid character sketches, he explores the quirks, flaws, and virtues of various individuals, offering a fascinating glimpse into the human condition. From the boastful braggart to the cunning flatterer, from the miserly penny-pincher to the gullible simpleton, each character comes to life with remarkable depth and authenticity. With wit and insight, Theophrastus delves into the complexities of human behavior, shedding light on the universal traits that define us all.

Genres: 
Philosophy, Satire, Character Studies

Themes: 
Human nature, Virtue and vice, Social observation
