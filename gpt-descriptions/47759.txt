Synopsis: 

In 'A History of England, Period III. Constitutional Monarchy', James Franck Bright takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous era of constitutional monarchy in England. From the gripping conflicts between the monarchy and Parliament to the rise of influential political figures, this book offers a riveting exploration of the power struggles and societal changes that shaped the nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Biography

Themes: Power dynamics, Political reform, Social change
