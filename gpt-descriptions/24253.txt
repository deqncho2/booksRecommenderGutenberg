Synopsis: 
In 'Folkways: A Study of the Sociological Importance of Usages, Manners, Customs, Mores, and Morals', William Graham Sumner delves into the fascinating world of human behavior and social norms. Through a captivating exploration of various cultural practices, Sumner uncovers the hidden intricacies that shape our societies. From the seemingly mundane to the extraordinary, he examines the customs, traditions, and unwritten rules that govern our daily lives. With thought-provoking examples and insightful analysis, Sumner challenges our preconceived notions and invites us to question the very fabric of our social existence. 

Genres: Sociology, Anthropology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Social norms, Cultural diversity, Human behavior, Societal structures
