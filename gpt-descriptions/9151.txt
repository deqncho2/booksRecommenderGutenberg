Synopsis:
Ruggles of Red Gap is a delightful and humorous novel that follows the unexpected journey of Marmaduke Ruggles, an English valet who finds himself in the wild and eccentric town of Red Gap, Washington. When Ruggles is inadvertently won in a poker game by Egbert Floud, a wealthy American, his life takes an unexpected turn. As Ruggles adjusts to his new surroundings and the peculiarities of American culture, he discovers a newfound sense of freedom and individuality. Through his encounters with a colorful cast of characters and his own personal growth, Ruggles learns valuable lessons about identity, class, and the true meaning of home.

Genres: Comedy, Satire, Historical Fiction

Themes: Cultural identity, Social class, Personal transformation
