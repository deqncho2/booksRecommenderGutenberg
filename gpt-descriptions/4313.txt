Synopsis: In the late 19th century, a group of "odd women" find themselves navigating the challenges of a society that expects them to conform to traditional gender roles. As they strive for independence and purpose, their lives intertwine in unexpected ways, leading to both heartbreak and triumph. With its richly drawn characters and thought-provoking exploration of societal norms, 'The Odd Women' is a captivating tale that delves into the complexities of gender, love, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Fiction, Social Commentary, Victorian Literature

Themes: Gender roles, Women's rights, Marriage, Social expectations, Individualism
