Synopsis: 
In "Engravers and Etchers: Six Lectures Delivered on the Scammon Foundation at the Art Institute of Chicago, March 1916," Fitz Roy Carrington takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of engraving and etching. Through a series of insightful lectures, Carrington explores the techniques, history, and artistic significance of these intricate art forms. From the delicate precision of the engraver's tools to the expressive power of etching, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the craftsmanship and creativity behind these timeless art forms.

Genres: Art, Non-fiction

Themes: Engraving, Etching, Artistic techniques, Art history
