Synopsis: In the fifth volume of the renowned Encyclopaedia Britannica, the reader is taken on a captivating journey from the life and achievements of the philanthropist Andrew Carnegie to the intricate concept of "Casus Belli." Delve into the fascinating world of Carnegie's rise to prominence and his lasting impact on society, followed by an exploration of the complex factors that can lead to the justification of war. This volume offers a wealth of knowledge and insights that will leave readers eager to uncover the depths of these captivating subjects.

Genres: Reference, Biography, History

Themes: Philanthropy, Wealth and Power, Justification of War
