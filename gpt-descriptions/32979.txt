Synopsis: 
'Interpreters' by Carl Van Vechten is a captivating novel that delves into the lives of a group of talented individuals who possess the unique ability to interpret the thoughts and emotions of others. Set in the vibrant and bustling city of New York during the 1920s, the story follows the intertwining lives of these interpreters as they navigate the complexities of love, ambition, and the pursuit of truth. As their paths converge, secrets are unveiled, alliances are tested, and the power of interpretation becomes both a blessing and a curse. With its richly drawn characters and evocative portrayal of a bygone era, 'Interpreters' is a mesmerizing exploration of the human psyche and the intricate connections that bind us all.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Psychological Drama

Themes: Perception, Identity, Relationships, Ambition, Truth
