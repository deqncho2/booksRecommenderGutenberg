Synopsis: In "Paul Verlaine" by Stefan Zweig, delve into the captivating life of the renowned French poet, Paul Verlaine. From his tumultuous relationships and scandalous affairs to his poetic genius and inner struggles, this biography offers a mesmerizing exploration of Verlaine's complex personality and the profound impact he had on the world of literature. As Zweig delves into Verlaine's life, readers are transported to the vibrant streets of Paris, where passion, art, and tragedy intertwine to create a captivating narrative.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic genius, Love and passion, Personal struggles, Societal norms and rebellion
