Synopsis: 

In 'Submarines, Mines and Torpedoes in the War', Charles W. Domville-Fife takes readers on a thrilling journey into the depths of naval warfare during World War I. Through vivid accounts and firsthand experiences, the author delves into the dangerous world of submarines, mines, and torpedoes, revealing the pivotal role they played in shaping the outcome of the war. From heart-pounding encounters beneath the waves to the ingenious strategies employed by both sides, this book offers a captivating exploration of the unseen battles that unfolded beneath the surface.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Naval warfare

Themes: Technology and warfare, Human ingenuity, The impact of naval tactics on war outcomes
