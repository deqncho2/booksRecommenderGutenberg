Synopsis: In 'Life in the Iron-Mills; Or, The Korl Woman', we are introduced to the gritty world of iron mills in the 19th century. The story revolves around Hugh Wolfe, a talented but impoverished iron-worker, and his cousin Deborah, a woman trapped in a life of hardship and despair. When a mysterious sculpture arrives at the mill, it sparks a chain of events that will challenge their beliefs and ignite a desire for change. As the characters navigate the harsh realities of their industrial town, they are forced to confront the stark contrast between their dreams and the harshness of their existence.

Genres: Realism, Social Commentary, Tragedy

Themes: Social inequality, the dehumanizing effects of industrialization, the pursuit of dreams amidst adversity
