Synopsis: 
"The Dramatic Instinct in Children" explores the fascinating world of children's innate ability to engage in imaginative play and storytelling. Drawing on extensive research and real-life anecdotes, the American Institute of Child Life delves into the profound impact of dramatic play on a child's development, creativity, and emotional intelligence. From the enchanting realms of make-believe to the transformative power of role-playing, this book uncovers the hidden depths of children's dramatic instincts, revealing a captivating journey of self-discovery and growth.

Genres: Psychology, Child Development, Education

Themes: Imagination, Creativity, Emotional Intelligence, Play, Self-expression
