Synopsis: In 'The Pivot of Civilization', Margaret Sanger delves into the controversial topic of birth control and its impact on society. Through a thought-provoking exploration of the consequences of uncontrolled population growth, Sanger challenges societal norms and advocates for women's reproductive rights. With a blend of personal anecdotes, statistical analysis, and historical context, she presents a compelling argument for the necessity of accessible contraception. As she confronts the prevailing beliefs of her time, Sanger's work sparks a dialogue that continues to resonate today.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Feminism

Themes: Reproductive rights, Population control, Gender equality
