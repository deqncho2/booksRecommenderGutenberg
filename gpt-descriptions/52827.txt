Synopsis: 
'For the Sake of the Soldier: Voluntary Work of Brisbane Women' by Rita Macleod takes readers on a captivating journey through the untold stories of Brisbane women during a time of war. Set against the backdrop of World War II, this book delves into the lives of these courageous women who selflessly dedicated themselves to supporting the soldiers on the front lines. From knitting socks and sending care packages to providing emotional support, their voluntary work becomes a testament to the strength and resilience of the human spirit. As the war rages on, the women face personal challenges and triumphs, forging unbreakable bonds and discovering their own inner strength. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Women's Fiction
Themes: Sacrifice, Community, Empowerment, Resilience
