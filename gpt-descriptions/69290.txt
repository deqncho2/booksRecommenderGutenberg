Synopsis: In "American Slavery, and the Means of its Abolition," Jonathan Ward delves into the dark and complex history of slavery in America, exploring the various methods employed to bring about its eventual end. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Ward uncovers the untold stories of both enslaved individuals and abolitionists, shedding light on the struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices that paved the way for freedom. From the Underground Railroad to the fiery debates in Congress, this book offers a gripping account of the fight against one of the greatest injustices in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Social justice, African American studies

Themes: Slavery, Abolition, Civil rights, Resistance, Human dignity
