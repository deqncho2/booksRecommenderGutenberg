Synopsis: 
'Essays in Liberalism: Being the Lectures and Papers Which Were Delivered at the Liberal Summer School at Oxford, 1922' is a thought-provoking collection of essays that explores the fundamental principles and ideas of liberalism. Through a series of lectures and papers delivered by prominent thinkers, this book delves into the complexities of liberalism, its historical context, and its relevance in the modern world. From discussions on individual freedom and social progress to the role of the state and the importance of education, these essays offer a captivating exploration of liberalism's core values and its potential to shape a more inclusive and equitable society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Philosophy, Social Sciences

Themes: Liberalism, Individual Freedom, Social Progress, Role of the State, Education
