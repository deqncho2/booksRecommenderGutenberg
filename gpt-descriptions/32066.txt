Synopsis: In 'The Fundamental Principles of Old and New World Civilizations', Zelia Nuttall delves into the ancient Mexican religious, sociological, and calendrical systems, presenting a captivating comparative research that unveils the intriguing connections between the civilizations of the Old and New Worlds. Through meticulous study and analysis, Nuttall uncovers the profound similarities and shared principles that underlie these seemingly distinct cultures, shedding light on the fascinating interplay between ancient societies. This thought-provoking exploration offers a fresh perspective on the development of civilizations and invites readers to embark on a journey of discovery.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Comparative study, Ancient civilizations, Cultural connections
