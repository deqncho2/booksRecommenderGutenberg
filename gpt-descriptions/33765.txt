Synopsis: In 'The Church of England cleared from the charge of Schism: Upon Testimonies of Councils and Fathers of the first six centuries', T. W. Allies presents a compelling argument defending the Church of England against accusations of schism. Drawing upon the testimonies of councils and fathers from the first six centuries, Allies delves into the historical roots of the Church and its adherence to the true teachings of Christianity. Through meticulous research and analysis, Allies challenges prevailing notions and sheds light on the Church's rightful place within the broader Christian tradition.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, History

Themes: Church history, Schism, Christian tradition
