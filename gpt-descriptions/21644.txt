Synopsis: In 'Every Man for Himself' by Moorhouse, Herbert Joseph, we are transported to the luxurious and opulent setting of the Titanic in 1912. As the grand ship embarks on its maiden voyage, we follow the lives of a diverse cast of characters, each with their own secrets and desires. From the ambitious young journalist seeking a scoop, to the enigmatic artist haunted by his past, and the spirited young woman torn between duty and love, their paths intertwine in unexpected ways as they navigate the treacherous waters of love, betrayal, and survival. With the looming tragedy that awaits, the question arises: who will be saved and who will be left to face the depths of the ocean?

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: Ambition, Secrets, Love, Betrayal, Survival
