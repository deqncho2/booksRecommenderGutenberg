Synopsis: 

'Famous Frosts and Frost Fairs in Great Britain: Chronicled from the Earliest to the Present Time' takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of frost and the extraordinary fairs that emerged on frozen rivers in Great Britain. From ancient times to the present day, author William Andrews delves into the fascinating tales of these frozen wonders, recounting the incredible events and unique experiences that unfolded during these frosty periods. With vivid descriptions and historical anecdotes, Andrews paints a vivid picture of the vibrant atmosphere, bustling activities, and unexpected occurrences that took place on the icy landscapes. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travel, Cultural History

Themes: British history, Winter traditions, Community celebrations, Human adaptability to extreme weather conditions
