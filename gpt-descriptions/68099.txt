Synopsis: In "Malthus and his work," James Bonar delves into the life and ideas of one of the most controversial figures in economic history. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Bonar uncovers the fascinating journey of Thomas Malthus, a man whose theories on population growth and resource scarcity challenged the prevailing beliefs of his time. From his early years as a clergyman to his groundbreaking work on population dynamics, Malthus's ideas continue to provoke debate and shape our understanding of the world today. Bonar's exploration of Malthus's life and work offers a thought-provoking glimpse into the mind of a visionary thinker whose ideas still resonate in the modern age.

Genres: Biography, History, Economics

Themes: Population growth, Resource scarcity, Economic theory
