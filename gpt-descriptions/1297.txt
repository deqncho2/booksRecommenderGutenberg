Synopsis: 

In "The Iron Puddler: My Life in the Rolling Mills and What Came of It," James J. Davis takes us on a captivating journey through his life as a young iron puddler in the late 19th century. From his humble beginnings in a small Welsh village to his arduous work in the rolling mills of America, Davis shares the triumphs and hardships he faced in his pursuit of the American Dream. As he navigates the treacherous world of industrial labor, Davis encounters love, friendship, and unexpected opportunities that shape his destiny. This gripping memoir offers a vivid and raw portrayal of the iron industry, while also exploring the resilience of the human spirit and the pursuit of a better life.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Industrialization, Immigration, Social Mobility, Resilience
