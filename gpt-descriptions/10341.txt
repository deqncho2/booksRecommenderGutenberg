Synopsis: 
In 'The Great Events by Famous Historians, Volume 21: The Recent Days (1910-1914)', readers are transported to a pivotal period in history, where the world teeters on the brink of unprecedented change. This captivating volume delves into the tumultuous years leading up to World War I, exploring the political, social, and cultural events that shaped the destiny of nations. From the rise of new ideologies to the struggles for independence, from technological advancements to the shifting balance of power, this book offers a gripping account of the events that set the stage for one of the most significant conflicts in human history.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: World War I, Political upheaval, Technological advancements, Nationalism, Global diplomacy
