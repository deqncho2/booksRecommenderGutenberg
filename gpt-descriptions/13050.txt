Synopsis: 
"The Easiest Way: Representative Plays by American Dramatists: 1856-1911" is a captivating collection of plays curated by Eugene Walter. This anthology showcases a diverse range of American playwrights from the late 19th and early 20th centuries, offering a glimpse into the social, cultural, and political landscape of the time. From tales of love and ambition to stories of societal constraints and personal struggles, these plays explore the complexities of human nature and the pursuit of happiness. Each play presents a unique narrative, filled with compelling characters and thought-provoking dialogue, making this collection a must-read for theater enthusiasts and those interested in the evolution of American drama.

Genres: Drama, Theatre, Anthology

Themes: Love and relationships, Ambition, Social constraints, Personal struggles, Society and culture
