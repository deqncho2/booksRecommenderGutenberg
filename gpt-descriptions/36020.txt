Synopsis:
'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume I, Alabama Narratives' is a captivating collection of firsthand accounts that unveils the harrowing experiences of former slaves in Alabama. Through interviews conducted by the United States Work Projects Administration, this volume offers a poignant and unfiltered glimpse into the lives of those who endured the horrors of slavery. From tales of resilience and survival to stories of love and loss, these narratives paint a vivid picture of a dark chapter in American history.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Oral history, African American literature

Themes:
Slavery, Resilience, Identity, Freedom, Injustice
