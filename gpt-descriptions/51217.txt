Synopsis: 

'Footprints of the Red Men: Indian geographical names in the valley of Hudson's river, the valley of the Mohawk, and on the Delaware: their location and the probable meaning of some of them' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and cultural significance of Indian geographical names in the Hudson River Valley, the Mohawk Valley, and the Delaware Valley. Edward Manning Ruttenber delves into the origins and meanings of these names, shedding light on the deep connection between the land and the indigenous people who once inhabited it. As readers explore the landscapes and uncover the hidden stories behind these names, they will gain a profound understanding of the Native American presence in these regions and the enduring legacy they left behind.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Native American studies, Linguistics

Themes: Native American history, Cultural heritage, Place names, Indigenous languages
