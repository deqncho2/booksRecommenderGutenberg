Synopsis:
In 'Honeycomb: Pilgrimage, Volume 3' by Dorothy M. Richardson, the captivating journey of Miriam Henderson continues as she navigates the complexities of life in early 20th century London. Miriam finds herself torn between her desire for independence and the societal expectations placed upon her. As she encounters new people and experiences, she grapples with the ever-present question of identity and the search for meaning in a rapidly changing world. With each step of her pilgrimage, Miriam's innermost thoughts and emotions are beautifully woven into a tapestry of self-discovery and personal growth.

Genres: Literary fiction, Coming-of-age, Modernist literature

Themes: Identity, Independence, Society's expectations, Self-discovery, Personal growth
