Synopsis: 
'Letters from China and Japan' is a captivating collection of personal correspondence between John Dewey and his wife Harriet Alice Chipman, chronicling their experiences and observations during their travels in China and Japan. Through vivid descriptions and insightful reflections, the letters offer a unique glimpse into the rich cultures, traditions, and landscapes of these two fascinating countries. As the couple navigates the complexities of language barriers, cultural differences, and encounters with locals, they embark on a transformative journey that challenges their preconceptions and broadens their understanding of the world.

Genres: Travelogue, Memoir, Cultural Exploration

Themes: Cross-cultural experiences, Language and communication, Transformation through travel
