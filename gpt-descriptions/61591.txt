Synopsis: 
In 'Fors Clavigera (Volume 2 of 8): Letters to the workmen and labourers of Great Britain,' John Ruskin, a renowned Victorian art critic and social thinker, continues his series of letters addressing the working class. Through a collection of passionate and thought-provoking letters, Ruskin delves into various topics such as the importance of education, the impact of industrialization, and the need for social and economic reform. With his eloquent prose and insightful observations, Ruskin challenges the reader to question the prevailing societal norms and encourages them to strive for a more just and equitable world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social criticism, Philosophy

Themes: Social and economic inequality, Education, Industrialization, Reform, Justice
