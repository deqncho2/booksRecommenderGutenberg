Synopsis:
'Big and Little Sisters: A Story of an Indian Mission School' by Theodora R. Jenness is a captivating tale set in an Indian mission school. The story revolves around the lives of two sisters, one older and one younger, as they navigate the challenges and joys of their unique upbringing. From their initial struggles to adapt to the unfamiliar environment to the deep bonds they form with their fellow students, the sisters embark on a journey of self-discovery, friendship, and resilience. As they grow and learn, they encounter unexpected obstacles that test their strength and determination. Will they be able to overcome these hurdles and find their place in the world? This heartwarming narrative explores the power of sisterhood, the importance of cultural identity, and the transformative impact of education.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Sisterhood, Cultural Identity, Education, Resilience
