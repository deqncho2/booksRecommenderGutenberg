Synopsis: In "Revolted Woman: Past, present, and to come," Charles G. Harper takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the evolution of women's roles and the challenges they have faced. From the suffragette movement to the modern-day fight for equality, this thought-provoking book delves into the struggles, triumphs, and aspirations of women throughout history. Through a combination of historical anecdotes, personal narratives, and insightful analysis, Harper paints a vivid picture of the past, examines the present state of women's rights, and offers a glimpse into the future. This engaging narrative is sure to captivate readers, leaving them inspired and eager to join the ongoing revolution for gender equality.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Feminism

Themes: Women's rights, Gender equality, Social progress
