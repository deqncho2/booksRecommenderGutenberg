Synopsis: In "Alcohol: A Dangerous and Unnecessary Medicine, How and Why: What Medical Writers Say," Martha Meir Allen delves into the controversial topic of alcohol as a medicine. Through a comprehensive exploration of medical literature, Allen challenges the widely accepted notion that alcohol has medicinal benefits. With thought-provoking arguments and compelling evidence, the author sheds light on the dangers and unnecessary use of alcohol in medical practices. This eye-opening book will make readers question their beliefs about alcohol's role in medicine and its potential consequences.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical literature, Health

Themes: Alcoholism, Medical ethics, Substance abuse
