Synopsis: 

In 'The Life of Lazarillo of Tormes: His Fortunes and Misfortunes as Told by Himself', we are introduced to the witty and resourceful Lazarillo, a young boy who navigates through a series of unexpected and often humorous adventures. From his humble beginnings as the son of a poor town crier, Lazarillo finds himself in the service of a variety of eccentric and sometimes unscrupulous masters. Through his cleverness and cunning, he learns to survive in a world filled with deception and hardship. As Lazarillo recounts his experiences, we are taken on a journey that explores the complexities of social class, morality, and the human capacity for resilience.

Genres: Picaresque novel, Satire, Realism

Themes: Social class, Morality, Resilience
