Synopsis: In "The prophete Ionas with an introduccion," William Tyndale explores the understanding and proper use of scripture, focusing on the book of Jonah. He delves into why the scripture was written and what seekers should look for within its pages. Tyndale also reveals the barriers that prevent readers from comprehending the scripture, even if they study it extensively. However, he provides the keys to unlock its true meaning, ensuring that readers are not misled by false doctrines or cunning interpretations. 

Genres: Religious literature, Biblical commentary

Themes: Interpretation of scripture, the purpose of scripture, unlocking the true meaning of scripture, avoiding false doctrines
