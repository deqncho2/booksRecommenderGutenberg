Synopsis: 

In 'History of the Negro Race in America from 1619 to 1880. Vol. 2: Negroes as Slaves, as Soldiers, and as Citizens', George Washington Williams delves into the complex and often overlooked history of African Americans in America. This volume explores the experiences of Negroes as slaves, soldiers, and citizens, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and contributions throughout the centuries. From the horrors of slavery to the bravery of black soldiers in the Civil War, Williams presents a comprehensive account of the African American experience during this pivotal period in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, African American studies, Biography

Themes: Slavery, Civil Rights, Identity, Resilience, Social Justice
