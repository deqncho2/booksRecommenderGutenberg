Synopsis: 

In 'An elementary manual of New Zealand entomology', George Vernon Hudson takes readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of native insects in New Zealand. This comprehensive manual serves as an introduction to the study of these remarkable creatures, providing a wealth of information and insights that will leave readers in awe of the intricate and diverse insect life found in this unique part of the world. From the delicate beauty of butterflies to the mysterious habits of beetles, Hudson's vivid descriptions and detailed illustrations bring these insects to life, making this manual a must-read for nature enthusiasts, students, and anyone curious about the wonders of entomology.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Nature

Themes: Biodiversity, Natural History, Environmental Conservation
