Synopsis: 

In 'Field's Chromatography: or Treatise on Colours and Pigments as Used by Artists', George Field takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of colors and pigments, revealing the secrets behind their creation and application in art. Through a combination of scientific analysis and artistic insight, Field explores the fascinating history, properties, and techniques of various pigments, providing artists with invaluable knowledge to enhance their creative endeavors. From the vibrant hues of the Renaissance to the subtle shades of watercolors, this book unveils the hidden stories behind the colors that have shaped the art world for centuries.

Genres: Art, Science, History

Themes: Color theory, Artistic techniques, Historical significance of pigments
