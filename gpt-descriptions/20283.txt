Synopsis: In "Women's Wild Oats: Essays on the Re-fixing of Moral Standards," Catherine Gasquoine Hartley delves into the intriguing world of women's experiences and challenges societal norms. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, she explores the complexities of female desires, ambitions, and the consequences of defying traditional moral standards. With a blend of wit, wisdom, and personal anecdotes, Hartley invites readers to question and reevaluate the societal expectations placed upon women, ultimately challenging the notion of what it means to be a "proper" woman.

Genres: Non-fiction, Essays, Feminist Literature

Themes: Gender roles, Female sexuality, Societal expectations, Feminism
