Synopsis: 

In 'Fiends, Ghosts, and Sprites: Including an Account of the Origin and Nature of Belief in the Supernatural', John Netten Radcliffe takes readers on a captivating journey through the realms of the supernatural. From ancient folklore to modern-day encounters, this book explores the fascinating and often chilling tales of fiends, ghosts, and sprites that have haunted human imagination for centuries. Radcliffe delves into the origins of these beliefs, unraveling the intricate web of superstition and fear that has shaped our understanding of the supernatural. With a blend of historical accounts, personal anecdotes, and thought-provoking analysis, this book offers a captivating exploration of the mysterious and otherworldly.

Genres: Non-fiction, Folklore, Supernatural

Themes: Belief systems, Superstition, Human imagination
