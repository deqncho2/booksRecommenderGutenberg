Synopsis: "1683-1920" is a comprehensive exploration of various historical topics, including the Fourteen Points, foreign propaganda in public schools, the rewriting of US history, the Espionage Act, the blockade of the Central Powers, the impact of starvation during the war, the debt owed to France and Germany, the war vote in Congress, the truth about Belgian atrocities, the observance of the treaty with Germany, the Alien Property Custodianship, the secret will of Cecil Rhodes, racial strains in American life, and the Germantown Settlement of 1683. This book delves into a thousand other intriguing topics, providing a thought-provoking and eye-opening perspective on history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Historical revisionism, Propaganda, International relations, War and its consequences, National identity, Cultural diversity
