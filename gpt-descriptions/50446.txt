Synopsis: In 'Dictionary of Explosives' by Marshall, Arthur, readers are taken on a thrilling journey through the world of explosives, where danger lurks at every turn. This meticulously researched and expertly written dictionary unveils the secrets behind various explosive materials, their properties, and their potential applications. From the historical significance of gunpowder to the cutting-edge advancements in modern explosives, this book offers a captivating exploration of the explosive world that will leave readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Non-fiction, Reference, Thriller

Themes: Danger, Scientific Discovery
