Synopsis: 

'From Xylographs to Lead Molds; A.D. 1440-A.D. 1921' takes readers on an enthralling journey through the evolution of printing technology, spanning over four centuries. From the humble beginnings of xylographs, wooden blocks used for printing, to the revolutionary invention of lead molds, this captivating narrative explores the fascinating history and advancements in the world of printing. With vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, the author delves into the lives of the inventors, the challenges they faced, and the impact their innovations had on society. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Technology, Biography

Themes: Innovation, Communication, Technological Progress
