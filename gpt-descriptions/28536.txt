Synopsis: In "The Astronomy of the Bible," E. Walter Maunder explores the fascinating astronomical references found within the Holy Scripture. From the star of Bethlehem to the sun standing still, Maunder delves into the celestial events mentioned in the Bible, providing an insightful and accessible commentary. Through his exploration, he uncovers the profound connections between astronomy and the religious texts, shedding light on the ancient understanding of the cosmos and its significance in biblical narratives.

Genres: Non-fiction, Astronomy, Religion

Themes: Astronomy in religious texts, Biblical interpretation, Ancient cosmology
