Synopsis: In 'A Voice from the South: By a Black Woman of the South', Anna J. Cooper presents a powerful and thought-provoking collection of essays that challenge societal norms and advocate for the rights and empowerment of African American women. Through her eloquent and passionate writing, Cooper delves into various topics such as education, race, gender, and the intersectionality of these identities. With a unique perspective as a black woman living in the South during the late 19th century, Cooper's voice resonates with authenticity and urgency, making this work a compelling and enlightening read.

Genres: Non-fiction, Essay collection, Feminist literature

Themes: African American identity, Gender equality, Education, Intersectionality, Social justice
