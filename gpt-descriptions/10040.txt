Synopsis: 
In 'Aleutian Indian and English Dictionary', Charles A. Lee takes readers on a linguistic journey through the dialects of the Aleutian Indian language as spoken by various tribes around the Sulima River and neighboring parts of the Alaska Peninsula. This comprehensive dictionary provides a fascinating insight into the common words used by the Oogashik, Egashik, Anangashuk, and Misremie tribes, shedding light on their unique cultural and linguistic heritage. From everyday vocabulary to specialized terms, this dictionary serves as a valuable resource for anyone interested in the Aleutian Indian language and its rich history.

Genres: Reference, Linguistics, Indigenous Studies

Themes: Cultural preservation, Language revitalization, Indigenous knowledge
