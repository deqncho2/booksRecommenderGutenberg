Synopsis: In "The Fighting Governor: A Chronicle of Frontenac," Charles W. Colby takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Louis de Buade, Comte de Frontenac, a charismatic and fearless governor of New France in the late 17th century. From his arrival in the colony to his relentless efforts to defend it against rival European powers and indigenous nations, Frontenac's indomitable spirit and strategic brilliance shine through. As political intrigue, military conflicts, and personal challenges intertwine, readers are immersed in a tale of power, ambition, and resilience set against the backdrop of a tumultuous era.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Adventure

Themes: Leadership, Colonialism, Conflict, Resilience
