Synopsis: 

'Company K, Twentieth Regiment, Illinois Volunteer Infantry: Roster and Record, April 24, 1861-July 16, 1865' by Brown, Andrew is a meticulously compiled historical record that delves into the experiences and stories of the soldiers who served in Company K during the American Civil War. Through detailed rosters, personal accounts, and official records, the book offers a vivid portrayal of the soldiers' lives, their struggles, and their triumphs on the battlefield.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history

Themes: Patriotism, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Resilience
