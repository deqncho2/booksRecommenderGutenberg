Synopsis: In "Scenes in the Life of Harriet Tubman," Sarah H. Bradford presents a captivating account of the remarkable life of Harriet Tubman, a courageous African American woman who escaped slavery and became a prominent abolitionist and conductor of the Underground Railroad. Through vivid storytelling, Bradford takes readers on a journey through Tubman's experiences as a slave, her daring escapes, and her tireless efforts to liberate others. This gripping narrative sheds light on Tubman's unwavering determination, her acts of heroism, and the profound impact she had on the fight for freedom.

Genres: Biography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Courage, Resistance, Empowerment
