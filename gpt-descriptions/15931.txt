Synopsis: In 'A History of English Romanticism in the Nineteenth Century', Henry A. Beers takes readers on a captivating journey through the influential movement that shaped literature, art, and culture during this transformative era. From the passionate poetry of Wordsworth and Coleridge to the rebellious spirit of Byron and Shelley, Beers explores the lives and works of the key figures who defined English Romanticism. Through vivid anecdotes and insightful analysis, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the Romantic movement and its lasting impact on the world of art and ideas.

Genres: Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Romanticism, Artistic Expression, Individualism, Nature, Imagination
