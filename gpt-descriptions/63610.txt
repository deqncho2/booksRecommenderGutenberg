Synopsis: 
In 'Speeches at the Constitutional Convention: With the Right of Suffrage Passed by the Constitutional Convention,' Robert Smalls presents a collection of powerful speeches delivered during the Constitutional Convention. These speeches delve into the crucial topic of suffrage and its implications for the future of the nation. Through passionate arguments and thought-provoking rhetoric, the delegates at the convention engage in a battle of ideas, challenging the status quo and advocating for a more inclusive democracy. As the debates unfold, tensions rise, alliances are formed, and the fate of suffrage hangs in the balance. Smalls captures the essence of this pivotal moment in history, offering readers a front-row seat to witness the birth of a new era.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Suffrage, Democracy, Constitutional Law, Civil Rights
