Synopsis: In "Isaac T. Hopper: A True Life," Lydia Maria Child introduces us to the remarkable story of a man who defied societal norms and dedicated his life to helping others. Set in the early 19th century, this captivating biography follows Isaac T. Hopper, a passionate abolitionist and advocate for the rights of the oppressed. From his daring efforts to aid fugitive slaves to his relentless fight against injustice, Hopper's unwavering commitment to equality and compassion shines through. As we delve into his extraordinary life, we witness the challenges he faces, the lives he touches, and the legacy he leaves behind.

Genres: Biography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Social justice, Activism, Compassion, Equality
