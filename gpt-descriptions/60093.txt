Synopsis: 
'Cane' by Jean Toomer is a captivating and lyrical work that weaves together the lives of various characters in the American South. Through a series of interconnected vignettes, the reader is transported to different settings and time periods, exploring the complexities of race, identity, and the human experience. From the rural landscapes of Georgia to the bustling streets of Washington, D.C., 'Cane' delves into the lives of African Americans and their struggles, hopes, and desires. With its poetic prose and rich imagery, this groundbreaking work offers a unique and profound exploration of the African American experience in the early 20th century.

Genres: 
Modernist literature, African American literature, Poetry

Themes: 
Race, identity, human experience, rural life, urban life
