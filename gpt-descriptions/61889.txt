Synopsis: In 'Lectures on the Principles of Political Obligation', Thomas Hill Green explores the complex and thought-provoking concept of political obligation. Reprinted from Green's Philosophical Works, vol. II., this book delves into the fundamental questions surrounding our duties and responsibilities as citizens. With a preface by Bernard Bosanquet, this work offers a compelling examination of the moral and philosophical foundations of political allegiance, challenging readers to reconsider their understanding of the social contract and the nature of political authority.

Genres: Philosophy, Political Science

Themes: Political obligation, Social contract, Moral responsibility
