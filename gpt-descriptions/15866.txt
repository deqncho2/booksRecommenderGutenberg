Synopsis: In 'Humanly Speaking' by Samuel McChord Crothers, a seemingly ordinary man finds himself caught in a web of extraordinary circumstances that challenge his perception of reality. As he navigates through a series of unexpected events, he is forced to confront the complexities of human nature and the power of personal choice. With each twist and turn, the protagonist's journey becomes a thought-provoking exploration of the human condition, leaving readers questioning their own understanding of what it means to be human.

Genres: Fiction, Mystery, Psychological Thriller

Themes: Identity, Morality, Perception, Free Will
