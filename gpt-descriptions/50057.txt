Synopsis: In 'The Early Cave-Men', Katharine Elizabeth Dopp takes readers on a captivating journey back in time to explore the lives of our ancient ancestors. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Dopp paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by early cave-dwelling humans. From their ingenious methods of survival to their complex social structures, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of these early humans. As readers delve into the pages, they will discover the remarkable ingenuity and resilience of our ancient ancestors, and gain a deeper appreciation for the foundations of human civilization.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Anthropology, Archaeology

Themes: Human evolution, Survival, Social structures
