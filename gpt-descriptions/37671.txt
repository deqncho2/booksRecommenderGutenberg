Synopsis: In "The Panama Canal and Its Makers" by Cornish, Vaughan, embark on a captivating journey through the remarkable history of one of the world's greatest engineering marvels. From the audacious vision of its creators to the immense challenges they faced, this book unveils the gripping tale behind the construction of the Panama Canal. Delve into the lives of the courageous individuals who dedicated themselves to this monumental project, as they battled against treacherous landscapes, political turmoil, and relentless obstacles. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Cornish, Vaughan brings to life the triumphs, tragedies, and sheer determination that shaped the Panama Canal into an enduring symbol of human ingenuity.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Biography

Themes: Engineering feats, Human perseverance, Political struggles, Global impact
