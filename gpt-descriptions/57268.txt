Synopsis: In 'Oliver Cromwell and the Rule of the Puritans in England', author C. H. Firth delves into the captivating story of one of England's most controversial figures and the transformative era he led. From Cromwell's rise to power to the tumultuous reign of the Puritans, this book offers a gripping account of political intrigue, religious upheaval, and the clash between tradition and reform. Firth's meticulous research and vivid storytelling bring to life the complex characters and events that shaped this pivotal period in English history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Power and politics, Religion and religious conflict, Social change
