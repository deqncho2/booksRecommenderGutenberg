Synopsis: In "The Swastika, the Earliest Known Symbol, and Its Migration," Thomas Wilson takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the origins and migration of one of the world's most ancient symbols. From its earliest appearances in prehistoric cultures to its surprising presence in diverse civilizations, Wilson unravels the mysteries surrounding the swastika, shedding light on its significance and the industries that accompanied its migration. This thought-provoking exploration delves into the symbol's cultural, religious, and historical contexts, offering a fascinating glimpse into the interconnectedness of human civilizations throughout history.

Genres: History, Anthropology, Archaeology

Themes: Symbolism, Cultural Exchange, Human Migration
