Synopsis: In "Annals of a Fortress," Viollet-le-Duc takes us on a captivating journey through the history and secrets of a medieval fortress. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, he unveils the fascinating stories of the people who lived within its walls, the strategic battles fought for its control, and the architectural marvels that made it impregnable. From hidden passageways to ingenious defense mechanisms, this book offers a captivating exploration of the fortress's secrets, bringing history to life in a truly immersive way.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Architecture, Military history

Themes: Architecture and engineering, Warfare and defense strategies, Human stories within historical contexts
