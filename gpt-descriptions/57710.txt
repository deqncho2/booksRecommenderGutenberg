Synopsis: In 'A Son of the State' by W. Pett Ridge, we follow the life of a young man named Dick, who grows up in the harsh conditions of a workhouse in Victorian England. As he navigates the challenges of poverty, injustice, and societal expectations, Dick's determination and resilience are put to the test. From his humble beginnings to unexpected encounters and life-altering decisions, this gripping tale takes readers on a journey through the underbelly of society, shedding light on the struggles faced by those born into poverty.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Social Realism, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Poverty, Social Inequality, Resilience, Identity
