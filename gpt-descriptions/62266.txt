Synopsis: 

In "The Road Past Kennesaw: The Atlanta Campaign of 1864," Richard M. McMurry takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most critical military campaigns of the American Civil War. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, McMurry delves into the strategic maneuvers, intense battles, and the human toll of the Atlanta Campaign, offering a fresh perspective on this pivotal moment in history. As the Union and Confederate forces clash in a desperate struggle for control of Atlanta, McMurry brings to life the experiences of soldiers and civilians alike, painting a vivid picture of the harrowing realities of war. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American Civil War

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, Strategy and tactics in warfare, The resilience of the human spirit
