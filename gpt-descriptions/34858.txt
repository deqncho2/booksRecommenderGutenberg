Synopsis: In "The Ordeal of Richard Feverel: A History of a Father and Son," George Meredith takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex relationship between a father and his son. Set in the backdrop of Victorian society, the story follows Richard Feverel, a young and impressionable boy, as he navigates the challenges of love, honor, and societal expectations. As Richard's father, Sir Austin, attempts to shape his son into the epitome of a gentleman, their relationship becomes a battleground of conflicting ideals and desires. With themes of love, ambition, and the struggle for independence, this novel delves into the intricate dynamics of family, society, and the pursuit of personal growth.

Genres: Bildungsroman, Victorian novel, Psychological fiction

Themes: Love and relationships, Family dynamics, Social expectations, Personal growth
