Synopsis: In "The Youth of Washington: Told in the Form of an Autobiography," Silas Weir Mitchell takes readers on a captivating journey through the formative years of one of America's most iconic figures, George Washington. Through the eyes of a young Washington, we witness his struggles, triumphs, and the pivotal moments that shaped him into the leader he would become. From his early encounters with Native Americans to his experiences as a surveyor and soldier, this autobiography offers a unique and intimate perspective on the man who would go on to become the first President of the United States.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Leadership, Identity, Patriotism, Growth and Development
