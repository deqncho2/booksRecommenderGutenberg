Synopsis: 
In the Flash Ranging Service: Observations of an American Soldier During His Service With the A.E.F. in France takes readers on a captivating journey through the eyes of Edward Alva Trueblood, an American soldier serving in France during World War I. Trueblood's vivid and detailed observations provide a unique perspective on the war, as he recounts his experiences in the flash ranging service, a crucial role in artillery operations. From the trenches to the front lines, Trueblood's narrative immerses readers in the harsh realities of war, the camaraderie among soldiers, and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, War, History

Themes: War experiences, Camaraderie, Resilience
