Synopsis: In "Memoirs of the Revolution in Bengal, Anno Dom. 1757," William Watts takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events that unfolded during the historic Battle of Plassey. Through his vivid and detailed account, we witness the rise of the British East India Company and the dramatic shift in power that forever changed the course of Bengal's history. As Watts delves into the political intrigues, military strategies, and personal stories of key figures involved, readers are transported to a time of great upheaval and transformation in the Indian subcontinent.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Political history

Themes: Colonialism, Power dynamics, Cultural clash, Resistance, Historical legacy
