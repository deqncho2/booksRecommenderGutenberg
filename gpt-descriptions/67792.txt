Synopsis: In 'A History of Magic and Experimental Science, Volume 1', Lynn Thorndike takes readers on a captivating journey through the first thirteen centuries of our era, exploring the fascinating world of magic and experimental science. From ancient civilizations to the Middle Ages, Thorndike delves into the beliefs, practices, and discoveries that shaped the early development of these fields. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this volume offers a comprehensive and enlightening account of humanity's quest for knowledge and understanding.

Genres: History, Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Magic, Science, Intellectual curiosity, Cultural beliefs, Historical development
