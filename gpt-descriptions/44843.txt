Synopsis: In 'The Book of Bulbs' by Samuel Arnott, a mysterious antique book is discovered, containing a wealth of knowledge about the fascinating world of bulbs. As the protagonist delves into its pages, they embark on a captivating journey through history, culture, and horticulture, uncovering the secrets and wonders of these vibrant and diverse plants. From the ancient civilizations that revered bulbs to the modern-day enthusiasts who cultivate them, this book offers a captivating exploration of the hidden stories behind these seemingly ordinary plants. As the protagonist's understanding deepens, they find themselves drawn into a world where beauty, science, and human connection intertwine, forever changing their perception of the natural world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Gardening, History

Themes: Botany, Cultural significance, Human connection with nature
