Synopsis: 

In "Coca and Cocaine: Their history, medical and economic uses, and medicinal preparations," Martindale delves into the captivating world of coca and its derivative, cocaine. This comprehensive exploration takes readers on a journey through time, uncovering the intriguing history, multifaceted medical applications, and surprising economic significance of these substances. From ancient civilizations to modern pharmaceutical advancements, Martindale uncovers the secrets behind coca and cocaine, shedding light on their complex roles in society. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into a topic that has both captivated and divided the world for centuries.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medicine

Themes: Drug history, Medical applications, Economic impact
