Synopsis: In 'The Daughters of Danaus' by Mona Caird, a group of sisters find themselves trapped in a suffocating and oppressive marriage. As they navigate the complexities of their relationships, they are faced with a choice that could change their lives forever. With secrets, betrayals, and unexpected twists, this gripping tale explores the power dynamics between men and women in Victorian society, challenging societal norms and expectations.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Feminist Literature

Themes: Gender roles, Marriage, Female empowerment, Societal expectations
