Synopsis: 

'The Book of Alfalfa: History, Cultivation and Merits: Its Uses as a Forage and Fertilizer' delves into the fascinating world of alfalfa, a plant that has played a significant role in agriculture for centuries. Written by Foster Dwight Coburn, this comprehensive guide explores the rich history, cultivation techniques, and numerous merits of alfalfa. From its origins in ancient civilizations to its modern-day applications as a valuable forage and fertilizer, this book offers a captivating journey through the world of this remarkable plant. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Agriculture, History

Themes: Agriculture, Sustainability, Plant Cultivation
