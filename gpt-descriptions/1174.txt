Synopsis: 
In 'Hellenica', Xenophon takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous world of ancient Greece. Set against the backdrop of the Peloponnesian War and its aftermath, this historical work follows the lives of prominent Greek figures as they navigate political intrigue, military campaigns, and personal ambitions. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous attention to detail, Xenophon brings to life the triumphs and tragedies of this pivotal period in Greek history, offering a gripping account of power struggles, alliances, and the enduring spirit of the Greek people.

Genres: Historical fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Power and politics, Loyalty and betrayal, Honor and glory, The impact of war
