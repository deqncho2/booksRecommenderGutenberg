Synopsis: In "A Study of American Beers and Ales," Riley and Tolman embark on a captivating exploration of the rich and diverse world of American brewing. From the bustling microbreweries of the Pacific Northwest to the historic beer halls of the East Coast, they delve into the fascinating stories behind some of America's most beloved beers. With meticulous research and a discerning palate, the authors uncover the secrets of brewing techniques, the cultural significance of different beer styles, and the passionate individuals who have shaped the American beer landscape. This comprehensive study is a delightful journey that will leave readers thirsty for more.

Genres: Non-fiction, Food and Drink, History

Themes: American brewing culture, Beer styles and techniques, Cultural significance of beer
