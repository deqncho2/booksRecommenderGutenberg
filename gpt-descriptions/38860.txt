Synopsis: In the heart of New York City, a young Jewish immigrant named Yetta arrives with dreams of a better life. As she navigates the bustling streets and crowded tenements, she becomes entangled in the world of labor activism and the fight for workers' rights. Yetta's journey takes her from sweatshops to union meetings, where she discovers the power of solidarity and the importance of standing up for what is right. Along the way, she encounters love, loss, and the resilience of the human spirit. Comrade Yetta is a captivating tale of one woman's determination to make a difference in a world that often seems stacked against her.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Social Justice, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Immigration, Labor Activism, Identity, Resilience
