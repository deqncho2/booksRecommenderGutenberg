Synopsis: 
'The Red Flower: Poems Written in War Time' by Van Dyke, Henry is a collection of poignant and evocative poems that capture the essence of war and its impact on the human spirit. Through vivid imagery and heartfelt verses, the author explores the themes of love, loss, courage, and the enduring power of hope amidst the chaos of battle. From the trenches to the home front, these poems offer a glimpse into the emotional landscape of those affected by war, leaving readers with a profound sense of empathy and a renewed appreciation for the resilience of the human soul.

Genres: Poetry, War Literature

Themes: Love, Loss, Courage, Hope
