Synopsis: In 'A History of Booksellers, the Old and the New' by Henry Curwen, embark on a captivating journey through time as the author delves into the fascinating world of booksellers. From the ancient scrolls of Alexandria to the bustling bookshops of modern cities, Curwen uncovers the secrets, triumphs, and challenges faced by these guardians of knowledge. Through vivid anecdotes and meticulous research, he paints a vivid picture of the evolution of bookselling, revealing the profound impact these individuals have had on society and culture. Prepare to be enthralled by the stories of passionate bibliophiles, literary treasures, and the ever-changing landscape of the book trade.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Biography

Themes: The evolution of bookselling, the preservation of knowledge, the influence of booksellers on society and culture
