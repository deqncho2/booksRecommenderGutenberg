Synopsis: 

In "History of the Forty-second regiment infantry, Massachusetts volunteers, 1862, 1863, 1864," Charles P. Bosson takes readers on a captivating journey through the experiences of the soldiers of the Forty-second regiment infantry during the American Civil War. From their initial enlistment to their courageous battles on the front lines, this historical account provides a vivid and detailed portrayal of the regiment's trials and triumphs. Through personal anecdotes, letters, and official records, Bosson brings to life the camaraderie, sacrifice, and resilience of these brave men who fought for their beliefs and for the future of their nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: Patriotism, Brotherhood, Sacrifice, Resilience
