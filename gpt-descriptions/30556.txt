Synopsis: 
In 'Sleep Walking and Moon Walking: A Medico-Literary Study', Sadger, J. delves into the fascinating realm of sleep disorders and their portrayal in literature. Exploring the intricate connection between the unconscious mind and the written word, this captivating study takes readers on a journey through the mysterious world of sleepwalking and moonwalking. From ancient myths to modern novels, Sadger examines how these nocturnal behaviors have been depicted and interpreted throughout history. With a blend of medical expertise and literary analysis, this book offers a unique perspective on the enigmatic phenomenon of sleepwalking and its profound impact on human experience.

Genres: Medical literature, Literary criticism, Psychology

Themes: Sleep disorders, Unconscious mind, Symbolism, Human behavior
