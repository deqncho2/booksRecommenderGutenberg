Synopsis: In "A Queen of Tears, vol. 1 of 2: Caroline Matilda, Queen of Denmark and Norway and Princess of Great Britain and Ireland," author W. H. Wilkins delves into the captivating life of Caroline Matilda, a young princess who becomes entangled in a web of political intrigue, forbidden love, and tragic consequences. Set against the backdrop of 18th-century Europe, this historical biography follows Caroline Matilda's journey from her royal upbringing in Britain to her ill-fated marriage to the mentally unstable King Christian VII of Denmark. As she navigates the treacherous court of Copenhagen, Caroline Matilda finds herself torn between duty, desire, and the pursuit of her own happiness. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Wilkins paints a vivid portrait of a queen whose life is marked by both triumph and tragedy.

Genres: Historical Biography, Royal Drama, Historical Fiction

Themes: Love and Betrayal, Power and Politics, Identity and Self-Discovery
