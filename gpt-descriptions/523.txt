Synopsis: 

Step into the captivating world of the Chinese court as Isaac Taylor Headland takes you on a mesmerizing journey through 'Court Life in China: The Capital, Its Officials and People'. Delve into the intricate web of power, politics, and intrigue that unfolds within the walls of the imperial palace. From the opulent ceremonies and lavish banquets to the secret alliances and hidden rivalries, this book offers a rare glimpse into the inner workings of one of the most enigmatic civilizations in history. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, Headland paints a vivid picture of court life, revealing the complex relationships between the emperor, his officials, and the common people. Prepare to be transported to a world of grandeur and mystery, where every decision carries weight and every action has consequences.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Cultural Exploration, Biography

Themes: Power and Politics, Intrigue and Betrayal, Cultural Traditions and Customs
