Synopsis: 
In 'Fors Clavigera (Volume 6 of 8): Letters to the workmen and labourers of Great Britain,' John Ruskin delivers a series of thought-provoking letters addressed to the working class of his time. Through his eloquent and impassioned writing, Ruskin explores various aspects of society, including the role of art, the importance of education, and the impact of industrialization. With a blend of social commentary, personal anecdotes, and philosophical musings, Ruskin challenges conventional wisdom and offers a unique perspective on the issues faced by the working class. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Philosophy

Themes: Social inequality, Art and aesthetics, Education, Industrialization
