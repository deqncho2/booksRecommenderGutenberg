Synopsis: In "Shylock reasons with Mr. Chesterton, and other poems," Wolfe Humbert presents a collection of thought-provoking and introspective poems that delve into the complexities of human nature and challenge societal norms. Through the lens of a conversation between Shylock, the iconic character from Shakespeare's "The Merchant of Venice," and the renowned writer G.K. Chesterton, the poems explore themes of identity, justice, prejudice, and the power of words. With a blend of wit, intellect, and emotional depth, this collection invites readers to question their own beliefs and perceptions, while offering a fresh perspective on timeless literary figures.

Genres: Poetry, Literary Fiction

Themes: Identity, Justice, Prejudice, Power of Words
