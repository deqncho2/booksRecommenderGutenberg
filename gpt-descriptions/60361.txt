Synopsis: In "The Wyoming Military Establishment," Charles Tubbs takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of the Twenty-fourth Regiment of Connecticut Militia. Delivered as an address before the Tioga Point Historical Society in 1901, Tubbs explores the regiment's formation, its role in various conflicts, and the impact it had on the Wyoming Valley. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Tubbs sheds light on the bravery, sacrifices, and triumphs of these unsung heroes, painting a vivid picture of the regiment's legacy.

Genres: Historical, Military, Non-fiction

Themes: Patriotism, Heroism, Local History
