Synopsis: In 'Radio-Active Substances', Marie Curie takes us on a captivating journey into the mysterious world of radioactivity. Through her groundbreaking research and personal experiences, she unveils the astonishing properties of these enigmatic substances, challenging the conventional understanding of matter and energy. From the mesmerizing glow of radium to the potential medical applications, Curie's narrative is filled with scientific intrigue and the thrill of discovery. As she delves deeper into the radioactive realm, she uncovers both the immense power and the potential dangers that lie within. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Biography

Themes: Scientific exploration, Discovery, Innovation, Risk and reward
