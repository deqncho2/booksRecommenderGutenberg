Synopsis:
'Joan and Peter: The story of an education' by H.G. Wells is a captivating coming-of-age novel that follows the lives of two siblings, Joan and Peter, as they navigate the tumultuous years of their education. Set against the backdrop of early 20th century England, the story delves into the challenges and triumphs they face in their pursuit of knowledge, love, and self-discovery. As they encounter a diverse cast of characters and grapple with societal expectations, the siblings must confront their own limitations and find their place in a rapidly changing world. With its richly drawn characters and thought-provoking narrative, this novel offers a poignant exploration of the transformative power of education and the complexities of personal growth.

Genres: Bildungsroman, Historical Fiction, Social Commentary

Themes: Education, Identity, Social Class, Gender Roles
