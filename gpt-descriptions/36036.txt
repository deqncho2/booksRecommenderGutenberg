Synopsis: 

In 'Metabolic Adaptation to Climate and Distribution of the Raccoon Procyon Lotor and Other Procyonidae', Mahlke-Johnson, Mugaas, and Seidensticker delve into the fascinating world of raccoons and their ability to adapt to different climates. Through extensive research and analysis, they uncover the remarkable metabolic adaptations that allow raccoons and other members of the Procyonidae family to thrive in diverse environments. From the dense forests of North America to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, this study sheds light on the evolutionary strategies employed by these intelligent and resourceful creatures. 

Genres: Scientific research, Zoology, Ecology

Themes: Adaptation, Climate change, Animal behavior
