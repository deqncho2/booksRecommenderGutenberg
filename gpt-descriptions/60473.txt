Synopsis: In "Wealth against Commonwealth," Henry Demarest Lloyd takes readers on a captivating journey through the dark underbelly of America's Gilded Age. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Lloyd exposes the hidden forces that manipulate the economy and exploit the common people. From the corrupt practices of monopolistic corporations to the ruthless tactics employed by powerful individuals, this gripping narrative sheds light on the stark realities of wealth inequality and its devastating consequences. As the battle between wealth and the commonwealth unfolds, readers are left questioning the very foundations of American society and the true meaning of democracy.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Economy

Themes: Wealth inequality, Monopoly power, Corruption, Social justice, Democracy
