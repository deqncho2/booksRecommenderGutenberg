Synopsis: In 'Don't Marry; or, Advice on How, When and Who to Marry', James W. Donovan presents a captivating exploration of the complexities and pitfalls of marriage. Through a series of thought-provoking anecdotes and insightful observations, Donovan challenges conventional wisdom and offers unconventional advice on the delicate art of choosing a life partner. With a blend of humor, wisdom, and a touch of irreverence, this book takes readers on a journey that will make them question their preconceived notions about love, commitment, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Self-help, Relationships, Humor

Themes: Marriage, Love, Compatibility, Personal Growth
