Synopsis: In 'Everlasting Pearl: One of China's Women' by Johannsen, Anna Magdalena, readers are transported to the vibrant world of 19th century China, where a young woman named Pearl embarks on a journey of self-discovery and resilience. Born into a society that values tradition and conformity, Pearl finds herself torn between the expectations placed upon her and her own desires for independence and freedom. As she navigates the complexities of love, family, and societal pressures, Pearl's story unfolds against the backdrop of a rapidly changing China, where old traditions clash with modern ideals. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this captivating novel explores the power of determination, the pursuit of personal identity, and the enduring strength of the human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Cultural

Themes: Tradition vs. Modernity, Gender Roles, Self-Discovery, Resilience
