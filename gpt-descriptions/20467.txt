Synopsis: 
In the "Handbook of the Trees of New England," Brooks and Dame take readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting forests of New England. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, this comprehensive guide introduces readers to the diverse and majestic trees that populate the region. From towering pines to delicate flowering trees, the authors delve into the unique characteristics, folklore, and ecological significance of each species. As readers explore the pages of this handbook, they will uncover hidden secrets, discover the interconnectedness of nature, and gain a newfound appreciation for the beauty and importance of trees in New England.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Reference

Themes: Biodiversity, Environmental Conservation, Natural History
