Synopsis: In "The Praise of Folly," Erasmus takes us on a witty and satirical journey through the follies of human nature. Through the perspective of Folly herself, a personified character, Erasmus humorously criticizes the vices and absurdities of society, including the clergy, scholars, and even kings. With sharp wit and clever wordplay, he challenges the reader to question the values and beliefs that often lead to foolishness. This thought-provoking and entertaining work offers a unique perspective on the human condition and the follies that we all succumb to.

Genres: Satire, Philosophy, Essay

Themes: Human folly, Critique of society, Satire of religious institutions, Intellectual arrogance, Moral corruption
