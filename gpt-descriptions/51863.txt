Synopsis: In 'Seven Centuries of Lace', Maria Margaret Pollen takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate world of lace-making. From its origins in the medieval era to its evolution as a symbol of luxury and status, this book delves into the fascinating history, techniques, and cultural significance of lace. Through vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Pollen explores the stories behind the delicate threads that have adorned garments, accessories, and even architecture for centuries. From the opulent lace of European royalty to the delicate lacework of everyday life, this book unveils the secrets and artistry behind this timeless craft.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Craftsmanship, Cultural Significance, Evolution of Fashion
