Synopsis: In "Twenty-six and One, and Other Stories" by Maksim Gorky, readers are transported to the gritty streets of late 19th-century Russia, where they encounter a diverse cast of characters facing the harsh realities of life. From a young boy's struggle to survive in a world of poverty and violence to a woman's quest for freedom and self-discovery, each story delves into the depths of human emotions and the complexities of societal norms. With vivid descriptions and poignant storytelling, Gorky paints a vivid picture of a society on the brink of change, capturing both the beauty and brutality of the human experience.

Genres: Literary Fiction, Realism, Short Stories

Themes: Poverty, Social Injustice, Human Resilience, Freedom, Identity
