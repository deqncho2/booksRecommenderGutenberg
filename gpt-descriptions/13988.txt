Synopsis: In "Fugitive Slave Law: The Religious Duty of Obedience to Law," Ichabod S. Spencer delivers a powerful sermon that challenges the moral conscience of his congregation. Set in the tumultuous era of 1850, Spencer explores the controversial Fugitive Slave Law and its implications on religious duty. With eloquence and conviction, he delves into the complexities of obedience to law, shedding light on the ethical dilemmas faced by both slaves and their sympathizers. As tensions rise and the congregation grapples with their own beliefs, Spencer's sermon becomes a catalyst for introspection and a call to action.

Genres: Sermon, Historical, Religious

Themes: Morality, Obedience, Religious Duty, Slavery, Ethical Dilemmas
