Synopsis: 
In 'Sleep and Its Derangements', William A. Hammond delves into the mysterious realm of sleep, exploring the various disorders that plague our nights and disrupt our days. From insomnia to sleepwalking, nightmares to sleep paralysis, Hammond takes readers on a captivating journey through the enigmatic world of sleep and its many derangements. With a blend of scientific research, historical anecdotes, and personal experiences, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the complexities of sleep and the impact it has on our lives.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Psychology

Themes: Sleep disorders, Neuroscience, Mental health
