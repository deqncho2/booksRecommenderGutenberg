Synopsis: In "The Origin and Development of Christian Dogma: An essay in the science of history," Charles A. H. Tuthill takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of Christian beliefs. Delving into the historical context and tracing the development of key doctrines, Tuthill explores the fascinating interplay between theology, philosophy, and societal influences. From the early debates surrounding the nature of Christ to the formation of creeds and the emergence of different Christian sects, this thought-provoking essay sheds light on the complex and often contentious process of shaping Christian dogma. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, Tuthill invites readers to explore the rich tapestry of ideas that have shaped one of the world's most influential religions.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Religious Studies

Themes: The evolution of Christian beliefs, Historical context, Theology and philosophy, Societal influences, Formation of creeds, Christian sects
