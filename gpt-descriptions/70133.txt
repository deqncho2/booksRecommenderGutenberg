Synopsis: 
'The Suppression of Tuberculosis' by Emil von Behring is a groundbreaking work that delves into the suppression of tuberculosis and its impact on both humans and animals. Behring presents his observations on phthisiogenesis, the development of tuberculosis, in both man and animals, shedding light on the similarities and differences between the two. He also provides suggestions on improving the hygiene of cow stables and the production of milk for infant-feeding, with a particular focus on preventing the spread of tuberculosis. This comprehensive study offers valuable insights into the prevention and treatment of tuberculosis, making it an essential read for anyone interested in public health and disease control.

Genres: Medical literature, Scientific research, Public health

Themes: Tuberculosis prevention, Phthisiogenesis, Hygiene, Disease control
