Synopsis: In "Anne Bradstreet and Her Time" by Helen Campbell, readers are transported to the 17th century to explore the life and works of one of America's first published poets, Anne Bradstreet. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Campbell delves into the challenges and triumphs faced by Bradstreet as a woman in a male-dominated society, as well as the societal and cultural context that shaped her writing. From her struggles with illness and loss to her deep love for her family and her unwavering dedication to her craft, this captivating biography offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of a remarkable woman who defied societal norms and left an indelible mark on American literature.

Genres: Biography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Gender roles, Creativity and expression, Family and love, Societal constraints
