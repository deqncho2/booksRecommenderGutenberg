Synopsis: In "A Colored Man's Reminiscences of James Madison," Paul Jennings, a former slave of President James Madison, shares his firsthand account of life in the White House during one of the most pivotal periods in American history. From the perspective of an African American man who witnessed the inner workings of power, Jennings offers a unique and captivating narrative that sheds light on the complexities of race, freedom, and the struggle for equality in early 19th-century America. Through his vivid recollections, readers are transported to a time when the nation was grappling with issues of slavery and the fight for independence, providing a compelling and thought-provoking glimpse into the life of one of America's founding fathers.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Race, Freedom, Equality
