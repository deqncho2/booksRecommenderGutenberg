Synopsis: In "The Tipster: 1901, From "Wall Street Stories"," Lefevre takes us back to the bustling streets of New York City during the early 20th century. The story follows a mysterious tipster who possesses an uncanny ability to predict the stock market's movements. As his predictions become increasingly accurate, he catches the attention of both Wall Street's elite and the common man. However, as the tipster's fame grows, so does the danger surrounding him. Will he be able to navigate the treacherous world of high finance and protect his secret? This gripping tale of intrigue and suspense will keep readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Thriller

Themes: Financial speculation, Power and influence, Deception and secrecy
