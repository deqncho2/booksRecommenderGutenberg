Synopsis: In 'Pan' by Knut Hamsun, we follow the story of Lieutenant Thomas Glahn, a solitary and enigmatic man who seeks refuge in the wilderness of northern Norway. As he immerses himself in the beauty of nature, Glahn becomes entangled in a passionate and tumultuous relationship with a young woman named Edvarda. Their intense connection leads to a series of events that challenge Glahn's perception of love, identity, and the boundaries of human existence.

Genres: Psychological fiction, Romance, Naturalism

Themes: Love and desire, Nature and wilderness, Isolation and solitude, Identity and self-discovery
