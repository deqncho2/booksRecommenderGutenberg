Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The life and correspondence of Sir Anthony Panizzi, K.C.B.', author Louis Fagan delves deeper into the remarkable life of Sir Anthony Panizzi, a man who rose from humble beginnings to become the principal librarian of the British Museum and a senator of Italy. This volume explores Panizzi's influential role in transforming the British Museum's library into one of the world's greatest repositories of knowledge, as well as his involvement in political and social affairs. Through a collection of letters and personal anecdotes, Fagan paints a vivid portrait of a man driven by his passion for literature, his unwavering dedication to his work, and his unwavering commitment to the pursuit of knowledge.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Ambition, Intellectualism, Cultural preservation
