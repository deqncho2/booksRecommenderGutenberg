Synopsis:
In "Harriet, the Moses of Her People," Sarah H. Bradford tells the captivating true story of Harriet Tubman, a remarkable woman who escaped slavery and then risked her life to help others find freedom on the Underground Railroad. From her daring escapes to her courageous missions, this biography explores the extraordinary life of Harriet Tubman and her unwavering determination to fight for justice and equality. As readers delve into the pages of this inspiring narrative, they will be drawn into the thrilling world of one of America's greatest heroes.

Genres:
Biography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes:
Slavery, Freedom, Courage, Heroism
