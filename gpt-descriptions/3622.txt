Synopsis: 
In "The Duke's Children" by Anthony Trollope, the Duke of Omnium, a powerful and wealthy aristocrat, faces a dilemma when his children rebel against the expectations of their noble lineage. As the Duke navigates the complexities of his family's desires and the pressures of society, he must confront the consequences of his own choices and the challenges of maintaining his legacy. With a cast of intriguing characters and a web of intricate relationships, this novel explores the clash between tradition and individuality, love and duty, and the pursuit of happiness in a rigid society.

Genres: 
Fiction, Historical Fiction, Family Drama

Themes: 
Social class, Family dynamics, Duty vs. personal desires, Tradition vs. modernity
