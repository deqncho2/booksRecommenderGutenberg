Synopsis: 
In "Three Years in the Service: A Record of the Doings of the 11th Reg. Missouri Vols." by D. McCall, readers are taken on a gripping journey through the experiences of the 11th Regiment Missouri Volunteers during the Civil War. Through the eyes of the author, who served as a soldier in the regiment, we witness the harrowing battles, the camaraderie among the troops, and the personal sacrifices made by these brave men. From the front lines to the home front, this captivating account offers a unique perspective on the realities of war and the indomitable spirit of those who fought.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Memoir, Military History

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, Brotherhood and camaraderie in the face of adversity, Sacrifice and resilience in times of conflict
