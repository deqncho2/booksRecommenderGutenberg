Synopsis: 
In "Audubon's Western Journal: 1849-1850," John Woodhouse Audubon takes readers on an extraordinary adventure as he embarks on a journey from New York to Texas, and then through Mexico and Arizona to the gold-fields of California. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, Audubon paints a vivid picture of the landscapes, people, and wildlife he encounters along the way. From treacherous river crossings to encounters with Native American tribes, Audubon's journal is a thrilling account of his daring expedition into the untamed West.

Genres: Travelogue, Adventure, Memoir

Themes: Exploration, Nature, Cultural encounters
