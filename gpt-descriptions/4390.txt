Synopsis: 

In 'A History of Greek Art: With an Introductory Chapter on Art in Egypt and Mesopotamia', Tarbell takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of Greek art, starting with its roots in ancient Egypt and Mesopotamia. From the awe-inspiring sculptures of gods and goddesses to the intricate details of pottery and architecture, this book delves into the rich history and cultural significance of Greek art. Tarbell's vivid descriptions and insightful analysis bring to life the masterpieces created by renowned artists such as Phidias and Praxiteles, while also exploring the influence of mythology, religion, and politics on artistic expression. Whether you are a history enthusiast, an art lover, or simply curious about the origins of Western art, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the timeless beauty and enduring legacy of Greek art.

Genres: Art History, Non-fiction, Cultural Studies

Themes: Evolution of Art, Cultural Influence, Mythology and Religion
