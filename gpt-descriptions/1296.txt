Synopsis: 
In 'The Provost' by John Galt, a small Scottish town becomes the stage for a gripping tale of ambition, love, and scandal. The story follows the life of George Sprott, a humble and hardworking man who unexpectedly finds himself appointed as the provost, or mayor, of the town. As he navigates the treacherous world of politics, Sprott becomes entangled in a web of deceit, power struggles, and personal vendettas. With his reputation and relationships at stake, Sprott must make difficult choices that will shape the future of the town and his own destiny. 

Genres: 
Historical Fiction, Political Drama, Romance

Themes: 
Ambition, Power, Morality, Betrayal
