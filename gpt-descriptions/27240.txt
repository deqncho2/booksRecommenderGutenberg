Synopsis: In 'Pyrrhus: Makers of History' by Abbott, Jacob, embark on a thrilling journey through the life of Pyrrhus, the legendary king of Epirus. From his early days as a young prince to his rise as a formidable warrior and cunning strategist, this captivating historical narrative unveils the triumphs and tribulations of a man destined for greatness. As Pyrrhus navigates the treacherous world of ancient politics and warfare, he must confront personal sacrifices, complex alliances, and the ever-present specter of his own mortality. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Abbott brings to life the extraordinary tale of Pyrrhus, a man whose legacy echoes through the annals of history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography

Themes: Ambition, Power, Legacy
