Synopsis: 

"The House Fly and How to Suppress It" is a captivating and informative book that delves into the fascinating world of house flies and offers practical solutions to control their population. Written by Bishopp, F. C. and Howard, L. O., this work explores the life cycle, habits, and potential dangers associated with these pesky insects. Through a combination of scientific research and practical advice, the authors provide valuable insights into the prevention and eradication of house flies, making it a must-read for anyone seeking to maintain a clean and hygienic environment.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Pest Control

Themes: Insect biology, Public health, Environmental hygiene
