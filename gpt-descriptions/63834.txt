Synopsis: 

In 'Surveying and Levelling Instruments, Theoretically and Practically Described', William Ford Stanley takes readers on a comprehensive journey through the world of surveying and leveling instruments. From the construction and qualities of these instruments to their selection, preservation, adjustments, and uses, Stanley provides a wealth of knowledge for civil engineers and surveyors in the field. This practical guide also explores various other apparatus and appliances used in the field, offering valuable insights and techniques for professionals in the industry. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, Reference

Themes: Surveying, Levelling, Instrumentation, Civil Engineering, Field Techniques
