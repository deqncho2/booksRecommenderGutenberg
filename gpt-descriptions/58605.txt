Synopsis: 

"The Boot and Shoe Manufacturers' Assistant and Guide" is a comprehensive and informative book that delves into the fascinating history of the trade, exploring the origins of boot and shoe manufacturing. It also provides a detailed account of the history and application of India-rubber and Gutta-percha in the production of footwear. The book offers step-by-step instructions, accompanied by diagrams and scales, to guide readers in mastering the art of boot and shoe making. Additionally, it covers important topics such as vulcanization and sulphurization, as well as English and American patents related to the industry. Lastly, the book includes an elaborate treatise on tanning, shedding light on this crucial aspect of the manufacturing process.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Craftsmanship

Themes: Trade history, Material innovation, Artisanal skills, Industrial processes
