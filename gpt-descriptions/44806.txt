Synopsis: 
'Portraits of Children of the Mobility' by Leigh Percival is a captivating collection of short stories that delves into the lives of children who constantly find themselves on the move. From the bustling streets of Tokyo to the remote villages of Africa, each story offers a unique glimpse into the challenges, dreams, and resilience of these young wanderers. Through vivid descriptions and heartfelt narratives, Percival explores the universal themes of identity, belonging, and the power of human connection. Prepare to be transported to different corners of the world and be moved by the untold stories of these remarkable children.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories, Contemporary Literature

Themes: Identity, Belonging, Resilience, Human Connection
