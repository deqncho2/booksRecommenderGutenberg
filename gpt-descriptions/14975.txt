Synopsis: In "Southern Horrors: Lynch Law in All Its Phases," Ida B. Wells-Barnett fearlessly exposes the dark underbelly of racial violence and injustice in the American South during the late 19th century. Through her meticulous research and personal experiences, she unveils the horrifying reality of lynching and its devastating impact on African Americans. As she delves into the motivations behind these acts of brutality, Wells-Barnett sheds light on the systemic racism and white supremacy that perpetuated such atrocities. With a powerful and unflinching voice, she calls for justice and equality, challenging the prevailing narrative and inspiring a movement for change.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Justice

Themes: Racial violence, Injustice, Systemic racism, Activism
