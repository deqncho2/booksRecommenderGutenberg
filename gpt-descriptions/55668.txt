Synopsis: 
In 'Twenty Years' Recollections of an Irish Police Magistrate', Frank Thorpe Porter takes us on a captivating journey through his experiences as a police magistrate in Ireland. From the bustling streets of Dublin to the remote corners of the countryside, Porter shares his encounters with a wide array of characters, from petty criminals to notorious gangsters. As he navigates the complex world of law enforcement, Porter finds himself entangled in a web of corruption, political intrigue, and personal dilemmas. With vivid descriptions and a keen eye for detail, this memoir offers a fascinating glimpse into the challenges and triumphs of a police magistrate in 19th-century Ireland.

Genres: Memoir, Crime, Historical

Themes: Justice and corruption, Law enforcement, Irish society and politics
