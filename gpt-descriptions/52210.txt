Synopsis: 

In 'The Hispaniola Plate (1683-1893)' by Bloundelle-Burton, John, readers are transported to the captivating world of the Caribbean, where the Hispaniola Plate becomes the backdrop for a tale of adventure, love, and treachery. Set against the backdrop of historical events spanning over two centuries, this gripping novel follows the lives of a diverse cast of characters as they navigate the tumultuous waters of colonialism, piracy, and revolution. From the bustling ports of Santo Domingo to the treacherous high seas, the story unfolds with unexpected twists and turns, keeping readers on the edge of their seats until the very last page.

Genres: Historical fiction, Adventure, Romance

Themes: Colonialism, Identity, Betrayal, Revolution
