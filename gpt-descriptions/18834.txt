Synopsis: 
'Prisoners: Fast Bound In Misery And Iron' by Mary Cholmondeley is a gripping and haunting tale that follows the lives of two individuals trapped in a web of despair and confinement. Set in the late 19th century, the story delves into the depths of human suffering and the lengths one will go to seek redemption and freedom. As secrets unravel and unexpected alliances form, the characters are forced to confront their pasts and make choices that will forever alter their lives. With its atmospheric setting and complex characters, this novel explores the dark corners of the human psyche and the power of hope in the face of adversity.

Genres: Gothic fiction, psychological thriller, historical fiction

Themes: Confinement, redemption, secrets, human suffering, hope
