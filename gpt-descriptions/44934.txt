Synopsis: 

'Captives Among the Indians' is a captivating collection of four firsthand accounts of individuals who were taken captive by Native American tribes during the colonial era. Mary White Rowlandson, Francesco Giuseppe Bressani, Massy Harbison, and James Smith each share their unique experiences of survival, resilience, and cultural adaptation in the face of adversity. From the harrowing tales of captivity to the unexpected friendships formed, these narratives offer a rare glimpse into the complex relationships between settlers and indigenous peoples during a tumultuous period in history. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, memoir, adventure

Themes: Survival, cultural clash, resilience, friendship
