Synopsis: In 'Turner: Five letters and a postscript,' C. Lewis Hind delves into the life and art of the renowned British painter, J.M.W. Turner. Through a series of intimate letters and a thought-provoking postscript, Hind uncovers the enigmatic personality and creative genius of this iconic artist. As the letters unfold, readers are transported to the vibrant world of 19th-century London, where Turner's artistic vision and personal struggles intertwine. With vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, Hind paints a vivid portrait of Turner's artistic process, his relationships, and the profound impact he had on the art world. This compelling exploration of Turner's life and work will leave readers captivated and inspired.

Genres: Biography, Art History, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic genius, Personal struggles, Influence and legacy
