Synopsis: 
'Object Lessons on the Human Body: A Transcript of Lessons Given in the Primary Department of School No. 49, New York City' takes readers on an engaging journey through the fascinating world of the human body. Through a series of captivating lessons, Buckelew and Lewis provide a comprehensive exploration of the various systems and functions that make up our bodies. From the intricate workings of the circulatory system to the marvels of the nervous system, this book offers a unique and educational experience for readers of all ages.

Genres: Non-fiction, Education, Science

Themes: Human anatomy, Physiology, Education, Science education
