Synopsis: 
In "Count Frontenac: Makers of Canada, Volume 3," William Dawson LeSueur takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Louis de Buade, Comte de Frontenac, a larger-than-life figure in Canadian history. From his early military career to his appointment as Governor of New France, Frontenac's indomitable spirit and unwavering determination are brought to life in this gripping biography. As readers delve into the pages of this book, they will witness Frontenac's relentless pursuit of power, his complex relationships with Indigenous peoples, and his relentless efforts to defend and expand French influence in North America. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, LeSueur paints a vivid portrait of a man who left an indelible mark on Canadian history.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Power and ambition, Colonialism, Indigenous relations
