Synopsis: In 'The Constitutional Amendment: or, The Sunday, the Sabbath, the Change, and Restitution', W. H. Littlejohn engages in a thought-provoking discussion with the editor of the Christian Statesman. As a Seventh-day Adventist, Littlejohn presents his perspective on the significance of the Sabbath and its place in the Constitution. The editor challenges his views, leading to a captivating exchange that explores the intersection of religion, law, and personal beliefs.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious, Legal

Themes: Religious freedom, Constitutional interpretation, Sabbath observance
