Synopsis: 

'Voltaire: A Sketch of His Life and Works' takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and literary achievements of one of history's most influential thinkers. Written by G. W. Foote and J. M. Wheeler, this engaging biography delves into the fascinating world of Voltaire, exploring his tumultuous personal life, his groundbreaking philosophical ideas, and his enduring impact on society. From his early years as a rebellious young man to his rise as a prominent Enlightenment figure, the book offers a vivid portrayal of Voltaire's wit, intellect, and unwavering commitment to challenging the status quo. Through a combination of historical anecdotes, insightful analysis, and excerpts from Voltaire's own writings, the authors paint a vivid picture of a man who fearlessly confronted religious and political authorities, championed freedom of thought, and left an indelible mark on the world of literature and philosophy.

Genres: Biography, History, Philosophy

Themes: Enlightenment, Freedom of thought, Critique of authority
