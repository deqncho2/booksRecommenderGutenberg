Synopsis: 
In the 'Elementary Course in Woodwork: Designed for use in high and technical schools, with one hundred and thirty-four illustrations: First Edition', author George Alexander Ross takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of woodworking. This comprehensive guide provides a step-by-step approach to mastering the art of working with wood, accompanied by a wealth of detailed illustrations. From understanding the different types of wood and their properties to learning essential techniques and tools, Ross equips readers with the knowledge and skills needed to create beautiful and functional wooden pieces. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced woodworker, this book is sure to inspire and guide you in your woodworking endeavors.

Genres: Educational, Crafts and Hobbies, Reference

Themes: Woodworking techniques, Wood properties, Skill development
