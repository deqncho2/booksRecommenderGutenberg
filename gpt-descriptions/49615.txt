Synopsis: In the final volume of his collected works, William Butler Yeats delves into the world of Edmund Spenser, exploring the poet's influence on tradition and his own poetic journey. Through a series of essays, Yeats examines the power of poetry and its connection to the past, offering insightful reflections on the role of the poet in society. This volume also includes a comprehensive bibliography of Yeats' works, providing a valuable resource for scholars and enthusiasts alike.

Genres: Literary criticism, poetry, essays

Themes: Influence of tradition, power of poetry, role of the poet in society
