Synopsis: 

'Fighting in France' by Kay Ross takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of a young soldier during World War I. Set against the backdrop of the trenches and battlefields of France, this captivating novel follows the protagonist's transformation from an innocent recruit to a hardened warrior. As he navigates the horrors of war, the protagonist forms deep bonds with his comrades and faces unimaginable challenges that test his courage and resilience. With vivid descriptions and emotional depth, 'Fighting in France' offers a poignant exploration of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: War, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Loss, Resilience
