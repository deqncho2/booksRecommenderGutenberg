Synopsis: In "Abraham Lincoln's Religion" by Peters, Madison Clinton, delve into the enigmatic spiritual journey of one of America's most iconic presidents. Uncover the hidden depths of Lincoln's beliefs as he navigates the tumultuous landscape of faith, doubt, and personal conviction. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, this book offers a fresh perspective on Lincoln's religious views, challenging conventional wisdom and shedding light on the complex inner world of a remarkable leader.

Genres: Biography, History, Religion

Themes: Faith and doubt, Personal conviction, Religious identity
