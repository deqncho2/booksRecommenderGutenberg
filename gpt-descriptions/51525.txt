Synopsis: In "The Influence of Darwin on Philosophy, and other essays in contemporary thought," John Dewey explores the profound impact of Darwin's theory of evolution on various branches of philosophy. From the implications for ethics and social theory to the reimagining of human nature and knowledge, Dewey delves into the far-reaching consequences of Darwin's revolutionary ideas. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Dewey invites readers to reconsider their understanding of the world and challenges them to embrace a more dynamic and interconnected view of existence.

Genres: Philosophy, Science, Essays

Themes: Evolution, Ethics, Social Theory, Human Nature, Knowledge
