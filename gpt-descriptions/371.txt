Synopsis: 

In "Literary Blunders: A Chapter in the 'History of Human Error'", Henry B. Wheatley takes readers on a captivating journey through the annals of literature, uncovering the most amusing and astonishing blunders committed by renowned authors throughout history. From Shakespeare's infamous misprints to Dickens' unintentional comedic moments, this book delves into the fascinating world of literary mishaps, shedding light on the human fallibility behind some of the greatest works of literature. With wit and charm, Wheatley invites readers to explore the humorous and sometimes cringe-worthy mistakes that have shaped the literary landscape.

Genres: Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, Humor

Themes: Human fallibility, Literary history, Humorous mistakes
