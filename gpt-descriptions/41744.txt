Synopsis: In 'The Egyptian Campaigns, 1882 to 1885', Charles Royle takes readers on a captivating journey through a pivotal period in history. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Royle delves into the military campaigns that unfolded in Egypt during the late 19th century. From the intense battles and strategic maneuvers to the political intrigue and cultural clashes, this book offers a gripping account of a conflict that shaped the region's future. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be immersed in the dramatic events and the complex characters who played key roles in this historical turning point.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Political history

Themes: War and conflict, Imperialism, Colonialism, Cultural clashes
