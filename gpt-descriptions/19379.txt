Synopsis: In 'The Leicestershires beyond Baghdad' by Edward John Thompson, readers are taken on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of a British soldier during World War I. Set in the midst of the Mesopotamian campaign, the story follows the protagonist as he navigates the treacherous landscapes of war-torn Iraq, facing not only the physical challenges of battle but also the moral dilemmas that arise in the face of conflict. As the soldier's loyalty and resilience are tested, unexpected alliances and heart-wrenching sacrifices emerge, painting a vivid picture of the human spirit amidst the chaos of war.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Loyalty, Morality, Resilience, Sacrifice
