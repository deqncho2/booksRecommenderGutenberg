Synopsis: In "The Suffragette: The History of the Women's Militant Suffrage Movement, 1905-1910," E. Sylvia Pankhurst provides a captivating account of the courageous women who fought for their right to vote during a pivotal period in history. Through vivid storytelling and personal anecdotes, Pankhurst delves into the challenges, sacrifices, and triumphs of the suffragettes, shedding light on their unwavering determination and the immense societal impact of their movement. From daring acts of civil disobedience to the relentless pursuit of equality, this book offers a compelling exploration of the suffragettes' fight for justice and their enduring legacy.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Women's Studies

Themes: Gender Equality, Activism, Social Change, Civil Rights
