Synopsis: In "The Power of Womanhood, or Mothers and Sons," Ellice Hopkins delves into the complex and profound relationship between mothers and their sons. Through a series of insightful anecdotes and thought-provoking analysis, Hopkins explores the unique influence that mothers have on shaping the lives of their sons. From the tender moments of nurturing and guidance to the challenges of letting go and allowing their sons to forge their own paths, this book offers a compelling exploration of the power and significance of womanhood in the lives of both mothers and sons.

Genres: Non-fiction, Parenting, Psychology

Themes: Motherhood, Parent-child relationships, Gender roles, Identity, Influence
