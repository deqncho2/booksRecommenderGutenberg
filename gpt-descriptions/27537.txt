Synopsis: 
In 'Sentimental Education; Or, The History of a Young Man. Volume 2' by Gustave Flaubert, we continue to follow the tumultuous life of Frédéric Moreau as he navigates the complexities of love, ambition, and societal expectations in 19th-century France. As Frédéric becomes entangled in a web of passionate affairs, political intrigue, and personal disappointments, he grapples with the consequences of his choices and the elusive pursuit of happiness. With vivid descriptions and a keen eye for human nature, Flaubert paints a captivating portrait of a young man's journey through the trials and tribulations of adulthood.

Genres: 
Realism, Bildungsroman, Historical Fiction

Themes: 
Love and Desire, Social Class and Ambition, Moral Corruption, Disillusionment, Youth and Coming of Age
