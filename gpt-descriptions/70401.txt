Synopsis: 
In "By-paths in Hebraic bookland," Abrahams takes readers on a captivating journey through the lesser-known corners of Hebrew literature. From ancient texts to modern works, he explores the hidden gems and forgotten masterpieces that have shaped the rich tapestry of Jewish literary tradition. With his insightful analysis and engaging storytelling, Abrahams uncovers the fascinating stories behind these literary treasures, shedding light on the cultural, historical, and religious significance they hold. From mystical tales to philosophical musings, this book is a captivating exploration of the diverse and enchanting world of Hebrew literature.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Jewish Studies, Cultural History

Themes: Hebrew literature, Jewish culture, Literary analysis, Historical significance
