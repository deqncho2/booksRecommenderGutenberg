Synopsis: In "The Confessions of Artemas Quibble," we are introduced to Artemas Quibble, a former practitioner in the New York Criminal Courts, who unveils the intriguing and unvarnished history of his life. Through his narrative, Quibble exposes the various cunning tactics, deceptions, and legal loopholes employed by himself and his fellow "shyster lawyers." As he recounts his experiences, the reader is drawn into a world of wiles, tricks, sophistries, technicalities, and artifices that are commonly utilized in the legal profession. This captivating account offers a unique insight into the inner workings of the legal system and the moral dilemmas faced by those who navigate it.

Genres: Legal fiction, Satire, Memoir

Themes: Legal ethics, Morality, Manipulation
