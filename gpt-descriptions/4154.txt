Synopsis: In the thirty-third volume of his famous diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a captivating journey through the months of January and February in 1664-65. As a high-ranking naval administrator, Pepys provides a unique insider's perspective on the political and social events of the time, including the ongoing war with the Dutch and the intrigue surrounding the royal court. Amidst the chaos, Pepys also delves into his personal life, sharing his thoughts on love, marriage, and the pursuit of happiness. With his characteristic wit and candidness, Pepys offers a vivid and intimate portrayal of a pivotal period in English history.

Genres: Diary, Historical, Memoir

Themes: Politics and power, Love and relationships, Personal growth
