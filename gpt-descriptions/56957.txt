Synopsis: In "A History of the Irish Poor Law, in Connexion with the Condition of the People," Sir George Nicholls delves into the complex and often overlooked history of Ireland's poor law system. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Nicholls uncovers the struggles and challenges faced by the Irish people, shedding light on the social and economic conditions that shaped their lives. From the origins of the poor law to its impact on poverty and welfare, this book offers a fascinating exploration of a crucial aspect of Irish history.

Genres: History, Social Sciences, Non-fiction

Themes: Poverty, Welfare, Social Inequality, Irish History
