Synopsis: 

In 'A history of the administration of the Royal Navy and of merchant shipping in relation to the Navy from MDIX to MDCLX, with an introduction treating of the preceding period', Michael Oppenheim takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate workings of the Royal Navy and its relationship with merchant shipping during the period from 1599 to 1660. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Oppenheim delves into the political, economic, and social factors that shaped the administration of the Navy and its impact on merchant shipping. From the rise of naval power to the challenges faced during times of war and peace, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of a crucial era in maritime history.

Genres: History, Maritime Studies, Military History

Themes: Naval administration, Merchant shipping, Political and economic influences, War and peace, Maritime power
