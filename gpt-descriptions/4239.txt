Synopsis: In 'An Essay on the Principle of Population', Malthus explores the delicate balance between population growth and available resources, presenting a thought-provoking analysis of the consequences of unchecked population expansion. Through a combination of historical examples and logical reasoning, Malthus challenges conventional wisdom and offers a unique perspective on the future of humanity.

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Social Sciences

Themes: Population growth, Resource scarcity, Sustainability, Human nature
