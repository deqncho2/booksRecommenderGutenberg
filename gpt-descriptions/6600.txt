Synopsis: In 'The Moccasin Maker' by Johnson, E. Pauline, a young woman named Mary embarks on a journey of self-discovery and resilience as she navigates the challenges of life in a small Native American community. When tragedy strikes, Mary finds solace and purpose in the art of moccasin making, uncovering a hidden talent that brings her closer to her heritage and the secrets of her ancestors. As she weaves together the threads of her past and present, Mary's journey becomes a testament to the power of tradition, identity, and the enduring strength of the human spirit.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Cultural Fiction

Themes: Identity, Heritage, Resilience, Tradition
