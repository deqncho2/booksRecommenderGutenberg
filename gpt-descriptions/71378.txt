Synopsis: In "Profile in Black and White: A Frank Portrait of South Carolina," Howard H. Quint takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex and often controversial history of South Carolina. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Quint delves into the state's racial tensions, political landscape, and cultural heritage, offering a thought-provoking exploration of the forces that have shaped this southern state. From the Civil War to the Civil Rights Movement, from the rise of the Ku Klux Klan to the emergence of influential African American leaders, this book uncovers the untold stories and hidden truths that lie beneath the surface of South Carolina's past.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Race relations, Politics, Cultural heritage
