Synopsis: In "The Peace Negotiations: A Personal Narrative," Robert Lansing takes readers on a captivating journey behind the scenes of one of the most significant diplomatic events in history. Drawing from his firsthand experiences as the United States Secretary of State during World War I, Lansing provides a unique and intimate account of the intense negotiations that shaped the post-war world. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, he unveils the complex dynamics, secret meetings, and high-stakes decisions that unfolded during this critical period. As Lansing navigates the treacherous waters of international diplomacy, readers are immersed in a gripping tale of power, intrigue, and the pursuit of peace.

Genres: Memoir, History, Political Non-fiction

Themes: Diplomacy, World War I, International Relations, Power and Politics
