Synopsis: 

In "History of the Twelfth West Virginia Volunteer Infantry: The Part It Took in the War of the Rebellion, 1861-1865," author William Hewitt delves into the gripping and often untold story of a regiment that played a significant role in the American Civil War. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, Hewitt brings to life the experiences of the brave men who served in the Twelfth West Virginia Volunteer Infantry, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices during this tumultuous period in history. From the harrowing battles they fought to the camaraderie they forged, this captivating narrative offers a unique perspective on the war and the indomitable spirit of those who fought for their beliefs.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Patriotism, Sacrifice, Brotherhood, Resilience
