Synopsis: 

In 'Battle Honours of the British Army: From Tangier, 1662, to the Commencement of the Reign of King Edward VII', Charles Boswell Norman takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of the British Army. From the exotic battles in Tangier to the dawn of a new era under King Edward VII, this meticulously researched book unveils the remarkable stories behind the battle honours earned by the British Army.

With vivid descriptions and engaging narratives, Norman brings to life the heroic exploits of soldiers who fought valiantly in various conflicts across the globe. From the iconic battles of Waterloo and Trafalgar to lesser-known but equally significant encounters, the book delves into the triumphs, sacrifices, and strategic brilliance that shaped the British Army's legacy.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history

Themes: Heroism, Warfare, British military history
