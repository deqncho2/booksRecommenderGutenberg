Synopsis: In "The Lost Tribes of the Irish in the South," Irvin S. Cobb takes us on a captivating journey through the lesser-known history of Irish immigrants in the American South. Through vivid storytelling and historical anecdotes, Cobb uncovers the fascinating and often overlooked contributions of the Irish community in the southern states. From their struggles to their triumphs, this address at the American Irish Historical Society's annual dinner in 1917 sheds light on a hidden chapter of Irish-American history.

Genres: Historical, Non-fiction

Themes: Immigration, Cultural Identity, Historical Legacy
