Synopsis: 

Index of the Project Gutenberg Works on Women's Suffrage is a comprehensive collection of four volumes edited by prominent suffragists Elizabeth Cady Stanton, Susan B. Anthony, and Matilda Joslyn Gage. This remarkable anthology brings together a diverse range of writings, speeches, and essays from the women's suffrage movement, providing a captivating glimpse into the struggles and triumphs of those who fought for women's rights. From passionate arguments for equality to personal accounts of activism, this collection offers a powerful exploration of the suffrage movement's impact on society and the enduring legacy of its courageous leaders.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Women's Studies

Themes: Women's rights, Equality, Activism, Social Change
