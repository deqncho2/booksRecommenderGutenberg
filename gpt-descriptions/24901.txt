Synopsis: In this captivating speech delivered in the Senate of the United States, Hon. John Fairfield Dryden explores the crucial topic of the American Type of Isthmian Canal. With eloquence and expertise, Dryden delves into the historical context, the challenges faced, and the potential benefits of constructing a canal. As he presents his arguments, he sheds light on the complex political and economic factors that surround this ambitious project. This thought-provoking speech offers a fascinating insight into the intricacies of canal construction and its implications for the United States.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Speech

Themes: Infrastructure development, American exceptionalism, International relations
