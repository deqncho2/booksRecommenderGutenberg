Synopsis: In 'The Uncommercial Traveller', Charles Dickens takes us on a captivating journey through the streets of Victorian London, sharing his unique encounters and observations as an anonymous wanderer. From the bustling markets to the hidden corners of the city, Dickens unveils the lives of ordinary people, their struggles, hopes, and the peculiarities of their daily existence. Through his vivid storytelling, he sheds light on the human condition, exploring themes of social inequality, compassion, and the power of empathy. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Travelogue, Memoir

Themes: Social inequality, Human condition, Empathy
