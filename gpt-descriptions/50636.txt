Synopsis: 

'Reports of Trials for Murder by Poisoning' is a gripping collection of true crime stories that delves into the dark world of murder by poison. From the notorious cases of Tawell, W. Palmer, Dove, Madeline Smith, Dr. Pritchard, Smethurst, and Dr. Lamson, this book presents a series of chilling trials that captivate the reader's imagination. With a chemical introduction and detailed notes on the poisons used, the authors provide a fascinating insight into the methods and motives behind these heinous crimes. As each trial unfolds, the reader is drawn into a web of deceit, suspense, and courtroom drama, eagerly awaiting the verdicts that will determine the fates of the accused. 

Genres: True Crime, Mystery, Legal Thriller

Themes: Murder, Poisoning, Criminal Psychology, Justice, Morality
