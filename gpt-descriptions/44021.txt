Synopsis: 
In 'Feudal England: Historical Studies on the Eleventh and Twelfth Centuries', John Horace Round takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of feudal England. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Round delves into the intricate web of power struggles, alliances, and rivalries that shaped the political and social landscape of the eleventh and twelfth centuries. From the Norman Conquest to the reign of Henry II, this book unveils the fascinating dynamics between kings, nobles, and commoners, shedding light on the complex feudal system that governed England during this era. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Round paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by individuals from all walks of life, offering a fresh perspective on a pivotal period in English history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Medieval history, Political history

Themes: Feudalism, Power dynamics, Social hierarchy, Political intrigue
