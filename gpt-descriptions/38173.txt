Synopsis: In "The American Indian as Slaveholder and Secessionist: An Omitted Chapter in the Diplomatic History of the Southern Confederacy," Annie Heloise Abel uncovers a hidden aspect of American history that has long been overlooked. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, Abel reveals the surprising role of Native American tribes in the Southern Confederacy, shedding light on their involvement as slaveholders and their support for secession. This eye-opening exploration challenges conventional notions of the Confederacy and offers a fresh perspective on the complex dynamics of the era.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Diplomatic history, Native American studies

Themes: Native American history, Slavery, Secession, Confederacy, Diplomacy
