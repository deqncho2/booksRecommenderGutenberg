Synopsis: In "God, the Invisible King," H.G. Wells explores the profound questions surrounding the existence of God and the nature of religion. Through a thought-provoking and introspective journey, Wells challenges conventional beliefs and invites readers to reconsider their understanding of spirituality and the divine. With his characteristic wit and intellectual prowess, Wells delves into the complexities of faith, reason, and the human quest for meaning, offering a fresh perspective on the age-old debate between science and religion.

Genres: Philosophy, Theology, Non-fiction

Themes: Existence of God, Spirituality, Religion and Science, Human Quest for Meaning
