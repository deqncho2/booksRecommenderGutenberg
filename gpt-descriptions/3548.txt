Synopsis: In 'The Pharisee and the Publican' by John Bunyan, two contrasting characters, a self-righteous Pharisee and a humble tax collector, find themselves in a thought-provoking encounter. As they each approach God with their prayers, their attitudes and beliefs are put to the test. This powerful allegorical tale explores the complexities of human pride, humility, and the true nature of righteousness.

Genres: Christian literature, allegory, moral tale

Themes: Pride and humility, righteousness, religious hypocrisy
