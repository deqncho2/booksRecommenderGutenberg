Synopsis: 

In 'The History of the United States from 1492 to 1910, Volume 1: From Discovery of America October 12, 1492 to Battle of Lexington April 19, 1775' by Julian Hawthorne, embark on a captivating journey through the early years of American history. From the moment Christopher Columbus set foot on the shores of the New World to the eve of the American Revolution, this volume delves into the triumphs, struggles, and pivotal events that shaped the nation.

Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Hawthorne brings to life the encounters between European explorers and Native American civilizations, the establishment of the thirteen colonies, and the growing tensions between the American colonies and the British Empire. With a keen eye for detail, the author paints a comprehensive picture of the diverse peoples, cultures, and landscapes that shaped the early United States.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, American history

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Colonialism, Cultural exchange, Revolution and independence
