Synopsis: In 'King Eric and the Outlaws, Vol. 1: or, the Throne, the Church, and the People in the Thirteenth Century,' Bernhard Severin Ingemann takes us on a captivating journey through medieval Denmark. Set in the thirteenth century, this enthralling tale follows the adventures of King Eric and a band of outlaws as they navigate the treacherous landscape of power struggles, religious conflicts, and the fight for justice. As the kingdom teeters on the brink of chaos, Eric and his unlikely companions must confront their own demons and make difficult choices that will shape the destiny of their land.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Political Drama

Themes: Power and Authority, Loyalty and Betrayal, Religion and Faith
