Synopsis: In "The Black-Bearded Barbarian: The Life of George Leslie Mackay of Formosa," Mary Esther Miller MacGregor takes readers on a captivating journey through the extraordinary life of George Leslie Mackay, a Scottish missionary who ventured into the unknown land of Formosa (modern-day Taiwan) in the late 19th century. From his initial struggles to gain acceptance among the locals to his tireless efforts in building schools, hospitals, and churches, Mackay's unwavering determination and deep connection with the people of Formosa make for a truly inspiring tale. As the story unfolds, readers will witness Mackay's encounters with pirates, his involvement in political conflicts, and his unwavering commitment to his mission, all while navigating the complexities of cultural differences and personal sacrifices. This gripping biography sheds light on the remarkable life of a man who left an indelible mark on the history of Taiwan.

Genres: Biography, History, Adventure

Themes: Missionary work, Cultural exchange, Perseverance, Sacrifice
