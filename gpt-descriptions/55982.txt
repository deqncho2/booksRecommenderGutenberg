Synopsis: 

In "Forward Pass: A Story of the 'New Football'" by Ralph Henry Barbour, readers are transported into the thrilling world of American football during the early 20th century. The story revolves around a talented young quarterback named Phil, who finds himself caught between the traditional style of football and the emerging strategy of the forward pass. As Phil navigates the challenges of the game, he must also confront personal obstacles and make difficult choices that will shape his future. With intense game sequences, unexpected twists, and a cast of memorable characters, this novel offers a captivating exploration of the evolving nature of football and the determination of one player to leave his mark on the sport.

Genres: Sports fiction, Historical fiction, Coming-of-age

Themes: Tradition vs. innovation, Personal growth and self-discovery, Pursuit of excellence
