Synopsis: In "Vermont: A Study of Independence," Robinson, Rowland Evans takes readers on a captivating journey through the picturesque landscapes and rich history of Vermont. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author delves into the unique character of this small New England state, exploring its struggle for independence and the remarkable individuals who shaped its destiny. From the early settlers and their encounters with Native Americans to the modern-day challenges faced by Vermonters, this book offers a compelling narrative that will leave readers eager to discover the untold stories and hidden treasures of Vermont.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travelogue, Biography

Themes: Independence, Identity, Resilience, Community
