Synopsis: In 'A Critical Exposition of the Popular 'Jihád'', Cherágh Ali delves into the historical context of Mohammad's wars, arguing that they were all defensive in nature. He further explores the notion that aggressive war and compulsory conversion are not permitted in the Koran. Through a meticulous analysis of religious texts, Ali challenges prevailing beliefs and sheds light on the true teachings of Islam. This thought-provoking work offers a fresh perspective on the concept of 'Jihád' and its implications in the modern world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, Historical Analysis

Themes: Religious interpretation, War and peace, Islamic teachings
