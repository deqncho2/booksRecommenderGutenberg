Synopsis: 

In 'Women of Early Christianity', Carroll, Mitchell, and Brittain delve into the lives of remarkable women who played pivotal roles in the early Christian movement. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, the authors bring to light the often overlooked contributions and struggles of these women, shedding new light on the history of Christianity. From the courageous female disciples who stood by Jesus during his crucifixion to the influential female leaders who shaped the early church, this book offers a captivating exploration of the untold stories of women in the early Christian world. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious studies, Biography

Themes: Gender and power dynamics, Women's agency and influence, Early Christian history and theology
