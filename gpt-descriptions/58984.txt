Synopsis: In the second volume of 'Audubon the Naturalist: A History of His Life and Time', Francis Hobart Herrick delves deeper into the fascinating life of John James Audubon, the renowned American ornithologist and painter. This captivating biography takes readers on a journey through Audubon's adventures in the wilderness, his tireless pursuit of knowledge, and his unwavering dedication to capturing the beauty of nature on canvas. From his encounters with exotic birds to his struggles to publish his monumental work, Audubon's story is one of passion, perseverance, and a deep love for the natural world.

Genres: Biography, History, Natural History

Themes: Nature, Art, Exploration, Conservation
