Synopsis: 
In 'The War-Workers' by E. M. Delafield, we are introduced to a group of women who find themselves thrust into the workforce during World War I. As they navigate the challenges of their new roles, they form unexpected friendships and face personal and professional dilemmas. With a mix of humor and poignancy, this novel explores the complexities of war, gender roles, and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Women's Literature

Themes: War, Gender Roles, Friendship, Resilience
