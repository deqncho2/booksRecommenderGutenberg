Synopsis: 
In 'The Radio Amateur's Hand Book: A Complete, Authentic and Informative Work on Wireless Telegraphy and Telephony', author A. Frederick Collins takes readers on an exhilarating journey into the world of wireless communication. With a wealth of knowledge and expertise, Collins provides a comprehensive guide that explores the intricacies of wireless telegraphy and telephony, offering readers a chance to delve into the fascinating realm of radio technology. From the basics of radio waves to the construction of transmitters and receivers, this handbook equips aspiring radio amateurs with the tools they need to navigate the exciting world of wireless communication. Packed with practical advice, historical anecdotes, and technical insights, this book is a must-read for anyone curious about the wonders of radio technology.

Genres: Non-fiction, Technology, Reference

Themes: Wireless communication, Radio technology, Amateur radio
