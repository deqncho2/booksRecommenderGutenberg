Synopsis: In "Paul Gauguin, His Life and Art," John Gould Fletcher takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and artistic evolution of the enigmatic French painter, Paul Gauguin. From his early struggles as a stockbroker to his decision to abandon everything and pursue his passion for art in the remote corners of the world, Gauguin's life is a fascinating tale of determination, self-discovery, and artistic rebellion. Fletcher delves into Gauguin's complex relationships, his quest for spiritual enlightenment, and the profound impact of his travels on his artistic style. Through vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, this biography offers a compelling exploration of Gauguin's life and the profound influence he had on the art world.

Genres: Biography, Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic rebellion, Self-discovery, Spiritual enlightenment
