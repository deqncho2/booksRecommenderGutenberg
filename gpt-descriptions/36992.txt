Synopsis: 
In 'The Ontario Archives: Scope of its Operations' by Fraser, Alexander, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the inner workings of the Ontario Archives, a treasure trove of historical documents and artifacts. From the meticulous preservation techniques to the fascinating stories behind each item, this book unveils the hidden world of archiving in a way that will leave readers spellbound. As the author delves into the challenges faced by archivists and the importance of preserving our collective memory, readers will gain a newfound appreciation for the invaluable role that archives play in shaping our understanding of the past.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Preservation, Historical Documentation, Memory, Cultural Heritage
