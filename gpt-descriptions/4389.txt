Synopsis: 
In "Roughing It in the Bush," Susanna Moodie takes readers on a captivating journey as she recounts her experiences of leaving behind her comfortable life in England to settle in the untamed wilderness of Canada. With vivid descriptions and a sharp wit, Moodie shares the challenges and triumphs she and her family face as they navigate the harsh realities of pioneer life. From encounters with wild animals to the struggles of building a home from scratch, this memoir offers a fascinating glimpse into the hardships and resilience of early settlers, while also exploring the complexities of identity and the power of nature.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Adventure

Themes: Survival, Identity, Nature, Resilience
