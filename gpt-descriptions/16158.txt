Synopsis: In "The Facts of Reconstruction," John Roy Lynch takes us on a captivating journey through one of the most pivotal periods in American history. Through his own personal experiences as a former slave turned congressman, Lynch provides a unique and intimate perspective on the challenges and triumphs of the Reconstruction era. From the struggles for civil rights to the political battles that shaped the nation, this memoir offers a compelling narrative that sheds light on a transformative period in American history.

Genres: Memoir, History, Political Nonfiction

Themes: Civil Rights, Reconstruction, Political Activism, Racial Equality
