Synopsis: In 'My Service in the U.S. Colored Cavalry: A Paper Read before the Ohio Commandery of the Loyal Legion, March 4, 1908', Frederick W. Browne takes us on a captivating journey through his experiences as a soldier in the U.S. Colored Cavalry during the American Civil War. From the challenges faced on the battlefield to the discrimination endured off the field, Browne's narrative sheds light on the untold stories of African American soldiers and their contributions to the war effort. Through his vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Browne offers a unique perspective on the war and the struggles faced by those fighting for freedom and equality.

Genres: Memoir, History, Military

Themes: Racism and discrimination, African American history, Civil War, Military service, Equality
