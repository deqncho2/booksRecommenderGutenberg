Synopsis: In "The Last Days of the Romanovs" by Nikolai Sokolov, the reader is taken on a gripping journey into the final moments of the Russian imperial family. Set against the backdrop of the Russian Revolution, this non-fiction account unveils the mysterious circumstances surrounding the tragic fate of Tsar Nicholas II and his family. Through meticulous research and firsthand testimonies, Sokolov delves into the web of conspiracy, betrayal, and political turmoil that ultimately led to the Romanovs' untimely demise. As the tension builds and the truth unfolds, the reader is left captivated by the shocking revelations and the haunting questions that linger long after the last page is turned.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, True Crime

Themes: Russian Revolution, Imperialism, Conspiracy, Betrayal
