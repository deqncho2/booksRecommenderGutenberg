Synopsis: 

'Principles of Orchestration, with Musical Examples Drawn from His Own Works' by Rimsky-Korsakov is a captivating and insightful guide that takes readers on a journey through the art of orchestration. With a wealth of musical examples from the composer's own works, this book offers a unique perspective on the techniques and principles behind creating rich and vibrant orchestral compositions. From the delicate interplay of instruments to the grandeur of symphonic arrangements, Rimsky-Korsakov's expertise shines through as he shares his knowledge and experiences. Whether you are a musician, composer, or simply a lover of music, this book is sure to inspire and deepen your understanding of the orchestral world.

Genres: Music, Non-fiction

Themes: Orchestration, Composition, Musical Techniques
