Synopsis: In 'Curiosities of Puritan Nomenclature', Charles Wareing Endell Bardsley delves into the intriguing world of Puritan names, unearthing a treasure trove of peculiar and sometimes baffling monikers. From names that reflect religious fervor to those that express moral values, Bardsley uncovers the stories behind these unique appellations, shedding light on the historical and cultural context in which they emerged. With wit and curiosity, the author takes readers on a fascinating journey through the Puritan naming practices, revealing the surprising and often humorous choices made by this devout community.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Linguistics

Themes: Naming practices, Puritan culture, Religious influence
