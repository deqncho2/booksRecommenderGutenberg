Synopsis: 

'Gunshot Roentgenograms: A Collection of Roentgenograms Taken in Constantinople During the Turko-Balkan War, 1912-1913, Illustrating Some Gunshot Wounds in the Turkish Army' takes readers on a captivating journey through the medical field during a tumultuous period in history. Clyde S. Ford presents a collection of roentgenograms, or X-ray images, captured in Constantinople during the Turko-Balkan War. These images vividly illustrate the devastating gunshot wounds suffered by soldiers in the Turkish Army. As readers delve into this unique historical document, they will be captivated by the stories behind each image, the medical advancements of the time, and the resilience of those affected by war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Medical literature, War documentation

Themes: War and its impact on individuals, Medical advancements and innovations, Human resilience in the face of adversity
