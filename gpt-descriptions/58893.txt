Synopsis: In 'Race Riot' by Ralph Williams, a small town is thrown into chaos when racial tensions reach a boiling point, leading to a violent and explosive race riot. As the community becomes divided along racial lines, the lives of several individuals are forever changed as they navigate through fear, prejudice, and the struggle for justice. With gripping intensity and raw emotion, this powerful novel explores the devastating consequences of hatred and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Social Drama

Themes: Racism, Identity, Community, Justice
