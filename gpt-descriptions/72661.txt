Synopsis: In "Playwrights on playmaking: $b and other studies of the stage," Matthews, Brander takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of playwriting. Through a collection of insightful essays and interviews, the author delves into the minds of renowned playwrights, exploring their creative processes, inspirations, and the challenges they face in bringing their visions to life on the stage. From Shakespeare to Ibsen, Chekhov to Williams, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the art of playmaking, providing valuable insights for both aspiring playwrights and theater enthusiasts alike.

Genres: Non-fiction, Drama, Literary Criticism

Themes: Playwriting, Creativity, Inspiration, Theatrical Artistry
