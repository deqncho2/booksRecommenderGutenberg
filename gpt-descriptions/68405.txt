Synopsis: 
In the October 1895 issue of 'The Philistine: A Periodical of Protest', readers are treated to a thought-provoking collection of essays, stories, and illustrations that challenge societal norms and ignite a spirit of rebellion. From scathing critiques of the art world to poignant reflections on the human condition, this volume delves into the depths of human existence, urging readers to question the status quo and embrace their individuality. With its bold and unapologetic voice, 'The Philistine' offers a refreshing perspective on the world and invites readers to join the ranks of the enlightened.

Genres: Literary magazine, Satire, Social commentary

Themes: Individualism, Nonconformity, Critique of society
