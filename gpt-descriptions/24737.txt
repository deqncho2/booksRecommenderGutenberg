Synopsis: In 'The Children of Odin: The Book of Northern Myths', Padraic Colum takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of Norse mythology. From the creation of the universe to the epic battles of gods and giants, this collection of tales introduces us to the legendary figures of Odin, Thor, Loki, and many others. As we delve into the rich tapestry of Norse mythology, we witness the triumphs and tragedies of these immortal beings, their complex relationships, and the profound impact they have on the mortal realm. With vivid storytelling and a touch of magic, Colum brings these ancient myths to life, transporting readers to a realm of adventure, heroism, and timeless wisdom.

Genres: Mythology, Fantasy, Folklore

Themes: Power and destiny, Good versus evil, Loyalty and betrayal, The cycle of life and death
