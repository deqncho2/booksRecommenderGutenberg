Synopsis: 
In 'The Public School Word-book', John Stephen Farmer takes readers on a fascinating journey through the historical glossary of words, phrases, and expressions that were once peculiar to the great public schools. From archaic terms to current usage, Farmer explores the linguistic evolution of these unique expressions, shedding light on their origins and meanings. Alongside the public school lexicon, he also delves into the modish language prevalent at universities. This comprehensive compilation offers a captivating glimpse into the linguistic heritage of these esteemed educational institutions, providing a valuable resource for language enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

Genres: 
Historical, Linguistics, Reference

Themes: 
Language evolution, Educational institutions, Cultural heritage
