Synopsis: 
In "The Journal of the Debates in the Convention which Framed the Constitution of the United States, May-September 1787. Volume 2," James Madison provides an intimate and detailed account of the Constitutional Convention that shaped the foundation of the United States. As the debates unfold, tensions rise, alliances form, and compromises are made, revealing the intricate process of crafting a constitution that would shape the nation's future. This volume offers a unique insight into the minds of the Founding Fathers and the challenges they faced in creating a government that would balance power and protect individual liberties.

Genres: 
Historical non-fiction, Political science, Legal literature

Themes: 
Constitutional law, Governance, Democracy, Individual rights, Compromise
