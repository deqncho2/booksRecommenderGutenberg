Synopsis: In 'Se-quo-yah; from Harper's New Monthly, V.41', the reader is transported to the fascinating world of the Cherokee people and their struggle for cultural preservation. This captivating narrative follows the life of Se-quo-yah, a remarkable figure who defied all odds to create a written language for his tribe. As the story unfolds, the reader is immersed in a tale of determination, resilience, and the power of language to shape identity and preserve heritage.

Genres: Historical fiction, Biography, Cultural heritage

Themes: Cultural preservation, Identity, Language, Resilience
