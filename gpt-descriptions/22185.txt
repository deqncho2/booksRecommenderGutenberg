Synopsis:
In "Sonnie-Boy's People" by James B. Connolly, a young man named Sonnie-Boy finds himself caught between two worlds - the rugged, wild West and the refined, sophisticated East. As he navigates the challenges and conflicts that arise from his dual identity, Sonnie-Boy must confront his own desires and ambitions, while also grappling with the expectations and traditions of his family and community. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this captivating novel explores the complexities of identity, the clash of cultures, and the pursuit of personal fulfillment.

Genres:
Western, Coming-of-age, Historical Fiction

Themes:
Identity, Cultural clash, Personal growth
