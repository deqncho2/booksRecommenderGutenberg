Synopsis: In 'The uses of astronomy: An oration delivered at Albany on the 28th of July, 1856', Edward Everett takes readers on a captivating journey through the vastness of the universe and its profound impact on humanity. Through eloquent storytelling and thought-provoking anecdotes, Everett explores the practical applications of astronomy and its ability to inspire wonder and curiosity. From the exploration of distant planets to the navigation of ships, astronomy's influence extends far beyond the realm of stargazing. This oration serves as a reminder of the immense power and beauty that lies within the study of the cosmos.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Astronomy

Themes: Wonder and curiosity, Practical applications of astronomy, The power of knowledge
