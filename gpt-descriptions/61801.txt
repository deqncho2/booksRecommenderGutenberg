Synopsis: 
'Mississippi Piney Woods: A Photographic Study of Folk Architecture' takes readers on a captivating visual journey through the unique and often overlooked world of folk architecture in the Mississippi Piney Woods region. Through stunning photographs, Patti Carr Black showcases the rich history and cultural significance of these humble yet remarkable structures, revealing the stories and traditions embedded within their walls. From charming cabins and churches to barns and bridges, this book offers a glimpse into a world where craftsmanship and heritage intertwine, inviting readers to appreciate the beauty and resilience of these architectural treasures.

Genres: Non-fiction, Photography, Art

Themes: Folk architecture, Cultural heritage, History, Craftsmanship
