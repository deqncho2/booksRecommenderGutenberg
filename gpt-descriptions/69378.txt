Synopsis: 
'Negro Workaday Songs' is a captivating collection of African American folk songs compiled by Howard Washington Odum and Guy Benton Johnson. This anthology takes readers on a musical journey through the rich cultural heritage of African American work songs, capturing the essence of labor, struggle, and resilience. From the cotton fields to the railroads, these songs provide a powerful glimpse into the daily lives and experiences of African American workers. With heartfelt lyrics and soulful melodies, 'Negro Workaday Songs' offers a poignant exploration of the human spirit and the enduring power of music.

Genres: Folklore, Music, African American Studies

Themes: Labor, African American culture, Resilience
