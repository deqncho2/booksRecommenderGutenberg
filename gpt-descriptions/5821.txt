Synopsis: In 'The Gilded Age, Part 4,' Twain and Warner continue their satirical exploration of American society, taking readers on a humorous and thought-provoking journey through the lives of their colorful characters. As the story unfolds, unexpected alliances are formed, secrets are revealed, and the consequences of greed and ambition become increasingly apparent. With their signature wit and sharp social commentary, the authors delve into the complexities of wealth, power, and the pursuit of the American Dream, leaving readers both entertained and enlightened.

Genres: Satire, Historical Fiction, Comedy

Themes: Wealth and Corruption, Social Mobility, American Dream
