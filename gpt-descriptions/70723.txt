Synopsis: In "Drugs that enslave: The opium, morphine, chloral and hashisch habits," H.H. Kane delves into the dark and captivating world of drug addiction. Through a series of gripping narratives and insightful analysis, Kane explores the allure and devastating consequences of opium, morphine, chloral, and hashish. From the opium dens of Shanghai to the opulent parlors of Victorian England, this book takes readers on a journey through the seductive grip of these substances and the lives they ensnare. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Kane sheds light on the physical, psychological, and societal impact of these drugs, leaving readers both fascinated and horrified by the power they hold over individuals and communities.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, History

Themes: Addiction, Substance abuse, Societal impact
