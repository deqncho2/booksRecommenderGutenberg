Synopsis: 

In "Policing the Plains: Being the Real-Life Record of the Famous North-West Mounted Police," R.G. MacBeth takes readers on an exhilarating journey through the untamed Canadian frontier. Set in the late 19th century, this gripping non-fiction work follows the courageous men of the North-West Mounted Police as they strive to establish law and order in the vast and lawless plains.

From thrilling encounters with outlaws and bandits to heartwarming interactions with indigenous communities, MacBeth's vivid storytelling brings to life the challenges and triumphs of these early Canadian law enforcement officers. Through their dedication, resourcefulness, and unwavering commitment to justice, the North-West Mounted Police become legendary figures in the annals of Canadian history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Adventure

Themes: Law enforcement, Frontier life, Indigenous relations, Justice
