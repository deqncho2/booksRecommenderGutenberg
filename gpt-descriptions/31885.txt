Synopsis: In 'Bible Myths and their Parallels in other Religions', T. W. Doane delves into the fascinating world of ancient myths and miracles, drawing intriguing comparisons between the stories found in the Old and New Testaments and those of other ancient civilizations. Exploring the origins and meanings behind these tales, Doane uncovers surprising parallels that challenge traditional religious interpretations. This thought-provoking work invites readers to question the uniqueness of biblical narratives and consider the broader context in which they emerged.

Genres: Comparative religion, mythology, historical analysis

Themes: Religious mythology, cultural influences, interpretation of scripture
