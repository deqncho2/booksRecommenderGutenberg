Synopsis: In 'The Belief in Immortality and the Worship of the Dead, Volume 2: The Belief Among the Polynesians', James George Frazer delves into the fascinating world of Polynesian beliefs and rituals surrounding death and the afterlife. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Frazer uncovers the intricate customs, myths, and practices that shape the Polynesians' profound belief in immortality. From the mysterious rituals of ancestor worship to the captivating tales of spirits and gods, this volume offers a captivating exploration of a culture deeply rooted in the supernatural.

Genres: Anthropology, Mythology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Belief systems, Death and afterlife, Cultural practices
