Synopsis: 

In "Schools, School-Books and Schoolmasters: A Contribution to the History of Educational Development in Great Britain," William Carew Hazlitt takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of education in Britain. From the early days of monastic schools to the rise of grammar schools and the emergence of private tutors, Hazlitt delves into the fascinating world of educational institutions, textbooks, and the influential figures who shaped the British educational system. Through meticulous research and engaging anecdotes, Hazlitt uncovers the challenges, innovations, and controversies that have marked the development of education in Great Britain.

Genres: History, Education, Non-fiction

Themes: Educational development, Evolution of schools, Influence of schoolbooks, Impact of schoolmasters, Historical changes in education
