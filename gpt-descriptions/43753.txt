Synopsis: 

In "Cyrus W. Field, His Life and Work [1819-1892]," readers are transported to the fascinating world of a visionary entrepreneur who defied all odds to connect continents and revolutionize global communication. This captivating biography delves into the life of Cyrus W. Field, a man driven by an audacious dream to lay the first transatlantic telegraph cable. From his humble beginnings as a young paperboy to his relentless pursuit of technological innovation, Field's journey is one of determination, setbacks, and triumphs that will leave readers in awe of his indomitable spirit. 

Genres: Biography, History, Technology

Themes: Perseverance, Innovation, Ambition
