Synopsis: In "An Address to the People of New-England," Samuel Hopkins explores the crucial significance of establishing a positive relationship with the Native Americans. He emphasizes the importance of treating them fairly and kindly, while also advocating for the spread of Christianity among them. Hopkins presents compelling arguments for why it is in the best interest of the New Englanders to form a strong bond with the indigenous population, urging them to take action in order to secure a prosperous future for both communities.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Religious

Themes: Indigenous relations, Justice, Christianity, Cultural assimilation
