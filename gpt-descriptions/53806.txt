Synopsis: 
In 'Suburban Residences, and How to Circumvent Them', Jane Ellen Panton takes readers on a captivating journey through the seemingly idyllic world of suburban life. Through a series of intriguing anecdotes and clever observations, Panton unveils the hidden secrets and unexpected challenges that lie beneath the surface of these seemingly perfect neighborhoods. From nosy neighbors to peculiar community traditions, this book offers a humorous and insightful exploration of the quirks and complexities of suburban living. As readers navigate through the pages, they will find themselves both entertained and enlightened, discovering that there is much more to suburban residences than meets the eye.

Genres: Humor, Non-fiction, Sociology

Themes: Suburban life, Community dynamics, Social expectations
