Synopsis: 

In "The Sovereignty of the Sea: An Historical Account of the Claims of England to the Dominion of the British Seas, and of the Evolution of the Territorial Waters," Thomas Wemyss Fulton takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of England's claims to the dominion of the British seas. From ancient times to the modern era, Fulton explores the evolution of territorial waters and the complex legal and political battles that have shaped the maritime boundaries we know today. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this book unveils the intriguing struggles, conflicts, and triumphs that have defined England's sovereignty over the sea.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Maritime history, Legal history

Themes: Sovereignty, Territorial disputes, Legal and political history, Evolution of maritime boundaries
