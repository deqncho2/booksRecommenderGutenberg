Synopsis: 

In 'Myths and Folk-lore of the Timiskaming Algonquin and Timagami Ojibwa', Frank G. Speck takes readers on a captivating journey into the rich cultural heritage of the indigenous peoples of Timiskaming Algonquin and Timagami Ojibwa. Through a collection of enchanting myths and folktales, Speck unveils a world filled with mystical creatures, ancient rituals, and timeless wisdom. From the creation of the world to the adventures of legendary heroes, these stories offer a glimpse into the spiritual beliefs and traditions of these indigenous communities. As readers delve into the pages of this book, they will be transported to a realm where nature and spirituality intertwine, where the past and present merge, and where the power of storytelling holds the key to understanding the mysteries of life.

Genres: Folklore, Mythology, Anthropology

Themes: Indigenous culture, Spirituality, Oral tradition
