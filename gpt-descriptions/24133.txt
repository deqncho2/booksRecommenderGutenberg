Synopsis: In 'The Long Run: 1916' by Edith Wharton, readers are transported to the tumultuous era of World War I. The story revolves around a young American man named Halwyn Forsyte, who finds himself caught between his duty to his country and his desire for personal freedom. As he navigates the complexities of love, loyalty, and the moral dilemmas of war, Halwyn's journey takes unexpected turns that challenge his beliefs and force him to confront the true cost of his choices. Set against the backdrop of a world in turmoil, this gripping tale explores the profound impact of war on individuals and society.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Romance

Themes: Duty and Responsibility, Personal Freedom, Love and Sacrifice
