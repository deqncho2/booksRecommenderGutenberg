Synopsis: In "Her Own Way: A Play in Four Acts," Fitch, Clyde takes us on a captivating journey through the life of a strong-willed young woman named Molly. Set in the early 20th century, the play explores Molly's determination to forge her own path in a society that expects women to conform to traditional roles. As she navigates the complexities of love, ambition, and societal expectations, Molly's choices challenge the status quo and force those around her to confront their own prejudices and limitations. With its compelling characters and thought-provoking narrative, "Her Own Way" offers a gripping exploration of personal freedom and the power of self-determination.

Genres: Drama, Romance, Social Commentary
Themes: Independence, Gender Roles, Societal Expectations
