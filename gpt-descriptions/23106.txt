Synopsis: In "Helen and Arthur; or, Miss Thusa's Spinning Wheel," the lives of two young orphans, Helen and Arthur, intertwine in a small New England town. Helen, a spirited and independent girl, finds solace in her love for nature and her determination to overcome adversity. Arthur, a kind-hearted and talented artist, struggles to find his place in a world that often underestimates him. As their paths cross, they embark on a journey of self-discovery, friendship, and unexpected love, all while facing the challenges and secrets that lie within their community. Set against the backdrop of the early 19th century, this captivating tale explores the power of resilience, the complexities of human relationships, and the pursuit of one's true identity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Resilience, Friendship, Identity, Love, Social Expectations
