Synopsis: In the second volume of "The Life of Johannes Brahms," author Florence May delves deeper into the fascinating life of the renowned composer. As Brahms navigates the complexities of his personal relationships and grapples with his artistic identity, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the highs and lows of his career. From his collaborations with other musical geniuses to his struggles with self-doubt and the pressures of fame, this volume offers a rich exploration of Brahms' life and legacy.

Genres: Biography, Music, Historical

Themes: Artistic identity, Relationships, Self-doubt, Fame
