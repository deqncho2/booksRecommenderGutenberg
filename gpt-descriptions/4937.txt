Synopsis:
'Hudibras, in Three Parts, Written in the Time of the Late Wars' by Samuel Butler is a satirical epic poem that follows the adventures of the eponymous character, Hudibras, a self-proclaimed knight errant. Set during the English Civil War, the poem humorously explores the absurdities and follies of the time through Hudibras' misadventures and encounters with various eccentric characters. As he embarks on his misguided quest for justice and righteousness, Hudibras finds himself entangled in a series of comical situations that challenge his beliefs and expose the hypocrisy of those around him. With its witty wordplay, clever satire, and sharp social commentary, 'Hudibras' offers a humorous and thought-provoking exploration of human nature and the complexities of society.

Genres:
Satire, Epic Poetry

Themes:
Satire, Hypocrisy, Absurdity, Justice, Human Nature
