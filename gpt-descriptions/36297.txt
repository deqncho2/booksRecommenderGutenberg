Synopsis: 

In 'Visual Illusions: Their Causes, Characteristics and Applications' by Matthew Luckiesh, prepare to embark on a mind-bending journey through the fascinating world of visual illusions. From optical tricks that deceive our perception to the mesmerizing ways our brain interprets reality, this book unveils the secrets behind these captivating phenomena. Luckiesh delves into the science behind visual illusions, exploring their causes and shedding light on their characteristics. Through a collection of intriguing examples and thought-provoking explanations, he invites readers to question their own perception and discover the hidden complexities of the visual world. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Psychology

Themes: Perception, Cognitive Science, Optical Illusions
