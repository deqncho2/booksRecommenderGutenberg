Synopsis: 

In "Maria Mitchell: Life, Letters, and Journals," we delve into the captivating world of Maria Mitchell, a pioneering astronomer who defied societal expectations and made groundbreaking discoveries in the 19th century. Through her own words, we witness her journey from a young girl with an insatiable curiosity to a renowned scientist who shattered glass ceilings. Mitchell's letters and journals offer a glimpse into her personal life, her struggles, and her triumphs, as she navigates the challenges of being a woman in a male-dominated field. From her early observations of the night sky to her pivotal role in the discovery of a new comet, this book takes us on an inspiring voyage through the life of a remarkable woman who left an indelible mark on the scientific community.

Genres: Biography, Autobiography, Science

Themes: Gender equality, Scientific discovery, Pursuit of knowledge
