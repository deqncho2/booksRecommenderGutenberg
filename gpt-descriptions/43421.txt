Synopsis: In "China in America: A study in the social life of the Chinese in the eastern cities of the United States," Culin, Stewart takes readers on a captivating exploration of the Chinese community in the eastern cities of the United States. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, the author delves into the social, cultural, and economic aspects of the Chinese immigrant experience, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and the unique contributions they have made to American society. From the bustling streets of Chinatown to the challenges faced by Chinese immigrants in their pursuit of the American Dream, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into a vibrant and often misunderstood community.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Immigration, Cultural assimilation, Identity, Community, Discrimination
