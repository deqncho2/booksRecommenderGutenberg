Synopsis: In "What's Wrong with the World," G.K. Chesterton takes a witty and thought-provoking journey through various aspects of society, challenging conventional wisdom and shedding light on the underlying issues that plague humanity. From the role of women in society to the flaws of modern education, Chesterton's sharp observations and clever arguments will captivate readers, urging them to question the status quo and seek a better understanding of the world around them.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Philosophy

Themes: Society, Tradition, Individualism, Feminism, Education
