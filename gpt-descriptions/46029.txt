Synopsis: 

'Charities and the Commons: The Pittsburgh Survey, Part I. The People' takes readers on a captivating journey through the city of Pittsburgh, exploring the lives and struggles of its diverse population. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the book delves into the social and economic conditions of the time, shedding light on the challenges faced by the working class, immigrants, and marginalized communities. As the survey unfolds, readers are introduced to a cast of compelling characters whose stories intertwine, painting a rich and nuanced portrait of a city in transition. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social History, Sociology

Themes: Social inequality, Urbanization, Community development, Immigration, Labor rights
