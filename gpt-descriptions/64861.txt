Synopsis: In 'The Love of Azalea' by Watanna, Onoto, a young woman named Azalea finds herself torn between duty and desire when she is forced into an arranged marriage. As she navigates the complexities of love, tradition, and societal expectations, Azalea discovers the power of her own voice and the strength to challenge the constraints placed upon her. Set against the backdrop of early 20th century Japan, this captivating tale explores the complexities of love, identity, and the pursuit of personal happiness.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Love and Duty, Tradition and Rebellion, Self-Discovery
