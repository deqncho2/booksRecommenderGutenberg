Synopsis: In 'The Fraud of Feminism', Ernest Belfort Bax delves into the controversial topic of feminism, challenging its core principles and exposing what he believes to be its deceptive nature. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Bax explores the impact of feminism on society, relationships, and gender dynamics, unraveling the intricate web of ideologies that surround this movement. With a critical eye, he questions the validity of feminist claims and offers a compelling argument against its prevailing influence. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Criticism, Gender Studies

Themes: Feminism, Gender Equality, Social Change, Ideology
