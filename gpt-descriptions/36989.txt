Synopsis: In 'Some Phases of Sexual Morality and Church Discipline in Colonial New England', Charles Francis Adams delves into the intriguing and scandalous world of sexual morality and church discipline in the early days of New England. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Adams uncovers the hidden secrets, forbidden desires, and the strict religious codes that governed the lives of the colonists. From illicit affairs to public shaming, this book sheds light on the complex web of relationships and the consequences faced by those who dared to defy societal norms. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, social history, cultural studies

Themes: Sexual morality, religious discipline, societal norms, gender roles, colonial America
