Synopsis: In "The Greater Inclination," Edith Wharton presents a collection of captivating short stories that delve into the complexities of human nature and the hidden desires that lie beneath the surface. From the struggles of a young woman torn between duty and love to the unexpected consequences of a secret affair, each story offers a glimpse into the inner workings of the human heart. With her keen observations and masterful storytelling, Wharton explores the delicate balance between societal expectations and personal desires, leaving readers eager to uncover the secrets that lie within each character's heart.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories

Themes: Love and desire, societal expectations, inner conflicts, human nature
