Synopsis: In "Pedestrianism; or, An Account of the Performances of Celebrated Pedestrians During the Last and Present Century," author Walter Thom takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of pedestrianism, showcasing the extraordinary feats of renowned walkers from the past and present. The book delves into the fascinating narrative of Captain Barclay's public and private matches, revealing the intense physical and mental training required for such remarkable achievements. With an additional essay on training, Thom provides valuable insights into the dedication and discipline necessary to excel in this unique sport. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Sports, Biography

Themes: Human endurance, Physical and mental training, Historical achievements
