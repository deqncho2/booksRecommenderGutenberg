Synopsis: In 'The Sanitary Evolution of London', Jephson takes readers on a captivating journey through the transformation of London's sanitation system. From the foul stench of the city's streets to the innovative solutions that revolutionized public health, this book uncovers the hidden history of London's battle against disease and filth. Through vivid descriptions and compelling anecdotes, Jephson sheds light on the unsung heroes and the remarkable progress made in improving the city's sanitation. Prepare to be amazed by the ingenuity and determination that shaped the modern metropolis we know today.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Urban Studies

Themes: Public health, Urban development, Technological advancements
