Synopsis:
'The Longest Journey' by E. M. Forster is a captivating novel that follows the life of Rickie Elliot, a young man struggling to find his place in the world. From his sheltered upbringing in a small English town to his experiences at university and beyond, Rickie embarks on a journey of self-discovery and personal growth. Along the way, he encounters a colorful cast of characters, each with their own unique perspectives on life and love. As Rickie navigates the complexities of relationships, societal expectations, and his own inner turmoil, he must confront the choices that will shape his future and ultimately lead him towards his own truth.

Genres:
Literary Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Bildungsroman

Themes:
Identity, Love and Relationships, Society and Conformity
