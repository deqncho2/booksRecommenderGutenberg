Synopsis: 
'The Book of the V.C.: A record of the deeds of heroism for which the Victoria Cross has been bestowed, from its institution in 1857 to the present time' is a captivating historical account that delves into the extraordinary acts of bravery and valor that have earned individuals the prestigious Victoria Cross. From the inception of this esteemed honor in 1857 to the present day, author A. L. Haydon meticulously chronicles the awe-inspiring stories of those who have risked their lives in the face of danger, showcasing their unwavering courage and selflessness. This book offers a gripping exploration of the human spirit and the remarkable feats accomplished by individuals in the most challenging circumstances.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Heroism, Bravery, Sacrifice, Human resilience
