Synopsis: 

'Half Hours with the Lower Animals: Protozoans, Sponges, Corals, Shells, Insects, and Crustaceans' takes readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of the smallest creatures that inhabit our planet. From the microscopic protozoans to the intricate structures of sponges, corals, and shells, and the diverse and often misunderstood world of insects and crustaceans, Charles Frederick Holder provides a series of engaging and informative encounters with these lower animals. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, the author unveils the hidden wonders and remarkable behaviors of these often overlooked creatures, shedding light on their unique adaptations, survival strategies, and their intricate roles within the natural world. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Science

Themes: Biodiversity, Animal Behavior, Natural Adaptations
