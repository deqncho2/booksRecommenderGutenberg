Synopsis: 

In 'Ornithological biography, Vol. 2 (of 5): An account of the habits of the birds of the United States of America,' John James Audubon takes us on a captivating journey through the diverse avian world of America. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Audubon delves into the lives and behaviors of various bird species, revealing their unique characteristics and the intricate relationships they have with their environment. From the majestic Bald Eagle to the elusive Carolina Parakeet, Audubon's meticulous observations and passionate storytelling bring these birds to life, offering readers a fascinating glimpse into the natural wonders of the United States.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural history, Biography

Themes: Nature appreciation, Environmental conservation, Scientific exploration
