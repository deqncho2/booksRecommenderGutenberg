Synopsis: In 'A History of the Peninsular War, Vol. 2, Jan.-Sep. 1809: From the Battle of Corunna to the End of the Talavera Campaign', Charles Oman delves into the tumultuous period of the Peninsular War, focusing on the events that unfolded between January and September 1809. This volume takes readers on a gripping journey through the aftermath of the Battle of Corunna and the subsequent British retreat, the French invasion of Portugal, and the intense Talavera Campaign. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Oman brings to life the strategic maneuvers, political intrigues, and heroic battles that shaped this pivotal phase of the war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War narrative

Themes: War and strategy, Leadership and command, National identity, Resilience and perseverance
