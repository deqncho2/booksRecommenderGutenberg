Synopsis: In 'The Fifth Queen: And How She Came to Court' by Ford, Ford Madox, we are transported to the court of King Henry VIII, where a young and intelligent woman named Katharine Howard finds herself caught in a dangerous game of power, love, and betrayal. As she navigates the treacherous waters of the Tudor court, Katharine must learn to navigate the intricate web of politics and alliances, all while trying to protect her own secrets. With vivid historical detail and compelling characters, this gripping tale explores the rise and fall of a queen who defied expectations and challenged the norms of her time.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Political Drama

Themes: Power and Politics, Love and Betrayal, Gender Roles and Expectations
