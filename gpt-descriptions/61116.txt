Synopsis: In "Pharos and Pharillon," E.M. Forster takes readers on a captivating journey through the landscapes of Alexandria and the Mediterranean. The story follows two characters, Pharos and Pharillon, as they navigate the complexities of love, identity, and cultural clashes. As their paths intertwine, secrets are unveiled, and the characters are forced to confront their own desires and the societal expectations that surround them. With Forster's signature wit and insightful observations, "Pharos and Pharillon" offers a thought-provoking exploration of human connections and the search for personal freedom.

Genres: Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-age

Themes: Love and desire, Identity and self-discovery, Cultural clashes and societal expectations
