Synopsis: 

'Penal Methods of the Middle Ages: Criminals, Witches, Lunatics' by Ives, George Burnham delves into the dark and intriguing world of punishment during the Middle Ages. From the gruesome methods used to discipline criminals to the persecution of alleged witches and the treatment of the mentally ill, this book uncovers the harsh realities of justice in a bygone era. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, the author paints a vivid picture of the brutal punishments and societal attitudes that prevailed during this tumultuous period. 

Genres: History, Non-fiction, Crime

Themes: Justice and punishment, Social attitudes, Mental health
