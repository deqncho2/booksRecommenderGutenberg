Synopsis: In the second volume of "Life of Johnson," James Boswell continues his captivating account of the renowned English writer and lexicographer, Samuel Johnson. Set between 1765 and 1776, this volume delves into Johnson's eventful life, chronicling his literary achievements, personal struggles, and his interactions with notable figures of the time. From his influential work on the English dictionary to his travels and encounters with prominent individuals, Boswell paints a vivid portrait of Johnson's complex character and his impact on the literary world.

Genres: Biography, Memoir, Literary Nonfiction

Themes: Literary genius, Friendship, Intellectual pursuits, Personal struggles, Historical context
