Synopsis: In 'The Theory and Practice of Brewing' by Combrune, Michael, a young aspiring brewer embarks on a journey to uncover the secrets of the ancient art of brewing. From the bustling streets of London to the tranquil countryside, he encounters eccentric characters, uncovers long-lost recipes, and delves into the science behind the perfect pint. As he navigates the challenges of mastering the craft, he discovers that brewing is not just about creating a delicious beverage, but also about preserving tradition and forging new paths in the ever-evolving world of beer.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Culinary

Themes: Tradition, Innovation, Pursuit of Knowledge
