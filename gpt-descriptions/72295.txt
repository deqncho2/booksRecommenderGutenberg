Synopsis: 

In "The Thames and its docks," Alexander Forrow takes readers on a captivating journey through the bustling world of London's iconic river and its vibrant docks. From the early days of trade and exploration to the modern era of globalization, this book unveils the hidden stories and untold secrets that have shaped the Thames and its surrounding areas. With vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, Forrow paints a vivid picture of the people, ships, and industries that have thrived along the river's banks. From the grandeur of the historic docks to the gritty realities of the working-class neighborhoods, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of the Thames and its enduring significance.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Travel

Themes: Maritime history, Urban development, Industrialization
