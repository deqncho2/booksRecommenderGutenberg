Synopsis: In "George Washington: Farmer: Being an Account of His Home Life and Agricultural Activities," author Paul Leland Haworth delves into the lesser-known aspects of the iconic figure's life. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, the book uncovers the fascinating world of George Washington as a farmer, providing insights into his daily routines, agricultural experiments, and the profound impact his farming practices had on the nation. From his innovative techniques to his struggles and triumphs, this captivating account sheds light on a side of Washington that is often overlooked.

Genres: Biography, History, Agriculture

Themes: Leadership, Innovation, Legacy
