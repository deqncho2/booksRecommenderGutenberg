Synopsis:
'Round about a Pound a Week' by Pember Reeves, Mrs. is a captivating novel that delves into the lives of working-class women in early 20th century London. Through the eyes of a young protagonist, the story explores the struggles, dreams, and resilience of these women as they navigate the harsh realities of poverty and inequality. As the protagonist immerses herself in the world of factory work, she encounters a diverse cast of characters, each with their own unique stories and aspirations. With vivid descriptions and poignant storytelling, this novel offers a compelling glimpse into the lives of those often overlooked in society.

Genres:
Social realism, Historical fiction, Feminist literature

Themes:
Poverty, Gender inequality, Social class, Empowerment
