Synopsis: In "A True Account of the Battle of Jutland, May 31, 1916," Thomas Goddard Frothingham takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most significant naval battles of World War I. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Frothingham brings to life the intense clash between the British Royal Navy and the German High Seas Fleet. As the battle unfolds, readers are immersed in the chaos, strategy, and bravery of the sailors on both sides, experiencing the heart-pounding moments that shaped the outcome of this historic confrontation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War narrative

Themes: Courage, Strategy, Historical significance, Naval warfare
