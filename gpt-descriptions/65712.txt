Synopsis:
In "The Tower of London," Lord Ronald Sutherland Gower takes readers on a captivating journey through the history and secrets of one of the most iconic landmarks in England. This first volume delves into the Tower's early years, exploring its origins as a royal palace and its transformation into a formidable fortress. Gower's meticulous research and vivid descriptions bring to life the stories of the Tower's famous prisoners, the intrigue surrounding its walls, and the legends that have shrouded it for centuries. From the chilling tales of imprisonment and execution to the opulent ceremonies and lavish banquets, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the Tower's rich and complex past.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Architecture

Themes: Power and Politics, Imprisonment and Punishment, British Monarchy, Architectural History
