Synopsis: 

In 'Myths of Babylonia and Assyria', Donald A. Mackenzie takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient civilizations of Babylonia and Assyria, unearthing the rich tapestry of myths and legends that have shaped these cultures. From the creation of the world to epic battles between gods and mortals, this book delves into the captivating stories that have been passed down through generations.

Discover the fascinating tales of powerful deities like Marduk, Ishtar, and Gilgamesh, as they navigate a world filled with magic, adventure, and divine intervention. Mackenzie's vivid storytelling brings these ancient myths to life, transporting readers to a time when gods walked among men and heroes embarked on extraordinary quests.

Genres: Mythology, History, Folklore

Themes: Creation, Heroism, Divine Intervention, Power of the Gods
