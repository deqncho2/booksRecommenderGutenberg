Synopsis: In "Count the Cost: An Address to the People of Connecticut," David Daggett delivers a thought-provoking and impassioned plea to the citizens of Connecticut, addressing various political subjects and a proposed new constitution. With eloquence and conviction, Daggett explores the potential consequences of this constitutional change, urging readers to carefully consider the impact it may have on their lives and the future of their state. As he delves into the intricacies of governance and the balance of power, Daggett's address serves as a call to action, challenging readers to weigh the cost of their decisions and the importance of preserving their rights and liberties.

Genres: Political discourse, Historical analysis

Themes: Constitutional change, Governance, Individual rights, Civic responsibility
