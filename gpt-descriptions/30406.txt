Synopsis: In "Benjamin Franklin: A Picture of the Struggles of Our Infant Nation One Hundred Years Ago," John S. C. Abbott takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of one of America's most influential figures. From Franklin's humble beginnings as a printer's apprentice to his pivotal role in shaping the nation's destiny, this biography delves into the struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices that Franklin endured for the sake of his beloved country. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Abbott paints a vivid picture of the challenges faced by the infant nation, offering readers a deeper understanding of the man who played a crucial role in its formation.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Patriotism, Perseverance, Nation-building
