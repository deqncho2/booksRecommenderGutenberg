Synopsis: 

In 'The History of Parliamentary Taxation in England' by Morgan, Shepard Ashman, embark on a captivating journey through the intricate web of England's tax system. From the early days of feudalism to the modern era, this book unveils the fascinating evolution of parliamentary taxation and its profound impact on the nation's political and economic landscape. Delve into the struggles, conflicts, and triumphs that shaped the course of taxation in England, as the authors skillfully navigate through the labyrinthine corridors of power, revealing the hidden motives and intricate negotiations behind each tax reform. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, this book sheds light on the pivotal role of taxation in shaping the destiny of a nation.

Genres: History, Politics, Economics

Themes: Power and Politics, Economic Development, Social Change
