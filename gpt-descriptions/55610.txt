Synopsis: In 'Why Men Fight: A method of abolishing the international duel,' Bertrand Russell delves into the complex and deeply rooted causes of war, exploring the psychological, social, and political factors that perpetuate this destructive human behavior. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Russell challenges conventional wisdom and offers a compelling argument for a new approach to resolving conflicts on a global scale. With his characteristic wit and intellectual rigor, he presents a fresh perspective that will leave readers questioning the very nature of war and its place in our world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Political Science

Themes: War, Conflict Resolution, International Relations, Human Nature
