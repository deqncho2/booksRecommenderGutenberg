Synopsis: In 'Food of the Crow, Corvus brachyrhynchos Brehm, in South-central Kansas' by Dwight R. Platt, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the world of crows in the heartland of America. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Platt explores the fascinating feeding habits and dietary preferences of the intelligent and resourceful Corvus brachyrhynchos. From their foraging techniques to their surprising culinary choices, this book unveils the hidden secrets of these enigmatic birds, offering a unique perspective on their role in the ecosystem of South-central Kansas. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Ornithology

Themes: Animal behavior, Ecology, Biodiversity
