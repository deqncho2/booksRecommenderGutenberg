Synopsis: 
Left to Themselves: Being the Ordeal of Philip and Gerald is a captivating coming-of-age novel that follows the journey of two young boys, Philip and Gerald, as they navigate the challenges of adolescence and self-discovery. Set in the late 19th century, the story unfolds against the backdrop of a strict boarding school, where the boys form a deep bond that transcends societal expectations. As they grapple with their emerging desires and the pressures of conformity, Philip and Gerald find solace in each other's company, embarking on a clandestine adventure that will test the limits of their friendship. With its richly drawn characters and poignant exploration of identity, Left to Themselves is a compelling tale of forbidden love and the pursuit of personal freedom.

Genres: 
Coming-of-age, LGBTQ+, Historical Fiction

Themes: 
Identity, Friendship, Self-discovery, Conformity, Forbidden love
