Synopsis: 

In "Jane Austen and Her Country-house Comedy," W. H. Helm delves into the fascinating world of Jane Austen's novels, exploring the hidden layers of her country-house comedies. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, Helm uncovers the intricate social dynamics, witty dialogues, and subtle humor that make Austen's works timeless classics. From the grand estates to the drawing rooms, readers are transported to the Regency era, where they will discover the secrets and scandals that lie beneath the polished veneer of Austen's characters. 

Genres: Literary Criticism, Biography, Historical Analysis

Themes: Social class, Satire, Romantic relationships, Gender roles
