Synopsis: In 'A Romance of Tompkins Square: 1891', Janvier takes us on a captivating journey through the vibrant streets of New York City in the late 19th century. The story revolves around a young artist named Paul, who finds himself entangled in a web of love, mystery, and intrigue when he encounters a mysterious woman in Tompkins Square Park. As Paul delves deeper into the secrets of this enigmatic woman, he becomes immersed in a world of art, passion, and danger, where nothing is as it seems. With vivid descriptions and a compelling narrative, Janvier paints a vivid picture of a bygone era and explores the complexities of love, identity, and the pursuit of artistic expression.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Mystery

Themes: Love and Desire, Art and Creativity, Identity and Self-Discovery
