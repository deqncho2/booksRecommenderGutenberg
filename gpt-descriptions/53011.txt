Synopsis: In "The Mentor: A little book for the guidance of such men and boys as would appear to advantage in the society of persons of the better sort," Alfred Ayres presents a captivating guidebook that offers invaluable advice for individuals seeking to navigate the complexities of high society. Through a series of anecdotes, Ayres explores the art of conversation, proper etiquette, and the cultivation of personal charm. With wit and wisdom, he imparts essential lessons on how to make a lasting impression and thrive in the company of the elite. This little book serves as a mentor, guiding readers towards social success and providing them with the tools to excel in the world of the upper class.

Genres: Self-help, Etiquette, Social Commentary

Themes: Social success, Etiquette and manners, Personal development
