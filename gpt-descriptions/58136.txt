Synopsis: In 'Conscience & Fanaticism: An Essay on Moral Values', George Henry Lane Fox Pitt-Rivers delves into the complex relationship between conscience and fanaticism, exploring the fine line that separates passionate conviction from dangerous extremism. Through thought-provoking analysis and compelling examples, Pitt-Rivers challenges readers to question their own moral values and consider the consequences of unchecked fanaticism. This captivating essay offers a fresh perspective on the power of conscience and the dangers of fanaticism, leaving readers with a deeper understanding of the complexities of human morality.

Genres: Philosophy, Ethics, Cultural Studies

Themes: Conscience, Fanaticism, Moral Values, Extremism, Human Morality
