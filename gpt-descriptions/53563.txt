Synopsis: 

From the early explorations of Jacques Cartier to the modern multicultural society, "Canada (1535-Present Day)" takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of this vast and diverse nation. This meticulously researched and beautifully written book delves into the struggles and triumphs of the Indigenous peoples, the French and British colonization, the formation of Canada as a nation, and its ongoing evolution into the present day. Through vivid storytelling and compelling anecdotes, the book explores the key events, influential figures, and defining moments that have shaped Canada's identity and contributed to its unique cultural mosaic. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Cultural studies

Themes: Identity, Colonialism, Multiculturalism, Nation-building
