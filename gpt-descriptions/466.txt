Synopsis: 
In 'The Gaming Table: Its Votaries and Victims', Andrew Steinmetz delves into the captivating world of gambling, exploring the lives of both those who are drawn to the gaming table and those who fall victim to its allure. Through a series of intriguing anecdotes and personal accounts, Steinmetz paints a vivid picture of the highs and lows experienced by gamblers from all walks of life. From the opulent casinos of Europe to the underground gambling dens of America, this volume takes readers on a thrilling journey through the fascinating history and psychology of gambling.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, History, Psychology

Themes: 
Addiction, Risk-taking, Social dynamics
