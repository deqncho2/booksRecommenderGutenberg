Synopsis: In "Was General Thomas Slow at Nashville?", Henry V. Boynton delves into the intriguing question of General George H. Thomas's speed and strategy during the Battle of Nashville. With meticulous research and analysis, Boynton explores the events leading up to the battle and provides a detailed account of the greatest cavalry movement of the war, led by General James H. Wilson. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be captivated by the tactical decisions, military maneuvers, and the ultimate outcome of this pivotal moment in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Leadership, Strategy, Military tactics, Historical analysis
