Synopsis: 

"The Theory of Moral Sentiments" by Adam Smith is a thought-provoking exploration of human nature and the principles by which individuals judge the conduct and character of others, as well as themselves. Smith delves into the intricacies of moral philosophy, examining the origins of our moral sentiments and how they shape our interactions with those around us. In addition, he includes a dissertation on the origin of languages, offering further insights into the development of human society and communication. 

Genres: Philosophy, Ethics, Social Sciences

Themes: Moral philosophy, Human nature, Judgment, Empathy, Language and communication
