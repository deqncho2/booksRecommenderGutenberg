Synopsis: In 'History of the World War, Vol. 3', Beamish and March delve into the tumultuous events that unfolded during the final years of World War I. From the gripping battles on the Western Front to the political maneuverings behind the scenes, this volume offers a comprehensive account of the war's climax. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the authors bring to life the struggles, sacrifices, and triumphs of both soldiers and civilians during this pivotal period in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War literature

Themes: War and its impact on society, Leadership and strategy, Human resilience and sacrifice
