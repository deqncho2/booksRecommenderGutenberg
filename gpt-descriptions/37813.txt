Synopsis: 
In "Martyria; or, Andersonville Prison" by Augustus C. Hamlin, the reader is transported to the harrowing reality of Andersonville Prison during the American Civil War. Through the eyes of a young Union soldier, we witness the unimaginable horrors and struggles faced by the prisoners of war. As the protagonist navigates the brutal conditions and forms unexpected alliances, the story delves into the depths of human resilience and the enduring power of hope. With each turn of the page, the reader is compelled to confront the true cost of war and the indomitable spirit of those who refuse to surrender.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Survival Fiction

Themes: Resilience, Human Spirit, Survival, Friendship, Sacrifice
