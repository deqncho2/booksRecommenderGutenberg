Synopsis: In 'The White Terror and The Red: A Novel of Revolutionary Russia', Abraham Cahan takes us on a gripping journey through the tumultuous years of the Russian Revolution. Set against the backdrop of political upheaval and social unrest, the story follows the lives of two individuals from vastly different backgrounds. As they navigate the treacherous waters of revolution, their paths intertwine, leading to unexpected alliances and heart-wrenching choices. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, Cahan paints a vivid picture of a nation in turmoil, where loyalty and betrayal are constantly at odds. This captivating novel explores the complexities of human nature and the enduring power of hope in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Drama, Literary Fiction

Themes: Revolution, Loyalty, Betrayal, Social Justice, Hope
