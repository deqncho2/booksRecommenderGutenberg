Synopsis: In 'The Devil in Britain and America', John Ashton delves into the fascinating history and cultural impact of the devil in both Britain and America. From ancient folklore to modern-day interpretations, this captivating exploration uncovers the various manifestations of the devil and the enduring fascination he holds in the collective imagination. Ashton's meticulous research and engaging storytelling shed light on the devil's role in religion, literature, art, and popular culture, revealing the complex and ever-evolving relationship between humanity and the embodiment of evil.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Religion, Folklore, Mythology, Good vs. Evil, Popular Culture
