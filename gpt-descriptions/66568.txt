Synopsis: In 'Jane--Our Stranger: A Novel' by Borden, Mary, a mysterious woman named Jane arrives in a small town, capturing the attention and curiosity of its inhabitants. As Jane's presence becomes more intertwined with the lives of the townspeople, secrets are unearthed, relationships are tested, and the true nature of Jane's identity is slowly revealed. With each revelation, the town's perception of Jane shifts, leaving them questioning their own assumptions and beliefs. This captivating novel explores the complexities of human connection, the power of perception, and the transformative nature of acceptance.

Genres: Mystery, Drama, Psychological Fiction

Themes: Identity, Perception, Secrets, Community
