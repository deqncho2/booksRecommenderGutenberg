Synopsis: 
In 'My Cave Life in Vicksburg, with Letters of Trial and Travel', Mary Ann Webster Loughborough takes us on a captivating journey through her experiences during the Civil War. As the city of Vicksburg becomes besieged, Mary Ann finds herself forced to seek refuge in a cave, where she must confront the harsh realities of war and the resilience of the human spirit. Through her poignant letters, she shares the struggles, fears, and moments of hope that she and her fellow cave dwellers endure, painting a vivid picture of life in the midst of chaos and uncertainty.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: Survival, Resilience, Human Spirit, War, Civilian Experience
