Synopsis: In "Moral Poison in Modern Fiction," R. Brimley Johnson delves into the dark underbelly of contemporary literature, exposing the hidden dangers that lie within its pages. With a keen eye for detail and a sharp wit, Johnson explores the moral decay and corrupting influences that permeate modern fiction, leaving readers questioning the true impact of the stories they consume. Through a series of thought-provoking analyses, he uncovers the subtle ways in which these poisonous narratives can seep into our lives, challenging our values and beliefs. As Johnson navigates the treacherous terrain of modern fiction, he invites readers on a journey of self-reflection and critical examination, urging them to question the moral compass of the stories they encounter.

Genres: Literary criticism, cultural analysis

Themes: Morality, corruption, influence of literature
