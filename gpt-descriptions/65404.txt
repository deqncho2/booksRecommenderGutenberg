Synopsis: 
'Of the Buildings of Justinian' by Procopius takes readers on a captivating journey through the architectural wonders of the Byzantine Empire. From grand palaces and awe-inspiring churches to magnificent bridges and impressive fortifications, Procopius unveils the ambitious construction projects commissioned by Emperor Justinian. As the narrative unfolds, readers are transported to a world of architectural marvels, where the author's vivid descriptions and meticulous attention to detail bring these structures to life. Amidst the splendor, however, lies a deeper exploration of power, legacy, and the enduring impact of human ingenuity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Architecture, Travelogue

Themes: Power and authority, Legacy and posterity, Human ingenuity and innovation
