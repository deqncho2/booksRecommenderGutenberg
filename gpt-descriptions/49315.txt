Synopsis: 
'Ourika' by Claire de Durfort, duchesse de Duras is a captivating novella that takes place in 19th century France. The story revolves around a young African girl named Ourika, who was rescued from slavery and raised in a French aristocratic household. As Ourika grows older, she becomes increasingly aware of her racial and social differences, leading her to question her place in society and her own identity. Through her introspective journey, the novella explores themes of love, identity, and the complexities of race and class in a deeply divided society.

Genres: 
Historical fiction, Coming-of-age, Psychological fiction

Themes: 
Identity, Social inequality, Love and longing
