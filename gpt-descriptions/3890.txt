Synopsis: 
In the seventh volume of "Memoirs of the Court of Marie Antoinette," Madam Campan, the First Lady in Waiting to the Queen, continues her captivating account of life within the opulent and tumultuous court of Versailles. As the French Revolution looms on the horizon, Campan provides a unique insider's perspective on the Queen's struggles, triumphs, and the intricate web of courtly politics that surrounded her. From the lavish balls and extravagant fashion to the growing unrest among the common people, this volume offers a riveting glimpse into the final years of Marie Antoinette's reign and the dramatic events that would ultimately shape the course of history.

Genres: Historical memoir, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Court life, French Revolution, Political intrigue, Royal history
