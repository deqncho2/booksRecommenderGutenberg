Synopsis: In "My Disillusionment in Russia," Emma Goldman takes readers on a captivating journey through her personal experiences in post-revolutionary Russia. As an anarchist and activist, Goldman initially embraced the ideals of the Russian Revolution, hoping for a society free from oppression and inequality. However, her firsthand encounters with the realities of the Bolshevik regime shattered her illusions and forced her to confront the harsh truth behind the revolution's promises. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, Goldman offers a unique perspective on the complexities and contradictions of the Russian Revolution, ultimately leading readers to question the nature of power and the pursuit of social change.

Genres: Autobiography, Political Non-fiction

Themes: Revolution, Idealism, Disillusionment, Power, Social Change
