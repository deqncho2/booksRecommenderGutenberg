Synopsis: In 'The Fruits of Victory: A Sequel to The Great Illusion', Angell, Norman takes readers on a thought-provoking journey that explores the aftermath of war and the true costs of victory. Set in a post-war world, the story follows a diverse cast of characters as they navigate the complex web of political intrigue, personal ambitions, and the consequences of their actions. As the characters grapple with the harsh realities of a world forever changed by conflict, they are forced to confront their own beliefs and question the very nature of victory itself. With unexpected twists and a captivating narrative, this sequel delves deep into the human psyche, challenging readers to reconsider their preconceived notions about war and its aftermath.

Genres: Political fiction, Historical fiction, Thriller

Themes: War and its consequences, Political power and manipulation, Personal growth and self-discovery
