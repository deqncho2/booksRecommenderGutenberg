Synopsis: 

In 'A War-time Journal, Germany 1914 and German Travel Notes', Lady Harriet Julia Campbell Jephson takes us on a captivating journey through her personal experiences during the tumultuous times of World War I. Through her vivid and intimate journal entries, she provides a unique perspective on the war, offering glimpses into the lives of both soldiers and civilians. As she travels through Germany, Lady Jephson encounters a wide range of individuals, each with their own stories and struggles. From the bustling streets of Berlin to the tranquil countryside, she paints a vivid picture of a nation torn apart by conflict. With her keen observations and heartfelt reflections, Lady Jephson's journal becomes a powerful testament to the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Travelogue

Themes: War and its impact on society, Personal resilience, Cultural exploration
