Synopsis: In 'The Corporation of London, Its Rights and Privileges' by William Ferneley Allen, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the fascinating history and inner workings of the renowned Corporation of London. From its ancient origins to its modern-day influence, this book unveils the secrets and power struggles that have shaped one of the world's oldest and most influential institutions. As the author delves into the intricate web of politics, economics, and tradition, readers will be enthralled by the tales of intrigue, power, and ambition that have defined the Corporation of London for centuries.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Politics

Themes: Power and influence, Tradition and heritage, Politics and governance
