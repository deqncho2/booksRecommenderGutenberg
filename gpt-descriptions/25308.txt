Synopsis: In "Eugenics and Other Evils," G. K. Chesterton takes a thought-provoking and satirical look at the dangerous ideology of eugenics that was gaining popularity in the early 20th century. Through his witty and insightful analysis, Chesterton exposes the flaws and dangers of this misguided movement, challenging the reader to question the very foundations of society's pursuit of perfection.

Genres: Non-fiction, Satire, Social Commentary

Themes: Eugenics, Social Darwinism, Individuality, Human Dignity
