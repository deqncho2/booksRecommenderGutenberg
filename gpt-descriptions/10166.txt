Synopsis: 

In 'What Germany Thinks; Or, The War as Germans see it', Thomas F. A. Smith takes readers on a captivating journey into the heart and mind of Germany during the tumultuous times of war. Through a series of firsthand accounts, interviews, and observations, Smith unveils a world that is both fascinating and deeply complex. From the trenches to the halls of power, he delves into the thoughts, motivations, and aspirations of the German people, shedding light on their perspectives and shedding new light on the war itself. As the narrative unfolds, readers will find themselves engrossed in the intricate web of alliances, ideologies, and personal stories that shaped Germany's role in the conflict. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War literature, Political analysis

Themes: National identity, Propaganda, War psychology, Cultural perspectives
