Synopsis: In "The Lives of the Twelve Caesars, Volume 04: Caligula," Suetonius delves into the life of one of Rome's most infamous emperors. From his early days as a beloved ruler to his descent into madness and tyranny, this gripping account explores the shocking and scandalous actions of Caligula. With tales of extravagant excess, political intrigue, and a reign marked by cruelty and debauchery, Suetonius paints a vivid portrait of a man whose lust for power knew no bounds.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and corruption, Madness and tyranny, Political intrigue
