Synopsis: 

'Catalogue of Violent and Destructive Earthquakes in the Philippines: With an Appendix: Earthquakes in the Marianas Islands 1599-1909' by Saderra Masó, Miguel is a meticulously researched and comprehensive account of the history of earthquakes in the Philippines and the Marianas Islands. 

This captivating book takes readers on a journey through centuries of seismic activity, documenting the devastating earthquakes that have shaped the region. From the earliest recorded earthquake in 1599 to the end of the 19th century, Masó delves into the causes, effects, and aftermaths of these cataclysmic events. The author's attention to detail and inclusion of firsthand accounts and scientific analysis make this work a fascinating exploration of the destructive power of nature.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Natural Disasters

Themes: Natural disasters, Geology, Philippine history, Human resilience
