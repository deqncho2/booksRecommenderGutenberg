Synopsis: In "The Subjection of Women," John Stuart Mill challenges the prevailing societal norms and argues for the emancipation of women. Through a thought-provoking exploration of gender inequality, Mill delves into the various ways in which women are subjugated and advocates for their equal rights and opportunities. With compelling arguments and a call for societal reform, this groundbreaking work challenges readers to question the status quo and envision a more equitable future.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Feminism

Themes: Gender inequality, Women's rights, Social reform
