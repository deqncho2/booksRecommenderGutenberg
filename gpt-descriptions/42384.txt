Synopsis: 

In "Scandinavians on the Pacific, Puget Sound" by Stine, Thomas Ostenson, embark on a captivating journey through the untold stories of Scandinavian immigrants who settled in the Pacific Northwest. Through a collection of vivid narratives and historical accounts, the book unveils the struggles, triumphs, and cultural contributions of these resilient individuals who left their homeland to forge a new life in a foreign land. From tales of perilous sea voyages to tales of resilience and determination, this book paints a rich tapestry of the Scandinavian immigrant experience in the Puget Sound region.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural studies, Immigration literature

Themes: Immigration, Cultural identity, Resilience, Community, Heritage
