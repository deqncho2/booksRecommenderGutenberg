Synopsis: In 'The Problems of Philosophy', Bertrand Russell takes readers on a captivating journey through the perplexing world of philosophy. With his signature clarity and wit, Russell explores fundamental questions about the nature of reality, knowledge, and existence. From the existence of God to the limits of human perception, he delves into the most profound puzzles that have puzzled thinkers for centuries. Through engaging examples and thought experiments, Russell challenges readers to question their own assumptions and invites them to embark on a quest for truth and understanding. 

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Reality, Knowledge, Existence, Perception, Truth
