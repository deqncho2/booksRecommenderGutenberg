Synopsis: 

"The Levellers: A Dialogue Between Two Young Ladies, Concerning Matrimony, Proposing an Act for Enforcing Marriage, for the Equality of Matches, and Taxing Single Persons" is a thought-provoking and engaging work that delves into the complexities of marriage and societal expectations. 

The story revolves around two young ladies who engage in a passionate dialogue about the institution of marriage and its impact on their lives. As they explore the challenges and limitations imposed by societal norms, they propose a radical act that aims to enforce marriage, ensure equality in matches, and even tax those who remain single. 

Through their discussions, the young ladies challenge traditional notions of love, gender roles, and the power dynamics within relationships. They delve into the consequences of societal pressure to marry, the importance of personal choice, and the potential benefits and drawbacks of their proposed act. 

Genres: Satire, Social Commentary, Feminist Literature
Themes: Marriage, Gender Roles, Societal Expectations, Personal Choice, Equality
