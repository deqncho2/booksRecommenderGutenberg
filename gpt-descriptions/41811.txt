Synopsis: In "Life of Thomas à Becket," Henry Hart Milman delves into the captivating story of a man who rose from humble beginnings to become one of the most influential figures in medieval England. This gripping biography explores the complex relationship between Thomas à Becket and King Henry II, as well as the profound impact of their clash over the power of the Church. As Becket navigates the treacherous waters of politics, religion, and personal loyalty, readers are taken on a thrilling journey through the corridors of power and the depths of human ambition.

Genres: Biography, History, Drama

Themes: Power and Politics, Religion and Faith, Loyalty and Betrayal
