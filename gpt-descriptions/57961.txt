Synopsis: In 'Mexican Copper Tools: The Use of Copper by the Mexicans Before the Conquest; and the Katunes of Maya History, a Chapter in the Early History of Central America, With Special Reference to the Pio Perez Manuscript,' Valentini delves into the fascinating world of pre-Columbian Mexico and Central America. Exploring the use of copper by the ancient Mexicans and the enigmatic Katunes of Maya history, this book uncovers hidden knowledge and sheds light on the rich cultural heritage of these civilizations. Through meticulous research and analysis of the Pio Perez Manuscript, Valentini presents a captivating narrative that will leave readers intrigued and eager to learn more about these ancient civilizations.

Genres: Historical research, Archaeology, Anthropology

Themes: Pre-Columbian civilizations, Ancient technology, Cultural heritage
