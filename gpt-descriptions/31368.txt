Synopsis: 

In 'Glimpses of the Past: History of the River St. John, A.D. 1604-1784', author William Odber Raymond takes readers on a captivating journey through time, uncovering the rich and often overlooked history of the River St. John. From the early European explorers who first set foot on its shores to the dramatic events that shaped the region during the 17th and 18th centuries, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the past.

Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Raymond brings to life the struggles and triumphs of the people who inhabited the River St. John area during this pivotal period. From the clashes between European settlers and Indigenous peoples to the impact of the American Revolution on the region, the book delves into the complex interactions and events that shaped the history of the River St. John.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Canadian history, Exploration and colonization

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Cultural encounters, Conflict and cooperation, Impact of historical events, Indigenous history
