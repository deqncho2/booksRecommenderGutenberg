Synopsis: In "Ten Girls from Dickens" by Kate Dickinson Sweetser, readers are introduced to ten captivating female characters from the works of Charles Dickens. From the resilient and resourceful Nancy in "Oliver Twist" to the spirited and independent Esther in "Bleak House," each girl's story unfolds with a blend of heartache, determination, and triumph. Through their trials and tribulations, these girls navigate the harsh realities of Victorian society, facing poverty, injustice, and societal expectations. As their lives intertwine with the iconic tales of Dickens, readers are transported to a world of vivid characters and rich storytelling, where the strength and resilience of these girls shine through.

Genres: Fiction, Literary Criticism, Biography

Themes: Resilience, Gender Roles, Social Injustice
