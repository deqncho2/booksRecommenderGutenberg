Synopsis: 
'Letters of Two Brides' by Honoré de Balzac is an epistolary novel that follows the correspondence between two childhood friends, Louise and Renée, who find themselves married to two very different men. Through their letters, they reveal their deepest thoughts, desires, and secrets, exploring the complexities of love, marriage, and the pursuit of happiness. As their lives unfold, the letters become a captivating window into the inner workings of their hearts and minds, creating a compelling narrative that keeps the reader eagerly turning the pages.

Genres: 
Epistolary fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: 
Love and marriage, Friendship, Identity, Society's expectations
