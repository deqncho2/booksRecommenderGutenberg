Synopsis: 
In 'A Catechism of the Steam Engine', John Bourne, a renowned civil engineer, takes readers on a captivating journey through the inner workings of the steam engine. Through a series of thought-provoking questions and detailed explanations, Bourne unravels the mysteries behind this revolutionary invention, shedding light on its immense power and potential. From the principles of combustion to the intricacies of steam pressure, readers will be enthralled by the fascinating world of steam technology. As Bourne delves deeper into the subject, he explores the various applications of steam engines, from transportation to manufacturing, and the profound impact they have had on society. With its engaging style and wealth of knowledge, this book is a must-read for anyone curious about the inner workings of one of the most influential inventions in history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, Technology

Themes: Industrial Revolution, Scientific Inquiry, Technological Advancement
