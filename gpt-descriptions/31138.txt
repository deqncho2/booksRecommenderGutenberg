Synopsis: In "The So-called Human Race," Taylor, Bert Leston takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the complexities of human nature and society. Through a series of witty and insightful observations, the author challenges conventional wisdom and sheds light on the absurdities of our everyday lives. From the quirks of human behavior to the follies of societal norms, this collection of essays offers a humorous and critical examination of the so-called human race.

Genres: Satire, Humor, Social Commentary

Themes: Human nature, Society, Absurdity, Critique
