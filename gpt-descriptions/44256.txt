Synopsis: In 'Brown of Moukden: A Story of the Russo-Japanese War', Strang Herbert takes readers on a thrilling journey through the tumultuous events of the Russo-Japanese War. Set in the city of Moukden, the story follows the adventures of a young British journalist named Brown, who finds himself caught in the midst of the conflict between two powerful nations. As Brown navigates the treacherous landscape of war, he encounters danger, espionage, and unexpected alliances, all while striving to uncover the truth behind the war's hidden motives. With vivid descriptions and heart-pounding action, 'Brown of Moukden' offers a gripping tale of courage, loyalty, and the pursuit of justice.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Adventure

Themes: War and its consequences, Loyalty and betrayal, Truth and deception
