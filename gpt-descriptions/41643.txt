Synopsis: In 'The Theory and Practice of Archery', authors Butt and Ford delve into the captivating world of archery, exploring its rich history, techniques, and philosophy. Through a combination of historical anecdotes, practical advice, and philosophical musings, they take readers on a journey that goes beyond the mere physical act of shooting arrows. From the ancient origins of archery to its modern-day applications, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of the art and science of archery, captivating readers with its fascinating insights and inspiring anecdotes.

Genres: Non-fiction, Sports, History

Themes: Mastery, Tradition, Philosophy
