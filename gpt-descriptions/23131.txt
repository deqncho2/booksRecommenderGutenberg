Synopsis: In 'Principle and Practice: The Orphan Family' by Harriet Martineau, readers are introduced to the lives of the Linwood family, who find themselves facing unexpected challenges and navigating the complexities of society. As the family's fortunes change, they must confront their own principles and adapt to new circumstances. With a cast of intriguing characters and a plot filled with twists and turns, this captivating novel explores the themes of morality, social class, and the power of resilience.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Family Drama, Social Commentary

Themes: Morality, Social Class, Resilience
