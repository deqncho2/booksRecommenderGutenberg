Synopsis: 

'Common Trees of Pennsylvania' is an enlightening and visually captivating guide that takes readers on a journey through the diverse and enchanting world of Pennsylvania's trees. Aughenbaugh, Ibberson, Mickalitis, and Morris expertly combine their knowledge and passion to provide a comprehensive exploration of the state's most prominent tree species. From the towering giants of the forests to the delicate blossoms of the woodlands, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the natural beauty that surrounds us. With stunning photographs and detailed descriptions, readers will discover the unique characteristics, habitats, and uses of each tree, making it an indispensable companion for nature enthusiasts, hikers, and anyone curious about the remarkable trees that grace Pennsylvania's landscapes.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Field Guide

Themes: Biodiversity, Environmental Conservation, Appreciation of Nature
