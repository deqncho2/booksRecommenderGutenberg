Synopsis: 
In 'Daisy' by Susan Warner, a young woman named Daisy finds herself caught between societal expectations and her own desires. As she navigates the complexities of love, family, and personal growth, Daisy must make difficult choices that will shape her future. Set against the backdrop of the 19th century, this captivating novel explores the challenges faced by women in a time of limited opportunities and the power of self-discovery.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Gender roles, Self-determination, Love and sacrifice
