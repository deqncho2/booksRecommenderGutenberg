Synopsis: 

In the "Report of the Chief Librarian for the Year Ended 31 March 1958: Special Centennial Issue," the General Assembly Library of New Zealand presents a captivating account of its activities during a significant year. This special centennial edition offers a unique glimpse into the library's role in preserving the nation's history and culture, as well as its efforts to adapt to the changing times. Through a series of engaging narratives and insightful analyses, the report highlights the challenges faced by the library and the innovative solutions implemented to meet the evolving needs of its patrons. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Cultural

Themes: Preservation of history, Adaptation to change, Cultural heritage
