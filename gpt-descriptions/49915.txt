Synopsis: In 'By Far Euphrates: A Tale' by Deborah Alcock, a young woman named Edith finds herself caught in the midst of a dangerous conflict during the Armenian Genocide. As she navigates the treacherous landscape of war-torn Turkey, Edith's courage and resilience are put to the ultimate test. With unexpected alliances and heart-wrenching choices, this gripping tale takes readers on a journey of love, loss, and the indomitable human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Survival, Sacrifice, Identity, Resilience
