Synopsis: In 'A Ten Years' War: An Account of the Battle with the Slum in New York', Jacob A. Riis takes readers on a gripping journey through the dark underbelly of New York City's slums. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, Riis exposes the harsh realities faced by the impoverished residents and the tireless efforts of those fighting to bring about change. This eye-opening narrative sheds light on the struggles, triumphs, and complexities of a decade-long battle against the slum, leaving readers both captivated and inspired.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Urbanization, Activism
