Synopsis: In "Main Currents in Nineteenth Century Literature - 5. The Romantic School in France," Georg Brandes takes us on a captivating journey through the vibrant and tumultuous world of French Romantic literature. From the passionate works of Victor Hugo to the introspective poetry of Charles Baudelaire, Brandes delves into the lives and writings of these influential figures, exploring the profound impact they had on the literary landscape of the time. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, he uncovers the essence of Romanticism in France, revealing the struggles, triumphs, and revolutionary spirit that defined this remarkable era.

Genres: Literary criticism, History, Biography

Themes: Romanticism, Individualism, Revolution, Artistic expression
