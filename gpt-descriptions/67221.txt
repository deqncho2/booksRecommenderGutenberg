Synopsis: In "Pueblo Pottery Making: A Study at the Village of San Ildefonso," Carl E. Guthe takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of traditional pottery making in the Pueblo village of San Ildefonso. Through meticulous research and firsthand observations, Guthe delves into the intricate techniques, cultural significance, and artistic expressions of this ancient craft. As he uncovers the secrets behind the creation of these exquisite pottery pieces, Guthe also explores the deep-rooted traditions, historical context, and the profound connection between the artisans and their craft. This immersive study offers a fascinating glimpse into the rich heritage and artistic mastery of Pueblo pottery making.

Genres: Non-fiction, Anthropology, Art

Themes: Cultural heritage, Traditional craftsmanship, Artistic expression
