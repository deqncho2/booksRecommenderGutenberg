Synopsis: In 'Susanna and Sue' by Kate Douglas Smith Wiggin, two young girls from very different backgrounds find themselves thrown together in a small New England town. Susanna, a spirited and imaginative orphan, is taken in by the kind-hearted Widow O'Neil, while Sue, a wealthy and privileged girl, is sent to live with her strict and demanding Aunt Jane. As their lives intertwine, Susanna and Sue embark on a journey of self-discovery, friendship, and the power of resilience. With unexpected twists and turns, this heartwarming tale explores the bonds that can be formed between unlikely companions and the transformative power of love and acceptance.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Historical

Themes: Friendship, Identity, Social class, Resilience
