Synopsis: 

In 'Indian Frontier Policy; an historical sketch', Sir John Adye takes us on a captivating journey through the complex and often turbulent history of India's frontier regions. From the ancient empires to the British Raj, Adye delves into the strategic decisions, diplomatic negotiations, and military campaigns that shaped the ever-shifting borders of this vast and diverse land. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, he brings to life the fascinating characters and events that have defined India's frontier policy, revealing the intricate balance between power, culture, and survival in this dynamic region.

Genres: History, Military, Politics

Themes: Colonialism, Diplomacy, Warfare, Cultural Identity
