Synopsis: In "On the inaccuracies which probably exist in the census returns of ages," Thomas A. Welton delves into the intriguing world of census data, uncovering potential discrepancies in the reported ages of individuals. Through meticulous analysis and thought-provoking examples, Welton challenges the reliability of this widely accepted information, raising questions about the accuracy of demographic records. As he explores the possible reasons behind these inaccuracies, readers are taken on a captivating journey that sheds light on the complexities of data collection and the implications it has on our understanding of society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, Statistics

Themes: Data accuracy, Demographic records, Societal implications
