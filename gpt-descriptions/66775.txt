Synopsis: In "Bottoms Up: An Application of the Slapstick to Satire," Nathan, George Jean takes readers on a hilarious and thought-provoking journey through the world of slapstick comedy. Through a series of uproarious anecdotes and clever observations, Nathan explores the power of physical humor to expose the absurdities of society and human nature. From vaudeville to silent films, he delves into the history and evolution of slapstick, highlighting its ability to both entertain and challenge societal norms. With wit and insight, Nathan invites readers to reconsider the value and impact of this often-underestimated form of comedy.

Genres: Satire, Comedy, Non-fiction

Themes: Social critique, Humor, Entertainment
