Synopsis: In this thought-provoking sermon, Andrew Snape delivers a powerful message to the Lord-Mayor, aldermen, and citizens of London on the anniversary fast for the Martyrdom of King Charles. With eloquence and passion, Snape explores the significance of this historical event and its relevance to the present day. Through his words, he challenges his audience to reflect on the virtues of loyalty, sacrifice, and the preservation of liberty. This sermon serves as a reminder of the enduring legacy of King Charles and the lessons that can be learned from his martyrdom.

Genres: Sermon, Historical, Political

Themes: Loyalty, Sacrifice, Liberty, Historical Legacy
