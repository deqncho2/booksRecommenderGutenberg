Synopsis: In 'Mary: A Fiction' by Wollstonecraft, the reader is introduced to the captivating and enigmatic character of Mary, a young woman who defies societal norms and expectations. Through a series of intriguing events and encounters, Mary embarks on a journey of self-discovery, challenging the limitations placed upon women in the 18th century. As the story unfolds, secrets are revealed, relationships are tested, and Mary's resilience and determination shine through. This thought-provoking tale explores themes of gender inequality, personal freedom, and the power of individual agency.

Genres: Fiction, Feminist Literature, Bildungsroman

Themes: Gender inequality, Personal freedom, Individual agency
