Synopsis: In 'Legends of the Bastille' by Funck-Brentano, Frantz, the reader is transported to the tumultuous era of the French Revolution, where the infamous Bastille prison stands as a symbol of oppression and rebellion. Through a collection of captivating stories, the author unveils the hidden secrets, heroic tales, and tragic events that unfolded within the walls of this historic fortress. From the daring escapes of prisoners to the fateful encounters of revolutionaries, each legend offers a glimpse into the complex and often brutal world of the Bastille. As the stories intertwine, the reader is left questioning the true nature of justice, freedom, and the human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Short Stories

Themes: Revolution, Oppression, Freedom, Justice
