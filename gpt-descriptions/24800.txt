Synopsis: 

In 'Darwin, and After Darwin, Volumes 1 and 3: An Exposition of the Darwinian Theory and a Discussion of Post-Darwinian Questions', George John Romanes delves into the groundbreaking theories of Charles Darwin and explores the implications and controversies that arose in the wake of his work. Through a comprehensive analysis, Romanes presents a thought-provoking examination of the Darwinian theory and its impact on various scientific disciplines. From the intricacies of natural selection to the debates surrounding human evolution, this book offers a captivating exploration of the evolutionary ideas that shaped our understanding of the natural world.

Genres: Science, Non-fiction, Evolutionary Biology

Themes: Evolution, Natural Selection, Controversy, Scientific Inquiry
