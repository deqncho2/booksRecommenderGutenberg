Synopsis: In "The Life and Letters of Walter H. Page, Volume I," Burton Jesse Hendrick delves into the captivating life of Walter H. Page, an influential American journalist and diplomat. From his humble beginnings in North Carolina to his rise as a prominent figure in the literary and political circles of the late 19th and early 20th centuries, this biography paints a vivid portrait of a man driven by his passion for social justice and his unwavering commitment to his ideals. Through a collection of Page's personal letters and insightful analysis, Hendrick offers readers a glimpse into the fascinating world of a man who played a pivotal role in shaping American history.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Social justice, Idealism, Diplomacy, Literary circles
