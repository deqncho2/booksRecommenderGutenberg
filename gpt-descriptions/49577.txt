Synopsis: In "Beleaguered in Pekin: The Boxer's War Against the Foreigner" by Coltman, Robert, a gripping tale unfolds amidst the chaos and violence of the Boxer Rebellion in 1900 China. As tensions rise between the Boxers, a secret society seeking to expel foreigners, and the foreign powers occupying Peking, a diverse group of individuals find themselves caught in the crossfire. From a British diplomat's wife to a Chinese scholar, their lives intertwine in unexpected ways as they navigate the treacherous landscape of war, betrayal, and cultural clashes. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this historical novel takes readers on a thrilling journey through a pivotal moment in Chinese history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Cultural Fiction
Themes: Conflict and Violence, Cultural Identity, Betrayal, Resilience
