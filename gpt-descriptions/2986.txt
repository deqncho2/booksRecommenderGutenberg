Synopsis: In "Mark Twain: A Biography. Volume III, Part 1: 1900-1907," Albert Bigelow Paine delves into the later years of the iconic American author, Mark Twain. This captivating biography explores Twain's life during a period of immense personal and professional challenges. From his struggles with financial setbacks and the loss of loved ones to his relentless pursuit of new literary ventures, Twain's journey unfolds against the backdrop of a rapidly changing world. Paine's meticulous research and vivid storytelling bring to life the complexities of Twain's character, revealing the man behind the legendary writer.

Genres: Biography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Personal resilience, Literary legacy, Social change
