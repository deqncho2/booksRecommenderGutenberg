Synopsis: 
'A Fortnight at the Front' takes readers on a captivating journey through the experiences of the Bishop of Birmingham during his two-week visit to the frontlines of World War I. Through vivid and poignant storytelling, the book offers a unique perspective on the realities of war, the resilience of the human spirit, and the power of faith in the face of adversity. As the Bishop encounters soldiers, witnesses the horrors of battle, and grapples with the moral complexities of war, readers are drawn into a compelling narrative that explores the profound impact of conflict on individuals and society as a whole.

Genres: Memoir, Historical, War

Themes: War, Faith, Human resilience, Morality
