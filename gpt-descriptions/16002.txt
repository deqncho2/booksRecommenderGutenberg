Synopsis: In 'Fardorougha, The Miser: The Works of William Carleton, Volume One', we are introduced to Fardorougha Sullivan, a wealthy and notorious miser living in rural Ireland. As the story unfolds, we witness the consequences of Fardorougha's obsession with money and his relentless pursuit of wealth. Amidst the backdrop of a changing society and the struggles of the Irish peasantry, the narrative delves into the complex relationships and moral dilemmas faced by the characters. With unexpected twists and turns, this captivating tale explores the depths of human greed and the power of redemption.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Drama, Satire

Themes: Greed, Wealth, Morality, Redemption
