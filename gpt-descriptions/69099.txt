Synopsis: In 'The Book of Edinburgh Anecdote' by Francis Watt, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the rich history and vibrant culture of Scotland's capital city. Through a collection of intriguing and often humorous anecdotes, Watt brings to life the fascinating characters, events, and landmarks that have shaped Edinburgh's unique identity. From famous writers and inventors to notorious criminals and ghostly encounters, this book offers a delightful exploration of the city's past and present, revealing the hidden stories that lie behind its iconic facade.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Travel

Themes: Scottish history, Cultural heritage, Urban legends
