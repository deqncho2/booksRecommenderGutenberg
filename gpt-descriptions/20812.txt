Synopsis: In 'History of the English People, Volume III: The Parliament, 1399-1461; The Monarchy 1461-1540', John Richard Green takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous periods of English history. From the rise and fall of monarchs to the power struggles within Parliament, this volume delves into the political and social dynamics that shaped England during the late medieval and early Renaissance eras. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Green paints a vivid picture of the challenges faced by the English people during this transformative time.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Power and politics, Monarchy and governance, Social change, Conflict and war
