Synopsis: 
In "The honey-bee: its nature, homes and products," William Hetherington takes readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of honey bees. From their intricate social structure to their remarkable ability to produce honey, Hetherington delves into the secrets of these remarkable creatures. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, he explores the various types of honey bees, their behavior, and the intricate workings of their hives. Along the way, readers will discover the surprising ways in which honey bees contribute to our ecosystem and the diverse range of products they create. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Science

Themes: Nature's wonders, Biodiversity, Environmental conservation
