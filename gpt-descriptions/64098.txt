Synopsis: In 'Human Work' by Charlotte Perkins Gilman, a young woman named Helen embarks on a journey of self-discovery and empowerment in a society that restricts women's roles. As she navigates through various challenges and societal expectations, Helen's determination to find her true purpose and contribute meaningfully to the world becomes a driving force. With unexpected twists and thought-provoking insights, 'Human Work' explores the complexities of gender roles, the pursuit of personal fulfillment, and the power of individual agency.

Genres: Feminist literature, social commentary, coming-of-age

Themes: Gender inequality, self-discovery, societal expectations, empowerment
