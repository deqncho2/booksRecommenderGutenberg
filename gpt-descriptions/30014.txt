Synopsis: 

In 'Native Son' by T. D. Hamm, we are introduced to Bigger Thomas, a young African American man living in 1930s Chicago. Bigger's life takes a dramatic turn when he is offered a job as a chauffeur for a wealthy white family. As he navigates the complexities of racial tension and class divide, Bigger finds himself caught in a web of fear, violence, and the consequences of his actions. This gripping novel explores the psychological and social struggles faced by Bigger, shedding light on the harsh realities of racism and the human condition.

Genres: Fiction, Social Commentary, Psychological Thriller

Themes: Racism, Identity, Fear, Violence, Class Divide
