Synopsis:
In 'Manners of the Age' by H. B. Fyfe, a young man named David finds himself thrust into a world where social norms and customs have been turned upside down. As he navigates this unfamiliar society, he must grapple with the consequences of his actions and the true meaning of personal freedom. With unexpected twists and thought-provoking dilemmas, this captivating novel explores the complexities of human behavior and the power of societal expectations.

Genres:
Science Fiction, Dystopian Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes:
Social conformity, Individuality, Morality, Personal freedom
