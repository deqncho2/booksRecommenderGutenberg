Synopsis: 

'Charles Frohman: Manager and Man' is a captivating biography that delves into the life and career of one of the most influential figures in the world of theater. Written by Isaac Frederick Marcosson and Daniel Frohman, the book takes readers on a journey through the fascinating and often tumultuous life of Charles Frohman, a renowned theater manager and producer.

From his humble beginnings to his rise as a prominent figure in the theater industry, the biography explores Frohman's relentless pursuit of excellence and his unwavering dedication to the art of storytelling. Readers will be enthralled by the behind-the-scenes anecdotes and the intricate web of relationships that shaped Frohman's career, including his collaborations with legendary playwrights and actors.

As the narrative unfolds, the biography also delves into the personal life of Charles Frohman, shedding light on his complex personality and the challenges he faced in balancing his professional success with his personal relationships. Through triumphs and tragedies, the book paints a vivid portrait of a man who left an indelible mark on the world of theater.

Genres: Biography, Theater, History

Themes: Ambition, Artistic Vision, Personal Sacrifice, Legacy
