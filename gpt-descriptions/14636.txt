Synopsis: In "Tragic Sense of Life," Miguel de Unamuno takes readers on a profound exploration of the human condition, delving into the depths of existential questions and the struggle to find meaning in a seemingly absurd world. Through a series of philosophical reflections and personal anecdotes, Unamuno invites readers to confront their own mortality, grapple with the tension between reason and faith, and confront the eternal conflict between the desire for immortality and the inevitability of death. This thought-provoking work challenges readers to confront the fundamental questions of existence and offers a unique perspective on the complexities of the human experience.

Genres: Philosophy, Existentialism, Autobiography

Themes: Existential angst, Mortality, Faith and reason, Immortality, Human nature
