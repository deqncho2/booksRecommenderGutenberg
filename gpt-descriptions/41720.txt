Synopsis: 
'Prisons and Prayer; Or, a Labor of Love' by Elizabeth Ryder Wheaton is a captivating novel that follows the journey of a young woman named Emily, who finds herself drawn into the world of prison ministry. As she navigates the challenges and complexities of working with inmates, Emily's own faith and beliefs are put to the test. Along the way, she forms unexpected connections and discovers the transformative power of compassion and forgiveness. This thought-provoking story delves into the depths of human nature and the redemptive possibilities that lie within even the darkest of circumstances.

Genres: Fiction, Christian Literature, Inspirational

Themes: Redemption, Faith, Compassion, Forgiveness
