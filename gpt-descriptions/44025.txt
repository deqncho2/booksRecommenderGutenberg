Synopsis: 

In "August Strindberg, the Spirit of Revolt: Studies and Impressions," Lizzy Lind-af-Hageby delves into the life and mind of the renowned Swedish playwright and author, August Strindberg. Through a series of captivating studies and personal impressions, Lind-af-Hageby explores the complexities of Strindberg's revolutionary spirit and the profound impact it had on his work. From his early struggles and controversial plays to his later philosophical musings, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the inner workings of a literary genius who challenged societal norms and pushed the boundaries of art.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Cultural Studies

Themes: Revolution, Artistic Expression, Social Critique
