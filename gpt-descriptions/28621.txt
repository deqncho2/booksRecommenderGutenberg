Synopsis:
In "The Metamorphoses of Publius Ovidus Naso in English blank verse Vols. I & II," Ovid takes us on a captivating journey through the world of Greek and Roman mythology. This epic poem weaves together countless tales of gods, heroes, and mortals, each one more enchanting than the last. From the creation of the universe to the fall of Troy, from the tragic love stories of Pyramus and Thisbe to the daring adventures of Perseus, Ovid's masterful storytelling transports us to a realm where gods meddle in the affairs of humans and transformations abound. With vivid imagery and lyrical language, Ovid explores the themes of love, desire, power, and the consequences of hubris, leaving readers spellbound by the timeless tales of ancient mythology.

Genres: Epic poetry, Mythology, Classical literature
Themes: Love, Desire, Power, Hubris, Transformation
