Synopsis: 

In "Kentucky in American Letters, 1784-1912," John Wilson Townsend takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich literary history of Kentucky. This second volume of the two-part series delves into the lives and works of renowned Kentucky authors, shedding light on their unique perspectives and contributions to American literature. From the early pioneers to the emerging voices of the 20th century, Townsend's meticulous research and engaging storytelling bring to life the vibrant literary landscape of the Bluegrass State. 

Genres: Literary criticism, historical non-fiction, biography

Themes: Kentucky literature, regional identity, American literary history
