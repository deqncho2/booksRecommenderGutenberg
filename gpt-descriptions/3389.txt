Synopsis: In 'Literature and Life (Complete)' by William Dean Howells, a young aspiring writer named Basil March embarks on a journey of self-discovery and artistic growth. Set in the vibrant literary scene of late 19th-century Boston, Basil encounters a diverse cast of characters, including established authors, struggling artists, and influential critics. As he navigates the complexities of love, friendship, and ambition, Basil grapples with the age-old question of how literature reflects and shapes our lives. Through thought-provoking discussions and unexpected twists, Howells invites readers to ponder the intricate relationship between art and reality, and the profound impact literature can have on our understanding of the world.

Genres: Fiction, Literary Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Artistic growth, Self-discovery, Love and relationships, Ambition, The power of literature
