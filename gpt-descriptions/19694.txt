Synopsis: 
In "A Smaller History of Rome: from the Earliest Times to the Establishment of the Empire," authors Smith and Lawrence take readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of Rome. From its humble beginnings as a small village to its rise as a powerful empire, this book offers a concise yet engaging account of the key events, influential figures, and remarkable achievements that shaped Rome's destiny. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the authors bring to life the triumphs and struggles of the Roman people, providing a captivating glimpse into one of the most influential civilizations in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Ancient History

Themes: Rise and fall of empires, Power and politics, Cultural and societal transformation, Leadership and governance
