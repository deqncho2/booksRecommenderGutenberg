Synopsis: In Hume: (English Men of Letters Series), Thomas Henry Huxley delves into the life and ideas of the renowned philosopher David Hume. Through a captivating exploration of Hume's intellectual journey, Huxley unveils the fascinating mind behind one of the most influential figures of the Enlightenment era. From Hume's groundbreaking theories on human nature and skepticism to his controversial views on religion and morality, this biography offers a thought-provoking glimpse into the life of a philosopher who challenged the conventional wisdom of his time.

Genres: Biography, Philosophy

Themes: Enlightenment, Skepticism, Human Nature, Religion, Morality
