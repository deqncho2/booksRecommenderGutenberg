Synopsis: 
"The Battle of Atlanta: and Other Campaigns, Addresses, Etc." by Grenville Mellen Dodge is a captivating collection of firsthand accounts and speeches that provide a vivid and immersive experience of the American Civil War. From the intense Battle of Atlanta to other significant campaigns, Dodge's narrative transports readers to the heart of the conflict, offering a unique perspective on the strategies, challenges, and sacrifices of the soldiers involved. With gripping storytelling and insightful analysis, this book sheds light on the pivotal moments that shaped the outcome of the war, leaving readers eager to delve deeper into the history and heroism of this tumultuous period.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: War and conflict, Leadership, Strategy and tactics, Sacrifice, Historical significance
