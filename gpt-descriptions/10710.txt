Synopsis: 

In 'Problems of Poverty: An Inquiry into the Industrial Condition of the Poor', J.A. Hobson delves into the harsh realities faced by the impoverished in society. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Hobson uncovers the underlying causes and consequences of poverty, shedding light on the intricate web of economic and social factors that perpetuate this pressing issue. With a compassionate and thought-provoking approach, the author challenges conventional wisdom and offers innovative solutions to alleviate the plight of the poor. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, Economics

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Economic justice, Social reform
