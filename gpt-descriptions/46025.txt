Synopsis: 

'Charities and the Commons: The Pittsburgh Survey, Part II. The Place and Its Social Forces' takes readers on a captivating journey through the city of Pittsburgh, exploring its unique social dynamics and the impact of various forces on its communities. This in-depth study delves into the intricate relationship between charities and the common people, shedding light on the challenges faced by both and the potential for positive change. Through vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, the book uncovers the hidden stories and struggles of Pittsburgh's residents, offering a thought-provoking exploration of the city's social fabric.

Genres: Non-fiction, Sociology, Urban Studies

Themes: Social inequality, Community development, Philanthropy, Urban poverty
