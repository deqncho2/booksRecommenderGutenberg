Synopsis: 

'Cotton is King, and Pro-Slavery Arguments: Comprising the Writings of Hammond, Harper, Christy, Stringfellow, Hodge, Bledsoe, and Cartrwright on this Important Subject' is a thought-provoking collection of writings that delves into the controversial topic of slavery during the 19th century. Through the perspectives of various influential figures of the time, the book explores the arguments and justifications put forth in support of the institution of slavery. 

Genres: Historical, Political, Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Racism, Social and Economic Power, Morality
