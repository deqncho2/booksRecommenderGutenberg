Synopsis: In "We Women and Our Authors" by Laura Marholm, a group of women gather to discuss and analyze the works of various authors, delving into the complexities of literature and the impact it has on their lives. As they navigate through the pages of these influential writers, they uncover hidden truths, challenge societal norms, and explore the power of storytelling. With each discussion, the women find themselves questioning their own identities and the roles they play in a world dominated by men. This thought-provoking exploration of literature and feminism will captivate readers, leaving them eager to join the conversation.

Genres: Literary criticism, Feminist literature, Women's studies

Themes: Gender roles, Feminism, Literature and society, Identity
