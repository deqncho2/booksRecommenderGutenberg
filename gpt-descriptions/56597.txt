Synopsis: 
In 'The Legends and Myths of Hawaii: The fables and folk-lore of a strange people', King David Kalakaua takes us on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of Hawaiian legends and myths. From the creation of the islands to the epic battles of gods and demigods, this collection of fables and folk-lore unveils the rich cultural heritage of a people deeply connected to their land and traditions. As we delve into the captivating tales of love, bravery, and supernatural beings, we are transported to a realm where gods walk among mortals and the forces of nature hold immense power. With each story, we gain a deeper understanding of the Hawaiian people's profound respect for nature and their unwavering belief in the interconnectedness of all things. 

Genres: Mythology, Folklore, Cultural Studies

Themes: Nature, Spirituality, Cultural Identity
