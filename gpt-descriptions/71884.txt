Synopsis: 
In "The Great Masters of Russian Literature in the Nineteenth Century," Ernest Dupuy takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives and works of some of the most influential Russian writers of the time. From the passionate and tumultuous love affairs of Pushkin to the introspective and philosophical musings of Dostoevsky, Dupuy delves into the depths of these literary giants, exploring their unique writing styles, personal struggles, and the societal context that shaped their masterpieces. Through vivid anecdotes and insightful analysis, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the minds of these great masters, leaving readers eager to discover or rediscover the timeless works that have shaped Russian literature.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Artistic expression, Love and passion, Social and political commentary, Existentialism
