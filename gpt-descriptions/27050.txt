Synopsis: 
In 'De Canibus Britannicis: Of Englishe Dogges', Caius, John takes us on a fascinating journey through the history, characteristics, and uses of English dogs. From the noble Greyhound to the loyal Mastiff, Caius delves into the origins and qualities of each breed, providing valuable insights into their roles in society and their unique abilities. Through vivid descriptions and anecdotes, he paints a vivid picture of the diverse and remarkable world of English dogs, captivating readers with his passion for these remarkable creatures.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Natural History

Themes: Animal companionship, Cultural significance of dogs, Evolution of dog breeds
