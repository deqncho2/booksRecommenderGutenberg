Synopsis: In the second volume of "The life and times of the Rev. John Wesley, M.A., founder of the Methodists," author Luke Tyerman delves deeper into the extraordinary life of the influential religious leader. This installment takes readers on a captivating journey through Wesley's tireless efforts to spread Methodism, his encounters with notable figures of the time, and the challenges he faced in his personal and professional life. As Wesley's movement gains momentum, the narrative explores the impact of his teachings on society and the controversies that arise. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Tyerman paints a vivid portrait of a man whose unwavering faith and determination shaped the course of religious history.

Genres: Biography, History, Religion

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Religious reform, Social impact
