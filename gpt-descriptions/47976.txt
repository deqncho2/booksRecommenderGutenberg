Synopsis: 

In 'Race Improvement; or, Eugenics: A Little Book on a Great Subject', La Reine Helen McKenzie Baker delves into the controversial and thought-provoking realm of eugenics. Through a concise and engaging exploration, she challenges conventional beliefs and presents a compelling argument for the improvement of the human race. With a blend of scientific research, historical analysis, and personal anecdotes, Baker invites readers to question their own understanding of genetics and the potential for human advancement. This little book offers a fresh perspective on a complex subject, leaving readers with a renewed sense of curiosity and a desire to delve deeper into the fascinating world of eugenics.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Social Commentary

Themes: Eugenics, Human Advancement, Genetics, Social Responsibility
