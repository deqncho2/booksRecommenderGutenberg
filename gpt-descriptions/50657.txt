Synopsis: 
"The Satires of Juvenal, Persius, Sulpicia, and Lucilius: Literally translated into English prose, with notes, chronological tables, arguments, &c." presents a collection of satirical works by renowned Roman poets. The book offers a fascinating glimpse into the social, political, and cultural aspects of ancient Rome through the witty and often scathing commentary of Juvenal, Persius, Sulpicia, and Lucilius. From the corruption of the ruling class to the hypocrisy of religious practices, these satires explore various aspects of Roman society with sharp humor and biting criticism. The translation, accompanied by extensive notes and additional materials, provides a comprehensive understanding of the context and themes of these influential works.

Genres: Satire, Poetry, Translation

Themes: Social criticism, Political corruption, Hypocrisy, Roman society
