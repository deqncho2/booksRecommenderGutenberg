Synopsis: 

In 'Italian Harpsichord-Building in the 16th and 17th Centuries' by John D. Shortridge, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of harpsichord craftsmanship in Italy during the Renaissance and Baroque periods. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the author unveils the secrets behind the creation of these exquisite musical instruments, delving into the lives of the master builders and the intricate techniques they employed. From the bustling workshops of Venice to the opulent courts of Florence, this book offers a captivating exploration of the artistry, innovation, and cultural significance of Italian harpsichord-building.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Music

Themes: Artistry, Craftsmanship, Cultural History
