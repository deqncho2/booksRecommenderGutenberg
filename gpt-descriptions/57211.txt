Synopsis: In 'The Cabinetmaker in Eighteenth-Century Williamsburg', author Johannes Heuvel takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of the city's chief craftsmen in the furniture industry. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Heuvel delves into the lives, tools, and methods of these skilled artisans, offering a fascinating glimpse into their craftsmanship and the bustling world of eighteenth-century Williamsburg. From the intricacies of their trade to the challenges they faced, this book paints a vivid picture of the cabinetmakers' artistry and the vibrant city that shaped their work.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Craftsmanship, Art history

Themes: Craftsmanship and skill, Historical preservation, Cultural heritage
