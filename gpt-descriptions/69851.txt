Synopsis: 

In "The Medieval Inquisition: A Study in Religious Persecution," Charles T. Gorham delves into the dark and complex history of the medieval Inquisition, captivating readers with a gripping exploration of religious persecution. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Gorham uncovers the origins, methods, and consequences of this infamous institution, shedding light on the motivations behind the persecution of individuals deemed heretics. From the rise of the Inquisition to its far-reaching impact on society, this book offers a fascinating and thought-provoking journey into a turbulent period of history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious studies, Cultural studies

Themes: Religious intolerance, Power dynamics, Social control, Historical injustice
