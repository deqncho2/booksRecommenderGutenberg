Synopsis: In "Bacon is Shake-Speare," Edwin Durning-Lawrence presents a compelling argument that challenges the traditional authorship of Shakespeare's works. Through meticulous research and analysis, Durning-Lawrence unveils a hidden connection between the renowned playwright and the enigmatic philosopher Francis Bacon. This groundbreaking work not only sheds light on the true identity of Shakespeare but also delves into the fascinating world of Elizabethan politics, secret societies, and the power of language. Prepare to have your perception of literary history turned upside down in this captivating exploration of one of the greatest literary mysteries of all time.

Genres: Literary criticism, historical analysis, conspiracy theory

Themes: Authorship, identity, power, language
