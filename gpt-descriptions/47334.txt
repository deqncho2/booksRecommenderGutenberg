Synopsis: 

In "Then and Now; or, Thirty-Six Years in the Rockies: Personal Reminiscences of Some of the First Pioneers of the State of Montana," Robert Vaughn takes us on a captivating journey through the untamed wilderness of Montana during its early settlement years. Through vivid and heartfelt personal reminiscences, Vaughn paints a vivid picture of the challenges, triumphs, and hardships faced by the first pioneers who dared to venture into this rugged land. From encounters with Native American tribes to the struggles of building communities from scratch, this memoir offers a unique perspective on the formation of the state of Montana and the indomitable spirit of its early settlers.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Adventure

Themes: Frontier life, Exploration and settlement, Native American relations, Resilience and determination
