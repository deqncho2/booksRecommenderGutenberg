Synopsis: In "Tragedy in Dedham: The story of the Sacco-Vanzetti case," Francis Russell delves into the gripping true story of two Italian immigrants, Nicola Sacco and Bartolomeo Vanzetti, who were accused of a crime that shook the nation. Set against the backdrop of post-World War I America, this book unravels the complex web of political unrest, social tensions, and prejudice that surrounded the controversial trial. As the case unfolds, Russell skillfully explores the personal lives of Sacco and Vanzetti, their struggles as immigrants, and the relentless pursuit of justice that captivated the world.

Genres: True Crime, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Immigration, Justice, Prejudice, Political Unrest
