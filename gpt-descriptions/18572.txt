Synopsis: In 'The President: A Novel' by Alfred Henry Lewis, the reader is taken on a thrilling journey into the world of politics and power. Set in the early 20th century, the story follows the rise of a charismatic and enigmatic figure who becomes the President of the United States. As the narrative unfolds, secrets, scandals, and unexpected alliances emerge, challenging the protagonist's leadership and forcing them to make difficult choices. With its intricate plot and richly developed characters, this novel delves into the dark underbelly of politics, revealing the hidden truths that lie behind the public facade.

Genres: Political thriller, Historical fiction, Drama

Themes: Power and corruption, Morality and ethics, Ambition and sacrifice
