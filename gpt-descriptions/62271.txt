Synopsis: In "A Boy's Experience in the Civil War, 1860-1865" by Thomas Hughes, readers are transported to the tumultuous era of the American Civil War through the eyes of a young boy. As the war unfolds, the protagonist finds himself thrust into the heart of the conflict, facing the harsh realities of battle, witnessing the sacrifices made by soldiers, and grappling with the moral complexities of war. Through his personal experiences, the reader gains a unique perspective on the war's impact on individuals and society as a whole.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, War

Themes: Courage, Loss, Identity, Patriotism, Morality
