Synopsis: 
In "The Turkish Jester: or, The Pleasantries of Cogia Nasr Eddin Effendi," readers are transported to the vibrant world of 13th-century Turkey, where they encounter the mischievous and witty character of Cogia Nasr Eddin Effendi. This collection of humorous anecdotes and tales follows the escapades of the beloved jester as he navigates the complexities of Turkish society, challenges conventional wisdom, and uses his quick wit to outsmart those around him. From his hilarious encounters with sultans and scholars to his clever solutions to everyday problems, Cogia Nasr Eddin Effendi's antics will leave readers both entertained and enlightened.

Genres: Humor, Satire, Folklore

Themes: Wit and Wisdom, Social Critique, Cultural Identity
