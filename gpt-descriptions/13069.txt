Synopsis: 
In "The Minister and the Boy: A Handbook for Churchmen Engaged in Boys' Work," author Allan Hoben takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of church ministry and its impact on young boys. Through a series of insightful anecdotes and practical advice, Hoben explores the unique challenges and rewards of working with boys in a religious setting. From fostering spiritual growth to building meaningful relationships, this handbook offers valuable guidance for ministers seeking to make a lasting difference in the lives of the boys they serve.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious, Self-help

Themes: Church ministry, Youth development, Spiritual guidance
