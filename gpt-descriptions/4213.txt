Synopsis: 

"The Founder of New France: A Chronicle of Champlain" is a captivating historical biography that delves into the life and adventures of Samuel de Champlain, the visionary explorer and founder of New France. Set in the early 17th century, the book takes readers on a thrilling journey across the vast wilderness of North America, as Champlain navigates treacherous waters, encounters indigenous peoples, and establishes the first permanent European settlement in Canada. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Charles W. Colby paints a vivid portrait of Champlain's determination, courage, and unwavering commitment to his mission, making this a compelling tale of exploration, discovery, and the forging of a new world.

Genres: Historical Biography, Adventure, Exploration

Themes: Exploration and Discovery, Cultural Encounter, Colonialism, Leadership
