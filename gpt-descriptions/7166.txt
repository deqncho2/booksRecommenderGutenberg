Synopsis: In "The Home and the World," Rabindranath Tagore takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex dynamics of love, loyalty, and nationalism. Set in early 20th-century Bengal, the story revolves around the lives of three main characters: Nikhil, a progressive zamindar (landlord); his beautiful and spirited wife, Bimala; and Sandip, a charismatic nationalist leader. As the Swadeshi movement gains momentum, Bimala finds herself torn between her loyalty to her husband and her growing infatuation with Sandip. The novel delves into the intricate web of emotions and ideologies that arise when personal desires clash with political fervor, ultimately leading to unexpected consequences.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Romance

Themes: Love and Desire, Nationalism and Patriotism, Gender Roles and Identity
