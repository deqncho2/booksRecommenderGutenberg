Synopsis:
'The Tryal of William Penn & William Mead for Causing a Tumult' takes readers back to the Old Bailey in London in 1670, where two men find themselves on trial for causing a disturbance. As the trial unfolds, the courtroom becomes a battleground of conflicting beliefs, challenging the very foundations of justice and freedom. With passionate arguments, unexpected twists, and a cast of colorful characters, this gripping account explores the power of dissent and the fight for individual rights in a time of political turmoil.

Genres:
Historical Non-fiction, Legal Drama, Political Commentary

Themes:
Freedom of Speech, Civil Liberties, Power of the Individual, Justice and Equality
