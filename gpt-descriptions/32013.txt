Synopsis: In "The Last Campaign of the Twenty-Second Regiment, N.G., S.N.Y. June and July, 1863," George Wood Wingate takes readers on a gripping journey through the intense and harrowing experiences of a regiment during the American Civil War. Set in the summer of 1863, the story follows the Twenty-Second Regiment as they face the brutal realities of battle, the bonds of brotherhood, and the sacrifices made in the name of duty. As the regiment marches forward, readers are immersed in the chaos and uncertainty of war, witnessing the courage and resilience of these soldiers in the face of overwhelming odds.

Genres: Historical fiction, War fiction

Themes: Courage, Sacrifice, Brotherhood, Duty
