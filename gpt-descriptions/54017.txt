Synopsis: In 'Two Women, 1862; a Poem' by Constance Fenimore Woolson, readers are transported to the tumultuous era of the American Civil War. The poem follows the lives of two women, each facing their own unique challenges and struggles amidst the backdrop of a nation divided. As their paths intertwine, secrets are unveiled, relationships are tested, and the true strength of these women is revealed. With vivid imagery and poignant storytelling, Woolson explores the complexities of love, loyalty, and resilience in a time of great upheaval.

Genres: Poetry, Historical Fiction

Themes: Love, Loyalty, Resilience, War, Gender Roles
