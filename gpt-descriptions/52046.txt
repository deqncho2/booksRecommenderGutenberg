Synopsis: 

'A Constitution in Making (1660-1714)' takes readers on a captivating journey through a pivotal period in British history. From the restoration of the monarchy to the end of the Stuart dynasty, this book delves into the intricate web of political, social, and cultural changes that shaped the nation. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author unveils the struggles, conflicts, and triumphs that paved the way for the modern constitutional framework. From the Great Fire of London to the Glorious Revolution, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the events and personalities that defined this transformative era.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Cultural history

Themes: Constitutional development, Monarchy and power, Social change, Political upheaval
