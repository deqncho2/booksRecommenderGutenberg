Synopsis: 

In the third volume of "The Chronicles of Enguerrand de Monstrelet," the reader is transported to a tumultuous period in French history. The narrative delves into the brutal civil wars between the houses of Orleans and Burgundy, which ravaged the kingdom. The account vividly describes the intense power struggles, political intrigues, and violent clashes that unfolded during this time. Additionally, the book explores the English occupation of Paris and Normandy, their eventual expulsion, and the significant events that occurred not only in France but also in other countries.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Chronicle, Memoir

Themes: Power struggles, Political intrigue, War, National identity
