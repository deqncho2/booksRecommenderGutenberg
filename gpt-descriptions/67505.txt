Synopsis:
"The Life of Clara Barton, Founder of the American Red Cross" is a captivating biography that delves into the extraordinary life of Clara Barton, a remarkable woman who defied societal norms and dedicated herself to humanitarian work. From her early years as a teacher to her experiences on the front lines of the Civil War, this volume provides a fascinating glimpse into the trials and triumphs of a woman who would go on to establish one of the most influential organizations in American history. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, William E. Barton paints a vivid portrait of Clara Barton, showcasing her unwavering determination, compassion, and unwavering commitment to helping others.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Humanitarianism, Women's Empowerment, Social Change
