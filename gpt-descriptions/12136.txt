Synopsis: 

In "Government and Administration of the United States," authors Willoughby and Willoughby delve into the intricate workings of the American government, providing a comprehensive analysis of its structure, functions, and processes. From the separation of powers to the role of political parties, this book offers a captivating exploration of the inner workings of the United States' political system. Through a combination of historical context, insightful analysis, and real-world examples, the authors shed light on the complexities and challenges faced by the American government, making it an essential read for anyone seeking a deeper understanding of the nation's governance.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, Government

Themes: Separation of powers, Political parties, Governance, Democracy
