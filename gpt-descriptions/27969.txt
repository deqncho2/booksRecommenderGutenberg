Synopsis: 

"The County Regiment: A Sketch of the Second Regiment of Connecticut Volunteer Heavy Artillery, Originally the Nineteenth Volunteer Infantry, in the Civil War" by Dudley Landon Vaill is a captivating historical account that delves into the experiences of the Second Regiment of Connecticut Volunteer Heavy Artillery during the Civil War. 

Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Vaill brings to life the trials and triumphs of this regiment as they navigate the brutal realities of war. From their initial formation as the Nineteenth Volunteer Infantry to their transformation into the Heavy Artillery, the reader is taken on a journey alongside these brave soldiers, witnessing their courage, camaraderie, and sacrifices.

This book offers a unique perspective on the Civil War, shedding light on the often overlooked role of heavy artillery units and the challenges they faced on the battlefield. From the horrors of battle to the bonds forged in the face of adversity, "The County Regiment" paints a vivid picture of the human experience during this tumultuous period in American history.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Military History, Biography

Themes: War and Conflict, Patriotism, Brotherhood, Resilience
