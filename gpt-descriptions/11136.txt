Synopsis: 
In 'A Discourse Upon the Origin and the Foundation of the Inequality Among Mankind', Rousseau delves into the fundamental question of why inequality exists among human beings. Through a thought-provoking exploration of human nature, societal structures, and the impact of civilization, Rousseau challenges conventional wisdom and offers a unique perspective on the origins of inequality. This captivating discourse invites readers to question their assumptions about the nature of humanity and the societal structures that shape our lives.

Genres: Philosophy, Political Theory, Social Science

Themes: Inequality, Human Nature, Society, Civilization
