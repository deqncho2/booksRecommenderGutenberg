Synopsis: 
In 'The Philistine: A Periodical of Protest (Vol. I, No. 2, July 1895)', readers are transported to a world where art, literature, and societal norms collide. This captivating periodical takes a bold stance against the conventional, challenging readers to question their own beliefs and embrace the unconventional. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, witty anecdotes, and captivating illustrations, 'The Philistine' offers a refreshing perspective on the human condition and the power of individuality.

Genres: Satire, Essay, Art Criticism

Themes: Nonconformity, Individualism, Social Critique
