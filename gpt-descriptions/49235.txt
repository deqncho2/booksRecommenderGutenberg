Synopsis: In "Hector Berlioz: A Romantic Tragedy," Herbert F. Peyser delves into the captivating life of the renowned composer, Hector Berlioz. From his tumultuous love affairs to his relentless pursuit of artistic perfection, this biography paints a vivid portrait of a man whose passion for music knew no bounds. As Berlioz navigates the highs and lows of his career, readers are taken on a journey through the vibrant world of 19th-century Europe, where love, ambition, and artistic genius collide.

Genres: Biography, Music, History

Themes: Love and obsession, Artistic pursuit and sacrifice, Romanticism
