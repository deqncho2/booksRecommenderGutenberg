Synopsis: In 'The Cords of Vanity: A Comedy of Shirking' by James Branch Cabell, we are introduced to Felix Kennaston, a talented writer who finds himself caught between the demands of society and his own desire for personal freedom. As he navigates the complexities of love, ambition, and artistic integrity, Kennaston's journey takes unexpected turns, leading him to question the very nature of success and the sacrifices it entails. With wit and humor, Cabell explores the intricate web of societal expectations and the pursuit of individual happiness, leaving readers pondering the true cords that bind us all.

Genres: Satire, Comedy, Social Commentary

Themes: Society and conformity, Artistic integrity, Personal freedom
