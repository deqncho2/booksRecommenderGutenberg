Synopsis:
In "The Indian Operation of Couching for Cataract," Robert Henry Elliot takes us on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of Indian medicine. Set against the backdrop of colonial India, the story follows a young British doctor who becomes enthralled by the ancient surgical technique of couching, used to treat cataracts. As he delves deeper into this mysterious practice, he uncovers a hidden world of tradition, spirituality, and the clash of cultures. With vivid descriptions and unexpected twists, Elliot's narrative explores the complexities of medical innovation, cultural exchange, and the power of ancient wisdom.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Medical Drama, Cultural Exploration

Themes: Tradition vs. Innovation, Cultural Exchange, Spirituality, Colonialism
