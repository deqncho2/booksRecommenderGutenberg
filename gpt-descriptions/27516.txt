Synopsis: In 'The Toilers of the Field' by Richard Jefferies, a young man named Felix finds himself torn between his love for the land and his desire for a different life. As he navigates the challenges of rural life, he becomes entangled in a web of love, ambition, and societal expectations. With vivid descriptions of the natural world and the struggles of the working class, this captivating novel explores the complexities of human relationships and the pursuit of personal fulfillment.

Genres: Fiction, Romance, Naturalism

Themes: Love and desire, Ambition, Social class, Nature and the environment
