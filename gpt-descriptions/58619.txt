Synopsis: In 'The Hymn-Book of the Modern Church: Brief studies of hymns and hymn-writers', Arthur E. Gregory delves into the fascinating world of hymns and hymn-writers, uncovering the stories behind some of the most beloved songs of the modern church. From the timeless classics to the lesser-known gems, Gregory explores the origins, inspirations, and impact of these hymns, shedding light on the lives of the talented individuals who penned them. With captivating anecdotes and insightful analysis, this book offers a unique perspective on the power of music in worship, inviting readers to discover the rich tapestry of hymns that have shaped the modern church.

Genres: Non-fiction, Music, Religious Studies

Themes: Hymnology, Worship, Christian History
