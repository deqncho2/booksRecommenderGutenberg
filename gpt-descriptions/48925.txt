Synopsis: 

In "A History of the Durham Miner's Association 1870-1904" by Wilson, John, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years of the mining industry in Durham. This meticulously researched and engagingly written book delves into the struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices of the miners and their association during a pivotal period in history. From the formation of the association to the challenges faced by the miners, the author paints a vivid picture of the social, economic, and political landscape that shaped their lives. As the story unfolds, readers will be captivated by the resilience and determination of the miners, as well as the impact they had on the labor movement and society as a whole.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Labor Studies

Themes: Labor rights, Social justice, Collective action, Industrialization, Working-class struggles
