Synopsis: In "Colonial Dames and Good Wives," Alice Morse Earle takes us on a captivating journey through the lives of women in colonial America. From the bustling streets of Boston to the quiet farms of New England, we meet a diverse cast of women who defied societal norms and left their mark on history. Through their stories, we uncover the untold tales of strength, resilience, and determination that shaped the foundations of our nation. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Earle paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by these remarkable women, offering a fresh perspective on the colonial era.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Women's history, Biography

Themes: Gender roles, Women's empowerment, Social history
