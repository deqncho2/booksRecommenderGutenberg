Synopsis: 

'The Rural Motor Express: To Conserve Foodstuffs and Labor and to Supply Rural Transportation' takes readers on a captivating journey through the challenges and triumphs of rural transportation during a critical period in American history. This insightful work, authored by the United States Council of National Defense's Highways Transport Committee, delves into the innovative solutions and strategies employed to ensure the efficient supply of foodstuffs and labor to rural areas. From the introduction of motorized vehicles to the implementation of new transportation networks, this book unveils the untold story of how rural communities were connected and sustained during a time of great need.

Genres: Historical, Transportation, Non-fiction

Themes: Rural development, Transportation infrastructure, Food security
