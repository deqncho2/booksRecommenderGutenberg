Synopsis: 
In 'The Book of Old-Fashioned Flowers: And Other Plants Which Thrive in the Open-Air of England', Harry Roberts takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of traditional English gardens. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Roberts introduces a wide array of old-fashioned flowers and plants that have thrived in the open-air of England for centuries. From delicate roses to vibrant tulips, each chapter unveils the fascinating history, cultivation techniques, and unique characteristics of these timeless botanical treasures. As readers delve into the pages of this book, they will be transported to a bygone era, where the beauty and fragrance of these flowers evoke a sense of nostalgia and a longing for simpler times. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Gardening, History

Themes: Traditional gardening, Botanical heritage, Nostalgia
