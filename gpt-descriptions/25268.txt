Synopsis:
In 'Child-life in Art', Estelle M. Hurll takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of art, exploring how children have been depicted throughout history. From the tender innocence of Renaissance paintings to the playful scenes of Impressionist masterpieces, Hurll delves into the various ways artists have captured the essence of childhood. Through vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, she invites readers to discover the hidden stories and emotions behind each artwork, shedding light on the universal experiences of children across time and cultures. This enchanting exploration of child-life in art is sure to captivate both art enthusiasts and those with a love for the beauty and innocence of childhood.

Genres: Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Childhood, Innocence, Artistic Representation
