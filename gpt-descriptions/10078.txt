Synopsis: 

In 'Plain Words from America: A Letter to a German Professor (1917)', Johnson, Douglas Wilson takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the tumultuous times of World War I. Through a heartfelt letter addressed to a German professor, the author delves into the complexities of war, nationalism, and the power of words. With a unique perspective from across the Atlantic, Johnson challenges conventional beliefs and offers a fresh perspective on the conflict that gripped the world. As the letter unfolds, readers are invited to question their own preconceptions and explore the profound impact of language and communication during times of crisis.

Genres: Historical, Epistolary, Political

Themes: War, Nationalism, Language and Communication
