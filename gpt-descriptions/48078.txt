Synopsis: 
In "History of Embalming: and of Preparations in Anatomy, Pathology, and Natural History; Including an Account of a New Process for Embalming," Jean-Nicolas Gannal takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of embalming. From ancient Egyptian practices to modern techniques, Gannal explores the evolution of this ancient art, revealing the secrets behind preserving the human body. Along the way, he introduces readers to a groundbreaking new process that promises to revolutionize the field of embalming. With vivid descriptions and historical anecdotes, Gannal offers a unique perspective on the intersection of science, art, and mortality.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Science

Themes: Mortality, Preservation, Scientific Advancement
