Synopsis: In "The Extermination of the American Bison," William T. Hornaday takes readers on a gripping journey through the tragic history of the American bison, once a symbol of the wild and untamed West. From the awe-inspiring herds that once roamed the plains to the devastating impact of human intervention, Hornaday's meticulous research and vivid storytelling shed light on the near-extinction of this iconic species. Through personal anecdotes, historical accounts, and scientific analysis, the author explores the complex relationship between humans and nature, raising thought-provoking questions about conservation, responsibility, and the consequences of our actions.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Environmental Studies

Themes: Conservation, Human impact on nature, Wildlife preservation, Responsibility, Environmental ethics
