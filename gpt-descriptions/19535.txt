Synopsis:
In "George Bernard Shaw," G. K. Chesterton delves into the life and mind of one of the most influential playwrights of the 20th century. Through his characteristic wit and insightful analysis, Chesterton paints a vivid portrait of Shaw, exploring his eccentricities, his controversial views, and his relentless pursuit of truth. From Shaw's early struggles to his rise to fame, Chesterton takes us on a captivating journey, revealing the complexities and contradictions of this enigmatic literary figure. As the pages turn, readers will find themselves captivated by the fascinating world of George Bernard Shaw.

Genres:
Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes:
Artistic genius, Social criticism, Intellectualism
