Synopsis: In "Lady Good-for-Nothing: A Man's Portrait of a Woman," Arthur Quiller-Couch takes us on a captivating journey through the life of a mysterious and enigmatic woman. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the unconventional Lady Joan Carthew as she navigates the complexities of society, love, and self-discovery. With her audacious spirit and refusal to conform to societal norms, Lady Joan challenges the expectations placed upon women of her time. As her story unfolds, secrets are revealed, passions ignite, and the boundaries of love and loyalty are tested. Quiller-Couch's masterful storytelling keeps readers on the edge of their seats, eager to uncover the truth behind Lady Joan's captivating persona.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Mystery

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, self-discovery, love and passion
