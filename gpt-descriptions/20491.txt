Synopsis: In 'Kafir Stories: Seven Short Stories', W. C. Scully takes readers on a captivating journey through the landscapes and cultures of South Africa. From the gripping tale of a young boy's encounter with a mysterious creature in the wilderness, to the heart-wrenching story of a forbidden love affair, each short story offers a unique and thought-provoking exploration of the human experience. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, Scully weaves together a collection of narratives that will leave readers both enthralled and deeply moved.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories, Historical Fiction

Themes: Identity, Colonialism, Love and Loss, Cultural Conflict
