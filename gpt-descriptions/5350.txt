Synopsis:
'Farmers of Forty Centuries; Or, Permanent Agriculture in China, Korea, and Japan' takes readers on a captivating journey through the agricultural practices of three ancient Asian civilizations. F. H. King, an American agricultural scientist, explores the remarkable methods and sustainable techniques employed by farmers in China, Korea, and Japan, revealing a wealth of knowledge that has been passed down for centuries. From intricate irrigation systems to innovative crop rotations, this book unveils the secrets behind the long-lasting success of these agricultural societies. Through vivid descriptions and insightful observations, King paints a vivid picture of the harmonious relationship between humans and the land, offering valuable lessons for modern-day farmers and environmentalists alike.

Genres:
Non-fiction, Agriculture, History

Themes:
Sustainability, Traditional farming practices, Cultural preservation
