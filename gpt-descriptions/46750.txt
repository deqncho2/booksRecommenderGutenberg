Synopsis: 
'Prison Journals During the French Revolution' by Louise Henriette Charlotte Philippine de Durfort, duchesse de Duras, offers a captivating and intimate glimpse into the tumultuous period of the French Revolution. Through her personal journals, the duchess recounts her experiences and emotions during her time imprisoned in the midst of political upheaval. As she navigates the harsh realities of confinement, the duchess's writings delve into the complexities of human resilience, the fragility of freedom, and the power of hope in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Resilience, Freedom, Hope, Political Upheaval
