Synopsis: 
'Potterism: A Tragi-Farcical Tract' by Macaulay, Rose is a gripping and satirical novel that delves into the world of journalism and the power of media influence. The story follows the ambitious and cunning journalist, Gideon, as he rises to fame by manipulating public opinion through his sensationalist articles. However, his actions have far-reaching consequences, leading to a series of unexpected events that challenge the very fabric of truth and integrity in the media. As the plot unfolds, the reader is taken on a rollercoaster ride of scandal, deception, and moral dilemmas, ultimately questioning the role of journalism in society.

Genres: Satire, Drama, Political Fiction

Themes: Media manipulation, Ethics in journalism, Power and corruption
