Synopsis: 

In 'The Abenaki Indians: Their Treaties of 1713 & 1717, and a Vocabulary' by Frederic Kidder, readers are transported to the fascinating world of the Abenaki Indians and their historical treaties. This captivating work delves into the intricacies of the treaties signed between the Abenaki Indians and the British colonizers in 1713 and 1717, shedding light on the complex dynamics and negotiations that took place during this pivotal period. Through meticulous research and a comprehensive vocabulary, Kidder provides a unique insight into the cultural, political, and social aspects of the Abenaki Indians, offering readers a deeper understanding of their rich heritage and the challenges they faced.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Indigenous studies, Linguistics

Themes: Indigenous history, Colonialism, Cultural preservation
