Synopsis: In 'An Experimental Translocation of the Eastern Timber Wolf', Hook, Mech, Robinson, and Weise take readers on a captivating journey into the world of wildlife conservation. Set in the rugged landscapes of North America, this book explores the groundbreaking efforts to reintroduce the endangered Eastern Timber Wolf to its historical range. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, the authors delve into the challenges faced by scientists, the resilience of the wolves, and the profound impact of this ambitious experiment. As the story unfolds, readers are immersed in a tale of hope, adversity, and the delicate balance between humans and nature.

Genres: Non-fiction, Conservation, Wildlife Biology

Themes: Endangered species, Wildlife conservation, Human-wildlife interaction
