Synopsis: In 'Collected Essays, Volume V: Science and Christian Tradition: Essays', Thomas Henry Huxley delves into the fascinating intersection between science and Christian tradition. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Huxley explores the compatibility of these seemingly contrasting realms, challenging conventional beliefs and offering fresh perspectives. From examining the origins of life to questioning the role of miracles, Huxley's insightful analysis invites readers to embark on a captivating intellectual journey that challenges their preconceived notions.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Religion

Themes: Science and religion, Compatibility, Evolution, Miracles, Intellectual inquiry
