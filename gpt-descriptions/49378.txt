Synopsis: 

In "Robert Schumann, Tone-Poet, Prophet and Critic" by Herbert F. Peyser, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the life and mind of one of the most influential composers of the Romantic era. This meticulously researched biography delves into the complex and often tumultuous world of Robert Schumann, exploring his artistic genius, his struggles with mental health, and his profound impact on the world of music. From his passionate love affair with Clara Wieck to his revolutionary compositions, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of a true musical genius.

Genres: Biography, Music, History

Themes: Artistic genius, Mental health, Love and passion
