Synopsis: 

In 'Vertebrates from the Barrier Island of Tamaulipas, México', Johnston, Raun, Selander, and Wilks take readers on an intriguing journey to the captivating Barrier Island of Tamaulipas, México. Through their meticulous research and vivid descriptions, they unveil a world teeming with diverse and fascinating vertebrate species. From the enchanting marine life that thrives in the surrounding waters to the remarkable land-dwelling creatures that inhabit the island, this book offers a captivating exploration of the island's rich biodiversity. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Zoology

Themes: Biodiversity, Conservation, Ecosystems
