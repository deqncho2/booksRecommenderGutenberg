Synopsis: In 'The Creed of the Old South 1865-1915', Basil L. Gildersleeve delves into the complex and often controversial history of the American South during the post-Civil War era. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Gildersleeve explores the evolving beliefs, values, and traditions that shaped the identity of the Old South in the face of immense social, political, and economic changes. From the struggles of Reconstruction to the rise of Jim Crow laws, this book offers a captivating exploration of the South's transformation and the enduring legacy of its creed.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural studies, Southern history

Themes: Identity, Tradition, Social change, Race relations
