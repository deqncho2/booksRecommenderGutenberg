Synopsis: 
'Songs and Ballads of the Southern People: 1861-1865' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years of the American Civil War. Through a collection of powerful and poignant songs and ballads, this work offers a unique perspective on the experiences, emotions, and struggles of the Southern people during this pivotal period in history. From heartfelt laments to rousing anthems, these songs provide a window into the hopes, fears, and resilience of those who lived through the war. As readers delve into the lyrics and melodies, they will be transported to a time of great upheaval and find themselves immersed in the rich cultural tapestry of the Southern states.

Genres: 
Historical, Music, Poetry

Themes: 
War and Conflict, Identity, Resilience, Cultural Expression
