Synopsis:
In 'Mary Broome: A Comedy, in Four Acts' by Monkhouse Allan, we are introduced to the Broome family, a seemingly ordinary household in early 20th century England. However, when their maid, Mary, becomes pregnant, their lives are turned upside down. As secrets are revealed and tensions rise, the Broomes must confront their own prejudices and societal expectations. With witty dialogue and unexpected twists, this comedy explores the complexities of class, gender, and family dynamics, leaving readers questioning their own assumptions.

Genres:
Comedy, Drama, Social Satire

Themes:
Class, Gender, Family, Prejudice
