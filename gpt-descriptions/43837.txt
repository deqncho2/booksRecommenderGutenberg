Synopsis: In 'The Man Who Was Good' by Leonard Merrick, we are introduced to a seemingly ordinary man named Edward, who leads a quiet and unremarkable life. However, as the story unfolds, we discover that Edward has a hidden past filled with secrets and unexpected twists. As his true nature is gradually revealed, the reader is taken on a thrilling journey of suspense and moral ambiguity. With each page, the line between good and evil becomes increasingly blurred, leaving us questioning our own perceptions of morality and the choices we make.

Genres: Mystery, Thriller, Psychological Fiction

Themes: Morality, Identity, Redemption
