Synopsis: In "The Simple Cobler of Aggawam in America," Nathaniel Ward takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the early days of colonial America. Through the eyes of a witty and opinionated cobbler, the author explores the challenges and contradictions of Puritan society, offering a unique perspective on religion, politics, and the human condition. As the cobler's sharp observations and clever wordplay unfold, readers are invited to reflect on the complexities of life in a new land, where tradition clashes with progress and individuality battles against conformity.

Genres: Satire, Historical Fiction, Political Commentary

Themes: Puritanism, Religion, Politics, Individuality, Tradition vs. Progress
