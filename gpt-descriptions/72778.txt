Synopsis: 

In 'A History of Brazil', James Henderson takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich tapestry of Brazil's past. From its awe-inspiring geography to its complex colonization, Henderson delves into the depths of Brazil's history, shedding light on its aboriginal inhabitants and the intricate web of commerce that shaped the nation. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, this book unveils the untold stories and hidden treasures of Brazil's past, leaving readers eager to uncover the secrets that lie within its pages.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travelogue, Cultural exploration

Themes: Geography and landscape, Colonialism and colonization, Indigenous cultures, Economic development
