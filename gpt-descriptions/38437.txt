Synopsis: 

In "The Woman's Part: A Record of Munitions Work," L. K. Yates takes us on a captivating journey into the lives of women who played a crucial role during World War I. Through a series of personal accounts and anecdotes, Yates unveils the untold stories of these remarkable women who defied societal norms and stepped into the male-dominated world of munitions work. From the challenges they faced to the triumphs they achieved, this book sheds light on the resilience, determination, and camaraderie that emerged amidst the chaos of war. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Women's history, War memoir

Themes: Gender roles, Women's empowerment, War and society
