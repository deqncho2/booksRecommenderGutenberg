Synopsis: 

'The Church of Sancta Sophia, Constantinople: A Study of Byzantine Building' takes readers on a captivating journey through the architectural marvels of the Byzantine Empire. Written by Lethaby and Swainson, this book delves into the intricate details and historical significance of the renowned Church of Sancta Sophia in Constantinople. From its construction to its transformation into a mosque, the authors explore the fascinating evolution of this iconic structure, shedding light on the cultural, religious, and artistic influences that shaped it. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the book offers a comprehensive study of Byzantine building techniques and the enduring legacy of Sancta Sophia.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Architecture, Art

Themes: Byzantine Empire, Religious architecture, Cultural heritage
