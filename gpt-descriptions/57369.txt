Synopsis: In "All Things are Possible," Shestov takes readers on a philosophical journey that challenges conventional wisdom and explores the depths of human existence. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, he delves into the nature of truth, the limits of reason, and the complexities of faith. With a blend of intellectual rigor and poetic prose, Shestov invites readers to question their own beliefs and embrace the uncertainties of life.

Genres: Philosophy, Existentialism, Literary Criticism

Themes: Truth and uncertainty, Reason and faith, Existential angst, Human existence
