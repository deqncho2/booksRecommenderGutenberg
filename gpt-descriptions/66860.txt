Synopsis: 
In 'My Diary: North and South (vol. 1 of 2)', Sir William Howard Russell takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years leading up to the American Civil War. As a renowned journalist, Russell provides a unique perspective on the political and social landscapes of both the North and the South, offering intimate insights into the lives of influential figures and ordinary citizens alike. Through his vivid descriptions and personal encounters, he paints a vivid picture of a nation on the brink of division, where tensions run high and the fate of a nation hangs in the balance.

Genres: 
Historical non-fiction, travelogue, memoir

Themes: 
American Civil War, social and political unrest, cultural differences, journalism
