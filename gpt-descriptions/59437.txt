Synopsis: In 'A Son at the Front' by Edith Wharton, we are transported to the tumultuous backdrop of World War I. The story revolves around a renowned American painter, John Campton, who finds himself torn between his duty as a father and his passion for art. When his beloved son, George, is called to serve in the war, John is faced with a heart-wrenching decision that will test the boundaries of love, loyalty, and sacrifice. As the war rages on, John's journey takes him through the harrowing realities of the front lines, where he must confront the true cost of his choices and the profound impact they have on his family and himself.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Family Drama

Themes: Duty and Responsibility, Art and Creativity, Sacrifice, Love and Loss
