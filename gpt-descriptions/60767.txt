Synopsis: 

In 'The Armourer and His Craft from the XIth to the XVIth Century', Charles John Ffoulkes takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of armor-making. From the medieval period to the Renaissance, Ffoulkes delves into the intricate techniques, tools, and materials used by armorers to create these magnificent works of art and protection. Through vivid descriptions and detailed illustrations, the author unveils the secrets behind the craftsmanship that shaped the history of warfare and fashion.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Art history, Craftsmanship

Themes: Medieval warfare, Renaissance art, Craftsmanship and skill
