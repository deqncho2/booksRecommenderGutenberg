Synopsis: 

'Good housing that pays: $b A study of the aims and the accomplishment of the Octavia Hill Association, 1896-1917' delves into the fascinating history of the Octavia Hill Association, an organization dedicated to providing good housing for the less fortunate during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Through meticulous research and analysis, author Fullerton L. Waldo uncovers the aims and accomplishments of this influential association, shedding light on the impact it had on society and the lives of those it served. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Sciences

Themes: Social reform, Housing inequality, Philanthropy
