Synopsis: In 'Addresses in America, 1919' by John Galsworthy, a renowned British author embarks on a journey to America, where he delivers a series of captivating speeches that delve into the complexities of society, politics, and human nature. Through his thought-provoking addresses, he challenges the status quo and offers a unique perspective on the issues plaguing America during this transformative period. As he navigates the diverse landscapes and encounters a myriad of individuals, the author's words resonate deeply, leaving a lasting impact on both himself and his audience.

Genres: Non-fiction, Travelogue, Social Commentary

Themes: Society and its flaws, Politics and power, Cultural exchange, Individualism and conformity
