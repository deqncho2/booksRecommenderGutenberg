Synopsis: 

In 'Sewage and sewerage of farm homes [1928]', George M. Warren takes readers on a fascinating journey into the often overlooked world of farm home sewage systems. Through meticulous research and practical insights, Warren explores the challenges faced by rural communities in managing waste disposal and offers innovative solutions to improve sanitation. From the intricacies of septic tanks to the intricacies of drainage systems, this book sheds light on the vital yet underappreciated aspect of farm life. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Agricultural literature, Environmental science

Themes: Sanitation, Rural living, Waste management
