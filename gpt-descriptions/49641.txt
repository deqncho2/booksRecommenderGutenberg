Synopsis: In 'Whole Body Counters', Lengemann and Woodburn take readers on a thrilling journey into the world of radiation detection and its implications for human health. As they delve into the fascinating history and development of whole body counters, the authors uncover a web of secrets, hidden dangers, and unexpected consequences. From the shadowy world of nuclear testing to the cutting-edge technology used in modern radiation detection, this book explores the high-stakes race to understand and protect against the invisible threat that surrounds us. With gripping anecdotes and thought-provoking insights, 'Whole Body Counters' will leave readers questioning the true extent of our exposure to radiation and its long-term effects.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: Radiation, Health, Technology, Ethics
