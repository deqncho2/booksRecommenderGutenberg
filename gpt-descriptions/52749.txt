Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The History of Korea', Homer B. Hulbert delves deeper into the captivating story of Korea, unveiling a rich tapestry of historical events, cultural traditions, and political struggles. From the rise of powerful dynasties to the influence of neighboring nations, this book takes readers on a fascinating journey through the complex and often turbulent history of Korea. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Hulbert paints a vivid picture of a nation shaped by its past, offering a comprehensive exploration of the forces that have shaped Korea into what it is today.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural studies, Asian history

Themes: National identity, Colonialism, Political power struggles, Cultural preservation
