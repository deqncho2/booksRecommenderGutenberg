Synopsis: In 'The Forest Runners: A Story of the Great War Trail in Early Kentucky' by Joseph A. Altsheler, readers are transported to the untamed wilderness of early Kentucky, where danger lurks at every turn. The story follows two young frontiersmen, Henry Ware and Paul Cotter, as they navigate the treacherous Great War Trail, facing hostile Native American tribes, rival settlers, and the harsh realities of survival. As they forge their path through the dense forests, their courage and resourcefulness are put to the ultimate test. Will they be able to overcome the perils of the wilderness and fulfill their destinies?

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Action

Themes: Survival, Friendship, Exploration, Conflict
