Synopsis: In 'Dream Psychology: Psychoanalysis for Beginners', Sigmund Freud delves into the mysterious realm of dreams, unraveling their hidden meanings and exploring the unconscious mind. Through captivating case studies and insightful analysis, Freud takes readers on a fascinating journey into the depths of the human psyche, revealing the complex interplay between desires, fears, and repressed memories. As he explores the symbolism and significance of dreams, Freud offers a unique perspective on the inner workings of the mind and the profound impact they have on our waking lives.

Genres: Psychology, Non-fiction

Themes: Dreams, Unconscious mind, Psychoanalysis, Symbolism
