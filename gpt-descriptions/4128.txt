Synopsis:
In the eleventh volume of his famous diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a captivating journey through the summer months of 1661. As he navigates the bustling streets of London, Pepys encounters a myriad of intriguing characters and finds himself entangled in a web of political intrigue, scandalous affairs, and personal triumphs. From attending lavish parties to witnessing the coronation of King Charles II, Pepys provides a vivid and intimate account of the vibrant social scene and the ever-changing landscape of Restoration England. With his sharp wit and keen observations, Pepys invites readers to experience the exhilaration and tumultuousness of this pivotal period in history.

Genres:
Memoir, Historical Fiction, Biography

Themes:
Social life, Politics, Personal growth
