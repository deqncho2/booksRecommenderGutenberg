Synopsis: In 'A Short View of the Frauds and Abuses Committed by Apothecaries', Christopher Merret exposes the deceitful practices of apothecaries in relation to both patients and physicians. He presents a compelling argument for physicians to take matters into their own hands by making their own medicines, offering a solution to the prevalent corruption in the medical field. Through a series of eye-opening revelations, Merret sheds light on the hidden world of apothecaries and the dire consequences of their fraudulent actions.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical literature, Historical critique

Themes: Corruption in the medical profession, Patient care, Physician autonomy
