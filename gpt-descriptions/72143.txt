Synopsis: 

In 'The manufacture of mineral and lake pigments', Josef Bersch takes readers on a fascinating journey into the world of artificial artists' and painters' colours. With detailed instructions, Bersch unveils the secrets behind creating a wide range of pigments, including enamel colours, soot, and metallic pigments. From the intricate processes to the precise measurements, this book offers a comprehensive guide for anyone interested in the art of pigment manufacturing. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Art, Science

Themes: Artistic techniques, Chemical processes, Creativity
