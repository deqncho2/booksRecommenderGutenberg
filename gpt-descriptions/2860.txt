Synopsis: 
In 'Framley Parsonage' by Anthony Trollope, the lives of the inhabitants of the small English village of Framley are intricately woven together, revealing a web of love, ambition, and scandal. Mark Robarts, the young and ambitious parson, finds himself entangled in a world of social politics and financial troubles, while his sister Lucy navigates the complexities of love and marriage. As the story unfolds, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and the characters must confront the consequences of their actions. Set against the backdrop of Victorian society, 'Framley Parsonage' is a captivating tale of personal and moral dilemmas that will keep readers eagerly turning the pages.

Genres: Fiction, Victorian Literature, Social Drama

Themes: Love and Marriage, Ambition, Social Class, Morality
