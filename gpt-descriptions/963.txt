Synopsis: 
'Little Dorrit' by Charles Dickens is a captivating tale that follows the life of Amy Dorrit, a young woman who grows up in the Marshalsea debtors' prison. Despite her challenging circumstances, Amy remains resilient and kind-hearted, navigating a world filled with secrets, betrayals, and unexpected connections. As she encounters a diverse cast of characters, including the enigmatic Arthur Clennam, the story takes us on a journey through the intricacies of Victorian society, exploring themes of social class, wealth, love, and redemption.

Genres:
Historical Fiction, Social Commentary, Bildungsroman

Themes:
Social class, Wealth and poverty, Love and relationships, Redemption
