Synopsis: In "The Friends of Voltaire," Evelyn Beatrice Hall takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and times of the renowned philosopher Voltaire. Through a series of witty and insightful anecdotes, Hall introduces us to the fascinating circle of intellectuals, artists, and free-thinkers who surrounded Voltaire during the Enlightenment era. From secret meetings to daring escapades, this book unveils the untold stories of the individuals who risked everything to defend Voltaire's ideas and challenge the oppressive forces of their time. As the narrative unfolds, readers are transported to a world of intellectual fervor, where the power of ideas and the pursuit of truth become paramount. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Philosophy

Themes: Freedom of thought, Intellectualism, Enlightenment, Friendship, Courage
