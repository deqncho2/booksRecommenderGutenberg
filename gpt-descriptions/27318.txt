Synopsis: 

In 'Pung Chow: The Game of a Hundred Intelligences', author Lew Lysle Harr takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of an ancient Chinese game known by many names. From Mah-Diao to Mah-Jong, Mah-Cheuk to Mah-Juck, and Pe-Ling, this game of strategy and skill has fascinated players for centuries. 

As the story unfolds, readers are introduced to a diverse cast of characters who become deeply immersed in the game, each with their own motivations and secrets. From the opulent parlors of Shanghai to the bustling streets of Hong Kong, the game of Pung Chow becomes a backdrop for intense rivalries, unexpected alliances, and even romance. 

With each move and countermove, the stakes rise higher, and the players find themselves entangled in a web of mystery and intrigue. As they strive to outwit their opponents and claim victory, they soon realize that the game of Pung Chow is not just about winning, but about unlocking the hidden depths of their own intelligence and intuition. 

Genres: Mystery, Historical Fiction, Drama
Themes: Strategy, Deception, Cultural Exploration
