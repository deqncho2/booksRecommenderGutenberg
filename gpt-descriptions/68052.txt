Synopsis: In this captivating address, President Roosevelt delivers a powerful speech during the laying of the corner stone of the Pilgrim Memorial Monument. With his characteristic eloquence, he explores the historical significance of the Pilgrims' arrival in Provincetown and their enduring impact on American society. Through his words, Roosevelt paints a vivid picture of the struggles and triumphs of the early settlers, reminding us of the values that shaped our nation. This address serves as a poignant tribute to the Pilgrims and a call to honor their legacy.

Genres: Historical, Speech

Themes: American history, patriotism, cultural heritage
