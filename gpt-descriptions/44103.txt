Synopsis: In 'The Expositor's Bible: The Book of Daniel', F. W. Farrar delves into the captivating story of Daniel, a young Hebrew exile who rises to prominence in the court of a foreign king. As Daniel faces numerous trials and tests of faith, including encounters with mysterious dreams and prophetic visions, the reader is taken on a thrilling journey through ancient empires and divine intervention. With its blend of historical narrative and prophetic insight, this book offers a thought-provoking exploration of the power of faith and the enduring struggle between good and evil.

Genres: Biblical commentary, religious literature, historical analysis

Themes: Faith and devotion, divine providence, the clash of civilizations, the power of prophecy
