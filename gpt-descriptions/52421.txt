Synopsis: 
In 'A Sermon Preached in York Minister, on St. Bartholomew's Day, Friday, August 24, 1877', Edward Hoare delivers a powerful sermon on a significant day in the church calendar. The sermon takes place during the consecration of the Right Rev. Rowley Hill as the Lord Bishop of Sodor and Man, and Hoare's words resonate with the congregation as he explores profound spiritual themes and challenges the listeners to reflect on their faith.

Genres: Religious, Sermon

Themes: Faith, Spiritual Reflection, Consecration
