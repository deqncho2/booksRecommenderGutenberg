Synopsis: 
In "The New Map of Europe (1911-1914): The Story of the Recent European Diplomatic Crises and Wars and of Europe's Present Catastrophe," Herbert Adams Gibbons takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous years leading up to the outbreak of World War I. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Gibbons unveils the intricate web of diplomatic crises, power struggles, and shifting alliances that ultimately plunged Europe into chaos. From the Balkan Wars to the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, this book offers a captivating account of the events that shaped the continent's destiny and set the stage for a catastrophic conflict.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Military history

Themes: Diplomacy and international relations, Nationalism and ethnic tensions, Causes and consequences of war
