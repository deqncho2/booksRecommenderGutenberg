Synopsis: 
'The Battle of Verdun (1914-1918)' takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most significant and brutal battles of World War I. Set against the backdrop of the Western Front, this historical account delves into the harrowing experiences of soldiers from both sides as they endure the relentless onslaught of artillery, the horrors of trench warfare, and the unyielding determination to hold or capture the strategic city of Verdun. Amidst the chaos and devastation, the story follows the lives of individuals who find themselves caught in the midst of this cataclysmic conflict, highlighting their courage, resilience, and the sacrifices they make in the face of unimaginable adversity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War, Military History

Themes: Heroism, Sacrifice, Human resilience, The futility of war
