Synopsis: In 'Garman and Worse: A Norwegian Novel', Alexander Lange Kielland takes us on a captivating journey through the lives of two wealthy families in 19th-century Norway. As the Garman and Worse families navigate the complexities of love, marriage, and societal expectations, a web of secrets and scandals begins to unravel, threatening to shatter their carefully constructed facades. Amidst the backdrop of a changing society, the characters must confront their own desires and confront the consequences of their actions. With its richly drawn characters and intricate plot, this novel explores the depths of human nature and the consequences of living in a world governed by appearances.

Genres: Historical fiction, Drama, Romance

Themes: Social class, Morality, Family dynamics, Love and desire
