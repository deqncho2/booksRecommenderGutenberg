Synopsis: 
In 'A Treatise of Human Nature: Being an Attempt to Introduce the Experimental Method Into Moral Subjects; and Dialogues Concerning Natural Religion', David Hume delves into the complexities of human nature and the foundations of morality. Through a series of thought-provoking dialogues, Hume explores the nature of religious belief and the role of reason in understanding the world. With his characteristic wit and incisive analysis, Hume challenges conventional wisdom and invites readers to question their own beliefs about human nature and the existence of a higher power.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Human nature, Morality, Religion, Reasoning
