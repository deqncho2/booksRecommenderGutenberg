Synopsis: 
In 'The Humour of Homer and Other Essays', Samuel Butler takes readers on a delightful journey through the world of ancient Greek literature. With his sharp wit and insightful observations, Butler explores the comedic elements found in the works of the legendary poet, Homer. From the mischievous antics of the gods to the humorous foibles of mortal heroes, Butler uncovers the hidden laughter within these timeless tales. Alongside his analysis of Homer, Butler also delves into other aspects of literature, including the art of translation and the role of humor in society. This collection of essays is a captivating exploration of the lighter side of classical literature, sure to entertain and enlighten readers.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Humor, Classics

Themes: Humor, Ancient Greek Literature, Translation, Satire
