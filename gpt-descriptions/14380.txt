Synopsis: In the fifth volume of 'The Life of John Milton', author David Masson delves into the tumultuous years of 1654-1660, exploring the intricate web of political, ecclesiastical, and literary events that shaped Milton's life and the world around him. From his involvement in the English government to his controversial writings and his personal struggles, this volume offers a captivating narrative that sheds light on the complex character of one of history's greatest poets.

Genres: Biography, History, Literary Criticism

Themes: Politics and power, Religion and spirituality, Artistic expression and censorship
