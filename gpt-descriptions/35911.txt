Synopsis: 
In 'Odd People: Being a Popular Description of Singular Races of Man', Reid, Mayne takes readers on a captivating journey around the world, introducing them to extraordinary individuals from various cultures and backgrounds. From the mysterious tribes of Africa to the enigmatic islanders of the Pacific, this book unveils the fascinating lives and customs of these unique races. With vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, Reid, Mayne paints a vivid picture of the diversity and wonder of humanity, leaving readers both enlightened and enthralled.

Genres: Non-fiction, Anthropology, Travelogue

Themes: Cultural diversity, Human uniqueness, Exploration
