Synopsis: In 'The Stamps of Canada', Bertram W. H. Poole takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of Canadian stamps. From the early colonial era to the modern day, this meticulously researched book unveils the hidden stories behind each stamp, revealing the rich history, cultural significance, and artistic beauty that lies within these tiny pieces of paper. As readers delve into the intricate details of each stamp, they will uncover surprising connections to Canada's past, its diverse regions, and its vibrant cultural heritage. With stunning visuals and engaging narratives, 'The Stamps of Canada' offers a unique perspective on the nation's history, making it a must-read for stamp collectors, history enthusiasts, and anyone curious about the power of symbols.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Canadian history, Cultural heritage, Symbolism
