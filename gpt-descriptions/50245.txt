Synopsis: 

The Philippine Islands, 1493-1898, Volume 45, 1736 takes readers on a captivating journey through the history and exploration of the Philippine Islands. This volume delves into the early navigators' explorations, vivid descriptions of the islands and their diverse peoples, and the records of Catholic missions. Through contemporaneous books and manuscripts, readers are transported to a time when European nations first established relations with the islands, and witness the political, economic, commercial, and religious conditions that shaped the archipelago until the close of the nineteenth century.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Exploration, Anthropology

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Cultural diversity, Colonialism, Religious influence
