Synopsis: 
In "Typesetting: A primer of information about working at the case, justifying, spacing, correcting, making-up, and other operations employed in setting type by hand," Alexander A. Stewart takes readers on a captivating journey into the intricate world of typesetting. Through detailed explanations and practical examples, Stewart unveils the secrets behind the art of setting type by hand, exploring the various techniques, tools, and operations involved in this fascinating craft. From the meticulous process of spacing and correcting to the creative task of making-up, readers will gain a deep understanding of the precision and skill required to produce beautifully typeset text. This primer serves as an invaluable resource for anyone interested in the history, techniques, and challenges of traditional typesetting.

Genres: Non-fiction, Art, Craft

Themes: Typography, Traditional Printing Techniques, Craftsmanship
