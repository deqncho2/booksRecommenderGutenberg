Synopsis: 

In the third volume of "South Africa and the Transvaal War," author Louis Creswicke takes readers on a gripping journey through the intense conflict that unfolded during the Battle of Colenso and Lord Roberts's subsequent advance into the Free State. As the war rages on, Creswicke vividly portrays the strategic maneuvers, fierce battles, and the resilience of both the British and Boer forces. With a keen eye for detail and a knack for storytelling, Creswicke brings to life the harrowing experiences of soldiers on the frontlines and the political complexities that shaped the war. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War memoir

Themes: War and conflict, Strategy and tactics, Resilience and perseverance
