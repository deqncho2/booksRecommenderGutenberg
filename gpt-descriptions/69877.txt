Synopsis: In "The Fire in the Flint" by Walter White, a young African American man named David is faced with the harsh realities of racial discrimination and injustice in the early 20th century. As he navigates through a world filled with prejudice and violence, David's journey takes him from the rural South to the bustling streets of Harlem, where he becomes entangled in a web of secrets and betrayal. With his determination and resilience, David must find the strength to fight for his dreams and overcome the obstacles that threaten to extinguish the fire within him.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Social Justice

Themes: Racial Discrimination, Identity, Resilience, Pursuit of Dreams
