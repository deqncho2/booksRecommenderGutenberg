Synopsis: 

In 'Sex in Education; or, A Fair Chance for Girls', Edward H. Clarke delves into a controversial topic that challenges societal norms and sheds light on the impact of education on young women. Through a series of thought-provoking anecdotes and compelling arguments, Clarke explores the potential consequences of intellectual pursuits on the physical and mental well-being of female students. As he navigates the delicate balance between education and femininity, the author presents a compelling case that will leave readers questioning the prevailing educational practices of the time.

Genres: Non-fiction, Education, Feminism

Themes: Gender equality, Women's rights, Education reform
