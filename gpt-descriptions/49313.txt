Synopsis: 

"The Lake Dwellings of Ireland: Or ancient lacustrine habitations of Erin, commonly called crannogs" takes readers on a captivating journey into the mysterious world of ancient Irish lake dwellings. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, Wood-Martin unveils the secrets of these fascinating structures, known as crannogs, which were once home to our ancestors. From their construction and purpose to the daily lives of those who inhabited them, this book offers a captivating exploration of a forgotten era. Prepare to be enthralled by the rich history and archaeological wonders that lie beneath the tranquil waters of Ireland's lakes.

Genres: Archaeology, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Architecture, Cultural heritage
