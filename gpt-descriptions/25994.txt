Synopsis: In "A Refutation of the Charges Made against the Confederate States of America of Having Authorized the Use of Explosive and Poisoned Musket and Rifle Balls during the Late Civil War of 1861-65," Horace Edwin Hayden delves into a controversial aspect of the American Civil War. With meticulous research and compelling arguments, Hayden challenges the accusations of the Confederate States of America employing explosive and poisoned ammunition during the conflict. Through a thorough examination of historical evidence, he aims to shed light on the truth behind these allegations, unraveling a complex web of intrigue and deception.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Legal analysis

Themes: Historical accuracy, Ethics of warfare, Propaganda and misinformation
