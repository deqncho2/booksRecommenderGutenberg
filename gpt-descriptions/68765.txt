Synopsis: 
In "Twenty-five years in the Secret Service: The recollections of a spy," Henri Le Caron takes readers on a thrilling journey through his life as a spy. From his early days as a double agent to his involvement in high-stakes espionage operations, Le Caron's gripping account unveils the dangerous world of international espionage. With vivid descriptions of covert missions, close calls, and the intricate web of deceit, this memoir offers a rare glimpse into the hidden world of spies and the sacrifices they make to protect their countries.

Genres: Memoir, Spy Thriller, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Espionage, Loyalty, Betrayal, Patriotism
