Synopsis: 

'From Manassas to Appomattox: Memoirs of the Civil War in America' by James Longstreet is a captivating firsthand account of one of the most tumultuous periods in American history. Longstreet, a prominent Confederate general, takes readers on a riveting journey through the Civil War, sharing his experiences, insights, and reflections on the events that unfolded from the Battle of Manassas to the surrender at Appomattox. With vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Longstreet provides a unique perspective on the strategies, battles, and key figures of the war, offering readers a deeper understanding of the complexities and human toll of this pivotal conflict.

Genres: Memoir, History, Military

Themes: War and its consequences, Leadership and decision-making, Loyalty and betrayal
