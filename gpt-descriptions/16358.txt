Synopsis: In 'The English Gipsies and Their Language', Charles Godfrey Leland takes readers on a captivating journey into the mysterious world of the English Gipsies. Through his encounters and interactions with this enigmatic community, Leland unveils their fascinating language, customs, and way of life. From their ancient origins to their present-day struggles, this book offers a unique insight into a hidden culture that has long captivated the imagination of many.

Genres: Non-fiction, Anthropology, Linguistics

Themes: Cultural identity, Language preservation, Social marginalization
