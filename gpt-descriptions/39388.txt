Synopsis: 
"The Columbia River: Its History, Its Myths, Its Scenery, Its Commerce" takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich tapestry of the Columbia River. From its ancient myths and legends to its breathtaking natural beauty, and from its pivotal role in commerce to its historical significance, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of one of North America's most iconic rivers. Through vivid descriptions and engaging storytelling, William Denison Lyman paints a vivid picture of the river's past, present, and future, leaving readers with a deep appreciation for its significance and allure.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Travel

Themes: Nature and environment, Cultural heritage, Economic development
