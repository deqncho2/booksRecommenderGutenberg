Synopsis:
In this installment of the Chronicles of England, Scotland and Ireland, we delve into the life of Iohn, the youngest son of King Henrie the Second. As a prince, Iohn faces numerous challenges and intrigues within the royal court, as well as external threats to his family's reign. With his father's health deteriorating, Iohn must navigate the treacherous waters of power and loyalty, all while trying to establish his own identity and secure his place in history. This captivating narrative takes us on a journey through the complexities of medieval England, revealing the struggles and triumphs of a young prince destined for greatness.

Genres:
Historical fiction, Biography, Political drama

Themes:
Power and succession, Loyalty and betrayal, Identity and self-discovery
