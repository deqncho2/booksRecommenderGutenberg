Synopsis: In 'Crimes and Punishments: Including a New Translation of Beccaria's 'Dei Delitti e delle Pene',' James Anson Farrer delves into the fascinating world of criminal justice, exploring the intricacies of crime, punishment, and the human condition. Through a meticulous translation of Cesare Beccaria's influential work, Farrer sheds light on the flaws of the legal system and challenges conventional notions of justice. With thought-provoking examples and insightful analysis, this book offers a captivating exploration of the complexities surrounding crimes and their corresponding punishments.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Law

Themes: Criminal justice, Legal reform, Human rights
