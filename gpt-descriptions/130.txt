Synopsis: In "Orthodoxy," G.K. Chesterton takes readers on a captivating intellectual journey as he explores his own personal transformation from skepticism to faith. Through a series of witty and thought-provoking essays, Chesterton challenges conventional wisdom and invites readers to question their own assumptions about the world. With his unique blend of humor and profound insights, he presents a compelling case for the relevance and vitality of orthodox Christianity in a modern age.

Genres: Autobiography, Philosophy, Religious Literature

Themes: Faith and doubt, the nature of reality, the role of tradition, the paradoxes of life, the power of imagination
