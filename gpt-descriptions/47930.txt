Synopsis:
'Brought Forward' by Cunninghame Graham is a captivating novel that takes readers on a journey through the life of a young man named Hector MacNairn. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows Hector as he navigates the complexities of love, politics, and personal identity. From his humble beginnings in Scotland to his adventures in South America, Hector's experiences shape his worldview and force him to confront his own beliefs. As he encounters a diverse cast of characters and becomes entangled in unexpected events, Hector's journey becomes a reflection on the nature of power, the pursuit of justice, and the search for one's true self.

Genres:
Historical Fiction, Adventure, Coming-of-Age

Themes:
Identity, Love, Politics, Justice
