Synopsis: In the Transactions of the American Society of Civil Engineers, Vol. LXVIII, Sept. 1910, authors Brown and Hewett delve into the fascinating world of the New York Tunnel Extension of the Pennsylvania Railroad and the North River Tunnels. Through meticulous research and detailed analysis, they uncover the challenges, triumphs, and groundbreaking engineering feats behind these monumental projects. From the intricate planning stages to the awe-inspiring construction process, this paper offers a captivating glimpse into the remarkable world of civil engineering in early 20th century America.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, History

Themes: Infrastructure development, Engineering innovation, Transportation advancements
