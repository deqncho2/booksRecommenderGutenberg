Synopsis: In "Alcohol and the Human Brain," Joseph Cook takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate relationship between alcohol and our most complex organ. From the initial effects of alcohol on the brain to the long-term consequences of excessive consumption, Cook delves into the fascinating science behind this ubiquitous substance. With a blend of scientific research, personal anecdotes, and thought-provoking insights, this book sheds light on the profound impact alcohol has on our cognitive abilities, emotions, and overall mental well-being. Prepare to be enlightened and challenged as you uncover the secrets of alcohol's influence on the human brain.

Genres: Science, Psychology, Non-fiction

Themes: Substance abuse, Neurobiology, Mental health
