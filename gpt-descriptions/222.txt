Synopsis:
In 'The Moon and Sixpence', Charles Strickland, a middle-aged stockbroker, abruptly abandons his comfortable life in London to pursue his passion for painting in Paris. Ignoring societal expectations and the disapproval of those around him, Strickland embarks on a tumultuous journey of self-discovery and artistic exploration. As his unconventional choices lead him to exotic locales and encounters with fascinating characters, the reader is drawn into a captivating tale of obsession, sacrifice, and the pursuit of artistic genius.

Genres:
Literary Fiction, Biographical Fiction, Psychological Drama

Themes:
Artistic Passion, Individualism, Society's Expectations, Sacrifice, Identity
