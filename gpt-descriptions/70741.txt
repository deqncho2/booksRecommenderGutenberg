Synopsis: 
"A Decade of Negro Extension Work, 1914-1924" by O.B. Martin is a captivating historical account that delves into the transformative efforts made by African American leaders during a crucial period in American history. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, Martin explores the challenges faced by the Black community and the tireless work undertaken to uplift and empower African Americans across the nation. From educational initiatives to economic development, this book sheds light on the remarkable achievements and resilience of a generation determined to overcome adversity and build a brighter future.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, African American studies, Social history

Themes: Civil rights, Community empowerment, Education, Economic development
