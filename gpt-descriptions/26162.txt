Synopsis: In "Peking Dust" by Ellen N. La Motte, a young American nurse finds herself in the midst of the chaos and turmoil of war-torn China during the early 20th century. As she navigates the treacherous streets of Peking, she becomes entangled in a web of political intrigue, forbidden love, and personal sacrifice. With vivid descriptions and a gripping narrative, this novel takes readers on a captivating journey through a city on the brink of collapse.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Romance

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, cultural clashes, love and sacrifice
