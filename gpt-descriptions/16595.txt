Synopsis: In "Charles Dickens and Music," James T. Lightwood delves into the fascinating relationship between the renowned author and the world of music. Through meticulous research and captivating anecdotes, Lightwood uncovers the profound influence that music had on Dickens' life and literary works. From his early days as a struggling writer to his rise to fame, Dickens' encounters with musicians, composers, and performers shaped his creative process and added depth to his characters. This insightful exploration offers a unique perspective on the intersection of literature and music, shedding light on the hidden melodies within Dickens' timeless tales.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Music History

Themes: Artistic inspiration, Creative process, Interdisciplinary connections
