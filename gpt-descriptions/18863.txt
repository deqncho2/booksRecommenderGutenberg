Synopsis: 
In 'The Loom of Youth' by Alec Waugh, we are introduced to a group of young boys at a prestigious British boarding school. As they navigate the challenges of adolescence, friendships are formed, rivalries are ignited, and secrets are unveiled. With a backdrop of academic pressures, social hierarchies, and the complexities of growing up, the boys must confront their own desires and the expectations placed upon them. Through a series of captivating events, the story explores the delicate balance between conformity and individuality, as well as the consequences of youthful indiscretions.

Genres: Coming-of-age, Bildungsroman, School story

Themes: Identity, Friendship, Conformity, Rebellion
