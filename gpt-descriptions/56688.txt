Synopsis: 

'Handbook of Railroad Construction; For the use of American engineers' is a comprehensive guide that provides essential rules, tables, and formulas for the location, construction, equipment, and management of railroads in the United States. Written by George L. Vose, this invaluable resource offers a wealth of knowledge for engineers involved in the planning and execution of railroad projects. From the intricacies of track layout to the selection of appropriate equipment, this handbook covers all aspects of railroad construction, ensuring that engineers have the necessary tools to successfully complete their projects.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, Reference

Themes: Railroad construction, Engineering principles, Project management
