Synopsis: 

In "Sporting Dogs: Their Points and Management in Health and Disease," author Frank Townend Barton takes readers on an enthralling journey into the world of sporting dogs. Through his extensive knowledge and experience, Barton delves into the intricacies of managing these remarkable canines, exploring their unique points and providing invaluable insights into their health and disease management. From training techniques to nutrition, breeding to exercise, this book offers a comprehensive guide for both novice and seasoned dog owners alike. With captivating anecdotes and practical advice, Barton's work is a must-read for anyone passionate about sporting dogs and their well-being.

Genres: Non-fiction, Animal Care, Sports

Themes: Canine health, Dog training, Sporting dog management, Animal welfare
