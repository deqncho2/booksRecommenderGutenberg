Synopsis: 

In 'Den Hunting as a Means of Coyote Control', Young and Dobyns take readers on a thrilling journey into the world of coyote hunting. Through vivid storytelling and expert analysis, they explore the age-old practice of den hunting and its effectiveness in controlling the coyote population. From the adrenaline-pumping chase to the strategic methods employed by hunters, this book offers a captivating glimpse into the world of predator control. As readers delve into the pages, they will uncover the secrets of successful den hunting and gain a deeper understanding of the complex relationship between humans and coyotes.

Genres: Non-fiction, Outdoor literature, Wildlife management

Themes: Predator control, Wildlife conservation, Human-wildlife interactions
