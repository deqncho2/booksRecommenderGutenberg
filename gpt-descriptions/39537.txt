Synopsis: 

In 'Medieval English Nunneries c. 1275 to 1535', Eileen Power delves into the fascinating world of medieval English nunneries, uncovering the hidden lives and experiences of the women who resided within their walls. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Power explores the daily routines, spiritual practices, and social dynamics of these religious communities, shedding light on the challenges and triumphs faced by the nuns during this tumultuous period in history. From the struggles against patriarchal authority to the pursuit of intellectual and artistic endeavors, this book offers a captivating glimpse into the lives of these remarkable women.

Genres: History, Women's Studies, Religious Studies

Themes: Gender and Power, Religious Life, Social History
