Synopsis: 

In "Germany's Dishonoured Army: Additional records of German atrocities in France" by J.H. Morgan, a shocking and deeply disturbing account of the German army's actions during World War I is revealed. Through meticulous research and firsthand testimonies, Morgan uncovers a series of previously undisclosed atrocities committed by the German soldiers in France. From brutal massacres to acts of unspeakable cruelty, the book exposes the dark underbelly of war and the devastating impact it had on innocent civilians. As the evidence mounts, the reader is left questioning the true nature of humanity and the depths to which it can sink in times of conflict.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: War crimes, Morality, Human nature
