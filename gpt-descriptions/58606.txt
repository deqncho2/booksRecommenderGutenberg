Synopsis: In 'An Essay on Laughter: Its Forms, Its Causes, Its Development and Its Value' by James Sully, the author delves into the fascinating world of laughter, exploring its various forms, the reasons behind its occurrence, how it evolves throughout our lives, and the significance it holds in our daily experiences. Through a combination of scientific research, philosophical musings, and insightful anecdotes, Sully invites readers on a thought-provoking journey that unveils the multifaceted nature of laughter and its profound impact on our well-being and social interactions.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Philosophy

Themes: Humor, Emotions, Social Interaction, Well-being
