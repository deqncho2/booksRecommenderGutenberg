Synopsis: 
In "The Forgotten Man, and Other Essays," William Graham Sumner takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through a collection of essays that challenge conventional wisdom and shed light on the forgotten individuals in society. From exploring the impact of social reform to dissecting the complexities of economic systems, Sumner's essays delve into the often overlooked aspects of human existence. With a keen eye for detail and a sharp wit, he presents a compelling argument for the importance of individual liberty and personal responsibility. Through his insightful analysis, Sumner encourages readers to question the prevailing narratives and consider the unintended consequences of well-intentioned policies. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Political philosophy, Social commentary

Themes: Individualism, Social reform, Economic systems, Personal responsibility
