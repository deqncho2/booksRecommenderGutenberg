Synopsis: In 'The Queen of Farrandale: A Novel' by Clara Louise Burnham, a young woman named Margaret finds herself thrust into a world of wealth and privilege when she unexpectedly inherits a grand estate in the small town of Farrandale. As she navigates the complexities of her new role as the queen of Farrandale, Margaret must confront family secrets, societal expectations, and the challenges of love and loyalty. With a cast of intriguing characters and a captivating setting, this novel explores the transformative power of self-discovery and the choices we make in pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Romance

Themes: Identity, Social class, Love and relationships
