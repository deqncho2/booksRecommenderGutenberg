Synopsis: 

In 'Broken Homes: A Study of Family Desertion and its Social Treatment', Joanna C. Colcord delves into the heart-wrenching issue of family desertion and its impact on society. Through a meticulous examination of real-life cases, Colcord uncovers the complex web of reasons behind family desertion, exploring the emotional, economic, and societal factors that contribute to this heartbreaking phenomenon. With a compassionate and insightful approach, the author sheds light on the struggles faced by both the deserting individuals and the abandoned families, offering a thought-provoking analysis of the social treatment surrounding this issue. 

Genres: Sociology, Social Sciences, Non-fiction

Themes: Family dynamics, Social responsibility, Economic inequality
