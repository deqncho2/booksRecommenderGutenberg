Synopsis: 

"The Bitter Cry of Outcast London: An Inquiry into the Condition of the Abject Poor" takes readers on a gripping journey through the dark underbelly of Victorian London. Written by Mearns and Preston, this eye-opening work delves into the lives of the destitute and marginalized, shedding light on the harsh realities faced by the city's most vulnerable inhabitants. Through vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, the authors expose the stark contrast between the opulence of the upper classes and the abject poverty endured by those on the fringes of society. As they delve deeper into the lives of the outcasts, the authors uncover shocking truths and reveal the systemic issues that perpetuate their suffering. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical investigation

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Urban decay, Class divide
