Synopsis: 
'A Literary History of Ireland, from Earliest Times to the Present Day' by Douglas Hyde takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse literary heritage of Ireland. From ancient myths and legends to modern poetry and prose, Hyde explores the evolution of Irish literature, shedding light on the cultural, social, and political influences that have shaped it. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, the book unveils the hidden gems of Irish literary tradition, introducing readers to renowned authors and lesser-known voices alike. As the narrative unfolds, readers will discover the power of storytelling as a means of preserving history, expressing identity, and challenging societal norms. 

Genres: Literary Criticism, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Irish identity, Cultural heritage, Literary tradition, Social and political influences, Power of storytelling
