Synopsis: In "The Kiss and Its History," Kristoffer Nyrop takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the significance and symbolism behind one of the most intimate gestures known to humanity. From ancient civilizations to modern society, Nyrop delves into the various cultural, social, and emotional dimensions of the kiss, unraveling its mysteries and unveiling its power to transcend language and connect souls. Through vivid anecdotes and historical accounts, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the kiss and its profound impact on human relationships.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Love, Intimacy, Communication, Symbolism
