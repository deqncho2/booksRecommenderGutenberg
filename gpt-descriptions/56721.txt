Synopsis: In 'On Mr. Spencer's Data of Ethics' by Malcolm Guthrie, the reader is taken on a thought-provoking journey through the philosophical ideas of Herbert Spencer. As Guthrie delves into Spencer's theories on ethics, he challenges the reader to question their own moral compass and consider the implications of Spencer's controversial viewpoints. With captivating arguments and insightful analysis, Guthrie presents a compelling exploration of ethics that will leave readers pondering the complexities of right and wrong.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Ethics, Morality, Philosophical Inquiry
