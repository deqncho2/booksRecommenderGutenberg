Synopsis: In 'The Expositor's Bible: The Second Book of Kings', Farrar delves into the captivating narratives of the kings of Israel and Judah, exploring their triumphs, failures, and the divine intervention that shaped their destinies. From the rise and fall of powerful monarchs to the prophetic voices that guided them, this book offers a compelling account of the political and spiritual struggles of ancient Israel. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, Farrar brings to life the complex tapestry of power, faith, and human nature that unfolds in the pages of this biblical book.

Genres: Biblical commentary, religious literature, historical analysis

Themes: Divine intervention, leadership, faith, prophecy, political intrigue
