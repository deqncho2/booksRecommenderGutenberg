Synopsis: 
'The Field and Garden Vegetables of America: Containing Full Descriptions of Nearly Eleven Hundred Species and Varieties; With Directions for Propagation, Culture and Use' is a comprehensive guidebook that explores the vast world of vegetables in America. Written by Burr, Fearing, this book provides detailed descriptions of nearly eleven hundred species and varieties of vegetables, along with instructions on how to propagate, cultivate, and utilize them. From common vegetables to rare and exotic ones, this guidebook offers a wealth of knowledge for both amateur and experienced gardeners, as well as anyone interested in the diverse world of vegetables.

Genres: Non-fiction, Gardening, Reference

Themes: Horticulture, Plant Propagation, Culinary Uses of Vegetables
