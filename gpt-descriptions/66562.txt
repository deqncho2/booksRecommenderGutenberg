Synopsis: 

'Mission Work among the Negroes and the Indians: What Is Being Accomplished by Means of the Annual Collection Taken Up for Our Missions' explores the remarkable efforts of the Catholic Church's Commission for Catholic Missions among the Colored People and the Indians. This insightful work delves into the various missions undertaken by the Church to uplift and support these marginalized communities. Through vivid accounts and inspiring anecdotes, the book sheds light on the incredible accomplishments achieved through the annual collection taken up for these missions. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious, Historical

Themes: Missionary work, Social justice, Empowerment
