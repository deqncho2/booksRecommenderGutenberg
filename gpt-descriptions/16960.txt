Synopsis: In "History of the United States," Charles A. Beard and Mary Ritter Beard take readers on a captivating journey through the nation's past, uncovering hidden narratives and challenging conventional historical interpretations. From the early colonial period to the tumultuous events of the 20th century, the authors delve into the complex web of political, economic, and social forces that shaped the United States. With meticulous research and thought-provoking analysis, they shed light on the struggles for power, the impact of economic interests, and the evolving role of ordinary citizens in shaping the nation's destiny. This groundbreaking work offers a fresh perspective on American history, inviting readers to question long-held assumptions and discover the untold stories that have shaped the nation.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and politics, Economic interests, Social change, Historical interpretation
