Synopsis: 

In "McGill and its Story, 1821-1921" by Cyrus MacMillan, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the rich history of McGill University. From its humble beginnings as a small college in Montreal to its transformation into one of Canada's most prestigious institutions, this book delves into the fascinating stories, triumphs, and challenges that shaped McGill over its first century.

Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, MacMillan brings to life the visionaries, educators, and students who played pivotal roles in McGill's growth and development. From the struggles faced by the early founders to the groundbreaking discoveries made by its renowned faculty, the book paints a vivid picture of McGill's evolution and its impact on Canadian society.

As the narrative unfolds, readers will be engrossed by the tales of resilience, innovation, and intellectual pursuit that have defined McGill's journey. From the establishment of groundbreaking programs to the university's involvement in pivotal historical events, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of McGill's contributions to academia, culture, and society.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Education

Themes: History, Education, Progress, Resilience, Legacy
