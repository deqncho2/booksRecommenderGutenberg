Synopsis: In "Shakespeare's Bones: The Proposal to Disinter Them, Considered in Relation to Their Possible Bearing on His Portraiture: Illustrated by Instances of Visits of the Living to the Dead," C. M. Ingleby delves into the intriguing proposal of exhuming Shakespeare's bones and explores the potential impact on his portraiture. Through captivating instances of visits from the living to the dead, Ingleby presents a thought-provoking analysis that will leave readers questioning the mysteries surrounding the Bard's life and art.

Genres: Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, Biography

Themes: Shakespearean authorship, Portraiture, Death and immortality
