Synopsis: 

'From India to the planet Mars: A study of a case of somnambulism with glossolalia' by Flournoy, Théodore is a captivating exploration of a mysterious case that takes the reader on a mesmerizing journey. The story revolves around a young woman named Hélène Smith, who exhibits extraordinary abilities while in a state of somnambulism. As she delves into trances, Hélène claims to have vivid memories of past lives on Mars, speaking in an unknown language and sketching intricate Martian landscapes. 

Flournoy, a renowned psychologist, becomes fascinated by Hélène's case and embarks on a quest to unravel the truth behind her experiences. Through meticulous analysis and deep investigation, he attempts to decipher the enigmatic language and understand the connection between Hélène's visions and her subconscious mind. As the narrative unfolds, the reader is drawn into a world of mystery, psychology, and the exploration of the human psyche.

Genres: Psychological thriller, Mystery, Non-fiction

Themes: Reincarnation, Subconscious mind, Extraordinary abilities, Exploration of the unknown
