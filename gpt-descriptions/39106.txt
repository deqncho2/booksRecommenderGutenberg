Synopsis: 

'Creation Myths of Primitive America: In relation to the Religious History and Mental Development of Mankind' by Curtin, Jeremiah explores the fascinating world of creation myths from various indigenous cultures in America. From the ancient stories of the Aztecs and the Mayans to the legends of the Native American tribes, this book delves into the rich tapestry of beliefs and traditions that shaped the religious history and mental development of mankind in primitive America. Through a meticulous examination of these myths, Curtin uncovers the profound insights they offer into the human psyche and the universal questions of existence.

Genres: Mythology, Anthropology, History

Themes: Creation, Religion, Cultural Diversity, Human Psychology
