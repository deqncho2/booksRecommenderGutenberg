Synopsis: In 'Military Reminiscences of the Civil War, Volume 2: November 1863-June 1865', Jacob D. Cox takes readers on a captivating journey through the final years of the American Civil War. Through his vivid and personal recollections, Cox provides a unique perspective on the major battles, strategies, and key figures that shaped this pivotal period in American history. From the intense fighting at Chattanooga to the decisive campaigns that led to the fall of Richmond, Cox's firsthand accounts offer a gripping and insightful narrative of the war's final stages.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Military history

Themes: War and its impact, Leadership and strategy, Personal experiences in conflict
