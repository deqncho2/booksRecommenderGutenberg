Synopsis: 
In 'Peter Plymley's Letters, and Selected Essays', Smith, Sydney takes on the role of a witty and satirical observer, addressing a range of pressing social and political issues of his time. Through a series of letters and essays, he tackles topics such as religious intolerance, political corruption, and the absurdities of war. With his sharp and humorous writing style, Smith offers insightful commentary on the state of society, challenging readers to question the status quo and consider alternative perspectives.

Genres: Satire, Political Commentary, Essay Collection

Themes: Social Criticism, Religious Tolerance, Political Corruption, War and its Absurdities
