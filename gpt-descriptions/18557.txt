Synopsis: In "The Battle of Principles: A Study of the Heroism and Eloquence of the Anti-Slavery Conflict," Newell Dwight Hillis delves into the captivating world of the anti-slavery movement, exploring the remarkable heroism and powerful rhetoric that shaped this pivotal period in American history. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Hillis brings to life the courageous individuals who fought against the institution of slavery, highlighting their unwavering dedication and the profound impact they had on society. This thought-provoking study offers a compelling examination of the moral and intellectual battles that defined the anti-slavery conflict, shedding light on the enduring power of principles and the transformative potential of collective action.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Social justice

Themes: Heroism, Eloquence, Anti-slavery movement, Moral principles, Social change
