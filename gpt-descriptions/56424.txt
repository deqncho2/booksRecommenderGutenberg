Synopsis: 

In "Shores of the Polar Sea: A Narrative of the Arctic Expedition of 1875-6," Edward L. Moss takes readers on a thrilling journey to the icy and treacherous Arctic. Joining a daring expedition, Moss recounts the harrowing experiences, breathtaking landscapes, and encounters with indigenous peoples that he and his fellow explorers face as they push the boundaries of human exploration. As they navigate through the unforgiving polar sea, battling extreme weather conditions and the constant threat of danger, the expedition becomes a test of their resilience, courage, and determination. Will they conquer the challenges that lie ahead and uncover the secrets hidden within the frozen wilderness?

Genres: Adventure, Non-fiction, Travelogue

Themes: Exploration, Survival, Human resilience, Cultural encounters
