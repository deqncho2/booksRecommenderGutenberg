Synopsis: In 'The Coward: A Novel of Society and the Field in 1863', Morford, Henry takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous times of the American Civil War. Set against the backdrop of both high society and the battlefield, this gripping novel follows the life of a young man torn between his duty to his country and his own fears. As he navigates the complexities of love, honor, and courage, the reader is drawn into a world of secrets, betrayals, and unexpected alliances. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, 'The Coward' offers a unique perspective on the human experience during a time of great turmoil.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Romance

Themes: Courage, Identity, Love and Sacrifice
