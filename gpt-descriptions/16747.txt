Synopsis: 

'A Canadian Manor and Its Seigneurs: The Story of a Hundred Years, 1761-1861' takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of a Canadian manor and the influential families who inhabited it. From the early days of settlement to the tumultuous years of political change, this book delves into the lives, struggles, and triumphs of the manor's seigneurs and their impact on the surrounding community. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, George McKinnon Wrong paints a vivid picture of a bygone era, filled with intrigue, romance, and the ever-present quest for power.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Canadian history

Themes: Power and influence, Family dynamics, Social change, Historical legacy
