Synopsis: 

In 'Niagara: An Aboriginal Center of Trade', Peter A. Porter takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of Niagara, revealing a hidden world of trade, culture, and power. From the bustling markets to the majestic waterfalls, Porter uncovers the untold stories of the indigenous peoples who once thrived in this vibrant center of commerce. As he delves into the intricate web of alliances, rivalries, and negotiations, a fascinating tapestry of ancient traditions and modern ambitions unfolds. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Porter paints a vivid picture of a forgotten era, where the convergence of cultures shaped the destiny of a land.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Anthropology, Indigenous Studies

Themes: Indigenous history, Trade and commerce, Cultural exchange, Power dynamics
