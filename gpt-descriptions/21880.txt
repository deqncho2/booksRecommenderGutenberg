Synopsis: 

In 'The Medallic History of the United States of America 1776-1876', Joseph Florimond Loubat takes readers on a captivating journey through the first century of American history, using an unconventional and fascinating lens - medals. This meticulously researched and beautifully illustrated book explores the significance of medals as historical artifacts, shedding light on the events, individuals, and ideals that shaped the nation.

From the Revolutionary War to the Centennial Exhibition, Loubat delves into the stories behind the creation and distribution of these commemorative pieces, revealing the intricate details and symbolism embedded within each medal. Through his vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, he brings to life the struggles, triumphs, and defining moments of America's past, offering a unique perspective on the nation's development.

Genres: History, Art, Numismatics

Themes: American history, symbolism, commemoration, national identity
