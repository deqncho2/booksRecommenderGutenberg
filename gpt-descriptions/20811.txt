Synopsis: 

In "Two Decades: A History of the First Twenty Years' Work of the Woman's Christian Temperance Union of the State of New York," authors Georgeanna M. Gardenier and Frances W. Graham take readers on a captivating journey through the early years of the influential Woman's Christian Temperance Union (WCTU) in New York. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, the authors shed light on the struggles, triumphs, and transformative impact of this pioneering organization. From the passionate speeches and tireless activism of its members to the challenges they faced in a male-dominated society, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the birth of a powerful movement that sought to combat the destructive effects of alcohol and promote social reform. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Women's history, Social activism

Themes: Women's empowerment, Social reform, Prohibition, Activism
