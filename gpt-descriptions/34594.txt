Synopsis: 

In "Fugitive Slaves (1619-1865)" by Marion Gleason McDougall, embark on a gripping journey through the tumultuous history of enslaved individuals who dared to escape their captors. From the earliest days of slavery in America to the Civil War era, this meticulously researched account unveils the harrowing stories of those who risked everything for freedom. Through vivid narratives and compelling anecdotes, McDougall sheds light on the resilience, courage, and resourcefulness of these fugitive slaves, as well as the complex network of abolitionists and allies who aided them in their quest for liberation. 

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, African American Studies

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Resistance, Human Rights
