Synopsis: 

In "Phiz" (Hablot Knight Browne), a Memoir, Frederic George Kitton delves into the fascinating life of the renowned Victorian artist and illustrator, Hablot Knight Browne, better known by his pen name "Phiz." From his humble beginnings to his rise as one of the most celebrated illustrators of his time, this memoir offers a captivating glimpse into the world of a talented artist who brought characters to life through his intricate and evocative drawings. Kitton's narrative takes us on a journey through Browne's collaborations with Charles Dickens, his struggles and triumphs, and the lasting impact he left on the world of art and literature.

Genres: Biography, Art, History

Themes: Artistic expression, Collaboration, Victorian society
