Synopsis: In '100 New Yorkers of the 1970s', Max Millard takes us on a captivating journey through the vibrant and diverse cityscape of New York during a transformative decade. Through a collection of intimate and revealing interviews, Millard introduces us to a hundred fascinating individuals who shaped the cultural, artistic, and social fabric of the city. From iconic musicians and artists to activists and everyday New Yorkers, each person's story offers a unique glimpse into the struggles, triumphs, and dreams that defined this tumultuous era. As we delve into their lives, we witness the resilience, creativity, and resilience that emerged from a city on the brink of bankruptcy, grappling with crime, and undergoing profound social change. '100 New Yorkers of the 1970s' is a captivating exploration of the human spirit and the power of community in the face of adversity.

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, History

Themes: Cultural transformation, Urban life, Resilience, Social change
