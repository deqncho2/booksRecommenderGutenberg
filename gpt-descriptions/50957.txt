Synopsis: In 'Man and the Glacial Period', G. Frederick Wright takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the fascinating relationship between humanity and the ancient glacial period. Through a combination of scientific research, geological evidence, and thought-provoking analysis, Wright unveils a compelling narrative that challenges conventional wisdom and sheds light on the profound impact of the glacial period on human civilization. As he delves into the mysteries of the past, the author presents a compelling argument that will leave readers questioning their understanding of history and the world around them.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: Human evolution, Climate change, Geological impact on civilization
