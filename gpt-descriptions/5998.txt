Synopsis:
In 'Waverley; Or, 'Tis Sixty Years Since' by Walter Scott, a young Englishman named Edward Waverley finds himself caught up in the tumultuous events of the Jacobite uprising in 18th-century Scotland. As he navigates the treacherous world of divided loyalties and political intrigue, Edward becomes entangled with both the Highland clans and the British army, torn between his own desires and the expectations of his family. With vivid descriptions of the Scottish landscape and a cast of memorable characters, this historical novel takes readers on a thrilling journey through a pivotal moment in British history.

Genres: Historical fiction, Adventure, Romance

Themes: Loyalty, Identity, Conflict, Honor
