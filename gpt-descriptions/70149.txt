Synopsis: 

'Under England's Flag: From 1804-1809' is a captivating memoir, diary, and correspondence compiled by the last surviving members of Charles Boothy's family. The book takes readers on a remarkable journey through the life of Captain Charles Boothy, a distinguished member of the Royal Engineers during the early 19th century. Through his personal accounts, readers are immersed in the tumultuous events and adventures that shaped his life, providing a unique perspective on the historical context of the time. From his military exploits to his encounters with influential figures, the memoirs offer a vivid portrayal of a man dedicated to serving his country under the flag of England.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Memoir, Military History

Themes: Patriotism, Duty, Personal Sacrifice, Historical Events
