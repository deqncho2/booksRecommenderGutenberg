Synopsis: In 'Ten Thousand a-Year. Volume 1.' by Samuel Warren, we are introduced to the intriguing world of William Tressilian, a young man who unexpectedly inherits a substantial fortune. As he navigates his newfound wealth, Tressilian encounters a series of unexpected challenges and temptations that threaten to unravel his life. From the bustling streets of London to the opulent estates of the aristocracy, this gripping tale takes readers on a journey filled with mystery, romance, and moral dilemmas. As Tressilian grapples with the complexities of his new lifestyle, he must confront the true meaning of wealth and the consequences it brings.

Genres: Fiction, Mystery, Romance

Themes: Wealth and its consequences, Morality, Social class
