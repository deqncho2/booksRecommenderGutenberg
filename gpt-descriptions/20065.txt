Synopsis: 

In 'Twenty Years of Congress, Volume 2: From Lincoln to Garfield, with a Review of the Events Which Led to the Political Revolution of 1860', James Gillespie Blaine takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of American history from the presidency of Abraham Lincoln to the assassination of James A. Garfield. Blaine, a prominent politician and historian, provides a detailed account of the political landscape, shedding light on the events that shaped the nation during this critical era. From the Civil War and Reconstruction to the rise of industrialization and the challenges faced by the nation, Blaine's narrative offers a comprehensive and engaging exploration of the political revolution that unfolded in the mid-19th century.

Genres: History, Political Science, Biography

Themes: American politics, Civil War, Reconstruction, Industrialization, Political revolution
