Synopsis: 
In 'How We Think', John Dewey takes readers on a captivating journey into the depths of human cognition and the art of intelligent thinking. Through insightful anecdotes and thought-provoking examples, Dewey explores the various ways in which our minds process information, make connections, and form ideas. From the power of curiosity to the importance of reflection, this book challenges readers to reconsider their own thinking processes and embrace a more active and engaged approach to learning. Prepare to be inspired and enlightened as Dewey unveils the secrets behind our most profound thoughts and offers practical strategies for enhancing our intellectual abilities.

Genres: Philosophy, Education, Psychology

Themes: Critical thinking, Inquiry-based learning, Reflective thinking, Intellectual growth
