Synopsis: In "Landmarks in Russian Literature," Maurice Baring takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich tapestry of Russian literary masterpieces. From the enchanting tales of Pushkin to the profound introspection of Dostoevsky, Baring delves into the lives and works of the greatest Russian writers, revealing the cultural and historical context that shaped their genius. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the literary landmarks that have defined Russian literature for centuries.

Genres: Literary criticism, Biography, History

Themes: Russian culture, Literary movements, Artistic expression, Historical context
