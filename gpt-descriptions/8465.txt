Synopsis: In 'The Russian Revolution; The Jugo-Slav Movement', Golder, Harper, Kerner, and Petrunkevitch delve into the tumultuous events of the Russian Revolution and the lesser-known Jugo-Slav Movement. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, they bring to life the political upheaval, social unrest, and ideological clashes that shaped these pivotal moments in history. From the rise of Lenin and the Bolsheviks to the struggles of the Jugo-Slav people for self-determination, this book offers a gripping account of revolution and nationalism in early 20th-century Europe.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Biography

Themes: Revolution, Nationalism, Ideological conflict, Self-determination
