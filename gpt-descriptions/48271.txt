Synopsis: In 'Old People and the Things That Pass' by Louis Couperus, we are introduced to the Van Lowe family, a wealthy and respected Dutch family living in The Hague. As the story unfolds, we witness the complex dynamics and hidden secrets that lie beneath the surface of their seemingly perfect lives. Through the eyes of the family's patriarch, we delve into the themes of love, betrayal, and the inevitable passage of time, as the characters navigate the challenges of aging and the consequences of their choices.

Genres: Literary fiction, family drama, psychological fiction

Themes: Love and relationships, family dynamics, secrets and betrayal, the passage of time
