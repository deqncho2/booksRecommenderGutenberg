Synopsis: 
In 'Serpent-Worship, and Other Essays, with a Chapter on Totemism', C. Staniland Wake delves into the fascinating world of ancient religious practices and beliefs. From the mysterious worship of serpents to the enigmatic concept of totemism, this collection of essays offers a captivating exploration of these ancient rituals and their significance in human history. With meticulous research and thought-provoking analysis, Wake uncovers the hidden connections between these seemingly disparate practices, shedding light on the profound influence they had on early civilizations. Prepare to be enthralled by the secrets of serpent-worship and totemism, as Wake takes you on a journey through the intriguing tapestry of ancient religious traditions.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Ancient religions, Rituals and beliefs, Cultural significance
