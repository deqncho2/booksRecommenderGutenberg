Synopsis: In 'On English Poetry: Being an Irregular Approach to the Psychology of This Art, from Evidence Mainly Subjective', Robert Graves takes readers on a captivating journey into the depths of English poetry. Through his unique and unconventional perspective, Graves delves into the psychology behind this art form, exploring the intricate connections between the poet's mind and the words they create. With a blend of personal anecdotes, insightful analysis, and thought-provoking observations, Graves offers a fresh and engaging exploration of the power and beauty of English poetry.

Genres: Literary criticism, Poetry analysis, Psychology

Themes: The psychology of poetry, Artistic expression, Subjectivity in interpretation
