Synopsis: 

'Life and public services of Martin R. Delany: Sub-Assistant Commissioner Bureau Relief of Refugees, Freedmen, and of Abandoned Lands, and late Major 104th U.S. Colored Troops' by Frank A. Rollin is a captivating biography that delves into the remarkable life and achievements of Martin R. Delany. From his role as a Sub-Assistant Commissioner in the Bureau of Relief of Refugees, Freedmen, and of Abandoned Lands to his service as a Major in the 104th U.S. Colored Troops, Delany's journey is one of resilience, determination, and unwavering commitment to the cause of freedom. This book offers a fascinating exploration of Delany's contributions to the African American community during a pivotal period in American history.

Genres: Biography, History, African American Studies

Themes: Civil Rights, Leadership, Identity, Resilience
