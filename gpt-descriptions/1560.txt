Synopsis: In "The San Francisco Calamity by Earthquake and Fire," Morris, Charles takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most devastating natural disasters in American history. Set against the backdrop of the 1906 San Francisco earthquake and subsequent fire, this non-fiction account delves into the chaos, destruction, and resilience of a city in crisis. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, Morris paints a vivid picture of the harrowing events that unfolded during those fateful days, capturing the human spirit and the indomitable will to rebuild amidst unimaginable devastation.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Disaster

Themes: Natural disasters, Resilience, Community, Survival
