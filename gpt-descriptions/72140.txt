Synopsis: In "The Home-Maker" by Dorothy Canfield Fisher, we are introduced to a seemingly ordinary family living in a small town. However, their lives take an unexpected turn when the roles of the husband and wife are reversed. As the husband becomes the homemaker and the wife enters the workforce, their unconventional arrangement challenges societal norms and forces them to confront their own desires and aspirations. This thought-provoking and emotionally charged novel explores the complexities of gender roles, the pursuit of happiness, and the power of self-discovery.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Social Commentary

Themes: Gender roles, Identity, Family dynamics, Societal expectations
