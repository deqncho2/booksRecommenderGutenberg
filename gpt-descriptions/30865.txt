Synopsis: 
In "My Three Years in America," Johann Heinrich, Graf von Bernstorff takes readers on a captivating journey through his experiences as a German ambassador in the United States during World War I. From his arrival in Washington D.C. to his encounters with influential figures such as President Woodrow Wilson, Bernstorff provides a unique perspective on the political and social landscape of America during this tumultuous period. As he navigates the challenges of diplomacy and attempts to maintain German-American relations, the reader is drawn into a world of intrigue, international tensions, and personal reflections. With vivid descriptions and insightful observations, Bernstorff's memoir offers a compelling glimpse into a pivotal moment in history.

Genres: Memoir, History, Politics

Themes: Diplomacy, International Relations, World War I
