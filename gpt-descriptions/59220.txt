Synopsis: 
In 'Trail-Tales of Western Canada', F. A. Robinson takes readers on an exhilarating journey through the untamed landscapes of Western Canada. From the rugged mountains to the vast prairies, this collection of captivating tales introduces a diverse cast of characters, each with their own unique story to tell. From daring explorers and resilient pioneers to indigenous peoples and wild animals, the author weaves together a tapestry of adventure, hardship, and triumph. As readers delve into these tales, they will be transported to a time when the West was still a frontier, and the spirit of exploration and resilience reigned supreme.

Genres: Adventure, Historical Fiction, Short Stories

Themes: Exploration, Resilience, Frontier Life
