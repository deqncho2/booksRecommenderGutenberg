Synopsis:
In "The life and times of the Rev. John Wesley, M.A., founder of the Methodists," author Luke Tyerman delves into the captivating life of one of the most influential figures in religious history. This first volume of the three-part biography takes readers on a journey through the early years of John Wesley, exploring his upbringing, education, and the events that led to his spiritual awakening. From his encounters with the Moravians to his transformative experiences in America, Wesley's life is filled with unexpected twists and turns that shaped his mission to bring about a spiritual revival. Tyerman's meticulous research and vivid storytelling bring to life the struggles, triumphs, and controversies that surrounded Wesley, making this biography a compelling and enlightening read.

Genres: Biography, History, Religious Studies
Themes: Faith and spirituality, Religious revival, Personal transformation
