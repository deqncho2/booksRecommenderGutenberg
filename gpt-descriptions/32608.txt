Synopsis: In 'Some War-time Lessons: The Soldier's Standards of Conduct; The War As a Practical Test of American Scholarship; What Have We Learned?' Frederick P. Keppel explores the profound impact of war on society and the lessons learned during times of conflict. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Keppel delves into the soldier's code of conduct, the role of American scholarship in wartime, and the invaluable knowledge gained from the experience. This insightful work offers a unique perspective on the complexities of war and its lasting effects.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Military

Themes: War, Ethics, Education, Society
