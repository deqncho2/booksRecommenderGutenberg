Synopsis: 

'Pioneers of Evolution from Thales to Huxley: With an Intermediate Chapter on the Causes of Arrest of the Movement' takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of evolutionary thought. Edward Clodd delves into the lives and ideas of influential figures such as Thales, Aristotle, Lamarck, and Darwin, tracing the development of evolutionary theories from ancient times to the Victorian era. Along the way, he explores the obstacles and controversies that hindered the progress of this groundbreaking scientific movement. With insightful analysis and engaging storytelling, Clodd illuminates the intellectual struggles and triumphs that shaped our understanding of evolution.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Science

Themes: Evolutionary theory, Scientific progress, Intellectual history
