Synopsis: 

In 'Civil War Experiences, 1862-1865: Chickamauga, Mission Ridge, Buzzard Roost, Resaca, Rome, New Hope Church, Kenesaw Mountain, Peach Tree Creek, Atlanta, Jonesboro, Averysboro, Bentonville,' Edward Mott Robbins takes readers on a gripping journey through some of the most significant battles and events of the American Civil War. Through vivid and personal accounts, Robbins provides a firsthand perspective of the chaos, bravery, and sacrifice experienced by soldiers on both sides of the conflict. From the blood-soaked fields of Chickamauga to the decisive Battle of Atlanta, the author captures the intensity and human drama of these historic moments, shedding light on the individuals who fought and the impact of their actions.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: War and its consequences, Heroism and sacrifice, The human experience in conflict
