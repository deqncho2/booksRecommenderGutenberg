Synopsis: 

"The Glory That Was Greece: a survey of Hellenic culture and civilization" takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and influential history of ancient Greece. From the birth of democracy to the awe-inspiring achievements in art, literature, and philosophy, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of the brilliance and legacy of the Greek civilization. Delving into the lives of great thinkers, warriors, and artists, Stobart paints a vivid picture of a society that continues to shape our world today.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Democracy, Art and Architecture, Philosophy, Influence and Legacy
