Synopsis: In 'Navaho Houses: Seventeenth Annual Report of the Bureau of Ethnology', Mindeleff takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of Navajo architecture. Through meticulous research and detailed descriptions, he unveils the intricate and fascinating structures that make up Navajo homes. From the construction techniques to the symbolic significance of each element, Mindeleff provides a comprehensive exploration of Navajo houses, shedding light on the rich cultural heritage of the Navajo people.

Genres: Anthropology, Ethnography, Architecture

Themes: Cultural heritage, Architecture and symbolism
