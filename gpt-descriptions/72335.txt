Synopsis: 
In "The Old English Dramatists" by James Russell Lowell, embark on a captivating journey through the world of Elizabethan and Jacobean drama. Lowell delves into the lives and works of renowned playwrights such as Shakespeare, Marlowe, and Jonson, shedding light on their unique writing styles and the societal influences that shaped their plays. From the grand tragedies to the uproarious comedies, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the vibrant and dynamic world of old English theater.

Genres: Literary criticism, Drama, History

Themes: The power of language and storytelling, societal and political influences on art, the evolution of theater as a reflection of society
