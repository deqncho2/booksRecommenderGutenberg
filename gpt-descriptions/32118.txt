Synopsis: In 'Child Versus Parent: Some Chapters on the Irrepressible Conflict in the Home', Stephen S. Wise delves into the complex dynamics between children and their parents, exploring the conflicts that arise and the battles that ensue. Through a series of thought-provoking chapters, Wise examines the power struggles, misunderstandings, and clashes of wills that often occur within the family unit. With insightful anecdotes and astute observations, the author sheds light on the timeless struggle between generations, offering a fresh perspective on the age-old question of who truly holds the reins in the home.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Family

Themes: Parent-child relationships, Power dynamics, Communication, Conflict resolution
