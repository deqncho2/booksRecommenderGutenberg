Synopsis: 

'A Diary Kept by Mrs. R. C. Germon, at Lucknow, Between the Months of May and December, 1857' is a captivating memoir that takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the Indian Rebellion of 1857. Written by Maria Germon, the wife of a British officer stationed in Lucknow, this diary offers a firsthand account of the harrowing experiences and challenges faced by the British residents during the uprising.

As the rebellion unfolds, Mrs. Germon's diary becomes a window into the chaos, fear, and resilience of those caught in the midst of the conflict. Through her vivid descriptions, readers witness the siege of Lucknow, the constant threat of violence, and the desperate struggle for survival. Mrs. Germon's diary also provides a glimpse into the relationships and camaraderie that develop among the British residents as they band together to face the overwhelming odds.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: War and Conflict, Survival, Resilience, Community
