Synopsis: 

In "Trout Flies of Devon and Cornwall, and When and How to Use Them," George W. Soltau takes us on a captivating journey into the world of fly fishing in the picturesque regions of Devon and Cornwall. With his wealth of knowledge and expertise, Soltau delves into the art of crafting and selecting the perfect trout flies, revealing the secrets behind their effectiveness and the best times and techniques to employ them. Through vivid descriptions and insightful anecdotes, he paints a vivid picture of the rivers, lakes, and streams that teem with trout, enticing readers to explore these idyllic fishing spots for themselves. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Sports, Outdoor Recreation

Themes: Fly fishing, Trout fishing, Nature exploration
