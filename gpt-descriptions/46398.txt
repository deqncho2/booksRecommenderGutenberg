Synopsis: In 'The Manchester Rebels of the Fatal '45' by Ainsworth, William Harrison, a group of rebels in Manchester during the Jacobite uprising of 1745 find themselves caught in a dangerous game of loyalty, betrayal, and love. As tensions rise and the rebellion gains momentum, the rebels must navigate treacherous paths, risking their lives and relationships in the pursuit of freedom. With vivid historical detail and captivating characters, this thrilling tale will transport readers to a time of political unrest and personal sacrifice.

Genres: Historical fiction, Adventure, Romance

Themes: Loyalty, Betrayal, Rebellion, Love, Sacrifice
