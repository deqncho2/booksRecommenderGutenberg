Synopsis: 
In 'Steel: The Diary of a Furnace Worker', Charles R. Walker takes us on a gripping journey into the heart of the steel industry. Through the eyes of a furnace worker, we witness the intense heat, the deafening noise, and the relentless physical demands of the job. As the diary entries unfold, we delve into the personal struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices of the workers, as they navigate the dangerous and unforgiving world of steel production. With vivid descriptions and raw emotions, Walker paints a vivid picture of an industry that shapes lives and communities, revealing the untold stories behind the steel that built nations.

Genres: Memoir, Non-fiction, Industrial Literature

Themes: Hardship and resilience, Industrialization and its impact, Human connection and camaraderie
