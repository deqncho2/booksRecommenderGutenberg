Synopsis: 
In 'The Young Man and Journalism' by Lord, Chester Sanders, we follow the journey of a determined and ambitious young man named James as he navigates the thrilling world of journalism. Fresh out of college, James lands a coveted internship at a prestigious newspaper, where he quickly learns that the industry is not as glamorous as he had imagined. As he faces numerous challenges, from cutthroat competition to ethical dilemmas, James must find his voice and make difficult choices that will shape his career and personal life. With a captivating blend of suspense, romance, and moral dilemmas, this novel takes readers on a gripping exploration of the trials and triumphs of a young journalist.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Drama

Themes: Ambition, Ethics, Identity, Love, Professionalism
