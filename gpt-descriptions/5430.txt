Synopsis: In "Preface to a Dictionary of the English Language," Samuel Johnson takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricacies and evolution of the English language. With wit, insight, and meticulous research, Johnson explores the challenges of defining and documenting a language that is constantly evolving. From the origins of words to the nuances of their meanings, he delves into the rich tapestry of English, shedding light on its idiosyncrasies and complexities. Through his engaging prose, Johnson invites readers to appreciate the beauty and power of language, while also reflecting on the role of dictionaries in shaping our understanding of words and their significance.

Genres: Non-fiction, Linguistics, Literary Criticism

Themes: Language and its evolution, Lexicography, The power of words
