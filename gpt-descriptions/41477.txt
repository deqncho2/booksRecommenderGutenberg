Synopsis: In "Humphrey, Duke of Gloucester: A Biography," Kenneth Hotham Vickers delves into the captivating life of one of England's most enigmatic historical figures. From his humble beginnings to his rise as a powerful nobleman, Humphrey's story is filled with political intrigue, scandal, and a relentless pursuit of power. As the younger brother of a king, he navigates the treacherous waters of court politics, forming alliances and facing betrayals that shape his destiny. Vickers skillfully unravels the complexities of Humphrey's character, shedding light on his motivations and the impact he had on the tumultuous events of his time. This biography offers a fascinating exploration of a man who defied expectations and left an indelible mark on English history.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Power, Ambition, Loyalty, Betrayal
