Synopsis: 
In "As We Were Saying" by Charles Dudley Warner, readers are transported to a charming New England town where a group of friends engage in lively conversations and debates about various topics. From politics and religion to literature and social issues, these witty and insightful discussions offer a glimpse into the intellectual and cultural climate of the late 19th century. As the friends navigate their way through these conversations, they also explore the complexities of human nature and the ever-evolving dynamics of friendship. With humor and thought-provoking insights, Warner invites readers to join this captivating group of friends as they ponder life's big questions.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Social Commentary

Themes: Intellectual discourse, Friendship, Human nature, Social criticism
