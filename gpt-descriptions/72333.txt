Synopsis: In "Mr. Belloc objects to 'The outline of history'" by H. G. Wells, the renowned author finds himself at the center of a heated debate with the formidable Mr. Belloc. As the two intellectuals clash over the merits and shortcomings of Wells' monumental work, "The Outline of History," their arguments delve into the realms of philosophy, religion, and the very nature of historical interpretation. With wit, intellect, and a touch of humor, this engaging exchange challenges readers to question their own understanding of the past and the role it plays in shaping the present.

Genres: Non-fiction, Essay, Historical Critique

Themes: Historical interpretation, Philosophy, Religion, Intellectual debate
