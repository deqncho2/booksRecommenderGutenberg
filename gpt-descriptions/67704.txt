Synopsis: In 'Doctors: An Address delivered to the Students of the Medical School of the Middlesex Hospital, 1st October, 1908', Rudyard Kipling takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of medicine. Through vivid storytelling and insightful anecdotes, Kipling explores the challenges, triumphs, and ethical dilemmas faced by doctors. From the intense pressure of making life-or-death decisions to the profound impact they have on their patients' lives, Kipling delves into the complex and often overlooked aspects of the medical profession. This address serves as a thought-provoking exploration of the noble and demanding world of doctors, shedding light on the sacrifices they make and the profound responsibility they bear.

Genres: Non-fiction, Speech, Medical Literature

Themes: Medicine, Ethics, Responsibility, Sacrifice
