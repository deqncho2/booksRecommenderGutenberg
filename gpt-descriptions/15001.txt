Synopsis: 
'The Sources and Analogues of 'A Midsummer-night's Dream'' delves into the origins and inspirations behind one of Shakespeare's most beloved comedies. This scholarly work explores the various literary sources and folk traditions that influenced the creation of 'A Midsummer Night's Dream', shedding light on the rich tapestry of ideas and stories that shaped the play. From ancient mythology to medieval romances, this book uncovers the fascinating connections between Shakespeare's work and the literary traditions that came before it. 

Genres: Literary criticism, Shakespearean studies, Comparative literature

Themes: Love and desire, Magic and enchantment, The power of imagination
