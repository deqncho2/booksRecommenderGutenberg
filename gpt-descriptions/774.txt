Synopsis: In 'Essays and Lectures' by Oscar Wilde, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the brilliant mind of one of the most celebrated writers of the 19th century. This collection of thought-provoking essays and engaging lectures offers a unique insight into Wilde's views on art, literature, society, and the human condition. From exploring the nature of beauty and the role of the artist to delving into the complexities of love and the importance of individuality, Wilde's words resonate with wit, wisdom, and a touch of irreverence. With his signature style, he challenges conventional thinking and invites readers to question the norms of their time. Genres: Essay, Lecture, Non-fiction. Themes: Art and Aesthetics, Society and Morality, Individuality and Identity.
