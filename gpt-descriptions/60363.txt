Synopsis: 

In 'Ninth Cavalry: One Hundred and Twenty-first Regiment Indiana Volunteers', author Daniel Webster Comstock takes readers on a gripping journey through the American Civil War. This historical account follows the experiences of the Ninth Cavalry, a regiment of Indiana volunteers, as they navigate the treacherous battlefields and face the harsh realities of war. Comstock's vivid storytelling brings to life the camaraderie, bravery, and sacrifices made by these soldiers, shedding light on the often overlooked contributions of the cavalry during this tumultuous period in American history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War, Military History

Themes: Courage, Brotherhood, Sacrifice, Historical Perspective
