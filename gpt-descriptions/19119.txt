Synopsis: In 'Cerberus, the dog of Hades: The history of an idea', Maurice Bloomfield delves into the captivating origins and evolution of the mythical creature that guards the gates of the Underworld. From its earliest appearances in ancient Greek and Roman literature to its enduring presence in art, literature, and popular culture, Bloomfield unravels the fascinating history behind Cerberus. As he explores the various interpretations and symbolic meanings associated with this three-headed canine, the reader is taken on a journey through time, encountering surprising connections and uncovering the profound significance of this legendary creature.

Genres: Mythology, History, Literary Criticism

Themes: Myth and Legend, Symbolism, Cultural Significance
