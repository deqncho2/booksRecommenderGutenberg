Synopsis: In "Fundamental Principles of the Metaphysic of Morals," Immanuel Kant delves into the realm of ethics, exploring the fundamental principles that guide human actions and moral decision-making. Through a rigorous examination of reason and the concept of duty, Kant challenges conventional notions of morality and offers a thought-provoking perspective on the nature of moral obligations. With his characteristic clarity and logical reasoning, Kant presents a compelling argument that will leave readers questioning their own moral compass and the principles that underpin their actions.

Genres: Philosophy, Ethics

Themes: Moral philosophy, Duty, Reason, Ethics
