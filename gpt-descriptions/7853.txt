Synopsis: In "Quentin Durward" by Sir Walter Scott, the reader is transported to 15th-century France, where the young Scottish archer Quentin Durward finds himself entangled in a web of political intrigue, forbidden love, and treacherous alliances. As he navigates the dangerous court of King Louis XI, Quentin must rely on his wit, courage, and archery skills to survive and protect those he holds dear. Amidst the backdrop of a tumultuous era, filled with knights, damsels in distress, and secret plots, Quentin's journey unfolds, revealing the true nature of loyalty, honor, and the power of love.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Romance

Themes: Loyalty, Honor, Love, Political Intrigue
