Synopsis: 
'Indian Speeches (1907-1909)' by John Morley is a captivating collection of speeches delivered by the author during his tenure as the Secretary of State for India. Through his eloquent and thought-provoking speeches, Morley delves into the complex issues surrounding British rule in India, addressing topics such as nationalism, self-governance, and the relationship between the British and Indian people. As he navigates the intricacies of colonialism and the aspirations of the Indian population, Morley's speeches offer a unique insight into the political landscape of the time and the challenges faced by both the rulers and the ruled.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Colonialism, Nationalism, Governance, Cultural Identity
