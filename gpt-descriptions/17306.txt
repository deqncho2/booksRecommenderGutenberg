Synopsis: 

In "The Condition of the Working-Class in England in 1844: with a Preface written in 1892," Friedrich Engels takes readers on a gripping journey through the harsh realities of the working-class in England during the mid-19th century. Through meticulous research and firsthand observations, Engels paints a vivid and shocking picture of the deplorable living and working conditions endured by the working-class population. From the squalid slums to the dangerous factories, Engels exposes the exploitation and suffering faced by these individuals, shedding light on the stark contrast between the wealthy elite and the impoverished masses. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical analysis

Themes: Social inequality, Industrialization, Class struggle, Exploitation, Poverty
