Synopsis: 

'Abstracts of Papers Read at the First International Eugenics Congress: University of London, July, 1912' provides a fascinating glimpse into the groundbreaking research and discussions that took place during the inaugural International Eugenics Congress. This collection of abstracts showcases the diverse range of topics explored by leading scientists, scholars, and thinkers of the time, shedding light on the emerging field of eugenics and its potential implications for society. From discussions on heredity and the role of genetics in shaping human traits to debates on the ethics and practical applications of eugenics, this compilation offers a thought-provoking exploration of a controversial and influential movement that continues to resonate today.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: Eugenics, Heredity, Genetics, Ethics, Social Impact
