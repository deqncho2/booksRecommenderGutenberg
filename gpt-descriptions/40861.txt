Synopsis: 
"The Journal of the Debates in the Convention which Framed the Constitution of the United States, May-September 1787. Volume 1" offers a fascinating glimpse into the inner workings of the Constitutional Convention that shaped the foundation of the United States. Written by James Madison, one of the key figures in the convention, this volume provides a detailed account of the debates, discussions, and compromises that took place during this pivotal moment in American history. From the clash of ideas to the forging of a new nation, Madison's journal immerses readers in the intense intellectual and political battles that ultimately led to the creation of the U.S. Constitution.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Primary source

Themes: Constitutional law, Democracy, Governance, Founding principles
