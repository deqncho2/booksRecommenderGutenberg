Synopsis: In 'Edith and John: A Story of Pittsburgh' by Farquhar, Franklin Smith, readers are transported to the bustling city of Pittsburgh in the late 19th century. The story revolves around the lives of Edith and John, two individuals from vastly different backgrounds who find themselves drawn to each other amidst the backdrop of industrialization and social change. As their paths intertwine, they navigate the complexities of love, ambition, and societal expectations, all while facing the challenges and opportunities that arise in a rapidly evolving city. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this captivating tale explores the intertwining of personal and societal transformation in the face of progress.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: Love and Relationships, Social Change, Ambition, Industrialization
