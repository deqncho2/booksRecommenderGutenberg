Synopsis:
In 'Baboe Dalima; or, The Opium Fiend' by Perelaer, M. T. H., readers are transported to the exotic and mysterious world of 19th-century Java. The story revolves around the enigmatic character of Baboe Dalima, a young Javanese woman who becomes entangled in a web of opium addiction and forbidden love. As her life takes a dark and dangerous turn, the reader is drawn into a thrilling narrative filled with intrigue, betrayal, and unexpected twists. Set against the backdrop of colonial Indonesia, this gripping tale explores the complexities of cultural clashes, personal desires, and the destructive power of addiction.

Genres:
Historical Fiction, Adventure, Romance

Themes:
Colonialism, Addiction, Forbidden Love
