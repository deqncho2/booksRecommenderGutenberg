Synopsis: 

In 'Attack: An Infantry Subaltern's Impression of July 1st, 1916', Edward G. D. Liveing takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing events of one fateful day during World War I. Through the eyes of a young infantry subaltern, we witness the intense anticipation, the camaraderie among soldiers, and the sheer terror of going over the top into no man's land. As the battle unfolds, Liveing's vivid descriptions and personal reflections bring to life the chaos, the bravery, and the devastating consequences of war.

Genres: Historical fiction, War literature, Memoir

Themes: War and its impact on individuals, Camaraderie and brotherhood in the face of adversity, The horrors of trench warfare
