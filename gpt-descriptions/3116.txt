Synopsis: 
In 'Literary Copyright' by Charles Dudley Warner, a renowned author delves into the complex world of copyright laws and their impact on the literary community. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Warner explores the ethical and legal dilemmas faced by writers, publishers, and readers in an ever-evolving literary landscape. With wit and insight, he examines the tension between artistic freedom and the need for protection, shedding light on the fascinating history and controversies surrounding literary copyright.

Genres: Non-fiction, Legal, Literary Criticism

Themes: Copyright law, Intellectual property, Artistic freedom, Ethics
