Synopsis: In 'Nets to Catch the Wind' by Elinor Wylie, a young woman named Helen embarks on a journey of self-discovery and liberation in the midst of societal expectations and personal struggles. As she navigates through love, loss, and the pursuit of her dreams, Helen finds herself torn between the conventional path laid out for her and the desire to break free from the constraints of her time. With vivid prose and captivating characters, this novel takes readers on a captivating exploration of one woman's quest for independence and fulfillment.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Self-discovery, Liberation, Love and Loss, Societal Expectations
