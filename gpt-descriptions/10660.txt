Synopsis: In "Lives of the English Poets: From Johnson to Kirke White, Designed as a Continuation of Johnson's Lives," Henry Francis Cary delves into the lives and works of some of the most influential poets in English literature. From the tumultuous lives of Samuel Johnson and William Cowper to the tragic story of Robert Burns and the promising talent of Henry Kirke White, Cary paints a vivid picture of the struggles, triumphs, and creative genius that shaped these poets. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Cary invites readers to explore the fascinating world of these literary giants, uncovering the inspirations behind their verses and the impact they had on the literary landscape.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Artistic expression, Personal struggles, Literary legacy
