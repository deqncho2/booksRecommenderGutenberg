Synopsis: In 'Young People's Pride: A Novel' by Stephen Vincent Benét, we are transported to a small town in the early 20th century, where the lives of a group of young individuals intertwine in unexpected ways. As they navigate the challenges of adolescence, their dreams, ambitions, and desires clash with the realities of the world around them. Secrets, love, and betrayal lurk beneath the surface, threatening to shatter their youthful innocence. With vivid storytelling and rich character development, Benét explores the complexities of human nature and the transformative power of self-discovery.

Genres: Coming-of-age, Drama, Romance

Themes: Youthful idealism, Identity, Love and heartbreak, Society's expectations
