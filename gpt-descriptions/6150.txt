Synopsis: 
The Iliad, an epic poem by Homer, transports readers to the ancient city of Troy during the final year of the Trojan War. This gripping tale follows the clash between the mighty Greek hero Achilles and the Trojan prince Hector, as they lead their respective armies into battle. Amidst the chaos of war, the poem delves into the complex emotions, personal sacrifices, and tragic consequences faced by both the warriors and the gods who intervene in their affairs. With its vivid descriptions of heroism, honor, and the devastating consequences of pride, The Iliad offers a timeless exploration of the human condition and the consequences of war.

Genres: Epic poetry, Historical fiction

Themes: Heroism, Honor, Fate, Pride, War, Mortality
