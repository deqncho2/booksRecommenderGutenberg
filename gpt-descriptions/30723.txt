Synopsis: 
Fathers and Children is a captivating novel that delves into the complex relationships between generations, exploring the clash of ideologies and the struggle for identity in 19th-century Russia. Set against the backdrop of social and political change, the story follows the intellectual Bazarov, a nihilist who challenges the traditional values of his parents' generation. As he becomes entangled in a web of love, friendship, and conflicting beliefs, the novel takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the intricacies of human nature and the consequences of revolutionary ideas.

Genres: 
Realistic fiction, Bildungsroman, Social commentary

Themes: 
Generational conflict, Nihilism, Love and relationships, Social change
