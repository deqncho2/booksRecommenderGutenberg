Synopsis: 

'New Zealanders at Gallipoli' by Fred Waite takes readers on a gripping journey to the battlefields of Gallipoli, where New Zealand soldiers fought valiantly during World War I. Through vivid storytelling and firsthand accounts, Waite brings to life the harrowing experiences, camaraderie, and resilience of the New Zealand troops as they face the challenges of war. From the initial landing to the grueling trench warfare, the book provides a compelling narrative that captures the bravery and sacrifices made by these soldiers in the face of unimaginable adversity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War literature, Military history

Themes: Courage, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Resilience, The horrors of war
