Synopsis: In 'Transactions of the American Society of Civil Engineers, vol. LXXII, June, 1911', William Cain presents a captivating exploration of experiments on retaining walls and pressures on tunnels. Through a series of intriguing experiments, Cain delves into the complexities of these structures, shedding light on their behavior and the forces they withstand. This work offers valuable insights into the engineering challenges faced during the early 20th century, providing a fascinating glimpse into the world of civil engineering.

Genres: Scientific research, Engineering, Technical literature

Themes: Structural stability, Forces and pressures, Experimental methodology
