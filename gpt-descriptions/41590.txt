Synopsis: 
'Negro Tales' by Joseph S. Cotter is a captivating collection of short stories that delves into the lives and experiences of African Americans in the early 20th century. From the struggles of everyday life to the triumphs of resilience and hope, each tale offers a unique glimpse into the rich tapestry of African American culture. Through vivid storytelling and compelling characters, Cotter explores themes of identity, community, and the power of storytelling itself. Prepare to be transported to a world where the human spirit shines brightly, even in the face of adversity.

Genres: 
Short stories, African American literature, Historical fiction

Themes: 
Identity, Community, Resilience, Hope, Storytelling
