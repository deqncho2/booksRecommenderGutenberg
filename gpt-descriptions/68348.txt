Synopsis: 

'England under the Angevin Kings, Volumes I and II' by Kate Norgate takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous reigns of the Angevin kings in medieval England. From the rise of Henry II to the reign of his grandson, Richard the Lionheart, this meticulously researched work delves into the complex political landscape, power struggles, and dynastic conflicts that shaped the destiny of England during this period. Norgate's vivid storytelling and insightful analysis bring to life the fascinating characters, including the formidable Eleanor of Aquitaine and the enigmatic Thomas Becket, as well as the pivotal events that forever altered the course of English history. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Medieval history

Themes: Power and politics, Royal dynasties, Medieval society and culture
