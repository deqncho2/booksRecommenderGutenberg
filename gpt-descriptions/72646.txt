Synopsis: 
In 'The Philistine: A periodical of protest (Vol. II, No. 5, April 1896)', readers are transported to a world where art, literature, and societal norms collide. This captivating periodical takes a bold stance against the conventional, challenging readers to question their own beliefs and values. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, captivating stories, and striking illustrations, 'The Philistine' offers a unique perspective on the human condition and the power of individual expression. Prepare to be captivated by this rebellious and enlightening journey into the depths of creativity and dissent.

Genres: 
Literary magazine, Satire, Social commentary

Themes: 
Nonconformity, Artistic expression, Social criticism
