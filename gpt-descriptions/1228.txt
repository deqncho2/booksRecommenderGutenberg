Synopsis: 

In "On the Origin of Species By Means of Natural Selection: Or, the Preservation of Favoured Races in the Struggle for Life," Charles Darwin takes readers on a captivating journey through the natural world, unveiling a groundbreaking theory that challenges long-held beliefs. Through meticulous observations and compelling evidence, Darwin presents a revolutionary explanation for the diversity of life on Earth, shedding light on the intricate mechanisms of evolution and the survival of species. As he delves into the intricate web of nature, Darwin's work not only challenges conventional wisdom but also invites readers to question their own understanding of the world around them.

Genres: Science, Non-fiction, Biology

Themes: Evolution, Natural selection, Adaptation, Survival, Biodiversity
