Synopsis: In "The Little Man: A Farcical Morality in Three Scenes," Galsworthy introduces us to a peculiar character named Mr. Boldero, a man of small stature but immense determination. As he navigates through a series of comical and absurd situations, we witness the unexpected impact he has on the people around him. With wit and satire, Galsworthy explores the complexities of human nature and the power of individual agency, leaving readers intrigued by the unpredictable journey of this little man.

Genres: Comedy, Satire, Morality play

Themes: Individual agency, Human nature, Social satire
