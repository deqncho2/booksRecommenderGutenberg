Synopsis: In 'The Oaths, Signs, Ceremonies and Objects of the Ku-Klux-Klan: A Full Expose', an anonymous former member unveils the secretive world of the notorious Ku Klux Klan. Through a firsthand account, the author delves into the clandestine rituals, symbols, and practices of this infamous organization, shedding light on their inner workings and motivations. As the narrative unfolds, readers are taken on a gripping journey that explores the dark underbelly of racism, hatred, and the lengths some will go to maintain power and control. This eye-opening expose challenges readers to confront the disturbing reality of the Klan's existence and the lasting impact it has had on society.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Commentary

Themes: Racism, Hatred, Power, Control, Secrecy
