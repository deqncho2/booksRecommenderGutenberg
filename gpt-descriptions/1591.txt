Synopsis: In Plato's "Protagoras," a young philosopher named Socrates engages in a thought-provoking dialogue with the renowned sophist Protagoras. As they delve into the nature of virtue and the art of rhetoric, their intellectual battle takes unexpected turns, challenging conventional wisdom and exploring the complexities of human knowledge and morality. Through their captivating discourse, Plato invites readers to question the foundations of truth and the role of education in shaping individuals and society.

Genres: Philosophy, Dialogue, Classical Literature

Themes: Virtue, Knowledge, Rhetoric, Education, Morality
