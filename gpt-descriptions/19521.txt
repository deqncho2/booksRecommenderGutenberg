Synopsis: 

'Letters from France' by Mack, Isaac Alexander is a captivating epistolary novel that takes readers on a journey through the heartfelt correspondence between two individuals separated by distance and time. The story unfolds through a series of letters exchanged between a young American woman and a French soldier during World War II. As their letters become a lifeline, they share their hopes, fears, dreams, and the profound impact the war has on their lives. Through their intimate and poignant exchanges, the reader is transported to a tumultuous era, witnessing the power of love, resilience, and the indomitable human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, War

Themes: Love, Resilience, Human Connection, War's Impact on Lives
