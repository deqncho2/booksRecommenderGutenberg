Synopsis: In 'The Thirteenth Hussars in the Great War', author Durand, H. Mortimer takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of the Thirteenth Hussars during World War I. From the trenches of the Western Front to the battlefields of Europe, this captivating account follows the brave soldiers as they face unimaginable challenges, forge unbreakable bonds, and confront the true cost of war. With vivid descriptions and heartfelt narratives, this book offers a unique perspective on the Great War, shedding light on the untold stories of heroism and sacrifice.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Military History

Themes: Courage, Brotherhood, Sacrifice, The Impact of War
