Synopsis: 

'Under the Turk in Constantinople: A record of Sir John Finch's Embassy, 1674-1681' takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and mysterious city of Constantinople during the late 17th century. Through the eyes of Sir John Finch, an English ambassador, we witness the intricate web of political intrigue, cultural clashes, and personal struggles that unfold within the walls of the Ottoman Empire. As Finch navigates the complex world of diplomacy, he becomes entangled in a series of unexpected events that challenge his loyalty, test his resilience, and force him to confront his own beliefs. With vivid descriptions and compelling storytelling, this historical account offers a fascinating glimpse into a pivotal period in European-Ottoman relations.

Genres: Historical fiction, Travelogue, Political intrigue

Themes: Cultural clash, Diplomacy, Loyalty, Personal growth
