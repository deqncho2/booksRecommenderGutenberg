Synopsis: 

In "Buddhism and Buddhists in China," Hodous, Lewis takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and profound influence of Buddhism in China. From its arrival in the country to its assimilation into Chinese culture, this book explores the fascinating encounters, transformations, and challenges faced by Buddhism and its followers in China. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, Hodous, Lewis paints a vivid picture of the vibrant Buddhist communities, the interactions with other religions, and the enduring impact of Buddhism on Chinese society. 

Genres: History, Religion, Cultural Studies

Themes: Buddhism, Cultural assimilation, Religious encounters, Influence of religion on society
