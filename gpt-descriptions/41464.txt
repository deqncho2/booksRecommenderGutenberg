Synopsis: In "Lords of the World: A story of the fall of Carthage and Corinth," Alfred John Church takes us on a captivating journey through the ancient world, where two mighty civilizations, Carthage and Corinth, face their inevitable downfall. Through the eyes of courageous and conflicted characters, we witness the clash of empires, the rise of new powers, and the tragic consequences of pride and ambition. As the fates of Carthage and Corinth hang in the balance, Church masterfully weaves a tale of political intrigue, epic battles, and personal sacrifices that will keep readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Epic

Themes: Power and Politics, Loyalty and Betrayal, Fate and Destiny
