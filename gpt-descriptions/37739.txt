Synopsis: 

In 'Egerton Ryerson and Education in Upper Canada' by J. Harold Putman, delve into the fascinating life and influential work of Egerton Ryerson, a prominent figure in the development of education in Upper Canada during the 19th century. This captivating biography explores Ryerson's tireless efforts to establish a comprehensive and accessible education system, his encounters with political and religious opposition, and his unwavering commitment to social progress. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Putman sheds light on the challenges and triumphs that shaped Ryerson's legacy, making this book a must-read for anyone interested in the history of education and social reform.

Genres: Biography, History, Education

Themes: Education reform, Social progress, Political and religious opposition
