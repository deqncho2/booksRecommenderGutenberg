Synopsis: 
In 'Oxford poetry, 1918', readers are transported to the hallowed halls of Oxford University during a time of great upheaval and transformation. Through a collection of evocative poems, this work captures the essence of a generation grappling with the aftermath of World War I, exploring themes of love, loss, and the search for meaning in a rapidly changing world. From poignant reflections on the horrors of war to lyrical odes to the beauty of nature, this anthology offers a profound and thought-provoking journey through the hearts and minds of the poets of Oxford in 1918.

Genres: Poetry, Historical, Literary

Themes: War, Love, Loss, Transformation, Nature
