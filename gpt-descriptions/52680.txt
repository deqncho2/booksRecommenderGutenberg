Synopsis: In 'The Caillaux Drama', Raphael, John N. delves into the scandalous and captivating true story of Henriette Caillaux, a woman whose actions shocked the world. Set in early 20th century France, this gripping narrative explores the complex web of love, power, and betrayal that led to a shocking crime. As the tension builds, readers will be drawn into the scandalous affair that unfolds, leaving them questioning the limits of passion and the consequences of one's actions.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Biography

Themes: Love and obsession, Power and influence, Morality and ethics
