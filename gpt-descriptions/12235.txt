Synopsis: 

In "Popular Law-making: A study of the origin, history, and present tendencies of law-making by statute," Frederic Jesup Stimson takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of law-making. Delving into the origins and evolution of statutes, Stimson explores the intricate web of factors that have shaped the way laws are created and implemented. From ancient civilizations to modern societies, this thought-provoking study uncovers the hidden forces behind the development of legal systems, shedding light on the present tendencies that continue to shape our laws today. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Legal Studies, History

Themes: Law-making, Statutes, Legal Evolution, Societal Influence
