Synopsis: In "Homes of the London Poor," Octavia Hill takes us on a captivating journey through the hidden corners of Victorian London, revealing the harsh realities faced by the city's impoverished residents. Through her vivid descriptions and personal encounters, Hill exposes the squalid living conditions, the struggles for survival, and the resilience of those living in the shadows of society. From the cramped tenements to the overcrowded workhouses, this eye-opening account sheds light on the forgotten lives of the London poor, challenging our perceptions and urging us to confront the social injustices of the time.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Urban living conditions, Empathy, Social reform
