Synopsis: In the second volume of his memoirs, Charles-Lewis, Baron de Pollnitz takes us on a captivating journey through various European countries, including Prussia, Germany, Italy, France, Flanders, Holland, and England. Through a series of letters written to his friend, he provides vivid and insightful observations about the present state of the chief cities and towns he visits, as well as the intriguing characters he encounters at the different courts. From the opulence of the French court to the bustling streets of London, Pollnitz's letters offer a unique perspective on the social, political, and cultural landscapes of Europe during his travels.

Genres: Memoir, Travelogue, Historical

Themes: European society and culture, Court life, Travel and exploration, Social observation
