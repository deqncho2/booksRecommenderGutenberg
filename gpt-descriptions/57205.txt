Synopsis: In "The Little Black Princess: A True Tale of Life in the Never-Never Land," Jeannie Gunn takes readers on a captivating journey to the remote Australian Outback. Set in the early 20th century, this remarkable true story follows the author's experiences as she immerses herself in the vibrant culture of the indigenous people, particularly a young Aboriginal girl named Bett-Bett. Through her encounters with the harsh realities of life in the Never-Never Land, Gunn discovers the power of friendship, resilience, and the beauty of embracing different cultures.

Genres: Memoir, Adventure, Cultural Exploration

Themes: Indigenous culture, Friendship, Resilience, Cultural diversity
