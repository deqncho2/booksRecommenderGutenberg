Synopsis: In 'The School and Society: Being three lectures', John Dewey explores the transformative power of education and its impact on society. Through a series of thought-provoking lectures, Dewey delves into the importance of experiential learning, the role of the school in shaping the individual, and the need for education to adapt to the changing needs of society. With insightful observations and practical examples, Dewey challenges traditional notions of education and offers a compelling vision for a more inclusive and progressive educational system.

Genres: Education, Philosophy

Themes: Experiential learning, Social progress, Educational reform
