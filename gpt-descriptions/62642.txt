Synopsis: 

'War Prisoner Money and Medals' by Guido Kisch is a captivating exploration of the fascinating world of currency and medals used in prisoner-of-war camps during World War II. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Kisch delves into the intricate details of how prisoners created their own monetary systems and crafted unique medals as a means of survival and resistance. From makeshift coins to intricate artwork, this book unveils the ingenuity and resourcefulness of those held captive during the war. 

Genres: History, War, Numismatics

Themes: Survival, Resistance, Ingenuity
