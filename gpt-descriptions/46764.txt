Synopsis: 

"The Birds of Washington (Volume 1 of 2): A complete, scientific and popular account of the 372 species of birds found in the state" takes readers on an immersive journey into the diverse avian world of Washington. Written by renowned ornithologists Bowles and Dawson, this comprehensive volume provides a captivating blend of scientific research and engaging storytelling. From the majestic bald eagle to the elusive spotted owl, the authors delve into the lives, habitats, and behaviors of the 372 bird species that call Washington home. With stunning illustrations and detailed descriptions, this book offers a unique opportunity to explore the rich birdlife of the state, captivating both bird enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Science

Themes: Biodiversity, Conservation, Wildlife Observation
