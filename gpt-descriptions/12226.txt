Synopsis: In "What Eight Million Women Want," Rheta Childe Dorr takes us on a captivating journey through the lives of women in early 20th century America. Through a series of vivid and intimate portraits, Dorr explores the dreams, struggles, and desires of these women, revealing a world of untapped potential and unspoken yearnings. From the suffragettes fighting for their right to vote, to the working-class women striving for economic independence, and the socialites navigating the complexities of high society, Dorr's narrative weaves together a tapestry of diverse experiences that will leave readers spellbound.

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, History

Themes: Women's rights, Gender roles, Social change, Identity, Class struggle
