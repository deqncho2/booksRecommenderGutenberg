Synopsis: In 'The Negro: What is His Ethnological Status? 2nd Ed.', Ariel delves into a thought-provoking exploration of the complex and often misunderstood history and identity of the African American community. Through meticulous research and compelling arguments, Ariel challenges prevailing notions and sheds light on the true ethnological status of the Negro, inviting readers to question their own preconceptions and biases. This enlightening work offers a fresh perspective on a subject that continues to shape our society today.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Race, Identity, Prejudice, Cultural Heritage
