Synopsis: In "A History of Postal Agitation from Fifty Years Ago till the Present Day," Swift, H. G. takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous world of postal services. From the early days of protests and strikes to the modern era of digital communication, this book unveils the hidden stories behind the scenes of the postal industry. With intriguing anecdotes and compelling narratives, Swift explores the struggles, triumphs, and controversies that have shaped the postal system over the past half-century. Delve into the fascinating world of postal agitation and discover the untold history that has shaped our means of communication today.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Commentary

Themes: Labor rights, Technological advancements, Communication, Social change
