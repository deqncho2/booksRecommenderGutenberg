Synopsis: 

In "The Microscope. Its History, Construction, and Application," Jabez Hogg takes readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of microscopical science. This 15th edition serves as a familiar introduction to the instrument's use and the study of microscopic phenomena. From the early origins of the microscope to its modern construction, Hogg delves into the intricate details of this remarkable tool. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, he explores the various applications of the microscope, unveiling a hidden universe that exists beyond the naked eye. 

Genres: Science, Non-fiction, History

Themes: Scientific discovery, Instrumentation, Exploration of the unseen world
