Synopsis: In 'Symmes's Theory of Concentric Spheres', McBride explores the groundbreaking ideas of John Cleves Symmes, who boldly proposes that the Earth is not a solid sphere, but rather hollow and habitable within. As McBride delves into Symmes's theories, he takes readers on a captivating journey through scientific evidence, historical accounts, and personal anecdotes, all pointing towards a mind-boggling possibility: that the Earth's poles are not closed, but rather widely open. With each page, McBride's narrative unravels the mysteries of Symmes's theory, leaving readers questioning everything they thought they knew about our planet.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: Scientific exploration, Alternative theories, Paradigm shifts
