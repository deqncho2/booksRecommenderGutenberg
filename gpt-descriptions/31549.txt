Synopsis: In 'The Battle of the Big Hole: A History of General Gibbon's Engagement with Nez Percés Indians in the Big Hole Valley, Montana, August 9th, 1877,' author G. O. Shields takes readers on a gripping journey through a little-known but significant battle between General Gibbon's forces and the Nez Percés Indians. Set in the stunning Big Hole Valley of Montana, this historical account unveils the intense clash that unfolded on August 9th, 1877, revealing the bravery, strategies, and sacrifices made by both sides. As the battle unfolds, readers will be captivated by the suspense and the intricate details of this pivotal moment in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history

Themes: Conflict and warfare, Cultural clashes, Bravery and sacrifice
