Synopsis: 

In "Theodicy: Essays on the Goodness of God, the Freedom of Man and the Origin of Evil," Leibniz delves into the profound questions surrounding the existence of evil in a world created by a benevolent God. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, he explores the intricate relationship between God's goodness, human freedom, and the presence of evil, offering a unique perspective on the age-old problem of theodicy. Leibniz's insightful analysis challenges readers to reconsider their understanding of the divine and grapple with the complexities of human existence.

Genres: Philosophy, Theology

Themes: Theodicy, God's goodness, Human freedom, Existence of evil
