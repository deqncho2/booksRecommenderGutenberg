Synopsis:
In 'Beethoven's Letters 1790-1826, Volume 2', the renowned composer Ludwig van Beethoven invites readers into his personal world through a collection of his intimate letters. Spanning over three decades, this volume delves into Beethoven's struggles, triumphs, and the profound emotions that shaped his life and music. From his passionate love affairs to his battles with deafness and societal expectations, these letters offer a captivating glimpse into the mind of a musical genius. As Beethoven's correspondence unfolds, readers are transported into a world of artistic brilliance, personal turmoil, and the relentless pursuit of musical perfection.

Genres:
Biography, Music, Letters

Themes:
Artistic expression, Love and relationships, Personal struggles, Perseverance, Creativity
