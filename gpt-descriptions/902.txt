Synopsis: In 'The Happy Prince, and Other Tales', Oscar Wilde weaves together a collection of enchanting and thought-provoking stories that will transport readers to a world of beauty, compassion, and moral dilemmas. From the tale of a selfless statue who sacrifices everything for the sake of others, to the story of a devoted nightingale who seeks to bring joy to a young lover, each narrative is filled with vivid imagery and profound insights into the human condition. As the characters navigate themes of love, sacrifice, and the nature of true happiness, readers will be captivated by Wilde's masterful storytelling and the timeless lessons that lie within.

Genres: Fairy tales, Fantasy, Moral fiction

Themes: Love, Sacrifice, Compassion, Materialism, Social inequality
