Synopsis: In "History of the United Netherlands from the Death of William the Silent to the Twelve Year's Truce, 1585c," John Lothrop Motley takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period following the assassination of William the Silent, the founding father of the Dutch Republic. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Motley delves into the political intrigues, military conflicts, and religious tensions that shaped the destiny of the United Netherlands. From the rise of the formidable Dutch navy to the epic battles against Spanish forces, this historical account paints a vivid picture of a nation fighting for its independence and religious freedom. As the Dutch Republic emerges as a major European power, Motley explores the complex relationships between the Dutch, Spanish, English, and French, revealing the intricate web of alliances and betrayals that defined this era. With its rich tapestry of characters and events, "History of the United Netherlands" offers a gripping narrative that will leave readers eager to uncover the fate of this remarkable nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Military history

Themes: Independence, Religious freedom, Political intrigue, War and conflict, Diplomacy
