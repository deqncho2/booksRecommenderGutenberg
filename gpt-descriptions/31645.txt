Synopsis: In 'The Tatler, Volume 3', Sir Richard Steele and Joseph Addison continue their witty and satirical exploration of 18th-century London society through a series of fictional characters and their amusing anecdotes. From the scandalous affairs of the upper class to the comical mishaps of the lower class, this collection of essays offers a captivating glimpse into the social dynamics and peculiarities of the time. With their sharp observations and clever commentary, Steele and Addison invite readers to laugh, reflect, and perhaps even recognize themselves in the colorful tapestry of characters that populate their world.

Genres: Satire, Essay, Periodical

Themes: Social satire, Morality, Human nature
