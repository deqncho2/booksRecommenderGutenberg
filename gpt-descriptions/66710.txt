Synopsis: In 'An Inquiry into the Principles of Political Oeconomy', Sir James Steuart delves into the intricate workings of domestic policy in free nations. Volume 2 of this enlightening essay explores a wide range of topics including population, agriculture, trade, industry, money, coin, interest, circulation, banks, exchange, public credit, and taxes. Steuart's analysis provides a comprehensive understanding of the science behind these crucial aspects of political economy, shedding light on their interconnections and implications for society. This thought-provoking work offers valuable insights into the complexities of managing a nation's economy and the challenges faced by policymakers.

Genres: Economics, Political Science, Philosophy

Themes: Political economy, Domestic policy, Society and governance
