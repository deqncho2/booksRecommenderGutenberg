Synopsis: In "When the Movies Were Young" by Linda Arvidson, readers are transported back to the early days of the film industry, where the magic of cinema was just beginning to unfold. Through the eyes of a young actress, we witness the birth of silent films, the struggles and triumphs of the pioneers, and the captivating allure of the silver screen. As the protagonist navigates the evolving world of moviemaking, she discovers the power of dreams, the complexities of fame, and the sacrifices required to bring stories to life. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Drama, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Ambition, Creativity, Nostalgia, Identity
