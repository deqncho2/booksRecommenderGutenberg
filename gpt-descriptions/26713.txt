Synopsis: 

'Norman Ten Hundred: A Record of the 1st (Service) Bn. Royal Guernsey Light Infantry' by A. Stanley Blicq is a captivating historical account that takes readers on a remarkable journey through the experiences of the soldiers of the Royal Guernsey Light Infantry during World War I. 

From the trenches of the Western Front to the battlefields of Gallipoli, the book vividly portrays the courage, camaraderie, and sacrifices made by these brave men. Through personal anecdotes, letters, and diary entries, the author brings to life the harrowing realities of war, the bonds formed between soldiers, and the resilience they displayed in the face of unimaginable adversity.

As the narrative unfolds, readers are immersed in the daily lives of the soldiers, witnessing their triumphs and tragedies, their moments of despair and hope. The author skillfully captures the human side of war, highlighting the individual stories that make up the collective history of the battalion.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War memoir

Themes: Courage, camaraderie, sacrifice, resilience, human experience in war
