Synopsis: 

In "Reminiscences, Incidents, Battles, Marches and Camp Life of the Old 4th Michigan Infantry in War of Rebellion, 1861 to 1864," Orvey S. Barrett takes readers on a captivating journey through the experiences of the soldiers of the 4th Michigan Infantry during the American Civil War. Through vivid storytelling and personal anecdotes, Barrett paints a vivid picture of the hardships, triumphs, and camaraderie that defined the lives of these brave men. From the chaos of the battlefield to the monotony of camp life, this book offers a unique perspective on the realities of war and the indomitable spirit of those who fought.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, Brotherhood and camaraderie in the face of adversity
