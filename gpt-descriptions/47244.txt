Synopsis: In "Woman and Socialism," August Bebel delves into the complex relationship between women and the socialist movement, exploring the challenges they face in a patriarchal society. Through a combination of historical analysis, sociological insights, and personal anecdotes, Bebel sheds light on the struggles and aspirations of women, advocating for their equal rights and liberation. This thought-provoking work offers a compelling examination of the intersectionality of gender and class, and the potential for social change.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Theory, Feminism

Themes: Gender Equality, Socialism, Women's Liberation, Patriarchy
