Synopsis: 

In 'The City of the Sultan; and Domestic Manners of the Turks, in 1836, Vol. 1 (of 2)', Miss Pardoe takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and mysterious city of Istanbul. Through her vivid descriptions and keen observations, she unveils the hidden world of the Ottoman Empire, its opulent palaces, bustling bazaars, and the intriguing lives of its inhabitants. As she delves deeper into the heart of the city, she encounters a web of secrets, political intrigues, and forbidden romances that will keep readers on the edge of their seats. With each turn of the page, the reader becomes immersed in the exotic and enchanting world of the Sultan, where danger and desire intertwine.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Travelogue, Romance

Themes: Cultural exploration, Forbidden love, Political intrigue
