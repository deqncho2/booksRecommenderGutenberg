Synopsis: In "Is Shakespeare Dead?: From My Autobiography," Mark Twain embarks on a witty and thought-provoking exploration of the life and works of William Shakespeare. With his signature humor and sharp observations, Twain delves into the mysteries surrounding the Bard's identity, questioning the authenticity of his authorship and shedding light on the enigmatic figure that has captivated the world for centuries. Through a series of captivating anecdotes and historical analysis, Twain challenges conventional wisdom and invites readers to reconsider their perception of one of the greatest literary icons of all time.

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: Identity, Authorship, Literary Legacy
