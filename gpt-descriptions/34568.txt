Synopsis: In "Johann Sebastian Bach: The story of the boy who sang in the streets," author Thomas Tapper takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of one of the greatest composers in history. Set in the vibrant streets of 18th-century Germany, this biographical tale follows young Johann as he navigates the challenges of poverty, loss, and his unwavering passion for music. From his humble beginnings as an orphaned boy singing for coins to his rise as a renowned musician, Bach's extraordinary talent and determination shine through every page. Tapper's vivid storytelling and rich historical details bring to life the struggles and triumphs of a musical genius, leaving readers inspired by the indomitable spirit of a young boy who dared to dream.

Genres: Biography, Historical Fiction, Music

Themes: Perseverance, Passion, Music as a form of expression, Overcoming adversity
