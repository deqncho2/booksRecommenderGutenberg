Synopsis: In 'The Homosexual Neurosis' by Wilhelm Stekel, a renowned psychoanalyst delves into the complex world of homosexuality, exploring the hidden desires and struggles of individuals who grapple with their sexual orientation. Through a series of intriguing case studies, Stekel uncovers the deep-rooted psychological factors that contribute to the development of homosexual neurosis, shedding light on the inner turmoil faced by those who feel trapped between societal expectations and their own authentic selves. As he unravels the intricate layers of this controversial topic, Stekel challenges conventional beliefs and offers a fresh perspective on the complexities of human sexuality.

Genres: Psychology, Non-fiction

Themes: Homosexuality, Psychoanalysis, Identity, Society
