Synopsis: 
'Pharsalia; Dramatic Episodes of the Civil Wars' by Lucan is an epic poem that takes readers on a thrilling journey through the tumultuous times of the Roman Civil Wars. Filled with political intrigue, intense battles, and complex characters, this work delves into the depths of human ambition and the destructive consequences of power struggles. As the Roman Republic crumbles under the weight of its own internal conflicts, the poem explores the moral dilemmas faced by individuals caught in the midst of war, highlighting the tragic consequences of their choices. With vivid descriptions and poetic language, Lucan paints a vivid picture of a society on the brink of collapse, leaving readers captivated by the dramatic events that unfold.

Genres: Epic poetry, Historical fiction, Drama

Themes: Power and ambition, Morality and ethics, Fate and free will
