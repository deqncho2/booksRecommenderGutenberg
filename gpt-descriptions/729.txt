Synopsis: 
In the first two chapters of "Hackers, Heroes of the Computer Revolution," Steven Levy takes us on a captivating journey into the world of early computer enthusiasts and their groundbreaking achievements. From the rebellious hackers at MIT who challenged the status quo to the creation of the first personal computers, Levy unveils the untold stories of these brilliant minds and their relentless pursuit of technological innovation. With tales of late-night coding sessions, epic battles against corporate giants, and the birth of a counterculture movement, this book offers a thrilling glimpse into the birth of the digital age.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, History, Technology

Themes: 
Innovation, Counterculture, Technological Revolution
