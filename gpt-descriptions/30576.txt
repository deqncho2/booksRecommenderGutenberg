Synopsis: 

In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume XVI, Texas Narratives, Part 1', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a captivating collection of firsthand accounts from former slaves in Texas. These narratives offer a unique and powerful insight into the experiences, struggles, and resilience of those who endured the horrors of slavery. From tales of escape and resistance to stories of family bonds and cultural traditions, this volume paints a vivid picture of the lives of enslaved individuals in Texas. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Oral History

Themes: Slavery, Resistance, Family, Cultural Heritage
