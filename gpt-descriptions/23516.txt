Synopsis: In 'Bred in the bone: 1908', Thomas Nelson Page takes us on a captivating journey through the lives of two families in the post-Civil War South. As the story unfolds, secrets, betrayals, and unexpected alliances emerge, painting a vivid picture of the complex social dynamics and moral dilemmas faced by the characters. With richly drawn characters and a gripping plot, this novel explores the enduring legacy of the past and the power of love and redemption.

Genres: Historical fiction, Southern Gothic, Family saga

Themes: Legacy, Morality, Social dynamics, Love, Redemption
