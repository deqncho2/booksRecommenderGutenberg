Synopsis: 
In 'Fossil Butterflies: Memoirs of the American Association for the Advancement of Science, I.', Samuel Hubbard Scudder takes readers on a captivating journey through time, unveiling the ancient secrets of butterflies preserved in stone. As he meticulously examines these fossilized creatures, Scudder uncovers a world long forgotten, filled with stunning beauty and evolutionary wonders. From the delicate wings of extinct species to the clues they hold about Earth's history, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the hidden treasures found within the fossil record.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Paleontology

Themes: Evolution, Natural History, Fossilization
