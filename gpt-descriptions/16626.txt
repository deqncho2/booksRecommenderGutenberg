Synopsis: 
'Letters to Helen: Impressions of an Artist on the Western Front' by Henderson, Keith is a captivating collection of letters written by an artist serving on the Western Front during World War I. Through vivid descriptions and heartfelt reflections, the artist shares his experiences, struggles, and the profound impact of war on his art. As the letters unfold, the reader is transported to the trenches, witnessing the horrors of war and the resilience of the human spirit. This poignant and thought-provoking collection offers a unique perspective on the intersection of art and war, and the power of creativity to transcend even the darkest of times.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Literature, Epistolary

Themes: Art and Creativity, War and its Impact, Resilience and Survival
