Synopsis: 
In 'A Short History of Spain', Mary Platt Parmele takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and tumultuous history of Spain. From the ancient civilizations that once thrived on its soil to the rise and fall of powerful empires, Parmele weaves together a narrative that is both informative and engaging. Exploring the country's vibrant culture, complex politics, and significant contributions to art and literature, this book offers a concise yet comprehensive overview of Spain's past. Whether delving into the Spanish Inquisition or the exploration of the New World, Parmele's vivid storytelling brings history to life, making this a must-read for anyone seeking to understand the captivating story of Spain.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Cultural identity, Power and politics, Art and literature
