Synopsis: In 'Peace Theories and the Balkan War', Angell, Norman delves into the complex dynamics of the Balkan War and explores the various peace theories that could have prevented the conflict. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Angell uncovers the underlying causes of the war and presents thought-provoking arguments on how peace could have been achieved. This captivating work offers a fresh perspective on the Balkan War and challenges conventional notions of conflict resolution.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Peace, Conflict Resolution, International Relations
