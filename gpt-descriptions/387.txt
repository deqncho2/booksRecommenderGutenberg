Synopsis: 
'Vailima Letters: Being Correspondence Addressed by Robert Louis Stevenson to Sidney Colvin, November 1890-October 1894' is a collection of personal letters written by the renowned author Robert Louis Stevenson to his close friend Sidney Colvin. Spanning over four years, these letters provide a fascinating glimpse into Stevenson's life and thoughts during his time in Samoa, where he settled with his family. From his struggles with illness and the challenges of island life to his reflections on literature, politics, and the human condition, Stevenson's letters offer a captivating and intimate portrait of a brilliant mind.

Genres: 
Biography, Correspondence, Non-fiction

Themes: 
Illness and resilience, Literary reflections, Cultural exploration
