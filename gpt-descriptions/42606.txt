Synopsis: In 'Mimicry in Butterflies', Punnett takes us on a captivating journey into the world of butterflies, unveiling the astonishing phenomenon of mimicry. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, he explores the intricate strategies these delicate creatures employ to deceive predators and survive in their natural habitats. From the dazzling colors of their wings to their uncanny ability to imitate other species, Punnett unravels the secrets behind this fascinating evolutionary adaptation. As he delves deeper into the world of mimicry, the reader is left in awe of the remarkable beauty and ingenuity found in nature.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Nature

Themes: Evolution, Adaptation, Survival, Biodiversity
