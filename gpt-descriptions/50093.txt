Synopsis: In 'Keeping Up with William: In which the Honorable Socrates Potter Talks of the Relative Merits of Sense Common and Preferred' by Bacheller, Irving, readers are introduced to the eccentric and witty character of Socrates Potter. As he navigates the social circles of a small town, Socrates engages in lively conversations about the merits of common sense versus preferred sense. Through his humorous anecdotes and sharp observations, Socrates challenges conventional wisdom and offers a fresh perspective on life's dilemmas. This delightful novel takes readers on a journey filled with wit, wisdom, and unexpected twists.

Genres: Fiction, Humor, Satire

Themes: Wisdom, Social conventions, Individuality
