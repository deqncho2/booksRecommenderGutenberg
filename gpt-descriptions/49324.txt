Synopsis: In "The Authoress of the Odyssey: Where and when she wrote, who she was, the use she made of the Iliad, and how the poem grew under her hands," Samuel Butler delves into the fascinating mystery surrounding the authorship of the ancient Greek epic, The Odyssey. Butler explores the possible identity of the authoress, her motivations, and the historical context in which she wrote. He also examines the influence of the Iliad on her work and how she skillfully expanded and developed the story. This thought-provoking analysis offers a fresh perspective on one of the greatest literary works of all time.

Genres: Literary criticism, historical analysis, classical studies

Themes: Authorship, creativity, influence, storytelling
