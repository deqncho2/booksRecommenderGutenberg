Synopsis: 
'Poems' by Wilfred Owen is a collection of powerful and poignant verses that delve into the raw emotions and experiences of soldiers during World War I. Through vivid imagery and evocative language, Owen captures the horrors and futility of war, exploring themes of sacrifice, loss, and the dehumanizing effects of conflict. From the trenches to the battlefield, these poems offer a haunting glimpse into the realities faced by those who fought in the Great War.

Genres: 
War poetry, Historical poetry, Anti-war literature

Themes: 
War, Sacrifice, Loss, Humanity, Futility
