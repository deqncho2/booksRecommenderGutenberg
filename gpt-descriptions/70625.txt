Synopsis: 

In "Debts Hopeful and Desperate: $b Financing the Plymouth Colony," Ruth A. McIntyre delves into the fascinating history of the Plymouth Colony and its financial struggles. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, McIntyre uncovers the challenges faced by the Pilgrims as they sought to establish a new life in the New World. From their initial voyage on the Mayflower to their efforts to secure funding for the colony, the book explores the intricate web of debts, negotiations, and desperate measures taken by the Pilgrims to survive and thrive. McIntyre's narrative sheds light on the lesser-known aspects of the Plymouth Colony's history, offering a fresh perspective on the early years of American settlement.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Colonial history, Economic history

Themes: Financial struggles, Survival, Early American settlement
