Synopsis: In "The Roman Poets of the Augustan Age: Virgil," W. Y. Sellar delves into the life and works of one of the greatest poets of ancient Rome. This captivating exploration takes readers on a journey through the tumultuous times of Augustus' reign, where Virgil's poetry becomes a powerful tool for political and social commentary. From the epic tale of Aeneas to the pastoral beauty of the Georgics, Sellar unveils the genius behind Virgil's words, revealing the profound impact his poetry had on the Roman Empire and beyond.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Politics, Society, Artistic Expression
