Synopsis: In 'The Sceptics of the Old Testament: Job - Koheleth - Agur', Emile Joseph Dillon delves into the intriguing minds of three enigmatic figures from the Old Testament. Through a meticulous analysis of their writings, Dillon explores the doubts, questions, and philosophical ponderings that these sceptics bring to the forefront. From Job's profound suffering and search for meaning, to Koheleth's contemplation of life's fleeting nature, and Agur's humble wisdom, this book offers a captivating exploration of the sceptical voices within the biblical texts.

Genres: Religious Studies, Philosophy, Biblical Criticism

Themes: Doubt, Existentialism, Wisdom, Human Suffering
