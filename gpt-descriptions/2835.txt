Synopsis: 

'The Canadian Dominion: A Chronicle of Our Northern Neighbor' by Oscar D. Skelton is a captivating exploration of Canada's rich history, culture, and development. From its early days as a British colony to its emergence as a modern nation, this chronicle delves into the fascinating stories and events that have shaped Canada into what it is today. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Skelton takes readers on a journey through time, unveiling the struggles, triumphs, and unique characteristics that define the Canadian identity. From the fur trade and the building of the Canadian Pacific Railway to the challenges of Confederation and the country's role in global affairs, this book offers a comprehensive and engaging account of Canada's evolution.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Canadian identity, Nation-building, Historical events and figures, Cultural development
