Synopsis: In 'The Brass Check: A Study of American Journalism', Upton Sinclair takes readers on a gripping exploration of the dark underbelly of American journalism. Through a series of eye-opening anecdotes and personal experiences, Sinclair exposes the corruption, sensationalism, and manipulation that often lurk behind the headlines. From the exploitation of workers to the manipulation of public opinion, this thought-provoking work shines a light on the power and influence wielded by the media, challenging readers to question the integrity of the news they consume.

Genres: Non-fiction, investigative journalism, social criticism

Themes: Media corruption, sensationalism, manipulation, exploitation, power and influence
