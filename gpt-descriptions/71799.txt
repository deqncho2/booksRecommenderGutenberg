Synopsis: 

In 'New-Englands plantation: with the sea journal and other writings', Francis Higginson takes us on a captivating journey to the early days of the New England colonies. Through his vivid descriptions and personal experiences, Higginson paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by the settlers as they establish their new lives in a foreign land. From encounters with Native Americans to the harsh realities of survival in a harsh environment, Higginson's writings offer a unique perspective on the early years of the New England colonies.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, travelogue, memoir

Themes: Exploration and colonization, cultural encounters, survival and adaptation
