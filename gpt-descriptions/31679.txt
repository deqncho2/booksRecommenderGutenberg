Synopsis: 

'North American Yellow Bats, 'Dasypterus,' and a List of the Named Kinds of the Genus Lasiurus Gray' is a comprehensive and captivating exploration of the fascinating world of North American yellow bats. Written by E. Raymond Hall and J. Knox Jones, this book delves into the taxonomy, behavior, and distribution of these unique creatures, providing a wealth of information for both bat enthusiasts and researchers alike. From their distinctive yellow fur to their intriguing roosting habits, the authors shed light on the lesser-known aspects of these elusive bats, offering a captivating glimpse into their mysterious lives.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural history, Zoology

Themes: Biodiversity, Animal behavior, Conservation
