Synopsis: In "Frenzied Liberty; The Myth of 'A Rich Man's War'" by Otto H. Kahn, a captivating exploration of history unfolds as the author delves into the widely held belief that wars are fought solely for the benefit of the wealthy. Through meticulous research and thought-provoking analysis, Kahn challenges this notion, revealing the complex motivations and intricate web of interests that drive nations into conflict. With vivid storytelling and compelling arguments, the book sheds light on the hidden forces behind war, inviting readers to question their preconceived notions and reconsider the true nature of power and liberty.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political analysis, Social commentary

Themes: War and its motivations, Wealth and power, Perception versus reality
