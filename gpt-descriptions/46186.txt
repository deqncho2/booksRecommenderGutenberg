Synopsis: In "Germany in War Time: What an American Girl Saw and Heard," Mary Ethel McAuley takes us on a captivating journey through her personal experiences as an American girl living in Germany during World War I. Through her keen observations and vivid storytelling, McAuley provides a unique perspective on the war, offering a glimpse into the daily life, struggles, and resilience of the German people. From the bustling streets of Berlin to the war-torn countryside, she encounters a world filled with both heart-wrenching tragedies and unexpected moments of hope. As she navigates the complexities of war, McAuley's narrative sheds light on the human spirit, the power of empathy, and the enduring strength of the human will.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: War, Resilience, Cultural Exchange, Human Spirit
