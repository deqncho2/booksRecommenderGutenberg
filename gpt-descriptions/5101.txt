Synopsis: In "Lives of the English Poets: Prior, Congreve, Blackmore, Pope," Samuel Johnson delves into the fascinating lives and literary achievements of four influential English poets. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Johnson uncovers the triumphs, struggles, and controversies that shaped the lives of these poets, offering a captivating glimpse into the world of 18th-century English literature. From the scandalous love affairs of Prior and Congreve to the political and religious tensions surrounding Blackmore and Pope, Johnson's narrative weaves together a tapestry of passion, ambition, and artistic brilliance. This engaging exploration of the lives and works of these poets is a must-read for anyone interested in the rich history of English poetry.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: Artistic creativity, Love and desire, Politics and religion
