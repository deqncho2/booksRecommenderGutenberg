Synopsis: 

In 'Children's Ways: Being selections from the author's "Studies of childhood," with some additional matter', James Sully takes readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of children. Through a collection of insightful observations and anecdotes, Sully explores the unique ways in which children think, learn, and interact with the world around them. From their imaginative play to their curious questions, Sully delves into the intricate workings of a child's mind, shedding light on the mysteries of childhood.

Genres: Psychology, Education, Non-fiction

Themes: Childhood development, Cognitive processes, Play and imagination
