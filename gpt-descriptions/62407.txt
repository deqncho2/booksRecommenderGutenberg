Synopsis: In 'The Measure of Value Stated and Illustrated', Malthus delves into the intriguing world of currency value and its fluctuations in England since 1790. Through a meticulous analysis, he explores the factors that have influenced the value of the English currency, providing valuable insights into the economic landscape of the time. Malthus presents his findings in a captivating manner, making this work a must-read for anyone interested in understanding the complexities of currency valuation and its impact on society.

Genres: Economics, Finance, Historical Analysis

Themes: Currency valuation, Economic fluctuations, Historical economics
