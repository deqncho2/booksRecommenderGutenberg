Synopsis: In 'The Epistle of Othea to Hector; or, The Boke of Knyghthode,' Christine de Pisan takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of chivalry and knighthood. Through a series of letters from the mythical goddess Othea to the legendary hero Hector, the book explores the virtues and responsibilities of a true knight. As Othea imparts her wisdom and guidance, readers are immersed in a world of honor, bravery, and courtly love, encountering tales of renowned knights and their noble deeds. This thought-provoking work offers a unique perspective on the ideals of knighthood and the importance of moral character in medieval society.

Genres: Epistolary literature, Medieval literature, Allegory

Themes: Chivalry, Virtue, Courtly love, Moral character
