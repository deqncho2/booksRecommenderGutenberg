Synopsis: 

'Jefferson and His Colleagues: A Chronicle of the Virginia Dynasty' by Johnson, Allen is a captivating historical narrative that delves into the lives and political careers of Thomas Jefferson and his fellow Virginian statesmen during the early years of the United States. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the book explores the complex relationships, rivalries, and triumphs of these influential figures as they navigate the challenges of nation-building, foreign affairs, and the delicate balance between personal liberty and the power of the federal government. From the creation of the Declaration of Independence to the Louisiana Purchase, this chronicle offers a fascinating glimpse into the inner workings of the Virginia Dynasty and the shaping of a young nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Founding fathers, American Revolution, Democracy, States' rights, Nation-building
