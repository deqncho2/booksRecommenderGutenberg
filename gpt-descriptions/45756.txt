Synopsis: 
Step back in time to the early 20th century and immerse yourself in the intellectual discussions and groundbreaking ideas that unfolded at the Thirty-Fourth Annual Meeting of the American Library Association. Held in the charming city of Ottawa, Canada, this collection of papers and proceedings offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of librarianship during a pivotal era. From debates on the role of libraries in society to discussions on the future of cataloging and classification systems, this gathering of brilliant minds will captivate readers with its rich insights and thought-provoking exchanges.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Academic

Themes: Libraries, Intellectual discourse, Professional development, Future of librarianship
