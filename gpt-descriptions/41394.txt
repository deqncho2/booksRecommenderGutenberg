Synopsis: 

In "Michigan Trees: A Handbook of the Native and Most Important Introduced Species," Charles Herbert Otis takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse and enchanting world of trees in Michigan. From towering giants to delicate saplings, this comprehensive guide provides a wealth of information about the native and introduced tree species found in the state. With vivid descriptions, detailed illustrations, and fascinating anecdotes, Otis brings these majestic beings to life, revealing their unique characteristics, ecological significance, and historical importance. Whether you are an ardent nature lover, a botany enthusiast, or simply curious about the natural wonders of Michigan, this handbook is an indispensable companion that will deepen your appreciation for the remarkable trees that grace the state's landscapes.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature Guide, Reference

Themes: Botany, Ecology, Natural History
