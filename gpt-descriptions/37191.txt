Synopsis: In "John Greenleaf Whittier: His Life, Genius, and Writings," William Sloane Kennedy delves into the captivating life and literary contributions of the renowned American poet. From Whittier's humble beginnings as a Quaker farm boy to his rise as a prominent abolitionist and influential poet, Kennedy paints a vivid portrait of a man whose words and actions shaped the course of American history. Through engaging anecdotes and insightful analysis, this biography offers a captivating exploration of Whittier's personal struggles, his unwavering commitment to social justice, and the timeless beauty of his poetry.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Social justice, Abolitionism, Poetry, American history
