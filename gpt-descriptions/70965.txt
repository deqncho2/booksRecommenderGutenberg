Synopsis: In "The First French Republic," Horace Mann Conaway delves into the fascinating origins and contents of the Declaration of the Rights of Man, the Constitution, and the adoption of the republican form of government in 1792. Through meticulous research and analysis, Conaway uncovers the intricate web of events and ideas that shaped this pivotal moment in French history. From the tumultuous atmosphere of the French Revolution to the profound impact of Enlightenment philosophy, this book offers a captivating exploration of the birth of the First French Republic.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Revolutionary studies

Themes: Revolution, Enlightenment, Constitutionalism, Republicanism
