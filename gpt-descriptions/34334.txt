Synopsis: In "The Numerical Strength of the Confederate Army," author Randolph H. McKim delves into the argument put forth by the Hon. Charles Francis Adams and others regarding the size and effectiveness of the Confederate Army during the American Civil War. Through meticulous research and analysis, McKim challenges the prevailing beliefs and offers a fresh perspective on this crucial aspect of the war. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be captivated by the intriguing historical debate and the surprising revelations that emerge.

Genres: Historical analysis, Military history, Civil War studies

Themes: Confederate Army, American Civil War, Historical interpretation, Military strategy
