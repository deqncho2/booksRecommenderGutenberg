Synopsis: In 'Galileo Galilei and the Roman Curia: From Authentic Sources' by Karl von Gebler, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the tumultuous relationship between the renowned scientist Galileo Galilei and the powerful Roman Curia. Drawing from authentic sources, this gripping account unveils the intense conflicts, intellectual debates, and political intrigues that shaped Galileo's life and ultimately led to his infamous trial. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the clash between scientific progress and religious orthodoxy, the pursuit of truth in the face of persecution, and the enduring legacy of one of history's most brilliant minds.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Science History

Themes: Science vs. Religion, Intellectual Freedom, Persecution, Legacy
