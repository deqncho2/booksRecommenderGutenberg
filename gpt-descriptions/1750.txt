Synopsis: In 'Laws' by Plato, a group of Athenian citizens embark on a journey to establish a new society, guided by a mysterious stranger. As they grapple with the complexities of law, justice, and governance, they are forced to confront their own beliefs and question the very nature of morality. With thought-provoking dialogues and unexpected twists, 'Laws' takes readers on a captivating exploration of the human condition and the pursuit of an ideal society.

Genres: Philosophy, Political Fiction, Dialogue

Themes: Law and Justice, Morality, Governance, Ideal Society
