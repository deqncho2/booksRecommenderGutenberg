Synopsis: In 'Human Nature in Politics: Third Edition', Graham Wallas delves into the intricate relationship between human behavior and the political landscape. Through a thought-provoking exploration of historical events and psychological insights, Wallas uncovers the underlying motivations and instincts that drive political decision-making. From the complexities of power struggles to the dynamics of public opinion, this book offers a captivating analysis of the intricate interplay between human nature and politics.

Genres: Political Science, Psychology, Sociology

Themes: Human behavior, Political decision-making, Power dynamics, Public opinion
