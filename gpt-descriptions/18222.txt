Synopsis: In 'The Religion of Numa: And Other Essays on the Religion of Ancient Rome', Jesse Benedict Carter delves into the fascinating world of ancient Roman religion, exploring the beliefs, rituals, and practices that shaped the lives of its people. From the mysterious origins of the Roman gods to the role of sacrifice and divination, Carter uncovers the intricate tapestry of religious life in ancient Rome. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, he invites readers to embark on a journey of discovery, shedding light on the religious customs that influenced Roman society and its enduring legacy.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Religion

Themes: Ancient Roman religion, Mythology, Rituals and practices, Cultural influence
