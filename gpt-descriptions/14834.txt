Synopsis: 
In 'The Whence and the Whither of Man: A Brief History of His Origin and Development through Conformity to Environment; Being the Morse Lectures of 1895', John M. Tyler takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of mankind. Drawing from scientific research and historical evidence, Tyler explores the fascinating story of how humans have adapted and thrived in various environments throughout history. From the earliest stages of human existence to the complex societies of the present, this book offers a thought-provoking exploration of our origins and the forces that have shaped us.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: 
Evolution, Adaptation, Human Development
