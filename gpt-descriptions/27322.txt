Synopsis: In "An Appeal to the British Nation on the Humanity and Policy of Forming a National Institution for the Preservation of Lives and Property from Shipwreck," Sir William Hillary presents a compelling argument for the establishment of a national institution dedicated to saving lives and protecting property during shipwrecks. Drawing on his own experiences and extensive research, Hillary highlights the urgent need for a coordinated and well-equipped rescue system, emphasizing the devastating consequences of maritime disasters. Through a combination of persuasive reasoning and heartfelt anecdotes, he appeals to the British nation to recognize the importance of this humanitarian cause and take action to prevent further loss of life and property at sea.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Advocacy

Themes: Humanitarianism, Maritime safety, National responsibility
