Synopsis: In 'The Franco-German War of 1870-71', Helmuth von Moltke, a renowned military strategist, takes us on a gripping journey through one of the most significant conflicts in European history. With vivid descriptions and meticulous analysis, Moltke unveils the intricate strategies, decisive battles, and political intrigues that shaped the outcome of this war. From the initial clashes to the final triumph, this book offers a captivating account of the Franco-German War, revealing the untold stories and hidden complexities that unfolded on the battlefield and behind closed doors.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: War and diplomacy, Military strategy, Nationalism, Political power dynamics
