Synopsis: In "The Negro: The Southerner's Problem," Thomas Nelson Page delves into the complex and controversial issue of race relations in the American South. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Page explores the historical, social, and economic factors that have shaped the relationship between African Americans and white Southerners. With a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of the region's history, Page offers a nuanced perspective on the challenges and opportunities that arise from this enduring problem.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Race relations, Southern identity, Social inequality
