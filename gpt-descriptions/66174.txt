Synopsis: In 'The English Prison System', Sir Evelyn Ruggles-Brise takes readers on a captivating journey through the inner workings of the British prison system. From the dark and mysterious cells to the intricate web of rules and regulations, this book unveils the hidden truths behind the walls of justice. Through personal anecdotes and extensive research, Ruggles-Brise sheds light on the complex dynamics between prisoners and their keepers, offering a thought-provoking exploration of the human condition within the confines of incarceration.

Genres: Non-fiction, Crime, Sociology

Themes: Justice, Rehabilitation, Human Rights
