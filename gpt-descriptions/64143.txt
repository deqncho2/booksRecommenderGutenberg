Synopsis: 

In "Soil and Water Pollution: Presented to the American Public Health Association at New Orleans, Dec. 1880," author M. T. Runnels delves into a captivating exploration of a pressing environmental issue. Set in the late 19th century, this thought-provoking work takes readers on a journey through the detrimental effects of pollution on soil and water resources. Through meticulous research and compelling evidence, Runnels sheds light on the alarming consequences of human activities on the environment, leaving readers with a sense of urgency to address this growing problem.

Genres: Non-fiction, Environmental Science, Historical

Themes: Environmental degradation, Public health, Human impact on nature
