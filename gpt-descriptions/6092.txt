Synopsis: In 'The Fair Haven' by Samuel Butler, a young man named Ernest Pontifex embarks on a journey of self-discovery and rebellion against societal norms. As he navigates through various experiences and encounters, Ernest questions the conventional beliefs and values that have been imposed upon him. With wit and satire, Butler explores the complexities of religion, education, and morality, challenging readers to reconsider their own assumptions about life and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Satire, Bildungsroman, Philosophical Fiction

Themes: Rebellion against societal norms, Critique of religion and morality, Self-discovery and personal growth
