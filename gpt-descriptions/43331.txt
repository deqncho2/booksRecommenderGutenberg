Synopsis: 

In "The Domestic Life of Thomas Jefferson Compiled From Family Letters and Reminiscences," Sarah N. Randolph delves into the personal life of one of America's most enigmatic founding fathers. Through a collection of intimate family letters and reminiscences, the reader is transported into the private world of Thomas Jefferson, exploring his relationships, struggles, and triumphs. From his early years as a widower raising his daughters to his later years as a statesman and president, this captivating account sheds light on the lesser-known aspects of Jefferson's life, revealing a man who was not only a political genius but also a devoted family man.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Family, Personal Relationships, Political Life, Historical Context
