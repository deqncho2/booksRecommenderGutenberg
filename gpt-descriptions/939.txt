Synopsis: 

"The Life of Thomas Telford, Civil Engineer: With an Introductory History of Roads and Travelling in Great Britain" by Samuel Smiles takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and achievements of one of Britain's most influential civil engineers. From his humble beginnings as a shepherd boy in Scotland to his rise as a renowned architect and builder, Telford's story is one of determination, innovation, and the transformative power of infrastructure. 

This biography delves into Telford's groundbreaking projects, including the construction of roads, bridges, canals, and harbors that revolutionized transportation in Great Britain. Through vivid descriptions and anecdotes, Smiles paints a vivid picture of Telford's tireless dedication to his craft, his ability to overcome challenges, and his unwavering commitment to improving the lives of his fellow citizens. 

Genres: Biography, History, Engineering

Themes: Perseverance, Innovation, Infrastructure development
