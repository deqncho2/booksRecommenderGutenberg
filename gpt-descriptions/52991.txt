Synopsis: 

In "Waterloo Days: The narrative of an Englishwoman resident at Brussels in June 1815," Charlotte A. Eaton takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events leading up to the Battle of Waterloo. Through the eyes of an Englishwoman living in Brussels, we witness the city's transformation from a vibrant hub of society to a war-torn battleground. As the tension builds and the fate of nations hangs in the balance, our protagonist finds herself caught in a web of intrigue, romance, and danger. With vivid descriptions and a keen eye for detail, Eaton brings to life the extraordinary events that unfolded during those fateful days in June 1815.

Genres: Historical fiction, War fiction, Romance

Themes: Love and loss, Courage in the face of adversity, The impact of war on civilians
