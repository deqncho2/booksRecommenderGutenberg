Synopsis: 
In 'The Letters of Henry James (Vol. II)', readers are granted a captivating glimpse into the private world of one of the most influential American writers of the 19th century. This collection of letters, spanning a significant period of James' life, unveils his thoughts, struggles, and triumphs as he navigates the literary landscape and grapples with personal relationships. From his correspondence with fellow authors and publishers to his intimate letters to family and friends, this volume offers a rich tapestry of James' inner life, shedding light on the inspirations behind his timeless works.

Genres: 
Biography, Epistolary, Literary Criticism

Themes: 
Artistic process, Personal relationships, Literary influence
