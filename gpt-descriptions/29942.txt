Synopsis: 

In 'A Letter to Hon. Charles Sumner, with 'Statements' of Outrages upon Freedmen in Georgia', Hamilton W. Pierson pens a compelling letter that unveils a series of shocking and disturbing events taking place in Georgia. Through a collection of firsthand accounts and eyewitness testimonies, Pierson exposes the brutal mistreatment and injustices suffered by freedmen in the aftermath of the Civil War. As the narrative unfolds, the reader is drawn into a world of violence, discrimination, and oppression, where the struggle for equality and justice becomes a matter of life and death.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Political

Themes: Civil rights, Racism, Social justice, Post-Civil War America
