Synopsis:
In "British Freewomen: Their Historical Privilege," Charlotte Carmichael Stopes takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives of remarkable women who defied societal norms and embraced their freedom in British history. From the suffragettes who fought for women's right to vote to the trailblazing female scientists and artists who shattered glass ceilings, this book unveils the untold stories of these extraordinary women. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Stopes sheds light on the struggles, triumphs, and contributions of these freewomen, painting a vivid picture of their indomitable spirit and the impact they had on shaping British society. 

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Women's Studies
Themes: Women's Rights, Gender Equality, Social Change
