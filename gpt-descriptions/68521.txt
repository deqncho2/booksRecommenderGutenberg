Synopsis: 
In "Through the Mill: The Life of a Mill-Boy" by Frederic Kenyon Brown, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the world of a young mill-boy named Jack. Set in the bustling industrial era, the story follows Jack's struggles and triumphs as he navigates the harsh realities of working in a mill. From the dangerous machinery to the long hours and meager wages, Jack's determination and resilience are put to the test. Along the way, he encounters unexpected friendships, faces moral dilemmas, and discovers the power of hope amidst adversity. This gripping tale offers a vivid portrayal of the mill life, capturing the essence of an era and the indomitable spirit of its protagonist.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Drama

Themes: Industrialization, Social Inequality, Friendship, Resilience
