Synopsis: In "The Social History of Smoking," Apperson takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the fascinating and often controversial role that smoking has played in society. From ancient rituals to modern-day habits, this book delves into the intricate web of cultural, social, and economic factors that have shaped our relationship with tobacco. Through a meticulous examination of historical records, personal anecdotes, and thought-provoking analysis, Apperson uncovers surprising connections between smoking and various aspects of human existence. From the allure of smoking in literature and art to the rise of tobacco as a symbol of rebellion, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of the complex tapestry that surrounds this ubiquitous habit.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Social customs and norms, Addiction, Cultural symbolism, Health and wellness
