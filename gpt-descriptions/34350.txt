Synopsis: 
In 'A Manual of Elementary Geology', Sir Charles Lyell takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient changes of the Earth and its inhabitants, as revealed by geological monuments. From the formation of mountains and the carving of valleys to the emergence and extinction of various species, Lyell presents a comprehensive exploration of the Earth's geological history. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous observations, he unveils the fascinating processes that have shaped our planet over millions of years. This enlightening manual offers a captivating blend of scientific knowledge and storytelling, making it a must-read for anyone curious about the Earth's ancient past.

Genres: 
Science, Non-fiction, Geology

Themes: 
Geological history, Earth's formation, Evolution, Extinction, Natural processes
