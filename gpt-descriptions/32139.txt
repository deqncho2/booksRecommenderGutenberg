Synopsis: In "An Appeal to Honour and Justice, Though It Be of His Worst Enemies," Daniel Defoe presents a gripping and revealing account of his own actions and involvement in public affairs. Through a series of compelling narratives, he unveils the truth behind his conduct, shedding light on the motivations and challenges he faced. This captivating work delves into the complexities of political intrigue, personal integrity, and the pursuit of justice, leaving readers eager to uncover the secrets and revelations that lie within.

Genres: Autobiography, Political Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Personal integrity, Political intrigue, Pursuit of justice
