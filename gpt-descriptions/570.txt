Synopsis: In 'The Moravians in Georgia, 1735-1740' by Adelaide L. Fries, embark on a captivating journey through the early days of Georgia as a group of Moravian settlers face numerous challenges and forge a unique path in the New World. From their arrival in Savannah to their encounters with Native American tribes and the establishment of their own community, this historical account unveils the struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices of these resilient pioneers. As tensions rise and unexpected alliances form, the Moravians must navigate cultural differences, religious conflicts, and the ever-present threat of war. Will their unwavering faith and determination be enough to overcome the obstacles they face? Discover the untold story of the Moravians in Georgia and their lasting impact on the region.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Colonial History

Themes: Religious Persecution, Cultural Exchange, Community Building, Survival
