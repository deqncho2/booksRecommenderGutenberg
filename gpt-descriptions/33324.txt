Synopsis: 
'K. K. K. Sketches, Humorous and Didactic' takes readers on a captivating journey through the events of the Ku-Klux-Klan movement in the South. With a unique blend of humor and didacticism, James Melville Beard explores the causes that led to the rise of the Klan and delves into the social and political issues that emerged from it. Through a series of sketches, the author sheds light on the more important events of this controversial movement, offering a thought-provoking and engaging perspective on a dark chapter in American history.

Genres: 
Historical Fiction, Satire, Political Commentary

Themes: 
Racism, Social Injustice, Political Extremism
