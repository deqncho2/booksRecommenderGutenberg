Synopsis: In 'The Prince and the Pauper, Part 1' by Mark Twain, two boys from vastly different backgrounds, one a prince and the other a pauper, accidentally switch places and find themselves living each other's lives. As they navigate the challenges and adventures of their new roles, they discover the true meaning of identity, friendship, and the power of empathy. Set in Tudor England, this captivating tale explores the complexities of social class and the transformative nature of personal experiences.

Genres: Historical fiction, adventure, coming-of-age

Themes: Identity, social class, friendship, empathy
