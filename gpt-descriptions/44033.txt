Synopsis:
In "Raphael" by Paul G. Konody, a renowned art critic and historian, we are taken on a captivating journey through the life and works of one of the greatest painters of the Italian Renaissance. Through vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Konody delves into the enigmatic world of Raphael, exploring the artist's early influences, his rise to fame, and the profound impact he had on the art world. From his masterpieces such as "The School of Athens" to his complex relationships with patrons and fellow artists, this book offers a fascinating exploration of Raphael's artistic genius and the tumultuous era in which he lived.

Genres: Biography, Art History, Non-fiction

Themes: Renaissance art, artistic genius, influence and legacy
