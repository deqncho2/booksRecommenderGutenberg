Synopsis: In 'Table Talk: Essays on Men and Manners', William Hazlitt takes readers on a captivating journey through his insightful and often humorous observations of society, literature, and human nature. From dissecting the quirks of famous individuals to exploring the essence of art and the complexities of love, Hazlitt's essays offer a thought-provoking exploration of the human condition. With his sharp wit and eloquent prose, Hazlitt invites readers to engage in lively conversations about the world around them, leaving them both entertained and enlightened.

Genres: Essay, Literary Criticism, Non-fiction

Themes: Society and Culture, Human Nature, Art and Literature
