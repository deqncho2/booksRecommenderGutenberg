Synopsis: In 'The Fairfax County Courthouse', Netherton and Waldeck take readers on a captivating journey through the history of one of Virginia's most iconic landmarks. From its humble beginnings as a log cabin to its transformation into a grand courthouse, this book unveils the stories of the people who walked its halls and the cases that shaped the course of justice. With vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, the authors bring to life the trials, triumphs, and scandals that unfolded within the walls of the Fairfax County Courthouse.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Architecture

Themes: Legal history, Community development, Architectural heritage
