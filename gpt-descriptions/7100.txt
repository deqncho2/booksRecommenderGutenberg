Synopsis:
In the Adventures of Huckleberry Finn, we are introduced to the mischievous and independent-minded Huck, who embarks on a thrilling journey down the Mississippi River. Along the way, he encounters a colorful cast of characters, including the charismatic and cunning runaway slave, Jim. As Huck navigates through dangerous situations and moral dilemmas, he begins to question the society he was raised in and grapples with the concept of freedom. With humor, adventure, and a touch of social commentary, this classic American novel takes readers on an unforgettable ride.

Genres:
Adventure, Coming-of-age, Satire

Themes:
Freedom, Racism, Morality
