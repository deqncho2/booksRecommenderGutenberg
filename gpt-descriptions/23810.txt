Synopsis: 
'At Fault' by Kate Chopin is a captivating novel that delves into the complex lives of a diverse group of characters in rural Louisiana. The story revolves around Therese, a young woman torn between her duty to her family and her desire for personal freedom. As she navigates through the intricacies of love, loyalty, and societal expectations, Therese finds herself entangled in a web of secrets and forbidden desires. Set against the backdrop of a changing South, this thought-provoking tale explores the consequences of societal norms and the pursuit of personal happiness.

Genres: 
Literary Fiction, Southern Gothic, Historical Fiction

Themes: 
Love and Desire, Duty and Responsibility, Gender Roles, Social Expectations
