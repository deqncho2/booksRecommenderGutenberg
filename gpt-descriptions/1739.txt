Synopsis: In 'The Black Death, and The Dancing Mania', Hecker delves into the dark and mysterious events that plagued Europe during the Middle Ages. From the devastating Black Death that swept across the continent, leaving death and despair in its wake, to the inexplicable phenomenon of the Dancing Mania that gripped the minds and bodies of its victims, Hecker uncovers the chilling and enigmatic truths behind these historical events. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, he takes readers on a journey through the horrors and mysteries of these epidemics, revealing the profound impact they had on society and the human psyche.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Medical history, Cultural history

Themes: Disease and its impact on society, Historical epidemics, Psychological and sociological effects of mass hysteria
