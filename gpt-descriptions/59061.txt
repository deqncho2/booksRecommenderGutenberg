Synopsis: 

In 'Poisonous Snakes of Kansas', author Robert F. Clarke takes readers on a thrilling journey through the treacherous world of venomous serpents. From the secretive timber rattlesnake to the elusive copperhead, Clarke delves into the captivating lives of these deadly creatures, exploring their habitats, behaviors, and the potential dangers they pose to humans. With vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, this book serves as a gripping guide to understanding and navigating the snake-infested landscapes of Kansas.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Adventure

Themes: Wildlife, Survival, Environmental awareness
