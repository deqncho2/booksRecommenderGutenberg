Synopsis: 
'Old Familiar Faces' by Theodore Watts-Dunton is a gripping and haunting tale that follows the life of a young artist named Philip. As Philip navigates the vibrant and bohemian world of 19th-century London, he becomes entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and dark secrets. Haunted by his past and tormented by his own demons, Philip must confront the consequences of his actions and make choices that will shape his future. With its richly drawn characters and atmospheric setting, 'Old Familiar Faces' is a captivating exploration of the human psyche and the complexities of personal identity.

Genres: 
Gothic fiction, Psychological thriller, Historical fiction

Themes: 
Identity, Love and betrayal, Redemption, Psychological turmoil
