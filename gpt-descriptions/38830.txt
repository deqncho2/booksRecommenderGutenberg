Synopsis: 

In "Unfettered: A Novel" by Sutton E. Griggs, a young African American man named Frederick Douglass Washington is determined to rise above the limitations imposed by society and achieve success against all odds. Set in the late 19th century, this gripping tale follows Frederick's journey as he navigates through racial prejudice, economic hardships, and personal sacrifices in pursuit of his dreams. As he faces numerous challenges and encounters unexpected allies and adversaries, Frederick's resilience and unwavering determination are put to the ultimate test. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, African American Literature, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Racial inequality, Perseverance, Social mobility, Identity
