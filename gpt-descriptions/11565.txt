Synopsis: 

In 'Friends, though divided: A Tale of the Civil War' by G. A. Henty, two close friends find themselves on opposite sides of the American Civil War. Ned and Dick, both young and idealistic, are torn apart by their loyalties to the Union and the Confederacy. As they navigate the treacherous and brutal realities of war, their friendship is put to the ultimate test. Amidst the chaos and bloodshed, they must grapple with their own beliefs, confront the horrors of battle, and face the consequences of their choices. Will their bond survive the ravages of war, or will it be forever shattered?

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Friendship, Loyalty, Morality, Conflict, Consequences
