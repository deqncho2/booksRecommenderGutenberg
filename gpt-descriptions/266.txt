Synopsis: In 'Confessio Amantis; Or, Tales of the Seven Deadly Sins', Gower weaves a captivating tapestry of stories that explore the depths of human nature and the consequences of our actions. Through the lens of a confessional dialogue between a lover and a priest, the reader is taken on a journey through tales of love, betrayal, power, and redemption. As the sins are revealed one by one, the characters grapple with their own desires and the moral dilemmas they face. With each story, the reader is left questioning their own capacity for good and evil, and the choices that shape our lives.

Genres: Medieval literature, poetry, allegory

Themes: Morality, sin, love, power, redemption
