Synopsis: 
In 'The Regent' by Arnold Bennett, a young man named Edward Henry Machin finds himself unexpectedly thrust into a position of power and influence. As he navigates the complexities of his new role, he must confront the challenges of politics, ambition, and the temptations that come with wealth and authority. With his unique blend of wit and determination, Edward Henry sets out to make his mark on the world, but soon discovers that the path to success is not without its sacrifices and moral dilemmas. 

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Social Commentary

Themes: Power and Influence, Ambition, Morality, Social Class
