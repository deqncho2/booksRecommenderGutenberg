Synopsis:
In 'Alcibiades I' by Plato, a young and ambitious Athenian politician named Alcibiades seeks guidance from the renowned philosopher Socrates. As their conversation unfolds, Alcibiades is forced to confront his own ignorance and the true nature of wisdom. Through a series of thought-provoking dialogues, Plato explores the complexities of power, ambition, and self-knowledge, ultimately challenging the reader to question the nature of true leadership and the pursuit of knowledge.

Genres:
Philosophical dialogue, Political drama

Themes:
Wisdom and ignorance, Power and ambition, Self-knowledge, Leadership
