Synopsis: In 'The Empresses of Rome' by Joseph McCabe, readers are transported to the captivating world of ancient Rome, where power, ambition, and intrigue reign supreme. This enthralling historical narrative delves into the lives of the remarkable women who held immense influence over the Roman Empire. From the cunning Livia, who navigated the treacherous political landscape to secure her son's ascent to the throne, to the enigmatic and controversial Empress Messalina, whose scandalous reputation still echoes through history, McCabe unveils the captivating stories of these formidable empresses. As the pages turn, readers will be immersed in a world of opulence, betrayal, and the relentless pursuit of power, where these extraordinary women left an indelible mark on the annals of Roman history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Power, Ambition, Gender Roles, Politics, Betrayal
