Synopsis: In 'The Cherries of New York', Hedrick takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of cherries, exploring their origins, varieties, and the fascinating history behind their cultivation. From the vibrant cherry orchards of New York to the scientific intricacies of cherry breeding, this book unveils the secrets and wonders of this beloved fruit. With vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, Hedrick paints a vivid picture of the cherry industry, revealing the challenges faced by growers and the remarkable efforts made to create new and improved cherry varieties. Whether you're a cherry enthusiast or simply curious about the fruit's rich heritage, 'The Cherries of New York' is a delightful exploration that will leave you craving for more.

Genres: Non-fiction, Horticulture, Food and Drink

Themes: Agriculture, Botany, History, Culinary Arts
