Synopsis: 

'Presiding Ladies of the White House' is a captivating non-fiction book that delves into the lives of the remarkable women who have graced the role of First Lady. From Martha Washington to Eleanor Roosevelt, author Lila G. A. Woolfall presents biographical appreciations of these influential women, while also providing a fascinating glimpse into the history of the Executive mansion. Additionally, the book explores the intricate etiquette and customs that have shaped the White House throughout the years. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, History

Themes: Women's history, Leadership, American politics
