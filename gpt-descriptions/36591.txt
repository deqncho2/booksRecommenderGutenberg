Synopsis:
In 'Marital Power Exemplified in Mrs. Packard's Trial, and Self-Defence from the Charge of Insanity', Elizabeth Parsons Ware Packard takes us on a gripping journey through her own harrowing experience. After being unjustly committed to an insane asylum by her husband, Mrs. Packard fights for her freedom and sanity in a society that denies her agency. This powerful memoir explores the depths of marital power and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres:
Memoir, Autobiography, Feminist Literature

Themes:
Marital Power, Gender Inequality, Mental Health Advocacy
