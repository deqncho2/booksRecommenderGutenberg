Synopsis:
In "Wages in 1873: Address read before the Social Science Association at Norwich," Thomas Brassey, Earl delves into the complex and pressing issue of wages during the year 1873. Through a meticulous analysis of economic data and firsthand accounts, Brassey provides a thought-provoking exploration of the challenges faced by workers and the impact of economic fluctuations on their livelihoods. With a keen eye for detail and a compelling narrative style, Brassey sheds light on the struggles and triumphs of individuals navigating the turbulent economic landscape of the time.

Genres:
Non-fiction, Economic analysis, Historical research

Themes:
Labor rights, Economic inequality, Historical perspectives
