Synopsis: In 'Navajo Silversmiths', Washington Matthews takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of the Navajo people and their remarkable artistry. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, Matthews unveils the secrets behind the intricate silver jewelry crafted by these skilled artisans. From the historical significance of their craft to the spiritual beliefs intertwined within each piece, this report offers a fascinating glimpse into the Navajo silversmithing tradition.

Genres: Ethnography, History, Art

Themes: Native American culture, Traditional craftsmanship, Cultural preservation
