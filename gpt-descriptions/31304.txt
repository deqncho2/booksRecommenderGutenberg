Synopsis: In 'Euphorion - Vol. II: Being Studies of the Antique and the Mediaeval in the Renaissance', Lee, Vernon takes readers on a captivating journey through the Renaissance period, exploring the intricate interplay between the ancient world and the medieval era. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Lee delves into the minds of artists, scholars, and thinkers of the time, unraveling the profound impact of classical and medieval influences on the cultural and intellectual landscape of the Renaissance. From the revival of ancient texts to the reinterpretation of medieval legends, this volume offers a fascinating exploration of the Renaissance's complex relationship with its historical predecessors.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Literary criticism, Cultural studies

Themes: Renaissance, Classical influences, Medieval influences, Art and literature, Intellectual history
