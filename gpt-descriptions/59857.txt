Synopsis: 
In "The Evening Post: A Century of Journalism," Allan Nevins takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of one of America's most influential newspapers. From its humble beginnings in the early 19th century to its rise as a powerful force in shaping public opinion, this book delves into the triumphs, scandals, and challenges faced by The Evening Post and its dedicated journalists. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Nevins paints a vivid picture of the newspaper's evolution, revealing the inner workings of a publication that played a pivotal role in American society. As readers delve into the pages of this enthralling narrative, they will witness the birth of investigative journalism, the impact of technological advancements, and the complex relationship between the press and politics. 

Genres: Biography, History, Journalism

Themes: Media influence, Journalism ethics, Technological advancements, Political power
