Synopsis:
"The Record of a Quaker Conscience, Cyrus Pringle's Diary" is a captivating and introspective account of one man's journey through faith, activism, and self-discovery. Through the pages of his diary, Cyrus Pringle offers a unique perspective on the challenges and triumphs of being a Quaker in the late 19th century. From his encounters with social injustice to his personal struggles with doubt and conviction, Pringle's diary provides a thought-provoking exploration of the complexities of conscience and the power of individual action.

Genres:
Memoir, Historical Fiction, Religious Literature

Themes:
Faith and Doubt, Social Justice, Individual Conscience
