Synopsis:
'Jane Austen and Her Times' by G. E. Mitton takes readers on a captivating journey into the life and era of one of the most beloved authors in literary history. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Mitton paints a vivid picture of Jane Austen's world, exploring the social customs, political climate, and cultural nuances that shaped her writing. From the opulent ballrooms of Regency England to the quiet corners of Austen's own home, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of a remarkable woman and the times in which she lived.

Genres:
Biography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes:
Literary history, Women's role in society, Regency England
