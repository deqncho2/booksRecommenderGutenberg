Synopsis: In 'Mark Twain: A Biography. Volume II, Part 2: 1886-1900', Albert Bigelow Paine delves into the later years of the iconic American author, Mark Twain. This captivating biography takes readers on a journey through Twain's life as he navigates the challenges of fame, financial setbacks, and personal tragedies. From his travels around the world to his literary successes and failures, Paine paints a vivid portrait of Twain's complex personality and his enduring impact on American literature. As Twain faces both triumphs and tribulations, readers are drawn into a world of wit, humor, and profound insights that continue to resonate today.

Genres: Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Fame and its consequences, Personal struggles and resilience, Literary legacy
