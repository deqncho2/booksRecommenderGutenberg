Synopsis: 
In 'The Italians: A Novel', Frances Minto Dickinson Elliot takes us on a captivating journey through the vibrant streets of Italy. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the lives of two young women, Lucy and Violet, as they navigate the complexities of love, family, and societal expectations. As they immerse themselves in the rich Italian culture, they encounter a cast of intriguing characters, each with their own secrets and desires. From the bustling city of Florence to the picturesque countryside of Tuscany, the novel paints a vivid portrait of a country on the brink of change. As Lucy and Violet's paths intertwine with those around them, they must confront their own desires and confront the consequences of their choices. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Love and Desire, Identity and Self-Discovery, Societal Expectations
