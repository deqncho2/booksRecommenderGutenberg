Synopsis: In 'The White House', Paul de Kock takes us on a captivating journey through the scandalous and intriguing world of 19th-century Parisian society. The story revolves around the lives of two young women, one virtuous and innocent, the other cunning and manipulative, as they navigate the treacherous waters of love, betrayal, and social status. As their paths intertwine with a cast of colorful characters, secrets are revealed, alliances are formed, and the true nature of human desires is exposed. With its vivid portrayal of the decadence and hypocrisy of the era, 'The White House' is a gripping tale that will keep readers eagerly turning the pages.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: Love and Betrayal, Social Class, Morality, Deception
