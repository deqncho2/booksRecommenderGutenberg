Synopsis: 

In "A Constitutional League of Peace in the Stone Age of America: The League of the Iroquois and its Constitution," author J.N.B. Hewitt delves into the fascinating world of the Iroquois Confederacy, exploring their unique political system and the principles that guided their society. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Hewitt uncovers the secrets of this ancient civilization, shedding light on their remarkable constitution and the profound impact it had on their way of life. This thought-provoking work offers a fresh perspective on the history of the Americas, revealing the enduring legacy of the Iroquois Confederacy and its contributions to the development of democracy.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Anthropology, Native American studies

Themes: Indigenous governance, Democracy, Cultural preservation
