Synopsis:
'Given in Marriage' by B.M. Croker is a captivating novel that follows the life of a young woman named Muriel St. John. Set in the vibrant backdrop of colonial India, Muriel finds herself torn between duty and desire as she navigates the complexities of love, marriage, and societal expectations. As she grapples with her own desires and the pressures of her family, Muriel embarks on a journey of self-discovery that will challenge her beliefs and test the boundaries of her relationships. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, 'Given in Marriage' is a tale of passion, sacrifice, and the pursuit of happiness in a world where tradition and modernity collide.

Genres: Romance, Historical Fiction, Drama

Themes: Love and Desire, Duty and Responsibility, Tradition versus Modernity
