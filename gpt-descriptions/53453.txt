Synopsis: 

'Instincts of the Herd in Peace and War' by W. Trotter is a thought-provoking exploration of human behavior and the power of collective mentality. Through a captivating analysis of historical events and psychological theories, Trotter delves into the complex dynamics of group behavior and the influence it has on individuals. From the rise and fall of empires to the psychology behind war, this book offers a fascinating perspective on the instincts that drive human societies. 

Genres: Psychology, Sociology, History

Themes: Group psychology, Collective behavior, Power dynamics, War, Human nature
