Synopsis: In 'The Book of the Native' by Charles G. D. Roberts, a young man named David embarks on a journey to the untamed wilderness of Canada, seeking adventure and a deeper connection with nature. As he immerses himself in the rugged landscapes and encounters the indigenous people who call it home, David's perception of the world is forever transformed. Through his encounters with the native inhabitants and the challenges he faces, David learns profound lessons about the power of nature, the importance of cultural understanding, and the true meaning of belonging.

Genres: Adventure, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Nature and wilderness, Cultural exploration, Identity and belonging
