Synopsis: 
'L'Aiglon' by Edmond Rostand is a captivating play that delves into the life of Napoleon II, the son of Napoleon Bonaparte. Set in the aftermath of the French Revolution, the story follows the young prince as he grapples with his identity, torn between his desire to live up to his father's legacy and his longing for a normal life. As he navigates the complexities of power, love, and duty, the audience is taken on a poignant journey filled with political intrigue, personal struggles, and the eternal quest for self-discovery.

Genres: Drama, Historical Fiction, Tragedy

Themes: Identity, Legacy, Power, Love, Duty
