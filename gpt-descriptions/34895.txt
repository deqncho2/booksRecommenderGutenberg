Synopsis: 
In "History of the Second Massachusetts Regiment of Infantry: A prisoner's diary," Samuel M. Quincy takes us on a captivating journey through the eyes of a soldier during the American Civil War. Through his vivid and personal diary entries, we witness the harrowing experiences of the Second Massachusetts Regiment as they face the brutal realities of war, including the horrors of being taken prisoner. Quincy's account offers a unique perspective on the sacrifices, camaraderie, and resilience of those who fought in one of the most significant conflicts in American history.

Genres: 
Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Military history

Themes: 
War and its impact, Sacrifice, Brotherhood, Resilience
