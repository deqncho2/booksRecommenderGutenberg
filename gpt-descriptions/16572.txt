Synopsis: 

'Indians of the Yosemite Valley and Vicinity: Their History, Customs and Traditions' takes readers on a captivating journey into the rich cultural heritage of the indigenous people who once inhabited the breathtaking Yosemite Valley and its surrounding areas. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, author Galen Clark unveils the fascinating history, customs, and traditions of these Native American tribes, offering a unique perspective on their way of life and their deep connection to the land. From their ancient legends and spiritual beliefs to their intricate social structures and artistic expressions, this book provides a comprehensive exploration of the indigenous cultures that flourished in this awe-inspiring natural wonder. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Native American culture, Indigenous history, Environmentalism
