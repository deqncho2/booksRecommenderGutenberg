Synopsis: 

'A Guide to Plymouth and its History' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse history of Plymouth, a city steeped in fascinating stories and remarkable landmarks. From its humble beginnings as a bustling port town to its pivotal role in world-changing events, this guide offers a comprehensive exploration of Plymouth's past, present, and future. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, the authors, Helen T. Briggs and Rose T. Briggs, bring to life the captivating tales of explorers, artists, and revolutionaries who have shaped the city's identity. From the iconic Mayflower Steps to the historic Barbican district, readers will discover hidden gems and uncover the secrets that lie within Plymouth's streets. Whether you are a history enthusiast, a curious traveler, or a local resident, this guide is an essential companion for anyone seeking to unravel the captivating tapestry of Plymouth's past.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Travel

Themes: Local history, Exploration, Cultural heritage
