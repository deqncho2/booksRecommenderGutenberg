Synopsis: 
In "Shakespeare the Boy: With Sketches of the Home and School Life, Games and Sports, Manners, Customs and Folk-lore of the Time," W. J. Rolfe takes readers on a captivating journey into the childhood of the world's greatest playwright. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, Rolfe paints a vivid picture of the vibrant Elizabethan era, exploring the daily life, education, and experiences of young William Shakespeare. From his humble beginnings in Stratford-upon-Avon to his early encounters with theater and literature, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the formative years of a literary genius.

Genres: Biography, Historical Fiction, Literary Criticism

Themes: Childhood and Education, Elizabethan Society, Literary Influence
