Synopsis: In 'Black Sheep: A Novel' by Edmund Yates, we are introduced to the intriguing world of London's high society in the 19th century. The story revolves around the life of a young man named Richard, who finds himself caught between the allure of wealth and the desire for true love. As he navigates through the complexities of society, Richard becomes entangled in a web of secrets, scandals, and unexpected alliances. With each twist and turn, the reader is drawn deeper into a world where appearances can be deceiving and where the pursuit of happiness comes at a great cost.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Mystery

Themes: Social class, Love and relationships, Deception and betrayal
