Synopsis: 
'Outdoor Sketching: Four Talks Given before the Art Institute of Chicago; The Scammon Lectures, 1914' by Smith, Francis Hopkinson takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of outdoor sketching. Through a series of four enlightening talks, the author explores the beauty and challenges of capturing nature's essence on canvas. From the bustling streets of Paris to the serene landscapes of the countryside, Smith shares his experiences, techniques, and insights, offering a unique perspective on the art of sketching en plein air. With vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, this book invites readers to join the author in his artistic exploration of the natural world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Art, Memoir

Themes: Nature, Artistic process, Observational skills
