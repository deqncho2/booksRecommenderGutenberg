Synopsis: In 'The Childrens' Story of the War, Volume 2: From the Battle of Mons to the Fall of Antwerp,' author Edward Parrott takes young readers on a gripping journey through the early stages of World War I. As the conflict intensifies, the lives of children are forever changed, and they must navigate the chaos and uncertainty of war. From the harrowing Battle of Mons to the heartbreaking Fall of Antwerp, this volume explores the resilience and courage of young individuals caught in the midst of history's greatest conflict.

Genres: Historical fiction, War, Children's literature

Themes: Resilience, Courage, Innocence, Loss, Survival
