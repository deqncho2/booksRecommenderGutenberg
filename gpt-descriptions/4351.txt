Synopsis: In 'The English Constitution', Bagehot takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricacies of the British political system. With a keen eye for detail and a sharp wit, he delves into the inner workings of the monarchy, the House of Lords, and the House of Commons, unraveling the fascinating dynamics that shape the English Constitution. From the power struggles between the branches of government to the delicate balance between tradition and progress, Bagehot's exploration offers a thought-provoking analysis of the unique political landscape of England.

Genres: Political science, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Constitutional monarchy, Separation of powers, Evolution of political institutions
