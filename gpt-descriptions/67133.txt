Synopsis: In 'Waheenee: An Indian Girl's Story' by Gilbert Livingstone Wilson, readers are transported to the captivating world of Waheenee, a young Indian girl living in the late 19th century. Through her eyes, we witness the struggles and triumphs of her life as she navigates the complexities of her cultural heritage, confronts societal expectations, and embarks on a journey of self-discovery. As Waheenee's story unfolds, readers are drawn into a rich tapestry of love, loss, resilience, and the enduring power of the human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Cultural

Themes: Identity, Tradition, Resilience, Cultural Heritage
