Synopsis: In "Houston: The Feast Years. An Illustrated Essay" by Fuermann, George, embark on a captivating journey through the vibrant and ever-evolving city of Houston. Through a collection of stunning illustrations and insightful essays, Fuermann delves into the city's rich history, exploring its rise as a cultural and economic powerhouse. From its humble beginnings as a small trading post to its transformation into a bustling metropolis, Houston's story is brought to life through vivid imagery and engaging narratives. Discover the hidden gems, iconic landmarks, and diverse communities that have shaped the city's identity, all while uncovering the untold stories of the people who have called Houston home. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Urban development, Cultural diversity, Identity
