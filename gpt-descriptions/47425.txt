Synopsis: In 'The Shakespeare Myth' by Edwin Durning-Lawrence, Sir, a renowned scholar embarks on a captivating journey to uncover the truth behind the enigmatic figure of William Shakespeare. As he delves into historical records and explores hidden clues, he unravels a web of secrets that challenge the conventional narrative surrounding the Bard's identity. With each revelation, the reader is drawn deeper into a world of mystery and intrigue, questioning everything they thought they knew about one of history's greatest literary icons.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Literary Criticism

Themes: Identity, Deception, Literary Legacy
