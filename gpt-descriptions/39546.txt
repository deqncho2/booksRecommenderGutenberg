Synopsis: In 'Wit and Humor of the Bible: A Literary Study', Marion D. Shutter delves into the often overlooked comedic elements found within the pages of the Bible. Through a meticulous analysis, Shutter uncovers the clever wordplay, satirical situations, and humorous anecdotes that are scattered throughout the sacred text. From the witty banter between biblical characters to the unexpected twists and turns that elicit laughter, this book sheds light on the lighter side of the Bible, offering a fresh perspective on its timeless stories.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Religious Studies, Humor

Themes: Wit and Humor in Literature, Biblical Interpretation, Satire and Irony in the Bible
