Synopsis: In "The Hermitage, Home of Old Hickory" by Stanley F. Horn, readers are transported to the historic estate of Andrew Jackson, the seventh President of the United States. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, the author unveils the hidden stories and secrets that lie within the walls of The Hermitage. From the grandeur of the mansion to the sprawling gardens, readers will discover the fascinating life and legacy of this iconic figure in American history. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the rich historical context and the personal struggles and triumphs of Andrew Jackson, offering a unique glimpse into the life of a remarkable leader.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography

Themes: American history, Presidential legacy, Personal struggles and triumphs
