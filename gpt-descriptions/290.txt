Synopsis: 

'The Stark Munro Letters' is a captivating collection of twelve letters written by J. Stark Munro, a young medical student, to his friend Herbert Swanborough. Set in the late 19th century, the letters chronicle Munro's experiences and adventures as he navigates the challenges of his medical career and explores the complexities of life. From his struggles with poverty and the pursuit of success to his encounters with love and the darker side of human nature, Munro's letters offer a compelling glimpse into his personal growth and the world around him.

Genres: Epistolary fiction, Historical fiction, Coming-of-age

Themes: Ambition, Friendship, Love, Social class, Morality
