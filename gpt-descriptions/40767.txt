Synopsis: 

In "Old Plantation Days: Being Recollections of Southern Life Before the Civil War," Nancy Bostick De Saussure takes readers on a captivating journey through the antebellum South. Through vivid and heartfelt recollections, she paints a rich tapestry of life on a plantation, offering a glimpse into a world that has long since vanished. From the bustling slave quarters to the opulent plantation house, De Saussure's narrative immerses readers in the daily lives, traditions, and struggles of both the enslaved and the slaveholders. With a keen eye for detail and a compassionate voice, she explores the complexities of race, class, and gender, shedding light on the intricate dynamics that shaped the Old South. 

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Southern Literature
Themes: Antebellum South, Plantation Life, Slavery, Race Relations, Gender Roles
