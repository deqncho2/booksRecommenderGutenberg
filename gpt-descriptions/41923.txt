Synopsis: In 'The Golden Bough: A Study in Magic and Religion', James George Frazer delves into the fascinating world of ancient beliefs and rituals, exploring the connections between magic, religion, and society. This third edition, Volume 06 of 12, takes readers on a captivating journey through various cultures and civilizations, uncovering the intricate web of myths, rites, and superstitions that have shaped human history. From the mystical practices of ancient priests to the curious customs of tribal communities, Frazer's meticulous research and insightful analysis shed light on the universal human quest for meaning and the power of belief.

Genres: Anthropology, History, Comparative Religion

Themes: Magic, Religion, Ritual, Mythology, Cultural Anthropology
