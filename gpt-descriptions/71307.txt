Synopsis: 
In "Slang and cant in Jerome K. Jerome's works: A study" by Bosson, Olof E., readers are taken on a fascinating exploration of the use of slang and cant in the works of renowned author Jerome K. Jerome. This study delves into the various forms of language that Jerome employs, from colloquialisms to specialized jargon, and examines how they contribute to the overall tone and atmosphere of his writings. Through meticulous analysis and insightful commentary, Bosson sheds light on the significance of slang and cant in Jerome's works, offering readers a deeper understanding of the author's unique style and the social context in which his stories are set.

Genres: Literary criticism, Linguistics

Themes: Language and communication, Social context
