Synopsis: In 'Walled In: A True Story of Randall's Island' by William O. Stoddard, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the haunting history of Randall's Island, a small piece of land in the heart of New York City. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Stoddard uncovers the forgotten tales of the island's past, revealing a dark and mysterious world that has long been hidden from public view. From its origins as a Native American burial ground to its transformation into a notorious psychiatric institution, the island's secrets are gradually unveiled, leaving readers both fascinated and disturbed. As the author delves deeper into the island's history, he uncovers shocking truths and encounters unexpected characters, ultimately leading to a chilling climax that will leave readers questioning the nature of reality and the power of the human mind.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Mystery

Themes: Forgotten history, Mental health, Urban legends
