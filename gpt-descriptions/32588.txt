Synopsis: In "A Problem in Modern Ethics," John Addington Symonds delves into the controversial topic of sexual inversion, exploring its psychological and legal implications. Through a meticulous inquiry, he addresses medical psychologists and jurists, shedding light on the phenomenon and its impact on society. With a thought-provoking approach, Symonds challenges conventional beliefs and offers a unique perspective on this complex issue.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Ethics

Themes: Sexual inversion, Psychology, Morality, Social norms
