Synopsis: In 'The Soul of Man under Socialism', Oscar Wilde presents a thought-provoking exploration of the individual's place in society and the potential for true freedom. Through a series of insightful essays, Wilde challenges conventional notions of work, wealth, and progress, offering a radical vision of a society where art, beauty, and self-expression reign supreme. With his characteristic wit and wisdom, Wilde delves into the complexities of human nature and the transformative power of embracing one's true self. 

Genres: Essay, Social criticism, Philosophy

Themes: Individualism, Socialism, Artistic freedom, Human nature, Self-expression
