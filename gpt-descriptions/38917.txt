Synopsis: 

In 'Life and Writings of Maurice Maeterlinck', Jethro Bithell delves into the captivating world of the renowned Belgian playwright and essayist, Maurice Maeterlinck. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Bithell unveils the enigmatic life and literary contributions of this influential figure. From Maeterlinck's early struggles to his meteoric rise to fame, the book offers a fascinating exploration of the man behind the words. With glimpses into his personal relationships, creative process, and the societal context in which he thrived, readers are transported into the captivating world of this literary genius. 

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: Artistic expression, Existentialism, Symbolism
