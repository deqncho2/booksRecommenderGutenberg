Synopsis: In "The Life and Times of Alfred the Great: Being the Ford lectures for 1901," Charles Plummer takes readers on a captivating journey through the remarkable life of one of England's most revered historical figures. From Alfred's early struggles and unexpected rise to power, to his relentless efforts to defend his kingdom against Viking invasions, this book offers a vivid portrayal of a man who defied the odds and left an indelible mark on history. Plummer's meticulous research and engaging storytelling bring to life the challenges, triumphs, and complexities of Alfred's reign, shedding light on the man behind the legend.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Leadership, Warfare, Cultural Identity
