Synopsis: 

"The bedbug: Its relation to public health, its habits and life history, and methods of control" is a comprehensive and intriguing exploration of the notorious bedbug and its impact on public health. This informative work delves into the fascinating habits and life cycle of these blood-sucking pests, shedding light on their elusive nature and the challenges they pose to society. The book also provides valuable insights into various methods of control, offering readers a glimpse into the ongoing battle against these resilient creatures. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Public Health, Entomology

Themes: Public health, Pest control, Entomology
