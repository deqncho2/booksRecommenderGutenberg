Synopsis: In 'A Little Journey in the World', Charles Dudley Warner takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate web of politics, business, and society in a small New England town. The story follows the ambitious and cunning protagonist, Richard Shackford, as he navigates the complex world of power, wealth, and social status. As Richard rises through the ranks, he encounters love, betrayal, and moral dilemmas that force him to question his own values and the true nature of success. With vivid characters and a compelling narrative, this novel explores the dark underbelly of ambition and the consequences of one's choices in a rapidly changing world.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Social Commentary

Themes: Ambition, Power, Morality, Social Status, Betrayal
