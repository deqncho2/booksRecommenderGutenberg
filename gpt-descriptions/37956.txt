Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The Life and Letters of Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley', Julian Marshall delves into the fascinating life of the renowned author, Mary Shelley. Through a collection of her personal letters and insightful analysis, Marshall uncovers the untold stories and hidden emotions behind Shelley's literary genius. From her tumultuous relationships to her struggles with fame and loss, this volume offers a captivating glimpse into the complex world of one of the most influential female writers of the 19th century.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Letters

Themes: Feminism, Romanticism, Creativity, Love and Loss
