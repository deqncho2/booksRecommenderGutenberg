Synopsis: In "Facts and Fancies in Modern Science," Sir John William Dawson delves into the fascinating intersection of science, prevalent speculations, and religious belief. Through a series of thought-provoking studies, he explores the complex relationship between scientific discoveries and the prevailing beliefs of his time. From the mysteries of evolution to the wonders of geology, Dawson presents a captivating exploration of the ever-evolving landscape of scientific thought and its implications for religious faith. This book offers a compelling journey through the realms of science and belief, challenging readers to reconsider their own perspectives on the nature of truth and the boundaries of human knowledge.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Philosophy

Themes: Science and religion, Evolution, Philosophy of science, Belief systems
