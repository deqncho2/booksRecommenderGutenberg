Synopsis: In 'London in the Jacobite times, Volume I', Dr. Doran takes us on a captivating journey through the bustling streets of 18th-century London, a city teeming with political intrigue, secret societies, and hidden agendas. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the author unveils the hidden underbelly of the city during the tumultuous Jacobite era, where loyalty and betrayal walk hand in hand. From the opulent salons of the aristocracy to the grimy taverns of the common folk, this immersive narrative paints a vivid picture of a city on the brink of revolution, where loyalties are tested and alliances are forged in the shadows. As the plot unfolds, readers will find themselves entangled in a web of mystery, danger, and forbidden romance, as the fate of London hangs in the balance.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Romance

Themes: Political intrigue, Loyalty, Betrayal, Revolution
