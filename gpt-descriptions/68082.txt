Synopsis: 

"The blackest page in modern history: Events in Armenia in 1915: the facts and the responsibilities" by Herbert Adams Gibbons delves into the tragic events that unfolded in Armenia in 1915. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Gibbons uncovers the facts surrounding this dark chapter, shedding light on the responsibilities of those involved. From the harrowing accounts of survivors to the political motivations behind the atrocities, this book offers a gripping exploration of a forgotten tragedy that continues to resonate today.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Genocide studies

Themes: Human rights, Responsibility, Historical memory, Political accountability
