Synopsis: 
In 'Stage-Land' by Jerome K. Jerome, the reader is taken on a whimsical journey through the world of the theater. The protagonist finds himself transported to a parallel universe where the characters from various plays come to life. As he navigates this strange land, he encounters a colorful cast of characters, including Shakespearean heroes, melodramatic villains, and even a few ghosts. With humor and wit, Jerome explores the quirks and idiosyncrasies of the theatrical world, offering a satirical commentary on the art of acting and the nature of performance.

Genres: Comedy, Satire, Fantasy

Themes: Theatricality, Performance, Satire of Victorian Society
