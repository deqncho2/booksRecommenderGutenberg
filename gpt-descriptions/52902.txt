Synopsis: 
In 'A Naval Expositor: Shewing and Explaining the Words and Terms of Art Belonging to the Parts, Qualities and Proportions of Building, Rigging, Furnishing, & Fitting a Ship for Sea,' Thomas Riley Blanckley takes readers on a fascinating journey into the intricate world of shipbuilding and naval terminology. Through detailed explanations and illustrations, Blanckley unveils the secrets behind the construction, rigging, and outfitting of ships, providing a comprehensive guide for both aspiring sailors and curious landlubbers. From the intricacies of ship design to the art of navigating the open seas, this book offers a captivating exploration of the maritime world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Reference, Maritime literature
Themes: Shipbuilding, Naval terminology, Maritime exploration
