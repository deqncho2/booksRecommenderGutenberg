Synopsis: 
"One Hundred Years in Yosemite: The Story of a Great Park and Its Friends" by Carl Parcher Russell takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of Yosemite National Park. From its early days as a pristine wilderness to the challenges it faced in becoming a protected area, this book delves into the stories of the people who fought to preserve its natural beauty. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Russell paints a vivid picture of the park's evolution, highlighting the triumphs and setbacks that have shaped its legacy. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Environmental Studies

Themes: Conservation, Wilderness, Preservation, Human-Nature Relationship
