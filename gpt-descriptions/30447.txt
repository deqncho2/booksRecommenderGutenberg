Synopsis: In "Snow on the Headlight: A Story of the Great Burlington Strike," Cy Warman takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the Great Burlington Strike. Set against the backdrop of the late 19th century, this captivating narrative follows the lives of railroad workers and their families as they face the challenges of labor disputes, social inequality, and the harsh winter conditions that threaten their livelihoods. As tensions rise and the strike intensifies, the characters find themselves caught in a web of personal and political conflicts, testing their resilience and determination. With vivid descriptions and compelling storytelling, Warman paints a vivid picture of a pivotal moment in American history, where ordinary individuals must confront extraordinary circumstances.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Drama, Social Commentary

Themes: Labor rights, Social inequality, Resilience, Community, Power dynamics
