Synopsis: 
In "Cicero's Tusculan Disputations: Also, Treatises On The Nature Of The Gods, And On The Commonwealth," the renowned Roman philosopher and orator, Marcus Tullius Cicero, engages in a series of captivating and thought-provoking discussions. Through his eloquent dialogues, Cicero explores profound questions about the nature of happiness, the existence of the gods, and the principles of a just society. Delving into the realms of philosophy, ethics, and politics, this collection of treatises offers a fascinating glimpse into the intellectual world of ancient Rome.

Genres: Philosophy, Political Science, Classics

Themes: Happiness, Religion, Justice, Ethics
