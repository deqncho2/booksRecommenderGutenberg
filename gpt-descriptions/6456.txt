Synopsis: In 'Public Opinion', Walter Lippmann delves into the complex world of public perception and its impact on democracy. Through insightful analysis and thought-provoking examples, Lippmann explores how individuals form their opinions, the role of media in shaping public sentiment, and the challenges faced by leaders in navigating this intricate landscape. As he uncovers the inherent limitations and biases of public opinion, Lippmann offers a compelling exploration of the delicate balance between the will of the people and the need for informed decision-making. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, Sociology

Themes: Public perception, Media influence, Democracy, Leadership
