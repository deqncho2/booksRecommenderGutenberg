Synopsis: 

'The First Canadians in France: The Chronicle of a Military Hospital in the War Zone' by F. McKelvey Bell takes readers on a gripping journey through the experiences of a military hospital in the midst of war. Set in France during World War I, the story follows a group of Canadian medical professionals who find themselves thrust into the chaos and devastation of the front lines. As they navigate the challenges of treating wounded soldiers and witnessing the horrors of war, they also grapple with their own personal struggles and the profound impact the war has on their lives. 

Genres: Historical fiction, War fiction, Medical fiction

Themes: War and its effects on individuals and society, Sacrifice and heroism, Human resilience and compassion
