Synopsis: In the small town of Dawson's Landing, a mysterious and enigmatic man named Pudd'nhead Wilson arrives, known for his peculiar habit of collecting fingerprints. As the story unfolds, a shocking crime takes place, leading to a series of unexpected events and revelations. Pudd'nhead Wilson finds himself entangled in a web of deceit, identity confusion, and racial tensions, all while trying to uncover the truth behind the crime. Twain's masterful storytelling keeps readers on the edge of their seats, questioning the nature of identity and the consequences of societal prejudices.

Genres: Mystery, Satire, Historical Fiction

Themes: Identity, Race, Prejudice, Social Injustice
