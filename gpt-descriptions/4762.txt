Synopsis: 

In 'Civil Government of Virginia', author William Fayette Fox presents a comprehensive and engaging text-book that delves into the intricacies of the Virginia Constitution of 1902 and the laws that were enacted in accordance with it. This educational resource is designed to provide students with a deep understanding of the civil government of Virginia, exploring topics such as the structure of government, the rights and responsibilities of citizens, and the role of the judiciary. Through clear and concise explanations, Fox offers readers a valuable insight into the workings of the state's government, fostering a sense of civic duty and encouraging active participation in the democratic process.

Genres: Educational, Non-fiction

Themes: Government structure, Civic duty, Democracy
