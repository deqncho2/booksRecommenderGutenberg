Synopsis: 
'Legends, Traditions, and Laws of the Iroquois, or Six Nations, and History of the Tuscarora Indians' by Elias Johnson is a captivating exploration of the rich cultural heritage and historical significance of the Iroquois Confederacy. Through a collection of legends, traditions, and laws, Johnson delves into the fascinating world of the Iroquois, shedding light on their customs, beliefs, and the profound impact they had on the formation of the United States. From the creation stories that explain the origins of the world to the intricate social and political structures of the Six Nations, this book offers a captivating journey into the heart of Iroquois culture and history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Cultural heritage, Indigenous history, Oral traditions, Social and political structures
