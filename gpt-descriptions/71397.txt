Synopsis: 

'Common Poisonous Plants of New England' is a comprehensive guidebook that unveils the hidden dangers lurking in the region's flora. Written by renowned botanists Karas and Youngken, this captivating work takes readers on a journey through the lush landscapes of New England, revealing the sinister secrets of its most toxic plants. From innocuous-looking wildflowers to seemingly harmless berries, the authors shed light on the potentially lethal properties of these plants, providing invaluable knowledge for nature enthusiasts, hikers, and gardeners alike. With vivid descriptions and stunning photographs, this book serves as a cautionary tale, reminding us to tread carefully amidst the beauty of nature.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Reference

Themes: Plant toxicity, Nature's hidden dangers, Environmental awareness
