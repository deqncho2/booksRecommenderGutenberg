Synopsis: 

'Tin-Types Taken in the Streets of New York' is a captivating collection of stories and sketches that offer a unique glimpse into the diverse and vibrant tapestry of metropolitan life. Through vivid descriptions and engaging narratives, author Lemuel Ely Quigg takes readers on a journey through the bustling streets of New York, capturing the essence of its people and their extraordinary experiences. From the struggles of immigrants to the aspirations of ambitious dreamers, from the hidden corners of the city to the grandeur of its landmarks, this collection paints a vivid portrait of the human condition in the urban landscape. 

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories, Urban Literature

Themes: City life, Diversity, Human experience, Ambition, Immigrant stories
