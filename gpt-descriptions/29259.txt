Synopsis: In "The Child and the Curriculum," John Dewey explores the dynamic relationship between education and the individual child. Through thought-provoking insights and engaging anecdotes, Dewey challenges traditional notions of curriculum and advocates for a more student-centered approach. As he delves into the complexities of education, Dewey invites readers to question the purpose of schooling and the role of the curriculum in fostering meaningful learning experiences. This thought-provoking work offers a fresh perspective on education and its impact on the development of young minds.

Genres: Education, Philosophy

Themes: Student-centered learning, Curriculum development, Educational philosophy
