Synopsis: In 'A House Divided Against Itself', the second volume of this gripping trilogy by Oliphant, Mrs. (Margaret), the lives of two families become entangled in a web of secrets, betrayals, and unexpected alliances. As tensions rise and loyalties are tested, the characters must navigate the treacherous waters of love, power, and ambition. With each page, the plot thickens, leaving readers on the edge of their seats, eagerly anticipating the final installment.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Drama, Mystery

Themes: Family dynamics, Social class, Morality, Deception
