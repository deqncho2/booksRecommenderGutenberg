Synopsis: In "A Journal of the Disasters in Affghanistan, 1841-2," Florentia Wynch Sale takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing events of the First Anglo-Afghan War. Through her vivid and intimate journal entries, we witness the triumphs and tragedies, the courage and despair, as the British forces face unimaginable challenges in the unforgiving Afghan terrain. With her keen observations and heartfelt reflections, Sale provides a unique perspective on the human cost of war and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, War literature

Themes: War and its consequences, Resilience, Cultural clash, Survival
