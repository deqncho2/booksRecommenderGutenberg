Synopsis: In 'Cups and Their Customs', Dr. Henry Porter and George Edwin Roberts take readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of cups and the diverse customs associated with them. From ancient civilizations to modern times, this book explores the rich history, symbolism, and cultural significance of cups across different societies. Delving into intriguing anecdotes and surprising facts, the authors unveil the hidden stories behind these everyday objects, revealing how cups have shaped human rituals, social interactions, and even superstitions. Whether it's the ceremonial cups of ancient Egypt or the humble tea cups of England, this book offers a delightful exploration of the remarkable role cups have played in shaping human civilization.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Cultural customs, Symbolism, Human rituals
