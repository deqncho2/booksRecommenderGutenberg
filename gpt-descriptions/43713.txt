Synopsis: 

In "The Butterflies of the British Isles," Richard South takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting world of butterflies found in the British Isles. From the vibrant colors of the Red Admiral to the delicate beauty of the Small Tortoiseshell, South's meticulous descriptions and stunning illustrations bring these winged creatures to life. As he explores their habitats, behaviors, and life cycles, South uncovers fascinating facts and anecdotes that will leave readers in awe of these remarkable insects. Whether you are a seasoned lepidopterist or simply curious about the natural wonders around us, this book is a delightful and informative guide to the butterflies of the British Isles.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Field Guide

Themes: Nature appreciation, Biodiversity, Environmental conservation
