Synopsis: In 'The United States and the War', Murray Gilbert takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex relationship between the United States and the global conflict that shook the world. From the nation's initial reluctance to get involved to its eventual pivotal role, Gilbert delves into the political, social, and economic factors that shaped America's decision-making process. Through a meticulous examination of key events and influential figures, this book offers a thought-provoking exploration of the United States' impact on the war and the war's impact on the United States.

Genres: History, Political Science, International Relations

Themes: American foreign policy, World War I, nationalism, diplomacy, war and society
