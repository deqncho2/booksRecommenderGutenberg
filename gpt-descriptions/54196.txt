Synopsis: 
In "The Practical Book of Oriental Rugs," Lewis G. Griffin takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and intricate craftsmanship of Oriental rugs. From the bustling bazaars of Persia to the serene villages of Turkey, Griffin unveils the secrets behind these mesmerizing works of art. With vivid descriptions and stunning visuals, he explores the various weaving techniques, patterns, and motifs that make each rug unique. Along the way, readers will discover the fascinating stories behind these rugs, from the ancient legends that inspired their designs to the skilled artisans who bring them to life. Whether you are a seasoned collector or simply appreciate the beauty of these timeless treasures, this book is a must-read for anyone seeking to unravel the mysteries of Oriental rugs.

Genres: Non-fiction, Art, History, Crafts

Themes: Cultural heritage, Artistry, Craftsmanship, Tradition
