Synopsis: In "What Diantha Did," we follow the journey of Diantha Bell, a young woman who defies societal expectations and embarks on a path of self-discovery and empowerment. Leaving behind her conventional life, Diantha takes on a new role as the manager of a cooperative housekeeping establishment, challenging traditional gender roles and advocating for women's rights. As she navigates the complexities of love, work, and personal growth, Diantha's determination and resilience shine through, inspiring readers to question the limitations placed upon them and embrace their own potential.

Genres: Feminist literature, social commentary, bildungsroman

Themes: Gender roles, women's empowerment, societal expectations, self-discovery
