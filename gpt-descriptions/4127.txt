Synopsis: In the tenth volume of his famous diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a captivating journey through the months of April and May in the year 1661. As Pepys navigates the bustling streets of London, he encounters a myriad of intriguing characters and finds himself entangled in a web of political intrigue, scandalous affairs, and personal triumphs. From attending grand masquerade balls to witnessing the coronation of King Charles II, Pepys provides a vivid and intimate account of the vibrant social scene and the tumultuous political landscape of Restoration England. With his sharp wit and keen observations, Pepys invites readers to experience the exhilarating highs and devastating lows of his eventful life during this pivotal period in history.

Genres: Diary, Historical Fiction, Memoir

Themes: Politics, Society, Personal Growth, Intrigue
