Synopsis: 

"The Silent Watchers: England's Navy during the Great War: What It Is, and What We Owe to It" takes readers on a captivating journey through the crucial role played by England's Navy during World War I. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Copplestone and Bennet shed light on the untold stories of the brave men and women who served in the Navy, their sacrifices, and the immense impact they had on the outcome of the war. From the treacherous battles at sea to the strategic planning behind the scenes, this book unveils the hidden heroes and the unwavering determination that shaped history.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Military History, War

Themes: Heroism, Sacrifice, Naval Warfare, Historical Legacy
