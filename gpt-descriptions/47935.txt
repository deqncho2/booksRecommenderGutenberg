Synopsis: 
In Turgenev's "Fathers and Sons," a young nihilist named Bazarov returns to his family's estate, challenging the traditional values and beliefs of his parents and their generation. As he forms an unlikely friendship with a fellow student, Arkady, their contrasting ideologies clash with the conservative society they find themselves in. Through passionate debates, romantic entanglements, and unexpected tragedies, the novel explores the complexities of generational conflict and the search for personal identity in a rapidly changing world.

Genres: 
Realistic fiction, Bildungsroman, Social commentary

Themes: 
Generational conflict, Nihilism, Love and relationships, Social change
