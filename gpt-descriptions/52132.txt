Synopsis: 

In "Sunshine and Storm in Rhodesia," Frederick Courteney Selous takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events in Matabeleland during the native insurrection. Through his vivid narrative, Selous provides a firsthand account of the challenges faced by the Bulawayo Field Force and the gripping moments leading up to their disbandment. From the peaceful days of sunshine to the violent storms of conflict, this book offers a compelling exploration of the trials and triumphs experienced in Rhodesia.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, adventure, memoir

Themes: Colonialism, war, resilience, exploration
