Synopsis: In "Little Women; Or, Meg, Jo, Beth, and Amy," we follow the lives of the four March sisters - Meg, Jo, Beth, and Amy - as they navigate the challenges and joys of growing up in 19th-century New England. Meg, the eldest, dreams of a traditional life filled with love and marriage. Jo, the tomboyish and fiercely independent second sister, aspires to become a successful writer. Beth, the gentle and kind-hearted third sister, finds solace in music and caring for others. And Amy, the youngest, is determined to become a refined and accomplished lady. Through their trials and triumphs, the March sisters learn the importance of family, friendship, and staying true to oneself.

Genres: Coming-of-age, family drama, historical fiction

Themes: Sisterhood, female empowerment, societal expectations, love and marriage, personal growth
