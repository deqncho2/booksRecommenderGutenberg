Synopsis: In 'Daring and Suffering: A History of the Great Railroad Adventure,' William Pittenger takes readers on a thrilling journey through one of the most audacious and perilous adventures of the Civil War era. Set during the height of the conflict, this gripping narrative follows a group of Union soldiers as they embark on a daring mission to sabotage Confederate railroads deep behind enemy lines. Facing constant danger, unexpected twists, and unimaginable hardships, these brave men must rely on their wits, courage, and camaraderie to survive. Pittenger's vivid storytelling and firsthand account of this incredible true story will keep readers on the edge of their seats, eager to discover the outcome of this extraordinary railroad adventure.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War, Adventure

Themes: Courage, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Survival
