Synopsis: In 'The Mutineers: A Tale of Old Days at Sea and of Adventures in the Far East as Benjamin Lathrop Set It Down Some Sixty Years Ago', Charles Boardman Hawes takes readers on a thrilling journey through the treacherous waters of the high seas and the exotic lands of the Far East. Set sixty years ago, the story follows Benjamin Lathrop, a young sailor who finds himself caught up in a mutiny aboard a merchant ship. As the mutineers take control, Benjamin must navigate the dangerous waters of loyalty, betrayal, and survival. Along the way, he encounters breathtaking adventures, encounters with pirates, and unexpected alliances. With vivid descriptions and captivating storytelling, Hawes paints a vivid picture of life at sea and the mysteries of the Far East.

Genres: Adventure, Historical Fiction

Themes: Loyalty, Betrayal, Survival, Exploration
