Synopsis: In the final volume of 'The seven books of Paulus Ægineta', the reader is transported back in time to explore the vast knowledge possessed by the ancient Greeks, Romans, and Arabians in the field of medicine and surgery. Through the translated writings of Paulus Ægineta, accompanied by a comprehensive commentary, readers are immersed in a world where ancient medical practices and surgical techniques are brought to life. From the intricate details of herbal remedies to the complexities of surgical procedures, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the historical development of medical knowledge and its practical applications.

Genres: Historical, Medical, Commentary

Themes: Ancient Medicine, Surgical Techniques, Historical Development of Medical Knowledge
