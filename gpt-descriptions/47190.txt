Synopsis: In "Foot-prints of a letter carrier; or, a history of the world's correspondence," James Rees takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of global communication. From ancient civilizations to modern times, this book unveils the untold stories behind the letters, postcards, and messages that have shaped our world. Through the eyes of a letter carrier, readers will discover the secrets, scandals, and triumphs hidden within the envelopes, shedding light on the profound impact of correspondence on individuals and societies. With each turn of the page, Rees uncovers the remarkable tales that have been etched into the footprints left by letter carriers throughout history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Epistolary literature, Cultural history

Themes: Communication, Human connection, Historical events, Technological advancements
