Synopsis:
In "The History of the Great Irish Famine of 1847 (3rd ed.) (1902): With Notices of Earlier Irish Famines," John O'Rourke, Canon delves into the tragic events of the Great Irish Famine, a devastating period in Irish history. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, O'Rourke uncovers the causes, consequences, and the human stories behind this catastrophic event. From the struggles of the Irish people to the political and economic factors that exacerbated the famine, this book offers a comprehensive account of one of the darkest chapters in Ireland's past.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Irish history, Social commentary

Themes:
Human suffering, Political and economic factors, Resilience and survival
