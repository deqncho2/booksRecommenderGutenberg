Synopsis: In the final installment of 'The Greville Memoirs', Charles Greville takes us on a captivating journey through the reign of Queen Victoria from 1852 to 1860. As a close observer and confidant of the Queen, Greville provides an intimate and revealing account of the political and social landscape of the time. From the tumultuous events surrounding the Crimean War to the rise of the Great Exhibition, Greville's journal offers a unique perspective on the challenges and triumphs of Queen Victoria's reign. With his sharp wit and astute observations, Greville paints a vivid picture of the era, filled with political intrigue, scandal, and the complexities of royal life.

Genres: Memoir, History, Biography

Themes: Monarchy, Politics, Society, Power, Victorian Era
