Synopsis: In 'Mark Rutherford's Deliverance' by William Hale White, we follow the life of Mark Rutherford, a man who struggles with his own identity and the constraints of society. As he navigates through various relationships and experiences, Mark grapples with the conflict between his inner desires and the expectations placed upon him. Set against the backdrop of Victorian England, this thought-provoking novel explores themes of self-discovery, societal norms, and the pursuit of personal freedom.

Genres: Fiction, Bildungsroman, Historical Fiction

Themes: Identity, Society, Freedom
