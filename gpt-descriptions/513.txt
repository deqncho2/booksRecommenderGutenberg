Synopsis: In "The Snow-Image, and Other Twice-Told Tales," Nathaniel Hawthorne weaves a collection of enchanting and mysterious stories that delve into the depths of human nature. From a young boy's creation of a magical snow-image to a man's encounter with a ghostly apparition, each tale explores the complexities of love, guilt, and the blurred lines between reality and imagination. With his signature blend of dark romanticism and psychological insight, Hawthorne invites readers into a world where ordinary lives are touched by extraordinary events, leaving them questioning the boundaries of the human experience.

Genres: Dark Romanticism, Gothic Fiction, Psychological Fiction

Themes: Love, Guilt, Imagination, Reality, Human Nature
