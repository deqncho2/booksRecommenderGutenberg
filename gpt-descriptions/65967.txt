Synopsis: 
In 'The History of Silk, Cotton, Linen, Wool, and Other Fibrous Substances', Clinton G. Gilroy takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of textiles. From the ancient origins of silk in China to the intricate techniques of dyeing and weaving, this book explores the rich history and cultural significance of various fibrous substances. Delving into the secrets of spinning and the art of dyeing, Gilroy uncovers the remarkable stories behind these materials and the people who have shaped their production over centuries. With vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, this book offers a comprehensive exploration of the evolution of textiles and their impact on human civilization.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Craft

Themes: Textile history, Cultural significance, Craftsmanship
