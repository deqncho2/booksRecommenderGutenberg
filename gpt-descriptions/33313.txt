Synopsis: 

In 'British Committees, Commissions, and Councils of Trade and Plantations, 1622-1675', Charles McLean Andrews delves into the intriguing world of British colonial governance during the early 17th century. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Andrews uncovers the hidden machinations and power struggles that shaped the British Empire's trade and plantation policies. From the halls of Parliament to the distant shores of the New World, this book unveils a web of political intrigue, economic ambition, and colonial expansion that will leave readers spellbound.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Political History, Colonial Studies

Themes: Colonial Governance, Economic Ambition, Political Power Struggles
