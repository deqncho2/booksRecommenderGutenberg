Synopsis: In 'Ex Voto: An Account of the Sacro Monte or New Jerusalem at Varallo-Sesia: With Some Notice of Tabachetti's Remaining Work at the Sanctuary of Crea', Samuel Butler takes readers on a captivating journey through the Sacro Monte, a religious complex in Italy. Through vivid descriptions and historical anecdotes, Butler explores the intricate sculptures and artwork that adorn the site, shedding light on the fascinating life and work of the artist Tabachetti. As the author delves into the mysteries and symbolism behind the Sacro Monte, readers are transported to a world where faith, art, and history intertwine.

Genres: Non-fiction, Travelogue, Art History

Themes: Religion, Artistic Expression, History
