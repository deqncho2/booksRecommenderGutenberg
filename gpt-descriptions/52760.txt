Synopsis: In 'Campaign for Petersburg' by Richard Wayne Lykes, a gripping tale unfolds as a group of soldiers find themselves thrust into the heart of the Civil War's most pivotal battle. As they navigate the treacherous landscape of Petersburg, Virginia, they must confront not only the physical dangers of war but also the moral dilemmas that arise in the face of adversity. With each passing day, the soldiers are tested in ways they never could have imagined, forcing them to question their loyalties and the true meaning of sacrifice. Will they emerge victorious, or will the weight of the war crush their spirits?

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Action/Adventure

Themes: Loyalty, Sacrifice, Morality, Camaraderie
