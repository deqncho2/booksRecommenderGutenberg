Synopsis: 

In 'Forerunners and rivals of Christianity: being studies in religious history from 330 B.C. to 330 A.D., Vol. 2 (of 2)', Francis Legge takes readers on a captivating journey through the religious landscape of the ancient world. Delving into the period from 330 B.C. to 330 A.D., Legge explores the diverse religious movements and figures that emerged as precursors and competitors to Christianity. From the mystical teachings of the Gnostics to the philosophical musings of the Stoics, Legge uncovers the rich tapestry of beliefs and practices that shaped the religious milieu of the time. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the intellectual and spiritual currents that influenced the development of Christianity.

Genres: Religious history, Comparative religion, Historical analysis

Themes: Religious syncretism, Intellectual and spiritual influences, Development of Christianity
