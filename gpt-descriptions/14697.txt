Synopsis: In "Lewis Rand" by Mary Johnston, the reader is transported to the political landscape of 19th-century America, where the ambitious and charismatic Lewis Rand rises through the ranks, driven by his desire for power and success. As he navigates the treacherous world of politics, Rand encounters love, betrayal, and moral dilemmas that challenge his convictions. With a cast of intriguing characters and a backdrop of political intrigue, this gripping novel explores the complexities of ambition and the consequences of one man's relentless pursuit of power.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Drama, Romance

Themes: Ambition, Power, Love, Betrayal
