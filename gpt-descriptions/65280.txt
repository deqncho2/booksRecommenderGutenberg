Synopsis: In 'Borgia: A Period Play' by Michael Field, the reader is transported to the captivating world of Renaissance Italy, where power, ambition, and scandal intertwine. Set against the backdrop of the infamous Borgia family, the play delves into the complex dynamics of their rise to prominence and the dark secrets that threaten to tear them apart. As the plot unfolds, the reader is drawn into a web of political intrigue, forbidden love, and moral dilemmas, where loyalty and betrayal walk hand in hand. With vivid characters and a gripping narrative, 'Borgia: A Period Play' offers a thrilling exploration of the human condition and the lengths people will go to in pursuit of power and survival.

Genres: Historical fiction, Drama, Political thriller

Themes: Power, Ambition, Family, Betrayal, Morality
