Synopsis: In 'Civilization the Primal Need of the Race, and The Attitude of the American Mind Toward the Negro Intellect: The American Negro Academy. Occasional Paper No. 3', Alexander Crummell delves into the crucial importance of civilization for the progress of humanity. He explores the complex relationship between the American mind and the intellectual capabilities of African Americans, shedding light on the prevailing attitudes of the time. Through thought-provoking analysis, Crummell challenges societal norms and offers insights into the potential of the African American intellect.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, African American studies

Themes: Civilization, Intellectualism, Racial attitudes, Progress, Equality
