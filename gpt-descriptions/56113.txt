Synopsis: 

In 'Irrigation Works: The Principles on Which Their Design and Working Should Be Based, with Special Details Relating to Indian Canals and Some Proposed Improvements', Edward Skelton Bellasis takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of irrigation systems. With a focus on Indian canals and proposed enhancements, Bellasis delves into the intricate principles and designs that underpin these vital structures. From the challenges faced in water management to the potential solutions that could revolutionize irrigation, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the subject. Whether you are an engineer, a history enthusiast, or simply curious about the mechanics of irrigation, Bellasis's work promises to captivate and enlighten.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, History

Themes: Irrigation systems, Water management, Engineering principles
