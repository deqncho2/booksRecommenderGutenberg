Synopsis: In 'The Man-Made World; Or, Our Androcentric Culture', Charlotte Perkins Gilman takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the intricacies of our society, challenging the deeply ingrained gender biases that shape our world. Through a series of insightful essays, Gilman explores the ways in which our androcentric culture perpetuates inequality and restricts the potential of both men and women. With her sharp wit and compelling arguments, she unveils the hidden consequences of a society built upon patriarchal norms, shedding light on the urgent need for change.

Genres: Non-fiction, Feminist literature, Social commentary

Themes: Gender inequality, Patriarchy, Feminism, Social norms, Cultural criticism
