Synopsis: 
In "My Three Years in a German Prison," Henri Béland takes us on a gripping journey through his harrowing experiences as a prisoner during World War II. Captured by the Germans while serving as a Canadian soldier, Béland finds himself thrust into a world of uncertainty, fear, and survival. From the brutal conditions of the prison camp to the unexpected friendships formed within its walls, Béland's story is one of resilience and the indomitable human spirit. As he navigates the complexities of life behind bars, Béland's tale offers a unique perspective on the horrors of war and the strength of the human will.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War

Themes: Survival, Resilience, Friendship, Human Spirit
