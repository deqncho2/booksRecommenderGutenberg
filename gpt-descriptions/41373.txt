Synopsis: In "Secret History of the English Occupation of Egypt: Being a Personal Narrative of Events," Wilfrid Scawen Blunt and Ahmad Urabi unveil a hidden side of history, recounting their firsthand experiences during the English occupation of Egypt. Through their personal narratives, the reader is transported to a tumultuous era filled with political intrigue, cultural clashes, and the struggle for independence. Blunt and Urabi's accounts shed light on the untold stories and secret machinations that shaped this pivotal period, offering a unique perspective on the events that unfolded. As the reader delves into this captivating narrative, they will uncover the hidden truths and untold tales that have long been concealed.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, memoir, political commentary

Themes: Colonialism, resistance, cultural identity, power dynamics
