Synopsis: 

In 'Sketches of the East Africa Campaign', Robert Valentine Dolbey takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed landscapes and turbulent events of the East Africa Campaign during World War I. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Dolbey provides a unique perspective on the challenges faced by both soldiers and civilians in this often overlooked theater of war. From the scorching heat of the African savannah to the treacherous jungles and encounters with local tribes, Dolbey's sketches paint a vivid picture of the harsh realities and unexpected beauty of war-torn East Africa.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War memoir, Travelogue

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and communities, the resilience of the human spirit, cultural encounters and exploration
