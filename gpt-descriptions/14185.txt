Synopsis: 

'Edward MacDowell: A Great American Tone Poet, His Life and Music' by Porte, John Fielder is a captivating biography that delves into the life and music of one of America's most celebrated composers. From his humble beginnings to his rise as a prominent figure in the world of classical music, this book offers a fascinating exploration of Edward MacDowell's journey, his creative process, and the impact of his compositions on the American musical landscape. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, the author paints a vivid portrait of MacDowell's life, revealing the triumphs, struggles, and the enduring legacy of this remarkable artist.

Genres: Biography, Music, History

Themes: Artistic inspiration, American identity, Musical legacy
