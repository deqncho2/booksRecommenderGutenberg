Synopsis: In "A Pilgrim Maid: A Story of Plymouth Colony in 1620" by Marion Ames Taggart, readers are transported back to the early days of the Plymouth Colony. The story revolves around a young girl named Priscilla, who embarks on a perilous journey across the Atlantic with her family in search of religious freedom. As they face numerous challenges and hardships, Priscilla's resilience and determination are put to the test. Along the way, she encounters fascinating characters, experiences the harsh realities of colonial life, and discovers the true meaning of community and sacrifice. This captivating historical novel offers a vivid portrayal of the struggles and triumphs of the Pilgrims, providing a glimpse into the early days of America's founding.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Religious Freedom, Resilience, Community, Sacrifice
