Synopsis: In 'Brother Francis; Or, Less than the Least' by Eileen Douglas, readers are introduced to the enigmatic Brother Francis, a man who has chosen a life of solitude and self-imposed exile. As the story unfolds, we delve into the depths of Brother Francis' past, uncovering the secrets and tragedies that have shaped him into the complex character he is today. Through a series of encounters and reflections, the reader is taken on a journey of self-discovery, exploring themes of redemption, forgiveness, and the power of human connection. 

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Psychological

Themes: Solitude, Redemption, Forgiveness, Human Connection
