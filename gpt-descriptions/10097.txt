Synopsis: In "The Healing of Nations and the Hidden Sources of Their Strife," Edward Carpenter delves into the complex web of conflicts that plague nations, uncovering the underlying causes that often go unnoticed. Through a thought-provoking exploration of history, politics, and human nature, Carpenter offers a fresh perspective on the healing of nations and the potential for lasting peace. With captivating anecdotes and insightful analysis, this book challenges readers to question their assumptions and consider the hidden sources of strife that hinder progress and unity.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, History

Themes: Conflict resolution, Peacebuilding, Sociopolitical analysis
