Synopsis: 

In 'Consumers and Wage-Earners: The Ethics of Buying Cheap', J. Elliot Ross delves into the complex moral dilemma that arises when we seek out the best deals as consumers. Through thought-provoking analysis and real-life examples, Ross explores the hidden costs behind our desire for cheap products, forcing readers to question the ethical implications of their purchasing choices. As he uncovers the interconnectedness of global economies and the impact on workers' wages, Ross challenges us to consider the true price we pay for our bargains. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Economics

Themes: Consumerism, Ethics, Globalization, Labor Rights
