Synopsis: In 'Discourse on Criticism and of Poetry: From Poems On Several Occasions', Samuel Cobb takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of poetry and criticism. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Cobb explores the intricacies of poetic expression, delving into the power of words and the art of interpretation. With a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of the human psyche, Cobb challenges conventional notions of criticism and invites readers to question their own perceptions of poetry. As the pages turn, readers will find themselves immersed in a world where words hold immense power and where the boundaries between poet and critic blur. 

Genres: Poetry, Literary Criticism

Themes: Power of language, Interpretation, Artistic expression
