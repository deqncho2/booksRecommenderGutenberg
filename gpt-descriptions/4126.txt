Synopsis: In the ninth volume of his famous diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a captivating journey through the early months of 1660-61. As the political landscape of England undergoes significant changes, Pepys finds himself caught in the midst of intrigue, scandal, and personal triumphs. From his encounters with influential figures to his own romantic escapades, Pepys provides a vivid and intimate account of the events that shape his life during this tumultuous period.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Fiction, Diary

Themes: Politics, Personal Relationships, Social Change
