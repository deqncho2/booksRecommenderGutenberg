Synopsis: In 'Hemp Hurds as Paper-Making Material', Dewey and Merrill delve into the fascinating world of hemp and its potential as a sustainable alternative for paper production. Through meticulous research and experimentation, they uncover the remarkable qualities of hemp hurds, the inner woody core of the hemp plant, and its ability to create high-quality paper. As they explore the history, cultivation, and processing of hemp, the authors shed light on the environmental benefits and economic potential of utilizing this versatile plant. This informative and thought-provoking work challenges conventional notions of paper-making materials and offers a compelling case for embracing hemp hurds as a viable and eco-friendly option.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Environmental Studies

Themes: Sustainability, Alternative Materials, Economic Potential
