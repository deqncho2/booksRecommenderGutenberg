Synopsis: In 'The Boyhood of Great Inventors' by Robertson, A. Fraser, embark on a captivating journey through the childhoods of some of the world's most brilliant inventors. From their humble beginnings to their groundbreaking discoveries, this book unveils the formative years of inventors such as Thomas Edison, Alexander Graham Bell, and the Wright brothers. Discover the trials, triumphs, and moments of inspiration that shaped these extraordinary minds, and be inspired by their determination and ingenuity. 

Genres: Biography, Non-fiction, History

Themes: Childhood dreams, Innovation, Perseverance
