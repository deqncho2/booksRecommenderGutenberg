Synopsis: In 'Joseph and His Friend: A Story of Pennsylvania' by Bayard Taylor, readers are transported to rural Pennsylvania in the mid-19th century, where they follow the captivating journey of Joseph Asten. Joseph, a young and wealthy landowner, finds himself torn between societal expectations and his own desires when he befriends a charismatic and unconventional stranger named Philip Held. As their friendship deepens, Joseph is forced to confront his own prejudices and confront the complexities of love, loyalty, and personal identity. Set against the backdrop of a changing America, this thought-provoking tale explores the power of friendship and the transformative nature of human connection.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, LGBTQ+

Themes: Friendship, Identity, Social Expectations, Love, Prejudice
