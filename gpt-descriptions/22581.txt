Synopsis: 

In 'The Child-Voice in Singing', Francis E. Howard delves into the fascinating world of children's singing voices, exploring both the physiological and practical aspects of nurturing and developing this unique talent. With a focus on schools and boy choirs, Howard provides valuable insights and techniques for teachers and choir directors to help young voices reach their full potential. From understanding the physical changes that occur in the child's voice to offering practical exercises and vocal training methods, this book is a comprehensive guide for anyone involved in nurturing young singers. 

Genres: Educational, Music, Non-fiction

Themes: Child development, Vocal training, Music education
