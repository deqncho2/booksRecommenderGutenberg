Synopsis: In 'The Story of the Great Fire in St. John, N.B., June 20th, 1877', George Stewart takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most devastating fires in Canadian history. Through vivid descriptions and personal accounts, the author paints a harrowing picture of the catastrophic event that engulfed the city of St. John, New Brunswick. As the flames spread rapidly, threatening lives and livelihoods, the resilience and courage of the community are put to the ultimate test. Will they be able to overcome this unprecedented disaster and rebuild their lives from the ashes?

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Disaster literature

Themes: Resilience, Community, Survival
