Synopsis: 

'Folk-lore of Shakespeare' by T. F. Thiselton-Dyer is a captivating exploration of the rich tapestry of folklore and superstitions that influenced the works of William Shakespeare. Delving into the fascinating world of ancient beliefs, customs, and traditions, this book uncovers the hidden meanings and cultural references embedded within Shakespeare's plays and poems. From the mystical significance of fairies and witches to the symbolic power of plants and animals, Thiselton-Dyer unravels the intricate web of folklore that shaped Shakespeare's imagination. With vivid anecdotes and meticulous research, this work offers a fresh perspective on the Bard's timeless tales, revealing the profound connections between folklore and literature.

Genres: Folklore, Literary Criticism, Cultural Studies

Themes: Folk beliefs, Superstition, Symbolism, Cultural influences
