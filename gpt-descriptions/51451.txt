Synopsis: 

In 'Capture and Escape: A Narrative of Army and Prison Life', John Azor Kellogg takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of war and captivity. As a soldier in the American Civil War, Kellogg finds himself captured by Confederate forces and thrown into a prison camp. With determination and resourcefulness, he navigates the treacherous landscape of confinement, constantly seeking opportunities to escape and return to his comrades. Through his vivid and poignant storytelling, Kellogg paints a vivid picture of the brutal realities of war and the indomitable spirit of those who refuse to surrender.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: Resilience, Survival, Comradeship, Human Spirit
