Synopsis: In 'The Boys' Life of Mark Twain', Albert Bigelow Paine takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating life of one of America's most beloved authors. From Twain's mischievous childhood adventures along the Mississippi River to his groundbreaking literary achievements, this biography offers a vivid and engaging portrait of the man behind the iconic characters of Tom Sawyer and Huckleberry Finn. Paine's storytelling prowess brings to life Twain's wit, humor, and profound observations about society, leaving readers eager to discover the remarkable legacy of this literary genius.

Genres: Biography, Autobiography, Historical

Themes: Childhood adventures, Literary genius, Social commentary
