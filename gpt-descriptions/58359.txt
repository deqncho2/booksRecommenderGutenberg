Synopsis: In 'The Mythology of All Races, Vol. 11: Latin-American', Alexander, Hartley Burr takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and enchanting folklore of Latin America. From the ancient civilizations of the Aztecs and Mayans to the mystical tales of the Amazon rainforest, this volume unveils a tapestry of mythical creatures, gods, and heroes. Discover the legends of Quetzalcoatl, the feathered serpent, and the hauntingly beautiful La Llorona, as well as the epic battles between light and darkness that shape the region's mythology. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, this book offers a mesmerizing exploration of Latin-American mythology that will leave readers spellbound.

Genres: Mythology, Folklore, Cultural Studies

Themes: Creation and Destruction, Heroism and Sacrifice, Supernatural Beings, Cultural Identity
