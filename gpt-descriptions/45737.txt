Synopsis: 
'A Popular Handbook to the National Gallery, Volume I, Foreign Schools: Including by Special Permission Notes Collected from the Works of John Ruskin' takes readers on a captivating journey through the renowned National Gallery, offering a unique perspective on various foreign schools of art. This volume, enriched with special notes collected from the works of John Ruskin, provides a fascinating exploration of the masterpieces housed within the gallery's walls. From the delicate brushstrokes of the Renaissance to the vibrant colors of the Baroque period, this handbook offers a comprehensive and engaging overview of the diverse artistic traditions represented in the collection.

Genres: Art history, Guidebook, Non-fiction

Themes: Art appreciation, Aesthetics, Cultural heritage
