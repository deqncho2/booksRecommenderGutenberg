Synopsis: 

In 'Project Gutenberg (1971-2009)' by Marie Lebert, embark on a captivating journey through the evolution of the digital age. This enthralling work delves into the fascinating history of Project Gutenberg, a groundbreaking initiative that aimed to digitize and make accessible an extensive collection of books. From its humble beginnings in 1971 to its remarkable growth over the next four decades, this book unveils the tireless efforts of countless individuals who dedicated themselves to the preservation and dissemination of knowledge. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Technology

Themes: Digital revolution, Information accessibility, Preservation of knowledge
