Synopsis: In 'The Defence of Guenevere and Other Poems', William Morris weaves a collection of captivating and thought-provoking poems that delve into the realms of love, chivalry, and betrayal. Through the eyes of Guenevere, the legendary queen of King Arthur's court, Morris explores the complexities of human relationships, the consequences of desire, and the power of personal agency. Each poem presents a unique perspective on the Arthurian legends, inviting readers to question traditional narratives and discover the untold stories hidden within. 

Genres: Poetry, Arthurian literature, Medieval literature

Themes: Love, betrayal, chivalry, personal agency, reinterpretation of myths and legends
