Synopsis: In 'The Spoilt Child: A Tale of Hindu Domestic Life' by Mitra, Peary Chand, we are introduced to the intriguing world of Hindu domestic life through the eyes of a spoilt child. Set in a traditional Indian household, the story follows the journey of a young boy who is accustomed to getting everything he desires. However, as he grows older, he begins to question the values and expectations placed upon him, leading to a series of unexpected events that challenge his privileged upbringing. This captivating tale explores the complexities of family dynamics, societal pressures, and the search for personal identity in a rapidly changing world.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Cultural

Themes: Family, Tradition, Identity, Social expectations
