Synopsis: In "Tolstoy on Shakespeare: A Critical Essay on Shakespeare," Leo Tolstoy, one of the greatest novelists of all time, delves into the enigmatic world of William Shakespeare's plays. With his characteristic insight and eloquence, Tolstoy explores the profound impact of Shakespeare's works on both literature and society. Through a meticulous analysis of the Bard's most famous plays, Tolstoy offers a thought-provoking perspective on the nature of art, the role of the artist, and the moral responsibility of storytelling. This captivating essay is a testament to Tolstoy's intellectual prowess and his unwavering quest for truth.

Genres: Literary criticism, essay, non-fiction

Themes: Art and its purpose, morality in literature, the role of the artist, the impact of storytelling
