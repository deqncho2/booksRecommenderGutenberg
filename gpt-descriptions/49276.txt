Synopsis: In the final volume of 'A History of Matrimonial Institutions', George Elliott Howard delves into the intricate tapestry of human relationships, exploring the evolution of marriage across different cultures and time periods. From ancient civilizations to modern societies, this captivating work uncovers the fascinating customs, rituals, and laws that have shaped the institution of marriage. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Howard sheds light on the complexities of love, commitment, and societal expectations, offering readers a thought-provoking journey through the ages.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Marriage, Culture, Tradition, Gender Roles, Social Norms
