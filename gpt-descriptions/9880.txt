Synopsis: In 'The Woman's Bible', Elizabeth Cady Stanton challenges traditional interpretations of religious texts, delving into the stories and teachings that have shaped women's roles throughout history. With a critical and thought-provoking approach, she examines the Bible through a feminist lens, questioning the patriarchal structures and oppressive narratives that have marginalized women for centuries. Through her insightful analysis, Stanton aims to empower women and inspire them to reclaim their rightful place in society.

Genres: Feminist literature, Religious studies, Social commentary

Themes: Gender equality, Feminism, Religious interpretation, Patriarchy, Women's empowerment
