Synopsis: In "Common Sense," Thomas Paine presents a compelling argument that challenges the status quo and ignites a revolutionary spirit. Through his persuasive and straightforward writing style, Paine addresses the fundamental principles of government and questions the authority of the British monarchy. With a focus on the American colonies, he explores the idea of independence and the necessity of breaking free from oppressive rule. Paine's thought-provoking ideas and powerful rhetoric make "Common Sense" a captivating read that will leave readers questioning their own beliefs and the role of government in society.

Genres: Political philosophy, Historical literature

Themes: Independence, Revolution, Government, Freedom, Equality
