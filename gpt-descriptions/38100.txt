Synopsis: 

In 'Ancient Faiths And Modern', Thomas Inman takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient world, exploring the worships, legends, and divinities of Central and Western Asia, Europe, and beyond. Delving into the rich tapestry of religious customs that existed before the Christian era, Inman reveals the fascinating connections between these ancient faiths and the religious practices that still endure today. From the enigmatic rituals of ancient civilizations to the enduring myths and legends that have shaped our understanding of the divine, this dissertation offers a thought-provoking exploration of the roots of religious belief.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Religious Studies

Themes: Ancient religions, Comparative mythology, Religious customs and practices
