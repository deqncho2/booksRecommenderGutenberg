Synopsis: 
Routledge's Manual of Etiquette is a captivating guide that takes readers on a journey through the intricate world of social decorum. Written by the esteemed George Routledge, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the rules and customs that govern society, providing invaluable advice on how to navigate various social situations with grace and elegance. From formal dinners to afternoon tea parties, from courtship rituals to mourning etiquette, this manual delves into every aspect of proper behavior, revealing the secrets to becoming a true gentleman or lady. Filled with delightful anecdotes and practical tips, Routledge's Manual of Etiquette is an essential companion for anyone seeking to master the art of social interaction.

Genres: Non-fiction, Self-help, Reference

Themes: Etiquette, Social norms, Manners, Social interaction
