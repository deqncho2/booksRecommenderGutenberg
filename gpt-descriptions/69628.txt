Synopsis: In 'The Princess Casamassima', James, Henry weaves a captivating tale of political intrigue and forbidden love set in the bustling streets of Victorian London. The story follows Hyacinth Robinson, a young bookbinder with a mysterious past, who becomes entangled in a secret society plotting a revolution. As Hyacinth navigates the complexities of his double life, he finds himself torn between his loyalty to the cause and his growing affection for a beautiful and enigmatic princess. With its richly drawn characters and atmospheric setting, this novel explores the clash between social classes, the struggle for identity, and the power of love in a world on the brink of change.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Thriller, Romance

Themes: Social class, Revolution, Identity, Love
