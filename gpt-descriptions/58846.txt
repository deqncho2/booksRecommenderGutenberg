Synopsis: In 'Kate Aylesford: A Story of the Refugees', Charles J. Peterson weaves a captivating tale of a young woman's journey through the tumultuous times of the American Civil War. Kate Aylesford, a spirited and determined protagonist, finds herself caught in the midst of the war's chaos, forced to confront the harsh realities of loss, love, and survival. As she navigates through the challenges of displacement and uncertainty, Kate's resilience and courage are put to the test, leading her on a path of self-discovery and unexpected alliances. Set against the backdrop of a nation torn apart, this gripping historical novel explores the profound impact of war on individuals and the enduring strength of the human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Resilience, Love and Loss, Identity, Survival
