Synopsis: In 'The Lives of the Twelve Caesars, Volume 12: Domitian' by Suetonius, the enigmatic and controversial reign of Emperor Domitian is brought to life. From his rise to power to his tyrannical rule, this gripping account delves into the depths of Domitian's character, revealing a complex and often misunderstood figure. With tales of political intrigue, military conquests, and personal scandals, Suetonius paints a vivid portrait of a ruler whose actions continue to captivate and perplex historians to this day.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and tyranny, political intrigue, leadership, personal ambition
