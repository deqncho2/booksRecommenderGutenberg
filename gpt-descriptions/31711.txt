Synopsis: In the final volume of this captivating biography, Morley delves into the later years of the remarkable statesman, William Ewart Gladstone. As the political landscape of Britain undergoes significant transformations, Gladstone finds himself at the center of it all, grappling with the challenges of a changing world. From his tireless efforts to secure Irish Home Rule to his passionate advocacy for social reform, Gladstone's unwavering commitment to his principles shines through. Morley's meticulous research and vivid storytelling bring to life the trials and triumphs of this influential figure, painting a portrait of a man who dedicated his life to public service and left an indelible mark on British history.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Leadership, Political Ideals, Social Reform, Legacy
