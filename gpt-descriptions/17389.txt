Synopsis: In "The Dreamer: A Romantic Rendering of the Life-Story of Edgar Allan Poe," Mary Newton Stanard takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of the enigmatic writer, Edgar Allan Poe. Through a blend of fact and fiction, the author delves into the depths of Poe's mind, exploring his hauntingly beautiful imagination and the demons that plagued him. From his troubled childhood to his tumultuous relationships and his relentless pursuit of literary greatness, this novel offers a mesmerizing portrayal of one of the most influential figures in American literature. As the story unfolds, readers are drawn into a world of mystery, darkness, and passion, where the line between reality and fantasy becomes blurred. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biographical Fiction, Gothic Fiction

Themes: Imagination, Madness, Love and Loss, Artistic Pursuit, Identity
