Synopsis: In 'Critical Strictures on the New Tragedy of Elvira, Written by Mr. David Malloch', Boswell, Dempster, and Erskine offer a scathing critique of a recently published tragedy. Through their witty and insightful analysis, they dissect the play's flaws, highlighting its lack of originality, weak character development, and questionable moral messages. As they delve into the intricacies of the plot and the playwright's choices, the authors provide a thought-provoking examination of the state of contemporary tragedy. 

Genres: Literary criticism, satire, theater

Themes: Artistic originality, character development, moral messages
