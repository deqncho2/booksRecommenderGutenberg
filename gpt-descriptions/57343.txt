Synopsis: 

'The Negro in Chicago: A Study of Race Relations and a Race Riot' by the Chicago Commission on Race Relations is a gripping and eye-opening examination of the racial tensions and violence that plagued Chicago in the early 20th century. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, this book delves into the causes and consequences of a significant race riot, shedding light on the complex dynamics between the African American community and the white population. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Racial inequality, Social justice, Community dynamics, Civil rights
