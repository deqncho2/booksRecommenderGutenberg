Synopsis: 

In "The Land of the Miamis: An Account of the Struggle to Secure Possession of the North-West from the End of the Revolution until 1812," author Barce, Elmore takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period following the American Revolution. Set in the untamed wilderness of the North-West, this historical account delves into the relentless struggle for control over this vast and resource-rich region. As various factions vie for power, alliances are formed, betrayals occur, and the fate of the land hangs in the balance. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Barce, Elmore brings to life the gripping tale of the battle for the North-West, shedding light on the untold stories and forgotten heroes of this pivotal era.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Adventure, Biography

Themes: Power struggles, Frontier exploration, Native American relations, American expansion
