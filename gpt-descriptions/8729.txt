Synopsis: In 'The Dancing Mouse: A Study in Animal Behavior', renowned psychologist Robert Mearns Yerkes takes readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of animal behavior. Through his meticulous observations and experiments, Yerkes unveils the surprising intelligence and complex social dynamics of a seemingly ordinary creature - the mouse. As he delves into the intricacies of their behavior, Yerkes uncovers a hidden world of communication, problem-solving, and even dance-like movements. This engaging study offers a fresh perspective on the animal kingdom, challenging our preconceived notions and inviting us to marvel at the wonders of nature.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Psychology

Themes: Animal behavior, Intelligence, Social dynamics
