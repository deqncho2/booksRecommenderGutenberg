Synopsis: 

In 'The Scottish Parliament Before the Union of the Crowns', Robert S. Rait takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous political landscape of Scotland during a pivotal period in its history. Delving into the inner workings of the Scottish Parliament, Rait unveils a world of power struggles, intrigue, and the clash of ideologies that shaped the destiny of a nation. From the rise and fall of influential figures to the fierce debates that echoed within the hallowed halls, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the Scottish Parliament's role in shaping Scotland's identity and its relationship with England. 

Genres: History, Politics, Biography

Themes: Power struggles, National identity, Political ideologies
