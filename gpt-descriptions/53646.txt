Synopsis: 
'Anthropology and the Classics: Six Lectures Delivered Before the University of Oxford' is a captivating collection of lectures by renowned scholars Lang, Murray, Fowler, Jevons, Evans, and Myres. Each lecture explores the fascinating intersection between anthropology and classical studies, shedding light on the ancient world through the lens of human culture and society. From examining the role of religion in ancient Greece to unraveling the mysteries of Roman law, these lectures offer a thought-provoking journey into the past, revealing surprising connections and insights that challenge our understanding of history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Cultural anthropology, Comparative studies
