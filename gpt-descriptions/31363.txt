Synopsis: 

In 'British Supremacy & Canadian Self-Government, 1839-1854', author J. L. Morison delves into a captivating period of Canadian history, exploring the intricate relationship between British rule and the growing desire for self-governance in Canada during the mid-19th century. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Morison uncovers the political struggles, cultural clashes, and pivotal events that shaped the path towards Canadian autonomy. From the rebellions of 1837-1838 to the landmark Durham Report and the subsequent implementation of responsible government, this book offers a fascinating account of the tensions and negotiations that ultimately led to the birth of Canadian self-government.

Genres: History, Political Science, Canadian Studies

Themes: British colonialism, Canadian nationalism, self-governance, political reform, cultural identity
