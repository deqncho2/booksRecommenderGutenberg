Synopsis: In "The Heroic Women of Early Indiana Methodism," author T. A. Goodwin takes readers on a captivating journey through the untold stories of remarkable women who played a pivotal role in the development of Methodism in Indiana. From their tireless efforts to establish churches in the wilderness to their unwavering commitment to social justice, these women defied societal norms and left an indelible mark on the history of Indiana Methodism. Through vivid anecdotes and historical accounts, Goodwin sheds light on the extraordinary lives of these unsung heroines, revealing their courage, resilience, and unwavering faith in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Women's History, Religion, Social Justice
