Synopsis: In 'Black and White: Land, Labor, and Politics in the South', Timothy Thomas Fortune takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex and often tumultuous history of the American South. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Fortune delves into the intertwined issues of race, land ownership, labor struggles, and political power dynamics that have shaped the region for centuries. From the aftermath of the Civil War to the Civil Rights Movement, this book offers a thought-provoking exploration of the South's social and economic landscape, shedding light on the struggles and triumphs of both black and white communities. 

Genres: History, Sociology, Political Science

Themes: Race relations, Land ownership, Labor movements, Political power dynamics
