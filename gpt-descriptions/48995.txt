Synopsis: In Luther, vol. 1 of 6, Grisar and Hartmann delve into the captivating life of Martin Luther, the influential figure who sparked the Protestant Reformation. This volume explores Luther's early years, his spiritual journey, and his eventual challenge to the Catholic Church. As Luther grapples with his own doubts and the corruption he witnesses, readers are taken on a thought-provoking journey through the complexities of faith, power, and the pursuit of truth.

Genres: Biography, History, Religion

Themes: Faith and doubt, Corruption and reform, Power and authority
