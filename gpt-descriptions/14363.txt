Synopsis: 
In 'The Worst Journey in the World: Antarctic, 1910-1913', Apsley Cherry-Garrard recounts his harrowing expedition to Antarctica with Captain Robert Falcon Scott. Battling extreme cold, treacherous terrain, and the constant threat of death, Cherry-Garrard's gripping narrative takes readers on a journey of endurance, sacrifice, and the indomitable human spirit. From the heart-stopping encounters with killer whales to the haunting beauty of the Antarctic landscape, this memoir offers a firsthand account of one of the most treacherous expeditions in history.

Genres: 
Memoir, Adventure, Non-fiction

Themes: 
Endurance, Exploration, Survival, Human resilience
