Synopsis: In "The Married Woman's Private Medical Companion," Mauriceau explores the delicate and often misunderstood aspects of women's health. From addressing the treatment of menstruation and its irregularities to providing guidance on determining pregnancy, the book delves into the various diseases associated with pregnancy and offers methods to prevent it. Additionally, it tackles the sensitive topics of miscarriage and abortion, discussing when they may be necessary and how to approach them safely. The work also delves into the causes and potential cures for barrenness or sterility, shedding light on a subject that many women face silently. 

Genres: Medical literature, Women's health, Reproductive health

Themes: Women's reproductive health, Pregnancy, Fertility, Medical treatments
