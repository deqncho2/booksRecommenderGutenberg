Synopsis: In 'Advice to Singers' by Frederick James Crowest, a renowned music critic and historian, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the world of singing. Through a series of insightful and practical tips, Crowest offers invaluable advice to aspiring singers, delving into the nuances of technique, interpretation, and stage presence. With anecdotes and examples from the lives of famous singers, this book serves as a comprehensive guide for anyone seeking to improve their vocal abilities and make a mark in the world of music.

Genres: Non-fiction, Music, Self-help

Themes: Singing technique, Performance skills, Musical interpretation, Artistic expression
