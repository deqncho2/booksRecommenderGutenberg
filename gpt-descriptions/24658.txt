Synopsis: In the second volume of "The Loyalists of America and Their Times," Egerton Ryerson delves into the captivating history of the loyalists from 1620 to 1816. This enthralling account takes readers on a journey through the tumultuous times of the American Revolution and its aftermath, exploring the lives of those who remained loyal to the British Crown. From the early settlements to the struggles faced by loyalists during the war, Ryerson paints a vivid picture of their experiences, sacrifices, and unwavering loyalty. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this volume offers a comprehensive look at a pivotal period in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, American Revolution

Themes: Loyalty, Identity, Conflict, Patriotism, Resilience
