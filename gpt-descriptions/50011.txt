Synopsis: 
In 'Myths and Folk-tales of the Russians, Western Slavs, and Magyars', Curtin delves into the rich tapestry of Russian, Western Slavic, and Hungarian folklore, transporting readers to a world of enchantment and wonder. From the mystical creatures that inhabit the forests to the heroic deeds of legendary figures, this collection of myths and folk-tales offers a captivating glimpse into the cultural heritage of these diverse Eastern European societies. With each tale, Curtin weaves a spellbinding narrative that explores themes of bravery, love, and the enduring power of folklore. 

Genres: 
Folklore, Mythology, Fantasy

Themes: 
Bravery, Love, Cultural Heritage
