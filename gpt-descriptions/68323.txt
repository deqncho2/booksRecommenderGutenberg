Synopsis: In "Celtic MSS. in relation to the Macpherson fraud: With a review of Professor Freeman's criticism of [P.B. Du Chaillu's] "The Viking Age," by the author of "Celticism a myth," author James Cruikshank Roger delves into the controversial topic of the Macpherson fraud and its connection to Celtic manuscripts. Through a critical examination of Professor Freeman's critique of P.B. Du Chaillu's "The Viking Age," Roger uncovers intriguing insights that challenge established beliefs. This thought-provoking work explores the authenticity of Celtic MSS and sheds light on the complex relationship between history, mythology, and cultural identity.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Literary Criticism

Themes: Authenticity, Mythology, Cultural Identity
