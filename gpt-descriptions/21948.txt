Synopsis: 
In 'Little Busybodies: The Life of Crickets, Ants, Bees, Beetles, and Other Busybodies', Marks and Moody take readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of insects. From the intricate social structures of ants to the mesmerizing dances of bees, this book unveils the hidden lives of these tiny creatures. Through vivid descriptions and engaging anecdotes, the authors shed light on the remarkable behaviors, survival strategies, and ecological importance of various insects. Discover the secret lives of these little busybodies and gain a newfound appreciation for the intricate web of life that exists all around us.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Science

Themes: Insect behavior, Ecology, Biodiversity
