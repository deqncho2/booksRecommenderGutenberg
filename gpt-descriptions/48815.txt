Synopsis: 

In "Mistress Spitfire: A Plain Account of Certain Episodes in the History of Richard Coope, Gent., and of His Cousin, Mistress Alison French, at the Time of the Revolution, 1642-1644," readers are transported to a tumultuous period in history. Set during the English Revolution, this captivating novel follows the intertwined lives of Richard Coope and his cousin, Mistress Alison French. As the country is torn apart by political and religious conflicts, Richard and Alison find themselves caught in a web of intrigue, danger, and forbidden love. 

Amidst the chaos and uncertainty, Richard and Alison must navigate treacherous alliances, secret missions, and personal sacrifices. Their journey takes them from the opulent courts of King Charles I to the battlefields of war, where they encounter both friends and foes. As their lives become increasingly entangled, they must confront their own desires, loyalties, and the consequences of their actions. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Adventure

Themes: Love and Betrayal, Loyalty and Honor, Political and Religious Conflict
