Synopsis: In the gripping second volume of 'The War with Mexico', author Justin Harvey Smith delves into the intense conflict between the United States and Mexico, exploring the pivotal battles, political maneuverings, and personal stories that shaped this historic war. From the daring exploits of American generals to the resilience of Mexican soldiers, Smith paints a vivid picture of the brutal realities faced by both sides. As tensions rise and the war reaches its climax, readers will be captivated by the strategic brilliance, heartbreaking sacrifices, and unexpected twists that unfold on the battlefield and in the corridors of power.

Genres: History, Military, Biography

Themes: War, Politics, National Identity
