Synopsis: 

'Indian Unrest' by Valentine Chirol, Sir is a gripping account of the political and social turmoil that engulfed India during the early 20th century. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand experiences, the author delves into the complex dynamics between the British colonial rulers and the Indian nationalists, painting a vivid picture of a nation on the brink of revolution. As tensions rise and the struggle for independence intensifies, the reader is taken on a journey through the heart of India, witnessing the clash of cultures, the rise of influential leaders, and the sacrifices made in the pursuit of freedom.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political commentary, Travelogue

Themes: Colonialism, Nationalism, Identity, Resistance, Power dynamics
