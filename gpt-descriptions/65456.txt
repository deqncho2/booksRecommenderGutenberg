Synopsis: 
'Life of James Mars, a Slave Born and Sold in Connecticut' is a captivating memoir that delves into the extraordinary life of James Mars, a slave who was born and sold in Connecticut. From his early years on a plantation to his eventual escape and fight for freedom, Mars's story is one of resilience, courage, and the enduring human spirit. Through his vivid recollections, readers are transported to a time and place where the institution of slavery was deeply entrenched, and witness the indomitable spirit of a man determined to forge his own path.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Resilience
