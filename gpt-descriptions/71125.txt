Synopsis: In "How the shortage of skilled mechanics is being overcome by training the unskilled," the United States Council of National Defense explores a groundbreaking solution to address the scarcity of skilled mechanics during a time of war. This informative work delves into the innovative strategies employed to train unskilled individuals and equip them with the necessary skills to fill the crucial role of mechanics. By highlighting the challenges faced and the transformative impact of this training program, the book offers a compelling narrative that sheds light on a vital aspect of the war effort.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Educational

Themes: Labor shortage, Industrial training, Skill development, War effort
