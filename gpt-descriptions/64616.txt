Synopsis: In 'Riches and Poverty (1910)', L. G. Chiozza Money takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex world of wealth and destitution. Through a series of thought-provoking anecdotes and insightful analysis, Money explores the stark contrasts between the lives of the rich and the poor, shedding light on the societal implications of economic inequality. From the opulent lifestyles of the elite to the struggles faced by the working class, this book offers a compelling exploration of the human condition in the early 20th century.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Economics

Themes: Economic inequality, Social class, Human nature
