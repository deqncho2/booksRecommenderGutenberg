Synopsis: In 'Sebastopol', Tolstoy takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing realities of war, as seen through the eyes of soldiers on the front lines. Through a series of interconnected stories, we witness the chaos, bravery, and despair that permeate the besieged city of Sebastopol during the Crimean War. From the adrenaline-fueled battles to the intimate moments of camaraderie and reflection, Tolstoy's vivid and visceral prose immerses us in the visceral experience of war, leaving us pondering the true nature of heroism and the cost of conflict.

Genres: Historical fiction, War literature

Themes: War, Heroism, Human resilience, Morality
