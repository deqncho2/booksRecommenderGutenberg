Synopsis: 

In 'Slave Narratives: A Folk History of Slavery in the United States from Interviews with Former Slaves, Volume XI, North Carolina Narratives, Part 2', the United States Work Projects Administration presents a captivating collection of firsthand accounts from former slaves in North Carolina. These narratives offer a unique and powerful insight into the lives, struggles, and triumphs of individuals who endured the horrors of slavery and emerged as resilient survivors. From tales of daring escapes to poignant reflections on family and community, this volume paints a vivid picture of the human spirit's indomitable strength in the face of oppression. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Oral History

Themes: Slavery, Resilience, Freedom, Family, Community
