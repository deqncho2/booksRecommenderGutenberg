Synopsis: In the second volume of "Marse Henry: An Autobiography," Henry Watterson, a prominent American journalist and editor, continues to regale readers with his captivating tales and insightful reflections. From his experiences as a war correspondent during the Civil War to his encounters with influential figures such as Abraham Lincoln and Mark Twain, Watterson's memoir takes us on a journey through the tumultuous times that shaped America. With his signature wit and charm, he offers a unique perspective on the political and social landscape of the late 19th and early 20th centuries, leaving readers eager to uncover the next chapter of his remarkable life.

Genres: Autobiography, Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Journalism, American History, Personal Growth
