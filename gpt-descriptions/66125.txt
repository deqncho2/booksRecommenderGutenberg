Synopsis: In "Impromptu"; or, How to Think on Your Feet, Kleiser Grenville takes readers on a captivating journey into the art of spontaneous speaking. Through a series of engaging anecdotes and practical advice, the author explores the power of quick thinking and effective communication in various situations. From impromptu speeches to unexpected conversations, this book offers valuable insights and techniques to help readers master the art of thinking on their feet. With wit and wisdom, Grenville encourages readers to embrace the unexpected and confidently express their thoughts in any given moment.

Genres: Self-help, Communication, Public Speaking

Themes: Spontaneity, Quick thinking, Effective communication
