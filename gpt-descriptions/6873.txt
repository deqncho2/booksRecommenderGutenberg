Synopsis: In "Mark Twain" by Henderson, Archibald, embark on a captivating journey through the life of one of America's most beloved literary figures. This enthralling biography delves into the fascinating world of Samuel Clemens, better known as Mark Twain, revealing the triumphs, struggles, and adventures that shaped his extraordinary career. From his humble beginnings as a Mississippi riverboat pilot to his iconic works such as "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer" and "Adventures of Huckleberry Finn," witness the evolution of a literary genius whose wit, humor, and social commentary continue to resonate with readers today. 

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: American literature, social commentary, humor
