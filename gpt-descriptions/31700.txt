Synopsis: 
In "Reflections of a Bachelor Girl," Helen Rowland takes readers on a witty and insightful journey through the trials and tribulations of being a single woman in the early 20th century. Through a collection of humorous and thought-provoking essays, she explores the complexities of love, relationships, societal expectations, and the pursuit of happiness. With her sharp observations and clever anecdotes, Rowland offers a refreshing perspective on the joys and challenges of navigating the world as an independent woman.

Genres: Humor, Essay, Non-fiction

Themes: Love and Relationships, Gender Roles, Society and Expectations
