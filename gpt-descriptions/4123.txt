Synopsis: In the seventh volume of his captivating diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a journey through the vibrant and tumultuous months of August and September 1660. As he navigates the bustling streets of London, Pepys encounters a myriad of intriguing characters, witnesses scandalous affairs, and finds himself entangled in political intrigue. From the grandeur of the royal court to the squalor of the city's underbelly, Pepys provides a vivid and intimate account of the Restoration era, offering a unique glimpse into the lives of both the powerful and the common folk.

Genres: Diary, Historical, Memoir

Themes: Restoration era, Politics, Society, Human nature
