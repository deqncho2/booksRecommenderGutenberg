Synopsis: In "The Russian Novelists," Eugène-Melchior de Vogüé takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of Russian literature. Through his insightful analysis and exploration of the works of renowned Russian authors such as Tolstoy, Dostoevsky, and Turgenev, Vogüé delves into the complexities of their characters, the richness of their storytelling, and the profound impact of their writings on Russian society. From the depths of human emotions to the intricate social dynamics, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the minds of these literary giants and the cultural context that shaped their masterpieces.

Genres: Literary criticism, cultural analysis, historical commentary

Themes: Russian literature, character analysis, societal impact, cultural context
