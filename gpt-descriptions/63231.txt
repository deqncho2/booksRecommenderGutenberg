Synopsis: In "Castes In India," B.R. Ambedkar delves into the intricate web of India's caste system, unraveling its historical origins, social implications, and the profound impact it has on the lives of millions. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Ambedkar sheds light on the oppressive nature of caste divisions, exposing the deep-rooted prejudices and discrimination that persist in Indian society. With a compelling narrative and thought-provoking anecdotes, this book offers a compelling exploration of a complex social structure that continues to shape the lives of individuals across the country.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, History

Themes: Caste system, Social inequality, Discrimination, Social justice, Indian society
