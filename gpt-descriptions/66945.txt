Synopsis: 

In 'Memoirs and Services of the Eighty-third Regiment, County of Dublin, from 1793 to 1907', Edward William Bray takes readers on an enthralling journey through the remarkable history of the regiment. From their early days in the West Indies to their campaigns in Africa, the Peninsula, Ceylon, Canada, and India, the regiment's adventures and triumphs unfold in vivid detail. Through personal accounts, historical records, and captivating anecdotes, Bray paints a captivating picture of the regiment's courage, resilience, and unwavering dedication to duty. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: Courage, Resilience, Duty, Military history, Colonialism
