Synopsis: 

In 'Oriental Encounters: Palestine and Syria, 1894-6', Marmaduke William Pickthall takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting landscapes of Palestine and Syria during the late 19th century. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Pickthall immerses readers in the rich cultural tapestry of the region, exploring its history, people, and traditions. From bustling marketplaces to ancient ruins, from encounters with Bedouin tribes to conversations with religious scholars, the author's encounters offer a unique perspective on the complexities and beauty of the Orient. 

Genres: Travelogue, Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Cultural exploration, Historical context, Personal transformation
