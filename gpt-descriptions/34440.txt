Synopsis: In "A Jewish Chaplain in France," Lee J. Levinger takes readers on a captivating journey through his experiences as a Jewish chaplain during World War I. From the trenches of the Western Front to the bustling streets of Paris, Levinger's memoir offers a unique perspective on the war and the challenges faced by Jewish soldiers. Through poignant anecdotes and heartfelt reflections, he explores the complexities of faith, identity, and the power of human connection in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Identity, War and its impact, Human connection
