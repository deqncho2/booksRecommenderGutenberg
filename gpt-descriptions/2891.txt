Synopsis:
Howards End is a captivating novel that delves into the lives of three families from different social classes in early 20th century England. The story revolves around the intertwined lives of the Schlegel sisters, Margaret and Helen, and the wealthy Wilcox family. As their paths cross and their destinies become entangled, the novel explores themes of love, social class, cultural clashes, and the search for connection and understanding in a rapidly changing society.

Genres:
Fiction, Drama, Social Commentary

Themes:
Love and Relationships, Social Class, Cultural Differences, Connection and Understanding
