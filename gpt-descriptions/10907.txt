Synopsis:
In 'The History of Rome, Books 09 to 26' by Livy, the epic tale of Rome's rise to power continues with gripping narratives of political intrigue, military conquests, and the clash of civilizations. As the Roman Republic faces external threats from Carthage, Greece, and Gaul, internal struggles for power and the erosion of traditional values threaten to tear the empire apart. Livy's vivid storytelling transports readers to the heart of ancient Rome, where heroes and villains alike shape the destiny of a civilization on the brink of greatness.

Genres:
Historical fiction, Epic, Political drama

Themes:
Power and ambition, Loyalty and betrayal, Civilization and barbarism
