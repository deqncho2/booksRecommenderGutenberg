Synopsis: In "Gandhi and Anarchy" by Sankaran Nair, C. (Chettur), Sir, embark on a captivating exploration of the complex relationship between Mahatma Gandhi and the concept of anarchy. This thought-provoking work delves into the intriguing encounters and ideological clashes between Gandhi and prominent anarchist thinkers of his time. As the narrative unfolds, readers are taken on a journey through the intellectual battleground where Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolence and anarchism collide, revealing surprising insights into the evolution of his revolutionary ideas. With meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Nair sheds light on the lesser-known aspects of Gandhi's life and his quest for a just and peaceful society.

Genres: Biography, History, Political Philosophy

Themes: Nonviolence, Anarchism, Social Justice
