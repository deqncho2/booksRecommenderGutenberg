Synopsis: In "Mosada: A dramatic poem," Yeats takes us on a captivating journey to the ancient fortress of Masada, where a group of Jewish rebels make their last stand against the Roman Empire. Through vivid imagery and powerful dialogue, the poem explores the themes of sacrifice, heroism, and the eternal struggle for freedom. As the characters grapple with their impending doom, their unwavering determination and unwavering faith in their cause will leave readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Drama, Poetry

Themes: Sacrifice, Heroism, Freedom
