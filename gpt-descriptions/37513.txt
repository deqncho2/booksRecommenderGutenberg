Synopsis: In 'Spencer's Philosophy of Science: The Herbert Spencer Lecture Delivered at the Museum 7 November, 1913', C. Lloyd Morgan explores the profound ideas of Herbert Spencer, a prominent philosopher of the 19th century. Through a captivating analysis of Spencer's philosophy of science, Morgan delves into the intricate relationship between science and philosophy, shedding light on the fundamental questions that have intrigued thinkers for centuries. This thought-provoking lecture offers a fascinating glimpse into the mind of one of the most influential philosophers of his time.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Philosophy of science, Epistemology, Scientific method
