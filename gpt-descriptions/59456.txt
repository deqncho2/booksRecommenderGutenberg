Synopsis: 
In 'Fors Clavigera (Volume 1 of 8): Letters to the workmen and labourers of Great Britain,' John Ruskin, a renowned Victorian art critic and social thinker, pens a series of passionate and thought-provoking letters addressed to the working class of his time. Through his eloquent and persuasive writing, Ruskin delves into a wide range of topics, including the importance of craftsmanship, the impact of industrialization, the value of education, and the need for social justice. With a blend of personal anecdotes, philosophical musings, and practical advice, Ruskin's letters offer a unique perspective on the challenges faced by the working class and provide a call to action for a more just and equitable society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social criticism, Epistolary literature

Themes: Social justice, Craftsmanship, Industrialization, Education, Inequality
