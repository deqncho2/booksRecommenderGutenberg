Synopsis: 

In "Things Mother Used to Make: A Collection of Old Time Recipes, Some Nearly One Hundred Years Old and Never Published Before," Lydia Maria Gurney takes readers on a delightful journey through time, uncovering a treasure trove of long-forgotten recipes. From hearty stews to delicate pastries, this collection offers a glimpse into the culinary traditions of generations past. As readers explore these recipes, they will not only discover the secrets to creating delicious dishes but also gain insight into the lives and customs of those who came before us. With each turn of the page, Gurney invites readers to step into the kitchens of yesteryear and experience the flavors and aromas that once filled the air. 

Genres: Cookbook, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Culinary traditions, Nostalgia, Cultural heritage
