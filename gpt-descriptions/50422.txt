Synopsis: 

'History of the Thirty-sixth Regiment Massachusetts Volunteers. 1862-1865' is a captivating historical account that delves into the experiences and triumphs of the soldiers who served in the renowned Thirty-sixth Regiment during the American Civil War. Through vivid storytelling and firsthand narratives, the book takes readers on a journey through the regiment's formation, battles, and the personal sacrifices made by its members. From the initial recruitment to the final days of the war, this gripping narrative sheds light on the bravery, camaraderie, and resilience of these soldiers as they fought for their beliefs and the preservation of the Union.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: Patriotism, Sacrifice, Brotherhood, Resilience, Civil War history
