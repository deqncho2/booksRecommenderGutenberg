Synopsis: In "The Industrial Condition of Women and Girls in Honolulu: A Social Study," Frances Blascoer delves into the hidden world of women and girls working in the bustling city of Honolulu. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, Blascoer uncovers the challenges, triumphs, and untold stories of these individuals who toil behind the scenes. From the bustling factories to the opulent homes of the elite, this social study sheds light on the often overlooked struggles and resilience of women and girls in the industrial landscape of Honolulu.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, History

Themes: Gender inequality, Labor rights, Social justice, Women's empowerment
