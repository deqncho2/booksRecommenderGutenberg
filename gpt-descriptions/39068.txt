Synopsis: 

In 'Historic Shrines of America: Being the Story of One Hundred and Twenty Historic Buildings and the Pioneers Who Made Them Notable', John T. Faris takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the rich history and significance of some of America's most iconic landmarks. From colonial-era houses to grand mansions, each building holds a unique story that intertwines with the lives of the pioneers who shaped the nation. Through vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, Faris brings these historic shrines to life, shedding light on the struggles, triumphs, and legacies of those who left an indelible mark on American history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Architecture

Themes: American history, Cultural heritage, Architectural preservation
