Synopsis: In 'The Thirty-Ninth Regiment Massachusetts Volunteers, 1862-1865', Alfred S. Roe takes readers on a captivating journey through the American Civil War, following the experiences of the brave soldiers of the Thirty-Ninth Regiment. From their initial enlistment to their final battles, this gripping account delves into the personal stories, sacrifices, and triumphs of these men as they navigate the horrors of war and fight for their beliefs. With vivid descriptions and heartfelt narratives, Roe paints a vivid picture of the challenges faced by these soldiers, both on and off the battlefield, creating a compelling and unforgettable tale of courage, camaraderie, and resilience.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Patriotism, Brotherhood, Sacrifice, Resilience
