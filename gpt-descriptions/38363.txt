Synopsis: 
In 'A Little Brother to the Bear, and other Animal Studies', William J. Long takes readers on a captivating journey into the animal kingdom, exploring the lives and behaviors of various creatures. From the cunning fox to the majestic eagle, Long's vivid descriptions and engaging storytelling bring these animals to life, revealing their unique personalities and the intricate web of relationships they share with their environment. Through his observations, Long offers a deeper understanding of the natural world and the fascinating creatures that inhabit it.

Genres: 
Nature writing, Animal studies, Non-fiction

Themes: 
Animal behavior, Environmentalism, Interconnectedness of nature
