Synopsis: In 'Colored Branches of the Louisville Free Public Library', the Louisville Free Public Library takes readers on a captivating journey through the history and impact of their colored branches. From the challenges faced during segregation to the triumphs of community empowerment, this book unveils the untold stories of these vital institutions. Through a collection of photographs, personal accounts, and historical documents, readers will discover the resilience, determination, and cultural significance of these branches, shedding light on a lesser-known chapter of American library history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Segregation, Community Empowerment, Cultural Significance
