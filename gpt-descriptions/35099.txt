Synopsis: In 'The Color Line: A Brief in Behalf of the Unborn' by William Benjamin Smith, a thought-provoking exploration of race and identity unfolds as the author delves into the complex history of racial divisions. Through a compelling narrative, Smith challenges conventional notions and offers a fresh perspective on the impact of the color line on society. As the story unfolds, readers are taken on a journey that forces them to question their own beliefs and confront the uncomfortable truths that lie beneath the surface.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Commentary, History

Themes: Race, Identity, Social Inequality
