Synopsis: In 'A History of the French Novel, Vol. 2: To the Close of the 19th Century', George Saintsbury takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and diverse landscape of French literature. From the romantic tales of Alexandre Dumas to the introspective works of Gustave Flaubert, this volume explores the evolution of the French novel, delving into the lives and works of renowned authors who shaped the literary scene of the 19th century. With insightful analysis and engaging anecdotes, Saintsbury paints a vivid picture of the social, cultural, and political influences that shaped these novels, offering readers a deeper understanding of the French literary tradition.

Genres: Literary criticism, History, Non-fiction

Themes: French literature, Evolution of the novel, Cultural and social influences
