Synopsis: In "The Sign of the Prophet: A Tale of Tecumseh and Tippecanoe," author J.B. Naylor takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed wilderness of early 19th-century America. Set against the backdrop of the growing tensions between Native American tribes and the encroaching settlers, the story follows the life of Tecumseh, a visionary Shawnee warrior, and his quest to unite the tribes against the expanding white civilization. As Tecumseh's path intertwines with that of William Henry Harrison, the ambitious governor of the Indiana Territory, the stage is set for a dramatic clash that will forever shape the destiny of a nation.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Native American Literature

Themes: Cultural clash, Leadership and diplomacy, Manifest destiny, Identity and heritage
