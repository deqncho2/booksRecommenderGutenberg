Synopsis: In 'A Daughter of Fife' by Amelia E. Barr, we follow the journey of a young woman named Elsie, who grows up in the picturesque fishing village of Pittenloch in Fife, Scotland. As she navigates the challenges of love, loss, and societal expectations, Elsie's determination and resilience are put to the test. Set against the backdrop of the late 19th century, this captivating tale explores the complexities of family, the pursuit of happiness, and the power of self-discovery.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Love and Relationships, Identity and Self-Discovery, Social Expectations
