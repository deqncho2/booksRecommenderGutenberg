Synopsis: In 'The pronunciation of Greek; accent and quantity. A philological inquiry', John Stuart Blackie delves into the fascinating world of Greek pronunciation, unraveling the mysteries of accent and quantity. With meticulous research and a passion for language, Blackie explores the historical development of Greek pronunciation, shedding light on its intricate nuances and complexities. From the musicality of ancient Greek to the evolution of pronunciation over time, this philological inquiry offers a captivating journey through the sounds of an ancient language.

Genres: Linguistics, Philology, Historical Research

Themes: Language and Sound, Historical Linguistics, Cultural Evolution
