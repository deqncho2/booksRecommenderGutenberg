Synopsis: 

In "American Independence and the French Revolution (1760-1801)," the reader is taken on a captivating journey through two pivotal moments in history that shaped the world as we know it. This meticulously researched and engaging work explores the intertwined stories of the American Revolution and the French Revolution, shedding light on the political, social, and cultural forces that led to these transformative events. From the stirring speeches of American revolutionaries to the passionate debates of French revolutionaries, the reader is immersed in the drama and turmoil of these revolutionary periods. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, the author brings to life the key figures, the battles, the triumphs, and the tragedies that defined these revolutions. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Revolutionary history

Themes: Revolution, Independence, Ideology, Power struggle, Social change
