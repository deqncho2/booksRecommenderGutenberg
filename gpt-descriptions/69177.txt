Synopsis: In 'Mirth and Metre' by Frank E. Smedley and Edmund Yates, readers are transported to the vibrant world of the theater in Victorian London. The story follows the lives of two aspiring actors, each with their own dreams and ambitions, as they navigate the challenges and triumphs of the stage. With a backdrop of witty banter, humorous mishaps, and unexpected romance, this delightful tale explores the complexities of love, friendship, and the pursuit of artistic success in a world filled with mirth and metrical beauty.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Comedy

Themes: Ambition, Love and Romance, Friendship, Theatrical Life
