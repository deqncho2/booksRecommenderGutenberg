Synopsis: In 'Cordova: A city of the Moors', the reader is transported to the enchanting city of Cordova during the height of Moorish rule. Through vivid descriptions and captivating storytelling, the authors, Calvert and Gallichan, paint a mesmerizing picture of the city's rich history, vibrant culture, and architectural wonders. As the narrative unfolds, the reader is introduced to a diverse cast of characters, each with their own ambitions, secrets, and desires. Amidst political intrigue, forbidden love, and the clash of civilizations, Cordova becomes a backdrop for a tale of passion, betrayal, and the enduring power of human resilience. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Adventure

Themes: Cultural identity, Love and desire, Power and politics
