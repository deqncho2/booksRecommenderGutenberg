Synopsis: In 'Writings in Connection with the Donatist Controversy', Augustine, the renowned Bishop of Hippo, delves into a heated theological dispute that threatens to divide the early Christian Church. As he navigates the complexities of this controversy, Augustine's writings offer a captivating exploration of faith, forgiveness, and the nature of true Christian unity.

Genres: Theological treatise, Christian literature, Historical analysis

Themes: Religious schism, Ecclesiastical authority, Forgiveness, Unity in the Church
