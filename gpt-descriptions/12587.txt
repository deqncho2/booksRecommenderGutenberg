Synopsis: In "The Man Who Laughs: A Romance of English History," Victor Hugo takes us on a captivating journey through the life of Gwynplaine, a young man marked by a grotesque and permanent smile. Set against the backdrop of 17th-century England, this enthralling tale follows Gwynplaine's extraordinary adventures as he navigates the treacherous world of politics, love, and societal prejudices. From the grandeur of the royal court to the depths of the criminal underworld, Hugo weaves a spellbinding narrative that explores the complexities of identity, the pursuit of justice, and the enduring power of love.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Gothic Fiction

Themes: Identity, Social Injustice, Love and Sacrifice
