Synopsis: In "Memoirs of a Surrey Labourer: A Record of the Last Years of Frederick Bettesworth," George Sturt takes us on a captivating journey through the life of a humble labourer in the English countryside. Through the eyes of Frederick Bettesworth, we witness the struggles, triumphs, and the profound impact of societal changes on the lives of ordinary people. From the hardships of rural life to the emergence of industrialization, this poignant memoir offers a unique perspective on the human experience and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Fiction, Social Commentary

Themes: Rural life, Industrialization, Social class, Human resilience
