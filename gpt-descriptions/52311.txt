Synopsis: In 'The Little Navajo Herder' by Ann Nolan Clark, a young Navajo girl named Kee watches over her family's sheep in the vast and beautiful desert landscape. As she tends to her flock, she encounters unexpected challenges and learns valuable lessons about responsibility, courage, and the importance of tradition. Through Kee's journey, readers are transported to the rich cultural heritage of the Navajo people and the enduring spirit of a young girl determined to protect her family and way of life.

Genres: Children's Literature, Cultural Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Cultural identity, Tradition, Responsibility, Courage
