Synopsis: In 'The Genetic Effects of Radiation', two brilliant minds, Asimov and Dobzhansky, delve into the fascinating world of genetics and radiation. Through their collaboration, they explore the profound impact of radiation on the genetic makeup of living organisms, unraveling the mysteries of mutation and evolution. With captivating examples and thought-provoking insights, they shed light on the potential dangers and unforeseen consequences of radiation exposure. This enlightening work takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate relationship between radiation and genetics, unveiling the hidden secrets of our biological heritage.

Genres: Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Genetics, Radiation, Mutation, Evolution
