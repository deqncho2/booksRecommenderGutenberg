Synopsis: In "The Pearl of the Antilles, or An Artist in Cuba," Walter Goodman takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and complex world of Cuba. Through the eyes of an artist, the story unfolds, revealing the rich tapestry of Cuban culture, the struggles of its people, and the beauty that lies within its contradictions. As the protagonist immerses himself in the island's art scene, he becomes entangled in a web of love, politics, and personal discovery, ultimately leading him to question his own identity and purpose. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, Goodman paints a vivid portrait of Cuba that will leave readers yearning for more.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Cultural Fiction

Themes: Identity, Art and Creativity, Love and Relationships, Political and Social Issues
