Synopsis: In "Mary Magdalen: A Chronicle," Edgar Saltus delves into the enigmatic life of one of the most controversial figures in biblical history. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Saltus paints a captivating portrait of Mary Magdalen, exploring her journey from a young woman in Galilee to a pivotal figure in the life of Jesus Christ. As the narrative unfolds, readers are immersed in a world of political intrigue, religious fervor, and personal transformation. With a masterful blend of historical accuracy and imaginative storytelling, Saltus invites readers to question their preconceived notions about Mary Magdalen and discover the untold story of a woman who defied societal norms and played a crucial role in shaping Christianity.

Genres: Historical fiction, Religious fiction, Biographical fiction

Themes: Redemption, Faith, Gender roles, Power dynamics
