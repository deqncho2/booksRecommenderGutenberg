Synopsis: 

In 'A History of Modern Europe, 1792-1878' by Fyffe, Charles Alan, embark on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of European history spanning from the French Revolution to the aftermath of the Franco-Prussian War. Delve into the intricate web of political intrigue, social upheaval, and cultural transformation that shaped the continent during this pivotal era. From the rise and fall of empires to the struggles for national identity, witness the birth of modern Europe through the eyes of those who lived it.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Cultural history

Themes: Revolution and upheaval, Nation-building, Power struggles, Social change
