Synopsis: In "The Place of Magic in the Intellectual History of Europe," Thorndike takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of magic and its impact on European intellectual development. From ancient times to the Renaissance, he explores the intricate relationship between magic, science, religion, and philosophy, shedding light on the profound influence that magical beliefs and practices had on the minds of scholars and thinkers. Thorndike's meticulous research and engaging storytelling unveil a hidden side of European history, revealing how magic shaped the intellectual landscape and paved the way for scientific advancements. 

Genres: History, Philosophy, Cultural Studies

Themes: Magic and its influence, Intellectual history, Science and religion, Philosophy and belief systems
