Synopsis: 

In 'Treatise on Poisons: In relation to medical jurisprudence, physiology, and the practice of physic,' Sir Robert Christison delves into the dark and mysterious world of poisons, captivating readers with his extensive knowledge and captivating anecdotes. From deadly plants to lethal chemicals, Christison explores the various types of poisons, their effects on the human body, and their significance in the field of medicine and law. With a keen eye for detail and a passion for unraveling the truth, Christison takes readers on a thrilling journey through the history, science, and practical applications of poisons, leaving them both fascinated and wary of the hidden dangers that surround us.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical, True Crime

Themes: Poisonous substances, Medical jurisprudence, Forensic science, Toxicology
