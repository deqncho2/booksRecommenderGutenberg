Synopsis: In "The Future of Road-making in America," Hulbert, Archer Butler takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of road infrastructure in the United States. From the early days of dirt paths to the modern era of highways and interstates, this book explores the challenges, innovations, and controversies that have shaped America's road network. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, Hulbert delves into the political, economic, and social factors that have influenced road-making decisions, offering a thought-provoking exploration of the past, present, and future of transportation in America.

Genres: History, Transportation, Infrastructure

Themes: Technological advancements, Urban development, Government policies, Environmental impact, Social and economic implications
