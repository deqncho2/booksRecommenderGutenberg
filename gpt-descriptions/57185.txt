Synopsis: In "Napoleon's Russian Campaign of 1812," Edward Foord takes readers on a gripping journey through one of history's most monumental military campaigns. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Foord delves into the strategic decisions, political intrigues, and harrowing battles that shaped Napoleon's ill-fated invasion of Russia. From the scorching heat of the Russian summer to the bitter cold of the winter retreat, this captivating account unveils the triumphs and tragedies of both armies, revealing the true cost of Napoleon's ambition.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Ambition, Power, Consequences of war, Leadership
