Synopsis: In 'Progress and Poverty, Volumes I and II', George Henry delves into the perplexing question of why, despite technological advancements and increasing wealth, poverty and economic depressions persist. Through a thought-provoking inquiry, he explores the root causes of these societal issues, challenging conventional wisdom and proposing innovative solutions. With a blend of economic analysis, historical examples, and philosophical insights, George Henry presents a compelling argument that will make readers question the very foundations of our economic system.

Genres: Economic theory, social commentary, political philosophy

Themes: Wealth inequality, poverty, economic cycles, land ownership, social justice
