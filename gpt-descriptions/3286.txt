Synopsis: 
In 'Selections from the Speeches and Writings of Edmund Burke', readers are transported into the brilliant mind of one of the most influential political thinkers of the 18th century. This collection showcases a diverse range of Burke's speeches and writings, offering profound insights into the pressing issues of his time. From his passionate defense of the American colonies to his scathing critique of the French Revolution, Burke's eloquent words captivate readers, challenging their perspectives and provoking thought on the nature of society, government, and human rights.

Genres: 
Political philosophy, Historical non-fiction, Rhetoric

Themes: 
Conservatism, Natural law, Liberty, Revolution
