Synopsis: 
In "Frederick Law Olmsted: Landscape architect. Vol. 1, Early years and experiences, together with biographical notes," Frederick Law Olmsted takes us on a captivating journey through his formative years and the experiences that shaped his remarkable career. From his early struggles as a farmer and journalist to his transformative travels across Europe, Olmsted's narrative unveils the fascinating story of how he became one of the most influential landscape architects in history. With vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, this volume offers a glimpse into the mind of a visionary artist who sought to create harmonious and sustainable environments for generations to come.

Genres: Biography, History, Architecture

Themes: Nature and the environment, Art and design, Personal growth and perseverance
