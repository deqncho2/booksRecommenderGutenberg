Synopsis: 

In 'Ti-Ping Tien-Kwoh: The History of the Ti-Ping Revolution (Volume I)', Augustus F. Lindley takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous Ti-Ping Revolution in 19th-century China. This gripping historical account delves into the rise of the Ti-Ping Heavenly Kingdom, led by the charismatic Hong Xiuquan, and their quest to overthrow the ruling Qing Dynasty. Lindley's meticulous research and vivid storytelling bring to life the epic battles, political intrigues, and personal sacrifices that shaped this revolutionary movement. As the Ti-Ping Heavenly Kingdom gains momentum, readers will be enthralled by the complex characters, their motivations, and the challenges they face in their pursuit of a new China.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Revolution

Themes: Revolution, Power struggles, Ideology, Cultural clash
