Synopsis: 

In "Twice-born Men in America: or, The Psychology of Conversion as Seen by a Christian Psychologist in Rescue Mission Work," Harriet Earhart Monroe takes us on a captivating journey into the world of rescue missions and the psychology behind religious conversion. Through her experiences as a Christian psychologist, Monroe delves into the lives of individuals who have undergone profound transformations, exploring the complex factors that lead to their spiritual rebirth. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, she uncovers the inner workings of the human mind and the power of faith in shaping one's identity. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Religion

Themes: Religious conversion, Psychology of faith, Personal transformation
