Synopsis: In 'When Buffalo Ran' by George Bird Grinnell, embark on a thrilling journey through the untamed American West, where the thundering hooves of buffalo echo across the vast plains. Follow the gripping accounts of pioneers, Native Americans, and buffalo hunters as they navigate the treacherous landscapes and face the challenges of survival in a rapidly changing world. With vivid descriptions and captivating storytelling, this book immerses readers in the heart-pounding adventures and the profound impact of the buffalo on the history and culture of the American frontier.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Adventure, Western

Themes: Wilderness and survival, Native American culture, Environmental conservation
