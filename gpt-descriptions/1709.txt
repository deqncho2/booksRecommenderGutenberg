Synopsis:
'New Grub Street' by George Gissing is a gripping novel that delves into the harsh realities of the literary world in late 19th century London. The story follows the lives of several aspiring writers, each struggling to make a name for themselves in a society that values commercial success over artistic integrity. As they navigate the treacherous waters of the publishing industry, they face numerous challenges, including poverty, unrequited love, and the constant battle between their passion for writing and the need to earn a living. With its vivid portrayal of the struggles and sacrifices faced by artists, 'New Grub Street' offers a compelling exploration of the tension between art and commerce.

Genres:
Literary fiction, Realism

Themes:
The commercialization of art, the pursuit of success, the conflict between passion and practicality, the impact of poverty on creativity
