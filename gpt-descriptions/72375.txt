Synopsis: In "The historical novel: An essay," Herbert Butterfield takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of historical fiction. Drawing from his extensive knowledge as a historian, Butterfield explores the power and significance of historical novels, shedding light on their ability to transport readers to different eras and provide unique insights into the past. Through thought-provoking analysis and engaging examples, Butterfield delves into the complexities of historical fiction, examining its impact on our understanding of history and its role in shaping our collective memory. This enlightening essay invites readers to explore the rich tapestry of historical novels and discover the profound connections between literature and history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Historical fiction, Literature and history, Interpretation of the past
