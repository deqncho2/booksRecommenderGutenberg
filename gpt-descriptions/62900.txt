Synopsis: 
Opus 21: Descriptive Music for the Lower Kinsey Epoch of the Atomic Age, a Concerto for a One-man Band, Six Arias for Soap Operas, Fugues, Anthems & Barrelhouse is a captivating and unconventional musical composition by Wylie, Philip. This extraordinary opus takes the reader on a mesmerizing journey through a rich tapestry of sounds, blending various genres and styles into a symphony of emotions. From the intricate melodies of a one-man band to the dramatic arias inspired by soap operas, this composition pushes the boundaries of traditional music, offering a unique and immersive experience. As the music unfolds, the reader is transported to different worlds, each filled with its own set of characters and stories. With its innovative approach and masterful execution, Opus 21 is a true testament to the power of music to evoke deep emotions and ignite the imagination.

Genres: Experimental music, Contemporary classical, Opera

Themes: Innovation, Emotion, Storytelling
