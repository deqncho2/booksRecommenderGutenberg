Synopsis: 

'Women' by Booth Tarkington is a captivating novel that delves into the lives of three very different women in early 20th century America. The story follows the intertwined paths of Alice Adams, a young woman from a middle-class family striving to fit into high society; Mildred Palmer, a wealthy socialite with a secret past; and Mary Adams, Alice's mother, who navigates the challenges of being a single parent. As their lives intersect, the novel explores the complexities of love, ambition, and the pursuit of happiness in a rapidly changing society.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Coming-of-age

Themes: Social class, Gender roles, Family dynamics
