Synopsis: In "Beechenbrook: A Rhyme of the War," Margaret Junkin Preston takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous times of the American Civil War. Set in the idyllic Beechenbrook plantation, the story follows the lives of the Randolph family as they navigate the challenges and heartaches brought about by the war. Amidst the backdrop of battlefields and political turmoil, the characters must confront their own personal struggles, testing their loyalties, resilience, and the very essence of their humanity. With vivid descriptions and poetic prose, Preston weaves a tale that explores the profound impact of war on individuals and society, leaving readers spellbound until the very last page.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Poetry

Themes: Love and Loss, Loyalty and Betrayal, Resilience and Survival, The Human Cost of War
