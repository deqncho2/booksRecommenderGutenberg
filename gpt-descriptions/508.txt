Synopsis: In "Twice-Told Tales," Nathaniel Hawthorne weaves a collection of captivating short stories that delve into the mysterious and supernatural aspects of human existence. From haunted houses and cursed artifacts to encounters with the devil himself, each tale presents a unique and thought-provoking exploration of the human psyche. As readers journey through these haunting narratives, they will be drawn into a world where reality and imagination blur, leaving them questioning the boundaries of truth and the power of the human spirit.

Genres: Gothic fiction, supernatural fiction, psychological fiction

Themes: The nature of evil, the power of guilt, the duality of human nature, the consequences of sin, the limits of knowledge
