Synopsis: In 'Lives of the English Poets: Gay, Thomson, Young, Gray, &c.', Samuel Johnson delves into the fascinating lives and works of some of the most influential poets in English literature. From the witty and satirical John Gay to the melancholic and introspective Thomas Gray, Johnson uncovers the triumphs, struggles, and secrets that shaped these poetic geniuses. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, he paints a vivid portrait of each poet, revealing the complexities of their art and the impact they had on the literary landscape of their time. This captivating exploration of their lives and works offers a unique glimpse into the minds of these remarkable individuals and the world in which they lived.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism

Themes: Artistic expression, Literary influence, Personal struggles
