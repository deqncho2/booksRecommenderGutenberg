Synopsis: 

'Incidents in the Life of a Slave Girl, Written by Herself' is a powerful and heart-wrenching autobiography that takes readers on a journey through the life of a young slave girl named Linda. Told in a gripping narrative style, Linda shares her experiences of growing up in slavery, the hardships she endures, and her relentless pursuit of freedom. From the cruel and dehumanizing treatment she faces at the hands of her master to the complex relationships she forms with other slaves, Linda's story is one of resilience, courage, and the indomitable spirit of a woman determined to break free from the chains of bondage.

Genres: Autobiography, Slave narrative, Historical non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Gender and sexuality, Family and motherhood, Resistance and survival
