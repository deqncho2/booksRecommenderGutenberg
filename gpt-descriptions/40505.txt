Synopsis: In 'Scurvy, Past and Present' by Alfred F. Hess, embark on a fascinating journey through history and medicine as the author explores the mysterious disease of scurvy. From ancient civilizations to modern times, Hess uncovers the surprising impact scurvy has had on humanity, revealing the desperate measures taken to combat this deadly ailment. Through captivating anecdotes and meticulous research, this book sheds light on the forgotten chapters of scurvy's history, leaving readers with a newfound appreciation for the power of nutrition and the resilience of the human body.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medicine

Themes: Disease, Medical advancements, Historical perspectives, Nutrition
