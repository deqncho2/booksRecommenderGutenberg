Synopsis: 

'Masters of Space: Morse and the Telegraph; Thompson and the Cable; Bell and the Telephone; Marconi and the Wireless Telegraph; Carty and the Wireless Telephone' by Walter Kellogg Towers takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of communication technology. From Samuel Morse's invention of the telegraph to Thomas Edison's development of the telephone, and finally to the groundbreaking work of Guglielmo Marconi and Michael Carty in wireless communication, this book explores the brilliant minds and remarkable achievements that revolutionized the way we connect with one another. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Biography

Themes: Innovation, Communication, Technological Advancement
