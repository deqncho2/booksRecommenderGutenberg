Synopsis: 

In "The Mammals of Washtenaw County, Michigan: Occasional Papers of the Museum of Zoology, No. 123," author Norman Asa Wood takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse mammal species found in this specific region. From the secretive woodland creatures to the elusive predators, this comprehensive study unveils the fascinating lives and behaviors of the mammals that call Washtenaw County home. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, Wood paints a vivid picture of the intricate web of life that exists within this natural habitat. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Zoology

Themes: Biodiversity, Conservation, Wildlife Ecology
