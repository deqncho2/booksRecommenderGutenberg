Synopsis: In the second volume of "The Life of William Ewart Gladstone," author John Morley delves into the fascinating political career of one of Britain's most influential statesmen. From 1859 to 1880, Gladstone's life unfolds against the backdrop of a rapidly changing world, as he navigates the complexities of power, grapples with his own personal beliefs, and strives to leave a lasting impact on his nation. Morley's meticulous research and engaging narrative shed light on the triumphs, challenges, and inner conflicts that shaped Gladstone's journey, making this biography a captivating exploration of a remarkable figure in history.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Political leadership, Ideological evolution, Historical context
