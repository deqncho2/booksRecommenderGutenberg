Synopsis: 
In 'The Peril and the Preservation of the Home: Being the William L. Bull Lectures for the Year 1903', Jacob A. Riis delves into the pressing issues surrounding the home and its preservation in the early 20th century. Through a series of thought-provoking lectures, Riis explores the challenges faced by families, the impact of urbanization, and the importance of community in maintaining a healthy and thriving home. With vivid descriptions and compelling anecdotes, Riis sheds light on the perils that threaten the sanctity of the home, while also offering insightful solutions for its preservation.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Social commentary, History

Themes: 
Family, Urbanization, Community, Home preservation
