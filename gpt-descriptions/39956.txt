Synopsis: 

In 'The Expositor's Bible: Ezra, Nehemiah, and Esther', Walter F. Adeney delves into the captivating stories of three influential figures from the Old Testament. With meticulous analysis and insightful commentary, Adeney explores the historical context and spiritual significance of these biblical accounts. From the restoration of Jerusalem to the courageous acts of Esther, this book offers a thought-provoking exploration of faith, leadership, and the power of divine providence. 

Genres: Religious literature, Biblical commentary, Historical analysis

Themes: Faith and obedience, Divine providence, Leadership and courage
