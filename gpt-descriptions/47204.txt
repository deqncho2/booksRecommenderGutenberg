Synopsis: In "The Supernatural in Modern English Fiction," Dorothy Scarborough delves into the mysterious and captivating world of supernatural elements in English literature. From ghostly apparitions to eerie hauntings, Scarborough explores the ways in which modern authors have incorporated the supernatural into their stories, creating a sense of intrigue and suspense. Through a careful analysis of various works, she uncovers the underlying themes and motifs that make these supernatural tales so compelling. This book is a must-read for anyone fascinated by the supernatural and its role in shaping modern English fiction.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Supernatural Fiction, Gothic Fiction

Themes: Supernatural elements, Gothic atmosphere, Psychological exploration
