Synopsis: 

In 'The manufacture of earth colours: With thirty-one illustrations' by Josef Bersch, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the world of creating earth colours. From the ancient techniques used by early civilizations to the modern methods employed by contemporary artists, this book unveils the secrets behind the production of these mesmerizing pigments. With thirty-one stunning illustrations, readers will be enthralled by the intricate processes and vibrant hues that emerge from the pages. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Art, History

Themes: Artistic techniques, Historical preservation, Cultural significance
