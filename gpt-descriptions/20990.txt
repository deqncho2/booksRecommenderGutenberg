Synopsis:
In 'London and the Kingdom - Volume 2', author Reginald R. Sharpe takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of London, drawing primarily from the archives at Guildhall. This meticulously researched volume delves into the fascinating stories and events that have shaped the city, offering a unique perspective on its development and influence within the kingdom. From political intrigues to social upheavals, from grand architectural projects to the daily lives of its inhabitants, this book uncovers the hidden layers of London's past, shedding light on the city's enduring significance.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, archival research, urban history

Themes:
City development, political history, social history, architectural heritage
