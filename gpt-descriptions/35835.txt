Synopsis: 

In "History of Prince Edward Island" by Campbell, Duncan, embark on a captivating journey through the rich and tumultuous past of this enchanting Canadian province. From the early Indigenous settlements to the arrival of European explorers, and the subsequent struggles for control and identity, this book unveils the hidden stories and forgotten narratives that have shaped Prince Edward Island into the vibrant place it is today. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the author brings to life the island's captivating history, filled with tales of resilience, conflict, and the enduring spirit of its people.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Cultural studies

Themes: Identity, Resilience, Cultural heritage
