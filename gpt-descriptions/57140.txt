Synopsis:
In the second volume of "Memoirs of the Reign of King George the Third," Horace Walpole delves into the tumultuous and captivating world of 18th-century British politics. Through his sharp wit and keen observations, Walpole offers an intimate and behind-the-scenes account of the events and personalities that shaped the reign of King George III. From political intrigues and scandals to the rise of influential figures, this volume paints a vivid picture of a transformative era in British history.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, political memoir, biography

Themes:
Power and politics, monarchy, societal change
