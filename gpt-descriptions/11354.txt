Synopsis: In 'The Irrational Knot: Being the Second Novel of His Nonage', Shaw takes us on a captivating journey through the lives of a diverse group of characters, each grappling with their own personal struggles and societal expectations. From a young woman torn between love and duty, to a disillusioned artist seeking purpose, and a wealthy businessman questioning the meaning of success, their paths intertwine in unexpected ways, challenging conventional norms and exploring the complexities of human relationships. With Shaw's signature wit and sharp social commentary, this novel delves into the irrationalities that bind us and the knots we must untangle to find true fulfillment.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Social Commentary

Themes: Love and Duty, Individualism, Social Expectations, Success and Happiness
