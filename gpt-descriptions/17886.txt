Synopsis: In 'Jerome, A Poor Man: A Novel' by Mary Eleanor Wilkins Freeman, readers are introduced to Jerome, a humble and hardworking man living in a small New England town. Despite his poverty, Jerome possesses a kind heart and a deep love for his family. As he navigates the challenges of his circumstances, unexpected events unfold that test his resilience and force him to confront the harsh realities of life. Through Jerome's journey, the novel explores themes of poverty, family, resilience, and the power of love.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Realism

Themes: Poverty, Family, Resilience, Love
