Synopsis: 
'Negro Poets and Their Poems' by Robert Thomas Kerlin is a captivating collection that delves into the rich and diverse world of African American poetry. From the powerful verses of Langston Hughes to the lyrical beauty of Gwendolyn Brooks, this anthology showcases the brilliance and resilience of black poets throughout history. Through their words, readers are transported to a realm where emotions run deep, injustices are confronted, and the beauty of the human spirit shines through. With each poem, Kerlin provides insightful commentary, shedding light on the historical and cultural context that shaped these extraordinary works of art. 

Genres: Poetry, Literary Criticism, African American Literature

Themes: Identity, Racism, Social Justice, Cultural Heritage
