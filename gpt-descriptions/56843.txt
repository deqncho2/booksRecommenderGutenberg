Synopsis: In 'Silanus the Christian' by Edwin Abbott Abbott, a young Roman nobleman named Silanus finds himself torn between his loyalty to the Empire and his newfound faith in Christianity. As he navigates the treacherous political landscape of ancient Rome, Silanus must confront the clash between his personal beliefs and the expectations of society. With danger lurking at every corner, Silanus's journey becomes a thrilling tale of faith, love, and sacrifice.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Religious Fiction, Adventure

Themes: Faith and Doubt, Loyalty and Betrayal, Identity and Belonging
