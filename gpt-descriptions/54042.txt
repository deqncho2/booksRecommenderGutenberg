Synopsis: In "A Short History of the Salem Village Witchcraft Trials: Illustrated by a Verbatim Report of the Trial of Mrs. Elizabeth Howe," author M. V. B. Perley delves into the infamous witch trials that plagued Salem Village in the late 17th century. Through a captivating and detailed account of the trial of Mrs. Elizabeth Howe, readers are transported back in time to witness the hysteria, fear, and accusations that tore apart a community. As the trial unfolds, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and the true nature of human behavior is laid bare. This gripping historical account sheds light on the dark chapter of Salem's history, leaving readers questioning the power of belief and the consequences of mass hysteria.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Legal drama

Themes: Mass hysteria, Religious fanaticism, Social dynamics, Justice and injustice
