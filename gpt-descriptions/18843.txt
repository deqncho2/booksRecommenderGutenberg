Synopsis: In "Applied Psychology for Nurses" by Mary F. Porter, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the world of nursing through the lens of psychology. This insightful book delves into the intricate relationship between the mind and the healing process, exploring the psychological aspects of patient care and the impact it has on both nurses and their patients. Through real-life anecdotes and thought-provoking analysis, Porter skillfully highlights the importance of understanding human behavior, emotions, and motivations in the nursing profession. As readers delve into the pages of this book, they will gain a deeper appreciation for the vital role that psychology plays in providing holistic and compassionate care to patients.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Nursing

Themes: Psychology in healthcare, Patient care, Nurse-patient relationship, Emotional well-being, Holistic care
