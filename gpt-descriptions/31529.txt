Synopsis: In 'Modern Marriage and How to Bear It', Maud Churton Braby takes readers on a captivating journey through the complexities of modern relationships. Through a series of insightful anecdotes and thought-provoking observations, Braby explores the challenges faced by couples in the ever-evolving landscape of marriage. From the pressures of societal expectations to the delicate balance of power dynamics, this book offers a refreshing perspective on the intricacies of contemporary matrimony. With wit, wisdom, and a touch of humor, Braby delves into the heart of what it means to navigate the modern institution of marriage, leaving readers with a renewed understanding of love, commitment, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Non-fiction, Self-help, Relationships

Themes: Marriage, Relationships, Societal Expectations, Power Dynamics, Happiness
