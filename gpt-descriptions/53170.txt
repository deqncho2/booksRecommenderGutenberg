Synopsis: 
In "Betrayed Armenia" by Diana Agabeg Apcar, the reader is transported to the tumultuous era of the Armenian Genocide. Through the eyes of the author, we witness the harrowing experiences of a young Armenian woman named Diana, who finds herself caught in the midst of the atrocities committed against her people. As she navigates through the horrors of war, displacement, and loss, Diana's resilience and determination to survive become the driving force behind her journey. This gripping narrative unveils the untold stories of courage, sacrifice, and the enduring spirit of the Armenian people.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Memoir, War

Themes: Survival, Identity, Loss, Resilience, Cultural Heritage
