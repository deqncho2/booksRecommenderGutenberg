Synopsis: In "Early London: Prehistoric, Roman, Saxon and Norman," Walter Besant takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of London, from its prehistoric origins to the Norman conquest. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Besant unveils the fascinating tales of the city's early inhabitants, the Roman occupation, the rise of the Saxons, and the transformative impact of the Normans. From ancient rituals and mysterious artifacts to political intrigue and epic battles, this book offers a captivating exploration of London's formative years, shedding light on the city's evolution and the diverse cultures that shaped its identity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Archaeology, Anthropology

Themes: History, Urban development, Cultural assimilation, Power dynamics
