Synopsis: In "The High Place: A Comedy of Disenchantment," the reader is transported to the enchanting world of Poictesme, where the young hero, Florian de Puysange, embarks on a journey of self-discovery and ambition. As Florian navigates the treacherous paths of love, power, and the pursuit of immortality, he encounters a cast of eccentric characters and faces moral dilemmas that challenge his ideals. With wit and satire, James Branch Cabell weaves a tale that explores the complexities of human desires and the consequences of unchecked ambition.

Genres: Satire, Fantasy, Comedy

Themes: Ambition, Love, Morality, Self-discovery
