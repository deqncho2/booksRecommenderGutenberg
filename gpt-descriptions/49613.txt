Synopsis: In 'The Collected Works in Verse and Prose of William Butler Yeats, Vol. 6 (of 8): Ideas of Good and Evil', delve into the profound musings of one of the greatest poets of the 20th century. This collection showcases Yeats' exploration of the eternal struggle between good and evil, as he delves into the complexities of human nature and the forces that shape our world. Through his captivating verse and thought-provoking prose, Yeats invites readers on a journey that challenges conventional notions of morality and confronts the enigmatic nature of existence.

Genres: Poetry, Prose, Philosophy

Themes: Good vs. Evil, Human Nature, Morality, Existentialism
