Synopsis: 
'Poems, &c. (1790): Wherein It Is Attempted To Describe Certain Views Of Nature And Of Rustic Manners; And Also, To Point Out, In Some Instances, The Different Influence Which The Same Circumstances Produce On Different Characters' by Baillie, Joanna is a collection of poems that explores the beauty of nature and the simplicity of rural life. Through vivid descriptions and engaging narratives, Baillie delves into the various ways in which individuals are affected by their surroundings and circumstances. The poems offer glimpses into the lives of different characters, highlighting the contrasting impacts that similar situations can have on their emotions, actions, and perspectives.

Genres: Poetry, Nature Writing, Rural Literature
Themes: Nature, Rural Life, Human Emotions, Individual Perspectives
