Synopsis: 

'England, Canada and the Great War' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of World War I, exploring the intricate relationship between England and Canada during this pivotal time. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, author Desjardins delves into the political, social, and cultural dynamics that shaped the alliance between these two nations. From the trenches of the Western Front to the halls of power in London and Ottawa, this book offers a fresh perspective on the war and its impact on both countries. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, International relations

Themes: Alliance and cooperation, National identity, War and its consequences
