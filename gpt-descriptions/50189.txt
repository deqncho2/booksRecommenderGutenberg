Synopsis: In 'The Scientific Basis of Morals, and Other Essays', William Kingdon Clifford explores the intricate relationship between science, ethics, and religion. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, he delves into the nature of right and wrong, the ethics of belief, and the ethics of religion. Clifford challenges conventional wisdom and offers a fresh perspective on these timeless topics, inviting readers to question their own moral compass and consider the scientific foundations that underpin our understanding of morality. With a blend of philosophical inquiry and scientific reasoning, this collection of essays offers a captivating exploration of the complex interplay between morality, belief, and the pursuit of truth.

Genres: Philosophy, Ethics, Science

Themes: Morality, Belief, Religion, Science, Ethics
