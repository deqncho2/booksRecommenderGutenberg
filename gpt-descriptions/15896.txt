Synopsis: 

'Five Months at Anzac: A Narrative of Personal Experiences of the Officer Commanding the 4th Field Ambulance, Australian Imperial Force' takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing realities of war as seen through the eyes of Joseph Lievesley Beeston, an officer commanding the 4th Field Ambulance in the Australian Imperial Force. From the chaos of the battlefield to the struggles of providing medical care under extreme conditions, Beeston's personal experiences offer a unique and intimate perspective on the horrors and heroism of World War I.

Genres: Memoir, War, History

Themes: War experiences, Human resilience, Medical care in wartime, Camaraderie, Sacrifice
