Synopsis: In 'Rutledge' by Miriam Coles Harris, a young woman named Rutledge finds herself caught between societal expectations and her own desires. Set in the late 19th century, Rutledge must navigate the complexities of love, family, and personal growth in a world that seems determined to confine her. As she grapples with the choices before her, Rutledge discovers the power of self-discovery and the courage to challenge the status quo. With vivid characters and a captivating narrative, 'Rutledge' is a compelling tale of one woman's journey to find her own path in a restrictive society.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, self-discovery
