Synopsis: 

In 'Front Lines' by Cable, Boyd, a group of young women defy societal expectations and enlist in the military during World War II. As they navigate the challenges of training and combat, they face not only the physical dangers of war but also the prejudices and doubts of those who believe women are not fit for battle. With each passing battle, these courageous women prove their worth and challenge the very notion of gender roles in the military. As the war rages on, their individual stories intertwine, revealing the true strength and resilience of these trailblazing soldiers.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Gender Equality, Courage, Identity, Sacrifice
