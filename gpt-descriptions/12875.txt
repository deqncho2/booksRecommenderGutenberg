Synopsis: 
In "Life in the Roman World of Nero and St. Paul," Tucker takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous era of Emperor Nero and the influential figure of St. Paul. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Tucker paints a vivid picture of the contrasting lives and experiences of these two individuals, offering a unique perspective on the social, political, and religious dynamics of ancient Rome. From the opulent palaces of the Roman elite to the humble dwellings of early Christians, this book delves into the intricate web of power, faith, and survival in a society on the brink of transformation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Religious studies

Themes: Power and corruption, Religious persecution, Cultural clashes, Transformation and resilience
