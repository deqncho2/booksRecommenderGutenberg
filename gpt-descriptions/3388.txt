Synopsis: 
In "The Man of Letters as a Man of Business" by William Dean Howells, we are introduced to Bartley Hubbard, a struggling writer who finds himself caught between his passion for literature and the allure of the business world. As he navigates the cutthroat world of journalism, Bartley must confront the ethical dilemmas and compromises that arise when art collides with commerce. With a cast of intriguing characters and a captivating exploration of the clash between artistic integrity and financial success, this novel takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the complexities of the literary and business realms.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Realism

Themes: Art versus commerce, Ethics, Ambition, Integrity
