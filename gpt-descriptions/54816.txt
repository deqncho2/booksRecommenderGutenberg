Synopsis: In 'My .75: Reminiscences of a Gunner of a .75m/m Battery in 1914', Paul Lintier takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of a gunner during World War I. Through vivid and poignant storytelling, Lintier recounts the intense battles, the camaraderie among soldiers, and the personal sacrifices made on the front lines. As the war unfolds, the gunner's perspective offers a unique insight into the devastating impact of conflict on both individuals and nations. This powerful memoir captures the essence of the Great War, leaving readers captivated by the gunner's courage and resilience.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature
Themes: War, Sacrifice, Camaraderie, Human Resilience
