Synopsis: 

In 'The United Empire Loyalists: A Chronicle of the Great Migration', Wallace W. Stewart takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of the American Revolution and its aftermath. This meticulously researched and vividly written historical account delves into the lives of the United Empire Loyalists, a group of individuals who faced immense challenges and made the difficult decision to leave their homes and embark on a remarkable migration to find refuge in British North America.

Through personal narratives, letters, and historical records, Stewart paints a compelling portrait of the Loyalists' experiences, capturing the hardships they endured, the sacrifices they made, and the resilience they displayed in the face of adversity. From the chaos of war to the struggles of rebuilding their lives in a new land, the author skillfully weaves together the individual stories of these brave men and women, shedding light on a lesser-known aspect of American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Migration

Themes: Loyalty, Identity, Resilience, War and Conflict, Migration and Settlement
