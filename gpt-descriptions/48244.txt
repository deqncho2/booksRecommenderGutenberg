Synopsis: 

In 'Montreal, 1535-1914. Vol. 2. Under British Rule, 1760-1914', author William H. Atherton takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of Montreal during the period of British rule. From the aftermath of the Seven Years' War to the eve of World War I, this volume delves into the political, social, and cultural transformations that shaped the city.

Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Atherton explores the challenges and triumphs faced by Montrealers as they navigated the complexities of British governance. From the struggles for political representation to the rapid industrialization and urbanization, the book paints a vivid picture of a city in constant evolution.

Readers will be enthralled by the tales of influential figures such as James McGill, the founder of McGill University, and the vibrant communities that emerged within Montreal's diverse population. Atherton also delves into the city's cultural scene, highlighting the growth of literature, art, and music during this period.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Cultural history

Themes: British colonization, Political transformation, Urbanization, Cultural development
