Synopsis: 

In 'Traditions of the Tinguian: a Study in Philippine Folk-Lore', Cole, Fay-Cooper takes readers on a captivating journey into the rich and enchanting world of the Tinguian people of the Philippines. Through a meticulous study of their folklore, rituals, and customs, Cole unveils a tapestry of captivating tales and intriguing beliefs that have been passed down through generations. From mythical creatures and supernatural encounters to the significance of dreams and the power of ancestral spirits, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the cultural heritage of the Tinguian people.

Genres: Folklore, Anthropology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Mythology, Supernatural, Cultural Heritage
