Synopsis: In 'Jerry's Charge Account' by Wilson, Hazel Hutchins, a young boy named Jerry discovers the power of responsibility and the consequences of his actions when he is given the opportunity to have his own charge account at a local store. As he navigates the world of credit and purchases, Jerry learns valuable lessons about trust, accountability, and the importance of making wise choices. With unexpected twists and turns, this heartwarming tale explores the challenges and growth that come with newfound independence.

Genres: Children's Literature, Coming-of-Age, Realistic Fiction

Themes: Responsibility, Consequences, Trust, Independence
