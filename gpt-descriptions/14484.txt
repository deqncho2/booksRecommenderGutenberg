Synopsis: 
In 'The Seven Plays in English Verse' by Sophocles, readers are transported to ancient Greece, where they will encounter a collection of captivating tragedies that explore the depths of human nature and the consequences of one's actions. From the tale of a king's tragic downfall to the struggles of a cursed family, each play delves into the complexities of fate, morality, and the eternal battle between free will and destiny. As the characters grapple with their own flaws and confront the consequences of their choices, readers will be enthralled by the timeless themes and profound insights that continue to resonate through the ages.

Genres: Tragedy, Drama

Themes: Fate and free will, Morality, Consequences of actions
