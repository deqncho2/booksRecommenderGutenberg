Synopsis: 
In 'The Lives of the Poets of Great Britain and Ireland (1753) Volume II,' Cibber, Theophilus takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives and works of some of the most influential poets from Britain and Ireland. From the struggles and triumphs of their personal lives to the profound impact of their poetry on society, this volume delves into the fascinating stories behind these literary giants. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Cibber, Theophilus paints a vivid picture of the poets' lives, revealing the complexities and passions that fueled their creative genius.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Artistic inspiration, Personal struggles, Literary legacy
