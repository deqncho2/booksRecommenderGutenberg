Synopsis: In 'The Childrens' Story of the War, Volume 3', author Edward Parrott takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the First World War, specifically from the First Battle of Ypres to the end of the year 1914. Through vivid storytelling and historical accuracy, Parrott paints a vivid picture of the horrors and heroism experienced by children during this devastating conflict. As the war intensifies, young protagonists find themselves caught in the crossfire, facing unimaginable challenges and discovering the strength within themselves. From the trenches to the home front, this volume explores the resilience and courage of children amidst the chaos of war.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War, Children's Literature

Themes: Resilience, Courage, Innocence, Loss, Survival
