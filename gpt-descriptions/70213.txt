Synopsis: 
In 'Yashka: My Life as Peasant, Exile, and Soldier', Mariia Bochkareva takes us on a captivating journey through her extraordinary life as a peasant, an exile, and a soldier. From her humble beginnings in a small Russian village to her experiences as a political prisoner and her eventual enlistment in the Russian army during World War I, Bochkareva's story is one of resilience, determination, and unwavering patriotism. Through her vivid and heartfelt narrative, she provides a unique perspective on the tumultuous events that shaped early 20th-century Russia, offering a glimpse into the lives of ordinary people caught in the midst of war and revolution. As she navigates the challenges of war, love, and loss, Bochkareva's indomitable spirit shines through, inspiring readers with her unwavering commitment to her country and her unwavering belief in the power of the human spirit.

Genres: Autobiography, Historical Non-fiction, War Memoir
Themes: Resilience, Patriotism, War and Revolution, Gender Roles
