Synopsis: In 'The Mormons and the Theatre; or, The History of Theatricals in Utah', John S. Lindsay delves into the fascinating and often controversial relationship between the Mormon community and the world of theater in Utah. From the early days of the pioneers to the present, Lindsay uncovers the struggles, triumphs, and unexpected alliances that shaped the theatrical landscape in this unique religious and cultural setting. Through captivating anecdotes and historical accounts, the author explores the clash of values, the pursuit of artistic expression, and the power of storytelling that unfolded amidst the backdrop of a religiously conservative society. This captivating exploration of the intersection between faith and the arts will leave readers intrigued and enlightened.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Religion and the arts, Cultural clashes, Artistic expression, Identity and community
