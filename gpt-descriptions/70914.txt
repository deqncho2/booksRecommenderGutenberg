Synopsis: In the second volume of "Caroline the Illustrious," author W. H. Wilkins delves deeper into the captivating life of Queen-Consort Caroline, who also served as Queen-Regent during her husband George II's absences. This insightful study explores the remarkable events and influential figures that shaped Caroline's reign, offering a fascinating glimpse into the political and social landscape of her time. As her power and influence grow, Caroline faces numerous challenges and navigates complex relationships, leaving readers eager to uncover the twists and turns of her extraordinary journey.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and politics, Gender roles and expectations, Royal life and influence
