Synopsis: 
In the second volume of Cicero's Letters to Atticus, the renowned Roman statesman and orator continues his correspondence with his dear friend Atticus, offering a fascinating glimpse into the political and personal turmoil of ancient Rome. Through these letters, Cicero shares his thoughts on the rise of Julius Caesar, the shifting alliances of the Roman Senate, and the challenges he faces in navigating the treacherous world of politics. Amidst the political intrigue, Cicero also reveals his innermost fears, hopes, and desires, providing a deeply human portrait of a man caught in the midst of a turbulent era. 

Genres: 
Epistolary, Historical, Biography

Themes: 
Politics, Friendship, Ambition, Loyalty
