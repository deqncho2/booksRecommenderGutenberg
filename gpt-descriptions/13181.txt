Synopsis: In 'The Boy With the U.S. Census' by Rolt-Wheeler, Francis, a young boy embarks on a thrilling adventure as he joins the U.S. Census Bureau. As he travels across the country, he encounters a diverse range of people and experiences, uncovering surprising secrets and untold stories. With each encounter, the boy learns valuable lessons about the power of data, the importance of understanding society, and the impact of his own actions. This captivating tale takes readers on a journey that combines mystery, exploration, and the fascinating world of census-taking.

Genres: Adventure, Mystery, Historical Fiction

Themes: Exploration, Identity, Social Dynamics
