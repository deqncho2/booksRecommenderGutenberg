Synopsis: In "Morality Without God: A Lecture Delivered Before the Independent Religious Society," Mangasarian challenges conventional beliefs by exploring the possibility of leading a moral life without the need for religious guidance. Through thought-provoking arguments and insightful analysis, he delves into the nature of morality, the origins of ethical principles, and the role of religion in shaping our moral compass. This captivating lecture challenges readers to question their own beliefs and consider the possibility of a secular morality that is independent of religious doctrines.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Morality, Ethics, Religion, Secularism
