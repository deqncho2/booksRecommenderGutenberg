Synopsis: 
"The Goodness of St. Rocque, and Other Stories" by Alice Moore Dunbar-Nelson is a collection of captivating short stories that delve into the lives of African Americans in the early 20th century. From the struggles of love and marriage to the complexities of racial identity, each story presents a unique and thought-provoking narrative. Whether it's a tale of forbidden romance or a glimpse into the challenges faced by individuals in a racially divided society, Dunbar-Nelson's stories are filled with rich characters and poignant moments that will leave readers yearning for more.

Genres: 
Fiction, Short Stories, African American Literature

Themes: 
Love and Marriage, Racial Identity, Social Inequality
