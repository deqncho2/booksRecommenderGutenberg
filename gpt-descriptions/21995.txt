Synopsis: In "Philosophy and Religion: Six Lectures Delivered at Cambridge," Rashdall, Hastings takes readers on a captivating intellectual journey exploring the intricate relationship between philosophy and religion. Through a series of thought-provoking lectures, Rashdall delves into the profound questions that have puzzled humanity for centuries, challenging conventional wisdom and offering fresh perspectives. From the nature of God to the existence of evil, from the limits of human knowledge to the meaning of life, this book invites readers to embark on a profound exploration of the fundamental mysteries of existence.

Genres: Philosophy, Religion, Non-fiction

Themes: Philosophy of religion, Existence of God, Ethics, Epistemology, Meaning of life
