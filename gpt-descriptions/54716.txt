Synopsis: 

In 'The Horse in America: A practical treatise on the various types common in the United States, with something of their history and varying characteristics,' John Gilmer Speed takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and diverse characteristics of horses in the United States. From the majestic wild mustangs of the West to the elegant thoroughbreds of the racetrack, Speed delves into the fascinating world of these magnificent creatures, exploring their origins, development, and the unique qualities that make each breed special. With vivid descriptions and insightful anecdotes, this book offers a comprehensive and engaging exploration of the horse's role in shaping American culture and society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Animal Studies, History

Themes: Animal-human relationship, American culture, Horse breeding and training
