Synopsis: 

'Lead poisoning and lead absorption: The symptoms, pathology and prevention, with special reference to their industrial origin, and an account of the principal processes involving risk' delves into the alarming issue of lead poisoning and absorption, particularly in relation to its industrial origins. Written by Sir Thomas Morison Legge and Kenneth Weldon Goadby, this comprehensive work explores the symptoms, pathology, and prevention methods associated with lead exposure. It also provides an insightful account of the principal processes that pose a risk of lead absorption. 

Genres: Medical literature, Industrial health, Occupational safety

Themes: Lead poisoning, Industrial hazards, Public health, Occupational health and safety
