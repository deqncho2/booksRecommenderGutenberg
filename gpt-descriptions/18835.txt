Synopsis: In "The Concept of Nature: The Tarner Lectures Delivered in Trinity College, November 1919," Alfred North Whitehead takes readers on a captivating intellectual journey exploring the fundamental nature of the world around us. Through a series of thought-provoking lectures, Whitehead challenges conventional notions of reality, delving into the intricate relationship between science, philosophy, and our understanding of nature. With his characteristic clarity and depth, he invites readers to question their assumptions and embark on a quest for a more comprehensive understanding of the universe.

Genres: Philosophy, Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Nature of reality, Philosophy of science, Epistemology, Metaphysics
