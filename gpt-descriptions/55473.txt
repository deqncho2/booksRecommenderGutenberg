Synopsis: 
'Kotto: Being Japanese Curios, with Sundry Cobwebs' by Lafcadio Hearn is a captivating collection of essays that takes readers on a mesmerizing journey through the enchanting world of Japanese culture. From ancient legends and ghostly tales to intricate customs and traditional arts, Hearn delves into the depths of Japan's rich heritage, offering a unique perspective on its people and their way of life. With vivid descriptions and insightful observations, this book unveils the hidden treasures and peculiarities that make Japan a land of endless fascination.

Genres: Non-fiction, Travelogue, Cultural Studies

Themes: Japanese culture, Folklore, Tradition, Aesthetics
