Synopsis: In 'Monopolies and the People' by Cloud, D. C., a young journalist uncovers a shocking secret that threatens to upend the balance of power in a world dominated by corporate giants. As she delves deeper into the dark underbelly of monopolistic control, she finds herself entangled in a dangerous web of deceit, corruption, and personal vendettas. With her life on the line, she must navigate a treacherous landscape where loyalty is scarce and the truth is a rare commodity. This gripping tale of intrigue and suspense will keep readers on the edge of their seats until the very last page.

Genres: Thriller, Mystery, Suspense

Themes: Corporate power, Corruption, Journalism, Betrayal
