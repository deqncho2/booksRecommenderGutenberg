Synopsis: In 'Battle of the Crater; and Experiences of Prison Life', Sumner U. Shearman takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing events of the Battle of the Crater during the American Civil War. Through vivid and personal accounts, Shearman provides a unique perspective on the battle and its aftermath, including his own experiences as a prisoner of war. This captivating narrative explores the bravery, sacrifice, and resilience of soldiers on both sides of the conflict, shedding light on the untold stories of those who fought and suffered during this pivotal moment in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, war memoir

Themes: Courage, sacrifice, resilience, the human cost of war
