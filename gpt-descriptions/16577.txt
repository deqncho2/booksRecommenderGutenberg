Synopsis: In "The Business of Being a Woman," Ida M. Tarbell takes readers on a captivating journey through the challenges and triumphs of being a woman in the early 20th century. Through personal anecdotes and insightful observations, Tarbell explores the complexities of balancing career aspirations, societal expectations, and personal fulfillment. With wit and wisdom, she delves into the struggles faced by women in the business world, shedding light on the inequalities and prejudices they encounter. This thought-provoking book offers a compelling exploration of the evolving role of women in society and the pursuit of equality.

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, Feminism

Themes: Gender inequality, Women's empowerment, Career challenges, Societal expectations
