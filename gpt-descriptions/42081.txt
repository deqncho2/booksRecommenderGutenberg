Synopsis: 
In the second volume of "The Diary of John Evelyn," readers are transported back to 17th-century England, where they delve into the captivating personal journal of John Evelyn, a prominent diarist and intellectual of his time. This volume continues to offer a fascinating glimpse into Evelyn's life, filled with his observations, encounters with notable figures, and reflections on the political and cultural landscape of the era. From his travels across Europe to his involvement in the Royal Society, Evelyn's diary provides an intimate and vivid account of a remarkable period in history.

Genres: 
Autobiography, Historical Non-fiction, Memoir

Themes: 
Personal Reflection, Historical Events, Intellectual Pursuits
