Synopsis: In "Chinese vs. Negroes as American Citizens," Samuel Raymond Scottron presents a thought-provoking perspective on the advantages of proposed Negro colonization in South America. Through a compelling analysis, Scottron explores the potential benefits and challenges of this proposition, shedding light on the complex dynamics between different racial groups in America. This work delves into the intricacies of race, citizenship, and the potential for a new future for African Americans.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political discourse, Social commentary

Themes: Race relations, Colonization, Citizenship, Identity
