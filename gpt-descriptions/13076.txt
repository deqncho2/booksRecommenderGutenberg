Synopsis: 
"The Life of John Ruskin" by Collingwood, W. G. delves into the captivating journey of one of the most influential figures of the Victorian era. This biography takes readers on a mesmerizing exploration of Ruskin's multifaceted life, from his groundbreaking work as an art critic and social thinker to his tumultuous personal relationships. As the pages unfold, readers will witness Ruskin's unwavering dedication to his ideals, his relentless pursuit of beauty, and the profound impact he had on the world around him. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Collingwood paints a vivid portrait of a man whose life was as complex and captivating as his ideas.

Genres: Biography, History, Art

Themes: Art criticism, Social reform, Beauty, Idealism
