Synopsis: In "Cuba, Old and New" by Albert G. Robinson, embark on a captivating journey through the vibrant streets of Cuba, where the old and the new collide in a mesmerizing dance. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Robinson paints a vivid picture of the island's rich history, its resilient people, and the contrasting worlds that coexist within its borders. From the crumbling colonial architecture to the pulsating rhythms of salsa, this book offers a unique glimpse into the heart and soul of Cuba, capturing its beauty, complexity, and the indomitable spirit of its inhabitants.

Genres: Travel, Memoir, Cultural Studies

Themes: History, Identity, Resilience, Cultural Preservation
