Synopsis: In 'The Making of an American', Jacob A. Riis takes readers on a captivating journey through his own personal experiences as an immigrant in America. From his humble beginnings in Denmark to his struggles in the bustling streets of New York City, Riis paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs faced by countless immigrants during the late 19th century. Through his poignant storytelling and powerful photographs, Riis sheds light on the harsh realities of poverty, inequality, and the fight for social justice. This compelling memoir offers a unique perspective on the American Dream and the resilience of those who strive to achieve it.

Genres: Memoir, Social Commentary, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Immigration, Poverty, Social Justice, Resilience
