Synopsis: 

In 'Cacao Culture in the Philippines', Lyon, William Scrugham takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and fascinating world of cacao cultivation in the Philippines. From the ancient traditions of the indigenous people to the modern-day challenges faced by farmers, this book unveils the secrets behind the country's renowned cacao industry. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Lyon explores the intricate relationship between the land, the people, and the delectable cacao bean, revealing surprising connections that span centuries. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Cultural heritage, Agriculture, Sustainability, Economic development
