Synopsis: 

"The Fugitive Blacksmith: or, Events in the History of James W. C. Pennington, Pastor of a Presbyterian Church, New York, Formerly a Slave in the State of Maryland, United States" is a captivating autobiography that follows the remarkable journey of James W. C. Pennington. Born into slavery in Maryland, Pennington's life takes an unexpected turn when he escapes and becomes a fugitive blacksmith. As he navigates the perils of freedom, Pennington's story unfolds, revealing the challenges he faces, the people he encounters, and the profound impact he makes on society. 

Genres: Autobiography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Identity, Resilience
