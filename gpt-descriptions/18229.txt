Synopsis: In 'Transactions of the American Society of Civil Engineers, Vol. LXVIII, Sept. 1910: The New York Tunnel Extension of the Pennsylvania Railroad.; Paper No. 1150' by Charles W. Raymond, embark on a fascinating journey through the construction of the New York Tunnel Extension of the Pennsylvania Railroad. Delve into the challenges faced by engineers as they strive to create a groundbreaking transportation infrastructure, and witness the innovative solutions they employ to overcome these obstacles. This captivating account offers a glimpse into the world of civil engineering and the remarkable feats accomplished in the early 20th century.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, History

Themes: Infrastructure development, Engineering challenges, Innovation
