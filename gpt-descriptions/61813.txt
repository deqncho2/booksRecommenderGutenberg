Synopsis:
'Folklore of Wells: Being a Study of Water-Worship in East and West' takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of water-worship. Masani, Rustom Pestonji, an expert in the field, delves into the rich tapestry of beliefs and rituals surrounding wells in both Eastern and Western cultures. From ancient myths and legends to modern-day practices, this book uncovers the deep-rooted significance of water and its connection to spirituality and human existence. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Masani sheds light on the mystical allure of wells and the profound impact they have had on societies throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Folklore, Cultural Studies

Themes: Water-worship, Spirituality, Cultural Traditions, Mythology, Human Connection
