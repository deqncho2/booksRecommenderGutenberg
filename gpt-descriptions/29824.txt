Synopsis: 
In 'Diana' by Susan Warner, a young woman named Diana finds herself caught between societal expectations and her own desires. As she navigates the complexities of love, family, and personal growth, Diana must make difficult choices that will shape her future. Set against the backdrop of 19th-century England, this captivating novel explores the challenges faced by women in a time of rigid social norms and the power of self-discovery.

Genres: 
Romance, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: 
Gender roles, Love and sacrifice, Individuality and self-expression
