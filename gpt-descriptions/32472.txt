Synopsis: In 'Food in War Time' by Lusk, Graham, embark on a captivating journey through the history of food during times of conflict. From the trenches of World War I to the rationing of World War II, this book unveils the untold stories of how food became a weapon, a symbol of resilience, and a source of hope in the darkest of times. Discover the ingenious ways people adapted their diets, the secret recipes that sustained them, and the extraordinary lengths they went to ensure survival. Delve into the fascinating intersection of food, war, and human ingenuity in this gripping exploration.

Genres: History, Food and Drink, War

Themes: Resilience, Adaptation, Survival
