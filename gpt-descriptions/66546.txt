Synopsis: In 'The Ivory King: A popular history of the elephant and its allies', Charles Frederick Holder takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of elephants and their allies. From ancient civilizations to modern times, this popular history delves into the intricate relationship between humans and these majestic creatures. Through captivating anecdotes, Holder explores the cultural significance, ecological impact, and awe-inspiring qualities of elephants, shedding light on their intelligence, strength, and the challenges they face in the modern world. This book is a captivating exploration of the captivating world of elephants and their allies, offering a unique perspective on their history and their place in our shared world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Animal Studies

Themes: Human-animal relationships, Conservation, Cultural significance of animals
