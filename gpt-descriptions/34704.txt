Synopsis: 

In 'British Goblins: Welsh Folk-lore, Fairy Mythology, Legends and Traditions', Sikes, Wirt takes readers on a captivating journey into the enchanting world of Welsh folklore and fairy mythology. Delving into the rich tapestry of Welsh traditions, this book unveils a treasure trove of magical creatures, mythical legends, and age-old customs that have shaped the cultural heritage of Wales. From mischievous fairies and shape-shifting witches to ancient rituals and mysterious supernatural beings, the author presents a captivating exploration of the mystical realm that lies just beyond the veil of reality. With vivid descriptions and captivating storytelling, 'British Goblins' offers a fascinating glimpse into the hidden corners of Welsh folklore, leaving readers spellbound and eager to uncover more.

Genres: Folklore, Mythology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Welsh folklore, Fairy mythology, Cultural heritage, Supernatural beings
