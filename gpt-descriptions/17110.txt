Synopsis: In 'The Young Man and the World' by Albert J. Beveridge, follow the captivating journey of a young man as he embarks on a quest to discover his purpose and place in the world. From the bustling streets of New York City to the untamed wilderness of the American West, he encounters a diverse cast of characters and faces numerous challenges that test his resilience and determination. As he navigates through the complexities of life, love, and ambition, the young man must confront his own fears and insecurities, ultimately leading him to a profound realization about the true meaning of success and happiness.

Genres: Adventure, Coming-of-age, Historical Fiction

Themes: Self-discovery, Ambition, Love and Relationships, Society and Class
