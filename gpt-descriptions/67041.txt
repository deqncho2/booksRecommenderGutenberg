Synopsis: In 'Treatment of Cholera in the Royal Hospital, Haslar: during the months of July and August, 1849, with remarks on the name and origin of the disease,' John Wilson, Inspector of Naval Hospitals, delves into the gripping account of his experiences combating the deadly cholera outbreak. With vivid descriptions and insightful observations, Wilson explores the mysterious origins of the disease and provides a detailed analysis of the treatment methods employed during this critical period. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be captivated by the relentless battle against cholera and the quest for understanding its name and origins.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical History, Epidemiology

Themes: Disease outbreak, Medical treatment, Historical analysis
