Synopsis: 
In "The Wild Swans at Coole," W.B. Yeats takes us on a poetic journey through the enchanting Coole Park, where he encounters a flock of majestic swans. As he observes their graceful presence and reflects on the passing of time, Yeats delves into the depths of his own emotions, contemplating themes of love, loss, and the inevitable changes that come with age. Through his lyrical verses, he invites readers to join him in this poignant exploration of nature, beauty, and the complexities of the human heart.

Genres:
Poetry, Symbolism, Romanticism

Themes:
Nature, Time, Love, Loss, Aging
