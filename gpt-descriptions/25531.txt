Synopsis: In 'Representative Plays by American Dramatists: 1856-1911: The Moth and the Flame' by Clyde Fitch, a captivating tale unfolds as a young woman finds herself torn between duty and desire. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the journey of a passionate and ambitious actress who becomes entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and societal expectations. As she navigates the complexities of her relationships, she must confront the consequences of her choices and the sacrifices she is willing to make for her dreams.

Genres: Drama, Romance, Historical Fiction

Themes: Love and passion, societal expectations, ambition, sacrifice
