Synopsis: 
In "On chloroform and other anæsthetics: their action and administration," John Snow delves into the fascinating world of anæsthetics, exploring their mysterious effects and the methods of their administration. Through meticulous research and captivating anecdotes, Snow takes readers on a journey through the history, science, and practical applications of these remarkable substances. From the discovery of chloroform to the exploration of its impact on the human body, this book offers a captivating exploration of the world of anæsthetics that will leave readers spellbound.

Genres: Medical literature, Scientific literature, Historical non-fiction

Themes: Medical advancements, Scientific discovery, Human physiology
