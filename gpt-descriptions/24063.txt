Synopsis: 
'A Handbook of Some South Indian Grasses' is a comprehensive guide that takes readers on a fascinating journey through the diverse world of grasses found in South India. Written by Rangachari and Tadulinga Mudaliyar, this book offers a meticulous exploration of various grass species, their characteristics, and their ecological significance. From the enchanting landscapes of the Western Ghats to the vast plains of Tamil Nadu, the authors provide detailed descriptions and vivid illustrations that capture the beauty and importance of these often overlooked plants. Whether you are a botany enthusiast, a nature lover, or simply curious about the wonders of the natural world, this handbook will undoubtedly ignite your curiosity and deepen your appreciation for the remarkable diversity of South Indian grasses.

Genres: Non-fiction, Botany, Nature Guide

Themes: Biodiversity, Ecology, Botanical Exploration
