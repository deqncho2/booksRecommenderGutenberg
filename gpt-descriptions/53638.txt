Synopsis: In "The Gentle Art of Faking: A history of the methods of producing imitations & spurious works of art from the earliest times up to the present day," author Riccardo Nobili takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of art forgery. From ancient times to the modern era, this book unveils the secrets behind the creation of imitations and spurious works of art, exploring the techniques, motivations, and consequences of these deceptive practices. With intriguing anecdotes and historical examples, Nobili delves into the minds of forgers and the intricate web of deceit they weave, leaving readers questioning the authenticity of the art they encounter. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Art History, True Crime

Themes: Deception, Authenticity, Artistic Integrity, Criminality
