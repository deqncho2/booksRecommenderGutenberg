Synopsis: In "The Grand Inquisitor," Dostoyevsky delves into a thought-provoking encounter between Jesus Christ and a powerful figure from the Spanish Inquisition. Set in the heart of the Inquisition, the story unfolds as the Grand Inquisitor confronts Jesus, challenging his teachings and questioning his purpose. Through a captivating dialogue, the Grand Inquisitor presents a compelling argument against Christ's message of freedom and individual choice, raising profound questions about the nature of faith, human nature, and the role of religion in society.

Genres: Philosophical fiction, Religious fiction, Historical fiction

Themes: Faith and doubt, Freedom and authority, Religion and power, Human nature
