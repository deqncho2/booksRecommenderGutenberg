Synopsis: 

'Chronicles of Newgate, Vol. 1: From the twelfth to the eighteenth century' by Griffiths, Arthur takes readers on a captivating journey through the infamous Newgate Prison, spanning six centuries of history. From the medieval era to the Age of Enlightenment, this enthralling volume unveils the secrets, scandals, and struggles that unfolded within the walls of one of London's most notorious institutions. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Griffiths paints a vivid picture of the lives of both prisoners and prison staff, revealing the harsh realities, unexpected alliances, and surprising acts of resilience that characterized life in Newgate. As the pages turn, readers will be drawn into a world of intrigue, desperation, and redemption, where the line between right and wrong becomes blurred, and the human spirit is tested to its limits.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Biography

Themes: Crime and punishment, Social justice, Human resilience
