Synopsis: In this captivating biography, George Ticknor Curtis delves into the life of James Buchanan, the enigmatic fifteenth President of the United States. From his humble beginnings to his rise in politics, Curtis paints a vivid portrait of a man who navigated the turbulent waters of American history. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, the author explores Buchanan's personal and political struggles, shedding light on the complexities of his presidency and the lasting impact he had on the nation. This compelling narrative offers a fresh perspective on one of America's most controversial leaders.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Leadership, Political Legacy, American History
