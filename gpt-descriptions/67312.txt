Synopsis: 
'As Others See Us: Being the Diary of a Canadian Debutante' takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of a young Canadian woman as she navigates the world of high society and societal expectations. Through her diary entries, we witness her struggles, triumphs, and the secrets she keeps hidden from those around her. As she grapples with her identity and the pressures of conformity, the diary becomes a sanctuary where she can truly be herself. With each page, the reader becomes more entangled in her world, eagerly turning the pages to uncover the truth behind her carefully constructed facade.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Historical

Themes: Identity, Society, Conformity, Secrets
