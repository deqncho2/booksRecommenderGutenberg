Synopsis: 
In "Two Years in the Forbidden City," Der Ling, a young woman of mixed Chinese and European heritage, offers a captivating firsthand account of her time as a lady-in-waiting to Empress Dowager Cixi in the late 19th century. Through her vivid descriptions and intimate anecdotes, Der Ling unveils the opulent and secretive world of the Chinese imperial court, revealing the intricate rituals, political intrigues, and personal struggles that she witnessed during her two-year stay. As she navigates the treacherous waters of palace life, Der Ling's unique perspective sheds light on the clash between tradition and modernity, East and West, and the complexities of power and identity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Cultural Studies

Themes: Identity, Power, Tradition vs. Modernity, East vs. West, Gender Roles
