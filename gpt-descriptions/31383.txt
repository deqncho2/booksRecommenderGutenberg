Synopsis: 

In "The Industrial Canal and Inner Harbor of New Orleans: History, Description and Economic Aspects of Giant Facility Created to Encourage Industrial Expansion and Develop Commerce," Thomas Ewing Dabney takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history and economic significance of the Industrial Canal and Inner Harbor of New Orleans. From its inception as a visionary project to its role in transforming the city into a thriving industrial hub, Dabney explores the intricate details and grand scale of this monumental facility. 

Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Dabney unveils the challenges faced during the construction of the canal, the impact it had on the city's economy, and the subsequent development of the Inner Harbor. Readers will be enthralled by the tales of engineering triumphs, the bustling maritime activities, and the economic boom that followed the completion of this ambitious project. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Economics

Themes: Industrialization, Urban development, Economic growth
