Synopsis: In the second volume of his personal memoirs, General Philip Henry Sheridan takes readers on a captivating journey through his experiences during the American Civil War. From his pivotal role in the Battle of Chickamauga to his leadership in the Shenandoah Valley Campaign, Sheridan provides a vivid and intimate account of the challenges and triumphs he faced on the battlefield. As he recounts his encounters with notable figures such as General Grant and President Lincoln, Sheridan offers a unique perspective on the inner workings of the Union Army and the political landscape of the time. With his engaging storytelling and insightful reflections, this memoir offers a compelling glimpse into the life of one of the most influential military leaders in American history.

Genres: Autobiography, Military History, Memoir

Themes: Leadership, War, Politics, Personal Growth
