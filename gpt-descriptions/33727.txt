Synopsis: 

'Creative Intelligence: Essays in the Pragmatic Attitude' is a thought-provoking collection of essays written by a group of influential philosophers and educators, including John Dewey, George H. Mead, and Horace Meyer Kallen, among others. Each essay explores the concept of creative intelligence and its significance in our everyday lives, challenging traditional notions of intelligence and offering fresh perspectives on how we can cultivate and apply our creative abilities. Through engaging discussions on topics such as education, art, science, and social progress, the authors invite readers to embrace a pragmatic attitude that values innovation, adaptability, and the power of imagination. 

Genres: Philosophy, Education, Psychology

Themes: Creative intelligence, Pragmatism, Education reform, Innovation, Imagination
