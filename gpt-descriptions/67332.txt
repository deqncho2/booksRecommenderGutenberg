Synopsis: In 'Psychopathology of Everyday Life', Freud delves into the hidden meanings behind our everyday slip-ups, forgetfulness, and mistakes. Through a series of intriguing case studies and personal anecdotes, he uncovers the unconscious motivations and desires that shape our actions and behaviors. From the seemingly innocent misplacement of objects to the accidental slips of the tongue, Freud reveals the fascinating ways in which our unconscious mind influences our daily lives. This thought-provoking exploration of the human psyche will leave readers questioning the true nature of their own actions and the hidden depths of their own minds.

Genres: Psychology, Non-fiction

Themes: Unconscious mind, Human behavior, Psychoanalysis
