Synopsis: 
'The Plays of Roswitha' by Hrotsvitha is a collection of six plays written in the 10th century. These plays, set in various historical and mythological contexts, explore the complexities of human nature, the power of faith, and the triumph of virtue over vice. From tales of love and sacrifice to stories of redemption and divine intervention, each play presents a unique and thought-provoking narrative that captivates the reader's imagination.

Genres: Drama, Historical Fiction, Religious Literature

Themes: Morality, Faith, Redemption, Love and Sacrifice
