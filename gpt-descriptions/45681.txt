Synopsis: In this installment of the Chronicles of England, Scotland and Ireland, the focus is on the reign of King Henry VII, who ascended to the throne after the tumultuous period of Henry VI. The narrative explores the life and achievements of Henry VII, who was the son of Edmund Earl of Richmond and the brother of Henry VI. As the kingdom recovers from the Wars of the Roses, Henry VII faces numerous challenges, including political unrest, threats to his rule, and the need to establish a stable dynasty. This section of the Chronicles delves into the intricacies of Henry VII's reign, his diplomatic endeavors, and his efforts to consolidate power and bring stability to England.

Genres: Historical, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and Politics, Dynasty and Succession, Restoration and Stability
