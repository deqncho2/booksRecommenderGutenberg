Synopsis: In 'Delusion and Dream: an Interpretation in the Light of Psychoanalysis of Gradiva', Sigmund Freud delves into the fascinating world of Wilhelm Jensen's novel 'Gradiva' and offers a captivating psychoanalytic interpretation. As Freud explores the protagonist's delusions and dreams, he uncovers hidden desires, repressed memories, and the intricate workings of the human psyche. With his characteristic insight, Freud takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the realms of love, sexuality, and the unconscious mind, unraveling the mysteries that lie beneath the surface of Jensen's mesmerizing tale.

Genres: Psychoanalysis, Literary Criticism, Psychology

Themes: Dreams and their interpretation, Repression and the unconscious mind, Love and desire, Sexuality and its manifestations
