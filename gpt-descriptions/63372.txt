Synopsis: 

In 'Einstein's Theories of Relativity and Gravitation', J. Malcolm Bird presents a captivating selection of essays that delve into the groundbreaking ideas of one of the greatest scientific minds in history. This compilation features the essays submitted for the prestigious Eugene Higgins prize, offering readers a unique opportunity to explore the complexities of Einstein's theories and their profound impact on our understanding of the universe. From the mind-bending concepts of space-time curvature to the revolutionary notion of the equivalence principle, Bird's collection invites readers on a fascinating journey through the mind of a genius.

Genres: Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Theories of Relativity, Gravitation, Scientific Discovery
