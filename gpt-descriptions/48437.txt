Synopsis: In 'The Art and Practice of Hawking' by Michell, E. B., readers are transported into the captivating world of falconry, where the ancient art of hawking is explored in all its glory. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, the author delves into the intricacies of training and flying birds of prey, revealing the deep bond that forms between falconer and bird. From the exhilarating hunts to the challenges faced in preserving this age-old tradition, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the captivating world of hawking.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature Writing, Memoir

Themes: Nature, Tradition, Bond between humans and animals
