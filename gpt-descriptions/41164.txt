Synopsis: 

Step into the world of the most celebrated British poets as William Howitt takes you on a captivating journey through their homes and haunts. In this first volume of a two-part series, Howitt delves into the lives and surroundings of renowned poets such as Shakespeare, Milton, and Wordsworth, revealing the hidden stories and inspirations behind their timeless works. From the enchanting landscapes of the Lake District to the bustling streets of London, each chapter unveils the intimate connections between these literary giants and the places that shaped their art. With vivid descriptions and fascinating anecdotes, Howitt paints a vivid picture of the environments that nurtured the creative genius of these poets, offering readers a unique glimpse into their lives and the world that inspired their masterpieces.

Genres: Biography, Travel, Literary Criticism

Themes: Literary inspiration, Creative process, Connection between art and environment
