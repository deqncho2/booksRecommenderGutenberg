Synopsis: In 'A Tract for the Times: The Church and the Census', James Skinner takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the intersection of religion and data. As the church grapples with the implications of the upcoming census, a young clergyman finds himself caught in a web of secrets and moral dilemmas. With the fate of his congregation hanging in the balance, he must navigate the murky waters of faith, truth, and societal expectations. This gripping tale explores the power dynamics within religious institutions and the profound impact of numbers on our understanding of spirituality.

Genres: Fiction, Mystery, Religious

Themes: Religion and society, Morality, Truth and deception
