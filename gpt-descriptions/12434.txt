Synopsis: 

In "Twelve Studies on the Making of a Nation: The Beginnings of Israel's History," authors Kent and Jenks delve into the captivating origins of Israel, exploring its early history through twelve thought-provoking studies. From the legendary figures of Abraham and Moses to the tumultuous period of the Judges, this book takes readers on a fascinating journey through the formative years of a nation that would shape the course of history. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, the authors shed light on the struggles, triumphs, and defining moments that shaped the identity of ancient Israel.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biblical studies, Archaeology

Themes: Nation-building, Identity formation, Divine intervention, Leadership, Historical interpretation
