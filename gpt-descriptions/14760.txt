Synopsis: 

In 'Report of the Special Committee on Moral Delinquency in Children and Adolescents', the New Zealand Special Committee delves into the complex and often misunderstood world of moral delinquency among young individuals. Through extensive research, interviews, and case studies, the committee uncovers shocking truths about the factors that contribute to moral deviance in children and adolescents. From the influence of media and technology to the impact of family dynamics and societal pressures, this report sheds light on the multifaceted nature of moral delinquency. As the committee presents their findings, they also propose innovative strategies and interventions to address this pressing issue and ensure the well-being of future generations.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social Sciences, Psychology

Themes: Moral delinquency, Youth behavior, Social influences, Family dynamics, Intervention strategies
