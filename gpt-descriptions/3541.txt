Synopsis: In 'What to Do? Thoughts Evoked By the Census of Moscow', Tolstoy takes us on a captivating journey through the bustling streets of Moscow, where a young nobleman finds himself questioning the purpose and meaning of his life. As he encounters a diverse array of characters and experiences, he is forced to confront his own desires, ambitions, and the societal expectations that surround him. This introspective tale delves into the depths of human nature, exploring the complexities of identity, love, and the pursuit of happiness.

Genres: Fiction, Philosophical novel, Psychological novel

Themes: Identity, Existentialism, Social expectations, Love, Happiness
