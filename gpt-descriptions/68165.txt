Synopsis: In "Report of an autopsy on the bodies of Chang and Eng Bunker, commonly known as the Siamese twins," Allen Harrison delves into the fascinating lives and mysterious deaths of the famous conjoined twins. Through meticulous examination and analysis, the author uncovers shocking revelations that challenge the assumptions surrounding their unique condition. As the autopsy unfolds, secrets are unveiled, shedding light on the extraordinary bond between the twins and the impact it had on their lives. This gripping account offers a captivating exploration of the human body, identity, and the complexities of shared existence.

Genres: Non-fiction, Biography, Medical

Themes: Identity, Conjoined Twins, Human Anatomy, Individuality
