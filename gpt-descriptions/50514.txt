Synopsis: In 'Chronicles of Newgate, Vol. 2: From the eighteenth century to its demolition', Arthur Griffiths takes readers on a captivating journey through the infamous Newgate Prison, from its dark origins in the eighteenth century to its eventual demise. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Griffiths unveils the hidden stories of the prisoners, wardens, and the society that surrounded this notorious institution. From daring escapes to scandalous trials, this volume delves into the gripping tales of those who lived within Newgate's walls, shedding light on the harsh realities of life and justice in the past. As the prison's fate hangs in the balance, readers will be left on the edge of their seats, eager to uncover the secrets that lie within its crumbling walls.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Biography

Themes: Crime and punishment, Social justice, Historical preservation
