Synopsis: In "The Constitution of the United States: A Brief Study of the Genesis, Formulation and Political Philosophy of the Constitution," James M. Beck delves into the fascinating origins and development of the United States Constitution. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Beck explores the historical context, key debates, and influential figures that shaped this pivotal document. From the struggles of the Founding Fathers to the enduring principles that continue to guide the nation, this book offers a captivating exploration of the Constitution's significance and relevance in modern times.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Constitutional law, American history, Political philosophy
