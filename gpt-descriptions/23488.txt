Synopsis: 

In 'The Jacobite Rebellions (1689-1746): (Bell's Scottish History Source Books.)', author J. Pringle Thomson delves into the tumultuous period of Scottish history marked by the Jacobite rebellions. This gripping account takes readers on a journey through the political and military conflicts that shaped the destiny of Scotland and its people. From the failed attempts to restore the Stuart monarchy to the fierce battles fought on Scottish soil, Thomson's narrative brings to life the dramatic events and the individuals who played pivotal roles in this era of rebellion and resistance. 

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Rebellion, Politics, National Identity
