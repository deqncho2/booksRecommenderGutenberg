Synopsis: 

In "What Works: Schools Without Drugs," the United States Department of Education presents a compelling exploration of innovative strategies employed by schools to combat the pervasive issue of drug abuse among students. Through a collection of real-life case studies, this book delves into the inspiring stories of educators, administrators, and communities who have successfully created drug-free environments within their schools. From implementing comprehensive prevention programs to fostering strong relationships between students and staff, these schools have discovered effective methods to address the complex challenges of substance abuse. This enlightening work offers valuable insights and practical guidance for anyone seeking to create a safe and drug-free educational environment.

Genres: Non-fiction, Education, Self-help

Themes: Drug prevention, Education reform, Community engagement
