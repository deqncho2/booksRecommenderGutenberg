Synopsis: 
In "The popular superstitions and festive amusements of the Highlanders of Scotland," William Grant Stewart takes readers on a captivating journey into the mystical world of Scottish folklore and traditions. From ancient beliefs in fairies and witches to the vibrant celebrations that bring communities together, this book unveils the rich tapestry of superstitions and festive amusements that have shaped the Highlanders' way of life for centuries. Delving into the customs surrounding birth, marriage, and death, as well as the rituals performed during seasonal festivities, Stewart offers a fascinating glimpse into a world where myth and reality intertwine. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, readers will be transported to a land where ancient traditions still hold sway, and where the supernatural and the everyday coexist in a delicate balance.

Genres: Folklore, Cultural Studies, History

Themes: Superstition, Tradition, Community, Mythology
