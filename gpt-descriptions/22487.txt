Synopsis: 
'Journal of a Young Lady of Virginia, 1782' by Lucinda Lee Orr is a captivating historical novel that takes readers on a journey through the life of a young woman in Virginia during the tumultuous year of 1782. Through the pages of her journal, the protagonist unveils a world filled with secrets, forbidden love, and the struggle for independence. As she navigates the complexities of society, she discovers her own strength and resilience, challenging the norms of her time. With each entry, the reader becomes immersed in the vivid descriptions of the era, experiencing the joys and sorrows of a young lady's life in the midst of a changing world.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Romance
Themes: Independence, Love and Forbidden Desires, Societal Expectations
