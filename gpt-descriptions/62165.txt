Synopsis: 

'From Dixie to Canada: Romances and Realities of the Underground Railroad' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous era of the Underground Railroad. Homer Uri Johnson, a renowned historian, delves into the lives of courageous individuals who risked everything to escape slavery and find freedom in Canada. Through a series of captivating stories and real-life accounts, Johnson paints a vivid picture of the harrowing experiences faced by those seeking liberation, as well as the incredible acts of bravery and compassion exhibited by abolitionists along the way. From heart-wrenching escapes to unexpected alliances, this book offers a gripping exploration of the human spirit's resilience in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Adventure

Themes: Freedom and Liberation, Bravery and Resilience, Humanitarianism and Compassion
