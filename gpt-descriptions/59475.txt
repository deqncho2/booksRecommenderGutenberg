Synopsis: 
'Wild Animals of North America: Intimate Studies of Big and Little Creatures of the Mammal Kingdom' by Nelson, Edward William takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse wildlife of North America. From the majestic grizzly bear to the elusive mountain lion, and from the playful river otter to the tiny chipmunk, this book offers intimate and detailed studies of both big and little creatures that inhabit the continent. Through vivid descriptions and stunning photographs, Nelson brings these wild animals to life, revealing their behaviors, habitats, and the intricate web of life they are a part of. Whether you are an animal lover, a nature enthusiast, or simply curious about the fascinating world of North American mammals, this book will leave you in awe of the beauty and complexity of the animal kingdom.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Wildlife

Themes: Wildlife conservation, Biodiversity, Animal behavior, Ecosystems
