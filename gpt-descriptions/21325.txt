Synopsis: 

In 'Outlines of Greek and Roman Medicine', Sir James Elliott takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of ancient medical practices. From the temples of Asclepius to the renowned physicians of Rome, this book unveils the secrets of the healing arts that were developed centuries ago. Delving into the methods, beliefs, and discoveries of these ancient civilizations, Elliott paints a vivid picture of the medical practices that shaped the foundations of modern medicine. With intriguing anecdotes and historical accounts, this book offers a captivating exploration of the ancient medical world, shedding light on the remarkable achievements and enduring legacies of Greek and Roman medicine.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medicine

Themes: Ancient medicine, Medical practices, Historical exploration
