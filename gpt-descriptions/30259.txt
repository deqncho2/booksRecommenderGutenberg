Synopsis: In 'The Man Who Played to Lose' by Laurence M. Janifer, a brilliant but enigmatic chess player named Victor is invited to participate in a high-stakes international chess tournament. However, instead of playing to win, Victor deliberately makes moves that seem to lead to his own defeat. As the tournament progresses, the reasons behind Victor's unconventional strategy become increasingly mysterious, leaving the reader captivated by the mind games and psychological battles that unfold on and off the chessboard.

Genres: Mystery, Psychological Thriller, Suspense

Themes: Strategy, Deception, Identity, Psychological Warfare
