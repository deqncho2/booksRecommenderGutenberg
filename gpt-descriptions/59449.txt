Synopsis: 

In "History of the government of the island of Newfoundland," John Reeves takes readers on a captivating journey through the political and economic development of this remote island. From the early days of colonization to the establishment of a thriving fishing industry, Reeves delves into the intricate web of governance and trade that shaped the destiny of Newfoundland. With an appendix containing the Acts of Parliament related to trade and fishery, this comprehensive account offers a fascinating insight into the challenges and triumphs faced by the island's government and its people.

Genres: History, Politics, Economics

Themes: Colonialism, Governance, Trade, Fishery
