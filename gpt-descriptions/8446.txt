Synopsis: In 'The Enormous Room', E. E. Cummings takes us on a captivating journey through his own personal experience as a prisoner during World War I. Stripped of his freedom and thrown into a surreal and oppressive environment, Cummings encounters a diverse cast of characters who challenge his beliefs and reshape his perspective on life. Through his vivid and poetic prose, Cummings invites readers to explore the depths of human resilience, the power of friendship, and the indomitable spirit that can emerge even in the darkest of circumstances.

Genres: Autobiographical fiction, War literature, Modernist literature

Themes: Imprisonment, Freedom, Resilience, Friendship, Identity
