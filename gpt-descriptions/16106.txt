Synopsis: In "What Is Free Trade?: An Adaptation of Frederic Bastiat's "Sophismes Économiques" Designed for the American Reader," Bastiat, Frédéric presents a thought-provoking exploration of the concept of free trade. Through engaging anecdotes and clever reasoning, Bastiat challenges common misconceptions and reveals the true benefits of unrestricted commerce. This adaptation, tailored for the American reader, brings Bastiat's timeless ideas to a new audience, encouraging them to question prevailing economic fallacies and embrace the power of free trade.

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Political Philosophy

Themes: Free trade, Protectionism, Economic fallacies, Individual liberty, Government intervention
