Synopsis: 

In 'Migration of Birds (1979)', Lincoln and Peterson take readers on an awe-inspiring journey into the fascinating world of bird migration. Through vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, they explore the incredible feats of endurance and navigation that birds undertake during their annual migrations. From the Arctic tundra to the African savannah, readers will witness the remarkable spectacle of birds crossing vast distances, overcoming obstacles, and adapting to different environments. This captivating book offers a glimpse into the mysterious and captivating world of bird migration, revealing the astonishing abilities of these winged creatures.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Science

Themes: Migration, Animal Behavior, Natural Phenomena
