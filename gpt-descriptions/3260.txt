Synopsis: 
In "A Short History of Wales," Sir Owen Morgan Edwards takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and complex history of Wales. From the ancient Celts and Roman conquest to the struggles for independence and the modern era, Edwards weaves together a compelling narrative that brings this often overlooked nation to life. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, he explores the cultural, political, and social developments that have shaped Wales into the vibrant country it is today. From legendary figures like King Arthur to pivotal events such as the Welsh Revival, Edwards uncovers the fascinating stories and hidden gems that make Wales a truly unique and captivating place. 

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: National identity, Cultural heritage, Political struggles, Social development
