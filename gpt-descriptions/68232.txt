Synopsis: 

"The Story of the Thirty-Third N. Y. S. Vols: or Two Years Campaigning in Virginia and Maryland" by David Wright Judd is a captivating historical memoir that takes readers on a thrilling journey through the American Civil War. Through the eyes of the author, who served as a soldier in the Thirty-Third New York State Volunteers, the book vividly recounts the intense experiences, hardships, and triumphs of the regiment during their two years of campaigning in Virginia and Maryland. From the chaos of the battlefield to the camaraderie among comrades, Judd's narrative offers a gripping and personal account of the realities of war.

Genres: Historical memoir, Military history, American Civil War

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, Brotherhood and camaraderie in the face of adversity, The resilience and courage of soldiers
