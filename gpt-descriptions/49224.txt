Synopsis: In 'The Journals of Major-Gen. C. G. Gordon, C.B., at Kartoum,' readers are transported to the heart of 19th-century Sudan, where the enigmatic Major-General Charles George Gordon chronicles his experiences in the besieged city of Kartoum. Through his vivid and introspective journal entries, Gordon offers a captivating glimpse into the challenges, triumphs, and personal reflections of a man caught in the midst of a turbulent conflict. As the tension escalates and the fate of Kartoum hangs in the balance, readers will be enthralled by Gordon's unwavering determination and the complex web of political intrigue that surrounds him.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Memoir, Adventure

Themes: War and Conflict, Leadership, Personal Sacrifice
