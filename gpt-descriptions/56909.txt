Synopsis: 

In 'River and Canal Engineering, the characteristics of open flowing streams, and the principles and methods to be followed in dealing with them,' Edward Skelton Bellasis takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate world of river and canal engineering. With a keen eye for detail and a wealth of knowledge, Bellasis explores the unique characteristics of open flowing streams, unraveling the secrets that lie beneath their surface. From the mesmerizing flow patterns to the challenges posed by erosion and sedimentation, this book delves into the principles and methods that engineers must employ to effectively manage and manipulate these dynamic waterways. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, Environmental Science

Themes: River dynamics, Hydraulic engineering, Water management, Environmental sustainability
