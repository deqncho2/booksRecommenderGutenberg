Synopsis: 

In the sixth volume of "South Africa and the Transvaal War," author Louis Creswicke takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous period from the Occupation of Pretoria to Mr. Kruger's Departure from South Africa. This volume also provides a summarised account of the Guerilla War up until March 1901. As the conflict intensifies, Creswicke delves into the intricate details of the war, offering a comprehensive and riveting narrative that keeps readers on the edge of their seats. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the author paints a vivid picture of the events, the key players, and the strategies employed during this critical phase of the war. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War memoir

Themes: War and conflict, Colonialism, Leadership and strategy
