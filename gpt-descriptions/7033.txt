Synopsis: 

In 'Picturesque Quebec: a sequel to Quebec past and present', Sir James MacPherson Le Moine takes readers on a captivating journey through the enchanting landscapes and rich history of Quebec. Through vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, Le Moine paints a vivid picture of the city's architectural wonders, natural beauty, and cultural heritage. From the bustling streets of Old Quebec to the tranquil shores of the St. Lawrence River, this sequel delves deeper into the hidden gems and lesser-known aspects of Quebec's past and present. As readers explore the pages of this book, they will be transported to a world where history comes alive, and the beauty of Quebec unfolds before their eyes.

Genres: Travelogue, History, Art

Themes: Quebec's architectural heritage, Natural beauty, Cultural exploration
