Synopsis: 
Sentimental Education; Or, The History of a Young Man follows the life of Frédéric Moreau, a young man from a provincial town in France, as he navigates the complexities of love, ambition, and social class in 19th-century Paris. Frédéric becomes infatuated with a married woman, Madame Arnoux, and his pursuit of her becomes a central focus of his life. Along the way, he encounters various characters and experiences the disillusionment and disappointments that come with growing up and facing the realities of adulthood. Set against the backdrop of political upheaval and social change, this novel offers a poignant exploration of the human condition and the complexities of desire and ambition.

Genres: 
Realism, Bildungsroman, Historical Fiction

Themes: 
Love and Desire, Social Class, Ambition, Disillusionment, Political and Social Change
