Synopsis: In 'Royal Highness' by Thomas Mann, readers are transported to the secluded world of a European royal court, where the young and naive Prince Klaus Heinrich embarks on a journey of self-discovery and love. As he navigates the complexities of his royal duties, he finds himself torn between his duty to his country and his desire for personal happiness. With vivid descriptions and intricate character development, Mann explores the challenges faced by those born into privilege and the sacrifices they must make to maintain their position. 

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Duty and Responsibility, Love and Desire, Identity and Self-Discovery
