Synopsis: In 'Children's Rights: A Book of Nursery Logic', Kate Douglas Smith Wiggin takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of children's rights and the importance of nurturing their individuality. Through a series of heartwarming and thought-provoking stories, Wiggin explores the challenges faced by children and the need for adults to understand and respect their unique perspectives. From the power of imagination to the significance of play, this book offers a delightful exploration of the rights and needs of children, reminding readers of the profound impact they can have on shaping a child's future.

Genres: Children's Literature, Non-fiction, Education

Themes: Children's rights, Individuality, Imagination, Play, Parenting
