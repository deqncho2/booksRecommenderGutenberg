Synopsis: In 'The Slave Trade, Domestic and Foreign: Why It Exists, and How It May Be Extinguished', Henry Charles Carey delves into the dark and complex world of the slave trade. Through meticulous research and thought-provoking analysis, Carey explores the origins, motivations, and consequences of this abhorrent practice. From the domestic slave trade within the United States to the international trafficking of human beings, the author uncovers the intricate web of economic, social, and political factors that perpetuate this cruel institution. With a compelling narrative and a call to action, Carey offers insights into how the slave trade can be eradicated and humanity can triumph over this grave injustice.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Sciences

Themes: Slavery, Human rights, Economics, Social justice
