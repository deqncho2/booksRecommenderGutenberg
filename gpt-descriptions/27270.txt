Synopsis: In 'India's Problem, Krishna or Christ' by John P. Jones, a thought-provoking exploration of spirituality and identity unfolds as the author delves into the heart of India's religious landscape. Through vivid storytelling and personal experiences, Jones navigates the complex interplay between the ancient Hindu deity Krishna and the teachings of Jesus Christ, shedding light on the profound impact these two figures have had on Indian society. As he encounters diverse perspectives and engages in deep philosophical discussions, the author grapples with the question of whether India's spiritual future lies in embracing its rich cultural heritage or embracing a new path. This captivating narrative invites readers on a journey of self-discovery and challenges conventional notions of faith and belief.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious Studies, Cultural Studies

Themes: Spirituality, Identity, Religious Pluralism, Cultural Heritage
