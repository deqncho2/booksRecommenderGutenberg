Synopsis: In "Mary Tudor, Queen of France" by Mary Croom Brown, readers are transported to the captivating world of 16th-century Europe, where the young and spirited Mary Tudor finds herself thrust into a tumultuous marriage with the charismatic King Louis XII of France. As Mary navigates the treacherous waters of court politics and power struggles, she must also grapple with her own desires and ambitions. With vivid descriptions and rich historical detail, this gripping tale explores the complexities of love, loyalty, and the sacrifices one must make in the pursuit of power.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: Love and Desire, Power and Politics, Identity and Self-Discovery
