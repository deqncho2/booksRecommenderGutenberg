Synopsis: 

In "Charles Bradlaugh: a Record of His Life and Work, Volume 1," authors J.M. Robertson and Hypatia Bradlaugh Bonner delve into the captivating life of Charles Bradlaugh, a prominent figure in British politics and a champion of free thought. This seventh edition provides a comprehensive account of Bradlaugh's parliamentary struggle, his political endeavors, and his influential teachings. From his early years as a radical activist to his controversial battles in the House of Commons, this volume offers a fascinating exploration of Bradlaugh's life and the impact he had on society.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Free thought, Political activism, Parliamentary struggle
