Synopsis: In 'Catholic Churchmen in Science [First Series]', James J. Walsh delves into the fascinating lives of Catholic ecclesiastics who made significant contributions to the world of science. From astronomers to botanists, this book uncovers the hidden stories of these remarkable individuals who defied societal expectations and pursued their passion for knowledge. Through engaging sketches, Walsh sheds light on their struggles, achievements, and the impact they had on the scientific community. This captivating exploration of the intersection between faith and science will leave readers inspired and enlightened.

Genres: Biography, History, Science

Themes: Faith and reason, Scientific discovery, Overcoming societal barriers
