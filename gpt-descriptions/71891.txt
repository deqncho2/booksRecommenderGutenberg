Synopsis: 

In 'The Cambridge natural history, Vol. 02 (of 10)', authors Beddard, Hartog, Benham, Gamble, and Sheldon take readers on a captivating journey through the diverse and fascinating world of animals. From the smallest insects to the largest mammals, this volume delves into the intricate details of various species, their habitats, behaviors, and evolutionary adaptations. With vivid descriptions and stunning illustrations, the authors bring to life the wonders of the natural world, offering a comprehensive and engaging exploration of the animal kingdom.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Science

Themes: Biodiversity, Evolution, Animal Behavior, Ecology
