Synopsis: 
'Jataka Tales' by Ellen C. Babbitt is a collection of ancient Indian folk tales that have been passed down through generations. These captivating stories take readers on a journey through the lives of various animals, each with their own unique personalities and experiences. From wise elephants to mischievous monkeys, the tales explore themes of compassion, morality, and the consequences of one's actions. Through these enchanting narratives, readers are transported to a world where animals possess human-like qualities, teaching valuable lessons that are still relevant today.

Genres: 
Folklore, Fables, Children's Literature

Themes: 
Compassion, Morality, Consequences of Actions
