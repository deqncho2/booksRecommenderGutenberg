Synopsis: 
'Arrows of the Chace, vol. 2/2: being a collection of scattered letters published chiefly in the daily newspapers 1840-1880' by Ruskin, John is a captivating collection of letters that offers a unique glimpse into the social, cultural, and political landscape of the 19th century. Through these scattered letters, Ruskin takes readers on a thought-provoking journey, exploring various topics such as art, literature, architecture, and the impact of industrialization. Each letter is a window into the author's mind, revealing his keen observations, sharp wit, and profound insights. As readers delve into this collection, they will be enthralled by the breadth of Ruskin's knowledge and the eloquence of his writing, making this volume a must-read for anyone interested in the intellectual and cultural history of the Victorian era.

Genres: Non-fiction, Epistolary, Historical

Themes: Art and Aesthetics, Industrialization and its effects, Social and Cultural Commentary
