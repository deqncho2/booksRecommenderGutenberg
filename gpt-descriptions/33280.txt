Synopsis: 
In 'Civil War Experiences: under Bayard, Gregg, Kilpatrick, Custer, Raulston, and Newberry, 1862, 1863, 1864', Henry C. Meyer takes readers on a gripping journey through the American Civil War, recounting his personal experiences serving under various notable commanders. From the intense battles and harrowing skirmishes to the camaraderie and sacrifices made by soldiers, Meyer provides a vivid and firsthand account of the realities of war. Through his detailed narratives, readers will gain a deeper understanding of the courage, resilience, and human spirit that prevailed during this tumultuous period in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Military history
Themes: War, Courage, Camaraderie, Sacrifice
