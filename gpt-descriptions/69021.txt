Synopsis: In "Women and Economic Evolution: or, The Effects of Industrial Changes upon the Status of Women," Theresa Schmid McMahon explores the profound impact of industrialization on women's lives during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Through meticulous research and compelling analysis, McMahon delves into the various ways in which women's roles, opportunities, and challenges were transformed by the rapid economic changes of the time. From the rise of factory work to the emergence of new social movements, this thought-provoking book sheds light on the complex relationship between women and economic evolution, offering valuable insights into the struggles and triumphs of women in the face of societal transformation.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Women's Studies

Themes: Gender roles, Industrialization, Social change, Women's empowerment
