Synopsis: In 'Paris and the Parisians in 1835 (Vol. 2)', Frances Milton Trollope takes us on a captivating journey through the vibrant streets of Paris, immersing us in the lives of its diverse inhabitants. From the bustling markets to the opulent salons, Trollope paints a vivid picture of the city and its people, exploring the intricacies of their relationships, ambitions, and secrets. As we delve deeper into the lives of the Parisians, we witness the clash of social classes, the pursuit of love and power, and the struggle for identity in a rapidly changing society. With its richly detailed descriptions and compelling characters, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the heart of 19th-century Paris.

Genres: Historical fiction, Travelogue, Social commentary

Themes: Social class, Love and relationships, Identity, Cultural transformation
