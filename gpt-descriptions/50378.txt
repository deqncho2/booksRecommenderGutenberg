Synopsis: 
In 'Oxford poetry, 1919', readers are transported to the vibrant world of post-World War I Oxford, where a group of talented poets come together to redefine the boundaries of traditional verse. Through their evocative and thought-provoking poems, they explore themes of love, loss, identity, and the profound impact of war on the human spirit. As the poets navigate their personal struggles and aspirations, their words weave a tapestry of emotions that captivate and challenge readers, leaving them with a renewed appreciation for the power of poetry and its ability to shape the world.

Genres: Poetry, Historical Fiction

Themes: Love, Loss, Identity, War, Artistic Expression
