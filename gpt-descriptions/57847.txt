Synopsis: In 'The World's Illusion, Volume 2: Ruth', Wassermann takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Ruth, a young woman who finds herself torn between duty and desire. As she navigates the complexities of love, family, and societal expectations, Ruth's choices have far-reaching consequences that challenge her sense of self and force her to confront the illusions that surround her. With vivid prose and intricate character development, Wassermann weaves a tale of passion, sacrifice, and the search for personal truth that will leave readers eagerly turning the pages.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Romance

Themes: Love and Desire, Duty and Responsibility, Identity and Self-Discovery
