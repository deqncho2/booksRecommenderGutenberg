Synopsis: 

"The Loss of His Majesty's Frigate Anson: Which was Wrecked within Three Miles of Helston, December 28, 1807 ... Also, an Authentic Narrative of the Loss of the Sidney, Which Ran upon a Dangerous Rock or Shoal, May 20, 1808" is a gripping historical account that delves into the harrowing tales of two separate naval disasters. The book vividly recounts the tragic events surrounding the wreck of the Anson and the subsequent loss of the Sidney, both occurring in the early 19th century. Through detailed descriptions and firsthand narratives, the reader is transported to the treacherous waters where these calamities unfolded, experiencing the bravery, despair, and resilience of those involved. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, maritime history, disaster narrative

Themes: Perilous journeys, human resilience in the face of adversity, the power of nature, the impact of naval disasters on individuals and communities.
