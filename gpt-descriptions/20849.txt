Synopsis: In 'The Big Brother: A Story of Indian War' by George Cary Eggleston, readers are transported to the tumultuous era of the American Indian Wars. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the life of a young soldier named Ned, who finds himself caught between duty and compassion as he navigates the complexities of war and the clash of cultures. As Ned becomes entangled in the lives of the Native American tribes he encounters, he must confront his own beliefs and question the true meaning of loyalty. Filled with gripping action, emotional depth, and thought-provoking dilemmas, this captivating novel explores the bonds of brotherhood and the consequences of war.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, War

Themes: Loyalty, Cultural Conflict, Identity, Morality
