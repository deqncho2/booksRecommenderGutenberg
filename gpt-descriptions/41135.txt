Synopsis: In 'The Theory and Practice of Model Aeroplaning', Johnson, V. E. takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of model aeroplanes. From the exhilarating thrill of building and flying these miniature aircraft to the intricate techniques and principles behind their design, this book offers a comprehensive guide for both beginners and seasoned enthusiasts. With vivid descriptions and practical advice, Johnson explores the art of model aeroplaning, revealing the secrets to achieving soaring heights and executing breathtaking maneuvers. As readers delve into the pages, they will be inspired to take flight and experience the sheer joy of model aeroplaning themselves.

Genres: Non-fiction, Hobby, Technology

Themes: Passion for aviation, Skill development, Creative expression
