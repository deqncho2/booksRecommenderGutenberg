Synopsis: In "Abraham Lincoln's Cardinal Traits: A Study in Ethics, with an Epilogue Addressed to Theologians," Clark S. Beardslee delves into the remarkable character of one of America's most revered presidents. Through meticulous research and analysis, Beardslee explores the cardinal traits that defined Abraham Lincoln's ethical framework, shedding light on the principles that guided his actions and decisions. With an epilogue specifically aimed at theologians, this thought-provoking study offers a fresh perspective on Lincoln's moral compass and its relevance in today's world.

Genres: Biography, Ethics, History

Themes: Leadership, Morality, Ethical Decision-Making
