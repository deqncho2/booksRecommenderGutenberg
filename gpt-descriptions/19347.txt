Synopsis: In 'The Myths of the New World: A Treatise on the Symbolism and Mythology of the Red Race of America,' Daniel G. Brinton delves into the rich and captivating world of Native American mythology. Exploring the symbolism and folklore of various tribes, Brinton uncovers the hidden meanings behind their stories, rituals, and beliefs. From the creation myths to tales of heroic figures and supernatural beings, this treatise offers a fascinating glimpse into the spiritual and cultural heritage of the indigenous peoples of America.

Genres: Non-fiction, Anthropology, Mythology

Themes: Native American culture, Symbolism, Mythology, Spiritual beliefs
