Synopsis: 
"The De Coverley Papers, From 'The Spectator'" is a collection of essays written by Richard Steele, Joseph Addison, and Eustace Budgell. These papers revolve around the character Sir Roger de Coverley, a charming and eccentric country gentleman. Through a series of humorous and insightful anecdotes, the authors explore the adventures, quirks, and values of Sir Roger, providing a delightful glimpse into the life and manners of 18th-century England.

Genres: 
Satire, Essay, Fiction

Themes: 
Society and manners, Human nature, Tradition and modernity
