Synopsis: In "Jefferson and Hamilton: The Struggle for Democracy in America," author Claude G. Bowers delves into the intense rivalry between two of America's founding fathers, Thomas Jefferson and Alexander Hamilton. Set against the backdrop of the early years of the United States, this captivating narrative explores the clash of ideologies, personal ambitions, and political maneuverings that shaped the nation's future. As Jefferson and Hamilton navigate the complexities of power, their differing visions for America's future ignite a fierce battle that will leave a lasting impact on the country's democratic principles.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Democracy, Ideological conflict, Founding of America, Political rivalry
