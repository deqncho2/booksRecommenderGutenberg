Synopsis: In 'The Great Hunger' by Johan Bojer, we are introduced to Ola, a hardworking and ambitious farmer who dreams of owning his own land. Set in the harsh and unforgiving landscape of rural Norway, Ola's relentless pursuit of his goals leads him down a path of sacrifice, heartbreak, and ultimately, self-discovery. As he grapples with the challenges of poverty, societal expectations, and the complexities of human relationships, Ola's journey becomes a poignant exploration of the human spirit and the lengths one will go to in order to achieve their dreams.

Genres: Literary Fiction, Historical Fiction, Drama

Themes: Ambition, Poverty, Sacrifice, Human Spirit, Social Expectations
