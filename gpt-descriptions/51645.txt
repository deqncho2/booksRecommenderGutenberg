Synopsis: 

In 'Letters on Natural Magic; Addressed to Sir Walter Scott, Bart.', David Brewster takes readers on a captivating journey through the wonders of natural magic. Through a series of letters addressed to the renowned Sir Walter Scott, Brewster explores the fascinating world of optical illusions, mesmerizing experiments, and the secrets behind various magical tricks. From the enchanting art of shadow puppets to the mesmerizing effects of mirrors and lenses, Brewster unveils the hidden science behind these seemingly supernatural phenomena. As the letters unfold, readers are transported into a realm where science and magic intertwine, leaving them spellbound by the mysteries of the natural world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: Magic and illusion, Science and discovery
