Synopsis:
'Armenia and the War' by A. P. Hacobian is a gripping historical novel that takes readers on a journey through the tumultuous events of World War I and their impact on the Armenian people. Set against the backdrop of the Armenian Genocide, the story follows a group of individuals who find themselves caught in the crossfire of war and struggle to survive amidst unimaginable atrocities. As they navigate through the chaos and devastation, they are faced with difficult choices that test their loyalty, resilience, and humanity. This powerful narrative sheds light on a lesser-known chapter of history and explores the enduring strength of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres:
Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Cultural Fiction

Themes:
Survival, Identity, Loss, Resilience, Humanitarianism
