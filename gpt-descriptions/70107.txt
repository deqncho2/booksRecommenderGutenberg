Synopsis: In "The "free press" : Portrait of a Monopoly," Marion George takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of media conglomerates and their influence on society. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, George unveils the hidden truths behind the seemingly independent and unbiased news outlets. As the plot unfolds, readers are introduced to a cast of characters who navigate the intricate web of power, corruption, and manipulation within the media industry. With shocking revelations and thought-provoking insights, this book sheds light on the monopolistic control of information and its far-reaching consequences.

Genres: Non-fiction, Investigative journalism, Political analysis

Themes: Media monopoly, Corporate influence, Freedom of the press, Manipulation of information
