Synopsis: In "Saul of Tarsus: A Tale of the Early Christians" by Elizabeth Miller, readers are transported to the tumultuous world of ancient Rome, where a young and ambitious Saul finds himself on a path that will forever change his life. As a devout Pharisee, Saul is determined to eradicate the growing Christian movement, but a chance encounter with a mysterious figure on the road to Damascus sets him on a journey of self-discovery and redemption. Filled with political intrigue, religious fervor, and personal transformation, this captivating tale explores the inner struggles of a man torn between his duty and his newfound faith.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Religious Fiction, Drama

Themes: Faith and Conversion, Identity and Self-Discovery, Persecution and Tolerance
