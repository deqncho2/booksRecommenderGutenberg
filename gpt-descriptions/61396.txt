Synopsis: 
In "Why I Am a Vegetarian: An Address Delivered Before the Chicago Vegetarian Society," J. Howard Moore passionately explores the reasons behind his decision to adopt a vegetarian lifestyle. Through a thought-provoking address, Moore delves into the ethical, environmental, and health-related aspects of vegetarianism, challenging the reader to reconsider their own dietary choices. With compelling arguments and personal anecdotes, Moore sheds light on the profound impact that our food choices have on our bodies, the planet, and the lives of animals. This address serves as a powerful call to action, urging readers to reflect on their own values and consider the potential benefits of embracing a vegetarian lifestyle.

Genres: Non-fiction, Essay, Philosophy

Themes: Vegetarianism, Ethics, Environmentalism, Health, Animal Rights
