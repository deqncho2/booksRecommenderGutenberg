Synopsis: In 'Why Colored People in Philadelphia Are Excluded from the Street Cars', Benjamin P. Hunt delves into the deeply rooted discrimination faced by colored people in Philadelphia's streetcar system. Through meticulous research and personal anecdotes, Hunt uncovers the unjust practices that deny colored individuals their right to equal transportation. As he explores the historical context and legal battles surrounding this issue, the reader is drawn into a compelling narrative that sheds light on the struggles and resilience of a marginalized community.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Civil Rights

Themes: Discrimination, Civil Rights, Racial Equality
