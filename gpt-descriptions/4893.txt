Synopsis:
In "Life and Death of John of Barneveld, Advocate of Holland," John Lothrop Motley takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of the Thirty Years' War. Set in the years 1614-17, this historical work delves into the life of John of Barneveld, a prominent figure in Holland's political landscape. As the primary causes and movements of the war unfold, Motley skillfully weaves together a narrative that is both informative and gripping, shedding light on the complex web of alliances, power struggles, and religious conflicts that shaped this pivotal era. Through the lens of Barneveld's life, readers are transported to a time of political intrigue, religious tensions, and the relentless pursuit of power. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Power struggles, Religious conflicts, Political intrigue
