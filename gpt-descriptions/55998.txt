Synopsis: 

Step into the gritty streets of Victorian London as Mayhew, Henry takes you on a captivating journey through the lives of the city's most marginalized inhabitants. In this eye-opening volume, Mayhew delves deep into the world of London's laboring poor, uncovering their struggles, triumphs, and the hidden stories that lie beneath the surface. From street vendors and beggars to prostitutes and thieves, Mayhew's vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts paint a vivid picture of a society teetering on the edge of destitution. As you navigate the bustling streets and shadowy alleyways, you'll encounter a cast of unforgettable characters, each with their own unique tale to tell. Prepare to be both moved and shocked as Mayhew exposes the harsh realities of poverty and the indomitable spirit of those who call London home.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Urban life, Survival
