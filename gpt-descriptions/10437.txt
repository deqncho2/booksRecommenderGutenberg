Synopsis: In 'Pulpit and Press (6th Edition)', Mary Baker Eddy takes readers on a captivating journey through the power struggles between religious leaders and the media. Set in a time when the press held immense influence over public opinion, Eddy delves into the complex relationship between the pulpit and the press, exploring the clash of ideologies and the battle for truth. As the story unfolds, secrets are revealed, alliances are formed, and unexpected twists keep readers on the edge of their seats. This thought-provoking narrative sheds light on the timeless struggle between faith and skepticism, and the impact of media on shaping public perception.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Drama

Themes: Power struggles, Truth and deception, Influence of media, Clash of ideologies
