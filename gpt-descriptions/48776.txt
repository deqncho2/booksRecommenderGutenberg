Synopsis: 
In "Petrarch, the First Modern Scholar and Man of Letters," we delve into the captivating correspondence between Francesco Petrarca and his esteemed friend, Giovanni Boccaccio, along with other influential figures of the time. This selection of letters offers a fascinating glimpse into the beginnings of the Renaissance, as Petrarca and his companions discuss literature, philosophy, and the pursuit of knowledge. Through their intellectual exchanges, we witness the birth of a new era, where the power of human thought and the revival of classical learning shape the course of history.

Genres: 
Biography, Epistolary, Historical

Themes: 
Renaissance, Humanism, Intellectual Exchange
