Synopsis: 

In the Transactions of the American Society of Civil Engineers, vol. LXVIII, Sept. 1910, Start/End Papers: The New York Tunnel Extension of the Pennsylvania Railroad, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the groundbreaking construction of the New York Tunnel Extension. This remarkable engineering feat, undertaken by the Pennsylvania Railroad, unveils the challenges, triumphs, and innovations that shaped the creation of this monumental transportation infrastructure. From the intricate planning and design to the tireless efforts of the engineers and workers involved, this account offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of civil engineering at the turn of the 20th century.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, History

Themes: Innovation, Infrastructure development, Engineering challenges, Industrial progress
