Synopsis: In Hildreth's "Japan as It Was and Is": A Handbook of Old Japan, Volume 2 (of 2), readers are transported to the captivating world of ancient Japan. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Hildreth paints a rich tapestry of the country's history, culture, and traditions. From the bustling streets of Edo to the serene beauty of Kyoto, the author takes us on a journey that unveils the hidden gems and lesser-known aspects of this fascinating land. As we delve deeper into the pages, we encounter samurais, geishas, and emperors, each with their own captivating stories. With a keen eye for detail and a genuine appreciation for the Japanese way of life, Hildreth's work offers a unique and immersive experience that will leave readers yearning to explore the wonders of old Japan.

Genres: Travelogue, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Japanese culture, History, Exploration
