Synopsis: In "The Campaign of 1776 around New York and Brooklyn," Henry Phelps Johnston presents a captivating and detailed account of the pivotal events that unfolded during the American Revolutionary War. Focusing on the battle of Long Island and the subsequent loss of New York, Johnston offers a fresh perspective and unveils previously untold stories. With meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the author takes readers on a thrilling journey through the challenges, triumphs, and sacrifices of the soldiers and leaders involved in this critical campaign.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American Revolution

Themes: War and conflict, Leadership and strategy, Patriotism, Historical significance
