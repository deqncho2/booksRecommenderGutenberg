Synopsis: Step back in time and immerse yourself in the vibrant world of ancient Rome during the days of Cicero, one of history's most influential figures. In this captivating work, Alfred John Church takes us on a journey through the bustling streets, opulent villas, and political arenas of Rome, offering a vivid portrayal of the daily life, customs, and struggles of its citizens. From the grandeur of the Roman Forum to the treacherous intrigues of the Senate, Church's meticulous attention to detail brings this fascinating era to life, allowing readers to witness firsthand the triumphs and tribulations of the Roman people. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Ancient Rome, Politics, Society, Culture, Power
