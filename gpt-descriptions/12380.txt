Synopsis: 
In "Two Thousand Miles on an Automobile: Being a Desultory Narrative of a Trip Through New England, New York, Canada, and the West, By "Chauffeur,"" Arthur Jerome Eddy takes readers on an exhilarating journey across various landscapes and cultures. Through the eyes of the "Chauffeur," we embark on a thrilling adventure filled with unexpected encounters, breathtaking scenery, and humorous anecdotes. As the miles pass, the narrative unfolds, revealing the wonders and challenges of traveling by automobile in the early 20th century. From bustling cities to serene countryside, from encounters with locals to encounters with mechanical mishaps, this travelogue offers a captivating glimpse into a bygone era of exploration and discovery.

Genres: Travelogue, Adventure, Memoir
Themes: Exploration, Cultural diversity, Human resilience
