Synopsis: 
In "Henry Dunbar: A Novel," the renowned author M. E. Braddon takes readers on a thrilling journey through the complex world of family dynamics, identity, and the pursuit of justice. The story revolves around the sudden disappearance of Henry Dunbar, a wealthy and influential businessman, who is believed to have perished at sea. However, as the plot unfolds, it becomes clear that there is more to his disappearance than meets the eye. With a web of deceit, betrayal, and unexpected alliances, the novel delves into the dark secrets and hidden agendas of those closest to Henry Dunbar. As the truth slowly unravels, readers are left questioning the nature of loyalty and the lengths one will go to protect their own interests.

Genres: Mystery, Thriller, Suspense

Themes: Family dynamics, Identity, Justice
