Synopsis: 
'Letters of the Late Ignatius Sancho, an African: To Which Are Prefixed, Memoirs of His Life' is a collection of letters written by Ignatius Sancho, an African man who was born into slavery but later gained his freedom. These letters, addressed to various influential figures of his time, provide a unique perspective on the issues of race, identity, and social inequality. Through his eloquent and thought-provoking words, Sancho shares his experiences, challenges societal norms, and advocates for the rights and dignity of all individuals. This collection offers a powerful glimpse into the life and mind of a remarkable man who defied the odds and used his voice to inspire change.

Genres: 
Autobiography, Epistolary, Historical

Themes: 
Race, Identity, Social Inequality
