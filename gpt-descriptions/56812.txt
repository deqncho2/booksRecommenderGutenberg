Synopsis: In 'A History of the Peninsular War, Vol. 4, Dec. 1810-Dec. 1811: Massena's Retreat, Fuentes de Oñoro, Albuera, Tarragona', Charles Oman delves into the tumultuous period of the Peninsular War, focusing on the events from December 1810 to December 1811. This volume explores the dramatic retreat of French Marshal Massena, the intense battles at Fuentes de Oñoro, Albuera, and Tarragona, and the strategic implications of these conflicts. Oman's meticulous research and vivid storytelling bring to life the bravery, tactics, and sacrifices of both the French and British forces, providing a captivating account of this pivotal phase of the war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War narrative

Themes: War strategy, Leadership, Bravery, National identity, Consequences of conflict
