Synopsis: 

In 'Climatic Changes: Their Nature and Causes', Huntington and Visher take readers on a captivating journey through the intricate web of climatic changes that have shaped our planet over time. From the mysterious forces that drive these changes to the profound impact they have on ecosystems and human societies, this book unravels the enigma of climate transformation with scientific rigor and compelling storytelling. As the authors delve into the fascinating interplay between natural phenomena and human activities, readers will be left in awe of the immense power and fragility of our planet's climate system. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Environmental Studies

Themes: Climate change, Natural phenomena, Human impact, Environmental resilience
