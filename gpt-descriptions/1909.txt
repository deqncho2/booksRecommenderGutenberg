Synopsis: In "Darwin and Modern Science," A. C. Seward delves into the fascinating world of Charles Darwin's groundbreaking theories and their impact on modern science. Through a captivating exploration of Darwin's life, scientific discoveries, and the controversies surrounding his ideas, Seward offers a compelling narrative that sheds light on the profound influence Darwin's work continues to have on our understanding of the natural world. From the Galapagos Islands to the intricacies of natural selection, this book takes readers on a thought-provoking journey that challenges conventional wisdom and invites us to reconsider our place in the grand tapestry of life.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Biography

Themes: Evolution, Natural selection, Scientific discovery, Controversy, Impact of ideas
