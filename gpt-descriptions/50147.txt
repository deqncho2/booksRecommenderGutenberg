Synopsis: 
In 'Beaver: An Alphabet of Typical Specimens: Together with Notes and a Terminal Essay on the Manners and Customs of Beavering Men', author John Kettelwell takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of beavers. Through an alphabetically organized collection of typical specimens, Kettelwell provides insightful notes and observations that shed light on the habits, behaviors, and peculiarities of these industrious creatures. From their intricate dam-building skills to their social dynamics, readers will discover the hidden intricacies of beaver life. The terminal essay delves deeper into the cultural significance of beavers and their impact on human society. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Animal Studies
Themes: Animal behavior, Environmentalism, Human-animal relationships
