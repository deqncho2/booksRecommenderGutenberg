Synopsis: In "Children of Loneliness" by Anzia Yezierska, we are introduced to the vibrant and determined protagonist, Sonya Mendel. Sonya, a young Jewish immigrant living in the bustling Lower East Side of New York City, dreams of breaking free from the confines of her traditional upbringing and finding her own path in life. As she navigates the challenges of assimilation, poverty, and the expectations of her family, Sonya's journey takes her on a quest for self-discovery and independence. Will she be able to overcome the obstacles that stand in her way and forge her own destiny?

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Immigrant Literature

Themes: Identity, Assimilation, Family Expectations, Poverty, Self-Discovery
