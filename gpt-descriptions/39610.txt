Synopsis:
In 'Riding for Ladies' by W. A. Kerr, a spirited young woman named Emily embarks on a thrilling adventure that challenges societal norms and pushes the boundaries of what it means to be a lady in the late 19th century. Determined to prove her equestrian skills, she defies expectations and enters a prestigious horse race, facing both physical and emotional obstacles along the way. As Emily navigates the male-dominated world of horse racing, she discovers her own strength and resilience, inspiring those around her and leaving a lasting impact on the sport.

Genres:
Adventure, Historical Fiction, Women's Literature

Themes:
Gender roles, Independence, Perseverance
