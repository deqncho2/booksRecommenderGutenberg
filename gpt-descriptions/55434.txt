Synopsis: In 'Early Man in the New World', Hester and Macgowan take readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the fascinating history of the first humans to inhabit the Americas. From the discovery of ancient artifacts to the unraveling of mysterious archaeological sites, this book unveils the secrets of our early ancestors and their remarkable survival in a new and unfamiliar land. As the authors delve into the rich tapestry of human migration and adaptation, readers will be enthralled by the gripping tales of exploration, resilience, and the enduring spirit of early man.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Archaeology

Themes: Human migration, Adaptation, Survival
