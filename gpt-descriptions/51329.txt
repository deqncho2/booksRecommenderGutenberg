Synopsis: 

In 'Turkey; the Awakening of Turkey; the Turkish Revolution of 1908', E. F. Knight takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events that unfolded during the Turkish Revolution of 1908. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, Knight paints a vivid picture of the political and social upheaval that gripped the nation, as the Ottoman Empire struggled to adapt to the winds of change.

From the streets of Istanbul to the battlefields of Anatolia, Knight introduces readers to a diverse cast of characters, each with their own motivations and aspirations. As the revolution gains momentum, alliances are formed, loyalties are tested, and the fate of an empire hangs in the balance. Amidst the chaos, Knight explores the profound impact of the revolution on the lives of ordinary Turkish citizens, as they grapple with newfound freedoms and the challenges of forging a new national identity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Biography

Themes: Revolution, Nationalism, Identity, Political upheaval, Social change
