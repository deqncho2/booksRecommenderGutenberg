Synopsis: 

In 'The Works of George Berkeley. Vol. 1 of 4: Philosophical Works, 1705-21', the renowned philosopher George Berkeley presents a collection of his groundbreaking philosophical works from the early years of his career. This volume delves into Berkeley's exploration of the nature of reality, the existence of God, and the limits of human knowledge. Through a series of thought-provoking essays and treatises, Berkeley challenges traditional philosophical notions and invites readers to question their own perceptions and beliefs. With his eloquent prose and logical reasoning, Berkeley takes readers on a captivating intellectual journey that challenges the very foundations of our understanding of the world.

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Perception, Idealism, Existence of God, Empiricism
