Synopsis: In 'The World of Chance' by William Dean Howells, a young man named Silas Lapham finds himself caught between the allure of wealth and the importance of personal integrity. As he navigates the treacherous waters of high society, Silas must confront the consequences of his choices and grapple with the true meaning of success. Set against the backdrop of late 19th-century America, this captivating novel explores the complexities of ambition, morality, and the unpredictable nature of fate.

Genres: Realistic fiction, Satire, Social commentary

Themes: Wealth and materialism, Morality and ethics, Social class and status
