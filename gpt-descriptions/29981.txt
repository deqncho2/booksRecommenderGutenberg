Synopsis: In 'The Truth About Woman' by Hartley, C. Gasquoine, a young woman named Evelyn finds herself caught between societal expectations and her own desires. As she navigates the complexities of love, marriage, and independence, she discovers shocking truths about the world she thought she knew. With secrets, scandals, and unexpected twists, this captivating novel explores the power dynamics between men and women in early 20th century England.

Genres: Historical fiction, romance, drama

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, love and desire
