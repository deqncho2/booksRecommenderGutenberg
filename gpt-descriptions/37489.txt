Synopsis: In "An Historical Narrative of the Great and Terrible Fire of London, Sept. 2nd 1666," Gideon Harvey takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most devastating events in London's history. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Harvey paints a vivid picture of the chaos, destruction, and resilience that unfolded during the catastrophic fire. As the flames engulf the city, readers will follow the lives of various individuals, from the wealthy to the impoverished, as they navigate the treacherous streets and struggle to survive. With its blend of historical accuracy and compelling storytelling, this narrative offers a captivating glimpse into a pivotal moment in London's past.

Genres: Historical fiction, disaster fiction, historical non-fiction

Themes: Resilience, community, survival, historical events
