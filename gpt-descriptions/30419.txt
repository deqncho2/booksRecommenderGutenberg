Synopsis: 
In "How to Form a Library, 2nd ed," Henry B. Wheatley takes readers on a captivating journey through the art of building a personal library. With a wealth of knowledge and practical advice, Wheatley guides readers through the intricacies of selecting, organizing, and expanding their collection. From the importance of understanding one's own reading preferences to the art of acquiring rare and valuable books, this comprehensive guide offers invaluable insights for both novice and seasoned bibliophiles. Filled with fascinating anecdotes and historical references, Wheatley's work is a treasure trove for anyone seeking to create their own literary haven.

Genres: Non-fiction, Reference, Bibliography

Themes: Book collecting, Personal libraries, Literary history
