Synopsis: In 'The Book of Were-Wolves', S. Baring-Gould delves into the mysterious world of werewolves, exploring their origins, legends, and the chilling accounts of encounters with these shape-shifting creatures. From ancient folklore to modern-day sightings, this captivating book takes readers on a thrilling journey through the history and mythology surrounding werewolves, leaving them questioning the boundaries between reality and myth.

Genres: Non-fiction, Folklore, Supernatural

Themes: Mythology, Transformation, Fear of the Unknown
