Synopsis: In 'Are Parents People?' by Alice Duer Miller, a spirited young woman named Anne discovers that her parents are not the infallible figures she once believed them to be. As she navigates the complexities of love, marriage, and societal expectations, Anne begins to question the traditional roles and expectations placed upon parents. With wit and humor, Miller explores the challenges and contradictions of parenthood, challenging the notion that parents are always right and infallible. 

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Comedy

Themes: Parenthood, Love, Marriage, Gender Roles
