Synopsis: 

In the second volume of "Memoirs of the Court of Marie Antoinette," Madam Campan, the First Lady in Waiting to the Queen, continues her captivating account of life within the opulent and tumultuous court of Versailles. As the French Revolution looms on the horizon, Campan offers a unique perspective on the inner workings of the royal court, revealing the intricate web of politics, power, and personal relationships that shaped the destiny of the ill-fated queen. From the lavish balls and extravagant festivities to the whispered secrets and whispered conspiracies, Campan's memoirs provide a fascinating glimpse into the life of one of history's most iconic queens.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Biography

Themes: Royal court life, French Revolution, Power and politics, Personal relationships
