Synopsis: 

In 'The Valuation of Public Service Corporation Property: Transactions of the American Society of Civil Engineers', Henry Earle Riggs delves into the intricate world of valuing public service corporation property. Through a comprehensive analysis of various case studies and methodologies, Riggs explores the challenges and complexities faced by civil engineers in determining the worth of these essential assets. From the evaluation of physical infrastructure to the assessment of intangible factors, this work offers valuable insights into the valuation process and its implications for public service corporations.

Genres: Non-fiction, Engineering, Economics

Themes: Valuation, Public Service, Infrastructure, Economic Analysis
