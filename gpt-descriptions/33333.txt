Synopsis: 
In the midst of the 18th century, Great Britain finds itself at a crossroads between tradition and progress. The Statute of Anne, a groundbreaking legislation, emerges as a catalyst for change, challenging the established norms and paving the way for a new era of intellectual property rights. As the battle between creators and publishers unfolds, a gripping tale of power, innovation, and the pursuit of artistic freedom takes center stage. With unexpected twists and turns, this historical account sheds light on the birth of copyright law and its profound impact on the world of literature.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Legal Drama, Political Thriller

Themes: Intellectual Property, Innovation, Power Struggles, Artistic Freedom
