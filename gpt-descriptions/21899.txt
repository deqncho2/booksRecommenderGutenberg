Synopsis: In 'A Rip Van Winkle of the Kalahari, and Other Tales of South-West Africa', Fred C. Cornell takes readers on a captivating journey through the vast and mysterious landscapes of South-West Africa. From the enchanting tale of a man who mysteriously falls into a deep sleep and wakes up to a world transformed, to the gripping accounts of encounters with dangerous wildlife and the struggles of survival in the harsh desert, this collection of stories offers a unique glimpse into the captivating beauty and challenges of life in the Kalahari. With vivid descriptions and unexpected twists, Cornell's narratives will leave readers eagerly turning the pages, eager to uncover the secrets and wonders that lie within the heart of South-West Africa.

Genres: Adventure, Travel, Non-fiction

Themes: Exploration, Survival, Transformation
