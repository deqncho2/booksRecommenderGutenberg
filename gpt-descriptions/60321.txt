Synopsis: 

In "Prisoners of War in Britain 1756 to 1815: A record of their lives, their romance and their sufferings" by Abell, Francis, readers are transported to a tumultuous period in British history. This captivating non-fiction work delves into the lives of prisoners of war during the years of 1756 to 1815, shedding light on their experiences, relationships, and the hardships they endured. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Abell paints a vivid picture of the struggles faced by these individuals, exploring their resilience, their hopes, and the unexpected romances that blossomed amidst the chaos of war.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Biography

Themes: Resilience, Love and Romance, Human Spirit, War and Conflict
