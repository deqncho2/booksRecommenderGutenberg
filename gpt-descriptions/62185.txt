Synopsis: In 'An Address to Lord Teignmouth', Thomas Sikes responds to the president of the British and Foreign Bible Society's address to the clergy of the Church of England. Sikes presents a thought-provoking and impassioned argument that challenges the ideas put forth by Lord Teignmouth, leading to a compelling exploration of the role of the Bible in society and the implications for the Church. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious, Criticism

Themes: Religion, Authority, Interpretation, Society
