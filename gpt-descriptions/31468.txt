Synopsis: In "The Battle of Franklin, Tennessee, November 30, 1864," author John K. Shellenberger presents a compelling analysis of the events leading up to the battle, focusing on the erroneous claims made by General Schofield and the blunder that ultimately opened the battle. Through meticulous research and insightful commentary, Shellenberger sheds light on the strategic mistakes and miscommunications that shaped this pivotal moment in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history

Themes: War strategy, Leadership, Historical accuracy
