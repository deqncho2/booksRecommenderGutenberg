Synopsis: In "Eneas Africanus," Harry Stillwell Edwards takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Eneas, a young African American boy born into slavery. From his humble beginnings on a plantation in Georgia to his eventual escape and journey to freedom, Eneas faces numerous challenges and encounters a diverse cast of characters along the way. As he navigates the complexities of race, identity, and love, Eneas's story unfolds with both heart-wrenching moments and inspiring triumphs, leaving readers eager to discover the outcome of his remarkable journey.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Adventure

Themes: Slavery, Freedom, Identity, Love, Resilience
