Synopsis: In Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein; Or, The Modern Prometheus," a young scientist named Victor Frankenstein becomes obsessed with the idea of creating life. Through his experiments, he succeeds in bringing a creature to life, but soon realizes the consequences of his actions. As the creature struggles to find acceptance and understanding in a world that rejects him, Victor is haunted by the consequences of his creation. This gripping tale explores the boundaries of science, the pursuit of knowledge, and the moral responsibility that comes with playing god.

Genres: Gothic fiction, science fiction, horror

Themes: The dangers of unchecked ambition, the consequences of isolation and rejection, the ethics of scientific exploration, the nature of humanity and identity
