Synopsis: In 'The Novel; what it is', Crawford takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of literature, exploring the power and impact of the novel. Through insightful analysis and engaging anecdotes, he delves into the origins of the novel, its evolution, and its significance in society. From the works of classic authors to contemporary masterpieces, Crawford unravels the magic behind storytelling, revealing the profound ways in which novels shape our lives and challenge our perspectives. This thought-provoking exploration will leave readers with a newfound appreciation for the art of the novel.

Genres: Literary criticism, Non-fiction

Themes: The power of storytelling, Evolution of literature, Impact of novels on society
