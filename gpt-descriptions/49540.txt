Synopsis: 

'Days to Remember: The British Empire in the Great War' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years of World War I, as seen through the eyes of two renowned authors, Buchan and Newbolt. This gripping account delves into the heart of the British Empire's involvement in the war, exploring the bravery, sacrifice, and resilience of its soldiers and citizens. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the authors paint a vivid picture of the war's impact on individuals and nations, offering a unique perspective on one of the most significant events in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War literature, Biography

Themes: Patriotism, Heroism, Sacrifice, National identity, War's impact on society
