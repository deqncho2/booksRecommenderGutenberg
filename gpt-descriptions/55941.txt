Synopsis: 
In "Beethoven" by Romain Rolland, the reader is transported into the life of one of the greatest composers of all time. This captivating biography delves into the complex and tumultuous world of Ludwig van Beethoven, exploring his struggles, triumphs, and the profound impact his music had on the world. From his early years as a prodigious talent to his battle with deafness and his relentless pursuit of artistic perfection, this book offers a vivid portrayal of Beethoven's genius and the profound emotions that fueled his compositions. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Rolland paints a vivid portrait of a man whose music continues to resonate with audiences centuries later.

Genres: Biography, Music, Historical

Themes: Artistic genius, Perseverance, Human emotions, Deafness, Legacy
