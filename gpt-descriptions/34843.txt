Synopsis: 

"The Boys of '61: or, Four Years of Fighting, Personal Observations with the Army and Navy" by Charles Carleton Coffin is a captivating historical account that takes readers on a journey through the American Civil War. Through the eyes of the author, who served as a war correspondent, this book provides a vivid and intimate portrayal of the soldiers and sailors who fought during this tumultuous period in American history. From the battlefields to the naval engagements, Coffin's personal observations offer a unique perspective on the bravery, sacrifice, and camaraderie of those who served.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War memoir

Themes: Patriotism, Courage, Brotherhood, Sacrifice
