Synopsis: In 'The Blight of Respectability: An Anatomy of the Disease and a Theory of Curative Treatment' by Geoffrey Mortimer, a renowned psychologist delves into the dark underbelly of society's obsession with respectability. Through a series of captivating case studies and thought-provoking analysis, Mortimer uncovers the hidden consequences of this societal pressure and proposes a groundbreaking theory for its cure. As the reader is taken on a journey through the complexities of human behavior and the destructive power of societal expectations, they are left questioning their own notions of respectability and the true cost of conformity.

Genres: Psychology, Sociology, Self-help

Themes: Societal pressure, Conformity, Individuality, Mental health
