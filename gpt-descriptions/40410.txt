Synopsis: 

In 'Dickens and His Illustrators: Cruikshank, Seymour, Buss, "Phiz," Cattermole, Leech, Doyle, Stanfield, Maclise, Tenniel, Frank Stone, Landseer, Palmer, Topham, Marcus Stone, and Luke Fildes; 2nd. Ed.', Frederic George Kitton delves into the fascinating world of Charles Dickens and the talented artists who brought his iconic characters to life. From the whimsical illustrations of George Cruikshank to the detailed portrayals of John Tenniel, this book explores the collaborative relationship between Dickens and his illustrators, shedding light on the creative process behind some of the most beloved literary works of all time.

Genres: Biography, Art, Literary Criticism

Themes: Collaboration, Visual storytelling, Literary influence
