Synopsis: In "The Mule-Bone: A Comedy of Negro Life in Three Acts," Zora Neale Hurston and Langston Hughes take readers on a hilarious journey through the lives of two friends in a small Southern town. When a dispute over a mule-bone arises, it sets off a chain of events that tests the limits of friendship, love, and community. As the townspeople get involved, secrets are revealed, and the true nature of human relationships is explored. With witty dialogue, colorful characters, and unexpected twists, this comedic play will keep readers entertained from start to finish.

Genres: Comedy, Drama, African-American Literature

Themes: Friendship, Love, Community, Identity, Conflict
