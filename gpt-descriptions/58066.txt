Synopsis: In 'The Silversmith in Eighteenth-Century Williamsburg: An Account of His Life & Times, & of His Craft' by Ford, Thomas K., readers are transported to the bustling streets of Williamsburg in the 18th century, where they follow the captivating journey of a skilled silversmith. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, the author delves into the intricacies of the silversmith's trade, the challenges he faces, and the vibrant society he navigates. As the silversmith's story unfolds, readers are immersed in a world of craftsmanship, ambition, and the pursuit of artistic excellence. This historical account offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of a silversmith and the vibrant tapestry of 18th-century Williamsburg.

Genres: Historical Non-Fiction, Biography, Craftsmanship

Themes: Artistry, Ambition, Historical Society
