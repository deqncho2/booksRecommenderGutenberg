Synopsis: 
The Correspondence of Thomas Carlyle and Ralph Waldo Emerson, 1834-1872, Vol. I offers a captivating glimpse into the intellectual and personal relationship between two literary giants of the 19th century. Through a collection of letters exchanged over nearly four decades, readers are transported into the minds and lives of Thomas Carlyle and Ralph Waldo Emerson, witnessing their deep friendship, shared ideas, and profound influence on each other's work. From discussions on philosophy and literature to reflections on society and politics, this volume presents an intimate and thought-provoking exchange between two brilliant minds that shaped the literary landscape of their time.

Genres: 
Biography, Correspondence, Literary Criticism

Themes: 
Friendship, Intellectual Exchange, Literary Influence
