Synopsis: 
In 'Second Treatise of Government', John Locke presents a thought-provoking exploration of the origins and nature of political power. Through a captivating analysis of human nature, property rights, and the social contract, Locke challenges conventional notions of authority and advocates for the fundamental rights of individuals. With eloquent arguments and compelling examples, he delves into the delicate balance between government and the governed, ultimately offering a profound vision of a just and equitable society.

Genres: Political philosophy, Social commentary

Themes: Natural rights, Social contract, Property rights, Limited government
