Synopsis: In 'The Lion's Brood' by Osborne, Duffield, a young prince finds himself torn between duty and desire as he navigates the treacherous world of politics and power. With his father's legacy hanging in the balance, he must make difficult choices that will shape the destiny of his kingdom. As secrets unravel and alliances are tested, the prince must confront his own inner demons and discover the true meaning of courage and sacrifice.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Drama, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Power and Responsibility, Loyalty and Betrayal, Identity and Self-Discovery
