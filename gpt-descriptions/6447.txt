Synopsis: In "Proserpine and Midas," Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley weaves together two captivating tales that explore the depths of human desires and the consequences they entail. In the first story, "Proserpine," a young woman finds herself torn between the allure of the underworld and the constraints of the mortal world. In the second story, "Midas," a king is granted a wish that brings him unimaginable wealth, but at a great cost. These enthralling narratives delve into the complexities of power, temptation, and the pursuit of happiness, leaving readers spellbound until the very end.

Genres: Gothic fiction, fantasy, mythology

Themes: Temptation, power, desire, consequences
