Synopsis: 
"The Oxford Methodists: Memoirs of the Rev. Messrs. Clayton, Ingham, Gambold, Hervey, and Broughton, with biographical notices of others" delves into the lives and experiences of influential figures in the Methodist movement during the 18th century. Through the captivating narratives of these individuals, the book explores their spiritual journeys, struggles, and the impact they had on the religious landscape of their time. From their time at Oxford University to their subsequent roles as preachers and leaders, the memoirs provide a fascinating insight into the lives of these remarkable individuals.

Genres: Biography, History, Religious Studies

Themes: Religious revival, Spiritual transformation, Influence of individuals in religious movements
