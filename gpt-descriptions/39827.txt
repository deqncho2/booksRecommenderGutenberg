Synopsis: In 'The Conduct of Life', Emerson explores the complexities of human behavior and the choices we make in our daily lives. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, he delves into the realms of love, power, fate, and self-reliance, offering profound insights into the nature of human conduct. From examining the struggles of individuals in society to contemplating the pursuit of personal happiness, Emerson's words challenge readers to reflect on their own actions and the impact they have on the world around them.

Genres: Essay, Philosophy, Self-help

Themes: Human behavior, Morality, Individualism, Self-reliance, Society
