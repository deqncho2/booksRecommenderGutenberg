Synopsis: In 'Young Swaigder; or, The Force of Runes, and Other Ballads' by George Borrow, readers are transported to a world of enchantment and mystery. This collection of ballads takes us on a journey through ancient landscapes, where supernatural forces and mythical creatures intertwine with the lives of ordinary people. From the haunting tale of a young man's encounter with a ghostly apparition to the captivating story of a powerful rune that holds the key to unlocking hidden secrets, each ballad is a captivating exploration of the human spirit and the enduring power of folklore. As the characters grapple with love, loss, and the unknown, Borrow weaves a tapestry of lyrical storytelling that will leave readers spellbound.

Genres: Poetry, Folklore, Fantasy

Themes: Supernatural, Mythology, Love and Loss
