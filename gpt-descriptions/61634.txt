Synopsis: 
In 'Fors Clavigera (Volume 3 of 8): Letters to the workmen and labourers of Great Britain,' John Ruskin, a renowned Victorian art critic and social thinker, addresses the working class directly, offering them a powerful and thought-provoking series of letters. Through his eloquent and impassioned writing, Ruskin delves into a wide range of topics, including the importance of craftsmanship, the impact of industrialization, the value of education, and the need for social justice. With each letter, he challenges the workmen and labourers to question the prevailing societal norms and strive for a better future. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social criticism, Epistolary literature
Themes: Social justice, Craftsmanship, Industrialization, Education
