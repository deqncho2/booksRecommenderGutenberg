Synopsis: 
In Court and Kampong: Being Tales and Sketches of Native Life in the Malay Peninsula takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and diverse world of the Malay Peninsula. Sir Hugh Clifford, a seasoned colonial administrator, presents a collection of tales and sketches that offer a fascinating glimpse into the lives, customs, and traditions of the native people. From the bustling courtrooms of the colonial administration to the serene and picturesque kampongs (villages), Clifford weaves together stories that are both entertaining and thought-provoking. Through his vivid descriptions and engaging narratives, readers will find themselves immersed in a rich tapestry of cultural encounters, social dynamics, and personal struggles. 

Genres: Fiction, Travelogue, Cultural Studies
Themes: Colonialism, Cultural Identity, Social Dynamics, Tradition and Modernity
