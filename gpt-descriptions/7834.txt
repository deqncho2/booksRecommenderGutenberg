Synopsis: In "Wagner's 'Tristan und Isolde': An Essay on the Wagnerian Drama," George Ainslie Hight delves into the captivating world of Richard Wagner's renowned opera. Through a thought-provoking analysis, Hight explores the intricate layers of the opera's narrative, characters, and music, offering a fresh perspective on this timeless masterpiece. Drawing upon his deep understanding of Wagner's artistic vision, Hight invites readers to embark on a journey of passion, love, and transcendence, as he uncovers the profound themes that lie at the heart of "Tristan und Isolde."

Genres: Music criticism, Opera analysis, Literary criticism

Themes: Love and desire, Transcendence and spirituality, Artistic expression
