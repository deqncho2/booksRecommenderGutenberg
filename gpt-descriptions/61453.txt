Synopsis: 
In "Sixty Years a Bookman, With Other Recollections and Reflections," Joseph Shaylor takes readers on a captivating journey through his life as a passionate bookman. From his early days as a humble apprentice in a London bookshop to his encounters with famous authors and collectors, Shaylor's memoir is filled with fascinating anecdotes and insights into the world of books. As he reflects on his six decades in the trade, the reader is drawn into a world of literary treasures, rare editions, and the enduring power of the written word. With wit, charm, and a deep love for books, Shaylor's memoir is a delightful exploration of a life dedicated to the pursuit of knowledge and the joy of reading.

Genres: Memoir, Biography, Literary Nonfiction

Themes: Love of books, Literary history, Personal growth, Mentorship, Intellectual curiosity
