Synopsis: 
In Buchanan's Journal of Man, March 1887: Volume 1, Number 2, readers are taken on a captivating journey through a collection of thought-provoking essays, stories, and poems. From the exploration of the human psyche to the examination of societal norms, this volume delves into the depths of human nature, challenging readers to question their own beliefs and perceptions. With each page, the reader is transported to a world where the boundaries of reality and imagination blur, leaving them captivated and eager for more.

Genres: 
Literary fiction, philosophy, poetry

Themes: 
Human nature, societal norms, self-reflection
