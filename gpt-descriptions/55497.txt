Synopsis: 

In volume 04 of "The Historians' History of the World," readers are transported to the captivating era of ancient Greece, where the foundations of Western civilization were laid. From the rise of the city-states to the grandeur of Athens, this volume delves into the rich tapestry of Greek history, culture, and philosophy. As the narrative unfolds, the reader is then taken on a journey through the tumultuous period of the Roman conquest, witnessing the clash of empires and the transformation of the Mediterranean world. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Ancient history, World history

Themes: Power and politics, Cultural exchange, Military conquest, Intellectual and artistic achievements, Legacy of ancient civilizations
