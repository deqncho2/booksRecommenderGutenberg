Synopsis: 

In "England under the Tudors" by Arthur D. Innes, embark on a captivating journey through one of the most transformative periods in English history. From the rise of the Tudor dynasty to the reign of the iconic Queen Elizabeth I, this book delves into the political intrigues, religious upheavals, and cultural advancements that shaped England during this era. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Innes brings to life the larger-than-life figures such as Henry VIII, Mary I, and Elizabeth I, while exploring the complex dynamics of power, loyalty, and ambition that defined the Tudor court. As the Tudors navigate through wars, marriages, and religious conflicts, readers will be enthralled by the gripping narrative that unfolds, revealing the triumphs and tragedies of this remarkable period in English history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Power and politics, Religion and religious conflict, Royal succession, Gender and monarchy
