Synopsis: In 'The Captivi and the Mostellaria' by Plautus, two captivating comedies intertwine to bring forth a delightful tale of mistaken identities, clever schemes, and unexpected twists. In 'The Captivi,' a young man finds himself captured and enslaved, leading to a series of hilarious encounters and surprising revelations. Meanwhile, in 'The Mostellaria,' a cunning servant concocts a plan to outwit his master and win the heart of a beautiful woman. As the stories unfold, the audience is taken on a rollercoaster ride of laughter, romance, and unexpected outcomes.

Genres: Comedy, Farce, Roman Drama

Themes: Mistaken identity, Love and romance, Deception, Social class
