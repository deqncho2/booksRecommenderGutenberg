Synopsis: 
In the second edition of 'Glaciers of the Rockies and Selkirks', Arthur Philemon Coleman takes readers on an awe-inspiring journey through the majestic landscapes of the Canadian National Parks. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Coleman explores the breathtaking beauty and scientific wonders of the glaciers that adorn the Rockies and Selkirks. From the sparkling ice formations to the hidden secrets buried within, this book offers a captivating exploration of these natural wonders. Alongside his observations, Coleman provides insightful notes on five great glaciers, revealing their historical significance and the impact of climate change on their existence. 

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Travel, Science

Themes: 
Nature's beauty, Climate change, Environmental conservation
