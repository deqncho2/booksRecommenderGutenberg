Synopsis: In "The Earl of Beaconsfield," Froude delves into the life of one of the most enigmatic figures in British history, Benjamin Disraeli. From his humble beginnings to becoming a prominent statesman, Disraeli's journey is filled with political intrigue, personal struggles, and a relentless pursuit of power. Froude's vivid storytelling brings to life the complexities of Disraeli's character, his relationships, and the turbulent times in which he lived.

Genres: Biography, History, Political

Themes: Ambition, Power, Identity, Politics
