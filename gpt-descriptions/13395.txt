Synopsis: 
In "Since Cézanne," Bell, Clive takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of art, exploring the profound impact that the renowned painter Paul Cézanne had on the art world and its subsequent evolution. Through a series of insightful essays, Bell delves into the transformative power of Cézanne's work, examining how it challenged traditional artistic conventions and paved the way for new artistic movements. From the vibrant landscapes to the introspective still lifes, Bell's exploration of Cézanne's art is both enlightening and thought-provoking, offering readers a deeper understanding of the artist's genius and his lasting influence on the art world.

Genres: Art criticism, Art history, Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic innovation, Influence and legacy, Evolution of artistic movements
