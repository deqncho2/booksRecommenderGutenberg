Synopsis: In 'Picture-Show' by Siegfried Sassoon, a young soldier finds himself caught between the harsh realities of war and the captivating allure of the cinema. As he navigates the trenches of World War I, he becomes increasingly drawn to the escapism offered by the flickering images on the screen. However, the lines between reality and illusion blur, leading him to question the true nature of heroism and the cost of war. With vivid descriptions and poignant reflections, 'Picture-Show' explores the profound impact of war on the human psyche and the power of art to both inspire and deceive.

Genres: War fiction, Historical fiction, Psychological fiction

Themes: War and its effects, Escapism, Illusion vs reality, Heroism, Art and its influence
