Synopsis: In 'The Capitals of Spanish America', Curtis takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant cities of Spanish America, exploring their rich history, culture, and architecture. From the bustling streets of Mexico City to the colonial charm of Lima, and the grandeur of Buenos Aires, Curtis paints a vivid picture of these iconic capitals, delving into their unique stories and the people who shaped them. Through his engaging narrative, the author unveils the hidden gems and lesser-known aspects of these cities, offering a fresh perspective on their significance and influence. With a blend of historical anecdotes, personal observations, and captivating descriptions, 'The Capitals of Spanish America' is a captivating exploration of the heart and soul of these remarkable cities.

Genres: Travel, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Urbanism, Colonialism, Identity, Architecture, Cultural Heritage
