Synopsis: In "The Katcina Altars in Hopi Worship," Jesse Walter Fewkes takes readers on a captivating journey into the sacred world of the Hopi people. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, Fewkes unveils the intricate rituals and symbolism surrounding the Katcina altars, which play a central role in Hopi worship. From the mesmerizing dances to the awe-inspiring craftsmanship, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into a rich cultural tradition that has endured for centuries.

Genres: Anthropology, Cultural Studies, Religious Studies

Themes: Indigenous spirituality, Rituals and ceremonies, Cultural preservation
