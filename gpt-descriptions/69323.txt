Synopsis: In "Black Nick, the hermit of the hills; or, The expiated crime: A story of Burgoyne's surrender," Frederick Whittaker takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed wilderness of the American Revolution. Set against the backdrop of Burgoyne's surrender, the story follows the enigmatic figure of Black Nick, a hermit living in the hills. As secrets from his past are gradually unveiled, a tale of redemption, sacrifice, and the power of forgiveness unfolds. With vivid descriptions and unexpected twists, this historical novel delves into the complexities of human nature and the enduring consequences of one's actions.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Adventure

Themes: Redemption, Forgiveness, Consequences of Actions
