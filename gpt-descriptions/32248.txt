Synopsis: 
In 'The Standard Cantatas: Their Stories, Their Music, and Their Composers: A Handbook', George P. Upton takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of cantatas. From Bach to Handel, Mendelssohn to Brahms, Upton delves into the fascinating stories behind these beloved musical compositions. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, he explores the lives of the composers, the historical context in which the cantatas were created, and the intricate musical structures that make them timeless masterpieces. Whether you are a music enthusiast or simply curious about the origins of these iconic works, Upton's handbook is a treasure trove of knowledge and appreciation for the art of the cantata.

Genres: Music, Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Music appreciation, Historical context, Composers' lives
