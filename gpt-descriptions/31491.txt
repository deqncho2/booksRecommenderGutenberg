Synopsis: In 'The Baculum in the Chipmunks of Western North America', White, John A. takes readers on a captivating journey into the mysterious world of chipmunks. As a renowned biologist, White delves into the fascinating study of the baculum, a bone unique to male chipmunks, and its implications for their behavior and evolution. Through meticulous research and captivating anecdotes, White uncovers surprising secrets about these tiny creatures, shedding light on their complex social dynamics and the role of the baculum in their survival. This intriguing exploration of chipmunks offers a fresh perspective on the natural world and the intricate connections between anatomy, behavior, and adaptation.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Biology

Themes: Animal behavior, Evolution, Anatomy
