Synopsis: 

In "Hurrell Froude: Memoranda and Comments," Louise Imogen Guiney delves into the life and mind of the enigmatic Hurrell Froude, a prominent figure in the Oxford Movement of the 19th century. Through a collection of personal letters, diary entries, and insightful commentary, Guiney paints a vivid portrait of Froude's intellectual journey, his struggles with faith, and his profound impact on the religious landscape of his time. As the reader follows Froude's tumultuous path, they are drawn into a world of theological debates, personal introspection, and the search for spiritual truth. 

Genres: Biography, Religious Studies, Historical Non-fiction
Themes: Faith and Doubt, Intellectual Exploration, Religious Reform
