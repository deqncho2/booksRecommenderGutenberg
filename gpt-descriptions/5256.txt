Synopsis: In "The History of the Life of the Late Mr. Jonathan Wild the Great," Henry Fielding takes us on a thrilling journey through the life of a notorious criminal mastermind. Jonathan Wild, a self-proclaimed "great man," rises to power in the seedy underbelly of 18th-century London, manipulating both the law and the criminals to his advantage. As his empire grows, Wild becomes entangled in a web of deceit, betrayal, and treachery, leading to a dramatic climax that will leave readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Historical fiction, crime fiction, satire

Themes: Ambition, corruption, morality, the nature of power
