Synopsis: In 'Broken to the Plow: A Novel' by Charles Caldwell Dobie, a young man named John finds himself torn between duty and desire as he navigates the harsh realities of life in the rural South during the early 20th century. As he grapples with the weight of tradition and societal expectations, John embarks on a journey of self-discovery that will challenge his beliefs and force him to confront the deep-rooted prejudices of his community. With vivid storytelling and compelling characters, this novel explores the complexities of identity, love, and the pursuit of personal freedom in a world defined by its divisions.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Southern Literature

Themes: Identity, Tradition, Prejudice, Freedom
