Synopsis: In "The Everett Massacre: A history of the class struggle in the lumber industry," Smith, Walker C. delves into a gripping account of a pivotal event in American labor history. Set against the backdrop of the early 20th century, this book uncovers the intense conflict between labor unions and powerful corporations in the lumber industry. As tensions rise and the struggle for workers' rights escalates, the narrative takes a dramatic turn with the infamous Everett Massacre, a tragic event that forever changed the course of labor movements in the United States. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Smith, Walker C. brings to life the untold story of this harrowing chapter in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Labor history, Social justice

Themes: Class struggle, Labor rights, Power dynamics, Social inequality
