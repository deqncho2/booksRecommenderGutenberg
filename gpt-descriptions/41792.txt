Synopsis: 
In "Theodore Watts-Dunton: Poet, Novelist, Critic" by James Douglas, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the life and literary career of the enigmatic Theodore Watts-Dunton. From his early days as a struggling poet to his influential role as a critic and his unexpected friendship with a famous literary figure, this biography unveils the fascinating and often surprising aspects of Watts-Dunton's life. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be drawn into a world of artistic passion, personal struggles, and the power of friendship, leaving them eager to discover the untold stories behind this remarkable literary figure.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic pursuit, Friendship, Personal struggles, Literary influence
