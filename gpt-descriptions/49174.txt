Synopsis: In "Wagner and His Music-Dramas" by Robert C. Bagar, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the life and works of one of the most influential composers in history, Richard Wagner. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Bagar delves into Wagner's revolutionary music-dramas, exploring their profound impact on the world of opera and the cultural landscape of the time. From the epic tales of gods and heroes to the intricate web of emotions and passions, this book offers a fascinating exploration of Wagner's artistic genius and the enduring legacy of his music-dramas.

Genres: Music history, Biography, Cultural studies

Themes: Artistic innovation, Mythology and folklore, Emotional depth
