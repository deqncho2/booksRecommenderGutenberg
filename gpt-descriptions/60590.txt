Synopsis: 

'Dardistan in 1866, 1886 and 1893' takes readers on a captivating journey through the history, religions, customs, legends, fables, and songs of various regions in the Hindukush, including Gilgit, Chilas, Kandia (Gabrial), Dasin, Chitral, Hunsa, Nagyr, and more. Serving as a supplement to the second edition of the 'Hunza and Nagyr handbook' and an epitome of part III of the author's "The languages and races of Dardistan," this book offers a rich and comprehensive exploration of the diverse cultures and traditions found in these remote areas. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Travelogue, Anthropology

Themes: Cultural exploration, Religious diversity, Oral traditions, Linguistics
