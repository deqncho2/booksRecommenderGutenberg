Synopsis: 
In Racine's "Esther," the reader is transported to the opulent court of King Ahasuerus, where a young Jewish woman named Esther finds herself caught in a web of political intrigue and personal sacrifice. As she navigates the treacherous waters of power and love, Esther must make choices that will not only determine her own fate but also the destiny of her people. With its captivating characters and dramatic twists, "Esther" is a tale of ambition, loyalty, and the enduring strength of the human spirit.

Genres: Drama, Tragedy, Historical Fiction

Themes: Power and Politics, Love and Sacrifice, Identity and Belonging
