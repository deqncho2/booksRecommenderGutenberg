Synopsis: In 'The Progress of the Women's Suffrage Movement: Presidential Address to the Cambridge Branch of the C. & U. W. F. A. at the Annual Meeting on May 23rd, 1913', Eleanor Mildred Sidgwick delivers a powerful and thought-provoking speech that sheds light on the remarkable advancements made by the women's suffrage movement. Through her eloquent words, Sidgwick explores the challenges faced by women in their fight for equality and highlights the significant milestones achieved thus far. This address serves as a rallying cry for continued progress and inspires readers to reflect on the ongoing struggle for women's rights.

Genres: Non-fiction, Speech, Feminism

Themes: Women's suffrage, Gender equality, Social progress
