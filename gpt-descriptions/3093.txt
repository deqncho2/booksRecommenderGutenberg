Synopsis: In "The Eve of the Revolution; A Chronicle of the Breach with England," Carl L. Becker takes readers on a captivating journey through the events leading up to the American Revolution. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Becker delves into the political, social, and economic factors that ignited the flames of rebellion. From the Stamp Act to the Boston Tea Party, this chronicle unveils the tensions and conflicts that ultimately shaped the destiny of a nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, American history, political history

Themes: Revolution, independence, colonialism, resistance, patriotism
