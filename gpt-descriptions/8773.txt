Synopsis: In "Birth Control: A Statement of Christian Doctrine against the Neo-Malthusians," Sutherland and Halliday delve into the controversial topic of birth control from a Christian perspective. Through a thought-provoking exploration of historical, theological, and ethical aspects, the authors challenge the prevailing Neo-Malthusian ideology and present a compelling argument rooted in Christian doctrine. With a balanced and insightful approach, this book offers a fresh perspective on a contentious issue that continues to shape society today.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious, Social Commentary

Themes: Christian doctrine, Ethics, Population control, Religious perspectives on birth control
