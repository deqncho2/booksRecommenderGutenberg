Synopsis: 
'The Cyclopedia of the Colored Baptists of Alabama: Their Leaders and Their Work' by Charles Octavius Boothe is a captivating and comprehensive exploration of the influential leaders and impactful work of the colored Baptist community in Alabama. From the early days of slavery to the present, Boothe delves into the lives and achievements of these remarkable individuals, shedding light on their struggles, triumphs, and contributions to the religious and social fabric of the state. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Boothe paints a vivid picture of the rich history and enduring legacy of the colored Baptists in Alabama.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, African American Studies

Themes: African American history, Religion, Leadership, Social justice
