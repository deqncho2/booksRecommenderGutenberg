Synopsis: In this captivating biography, Horace Elisha Scudder delves into the life of James Russell Lowell, a prominent American poet, critic, and diplomat. From his early years as a precocious student to his influential role in the literary and political circles of 19th-century America, Lowell's journey is one of intellectual growth, artistic exploration, and passionate advocacy for social justice. Scudder's meticulous research and engaging storytelling bring to life the complexities of Lowell's character, his friendships with fellow writers, and his enduring impact on American literature and society. This first volume of the biography offers a fascinating glimpse into the formative years of a remarkable man who would leave an indelible mark on the cultural landscape of his time.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Intellectual development, Artistic exploration, Social justice
