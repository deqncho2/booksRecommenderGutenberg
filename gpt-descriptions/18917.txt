Synopsis: In 'Goldsmith: English Men of Letters Series', William Black takes us on a captivating journey through the life and works of the renowned 18th-century writer, Oliver Goldsmith. From his humble beginnings in Ireland to his adventures across Europe, we witness Goldsmith's struggles, triumphs, and the profound impact he had on the literary world. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Black paints a vivid portrait of a complex and enigmatic figure, revealing the untold stories behind Goldsmith's most beloved works. As we delve into the world of this literary genius, we are transported to a time of wit, charm, and intellectual fervor, leaving us with a deeper appreciation for Goldsmith's enduring legacy.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Literary genius, Personal struggles, Impact of literature
