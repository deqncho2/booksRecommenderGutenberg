Synopsis: 
In 'The Works of John Greenleaf Whittier, Volume VII, Complete: The Conflict with Slavery, Politics and Reform, the Inner Life, and Criticism', John Greenleaf Whittier presents a collection of his most influential writings, exploring the themes of slavery, politics, reform, and the inner struggles of the human spirit. Through his powerful and thought-provoking poetry and essays, Whittier delves into the conflicts and complexities of his time, shedding light on the injustices of slavery, the challenges of political activism, and the depths of the human soul. This volume offers a comprehensive and captivating insight into Whittier's literary genius and his unwavering commitment to social justice.

Genres: Poetry, Essays, Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Politics, Reform, Inner Life, Social Justice
