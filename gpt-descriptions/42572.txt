Synopsis: In "The Adopted Daughter: A Tale for Young Persons," Elizabeth Sandham weaves a captivating story of a young girl named Emily who finds herself torn between two worlds. When she is taken in by a wealthy family, Emily's life takes an unexpected turn as she navigates the complexities of her new surroundings. As she grows older, Emily must confront her own identity and make difficult choices that will shape her future. With themes of family, identity, and self-discovery, this tale will keep readers engrossed until the very end.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Family

Themes: Family, Identity, Self-discovery
