Synopsis: 

In 'Freedom In Service: Six Essays on Matters Concerning Britain's Safety and Good Government', F. J. C. Hearnshaw delves into the intricate web of issues surrounding Britain's safety and governance. Through a thought-provoking collection of essays, Hearnshaw explores the delicate balance between personal freedom and the collective responsibility of the government. From the challenges of maintaining national security to the complexities of maintaining a just society, this book offers a captivating exploration of the fundamental principles that underpin Britain's safety and good governance.

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, History

Themes: Freedom, Governance, National Security, Justice
