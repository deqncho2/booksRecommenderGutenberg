Synopsis: In 'My Kalulu, Prince, King and Slave: A Story of Central Africa', Henry M. Stanley takes readers on a captivating journey through the heart of Africa. The story follows the remarkable life of Kalulu, a young African boy who becomes Stanley's loyal companion and witness to the extraordinary events that unfold during their expedition. From encounters with tribal chiefs and dangerous wildlife to the exploration of uncharted territories, this gripping narrative offers a unique perspective on the complexities of African society and the challenges faced by both explorers and the indigenous people. As the bond between Stanley and Kalulu deepens, the story delves into themes of friendship, cultural exchange, and the impact of colonialism, painting a vivid picture of a continent on the brink of change.

Genres: Adventure, Biography, Historical Fiction

Themes: Friendship, Cultural Exchange, Colonialism
