Synopsis: In 'Time in the Play of Hamlet' by Edward Payson Vining, the author delves into the intricate relationship between time and the events that unfold in Shakespeare's iconic play, Hamlet. Vining explores the various ways in which time is manipulated and perceived by the characters, shedding light on the profound impact it has on their actions and decisions. Through a meticulous analysis of the play's structure and language, Vining uncovers hidden layers of meaning and invites readers to reconsider the significance of time in this timeless tragedy.

Genres: Literary criticism, Shakespearean studies

Themes: Time, fate, mortality, existentialism
