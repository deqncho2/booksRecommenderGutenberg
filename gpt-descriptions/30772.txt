Synopsis: 
In the "Minutes of the Proceedings of the Second Convention of Delegates from the Abolition Societies Established in Different Parts of the United States," a group of passionate abolitionists gather in Philadelphia in 1795 to discuss and strategize the fight against slavery. As the convention unfolds, tensions rise, alliances are formed, and powerful speeches ignite the flame of change. Through the minutes of these intense deliberations, readers are transported to a pivotal moment in history, witnessing the birth of a united movement that will shape the future of the nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, political discourse, social justice

Themes: Abolitionism, human rights, activism, unity, social change
