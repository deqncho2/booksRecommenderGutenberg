Synopsis: In 'The Sea-Witch; Or, The African Quadroon: A Story of the Slave Coast', Maturin M. Ballou takes readers on a captivating journey to the treacherous Slave Coast of Africa. The story follows the life of a young quadroon girl named Zelica, who finds herself caught between two worlds - the world of her African heritage and the world of slavery. As she navigates the perils of the slave trade, Zelica's strength and resilience are tested, leading her on a path of self-discovery and a quest for freedom. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this gripping tale explores the complexities of identity, the brutality of slavery, and the enduring power of hope.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Identity, Slavery, Freedom, Resilience
