Synopsis: In "Montaigne and Shakspere," J.M. Robertson delves into the fascinating connection between two literary giants of the Renaissance era. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Robertson uncovers surprising parallels and influences between the French philosopher Michel de Montaigne and the English playwright William Shakespeare. This captivating exploration takes readers on a journey through the minds of these extraordinary thinkers, revealing the profound impact they had on each other's work. As Robertson uncovers the hidden connections between Montaigne's essays and Shakespeare's plays, readers are left with a deeper understanding of the intellectual and artistic exchange that shaped the Renaissance period.

Genres: Literary criticism, Biography, Comparative literature

Themes: Influence and inspiration, Renaissance intellectual culture, Literary connections
