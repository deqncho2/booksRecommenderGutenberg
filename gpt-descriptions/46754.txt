Synopsis: 

'A Short History of the Royal Navy, 1217 to 1688' takes readers on a captivating journey through centuries of naval warfare, exploring the triumphs, challenges, and pivotal moments that shaped the Royal Navy into a formidable force. From the iconic Battle of Trafalgar to the daring exploits of legendary figures like Sir Francis Drake, this book delves into the fascinating world of naval strategy, technological advancements, and the courageous men who sailed the high seas. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, David Hannay paints a vivid picture of the Royal Navy's evolution, offering a captivating glimpse into the pivotal role it played in shaping the course of history.

Genres: History, Military, Non-fiction

Themes: Naval warfare, Strategy, Technological advancements, Courage, Historical significance
