Synopsis: In "Tween Snow and Fire: A Tale of the Last Kafir War," Bertram Mitford takes readers on a thrilling journey through the rugged landscapes of South Africa during the 19th century. The story follows the adventures of a young Englishman named Maurice Gerald as he becomes entangled in the conflict between the British settlers and the indigenous Kafir tribes. Amidst the chaos of war, Maurice finds himself torn between loyalty, love, and the pursuit of justice, leading to unexpected alliances and heart-wrenching choices. With vivid descriptions and captivating characters, this tale explores the complexities of colonialism, cultural clashes, and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, War

Themes: Colonialism, Identity, Loyalty, Love, Justice
