Synopsis: In the third volume of "A History of the Four Georges and of William IV," Justin McCarthy delves into the captivating lives and reigns of the British monarchs George III, George IV, and William IV. From political intrigues and scandals to the tumultuous events that shaped the nation, McCarthy paints a vivid picture of the Georgian era, exploring the personal lives and public personas of these influential rulers. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of these monarchs and the impact they had on British history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Monarchy, Politics, Society, Power, Personal relationships
