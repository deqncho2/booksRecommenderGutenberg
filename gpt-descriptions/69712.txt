Synopsis: 

When Africa awakes: The "inside story" of the stirrings and strivings of the new Negro in the Western world takes readers on a captivating journey through the experiences and aspirations of the new Negro in the Western world. Written by Hubert H. Harrison, this book delves into the untold stories of African individuals who have found themselves caught between their African heritage and the Western society they now inhabit. Through a series of personal narratives and historical accounts, Harrison sheds light on the struggles, triumphs, and awakening of the new Negro, offering a unique perspective on the complexities of identity, cultural assimilation, and the pursuit of equality.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Cultural Studies

Themes: Identity, Cultural assimilation, Equality, African diaspora
