Synopsis: In the second volume of "The Age of Justinian and Theodora: A History of the Sixth Century A.D.", author William Gordon Holmes delves deeper into the captivating era of the Byzantine Empire. This enthralling historical account takes readers on a journey through the reign of Emperor Justinian and his influential wife, Empress Theodora. From political intrigue and military conquests to religious conflicts and cultural transformations, Holmes paints a vivid picture of a pivotal period in history. As the empire faces internal and external challenges, the reader is immersed in a world of power struggles, love affairs, and the pursuit of greatness. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Holmes brings to life the remarkable individuals and events that shaped the sixth century A.D.

Genres: History, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and politics, Love and relationships, Religion and faith, Cultural transformation
