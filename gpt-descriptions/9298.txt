Synopsis: In "Life and Death of Harriett Frean" by May Sinclair, we are introduced to Harriett Frean, a woman who has always lived her life according to societal expectations. As she navigates through the ups and downs of her existence, we witness the profound impact that conformity and societal pressures can have on an individual's sense of self. Through Harriett's experiences, the novel explores the complexities of identity, the consequences of suppressing one's true desires, and the struggle to break free from the constraints of societal norms.

Genres: Literary fiction, Psychological fiction, Social commentary

Themes: Conformity, Identity, Societal expectations, Self-discovery
