Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The Life and Letters of Maria Edgeworth', the renowned author takes us on a captivating journey through her personal life and literary career. Through a collection of intimate letters, we gain insight into the challenges and triumphs that shaped Edgeworth's life, as well as her deep connections with family, friends, and fellow writers. From her early years in Ireland to her travels across Europe, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of a pioneering female writer whose influence extended far beyond her time.

Genres: Biography, Autobiography, Letters

Themes: Literary legacy, Family bonds, Female authorship, Cultural and social observations
