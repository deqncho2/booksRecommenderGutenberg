Synopsis: 

Step back in time and immerse yourself in the captivating world of ancient Rome with "The Grandeur That Was Rome: a survey of Roman culture and civilization" by J. C. Stobart. This enthralling book takes readers on a journey through the rise and fall of one of history's greatest empires, exploring the rich tapestry of Roman culture, art, architecture, politics, and daily life. From the grandeur of the Colosseum to the intricate mosaics of Pompeii, Stobart paints a vivid picture of a civilization that shaped the course of Western history. Delve into the lives of emperors, gladiators, philosophers, and everyday citizens as you uncover the secrets and wonders of ancient Rome.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural studies, Archaeology

Themes: Roman civilization, Art and architecture, Politics and governance, Daily life, Historical legacy
