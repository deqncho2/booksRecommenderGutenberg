Synopsis: 

'Armenia and Her People; or, The Story of Armenia by an Armenian' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history, culture, and struggles of the Armenian people. Written by George H. Filian, an Armenian himself, this book offers a unique perspective and an intimate portrayal of the Armenian experience. From ancient times to the present day, the narrative delves into the triumphs, tragedies, and resilience of a nation that has faced numerous challenges throughout its existence. Through vivid storytelling and personal anecdotes, Filian paints a vivid picture of Armenia's vibrant heritage, its contributions to civilization, and the enduring spirit of its people.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural exploration, Memoir

Themes: Identity, Resilience, Cultural heritage, Historical significance
