Synopsis: 

In 'Blacks and Bushrangers: Adventures in Queensland' by Kennedy, E. B., readers are transported to the rugged and untamed landscapes of Queensland, Australia, during the 19th century. This thrilling historical adventure follows the journey of a group of unlikely companions, including a resourceful young settler, a fearless Aboriginal tracker, and a notorious bushranger with a hidden agenda. As they navigate through treacherous terrain, encounter dangerous outlaws, and face the harsh realities of colonial life, their lives become intertwined in unexpected ways. With each twist and turn, the story delves into the complexities of loyalty, survival, and the clash of cultures, leaving readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Western

Themes: Exploration and colonization, Friendship and loyalty, Cultural clashes
