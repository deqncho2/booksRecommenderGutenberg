Synopsis: 

In 'Evolution and Ethics, and Other Essays', Thomas Henry Huxley delves into the fascinating intersection of science, philosophy, and morality. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, Huxley explores the implications of Darwin's theory of evolution on our understanding of ethics and human nature. From examining the relationship between science and religion to questioning the role of education in society, Huxley challenges conventional wisdom and offers fresh perspectives on the complex issues of his time. With his characteristic wit and intellectual rigor, Huxley invites readers on a captivating journey of exploration and introspection.

Genres: Philosophy, Science, Essays

Themes: Evolution, Ethics, Morality, Science and Religion, Education
