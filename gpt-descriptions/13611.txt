Synopsis: 

In 'Studies in the Psychology of Sex, Volume 2: Sexual Inversion', Havelock Ellis delves into the intriguing and often misunderstood topic of sexual inversion. Through a comprehensive exploration of case studies and scientific research, Ellis examines the phenomenon of individuals who experience a reversal of sexual instincts, challenging societal norms and preconceived notions of sexuality. With a blend of scientific analysis and compassionate understanding, Ellis offers a thought-provoking and enlightening perspective on this complex aspect of human sexuality.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Sexuality

Themes: Sexual identity, Human sexuality, Social norms
