Synopsis: In "Browning's Heroines" by Ethel Colburn Mayne, embark on a captivating journey through the lives of the remarkable women who inhabit the poetry of Robert Browning. From the passionate and enigmatic Porphyria to the resilient and independent Pompilia, this book delves into the complex and multifaceted characters that Browning created. Through insightful analysis and vivid storytelling, Mayne brings these heroines to life, exploring their desires, struggles, and triumphs. Discover the secrets and hidden depths of these iconic literary figures, as Mayne unravels the mysteries behind their actions and motivations. 

Genres: Literary criticism, Biography, Poetry analysis

Themes: Love and desire, Femininity and gender roles, Morality and justice
