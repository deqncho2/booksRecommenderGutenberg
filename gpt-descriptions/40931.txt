Synopsis: 

In "The Early History of the Scottish Union Question: Bi-Centenary Edition," George W. T. Omond delves into the captivating and often overlooked history surrounding the Scottish Union. This meticulously researched book takes readers on a journey through the political, social, and cultural landscape of Scotland, exploring the events and individuals that shaped the Union question in its early stages. From the heated debates in the Scottish Parliament to the clandestine negotiations behind closed doors, Omond uncovers the intricate web of alliances, rivalries, and ambitions that influenced the course of Scottish history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Cultural studies

Themes: Scottish identity, Nationalism, Political power dynamics, Historical significance
