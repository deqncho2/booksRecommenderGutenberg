Synopsis: 

In 'Catastrophe and Social Change: Based Upon a Sociological Study of the Halifax Disaster', Samuel Henry Prince delves into the aftermath of one of the most devastating disasters in Canadian history. Drawing from a comprehensive sociological study, Prince explores the profound impact of the Halifax Explosion of 1917 on the city's social fabric and the subsequent transformation of its community. Through vivid narratives and meticulous analysis, the author uncovers the untold stories of resilience, solidarity, and the unexpected ways in which catastrophe can shape society.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Disaster and its aftermath, Social transformation, Resilience, Community building
