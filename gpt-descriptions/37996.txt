Synopsis: In "Beethoven: A Memoir (2nd Ed.)", Graeme Elliott takes readers on an intimate journey through the life of one of the greatest composers in history. Through personal anecdotes, historical accounts, and insightful analysis, Elliott delves into the enigmatic world of Ludwig van Beethoven, exploring his genius, his struggles, and the profound impact he had on the world of music. From his early years as a prodigious talent to his later battles with deafness and personal demons, this memoir offers a captivating portrait of a complex and extraordinary artist.

Genres: Biography, Music, History

Themes: Genius and creativity, Triumph over adversity, Legacy and influence
