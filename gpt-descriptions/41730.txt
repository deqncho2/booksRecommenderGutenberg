Synopsis: 
In "Dixie After the War," Myrta Lockett Avary takes us on a captivating journey through the South during the twelve years following the fall of Richmond. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Avary exposes the social conditions that prevailed in the aftermath of the Civil War. From the struggles of the defeated Confederacy to the challenges faced by newly freed slaves, this eye-opening exposition offers a unique perspective on a tumultuous period in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, memoir, social commentary

Themes: Post-war reconstruction, racial tensions, social inequality, Southern identity
