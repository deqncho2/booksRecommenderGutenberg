Synopsis: 
"Following the Color Line: An account of Negro citizenship in the American democracy" takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the complex history of Negro citizenship in the United States. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, the author delves into the struggles, triumphs, and ongoing challenges faced by African Americans in their pursuit of equal rights and full citizenship. From the Reconstruction era to the present day, this book sheds light on the profound impact of race on American democracy, offering a fresh perspective on a crucial aspect of the nation's history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Civil Rights

Themes: Race, Citizenship, Equality, Civil Rights, Discrimination
