Synopsis: 

'Along Alaska's Great River' is a thrilling and captivating account of an Alaska exploring expedition led by Frederick Schwatka. The book takes readers on an unforgettable journey along the Great Yukon River, from its source to its mouth, through the British North-West Territory and the Territory of Alaska. Schwatka's vivid descriptions and personal experiences bring to life the untamed beauty of the Alaskan wilderness, as well as the challenges and dangers faced by the expedition. From encounters with native tribes to treacherous rapids and harsh weather conditions, the expedition's adventures will keep readers on the edge of their seats, eager to discover what lies around the next bend of the river.

Genres: Adventure, Travel, Non-fiction

Themes: Exploration, Survival, Cultural encounters
