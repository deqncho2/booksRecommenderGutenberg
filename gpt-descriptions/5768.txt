Synopsis: 

'Free as in Freedom: Richard Stallman's Crusade for Free Software' by Sam Williams is a captivating biography that delves into the life and mission of one of the most influential figures in the world of technology. This book takes readers on a journey through the fascinating life of Richard Stallman, a brilliant programmer and visionary who dedicated his life to advocating for free software and challenging the dominance of proprietary software companies. From his early days at MIT to the founding of the Free Software Foundation, Stallman's story is one of determination, passion, and unwavering commitment to the principles of freedom and user autonomy. 

Genres: Biography, Technology, Non-fiction

Themes: Freedom, Open-source movement, Software ethics
