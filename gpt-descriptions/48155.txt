Synopsis: 
In 'History of Ancient Pottery: Greek, Etruscan, and Roman. Volume 2 (of 2)', authors H. B. Walters and Samuel Birch delve into the captivating world of ancient pottery, exploring the rich traditions and techniques of the Greek, Etruscan, and Roman civilizations. This volume takes readers on a journey through time, uncovering the fascinating stories behind the creation and use of pottery in these ancient cultures. From the intricate designs and symbolism to the practical purposes and cultural significance, this comprehensive study offers a unique perspective on the artistry and craftsmanship of pottery in the ancient world.

Genres: 
Art History, Archaeology, Cultural Studies

Themes: 
Artistic expression, Cultural identity, Technological advancements
