Synopsis: In "Feuerbach: The roots of the socialist philosophy," Friedrich Engels delves into the intellectual journey of Ludwig Feuerbach, a prominent German philosopher of the 19th century. Engels explores Feuerbach's groundbreaking ideas and their profound impact on the development of socialist philosophy. Through a captivating narrative, Engels uncovers the hidden layers of Feuerbach's thought, revealing the intricate connections between religion, materialism, and the struggle for social justice. As Engels unravels Feuerbach's intellectual evolution, he sheds light on the profound implications of his ideas for the future of society.

Genres: Philosophy, Biography, Political Theory

Themes: Socialism, Materialism, Religion, Social Justice
