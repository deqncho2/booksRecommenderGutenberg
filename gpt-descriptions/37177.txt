Synopsis: In "Ludvig Holberg, The Founder of Norwegian Literature and an Oxford Student," author S. C. Hammer delves into the fascinating life of Ludvig Holberg, a man who played a pivotal role in shaping Norwegian literature. From his humble beginnings in Bergen to his time as a student at Oxford University, Holberg's journey is one of resilience, ambition, and intellectual curiosity. Hammer skillfully weaves together historical facts and personal anecdotes to paint a vivid portrait of this literary pioneer, leaving readers captivated by his remarkable story.

Genres: Biography, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Literary legacy, Intellectual curiosity, Personal growth
