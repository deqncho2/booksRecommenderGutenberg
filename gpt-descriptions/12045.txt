Synopsis: 

In 'Women in the fine arts, from the Seventh Century B.C. to the Twentieth Century A.D.', Clara Erskine Clement Waters takes us on a captivating journey through centuries of art history, shining a spotlight on the often overlooked contributions of women artists. From ancient Greece to the Renaissance and beyond, Waters unveils the remarkable stories of talented women who defied societal norms and made their mark in the world of fine arts. Through vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, she explores the challenges these women faced, the barriers they broke, and the lasting impact of their artistic endeavors. 

Genres: Art history, Biography, Women's studies

Themes: Gender equality, Artistic expression, Overcoming adversity
