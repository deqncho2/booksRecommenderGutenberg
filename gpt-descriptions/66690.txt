Synopsis: In 'Tiberius the Tyrant' by John Charles Tarver, a gripping historical fiction, the reader is transported to ancient Rome, where the notorious Emperor Tiberius reigns with an iron fist. Through the eyes of a young slave, we witness the dark and treacherous world of power, corruption, and betrayal that surrounds the tyrant. As the slave becomes entangled in a dangerous web of secrets and conspiracies, the tension rises, and the reader is left wondering who will survive in this ruthless game of power.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Thriller, Political Drama

Themes: Power and Corruption, Betrayal, Survival
