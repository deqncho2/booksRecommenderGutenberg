Synopsis: 
In 'The Theological Tractates and The Consolation of Philosophy', Boethius takes readers on a philosophical journey filled with profound questions and thought-provoking insights. The work follows the protagonist, who finds himself in a state of despair and seeks solace in the teachings of Philosophy. Through a series of dialogues, Boethius explores the nature of happiness, the existence of evil, and the role of divine providence in human affairs. As the protagonist grapples with his own predicament, readers are invited to ponder the timeless questions of human existence and the pursuit of true wisdom.

Genres: Philosophy, Theology, Classical Literature

Themes: Happiness, Evil, Divine Providence, Wisdom
