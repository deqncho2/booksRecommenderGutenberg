Synopsis: 
In the second volume of 'The Poems of Philip Freneau, Poet of the American Revolution,' readers are transported to a tumultuous era in American history through the powerful verses of Philip Morin Freneau. This collection showcases Freneau's poetic prowess as he delves into the themes of patriotism, freedom, and the struggles faced by the American people during the Revolution. From stirring odes to the heroes of the war to poignant reflections on the cost of independence, Freneau's poems capture the spirit and essence of a nation fighting for its identity. With each verse, readers are invited to experience the raw emotions and fervor that defined this pivotal period in American history.

Genres: Poetry, Historical Literature

Themes: Patriotism, Freedom, Struggle, American Revolution
