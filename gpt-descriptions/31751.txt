Synopsis: 
'What Bird is That?: A Pocket Museum of the Land Birds of the Eastern United States Arranged According to Season' is an engaging and informative guidebook that takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse avian species found in the eastern United States. Written by renowned ornithologist Frank M. Chapman, this pocket-sized museum offers a comprehensive collection of bird descriptions, illustrations, and seasonal observations. From the vibrant colors of spring to the silent beauty of winter, readers will discover the fascinating behaviors, habitats, and migratory patterns of these captivating creatures. Whether you are a seasoned birdwatcher or a curious nature enthusiast, this book will undoubtedly ignite your passion for the avian world and deepen your understanding of the feathered inhabitants of the eastern United States.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature Guide, Ornithology

Themes: Birdwatching, Nature appreciation, Environmental conservation
