Synopsis: 

In "Six Days of the Irish Republic: A Narrative and Critical Account of the Latest Phase of Irish Politics," author L. G. Redmond-Howard takes readers on a gripping journey through a pivotal period in Irish history. Set against the backdrop of intense political turmoil, this narrative delves into the events that unfolded over six crucial days, revealing the complex web of alliances, betrayals, and sacrifices that shaped the destiny of the Irish Republic. With vivid storytelling and meticulous analysis, Redmond-Howard offers a unique perspective on the key players and their motivations, painting a vivid picture of a nation on the brink of revolution.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political analysis, Narrative history

Themes: Irish nationalism, Political upheaval, Revolution, Power struggles, Patriotism
