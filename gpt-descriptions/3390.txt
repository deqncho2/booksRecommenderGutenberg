Synopsis: In "My Mark Twain," William Dean Howells takes readers on a captivating journey through his personal experiences and encounters with the legendary American author, Mark Twain. Through a series of anecdotes and reflections, Howells provides a unique and intimate glimpse into the life and character of Twain, revealing the complexities and contradictions of this literary genius. From their initial meeting to their enduring friendship, Howells paints a vivid portrait of Twain's wit, humor, and profound insights, while also delving into the challenges and struggles that shaped his remarkable career. As Howells shares his own observations and memories, readers are invited to explore the fascinating world of Mark Twain through the eyes of a close friend and fellow writer.

Genres: Biography, Memoir, Literary Criticism

Themes: Friendship, Literary Influence, Humor, Artistic Integrity
