Synopsis: 

In "Monarchs of Minstrelsy, from 'Daddy' Rice to Date," Edward Le Roy Rice takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of minstrelsy. From the early days of the genre with the iconic figure of "Daddy" Rice, to the present day, Rice explores the lives and careers of the most influential performers who shaped and defined this unique form of entertainment. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author delves into the triumphs, struggles, and controversies surrounding these minstrelsy monarchs, offering a comprehensive and engrossing account of their impact on American culture.

Genres: Biography, History, Performing Arts

Themes: Cultural influence, Racial representation, Evolution of entertainment
