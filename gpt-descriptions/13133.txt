Synopsis: In 'Ten Reasons Proposed to His Adversaries for Disputation in the Name: of the Faith and Presented to the Illustrious Members of Our Universities', Edmund Campion, a renowned scholar and Catholic martyr, presents a compelling argument defending the Catholic faith against its adversaries. Through ten thought-provoking reasons, Campion challenges the prevailing beliefs and invites his adversaries to engage in a disputation to explore the truth. As the plot unfolds, the reader is taken on a journey of intellectual exploration and religious conviction, as Campion's persuasive arguments confront the challenges faced by the Catholic Church during a tumultuous period in history.

Genres: Religious literature, Historical fiction, Philosophical discourse

Themes: Faith and reason, Religious persecution, Intellectual debate, Religious conviction
