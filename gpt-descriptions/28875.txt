Synopsis: In 'A Man's Value to Society: Studies in Self Culture and Character', Newell Dwight Hillis delves into the profound question of a man's worth in society. Through a series of captivating studies, Hillis explores the intricate relationship between self-culture and character, unraveling the secrets to personal growth and societal contribution. With thought-provoking anecdotes and insightful analysis, this book offers a compelling exploration of the individual's role in shaping the world around them.

Genres: Self-help, Philosophy, Sociology

Themes: Personal development, Social responsibility, Individualism, Moral character
