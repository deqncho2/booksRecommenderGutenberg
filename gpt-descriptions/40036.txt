Synopsis: 
In "Tramping with Tramps: Studies and Sketches of Vagabond Life," Josiah Flynt takes readers on a captivating journey into the hidden world of vagabonds and tramps. Through his firsthand experiences and encounters with these marginalized individuals, Flynt provides a unique and eye-opening perspective on their lives, struggles, and the societal factors that contribute to their existence. From hopping freight trains to living in makeshift camps, Flynt immerses himself in their world, shedding light on the complexities and contradictions of their nomadic lifestyle. With vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, this book offers a fascinating exploration of a subculture often overlooked and misunderstood.

Genres: Non-fiction, Travelogue, Social Commentary

Themes: Marginalization, Poverty, Subcultures, Social Inequality
