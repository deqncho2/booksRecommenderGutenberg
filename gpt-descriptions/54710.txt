Synopsis: In "The Shame of the Cities," investigative journalist Lincoln Steffens takes readers on a gripping journey through the corrupt underbelly of American cities at the turn of the 20th century. Through his fearless exposés, Steffens uncovers the shocking truth behind political machines, bribery, and the abuse of power that plague urban centers. From the backroom deals of politicians to the exploitation of the working class, this eye-opening account reveals the dark secrets that have long been hidden from the public eye.

Genres: Non-fiction, investigative journalism, political exposé

Themes: Corruption, power dynamics, social inequality, political reform
