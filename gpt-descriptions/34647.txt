Synopsis: 
'Letters of a Javanese Princess' is a captivating collection of letters written by Kartini, a young Javanese princess, during the late 19th century. Through her heartfelt correspondence, Kartini shares her dreams, struggles, and aspirations, providing a unique glimpse into the life of a woman in colonial Indonesia. As she navigates the constraints of her culture and the expectations placed upon her, Kartini's letters reveal her determination to break free from societal norms and fight for women's rights and education. With each letter, the reader becomes immersed in Kartini's world, witnessing her growth, resilience, and unwavering spirit.

Genres: 
Autobiography, Historical Fiction, Feminist Literature

Themes: 
Women's Rights, Cultural Identity, Education, Empowerment
