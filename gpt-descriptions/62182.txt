Synopsis: In "Good Councell against the plague," a learned physician presents a compelling guide to protect oneself from the deadly plague that has recently emerged in certain parts of the city. Through the use of various remedies such as fumes, drinks, vomits, and other internal treatments, the author offers valuable advice on prevention and even a potential cure for those already infected. This timely and essential work provides crucial information for every household to avoid the infection and safeguard their well-being.

Genres: Medical literature, self-help, historical

Themes: Disease prevention, health preservation, survival
