Synopsis: In 'The Clergyman's Hand-book of Law: The Law of Church and Grave' by Charles Martin Scanlan, a young clergyman finds himself entangled in a web of legal complexities when a mysterious incident occurs within the sacred walls of his church. As he delves deeper into the intricacies of church law and the rights of the deceased, he uncovers shocking secrets that challenge his faith and force him to question the very foundations of his calling. With each revelation, the clergyman's journey becomes a thrilling exploration of justice, morality, and the blurred lines between the spiritual and the legal realms.

Genres: Mystery, Legal Thriller, Religious Fiction

Themes: Faith and doubt, Legal ethics, Secrets and revelations
