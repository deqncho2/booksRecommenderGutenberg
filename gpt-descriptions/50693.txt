Synopsis: 

In 'The Marne Battle-fields (1914)', the reader is transported back to the intense and harrowing days of World War I. Through vivid and gripping storytelling, the author takes us on a journey through the historic Battle of the Marne, a pivotal moment in the war that changed the course of history. As the battle unfolds, we witness the bravery and sacrifice of soldiers on both sides, the strategic maneuvers of military leaders, and the devastating impact of war on the lives of ordinary people caught in the crossfire. With its compelling narrative and rich historical detail, this book offers a captivating exploration of one of the most significant battles of the Great War.

Genres: Historical fiction, War fiction, Military history

Themes: Courage, Sacrifice, Strategy, Human resilience, Impact of war on civilians
