Synopsis: In 'The Fine Lady's Airs', Thomas Baker takes us on a captivating journey through the world of high society in 18th century England. The story revolves around a young and enchanting lady who possesses a unique talent for singing. As she navigates the intricate web of social expectations and romantic entanglements, her captivating voice becomes both a blessing and a curse. With vivid descriptions and witty dialogue, Baker paints a vivid picture of the opulent lifestyles, secret affairs, and scandalous intrigues that lie beneath the surface of the fine lady's airs.

Genres: Historical fiction, Romance, Satire

Themes: Social class, Love and desire, Artistic expression, Deception and appearance
