Synopsis: In "The Two Tests: The Supernatural Claims of Christianity Tried by Two of Its Own Rules," Lionel Lisle presents a thought-provoking exploration of Christianity's supernatural claims. Through a captivating narrative, Lisle takes readers on a journey where two distinct tests are applied to examine the validity of these claims. As the tests unfold, unexpected twists and revelations challenge preconceived notions, leaving readers questioning their own beliefs and opening up new avenues of inquiry. This compelling work delves into the heart of Christianity, inviting readers to critically examine its supernatural claims and consider the implications they hold for faith and reason.

Genres: Philosophy, Religious Studies, Non-fiction

Themes: Supernatural, Christianity, Faith and Reason, Philosophy of Religion
