Synopsis: In "Martha Schofield, pioneer Negro educator: Historical and philosophical review of reconstruction period of South Carolina," Matilda A. Evans takes us on a captivating journey through the life of Martha Schofield, an influential African American educator during the Reconstruction period in South Carolina. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Evans explores the challenges and triumphs that Schofield faced as she dedicated her life to providing education and empowerment to the newly freed slaves. This historical and philosophical review offers a unique perspective on the Reconstruction era, shedding light on the resilience and determination of individuals like Schofield who played a crucial role in shaping the future of education and civil rights.

Genres: Biography, History, Education

Themes: Reconstruction, Education, Civil Rights, Resilience, Empowerment
