Synopsis:
In "Tancred; Or, The New Crusade" by Benjamin Disraeli, the reader is transported to the vibrant world of 19th-century Europe and the Middle East. The story revolves around Tancred, a young and enigmatic nobleman who embarks on a spiritual and political journey that takes him from the salons of London to the sacred lands of the East. As he encounters a diverse cast of characters, including powerful politicians, religious leaders, and captivating women, Tancred grapples with questions of identity, faith, and the clash of civilizations. With its richly detailed settings and thought-provoking narrative, "Tancred" offers a captivating exploration of the complexities of power, religion, and personal transformation.

Genres: Historical fiction, Political fiction, Philosophical fiction

Themes: Identity, Faith, Power, Clash of civilizations
