Synopsis: In 'The Enemies of Women', Blasco Ibáñez takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives of three women from different social backgrounds who find themselves entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and societal expectations. As their paths intertwine, secrets are revealed, and the true nature of the enemies they face becomes apparent. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century Spain, this gripping novel explores the complexities of relationships, the struggle for women's rights, and the power dynamics that shape society.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Romance

Themes: Love and Betrayal, Women's Rights, Social Class, Power Dynamics
