Synopsis: 

"The Morris Book, Part 1: A History of Morris Dancing, With a Description of Eleven Dances as Performed by the Morris-Men of England" takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and enchanting world of Morris dancing. Written by MacIlwaine and Sharp, this book delves into the origins, traditions, and significance of this ancient English folk dance. Alongside the fascinating historical account, the authors provide detailed descriptions of eleven mesmerizing Morris dances, as performed by the talented Morris-Men of England. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Performing Arts

Themes: Folk traditions, Cultural heritage, Dance as an expression of identity
