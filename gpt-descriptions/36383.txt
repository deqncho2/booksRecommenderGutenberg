Synopsis: In "A Canterbury Pilgrimage," Elizabeth Robins Pennell takes readers on a captivating journey through the English countryside, following in the footsteps of Chaucer's famous pilgrims. With her keen observations and witty anecdotes, Pennell paints a vivid picture of the people, places, and traditions encountered along the way. From humorous encounters with fellow travelers to poignant reflections on the historical significance of the pilgrimage, this travelogue offers a delightful and insightful exploration of both the medieval and modern worlds.

Genres: Travelogue, Memoir, Historical

Themes: Pilgrimage, Tradition, Cultural Heritage
