Synopsis: In 'The Colour of Life' by Alice Meynell, a collection of essays and observations, the reader is taken on a captivating journey through the vibrant tapestry of life. From the bustling streets of London to the serene countryside, Meynell's keen eye and lyrical prose paint a vivid picture of the world around us. Through her reflections on nature, art, and the human experience, she invites us to see the beauty in the ordinary and find solace in the fleeting moments that make up our lives. With each page, Meynell's words ignite a sense of wonder and inspire us to embrace the richness of existence.

Genres: Essay, Non-fiction, Philosophy

Themes: Beauty in the ordinary, Nature, Art, Human experience
