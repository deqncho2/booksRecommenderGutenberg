Synopsis: 
'Essays Before a Sonata' by Charles Ives is a thought-provoking collection of essays that explores the nature of music, the creative process, and the role of the artist in society. Through a series of philosophical musings and personal anecdotes, Ives delves into the complexities of composition, challenging conventional notions of harmony, rhythm, and structure. With a blend of wit, insight, and a touch of irreverence, he invites readers on a journey of discovery, encouraging them to question their preconceived ideas about music and art.

Genres: 
Essay, Music Theory, Philosophy

Themes: 
Creativity, Individuality, Artistic Expression, Innovation
