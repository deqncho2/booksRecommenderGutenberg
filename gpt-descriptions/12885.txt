Synopsis: 

In 'Bundling; Its Origin, Progress and Decline in America', Henry Reed Stiles takes us on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of a unique American courtship tradition. From its mysterious origins in colonial times to its eventual decline in the 19th century, Stiles explores the practice of bundling, a custom that allowed unmarried couples to share a bed while remaining fully clothed. Through meticulous research and engaging anecdotes, the author delves into the social, cultural, and religious aspects surrounding bundling, shedding light on its significance and impact on American society. As we delve into the pages of this intriguing book, we uncover a world of forbidden desires, societal norms, and the ever-changing dynamics of courtship in America.

Genres: History, Cultural Studies, Sociology

Themes: Courtship and Dating, Social Norms, Cultural Traditions
