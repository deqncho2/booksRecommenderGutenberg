Synopsis: 

In 'International Conference Held at Washington for the Purpose of Fixing a Prime Meridian and a Universal Day. October, 1884.: Protocols of the Proceedings', readers are transported to the fascinating world of international diplomacy and scientific collaboration. Set in the late 19th century, this gripping account unveils the intense negotiations and debates that took place among representatives from various nations, all striving to establish a universal standard for measuring time and determining the prime meridian. As the delegates grapple with conflicting interests and cultural differences, tensions rise and unexpected alliances form. With the fate of global timekeeping hanging in the balance, readers will be captivated by the high-stakes drama and the profound impact this conference had on the modern world.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Diplomatic literature, Scientific discourse

Themes: International cooperation, Cultural differences, Scientific progress
