Synopsis: 
In 'The Philistine: A Periodical of Protest (Vol. II, No. 4, March 1896)', readers are transported to a world where art, literature, and social commentary collide. This captivating periodical takes a bold stance against societal norms, challenging readers to question their own beliefs and values. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, captivating stories, and striking illustrations, 'The Philistine' offers a unique perspective on the human condition and the power of individual expression. As readers delve into its pages, they will find themselves captivated by the audacity and brilliance of this literary gem.

Genres: Literary fiction, social commentary, art criticism

Themes: Individualism, societal norms, artistic expression, rebellion against conformity
