Synopsis: 
In "Ireland In The New Century," Sir Horace Curzon Plunkett takes readers on a captivating journey through the complex and evolving landscape of Ireland at the turn of the 20th century. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, Plunkett delves into the social, political, and economic challenges faced by the Irish people during this transformative period. From the struggles of rural farmers to the rise of nationalist movements, the author paints a rich tapestry of Ireland's past while offering thought-provoking perspectives on its future. As the narrative unfolds, readers will find themselves immersed in a world of passion, resilience, and the indomitable spirit of a nation on the brink of change.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political analysis, Social commentary

Themes: Nationalism, Agrarian reform, Identity, Socio-economic transformation
