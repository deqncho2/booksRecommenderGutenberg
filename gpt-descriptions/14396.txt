Synopsis: In 'His Family' by Ernest Poole, we are introduced to Roger Gale, a successful New York businessman who seemingly has it all. However, when a sudden tragedy strikes, Roger is forced to confront the fragility of his seemingly perfect life. As he navigates through personal and professional challenges, he begins to question the true meaning of success and happiness. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century America, this poignant novel explores the complexities of family, ambition, and the pursuit of a meaningful life.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Family Saga

Themes: Family dynamics, Ambition, Identity, Social change
