Synopsis: In 'Theism or Atheism: The Great Alternative', Cohen and Chapman embark on a thought-provoking journey that challenges the reader's beliefs and explores the fundamental questions of existence. Through a captivating blend of philosophy, science, and personal anecdotes, the authors delve into the age-old debate between theism and atheism, presenting compelling arguments from both sides. As the narrative unfolds, readers are invited to critically examine their own beliefs and consider the profound implications of their worldview. With its engaging storytelling and intellectual depth, this book offers a captivating exploration of the ultimate question: is there a higher power or are we alone in the universe?

Genres: Philosophy, Non-fiction, Religion

Themes: Existence, Belief, Worldview, Faith, Science
