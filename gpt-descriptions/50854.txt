Synopsis:
In "Mary of Burgundy; or, The Revolt of Ghent," the reader is transported to the tumultuous world of 15th-century Flanders, where political intrigue, forbidden love, and a city on the brink of rebellion intertwine. Mary, the young and headstrong Duchess of Burgundy, finds herself caught between her duty to her people and her heart's desires, as she navigates the treacherous waters of power and love. As the city of Ghent rises in revolt against her rule, Mary must make difficult choices that will shape the destiny of her kingdom and her own fate. With its rich historical backdrop and captivating characters, this gripping tale of love, loyalty, and rebellion will keep readers on the edge of their seats until the very last page.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Political Drama

Themes: Power and Politics, Love and Sacrifice, Rebellion and Revolution
