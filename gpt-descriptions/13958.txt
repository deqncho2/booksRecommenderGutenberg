Synopsis: In "The Authoritative Life of General William Booth, Founder of the Salvation Army," George S. Railton delves into the extraordinary life of General William Booth, the visionary behind the Salvation Army. From his humble beginnings to his relentless pursuit of social justice, Railton paints a vivid portrait of a man driven by compassion and a desire to uplift the marginalized. Through captivating anecdotes and historical accounts, the book explores Booth's unwavering dedication to his mission, his innovative approach to philanthropy, and the challenges he faced along the way. This biography offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of a remarkable individual who left an indelible mark on society.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Social justice, Philanthropy, Faith
