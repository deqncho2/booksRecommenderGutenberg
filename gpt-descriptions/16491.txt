Synopsis: In 'Vergilius: A Tale of the Coming of Christ' by Irving Bacheller, readers are transported to ancient Rome where they follow the journey of Vergilius, a renowned poet and philosopher. As Vergilius navigates the complexities of Roman society, he becomes increasingly disillusioned with the emptiness and corruption that surrounds him. However, a chance encounter with a mysterious stranger sets him on a path of self-discovery and spiritual awakening. Through his encounters with various characters and his own introspection, Vergilius grapples with questions of faith, love, and the true meaning of life. This captivating tale explores the transformative power of belief and the search for higher purpose in a world filled with darkness and uncertainty.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Spiritual Fiction, Philosophical Fiction

Themes: Faith, Redemption, Moral Awakening, Spiritual Journey
