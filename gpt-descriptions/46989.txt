Synopsis: 
"A Soldier's Experience; or, A Voice from the Ranks: Showing the Cost of War in Blood and Treasure" is a gripping personal narrative that takes readers on a journey through the Crimean Campaign, the Indian Mutiny, and the Afghan Campaigns of 1863. Written by T. Gowing, a soldier who experienced these conflicts firsthand, the book offers a unique perspective from the standpoint of the ranks. Through vivid storytelling, Gowing recounts the horrors of war, the atrocities witnessed, and the sacrifices made by soldiers on the frontlines. This captivating narrative sheds light on the true cost of war, both in terms of lives lost and the toll it takes on the human spirit.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature
Themes: War, Sacrifice, Human Resilience
