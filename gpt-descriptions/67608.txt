Synopsis: In "Betty Alden: The first-born daughter of the Pilgrims," Jane G. Austin takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Betty Alden, the first-born daughter of the Pilgrims. Set in the early 17th century, this historical novel follows Betty's experiences as she navigates the challenges of growing up in a new and unfamiliar land, forging her own path amidst the struggles and triumphs of the Plymouth Colony. From her encounters with Native Americans to her blossoming relationships, Betty's story is one of resilience, love, and the indomitable spirit of the early settlers.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Biographical Fiction

Themes: Identity, Cultural Encounter, Family Dynamics, Resilience
