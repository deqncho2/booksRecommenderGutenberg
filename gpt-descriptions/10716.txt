Synopsis: In 'The Epic: An Essay', Abercrombie, Lascelles takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of epic poetry, exploring its origins, evolution, and enduring significance. From the ancient epics of Homer to the modern works of T.S. Eliot, the author delves into the power of storytelling and the impact of these grand narratives on human culture. Through insightful analysis and engaging anecdotes, Abercrombie, Lascelles invites readers to discover the magic and timeless allure of the epic genre.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Essay

Themes: The power of storytelling, Evolution of literature, Cultural significance
