Synopsis: In "An Essay on the Slavery and Commerce of the Human Species, Particularly the African," Thomas Clarkson presents a thought-provoking exploration of the dark history of slavery and the African slave trade. Translated from a Latin dissertation that won the prestigious first prize at the University of Cambridge in 1785, this work delves into the intricate details of the slave trade, shedding light on its devastating impact on individuals and societies. With compelling additions, Clarkson's essay challenges readers to confront the moral and ethical implications of this abhorrent practice, urging them to question the very foundations of human commerce.

Genres: Historical, Political, Philosophical

Themes: Slavery, Human rights, Morality, Ethics
