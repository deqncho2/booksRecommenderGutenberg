Synopsis: 

In 'To-morrow and to-morrow ... a novel' by Stephen McKenna, a captivating tale unfolds as we follow the lives of two individuals from vastly different backgrounds. Set against the backdrop of early 20th century England, this novel takes us on a journey of love, ambition, and the pursuit of happiness. 

As the story unravels, we are introduced to a young, ambitious artist named Julian, who dreams of making a name for himself in the art world. On the other hand, we meet the charming and enigmatic Lady Cynthia, a woman trapped in a loveless marriage and yearning for something more. 

Their paths cross unexpectedly, leading to a passionate and forbidden love affair that challenges societal norms and tests the boundaries of their own desires. As they navigate the complexities of their relationship, they must confront their own personal demons and make difficult choices that will shape their futures.

With richly drawn characters and a vividly depicted historical setting, 'To-morrow and to-morrow ... a novel' explores themes of love, sacrifice, societal expectations, and the pursuit of personal fulfillment. This captivating work of fiction will leave readers eagerly turning the pages, eager to discover the fate of Julian and Lady Cynthia.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Drama
Themes: Love, Ambition, Societal Expectations, Personal Fulfillment
