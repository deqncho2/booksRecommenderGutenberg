Synopsis: In "The Ebbing Of The Tide: South Sea Stories - 1896" by Louis Becke, readers are transported to the enchanting South Seas, where tales of adventure, love, and danger unfold. From the treacherous waters of the Pacific to the remote islands inhabited by indigenous tribes, this collection of stories captures the essence of life in the South Seas during the late 19th century. With vivid descriptions and captivating characters, Becke weaves a tapestry of thrilling and poignant narratives that explore the complexities of human nature and the clash of cultures in this exotic setting.

Genres: Adventure, Historical Fiction, Short Stories

Themes: Exploration, Colonialism, Love and Desire, Cultural Encounters
