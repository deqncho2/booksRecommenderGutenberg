Synopsis: In 'A Cursory History of Swearing', Julian Sharman takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of profanity. From ancient civilizations to modern times, Sharman explores the origins, evolution, and cultural significance of swearing, revealing surprising insights and anecdotes along the way. Delving into the psychology behind swearing and its impact on society, this book offers a thought-provoking exploration of a taboo subject that is both entertaining and enlightening.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Linguistics

Themes: Language and communication, Cultural taboos, Social norms
