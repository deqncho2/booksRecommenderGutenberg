Synopsis: In "Daisy Miller: A Study," the charming and enigmatic Daisy Miller captivates the attention of the American expatriate community in Europe. As she navigates the social conventions and expectations of the time, her unconventional behavior and flirtatious nature become a subject of fascination and controversy. Through the eyes of the reserved Winterbourne, the reader is drawn into a tale of love, innocence, and the clash between American and European values.

Genres: Fiction, Novella, Realism

Themes: Cultural clash, Innocence and corruption, Social conventions, Gender roles
