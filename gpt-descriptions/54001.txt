Synopsis: 

"Il nipotismo di Roma, or, The History of the Popes Nephews: from the time of Sixtus IV. to the death of the last Pope, Alexander VII" by Gregorio Leti is a captivating historical account that delves into the intriguing world of the papal court in Rome. Focusing on the period from Sixtus IV to Alexander VII, Leti uncovers the hidden stories and scandals surrounding the popes' nephews, who wielded immense power and influence during this time. From political maneuverings to personal ambitions, this book unveils the captivating tales of nepotism, corruption, and the pursuit of power within the Vatican. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Nepotism, Power, Corruption
