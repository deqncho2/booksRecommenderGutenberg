Synopsis: 
'Essays on the Constitution of the United States: Published during its Discussion by the People, 1787-1788' takes readers on a captivating journey through the intense debates and discussions surrounding the creation of the United States Constitution. This collection of essays, written by various authors during the pivotal years of 1787-1788, offers a unique insight into the minds of the Founding Fathers and the passionate arguments that shaped the foundation of American democracy. From the clash of ideas to the exploration of fundamental principles, this book provides a fascinating exploration of the birth of a nation.

Genres: 
Historical, Political, Non-fiction

Themes: 
Constitutional law, Democracy, Founding principles, Political philosophy
