Synopsis: In "The Negro as a Soldier," Christian A. Fleetwood delves into the untold stories of African American soldiers during the American Civil War. Through vivid accounts and personal narratives, Fleetwood sheds light on the bravery, resilience, and sacrifices of these unsung heroes. From the battlefields to the struggles within their own ranks, this book uncovers the remarkable contributions of African American soldiers and challenges the prevailing stereotypes of the time. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, African American studies

Themes: Bravery, Resilience, Sacrifice, Racial inequality, Historical perspective
