Synopsis: In "Monday or Tuesday," Virginia Woolf takes readers on a mesmerizing journey through a collection of short stories that explore the depths of human emotions and the complexities of everyday life. From the vivid descriptions of nature to the intimate glimpses into the minds of her characters, Woolf weaves a tapestry of moments that capture the essence of existence. Each story is a snapshot, a fleeting glimpse into a world that is both familiar and enigmatic, leaving readers pondering the profound beauty and mystery of the human experience.

Genres: Modernist literature, short stories, experimental fiction

Themes: Perception of reality, the fleeting nature of time, the inner lives of individuals, the power of language and imagery, the beauty in the ordinary
