Synopsis: In 'Fors Clavigera (Volume 5 of 8): Letters to the workmen and labourers of Great Britain,' John Ruskin pens a series of thought-provoking letters addressed to the working class of his time. Through his eloquent and impassioned writing, Ruskin delves into various topics, including the importance of craftsmanship, the impact of industrialization, and the need for social and economic justice. With a blend of personal anecdotes, philosophical musings, and practical advice, Ruskin's letters offer a unique perspective on the challenges faced by the working class and the potential for positive change. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Epistolary literature

Themes: Craftsmanship, Industrialization, Social and economic justice, Working class struggles
