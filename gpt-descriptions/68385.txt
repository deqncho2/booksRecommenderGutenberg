Synopsis: 

In "Civilization in the United States: An inquiry by thirty Americans," a diverse group of thirty individuals embark on a thought-provoking journey to explore the concept of civilization in the United States. Through engaging discussions, personal anecdotes, and insightful observations, they delve into the complexities of American society, examining its history, culture, politics, and values. As they navigate through various regions and interact with people from all walks of life, they confront their own biases and preconceptions, challenging the reader to do the same. This captivating exploration offers a fresh perspective on what it truly means to be part of a civilization as complex and diverse as the United States.

Genres: Non-fiction, Sociology, Travelogue

Themes: American identity, Cultural diversity, Social dynamics, National character, Prejudice and stereotypes
