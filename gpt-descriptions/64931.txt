Synopsis: 

'Psychopathia sexualis: With especial reference to contrary sexual instinct: A medico-legal study' delves into the intriguing and often misunderstood world of human sexuality. Written by renowned psychiatrist Richard von Krafft-Ebing, this groundbreaking work explores the complexities of sexual behavior, focusing particularly on those individuals whose desires deviate from societal norms. Through a series of captivating case studies and meticulous analysis, Krafft-Ebing sheds light on the various manifestations of contrary sexual instinct, challenging conventional beliefs and offering a fresh perspective on the subject. 

Genres: Psychology, Medicine, Non-fiction

Themes: Human sexuality, Deviant behavior, Societal norms
