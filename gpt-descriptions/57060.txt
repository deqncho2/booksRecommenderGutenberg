Synopsis: In 'London Labour and the London Poor, Vol. 3', Henry Mayhew delves into the hidden world of Victorian London's underbelly, exploring the lives and struggles of the city's most marginalized inhabitants. From street performers and beggars to prostitutes and thieves, Mayhew uncovers the harsh realities and unique stories of these individuals, shedding light on a society often overlooked. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, this volume offers a captivating glimpse into the lives of those living on the fringes of society, revealing the resilience, ingenuity, and hardships they face in their daily lives.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Survival, Urban life
