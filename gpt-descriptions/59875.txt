Synopsis: This groundbreaking work by Granville, A. B. delves into the sensitive topics of abortion and menstruation, presenting a collection of vivid graphic illustrations. Through twelve plates engraved on stone and colored by the talented Mr. J. Perry, along with two copper-plates from the Philosophical Transactions, readers are exposed to forty-five specimens of aborted ova and other uterine abnormalities. Accompanied by preliminary observations, explanations, and anatomical and physiological remarks, this book offers a unique and comprehensive exploration of these controversial subjects.

Genres: Medical literature, Scientific illustration, Historical non-fiction

Themes: Reproductive health, Women's health, Medical ethics
