Synopsis: 

'Asiatic Cholera: A treatise on its origin, pathology, treatment, and cure' delves into the mysterious and deadly disease that has plagued humanity for centuries. Written by the renowned medical experts, A. B. Whitney and Elijah Whitney, this comprehensive treatise explores the origins of Asiatic cholera, its devastating effects on the human body, and the various treatment methods employed throughout history. From ancient remedies to modern medical breakthroughs, the authors provide a captivating account of the ongoing battle against this relentless epidemic. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical literature, Historical research

Themes: Disease and epidemics, Medical advancements, Historical perspectives
