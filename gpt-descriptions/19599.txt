Synopsis: 

In 'Troublous Times in Canada: A History of the Fenian Raids of 1866 and 1870', John A. MacDonald delves into the gripping and little-known events that unfolded during the Fenian Raids in Canada. As tensions rise between Canada and the Fenian Brotherhood, a secret society of Irish nationalists, MacDonald takes readers on a thrilling journey through the daring raids, political intrigue, and the resilience of the Canadian people. With vivid descriptions and meticulous research, MacDonald brings to life the untold stories of heroism, sacrifice, and the struggle for national identity in the face of external threats.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Political history

Themes: Nationalism, Identity, Conflict, Resilience
