Synopsis: In 'Handicaps of Childhood' by Bruce H. Addington, a young boy named Jack navigates the challenges and obstacles of growing up in a world that often underestimates him. From facing societal prejudices to overcoming personal limitations, Jack's journey is one of resilience, determination, and the power of the human spirit. As he discovers his own unique strengths and talents, Jack's story becomes an inspiring testament to the indomitable nature of childhood.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Drama

Themes: Resilience, Identity, Prejudice, Self-discovery
