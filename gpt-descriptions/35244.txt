Synopsis: In 'Eugenics as a Factor in the Prevention of Mental Disease', Horatio M. Pollock delves into the controversial topic of eugenics and its potential role in preventing mental illness. Through a thought-provoking exploration of scientific research and historical examples, Pollock challenges conventional notions and raises important questions about the ethical implications of eugenics. This compelling work offers a unique perspective on the intersection of genetics, mental health, and societal progress.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Psychology

Themes: Eugenics, Mental health, Genetics, Ethics
