Synopsis: 

In 'Rules and regulations governing maternity hospitals and homes ... September, 1922', the California State Board of Charities and Corrections delves into the intricate world of maternity hospitals and homes during a time of great change and societal expectations. Through a comprehensive exploration of the rules and regulations in place, the reader is taken on a journey that uncovers the challenges faced by both the institutions and the women seeking their services. From the strict guidelines governing admission to the protocols for medical care and discharge, this work sheds light on the complex dynamics of maternity care in early 20th century California.

Genres: Non-fiction, Historical, Healthcare

Themes: Women's rights, Healthcare regulations, Social norms and expectations
