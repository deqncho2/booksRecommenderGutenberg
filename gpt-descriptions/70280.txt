Synopsis: In "The Story of the Congo Free State," Henry Wellington Wack delves into the captivating social, political, and economic aspects of the Belgian system of government in Central Africa. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Wack uncovers the hidden truths and complexities of this colonial era, shedding light on the struggles and triumphs of the Congolese people. From the exploitation of natural resources to the clash of cultures, this book offers a thought-provoking exploration of a dark chapter in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political analysis, Cultural studies

Themes: Colonialism, Power dynamics, Exploitation, Cultural assimilation
