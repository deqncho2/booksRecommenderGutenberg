Synopsis: 

In "Hyde Park, Its History and Romance" by Alec-Tweedie, Mrs. (Ethel), readers are transported to the heart of London, where the iconic Hyde Park comes alive with its rich history and captivating tales. From the park's humble beginnings as a hunting ground to its transformation into a beloved public space, the author delves into the park's past, unearthing fascinating stories of royalty, political intrigue, and scandal. As readers explore the park's hidden corners and iconic landmarks, they are immersed in a world of romance, mystery, and the vibrant tapestry of London life. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travelogue, Biography

Themes: History, Romance, Urban life, British monarchy, Cultural heritage
