Synopsis: 

In "Mental Diseases: A Public Health Problem" by James Vance May, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the complex world of mental illnesses. This thought-provoking book delves into the various aspects of mental diseases, shedding light on their impact on individuals, families, and society as a whole. May skillfully explores the challenges faced by those affected by mental illnesses, the stigma surrounding these conditions, and the urgent need for a comprehensive public health approach to address this growing problem. Through compelling narratives and insightful analysis, May presents a compelling case for the importance of understanding and addressing mental diseases as a critical public health issue.

Genres: Non-fiction, Psychology, Public Health

Themes: Mental health, Stigma, Public health intervention, Societal impact
