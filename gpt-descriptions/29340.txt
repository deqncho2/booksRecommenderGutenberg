Synopsis: 

In 'The Story of the Great War, Volume 4: Champagne, Artois, Grodno; Fall of Nish; Caucasus; Mesopotamia; Development of Air Strategy; United States and the War', the reader is taken on a gripping journey through the various theaters of World War I. From the intense battles in Champagne and Artois to the fall of Nish and the strategic developments in air warfare, this volume provides a comprehensive account of the war's progression. The narrative also delves into the lesser-known conflicts in the Caucasus and Mesopotamia, shedding light on the global scale of the war. Furthermore, the book explores the involvement of the United States, highlighting its impact on the conflict.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War literature

Themes: War and its consequences, Global conflict, Technological advancements, International alliances
