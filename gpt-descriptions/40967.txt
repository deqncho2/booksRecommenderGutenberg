Synopsis: In the second volume of 'The Trial of Jesus from a Lawyer's Standpoint', author Walter M. Chandler delves into the Roman trial of Jesus, providing a unique perspective on the legal proceedings that led to his crucifixion. Through meticulous research and analysis, Chandler uncovers fascinating details about the Roman legal system, the roles of Pontius Pilate and other key figures, and the political pressures that influenced the outcome of the trial. As the trial unfolds, the reader is taken on a gripping journey through the courtroom, witnessing the clash between justice and power, and the profound implications it has on history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Legal analysis, Religious studies

Themes: Justice and power, Political influence, Historical significance
