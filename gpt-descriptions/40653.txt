Synopsis: In 'A Will and No Will; or, A Bone for the Lawyers' and 'The New Play Criticiz'd, or the Plague of Envy', Macklin presents two satirical plays that delve into the world of lawyers and the theater. In the first play, a will becomes the center of a legal battle, leading to a series of humorous and unexpected events. In the second play, Macklin explores the envy and criticism that often plague the theater industry. Both works offer a witty and entertaining critique of these professions, filled with clever dialogue and amusing situations.

Genres: Comedy, Satire

Themes: Legal disputes, Envy, Criticism, Theater industry
