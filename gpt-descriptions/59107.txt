Synopsis: In 'Aristophanes' by Collins, W. Lucas, readers are transported to ancient Greece, where they are introduced to the life and works of the renowned playwright Aristophanes. Through a captivating narrative, the author delves into the fascinating world of comedy, politics, and social commentary that Aristophanes masterfully crafted in his plays. From the uproarious humor to the biting satire, this book offers a glimpse into the mind of a comedic genius and the impact his works had on the society of his time.

Genres: Biography, History, Literary Criticism

Themes: Comedy, Politics, Social Commentary
