Synopsis: In 'The Right to Be Lazy, and Other Studies', Lafargue challenges the prevailing notion of the virtue of hard work and advocates for the importance of leisure and idleness. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, he explores the detrimental effects of overwork on individuals and society, offering a radical perspective on the value of laziness and the need for a more balanced approach to life. From critiquing the capitalist system to examining the impact of industrialization, Lafargue's work presents a compelling argument for reevaluating our relationship with work and embracing the right to be lazy.

Genres: Political philosophy, social criticism, essay collection

Themes: Work ethic, leisure, capitalism, industrialization, societal norms
