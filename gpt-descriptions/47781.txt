Synopsis: In "Main Currents in Nineteenth Century Literature - 2. The Romantic School in Germany," Georg Brandes takes readers on a captivating journey through the vibrant and tumultuous world of German Romanticism. From the enchanting landscapes of the Rhine to the depths of the human soul, Brandes explores the lives and works of influential German writers such as Goethe, Schiller, and Novalis. Through his insightful analysis, he unveils the passionate ideals, the struggles for artistic freedom, and the profound impact of this literary movement on German society. As Brandes delves into the lives of these literary giants, he uncovers the intricate web of love, longing, and rebellion that shaped their works and their era.

Genres: Literary criticism, Biography, History

Themes: Romanticism, Artistic freedom, Love and longing, Rebellion, German literature
