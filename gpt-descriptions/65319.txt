Synopsis: In 'Golf Architecture: Economy in Course Construction and Green-Keeping', Alexander Mackenzie takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of golf course design and maintenance. With a keen eye for detail and a passion for the sport, Mackenzie explores the art of creating stunning golf courses while keeping costs and environmental impact in check. From strategic bunker placement to the delicate balance of nature and man-made elements, this book offers a fascinating insight into the intricate world of golf architecture.

Genres: Non-fiction, Sports, Architecture

Themes: Golf course design, Sustainability, Environmental impact, Cost-effective construction
