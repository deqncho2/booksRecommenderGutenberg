Synopsis: In 'The Epic of Hades, in Three Books' by Morris, Lewis, embark on a captivating journey through the realms of Greek mythology as Hades, the enigmatic ruler of the Underworld, reveals his untold story. As the narrative unfolds, we witness the clash of gods and mortals, the complexities of love and power, and the eternal struggle between darkness and light. With vivid imagery and poetic prose, this epic tale invites readers to explore the depths of the human soul and question the boundaries of life and death.

Genres: Epic Poetry, Mythology, Fantasy

Themes: Power and Control, Love and Desire, Life and Death
