Synopsis: In 'Not Guilty: A Defence of the Bottom Dog', Robert Blatchford takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the underbelly of society, challenging conventional notions of justice and morality. Through a series of gripping anecdotes and compelling arguments, Blatchford sheds light on the struggles faced by the marginalized and oppressed, ultimately questioning the very foundations of our social order. With a keen eye for detail and a powerful voice, the author invites readers to reevaluate their preconceptions and consider the true nature of guilt and innocence.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Philosophy

Themes: Social inequality, Justice, Morality, Empathy
