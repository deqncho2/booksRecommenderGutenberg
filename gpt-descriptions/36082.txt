Synopsis: In 'White House China of the Lincoln Administration in the Museum of History and Technology', Margaret Brown Klapthor takes readers on a captivating journey through the exquisite collection of china used during Abraham Lincoln's presidency. From the delicate patterns to the historical significance behind each piece, Klapthor unveils the hidden stories and intricate craftsmanship that adorned the White House during one of the most pivotal periods in American history. Through her meticulous research and vivid descriptions, she brings to life the elegance and grandeur of the Lincoln administration, offering a unique perspective on the personal and political aspects of the time.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Art history

Themes: Presidential history, Material culture, Symbolism
