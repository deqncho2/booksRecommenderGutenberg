Synopsis: In "A Short History of Rhode Island," George Washington Greene takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and often overlooked history of Rhode Island. From the early days of European settlement to the American Revolution and beyond, Greene weaves together a tapestry of fascinating stories, colorful characters, and pivotal events that shaped the smallest state in the United States. With a keen eye for detail and a knack for storytelling, Greene brings to life the struggles, triumphs, and unique cultural heritage of Rhode Island, offering readers a fresh perspective on this often underestimated corner of American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, American history

Themes: Colonialism, Religious freedom, Independence, Cultural diversity
