Synopsis: 

'West Port Murders: Or an Authentic Account of the Atrocious Murders Committed by Burke and His Associates' is a gripping true crime account that delves into the horrifying crimes committed by William Burke and his associates in 19th century Edinburgh. This book provides a detailed and chilling narrative of the murders, including the extraordinary circumstances surrounding them. It also offers a comprehensive report of the trial of Burke and M'Dougal, along with a description of Burke's execution, his shocking confessions, and the memoirs of his accomplices, including the proceedings against Hare. 

Genres: True Crime, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Crime and Punishment, Morality, Justice
