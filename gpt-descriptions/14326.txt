Synopsis: In 'Ulster's Stand For Union' by Ronald McNeill, a gripping tale unfolds as the author delves into the historical events surrounding the contentious issue of Irish Home Rule. Set against the backdrop of early 20th century Ireland, the story follows a diverse cast of characters who find themselves caught in the crossfire of political turmoil, personal loyalties, and the fight for national identity. As tensions rise and alliances are tested, the fate of Ulster hangs in the balance, making this a captivating exploration of the struggle for power and the resilience of a people.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Drama, War

Themes: Nationalism, Loyalty, Identity, Power Struggles
