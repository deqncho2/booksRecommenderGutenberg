Synopsis: 
In "On the Border with Crook," John Gregory Bourke takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed American West during the late 19th century. As a trusted aide to General George Crook, Bourke provides a firsthand account of the thrilling adventures, dangerous encounters, and complex relationships that defined the era. From skirmishes with Native American tribes to the challenges of frontier life, Bourke's vivid descriptions and insightful observations paint a vivid picture of a tumultuous time in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Adventure

Themes: Frontier life, Native American relations, Military campaigns, Exploration of the American West
