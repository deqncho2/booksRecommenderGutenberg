Synopsis:
'The Philippine Islands, 1493-1898: Volume 32, 1640' takes readers on a captivating journey through the exploration of the Philippine Islands by early navigators. It delves into vivid descriptions of the islands and their diverse peoples, providing a comprehensive account of their history and the records of Catholic missions. Drawing from contemporaneous books and manuscripts, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the political, economic, commercial, and religious conditions of the islands from their earliest interactions with European nations up until the close of the nineteenth century.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Exploration, Anthropology

Themes:
Colonialism, Cultural exchange, Religious missions, Historical documentation
