Synopsis:
In 'Manners and Social Usages', Mary Elizabeth Wilson Sherwood takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricacies of social etiquette. From the proper way to address a letter to the art of conversation, Sherwood provides a comprehensive guide to navigating the complex world of social interactions. With a delightful blend of wit and wisdom, she offers invaluable advice on how to make a favorable impression, handle delicate situations, and gracefully navigate the ever-changing social landscape. Whether you are attending a formal dinner or simply engaging in everyday interactions, this book is a must-read for anyone seeking to master the art of good manners.

Genres: Self-help, Etiquette, Non-fiction

Themes: Social etiquette, Communication, Personal development
