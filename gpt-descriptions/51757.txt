Synopsis: In "Samuel Pepys and the World He Lived In," Henry B. Wheatley takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and times of Samuel Pepys, a prominent figure in 17th-century England. Through vivid descriptions and meticulous research, Wheatley paints a vivid picture of the bustling city of London, the political intrigues of the Restoration era, and the personal triumphs and tribulations of Pepys himself. From his passionate love affairs to his encounters with famous historical figures, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of a man who witnessed and shaped a pivotal period in English history.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: 17th-century England, Restoration era, Personal and political intrigue, Historical figures, London society
