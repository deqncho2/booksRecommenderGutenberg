Synopsis: 

In the second volume of the 'Cyclopedia of Telephony and Telegraphy', the American School of Correspondence delves deeper into the fascinating world of telephony and telegraphy. This comprehensive reference work offers a wealth of knowledge and practical information, covering a wide range of topics including the history, principles, and advancements in communication technology. From the early days of Alexander Graham Bell's invention to the latest developments in wireless communication, this volume provides a captivating exploration of the evolution of telephony and telegraphy. Packed with detailed illustrations, diagrams, and explanations, readers will discover the inner workings of these revolutionary technologies and gain a deeper understanding of their impact on society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Reference, Technology

Themes: Communication technology, History of telephony and telegraphy, Advancements in communication
