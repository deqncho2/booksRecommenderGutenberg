Synopsis: In 'The Comedy of Errors: The Works of William Shakespeare [Cambridge Edition] [9 vols.],' Shakespeare takes us on a hilarious journey filled with mistaken identities, misunderstandings, and uproarious chaos. Set in the bustling city of Ephesus, the story follows two sets of identical twins who were separated at birth. As fate brings them together in the same city, their uncanny resemblance leads to a series of comical mix-ups, confusion, and absurd situations. With witty wordplay, slapstick humor, and a touch of romance, this play showcases Shakespeare's mastery of comedic storytelling.

Genres: Comedy, Farce, Drama

Themes: Identity, Mistaken Identity, Family, Love, Deception
