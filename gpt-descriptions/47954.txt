Synopsis: In "The Old World in the New: The Significance of Past and Present Immigration to the American People," Edward Alsworth Ross delves into the complex and fascinating history of immigration in the United States. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Ross explores the profound impact that immigration has had on the American people, both in the past and present. From the struggles and triumphs of early settlers to the challenges faced by contemporary immigrants, this thought-provoking work sheds light on the diverse experiences and contributions that have shaped the nation. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: Immigration, Cultural assimilation, Identity, Nationalism
