Synopsis: In "Nationality and Race from an Anthropologist's Point of View," Sir Arthur Keith delivers a captivating lecture that explores the complex relationship between nationality and race. Drawing on his expertise as an anthropologist, Keith delves into the origins and evolution of different races, challenging conventional notions and shedding light on the interconnectedness of human populations. Through thought-provoking analysis and compelling examples, he invites readers to question their preconceived notions about race and nationality, ultimately offering a fresh perspective on this fascinating subject.

Genres: Non-fiction, Anthropology, Sociology

Themes: Race, Nationality, Anthropology, Human Diversity, Cultural Identity
