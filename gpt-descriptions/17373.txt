Synopsis: In 'The Madonna in Art', Estelle M. Hurll takes readers on a captivating journey through the centuries, exploring the depiction of the Madonna in various forms of art. From the early Renaissance to the modern era, Hurll delves into the symbolism, emotions, and cultural significance behind these iconic representations. Through vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, she unveils the evolution of the Madonna's portrayal, revealing the profound impact she has had on both artists and society. This book is a visual feast that will leave readers with a deeper appreciation for the timeless beauty and spiritual power of the Madonna.

Genres: Art History, Religious Studies, Cultural Studies

Themes: Religious Iconography, Femininity, Devotion, Cultural Influence
