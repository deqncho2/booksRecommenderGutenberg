Synopsis: In the second volume of "William Cobbett: A Biography," author Edward Smith delves deeper into the life and accomplishments of the enigmatic figure, William Cobbett. This captivating biography takes readers on a journey through Cobbett's political career, his controversial writings, and his relentless pursuit of justice. As the narrative unfolds, Smith uncovers the untold stories and hidden motivations behind Cobbett's actions, shedding light on the complex character of this influential 19th-century figure. From his early struggles to his rise as a prominent journalist and politician, this volume offers a fascinating exploration of Cobbett's life and legacy.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Political activism, Freedom of speech, Social justice
