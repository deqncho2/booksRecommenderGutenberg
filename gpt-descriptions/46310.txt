Synopsis: In the second part of his memoirs, Charles Greville continues his captivating journal chronicling the reign of Queen Victoria from 1837 to 1852. As a close observer of the royal court, Greville provides an intimate and revealing account of the political intrigues, scandals, and personal relationships that shaped this transformative era. From the Queen's ascension to the challenges of governing a rapidly changing nation, Greville's memoirs offer a unique perspective on the inner workings of power and the complexities of Victorian society.

Genres: Memoir, History, Biography

Themes: Politics, Monarchy, Society, Power, Personal Relationships
