Synopsis: 

'A Treatise on Meteorological Instruments: Explanatory of Their Scientific Principles, Method of Construction, and Practical Utility' takes readers on a fascinating journey into the world of meteorology and the instruments that enable us to understand and predict the weather. Written by Enrico Angelo Lodovico Negretti and Joseph Zambra, this comprehensive guide explores the scientific principles behind meteorological instruments, delves into their construction methods, and highlights their practical applications. From barometers to anemometers, readers will gain a deeper understanding of how these instruments work and their significance in the field of meteorology. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Reference

Themes: Meteorology, Scientific principles, Instrumentation, Weather forecasting
