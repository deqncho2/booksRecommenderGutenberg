Synopsis: In "Simon Magus: An Essay on the Founder of Simonianism Based on the Ancient Sources With a Re-Evaluation of His Philosophy and Teachings," G.R.S. Mead delves into the enigmatic figure of Simon Magus, a controversial character from the early days of Christianity. Drawing from ancient sources, Mead explores the life, philosophy, and teachings of Simon Magus, shedding light on his influence and the impact he had on the development of early Christian thought. This thought-provoking essay offers a fresh perspective on a figure often misunderstood and overlooked in history.

Genres: Historical, Religious, Philosophical

Themes: Early Christianity, Gnosticism, Heresy, Religious Syncretism
