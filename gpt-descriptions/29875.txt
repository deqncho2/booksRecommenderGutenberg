Synopsis: 
'Dreamers of the Ghetto' by Israel Zangwill is a captivating novel that follows the lives of several Jewish families living in the East End of London during the late 19th century. The story revolves around the struggles, dreams, and aspirations of these individuals as they navigate the challenges of poverty, discrimination, and cultural identity. Through their intertwined lives, the novel explores themes of love, sacrifice, social inequality, and the pursuit of dreams in the face of adversity.

Genres: 
Historical Fiction, Social Realism, Family Drama

Themes: 
Love, Identity, Social Inequality, Cultural Assimilation, Dreams and Aspirations
