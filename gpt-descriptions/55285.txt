Synopsis: 

In 'Lives of Famous London Beggars: With Forty Portraits of the Most Remarkable,' author John Thomas Smith takes readers on a captivating journey through the gritty streets of London, introducing them to a cast of beggars who have become legends in their own right. From the enigmatic "King of the Beggars" to the cunning "Queen of the Gypsies," Smith's vivid descriptions and accompanying portraits bring these larger-than-life characters to life. As the stories unfold, readers are transported into a world of hardship, resilience, and unexpected triumphs, where the line between survival and artistry blurs. 

Genres: Biography, History, Art

Themes: Poverty, Survival, Identity
