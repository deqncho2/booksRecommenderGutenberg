Synopsis: In 'The Idea of a University Defined and Illustrated: In Nine Discourses Delivered to the Catholics of Dublin', John Henry Newman explores the concept of a university and its purpose in society. Through a series of insightful discourses, he delves into the importance of liberal education, the role of religion in intellectual pursuits, and the cultivation of a well-rounded individual. With eloquence and depth, Newman challenges conventional notions of education and offers a compelling vision of what a university should strive to be.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, Education

Themes: Liberal education, Religion and intellectual pursuits, Personal development, The purpose of a university
