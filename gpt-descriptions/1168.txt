Synopsis: In 'The Pool in the Desert' by Sara Jeannette Duncan, a young woman named Mary returns to her childhood home in the Canadian prairies after years of living abroad. As she reconnects with her family and the familiar landscape, she becomes entangled in a web of secrets and hidden desires. The discovery of a mysterious pool in the desert leads Mary on a journey of self-discovery and forbidden love, challenging societal norms and her own sense of identity. Set against the backdrop of the vast and unforgiving prairie, this captivating novel explores the complexities of family, love, and the pursuit of personal fulfillment.

Genres: Fiction, Romance, Historical

Themes: Identity, Forbidden Love, Family Dynamics, Societal Expectations
