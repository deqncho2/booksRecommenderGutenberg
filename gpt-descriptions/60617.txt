Synopsis: In "The Rise and Fall of Prohibition: The Human Side of What the Eighteenth Amendment and the Volstead Act Have Done to the United States," Charles Hanson Towne takes readers on a captivating journey through one of the most transformative periods in American history. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Towne explores the human impact of Prohibition, delving into the lives of both the proponents and opponents of this controversial era. From the rise of bootleggers and speakeasies to the struggles of law enforcement and the devastating consequences on society, this book offers a compelling and nuanced perspective on the complex web of events that shaped the United States during the Prohibition era.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, social history, cultural studies

Themes: Power and corruption, social change, individual freedoms, the impact of legislation on society
