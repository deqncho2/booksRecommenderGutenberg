Synopsis: In "What the Negro Has Done for Liberty in America," Professor John Moore delves into the often overlooked contributions of African Americans in the fight for liberty and equality in America. Through a captivating exploration of historical events and personal narratives, Moore sheds light on the pivotal role played by African Americans in shaping the nation's history. From the abolitionist movement to the Civil Rights era, this thought-provoking work uncovers the untold stories of courage, resilience, and determination that have shaped the American landscape.

Genres: History, African American Studies, Social Justice

Themes: African American history, Civil Rights, Equality, Social activism
