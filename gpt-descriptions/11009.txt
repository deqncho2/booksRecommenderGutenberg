Synopsis: 

"Labor's Martyrs: Haymarket 1887, Sacco and Vanzetti 1927" by Marcantonio, Vito delves into two pivotal moments in history where the fight for workers' rights clashed with the forces of power and injustice. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, the author uncovers the gripping stories of the Haymarket Affair in 1887 and the controversial trial of Sacco and Vanzetti in 1927. These two cases, separated by four decades, become intertwined as the book explores the lives, struggles, and ultimate sacrifices of those who fought for justice and equality. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Political history

Themes: Labor rights, Social justice, Political activism
