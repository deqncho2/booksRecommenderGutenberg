Synopsis: 
In 'Publications of the Scottish History Society, Volume 36: Journals of Sir John Lauder Lord Fountainhall with His Observations on Public Affairs and Other Memoranda 1665-1676', readers are transported back to 17th-century Scotland through the personal journals of Sir John Lauder, Lord Fountainhall. This captivating collection of writings offers a unique glimpse into the political and social landscape of the time, as Fountainhall shares his astute observations on public affairs and other intriguing memoranda. From political intrigues and legal disputes to personal reflections and societal changes, this volume presents a rich tapestry of historical events and personal insights that will leave readers eager to uncover the secrets and revelations hidden within Fountainhall's journals.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Political commentary

Themes: Scottish history, Politics, Society, Personal reflections
