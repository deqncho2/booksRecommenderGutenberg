Synopsis: 

In 'Educated working women: Essays on the economic position of women workers in the middle classes', Clara E. Collet delves into the lives of educated women in the middle classes, exploring their economic position and the challenges they face in the workforce. Through a series of insightful essays, Collet sheds light on the experiences of these women, their struggles for equal pay and opportunities, and the impact of societal expectations on their careers. As she uncovers the complexities of their lives, Collet invites readers to question traditional gender roles and consider the potential for change in a rapidly evolving society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Feminist literature, Social commentary

Themes: Gender inequality, Women's economic empowerment, Middle-class struggles, Societal expectations, Feminism
