Synopsis:
In "Swann's Way," Marcel Proust takes us on a mesmerizing journey through the memories and reflections of a young boy growing up in a wealthy French family. As the narrator delves into his past, he explores the intricate web of his own desires, the complexities of love, and the power of art to capture the essence of life. Through vivid descriptions and introspective musings, Proust invites us to immerse ourselves in the intricate tapestry of human emotions and the profound impact of our past experiences.

Genres:
Literary Fiction, Autobiographical Fiction, Psychological Fiction

Themes:
Memory, Time, Love, Art, Desire
