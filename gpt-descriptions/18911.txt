Synopsis: In this captivating lecture delivered at Columbia University in 1907, Edmund B. Wilson explores the intricate world of biology, unraveling the mysteries of life and its origins. With a keen eye for detail and a passion for scientific inquiry, Wilson takes readers on a journey through the fascinating realms of cells, organisms, and evolution. From the smallest microorganisms to the complex ecosystems that shape our planet, he delves into the interconnectedness of all living things, shedding light on the delicate balance of nature. With thought-provoking insights and captivating anecdotes, Wilson's lecture offers a captivating exploration of the wonders of biology.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Lecture

Themes: Life, Evolution, Interconnectedness, Nature
