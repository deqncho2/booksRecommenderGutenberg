Synopsis: "Sterminator Vesevo" (Vesuvius the great exterminator): Diary of the Eruption of April 1906 by Matilde Serao is a gripping account of the catastrophic eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 1906. Through the eyes of the protagonist, the reader is transported to the heart of the disaster, experiencing the terror, chaos, and resilience of the people caught in its path. As the eruption unfolds, secrets are revealed, relationships tested, and the true nature of humanity is laid bare. This diary-like narrative captures the raw emotions and vivid imagery of the eruption, leaving readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: Historical fiction, disaster fiction, diary/narrative

Themes: Natural disasters, human resilience, the fragility of life, the power of nature, the impact of secrets.
