Synopsis: In 'Have We No Rights?', Mabel Williamson fearlessly delves into the controversial topic of the "rights" of missionaries, challenging conventional notions and shedding light on the sacrifices and challenges they face. Through personal anecdotes and thought-provoking insights, Williamson explores the delicate balance between self-sacrifice and self-care, ultimately questioning the very definition of rights in the context of missionary work. This captivating exploration will leave readers questioning their own beliefs and perceptions about the rights of those who dedicate their lives to spreading the gospel.

Genres: Non-fiction, Christian literature, Memoir

Themes: Missionary work, Self-sacrifice, Human rights, Faith, Cultural adaptation
