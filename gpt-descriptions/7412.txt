Synopsis: 
'Coningsby; Or, The New Generation' by Benjamin Disraeli, Earl of Beaconsfield, is a captivating novel that takes readers on a journey through the political and social landscape of 19th-century England. The story revolves around the life of Harry Coningsby, a young man who finds himself thrust into the world of politics and power. As he navigates the complexities of society, Coningsby encounters a cast of intriguing characters, including the enigmatic and influential figure, Lord Monmouth. With themes of ambition, love, and the clash between old and new generations, this novel offers a compelling exploration of the political and personal struggles faced by the young protagonist.

Genres: 
Political fiction, Bildungsroman, Historical fiction

Themes: 
Ambition, Love, Generational conflict
