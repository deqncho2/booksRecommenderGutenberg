Synopsis: 

In 'Children of China' by C. Campbell Brown, a gripping tale unfolds as a young American journalist finds herself immersed in the heart of China's complex and mysterious culture. Drawn to the stories of abandoned children and the enigmatic world of Chinese orphanages, she embarks on a journey that will challenge her perceptions and ignite a passion for change. As she delves deeper into the lives of these children, she uncovers shocking secrets and faces unexpected obstacles that will test her determination and resilience. With each encounter, she becomes more entangled in the lives of these children, ultimately leading her to question her own identity and purpose. 

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Cultural

Themes: Identity, Cultural Differences, Social Justice
