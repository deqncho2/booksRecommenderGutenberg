Synopsis: 

In 'Wild Ducks: How to Rear and Shoot Them' by Oates, W. Coape, readers are taken on an exhilarating journey into the world of wild ducks, exploring the art of rearing and shooting these magnificent creatures. Through vivid descriptions and expert advice, the author delves into the intricacies of raising wild ducks, their behavior, and the techniques required to successfully hunt them. From the thrill of the chase to the satisfaction of a well-aimed shot, this book offers a captivating exploration of the relationship between humans and nature's most elusive waterfowl.

Genres: Non-fiction, Outdoor and Recreational, Hunting

Themes: Wildlife conservation, Hunting ethics, Nature appreciation
