Synopsis: In "Greece and Babylon: A Comparative Sketch of Mesopotamian, Anatolian and Hellenic Religions," Farnell delves into the fascinating world of ancient civilizations, exploring the religious practices and beliefs of three influential cultures. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Farnell uncovers striking similarities and intriguing differences between the religious traditions of Greece, Babylon, Mesopotamia, and Anatolia. From the worship of powerful deities to the rituals and ceremonies that shaped their societies, this comparative sketch offers a captivating journey into the spiritual realms of these ancient civilizations.

Genres: Historical, Comparative Religion, Archaeology

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Religious practices, Cultural exchange
