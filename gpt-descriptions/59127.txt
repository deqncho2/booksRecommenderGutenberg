Synopsis: 

"A Memoir of Robert Blincoe, an Orphan Boy" is a gripping and heart-wrenching account of a young boy's journey from the workhouse of St. Pancras, London, to the horrors of a cotton-mill. Sent away at the tender age of seven, Robert endures unimaginable sufferings throughout his infancy and youth. This memoir provides a minute and detailed exploration of his harrowing experiences, making it the first of its kind to be published.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Orphanhood, Child Labor, Social Injustice, Resilience
