Synopsis: 
'The Drama Of Three Hundred & Sixty-Five Days: Scenes In The Great War' takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of World War I. Written by Hall Caine, a renowned British author, this book presents a collection of vivid scenes that capture the essence of the war, from the trenches to the home front. Through the eyes of various characters, readers witness the horrors, sacrifices, and resilience of those caught in the midst of this global conflict. As the days unfold, the narrative weaves together personal stories, political intrigue, and the devastating impact of war on individuals and nations. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Drama, Epic

Themes: War and its consequences, Human resilience, Sacrifice, Political intrigue
