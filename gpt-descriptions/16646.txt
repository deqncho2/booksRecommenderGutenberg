Synopsis: 
In the second volume of 'The Letters of Elizabeth Barrett Browning', readers are invited into the intimate world of one of the most celebrated poets of the Victorian era. Through a collection of personal letters, Elizabeth Barrett Browning shares her thoughts, emotions, and experiences, offering a captivating glimpse into her life as a writer, a wife, and a woman. From her passionate love affair with fellow poet Robert Browning to her struggles with illness and societal expectations, these letters paint a vivid portrait of a remarkable woman who defied conventions and found her voice through her poetry.

Genres: Biography, Letters, Poetry

Themes: Love and Romance, Gender Roles, Artistic Expression, Illness and Resilience
