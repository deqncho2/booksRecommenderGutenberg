Synopsis: 

In 'A Residence in France During the Years 1792, 1793, 1794 and 1795, Part II., 1793: Described in a Series of Letters from an English Lady: with General and Incidental Remarks on the French Character and Manners', Charlotte Biggs takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years of the French Revolution. Through a series of letters, an English lady recounts her experiences and observations while living in France during this period of intense political and social upheaval. As she navigates the complexities of French society, the reader is drawn into a world of danger, intrigue, and personal transformation. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Epistolary Novel

Themes: Revolution, Identity, Cultural Differences
