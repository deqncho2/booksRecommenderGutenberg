Synopsis: In "The Tithe-Proctor: The Works of William Carleton, Volume Two," William Carleton takes readers on a captivating journey through the rural landscapes of Ireland. Set in the early 19th century, the story follows the life of a young tithe-proctor, a controversial figure responsible for collecting tithes from impoverished farmers. As the protagonist navigates the complexities of his role, he becomes entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and social unrest. Carleton's vivid descriptions and compelling characters bring to life the struggles and triumphs of a society on the brink of change.

Genres: Historical fiction, Drama, Romance

Themes: Social inequality, Love and betrayal, Cultural clashes, Rural life
