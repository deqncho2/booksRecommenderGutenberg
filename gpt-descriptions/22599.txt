Synopsis: In 'The Hindu-Arabic Numerals', Smith and Karpinski take us on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of numbers. From ancient civilizations to the groundbreaking discoveries of mathematicians, this book unveils the secrets behind the numerical system we use today. Delving into the cultural, religious, and scientific influences that shaped the development of these numerals, the authors shed light on the profound impact they have had on human civilization. With engaging anecdotes and thought-provoking insights, 'The Hindu-Arabic Numerals' is a captivating exploration of the origins and significance of our numerical language.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Mathematics

Themes: Cultural influences, Scientific discoveries, Evolution of numerical systems
