Synopsis: 

In 'American Pomology. Apples', author J. A. Warder takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of apples, exploring their history, cultivation, and the vast array of varieties that exist. From the origins of the apple tree to the intricacies of grafting and hybridization, Warder's meticulous research and engaging storytelling make this book a fascinating read for anyone interested in the world of pomology. Delving into the characteristics, flavors, and uses of different apple varieties, the author provides valuable insights for both amateur gardeners and professional horticulturists. With vivid descriptions and beautiful illustrations, 'American Pomology. Apples' is a delightful exploration of one of nature's most beloved fruits.

Genres: Non-fiction, Horticulture, Botany

Themes: History of apples, Cultivation techniques, Apple varieties, Pomology
