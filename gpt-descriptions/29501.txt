Synopsis: 

In 'Negro Migration during the War', Emmett J. Scott delves into the untold story of African American migration during World War I. Through vivid narratives and compelling firsthand accounts, Scott explores the profound impact of the war on the African American community, revealing the struggles, triumphs, and resilience of those who sought new opportunities in the face of adversity. From the bustling streets of northern cities to the battlefields of Europe, this captivating work sheds light on a pivotal moment in history that forever changed the course of African American life.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, African American studies, War history

Themes: Migration, Racism, Identity, Social change
