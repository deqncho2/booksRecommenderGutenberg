Synopsis: In 'The Pecan and its Culture' by Hume, H. Harold, embark on a fascinating journey into the world of pecans, where the humble nut takes center stage. From its origins in ancient civilizations to its modern-day significance, this captivating exploration delves into the secrets of pecan cultivation, revealing surprising facts and techniques that will leave readers in awe. As the story unfolds, readers will discover the untold history, the intricate science, and the passionate individuals who have dedicated their lives to the pecan's cultivation. Prepare to be enchanted by the pecan's rich heritage and its profound impact on culture, cuisine, and commerce.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Agriculture

Themes: Food and culture, Horticulture, Agricultural practices
