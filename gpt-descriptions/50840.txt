Synopsis: In "Higher Education for Women in Great Britain," Phoebe Sheavyn delves into the fascinating history of women's pursuit of knowledge and academic opportunities in Britain. From the struggles faced by early pioneers to the triumphs of the suffrage movement, this book uncovers the untold stories of women who fought for their right to education. Through captivating anecdotes and meticulous research, Sheavyn paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs that shaped the landscape of higher education for women in Great Britain.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Women's Studies

Themes: Gender equality, Education, Social progress
