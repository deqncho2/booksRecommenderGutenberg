Synopsis: 

'Little Books About Old Furniture. Volume II. The Period of Queen Anne' takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of antique furniture during the reign of Queen Anne. Written by J. P. Blake and A. E. Reveirs-Hopkins, this volume delves into the intricate details and historical significance of furniture from this period, offering a wealth of knowledge and insights. From elegant chairs and exquisite tables to ornate cabinets and delicate decorations, the authors explore the craftsmanship, styles, and influences that shaped Queen Anne furniture. With vivid descriptions and beautiful illustrations, this book invites readers to discover the allure and timeless beauty of these cherished pieces of history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Antique furniture, Queen Anne period, Craftsmanship, Historical significance
