Synopsis: In "The Life and Times of Kateri Tekakwitha, the Lily of the Mohawks," Ellen H. Walworth delves into the captivating story of a young Mohawk woman who defied societal norms and embraced her faith in the face of adversity. Set in the 17th century, this historical biography takes readers on a journey through Kateri Tekakwitha's remarkable life, from her early encounters with European settlers to her spiritual awakening and ultimate legacy. As Kateri navigates the complexities of her dual heritage, readers are transported to a world where cultural clashes, personal struggles, and spiritual devotion intertwine to shape the destiny of a remarkable young woman.

Genres: Biography, Historical Fiction

Themes: Cultural identity, Faith and spirituality, Resilience, Gender roles
