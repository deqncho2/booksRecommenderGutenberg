Synopsis: In "The Eve of the Reformation," Gasquet delves into the religious life and thought of the English people during the period leading up to Henry VIII's rejection of the Roman jurisdiction. Through meticulous research and analysis, Gasquet uncovers the complex web of religious beliefs, practices, and tensions that existed in England at this critical juncture. From the influence of the Lollards to the rise of humanism, Gasquet paints a vivid picture of the religious landscape and the factors that ultimately led to the Reformation. This thought-provoking study offers a fascinating glimpse into a pivotal moment in English history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious studies, Cultural studies

Themes: Religious reform, Church authority, Religious diversity, Political and social implications of religious change
