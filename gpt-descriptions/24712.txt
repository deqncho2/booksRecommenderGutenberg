Synopsis: In "The Negro at Work in New York City: A Study in Economic Progress," George Edmund Haynes delves into the fascinating world of African American labor in the bustling metropolis of New York City. Through meticulous research and firsthand accounts, Haynes uncovers the challenges, triumphs, and untapped potential of this vibrant community. From the struggles of finding employment to the emergence of black-owned businesses, this captivating study sheds light on the complex dynamics of race, labor, and economic progress in early 20th-century New York City.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Sociology

Themes: African American labor, Economic progress, Racial dynamics, Urban studies
