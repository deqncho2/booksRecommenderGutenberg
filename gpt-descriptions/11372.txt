Synopsis: In 'The Nine-Tenths' by James Oppenheim, a young woman named Helen embarks on a journey of self-discovery and rebellion against societal norms. Set in the early 20th century, Helen finds herself torn between the expectations placed upon her as a woman and her desire for personal freedom. As she navigates through love, friendship, and the pursuit of her dreams, Helen must confront the limitations imposed on her by a patriarchal society. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this captivating novel explores the complexities of identity, the power of individuality, and the courage it takes to defy convention.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Gender roles, Self-discovery, Rebellion, Societal expectations
