Synopsis: 

In 'The Black Phalanx: African American soldiers in the War of Independence, the War of 1812, and the Civil War', Joseph T. Wilson delves into the often overlooked and untold stories of African American soldiers who fought in three pivotal wars in American history. From the Revolutionary War to the Civil War, Wilson uncovers the struggles, triumphs, and sacrifices of these brave men who fought for their freedom and the ideals of a nation that often denied them their rights. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, Wilson sheds light on the significant contributions and the challenges faced by African American soldiers during these tumultuous times.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, African American studies

Themes: Racial inequality, Patriotism, Identity, Courage, Resilience
