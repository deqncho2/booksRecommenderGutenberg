Synopsis: 
'Legends of Vancouver' by E. Pauline Johnson is a captivating collection of Indigenous legends and folklore that transport readers into the rich cultural heritage of the First Nations people of Vancouver. Through enchanting tales of mythical creatures, heroic figures, and supernatural events, Johnson weaves a tapestry of storytelling that immerses readers in the beauty and mysticism of the Pacific Northwest. From the origins of the land and its inhabitants to the struggles and triumphs of the Indigenous people, this book offers a mesmerizing glimpse into a world where legends come alive.

Genres: 
Folklore, Indigenous literature, Historical fiction

Themes: 
Cultural heritage, Mythology, Nature, Identity
