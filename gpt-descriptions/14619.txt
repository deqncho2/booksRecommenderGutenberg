Synopsis: In 'An Account of the Battle of Chateauguay', Lighthall takes us back to a pivotal moment in Canadian history, where a small group of determined soldiers faced off against a formidable enemy. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, he brings to life the events of this lesser-known battle, revealing the bravery and resourcefulness of those involved. As the tension builds and the battle unfolds, readers will be captivated by the strategic maneuvers, unexpected twists, and the ultimate outcome of this historic clash.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Canadian history

Themes: Bravery, Resourcefulness, Historical significance
