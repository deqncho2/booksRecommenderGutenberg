Synopsis: 
'A Mind That Found Itself: An Autobiography' by Clifford Whittingham Beers takes readers on a captivating journey through the author's personal experiences with mental illness. From his early struggles with depression and anxiety to his eventual institutionalization, Beers provides a raw and honest account of his battle against the stigmatization of mental health. Through his poignant storytelling, he sheds light on the harsh realities of psychiatric treatment in the early 20th century, while also offering hope and advocating for reform. This powerful autobiography offers a unique perspective on the human mind and the resilience of the human spirit.

Genres: Autobiography, Memoir, Mental Health

Themes: Mental illness, Stigmatization, Institutionalization, Advocacy, Resilience
