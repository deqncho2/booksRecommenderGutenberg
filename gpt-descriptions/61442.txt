Synopsis: In 'A House Divided Against Itself', the first volume of a captivating trilogy by Margaret Oliphant, readers are transported to the heart of Victorian England. The story revolves around the lives of two sisters, Lucy and Phoebe, who find themselves caught in a web of family secrets, forbidden love, and societal expectations. As their paths diverge, Lucy becomes entangled in a scandalous affair, while Phoebe struggles to navigate the constraints of a rigid society. Set against the backdrop of a rapidly changing world, this gripping tale explores the complexities of love, loyalty, and the consequences of one's choices.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Family Drama

Themes: Love and Betrayal, Social Expectations, Women's Roles in Victorian Society
