Synopsis: In 'The Conflict of Religions in the Early Roman Empire', T.R. Glover delves into the fascinating clash of belief systems that occurred during the formative years of the Roman Empire. From the rise of Christianity to the persistence of traditional Roman religion and the influence of Eastern cults, Glover explores the complex interplay between these diverse faiths and the resulting conflicts that shaped the religious landscape of the time. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author uncovers the struggles, conversions, and ideological clashes that unfolded in this pivotal era, shedding light on the profound impact of religious beliefs on the Roman Empire.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious studies, Cultural history

Themes: Religious conflict, Cultural assimilation, Religious syncretism, Imperial power and religion
