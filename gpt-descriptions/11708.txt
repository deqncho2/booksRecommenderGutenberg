Synopsis: In the second volume of "Abraham Lincoln, a History," authors John Hay and John G. Nicolay continue their captivating exploration of the life and presidency of one of America's most iconic leaders. This volume delves into the tumultuous years of Lincoln's presidency, as he navigates the challenges of the Civil War, grapples with the complexities of emancipation, and strives to unite a divided nation. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Hay and Nicolay offer a compelling account of Lincoln's unwavering determination, political acumen, and profound impact on the course of American history.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Leadership, Politics, Civil War, Emancipation, National Unity
