Synopsis: 

In 'The Science of Fingerprints: Classification and Uses' by the United States Federal Bureau of Investigation, readers are taken on a captivating journey into the world of fingerprint analysis. This comprehensive guide explores the intricate details of fingerprint classification and its wide-ranging applications in solving crimes. From the historical origins of fingerprinting to the cutting-edge techniques used today, this book delves into the fascinating science behind this unique form of identification. Through real-life case studies and gripping anecdotes, readers will gain a deeper understanding of how fingerprints have revolutionized forensic investigations and played a pivotal role in bringing criminals to justice.

Genres: Non-fiction, Crime, Forensic Science

Themes: Identification, Criminal Investigation, Forensic Techniques
