Synopsis: In 'The Ne'er-Do-Well' by Rex Beach, we follow the captivating journey of a young man named Curtis Jadwin. Born into privilege, Curtis finds himself torn between his desire for a life of adventure and his family's expectations. As he navigates the tumultuous world of late 19th-century America, Curtis becomes entangled in a web of love, ambition, and moral dilemmas. From the bustling streets of Chicago to the untamed wilderness of Alaska, this gripping tale explores the choices we make and the consequences they bring.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Romance

Themes: Identity and self-discovery, societal expectations, the pursuit of dreams, love and sacrifice
