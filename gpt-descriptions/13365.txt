Synopsis: In 'The Ethics of Drink and Other Social Questions; Or, Joints In Our Social Armour' by James Runciman, readers are taken on a thought-provoking journey through various social issues of the time. From the ethics of alcohol consumption to the complexities of social relationships, Runciman delves into the heart of these topics with wit and insight. Through a series of engaging essays, he challenges societal norms and encourages readers to question their own beliefs and behaviors. With a blend of humor and intellectual depth, this book offers a captivating exploration of the moral dilemmas and social challenges that shape our lives.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Philosophy

Themes: Ethics, Morality, Social norms, Alcohol consumption, Relationships
