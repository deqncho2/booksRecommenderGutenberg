Synopsis: In 'A School History of the Great War', Coulomb, Gerson, and McKinley take readers on a captivating journey through one of the most significant events in human history. This meticulously researched and engagingly written book provides a comprehensive account of the First World War, offering a unique perspective that is both educational and enthralling. From the political tensions that led to the outbreak of war to the harrowing experiences of soldiers on the front lines, the authors skillfully weave together historical facts, personal anecdotes, and vivid descriptions to bring this monumental conflict to life. As readers delve into the pages of this remarkable work, they will gain a deeper understanding of the war's impact on nations, individuals, and the world as a whole.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War history, Educational

Themes: War and its consequences, Nationalism, Human resilience, Historical significance
