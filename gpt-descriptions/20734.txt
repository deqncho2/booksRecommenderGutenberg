Synopsis: 
In 'Roman Antiquities, and Ancient Mythology: For Classical Schools (2nd ed)', Charles K. Dillaway takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and mythology of ancient Rome. From the founding of the city by Romulus and Remus to the grandeur of the Roman Empire, this comprehensive guide provides a fascinating exploration of the people, events, and legends that shaped one of the greatest civilizations in history. With vivid descriptions and engaging narratives, Dillaway brings to life the triumphs and struggles of the Roman people, shedding light on their customs, beliefs, and achievements. Whether delving into the captivating tales of gods and goddesses or unraveling the intricacies of Roman politics and warfare, this book is a treasure trove of knowledge that will captivate both students and enthusiasts of ancient history.

Genres: History, Mythology, Education

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Mythological figures, Roman culture and society
