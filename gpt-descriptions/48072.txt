Synopsis: In "The Modern Malady; Or, Sufferers from 'Nerves'" by Cyril Bennett, we are introduced to a world where the affliction of "nerves" has become a prevalent and mysterious condition. The story follows a group of individuals who find themselves bound together by their shared struggles with this enigmatic ailment. As they navigate the complexities of their own lives and relationships, they must confront the societal stigmas and misconceptions surrounding their condition. With each character's unique journey, the narrative delves into the depths of human vulnerability, resilience, and the search for understanding in a world that often fails to comprehend the complexities of mental health.

Genres: Psychological fiction, Drama, Social commentary

Themes: Mental health, Stigma, Identity, Relationships, Society's perception of illness
