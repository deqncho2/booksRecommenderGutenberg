Synopsis: 
In 'Animal Intelligence: Experimental Studies', Edward L. Thorndike takes readers on a captivating journey into the fascinating world of animal intelligence. Through a series of groundbreaking experiments, Thorndike explores the cognitive abilities of various animal species, challenging conventional beliefs and shedding light on the remarkable capacities of our non-human counterparts. From clever problem-solving to unexpected displays of reasoning, the author unveils the astonishing depths of animal intelligence, leaving readers in awe of the remarkable minds that exist beyond our own species.

Genres: 
Non-fiction, Science, Psychology

Themes: 
Animal cognition, Experimental research, Comparative psychology
