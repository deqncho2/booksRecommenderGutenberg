Synopsis: In 'How the Poor Live; and, Horrible London: 1889', George R. Sims takes readers on a gripping journey through the dark underbelly of Victorian London. Through vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, Sims exposes the harsh realities faced by the impoverished and the horrors that lurk in the city's shadows. From the squalid slums to the dangerous streets, this book offers a haunting portrayal of the struggles and injustices endured by the poor. As the stories unfold, readers are confronted with the stark contrast between the opulence of the upper classes and the destitution of the working class, leaving them with a profound sense of empathy and a desire for social change.

Genres: Social realism, historical fiction, urban exploration

Themes: Poverty, social inequality, urban decay, class divide
