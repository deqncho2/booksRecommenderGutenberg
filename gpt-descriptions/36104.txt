Synopsis: 

In the 11th edition of the renowned Encyclopaedia Britannica, Volume 10, Slice 7 takes readers on an intriguing journey from the captivating life of George Fox, the founder of the Quaker movement, to the rich history and cultural tapestry of France. Delve into the fascinating world of religious dissent and spiritual awakening as you explore the life and teachings of George Fox, a man who challenged the established norms of his time. Then, immerse yourself in the vibrant and diverse landscape of France, from its ancient origins to its modern-day significance as a global powerhouse. Discover the country's art, literature, politics, and more, as you uncover the secrets and complexities that have shaped this influential nation.

Genres: Reference, History, Biography

Themes: Religion, Dissent, Cultural Heritage, National Identity
