Synopsis: 

In "Fields, Factories and Workshops," Kropotkin takes readers on a captivating journey through the interconnectedness of industry, agriculture, and brain work with manual labor. Through vivid descriptions and thought-provoking analysis, he explores the potential of combining these elements to create a more harmonious and sustainable society. From the bustling factories to the serene fields, Kropotkin unveils the hidden potential of human collaboration and the transformative power of integrating different forms of work. This book is a captivating exploration of the possibilities that arise when we break down the barriers between different sectors of society and harness the collective power of human ingenuity.

Genres: Political philosophy, Sociology, Economics

Themes: Anarchism, Social cooperation, Sustainable development
