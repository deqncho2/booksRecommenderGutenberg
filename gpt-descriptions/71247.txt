Synopsis: 
When the Tree Flowered is an authentic tale of the old Sioux world, written by John G. Neihardt. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the life of Black Elk, a young Lakota Sioux warrior, as he navigates the changing landscape of his people. From the battles against rival tribes to the encroachment of white settlers, Black Elk's journey is one of resilience, spirituality, and the struggle to preserve his culture in the face of adversity. Through vivid storytelling and rich cultural details, Neihardt paints a captivating portrait of a vanishing way of life.

Genres: Historical fiction, Native American literature, Biographical fiction

Themes: Cultural preservation, Spirituality, Resilience, Clash of cultures
