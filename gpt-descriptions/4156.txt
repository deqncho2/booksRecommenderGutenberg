Synopsis: In the thirty-fifth volume of his captivating diary, Samuel Pepys takes us on a thrilling journey through the tumultuous months of May and June in the year 1665. As the Great Plague ravages London, Pepys finds himself caught in the midst of chaos and uncertainty. With vivid descriptions and intimate details, he recounts his encounters with the disease, his efforts to protect himself and his loved ones, and the harrowing experiences of those around him. Amidst the backdrop of this deadly epidemic, Pepys also navigates the political landscape, witnessing the rise of tensions with the Dutch and the looming threat of war. With his characteristic wit and candor, Pepys offers a unique perspective on a time of immense hardship and resilience.

Genres: Diary, Historical Fiction

Themes: Epidemic, Survival, Political Intrigue
