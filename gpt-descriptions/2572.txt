Synopsis: In "On the Decay of the Art of Lying," Mark Twain explores the consequences of a world where lying has become a lost art. Through a series of humorous anecdotes and thought-provoking observations, Twain challenges the reader to reconsider the role of lies in society and the impact of truth-telling on human relationships. As he delves into the various forms of lies and their potential benefits, Twain's witty and insightful commentary sheds light on the complexities of honesty and deception.

Genres: Satire, Essay, Humor

Themes: Truth and deception, Ethics, Communication
