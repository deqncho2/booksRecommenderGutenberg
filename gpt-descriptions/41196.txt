Synopsis: In 'Self-control: A Novel' by Mary Brunton, we are introduced to the intriguing life of Laura Montreville, a young woman who finds herself torn between societal expectations and her own desires. Set in the early 19th century, Laura's journey takes her through a series of unexpected events, challenging her self-control and forcing her to confront the consequences of her choices. As she navigates love, betrayal, and personal growth, readers will be captivated by the twists and turns that shape Laura's path towards self-discovery.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Self-control, Society and Expectations, Personal Growth
