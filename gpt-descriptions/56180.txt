Synopsis: 

In "York and Lancaster, 1399-1485," the reader is transported to a tumultuous period in English history, where two powerful houses clash for control of the throne. This gripping narrative delves into the intricate web of political intrigue, betrayal, and bloodshed that defined the Wars of the Roses. As the Yorks and Lancasters wage a bitter struggle for power, the reader becomes immersed in the lives of the key players, witnessing their ambitions, loyalties, and personal sacrifices. From the charismatic figures of Richard III and Henry VII to the resilient women who shape the course of history, this captivating account unveils the dramatic events that forever altered the destiny of England.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Drama, Political Thriller

Themes: Power struggles, Loyalty, Betrayal, Fate, Women's roles in history
