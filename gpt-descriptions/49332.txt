Synopsis: 
In 'The Epicurean: A Tale' by Thomas Moore, we are transported to ancient Greece, where we follow the journey of Alciphron, a young philosopher who embarks on a quest for knowledge and self-discovery. As he navigates through the vibrant streets of Athens and encounters a diverse cast of characters, Alciphron finds himself torn between the pursuit of pleasure and the pursuit of wisdom. Along the way, he grapples with profound questions about the nature of happiness, the meaning of life, and the boundaries of human desire. With its richly detailed setting and thought-provoking narrative, this captivating tale invites readers to explore the complexities of human existence and the eternal quest for fulfillment.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Philosophical Fiction, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Philosophy, Hedonism, Morality, Self-Discovery
