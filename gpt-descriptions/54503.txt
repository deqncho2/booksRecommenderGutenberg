Synopsis: In the second volume of "Life of James Buchanan, Fifteenth President of the United States," George Ticknor Curtis delves into the complex and controversial presidency of Buchanan. From his early political career to his tumultuous term in office, this biography offers a captivating exploration of a leader caught in the midst of a nation on the brink of division. With meticulous research and insightful analysis, Curtis paints a vivid portrait of Buchanan's struggles, decisions, and the lasting impact he had on the United States.

Genres: Biography, History, Politics

Themes: Leadership, National Division, Presidential Legacy
