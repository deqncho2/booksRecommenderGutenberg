Synopsis: 
In 'Theaetetus', Plato takes us on a philosophical journey as Socrates engages in a series of dialogues with the young mathematician Theaetetus. Together, they explore the nature of knowledge and the concept of truth, delving into questions about perception, reality, and the limits of human understanding. As they navigate through various theories and definitions, the reader is invited to ponder the nature of knowledge and the complexities of human cognition.

Genres: Philosophy, Dialogue, Classical Literature

Themes: Knowledge, Perception, Truth, Epistemology
