Synopsis: 

'Chapters of Opera: Being historical and critical observations and records concerning the lyric drama in New York from its earliest days down to the present time' takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of opera in New York City. Written by Henry Edward Krehbiel, this book delves into the origins, development, and evolution of the lyric drama, providing fascinating insights into the world of opera and its impact on the cultural landscape of the city. From the grandeur of the early opera houses to the triumphs and controversies of renowned performers, Krehbiel's meticulous research and engaging narrative style bring to life the vibrant and ever-changing opera scene in New York. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Music History, Cultural History

Themes: Opera, New York City, Cultural Impact, Historical Development
