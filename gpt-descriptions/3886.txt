Synopsis: 
In the third volume of "Memoirs of the Court of Marie Antoinette," Madam Campan, the First Lady in Waiting to the Queen, continues her captivating account of life within the opulent and tumultuous court of Versailles. As the French Revolution looms on the horizon, Campan provides an intimate and revealing glimpse into the daily routines, intrigues, and scandals that surrounded the ill-fated Queen of France. From the lavish balls and extravagant fashion to the political tensions and growing unrest among the people, Campan's memoirs offer a compelling narrative that transports readers to a world of grandeur and impending chaos.

Genres: Historical memoir, Biography, Non-fiction

Themes: Court life, French Revolution, Royal scandals, Political unrest
