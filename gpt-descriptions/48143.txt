Synopsis: In "David: A Tragedy" by Rice, Cale Young, we are introduced to David, a young man whose life takes a dramatic turn when he becomes entangled in a web of secrets and forbidden desires. Set against the backdrop of a small town in the early 20th century, David's journey explores the depths of human nature, the consequences of societal expectations, and the power of personal identity. As the story unfolds, we witness David's struggle to navigate the complexities of love, loyalty, and self-discovery, leading to a tragic climax that will leave readers questioning the boundaries of morality and the price of conformity.

Genres: Tragedy, Drama, Coming-of-age

Themes: Identity, Forbidden love, Society's expectations, Morality, Conformity
