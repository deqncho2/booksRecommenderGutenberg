Synopsis: 

In "Henry Fielding: a Memoir," author G. M. Godden delves into the life and works of the renowned English novelist and playwright, Henry Fielding. Through the inclusion of newly discovered letters and records, as well as captivating illustrations from contemporary prints, Godden paints a vivid portrait of Fielding's fascinating journey. From his early struggles as a young writer to his rise to literary fame, the memoir explores the personal and professional challenges that shaped Fielding's remarkable career. With a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of the era, Godden brings to life the vibrant world of 18th-century England, providing readers with a captivating glimpse into the life of one of literature's most influential figures.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Literary legacy, 18th-century society, Artistic perseverance
