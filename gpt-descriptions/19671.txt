Synopsis: In 'The Annals of the Poor' by Richmond, Legh, we are introduced to the remarkable life of a humble clergyman who dedicates himself to serving the impoverished and destitute. Through a series of poignant and heartwarming anecdotes, we witness the transformative power of compassion and the resilience of the human spirit. From encounters with beggars and orphans to the struggles of the working class, this inspiring narrative sheds light on the hidden lives of those often overlooked by society. As the protagonist navigates the challenges of poverty and inequality, he discovers the true meaning of empathy and the profound impact one person can have on the lives of many.

Genres: Biography, Social Commentary, Inspirational

Themes: Poverty, Compassion, Social Justice, Empathy
