Synopsis: In 'My Year of the Great War', Frederick Palmer takes us on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of World War I. Through his vivid and personal accounts, we witness the horrors and heroism of the battlefield, the camaraderie and resilience of soldiers, and the profound impact of war on individuals and nations. From the trenches of France to the diplomatic halls of power, Palmer's narrative captures the essence of a defining moment in history, offering a unique perspective on the human experience during wartime.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War memoir

Themes: War and its consequences, Human resilience, Sacrifice and heroism
