Synopsis: 

In 'Report on the Cost of Living in Ireland, June 1922', the Ireland Ministry of Economic Affairs delves into the intricate details of the country's economic landscape during a pivotal time in its history. Through meticulous research and analysis, the report uncovers the various factors that contribute to the rising cost of living, shedding light on the struggles faced by the Irish population. As the narrative unfolds, readers are taken on a journey that explores the impact of political unrest, economic instability, and social disparities on the everyday lives of the Irish people. 

Genres: Historical, Economic Analysis, Non-fiction

Themes: Economic hardship, Political turmoil, Social inequality
