Synopsis: 

In the second volume of Cassell's History of England, the reader is transported to a tumultuous period in English history, from the Wars of the Roses to the Great Rebellion. This captivating account delves into the power struggles, political intrigues, and epic battles that shaped the destiny of the nation. From the rise and fall of influential figures to the social and economic transformations that swept across the land, this volume offers a vivid and comprehensive exploration of a pivotal era in England's past.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Power struggles, Political intrigue, Social and economic transformations
