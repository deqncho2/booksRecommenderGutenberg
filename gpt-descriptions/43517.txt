Synopsis: 

In 'Bell's Cathedrals: The Church of St. Martin, Canterbury: An Illustrated Account of its History and Fabric', author C. F. Routledge takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and intricate architecture of the Church of St. Martin in Canterbury. From its humble beginnings as a Roman mausoleum to its transformation into a magnificent cathedral, this book unveils the secrets and stories hidden within its walls. With stunning illustrations and detailed descriptions, Routledge brings to life the awe-inspiring beauty and spiritual significance of this ancient place of worship.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Architecture

Themes: Religious history, Architectural heritage, Cultural significance
