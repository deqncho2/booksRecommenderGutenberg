Synopsis: 

'A History of Economic Doctrines: from the time of the physiocrats to the present day' takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of economic thought, from its roots in the physiocrats' ideas to the modern-day theories that shape our understanding of the economy. Written by Charles Gide and Charles Rist, this enlightening work delves into the minds of influential economists, exploring their theories, controversies, and the impact they have had on society. From Adam Smith's invisible hand to Karl Marx's critique of capitalism, the authors skillfully navigate the complex landscape of economic doctrines, offering a comprehensive and thought-provoking analysis. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Economics

Themes: Evolution of economic thought, Influence of economic theories, Controversies in economic doctrines, Impact of economic ideas on society
