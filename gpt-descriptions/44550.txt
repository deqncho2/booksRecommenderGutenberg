Synopsis: In 'The Hammer: A Story of the Maccabean Times', Church and Seeley transport readers to the tumultuous era of the Maccabean revolt. Amidst political intrigue, religious persecution, and the struggle for freedom, a young Jewish warrior named Judas Maccabeus emerges as a beacon of hope for his people. As he leads a courageous rebellion against the oppressive Seleucid Empire, Judas faces personal sacrifices, battles of faith, and the weight of his people's expectations. This gripping historical novel delves into the epic tale of the Maccabees, exploring the complexities of faith, loyalty, and the enduring power of resistance.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Religious Fiction, War Fiction
Themes: Faith and Belief, Freedom and Resistance, Leadership and Sacrifice
