Synopsis: In "Napoleon the Little" by Victor Hugo, we are introduced to a captivating tale that revolves around a man who believes he is destined for greatness. Set in the aftermath of Napoleon Bonaparte's reign, the story follows the life of a seemingly ordinary individual who becomes obsessed with the idea of becoming a new Napoleon. As he navigates through the political and social landscape of his time, he faces numerous challenges and encounters unexpected allies and adversaries. This gripping narrative explores the complexities of ambition, power, and the human desire for recognition, ultimately leading the reader on a thought-provoking journey through the mind of a man determined to leave his mark on history.

Genres: Historical fiction, Political drama, Psychological thriller

Themes: Ambition, Power, Identity, Historical legacy
