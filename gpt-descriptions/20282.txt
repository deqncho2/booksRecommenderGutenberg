Synopsis: 

'The Working of Steel: Annealing, Heat Treating and Hardening of Carbon and Alloy Steel' delves into the fascinating world of metallurgy, exploring the intricate processes of annealing, heat treating, and hardening of carbon and alloy steel. Written by renowned experts Colvin and Juthe, this comprehensive guide takes readers on a journey through the science and art of transforming steel into strong, durable, and versatile materials. From understanding the molecular structure of steel to mastering the precise techniques of heat treatment, this book offers invaluable insights and practical knowledge for engineers, blacksmiths, and anyone interested in the secrets behind the strength of steel.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Engineering

Themes: Metallurgy, Material Science, Industrial Processes
