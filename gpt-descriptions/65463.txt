Synopsis: In 'The End: How the Great War Was Stopped. A Novelistic Vagary', Gratacap takes readers on a thrilling journey through an alternate history where the Great War takes an unexpected turn. As the conflict rages on, a group of unlikely heroes emerges, determined to put an end to the devastation. With a blend of suspense, action, and thought-provoking twists, this novel explores the power of human resilience and the potential for change in the face of overwhelming odds.

Genres: Historical fiction, speculative fiction, war fiction

Themes: Resilience, heroism, the consequences of war, the power of collective action
