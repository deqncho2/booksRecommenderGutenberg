Synopsis: 

In 'A Short History of England, Ireland and Scotland', Mary Platt Parmele takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and tumultuous past of these three nations. From the ancient Celts and Romans to the Norman Conquest and the Tudor dynasty, Parmele weaves together a tapestry of historical events, political intrigue, and cultural transformations. With vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, she explores the rise and fall of kingdoms, the clash of religions, and the struggles for independence that have shaped the destinies of England, Ireland, and Scotland. From legendary figures like King Arthur to iconic rulers such as Queen Elizabeth I, this concise history offers a fascinating glimpse into the complex and interconnected histories of these nations.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Nation-building, Power struggles, Cultural identity
