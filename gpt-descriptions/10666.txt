Synopsis: In 'The Public vs. M. Gustave Flaubert', a renowned author finds himself on trial for obscenity, as his controversial novel sparks a heated debate about art, morality, and the boundaries of freedom of expression. As the courtroom drama unfolds, the reader is taken on a gripping journey through the complexities of society's perception of art and the clash between artistic vision and societal norms.

Genres: Legal drama, Historical fiction, Literary fiction

Themes: Freedom of expression, Morality, Artistic integrity, Society's perception of art
