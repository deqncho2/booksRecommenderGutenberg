Synopsis: In 'Aspects of Modern Oxford, by a Mere Don', the author takes us on a captivating journey through the hallowed halls of Oxford University. Through witty anecdotes and insightful observations, he unveils the hidden quirks and idiosyncrasies of this esteemed institution, shedding light on the peculiarities of academic life, the eccentricities of the students, and the timeless traditions that shape the city. From the secret societies to the infamous rivalry between colleges, this delightful exploration of Oxford's past and present will leave readers enchanted and eager to uncover more.

Genres: Non-fiction, Memoir, Travelogue

Themes: Academic life, Tradition, Eccentricity
