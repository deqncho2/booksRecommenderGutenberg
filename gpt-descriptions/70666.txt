Synopsis: In "The Ethics of Hercules," Robert Chenault Givler delves into the fascinating exploration of man's body as the ultimate arbiter of ethical values. Through a captivating analysis of the legendary hero Hercules, Givler challenges conventional notions of morality and invites readers to question the very foundations of ethical decision-making. As the narrative unfolds, Givler skillfully weaves together ancient myths, philosophical insights, and scientific discoveries to present a thought-provoking argument that will leave readers pondering the intricate relationship between the physical and the moral. 

Genres: Philosophy, Mythology, Literary Criticism

Themes: Ethics, Morality, Human Nature
