Synopsis:
'The Story in Primary Instruction: Sixteen Stories and How to Use Them' is a captivating educational guide that combines storytelling with practical teaching strategies. Written by Samuel B. Allison and H. Avis Perdue, this book presents sixteen engaging stories specifically designed for primary instruction. Each story is accompanied by detailed instructions on how to effectively incorporate it into the classroom, making learning an enjoyable and interactive experience for young students. From tales of adventure and discovery to stories that promote empathy and problem-solving, this book offers a diverse range of narratives that will captivate young minds and enhance their educational journey.

Genres: Education, Instructional, Storytelling

Themes: Teaching strategies, Active learning, Imagination, Empathy, Problem-solving
