Synopsis: 
In "The Natural History of the Tea-Tree, with Observations on the Medical Qualities of Tea, and on the Effects of Tea-Drinking," John Coakley Lettsom takes readers on a fascinating journey through the origins, cultivation, and medicinal properties of tea. From the lush tea gardens of China to the bustling tea houses of Europe, Lettsom explores the cultural significance and health benefits of this beloved beverage. With meticulous research and captivating anecdotes, he delves into the effects of tea-drinking on the human body, offering valuable insights into its potential therapeutic uses. This enlightening work is a must-read for tea enthusiasts and those curious about the intriguing history and medicinal qualities of tea.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medicine

Themes: Tea culture, Medicinal properties of tea, Cultural significance of tea
