Synopsis: In 'Curious Myths of the Middle Ages', Sabine Baring-Gould takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating world of medieval legends and superstitions. From tales of mythical creatures and enchanted objects to stories of saints and miracles, this book unravels the mysterious and often bizarre beliefs that shaped the medieval mindset. With meticulous research and a touch of wit, Baring-Gould explores the origins and evolution of these curious myths, shedding light on the cultural and religious beliefs of the Middle Ages.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Folklore

Themes: Mythology, Superstition, Religion, Cultural Beliefs
