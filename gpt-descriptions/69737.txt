Synopsis: In 'Papa's Own Girl: A Novel' by Marie Stevens Howland, we are introduced to the captivating story of a young woman named Lily who finds herself torn between duty and desire. Set in the early 1900s, Lily's life takes an unexpected turn when she is forced to leave her small town and embark on a journey that will challenge her beliefs, test her resilience, and ultimately shape her identity. As she navigates through love, loss, and the pursuit of her dreams, Lily must confront the complexities of family, societal expectations, and the power of self-discovery. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this novel explores the timeless themes of sacrifice, independence, and the enduring bond between a father and his daughter.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Family Drama

Themes: Duty vs. Desire, Self-Discovery, Family Bonds
