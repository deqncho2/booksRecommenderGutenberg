Synopsis: 

'Journal of a West India Proprietor: Kept During a Residence in the Island of Jamaica' takes readers on a captivating journey through the eyes of the author, Lewis, as he immerses himself in the vibrant and complex world of Jamaica. Through his vivid descriptions and personal experiences, Lewis provides a unique perspective on the island's culture, society, and the intricate dynamics of plantation life. As he navigates the challenges and contradictions of being a West India Proprietor, Lewis's journal offers a thought-provoking exploration of power, identity, and the moral complexities of colonialism.

Genres: Memoir, Travelogue, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Colonialism, Power dynamics, Identity, Morality
