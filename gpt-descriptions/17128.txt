Synopsis: 
'Noteworthy Families (Modern Science): An Index to Kinships in Near Degrees between Persons Whose Achievements Are Honourable, and Have Been Publicly Recorded' is a fascinating exploration of the interconnectedness of notable individuals throughout history. Written by Francis Galton and Edgar Schuster, this book delves into the genealogical connections between accomplished individuals, revealing surprising and intriguing relationships. From renowned scientists and artists to influential politicians and thinkers, the authors uncover the hidden threads that link these exceptional individuals together. As the authors navigate through the intricate web of family ties, readers are taken on a captivating journey that sheds light on the power of lineage and the impact it can have on human achievement.

Genres: Non-fiction, Genealogy, History

Themes: Lineage, Achievement, Interconnectedness
