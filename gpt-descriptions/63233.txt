Synopsis: 

'China's Revolution, 1911-1912: A Historical and Political Record of the Civil War' by Edwin John Dingle takes readers on a captivating journey through one of the most pivotal periods in Chinese history. Set against the backdrop of a nation on the brink of change, this book delves into the intricacies of the civil war that ultimately led to the downfall of the Qing Dynasty. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Dingle paints a vivid picture of the political turmoil, social unrest, and ideological clashes that shaped the course of China's revolution. From the rise of revolutionary leaders to the battles fought on the streets, this gripping account offers a comprehensive exploration of the events that forever altered the destiny of a nation.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, War history

Themes: Revolution, Political upheaval, National identity, Power struggle
