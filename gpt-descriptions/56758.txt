Synopsis: In "Anton Tchekhov, and Other Essays," Lev Shestov delves into the enigmatic world of the renowned Russian playwright, Anton Chekhov, and explores the profound impact of his works on the human condition. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Shestov delves into the depths of Chekhov's characters, unraveling their complex emotions and existential dilemmas. From the melancholic to the absurd, Shestov's analysis sheds light on the timeless themes that permeate Chekhov's plays, leaving readers captivated by the profound insights into the human psyche.

Genres: Literary criticism, philosophy, biography

Themes: Existentialism, human nature, the search for meaning
