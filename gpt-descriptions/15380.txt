Synopsis: In "Mrs. Warren's Daughter: A Story of the Woman's Movement," Harry Johnston takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous world of the suffragette movement. Set in the early 20th century, the story follows the life of Vivie Warren, a young woman who discovers a shocking secret about her mother's past. As Vivie becomes entangled in the fight for women's rights, she must navigate societal expectations, personal relationships, and her own identity. With its compelling characters and thought-provoking plot, this novel delves into the complexities of feminism, family, and the pursuit of equality.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Women's Literature, Social Commentary

Themes: Women's Rights, Identity, Family Dynamics, Social Justice
