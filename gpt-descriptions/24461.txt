Synopsis: In "Third class in Indian railways," Mahatma Gandhi takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the bustling world of Indian railways. Through his personal experiences and observations, Gandhi sheds light on the stark realities faced by the third-class passengers, revealing the stark disparities and injustices prevalent in society. With his characteristic insight and empathy, Gandhi delves into the lives of the marginalized, offering a poignant exploration of the human condition and the need for social reform.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Travelogue

Themes: Social inequality, Empathy, Human rights, Advocacy
