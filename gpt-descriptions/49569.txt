Synopsis: 

'Bleeding Armenia: Its history and horrors under the curse of Islam' delves into the tumultuous past of Armenia, exploring the dark chapters of its history that have been overshadowed by the curse of Islam. Written by Gabrielean, M. Smbat and Augustus Warner, this gripping account uncovers the untold stories of suffering, resilience, and the enduring spirit of the Armenian people. From the ancient conflicts to the devastating massacres, the authors paint a vivid picture of the horrors endured by Armenia, shedding light on a history that has long been suppressed. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political commentary, Cultural studies

Themes: Armenian history, Religious conflict, Resilience and survival
