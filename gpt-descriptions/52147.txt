Synopsis: In the final installment of the second book of the Chronicles of England, Scotland and Ireland, Holinshed delves into the intriguing life and reign of Richard the Second, the second son of Edward Prince of Wales. From his ascension to the throne at a young age to his tumultuous relationships with his nobles and his eventual downfall, Richard's story is one of power, betrayal, and the struggle to maintain control in a kingdom divided. As the young king faces internal conflicts and external threats, the reader is taken on a captivating journey through the complexities of medieval politics and the consequences of unchecked ambition.

Genres: Historical fiction, Biography, Political drama

Themes: Power and authority, Betrayal and loyalty, Political instability, Personal growth and maturity
