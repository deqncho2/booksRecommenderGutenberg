Synopsis:
"Onanism Display'd" is a comprehensive exploration of the controversial topic of self-pollution, commonly known as masturbation. Divided into six parts, the book delves into the true nature of Onan's sin, examines the prevalence of modern Onanists, investigates the causes and consequences of self-pollution, presents three extraordinary cases of individuals addicted to this act, discusses nocturnal-pollutions, and concludes with a dissertation on generation and anatomical discoveries. Through a combination of historical analysis, medical insights, and theological perspectives, the author aims to shed light on the sinfulness and consequences of self-pollution.

Genres: Medical literature, Religious literature, Historical analysis

Themes: Sexual morality, Sin and guilt, Medical consequences of self-pollution
