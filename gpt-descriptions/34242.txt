Synopsis: In 'Chattanooga or Lookout Mountain and Missionary Ridge from Moccasin Point', Wood, Bradford Ripley, Jr. takes readers on a captivating journey through the historic battlefields of Chattanooga. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, the author brings to life the dramatic events that unfolded during the Civil War, showcasing the bravery and resilience of both Union and Confederate forces. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the strategic maneuvers, the intense battles, and the breathtaking landscapes that served as the backdrop to this pivotal moment in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Travelogue

Themes: Courage and sacrifice, Historical preservation, The impact of war on landscapes and communities
