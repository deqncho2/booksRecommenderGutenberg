Synopsis: 
'A Blockaded Family: Life in Southern Alabama during the Civil War' by Hague, Parthenia Antoinette is a captivating memoir that offers a unique perspective on the hardships and resilience of a family living in Southern Alabama during the Civil War. Through the eyes of the author, readers are transported to a time of great turmoil and uncertainty, as the family navigates the challenges of a blockaded region, scarcity of resources, and the constant threat of Union forces. With vivid descriptions and heartfelt anecdotes, the author paints a vivid picture of the daily struggles, sacrifices, and moments of hope that defined their lives during this tumultuous period. 

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Resilience, Survival, Family, War, Socioeconomic Impact
