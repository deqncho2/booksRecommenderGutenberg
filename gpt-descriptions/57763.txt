Synopsis: 
In "Daughters of Nijo: A Romance of Japan" by Watanna, Onoto, readers are transported to the enchanting world of feudal Japan. The story revolves around the lives of two sisters, O'Matsu and O'Yumi, who find themselves caught in a web of love, duty, and tradition. As they navigate the complexities of their society, they encounter forbidden romances, political intrigue, and the ever-present struggle between personal desires and societal expectations. With vivid descriptions of the Japanese landscape and captivating characters, this novel offers a captivating glimpse into a world of passion, sacrifice, and the enduring power of love.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Cultural Fiction

Themes: Love and Desire, Tradition and Duty, Forbidden Relationships, Gender Roles, Societal Expectations
