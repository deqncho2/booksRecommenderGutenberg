Synopsis: In 'A History of Matrimonial Institutions, Vol. 2 of 3' by George Elliott Howard, embark on a captivating journey through time as the author delves into the intricate tapestry of marriage customs and traditions across various cultures and eras. From ancient civilizations to modern societies, this volume uncovers the fascinating evolution of matrimonial practices, shedding light on the diverse ways in which love, commitment, and partnership have been perceived and celebrated throughout history. With vivid anecdotes and meticulous research, Howard invites readers to explore the complexities of human relationships and the profound impact that matrimonial institutions have had on shaping societies across the globe.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Anthropology, Sociology

Themes: Marriage customs, Cultural diversity, Evolution of societal norms
