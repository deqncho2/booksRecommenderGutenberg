Synopsis: In 'Julia France and Her Times: A Novel', Gertrude Franklin Horn Atherton takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Julia France, a strong-willed and independent woman living in the late 19th century. Set against the backdrop of a rapidly changing society, Julia navigates the complexities of love, ambition, and societal expectations, challenging the norms of her time. As she encounters various characters and experiences, Julia's determination and resilience are put to the test, leading her on a path of self-discovery and personal growth. With vivid descriptions and rich historical detail, Atherton paints a vivid portrait of a remarkable woman and the era in which she lived.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, self-discovery, love and relationships, ambition
