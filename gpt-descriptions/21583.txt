Synopsis: In 'Children of the Tenements', Jacob A. Riis takes readers on a captivating journey through the gritty streets of New York City's tenement neighborhoods. Through vivid descriptions and powerful photographs, Riis exposes the harsh realities faced by the children growing up in these overcrowded and poverty-stricken communities. From their struggles for survival to their dreams of a better future, this eye-opening account sheds light on the resilience and spirit of these young individuals, offering a poignant glimpse into a forgotten world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social documentary, Historical

Themes: Poverty, Immigration, Childhood, Social inequality
