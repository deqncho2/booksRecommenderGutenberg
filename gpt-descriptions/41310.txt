Synopsis: 
In 'Selections from the Prose Writings of John Henry Cardinal Newman: For the Use of Schools', readers are introduced to the profound and thought-provoking prose of John Henry Cardinal Newman. This collection brings together a diverse range of writings, including essays, sermons, and letters, showcasing Newman's intellectual prowess and his ability to engage with complex theological and philosophical ideas. From exploring the nature of faith and reason to delving into the challenges of religious belief in a secular world, Newman's writings offer a captivating glimpse into the mind of one of the most influential thinkers of the 19th century.

Genres: 
Philosophy, Theology, Essay Collection

Themes: 
Faith and Reason, Religious Belief, Intellectual Inquiry
