Synopsis: 
In "Personal reminiscences of early days in California, with other sketches," Stephen J. Field takes readers on a captivating journey through his experiences in the early days of California. From his arrival during the Gold Rush to his rise as a prominent lawyer and judge, Field shares fascinating anecdotes and encounters with notable figures of the time. However, the story takes a thrilling turn when Field reveals the shocking tale of his attempted assassination by a former associate on the supreme bench of the state. This gripping memoir offers a unique perspective on the tumultuous history of California and the personal challenges faced by one of its most influential figures.

Genres: 
Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: 
California history, Gold Rush, Legal system, Personal resilience
