Synopsis: In the Transactions of the American Society of Civil Engineers, vol. LXVIII, Sept. 1910, F. Lavis presents an intriguing paper titled "The Bergen Hill Tunnels" (Paper No. 1154). This captivating work delves into the challenges and triumphs of constructing the tunnels, offering a fascinating glimpse into the world of civil engineering during the early 20th century. Lavis skillfully explores the intricate details of the project, leaving readers eager to discover the innovative techniques employed and the ultimate outcome of this ambitious endeavor.

Genres: Technical literature, Engineering, Historical

Themes: Construction challenges, Innovation, Engineering marvels
