Synopsis: 
"The Wound Dresser: A Series of Letters Written from the Hospitals in Washington during the War of the Rebellion" is a poignant collection of letters written by Walt Whitman during his time as a volunteer nurse in the hospitals of Washington, D.C. during the American Civil War. Through his heartfelt and vivid descriptions, Whitman provides a unique and intimate perspective on the experiences of wounded soldiers and the emotional toll of war. As he tends to the physical and emotional wounds of the soldiers, he also reflects on the nature of suffering, the resilience of the human spirit, and the power of compassion.

Genres: Non-fiction, Memoir, Historical

Themes: War, Suffering, Compassion, Resilience
