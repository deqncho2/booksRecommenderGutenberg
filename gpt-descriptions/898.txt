Synopsis: In "The Lesson of the Master" by Henry James, a young writer named Paul Overt becomes infatuated with a renowned novelist, Henry St. George, whom he idolizes. Hoping to learn from his literary idol, Paul seeks out St. George and is invited to his grand country estate. As Paul spends more time with the esteemed author, he begins to uncover a hidden truth about St. George's personal life that challenges his perception of artistic integrity and the sacrifices one must make for success. This thought-provoking novella delves into the complexities of ambition, artistic influence, and the blurred lines between reality and fiction.

Genres: Fiction, Literary Fiction, Psychological Fiction

Themes: Artistic Integrity, Ambition, Influence, Sacrifice, Reality vs. Fiction
