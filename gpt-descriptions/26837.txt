Synopsis: In 'Love—Marriage—Birth Control: Being a Speech delivered at the Church Congress at Birmingham, October, 1921', Lord Dawson challenges societal norms and sparks controversy as he addresses the sensitive topic of birth control. With eloquence and conviction, he explores the impact of love, marriage, and the control of fertility on individuals and society as a whole. This thought-provoking speech delves into the complexities of personal choice, morality, and the role of religion in shaping our understanding of these issues.

Genres: Non-fiction, Speech, Social Commentary

Themes: Love, Marriage, Birth Control, Morality, Religion, Personal Choice, Society
