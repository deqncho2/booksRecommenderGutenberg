Synopsis: 

In 'Bricks Without Straw: A Novel' by Albion Winegar Tourgée, a gripping tale unfolds against the backdrop of post-Civil War America. The story follows the lives of two families, one African American and one white, as they navigate the complexities of Reconstruction and the challenges of building a new society. As tensions rise and old prejudices persist, the characters find themselves entangled in a web of political intrigue, personal struggles, and the fight for justice. With vivid descriptions and compelling storytelling, Tourgée explores the profound impact of race, power, and resilience in a society grappling with its past and striving for a better future.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Fiction, Social Commentary

Themes: Race Relations, Reconstruction, Social Justice, Power Dynamics
