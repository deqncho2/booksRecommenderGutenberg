Synopsis: In the second volume of "A Short History of Freethought Ancient and Modern," J.M. Robertson delves deeper into the fascinating journey of freethought, exploring its evolution from ancient times to the modern era. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Robertson uncovers the struggles, triumphs, and controversies surrounding the pursuit of intellectual freedom. From the ancient philosophers who challenged religious dogma to the modern thinkers who fought for secularism, this volume offers a captivating exploration of the individuals and ideas that have shaped the freethought movement throughout history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Philosophy

Themes: Intellectual freedom, Religious skepticism, Secularism, Evolution of ideas
