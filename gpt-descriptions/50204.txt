Synopsis: In "The Witchcraft Delusion in New England: Its Rise, Progress, and Termination," Cotton Mather and Robert Calef delve into the dark and mysterious world of witchcraft that plagued New England in the late 17th century. Through a collection of historical accounts, testimonies, and personal narratives, the authors explore the origins, escalation, and ultimate resolution of the witchcraft hysteria that gripped the region. As they navigate through the chilling events and examine the beliefs, fears, and motivations of the people involved, a captivating and haunting tale unfolds, shedding light on the complexities of human nature and the power of mass hysteria.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, Occult

Themes: Mass hysteria, Religious fanaticism, Power dynamics, Superstition, Human psychology
