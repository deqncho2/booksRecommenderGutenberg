Synopsis: In 'The Civil War' by James I. Robertson, embark on a gripping journey through one of the most pivotal periods in American history. This meticulously researched and vividly written account delves into the complex web of events, personalities, and ideologies that led to the outbreak of the Civil War. From the political maneuverings in Washington to the harrowing battles on the front lines, this book offers a captivating exploration of the war that tore a nation apart.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Conflict and division, Leadership and decision-making, Ideological differences, Sacrifice and resilience
