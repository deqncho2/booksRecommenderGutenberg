Synopsis: 
"The English Governess at the Siamese Court: Being Recollections of Six Years in the Royal Palace at Bangkok" is a captivating memoir that takes readers on a remarkable journey into the heart of 19th-century Siam. Anna Harriette Leonowens, an English governess, shares her extraordinary experiences and encounters while living in the opulent Royal Palace at Bangkok, serving as a teacher to the royal children. From navigating cultural differences and palace intrigues to witnessing the grandeur of Siamese traditions and customs, Leonowens provides a fascinating glimpse into a world few have ever seen.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Cultural Exploration

Themes: Cultural clash, Identity, Power dynamics, Tradition vs. modernity
