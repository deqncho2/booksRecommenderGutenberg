Synopsis: 

In 'The History of Rome, Books 27 to 36' by Livy, the ancient city of Rome finds itself at a critical turning point. As the Roman Republic faces external threats from powerful enemies and internal struggles for power, Livy takes us on a captivating journey through a tumultuous period in Roman history. From the rise of influential leaders to the dramatic battles that shape the destiny of Rome, this gripping narrative explores the triumphs and tragedies that define an empire on the brink of transformation.

Genres: Historical fiction, Epic, Political drama

Themes: Power and ambition, Loyalty and betrayal, War and conquest, Leadership and governance
