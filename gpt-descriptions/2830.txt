Synopsis: 
In "Reginald" by Saki, readers are introduced to the mischievous and witty character of Reginald, a young man who possesses a unique talent for causing chaos and amusement wherever he goes. Through a series of humorous and satirical short stories, Reginald navigates the absurdities of Edwardian society, effortlessly manipulating those around him with his sharp tongue and clever pranks. From dinner parties to country estates, Reginald's unconventional antics challenge the norms of polite society, leaving readers eagerly anticipating his next outrageous adventure.

Genres: Satire, Comedy, Short Stories

Themes: Social satire, Subversion of societal norms, Wit and humor
