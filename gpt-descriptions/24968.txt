Synopsis: 

'Behind the Scenes; or, Thirty years a slave, and Four Years in the White House' by Elizabeth Keckley is a captivating memoir that takes readers on a remarkable journey through the life of a former slave turned dressmaker for the elite. Keckley shares her experiences as a slave, her rise to prominence as a talented seamstress, and her unique perspective on the inner workings of the White House during the tumultuous years of the Civil War. With intimate details and poignant anecdotes, Keckley offers a behind-the-scenes look at the lives of both the powerful and the oppressed, shedding light on the complexities of race, class, and identity in nineteenth-century America.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Identity, Social Class, Resilience
