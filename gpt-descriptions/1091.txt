Synopsis:
In "On Heroes, Hero-Worship, and the Heroic in History," Thomas Carlyle delves into the fascinating realm of heroism, exploring the impact of extraordinary individuals on society and the course of history. Through captivating anecdotes and insightful analysis, Carlyle examines the various forms of heroism, from the hero as a divinity to the hero as a prophet, poet, or king. He delves into the hero-worship phenomenon, exploring how individuals are inspired and influenced by these exceptional figures. With a blend of historical examples and philosophical musings, Carlyle invites readers to ponder the profound role heroes play in shaping our collective destiny.

Genres: Philosophy, History, Biography

Themes: Heroism, Influence, Leadership, Society, Destiny
