Synopsis: 

In "Ten Years and Ten Months in Lunatic Asylums in Different States," Moses Swan takes us on a gripping journey through his harrowing experiences within various mental institutions. From the moment he is admitted against his will, Swan finds himself trapped in a world of darkness, uncertainty, and unimaginable cruelty. As he navigates the treacherous landscape of these asylums, he encounters a cast of characters that both inspire and terrify him, while constantly questioning his own sanity. With each passing day, Swan's determination to escape grows stronger, leading him to uncover shocking truths about the institutions and the people who run them. Will he ever find freedom, or will he be forever confined to the depths of madness?

Genres: Memoir, Psychological Thriller, Non-Fiction

Themes: Mental Health, Institutionalization, Survival
