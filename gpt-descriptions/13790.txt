Synopsis: 

In "Life And Letters Of John Gay (1685-1732), Author of "The Beggar's Opera,"" Lewis Melville delves into the fascinating life and literary career of John Gay, the brilliant mind behind the iconic "The Beggar's Opera." Through a collection of letters, anecdotes, and historical accounts, Melville paints a vivid portrait of Gay's struggles, triumphs, and the societal backdrop that influenced his work. From his humble beginnings to his rise as a celebrated playwright, this biography offers a captivating exploration of the man behind one of the most influential works in English literature.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic creativity, Social commentary, Literary influence
