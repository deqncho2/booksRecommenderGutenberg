Synopsis: 

In "What the Judge Saw: Being Twenty-Five Years in Manchester by One Who Has Done It," Sir Edward Abbott Parry takes us on a captivating journey through the bustling streets of Manchester, offering a unique perspective from the eyes of a judge. With vivid descriptions and insightful anecdotes, Parry delves into the heart of the city, uncovering its hidden secrets, scandals, and triumphs. From the courtroom to the back alleys, he introduces us to a diverse cast of characters, each with their own stories to tell. As we follow the judge's experiences and observations, we are drawn into a world of crime, justice, and the complexities of human nature. 

Genres: Memoir, Legal Drama, Historical Non-fiction

Themes: Justice, Society, Human Nature, City Life
