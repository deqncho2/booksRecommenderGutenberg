Synopsis: 

In 'London Labour and the London Poor, Vol. 4', Henry Mayhew delves into the hidden world of Victorian London, exploring the lives and struggles of the city's most marginalized inhabitants. From street performers and costermongers to prostitutes and beggars, Mayhew uncovers the harsh realities and unique stories of these individuals, shedding light on the underbelly of a rapidly industrializing society. Through vivid descriptions and firsthand accounts, the reader is transported to the bustling streets of London, witnessing the daily struggles and triumphs of those living on the fringes of society.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Historical

Themes: Poverty, Social inequality, Urban life, Survival, Human resilience
