Synopsis: In "Constantine the Great: The reorganization of the Empire and the triumph of the Church," John B. Firth takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and reign of one of history's most influential figures. From his rise to power as the first Christian Roman Emperor to his transformative reforms and the establishment of Christianity as the dominant religion, Firth delves into the complex political and religious landscape of the time. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, the author paints a vivid portrait of Constantine's struggles, triumphs, and the lasting impact he had on the Roman Empire and the world.

Genres: Biography, History, Religion

Themes: Power and politics, Religion and spirituality, Transformation and legacy
