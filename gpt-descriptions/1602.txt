Synopsis: 
In 'Dawn O'Hara: The Girl Who Laughed' by Edna Ferber, we follow the spirited and ambitious Dawn O'Hara as she navigates the world of journalism in early 20th century America. With her quick wit and determination, Dawn faces the challenges of being a female reporter in a male-dominated industry, while also grappling with personal relationships and the pursuit of her dreams. As she encounters love, heartbreak, and professional triumphs, Dawn's infectious laughter becomes both her armor and her strength, propelling her forward in a world that often tries to stifle her spirit.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Women's Literature

Themes: Gender equality, Pursuit of dreams, Resilience, Love and relationships
