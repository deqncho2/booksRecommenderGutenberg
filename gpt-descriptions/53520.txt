Synopsis: 

In 'The Author's Desk Book', William Dana Orcutt delves into the intricate world of publishing, copyright, and the mechanics of book production. This comprehensive reference volume explores the multifaceted relationship between authors and publishers, shedding light on the often complex dynamics that shape the literary world. From the arrangement of a book to the creation of an index, Orcutt provides invaluable insights into the various aspects of the writing and publishing process. With a wealth of practical advice and historical context, this book serves as an indispensable guide for both aspiring and established authors, offering a deeper understanding of the intricate workings behind the scenes of the literary industry.

Genres: Non-fiction, Reference

Themes: Publishing industry, Copyright, Book production, Author-publisher relationship
