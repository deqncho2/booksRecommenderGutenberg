Synopsis:
'The Railway Builders: A Chronicle of Overland Highways' by Oscar D. Skelton is a captivating historical account that takes readers on a thrilling journey through the construction of railways in North America. From the early days of surveying and planning to the monumental challenges faced by the builders, this chronicle delves into the triumphs and tribulations of those who dared to connect the vast continent by rail. With vivid descriptions and engaging narratives, Skelton brings to life the untold stories of the visionaries, engineers, and laborers who transformed the landscape and shaped the future of transportation.

Genres:
Historical Non-fiction, Adventure, Biography

Themes:
Innovation and Progress, Human Determination and Resilience, Impact of Infrastructure Development
