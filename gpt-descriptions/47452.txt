Synopsis: In "The Panama Canal" by McKinlay, Duncan E., embark on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of one of the world's most remarkable engineering feats. From its inception as a daring dream to its completion as a modern marvel, this book unveils the gripping tale of the Panama Canal, filled with political intrigue, technological challenges, and the indomitable human spirit. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the incredible obstacles faced by the visionaries behind the canal, as well as the triumphs and tragedies that shaped its construction. Delve into the untold stories of the countless individuals who dedicated their lives to this monumental project, and discover the profound impact the Panama Canal has had on global trade and international relations. 

Genres: History, Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Engineering and innovation, Political power and influence, Human perseverance and determination
