Synopsis: In 'The Shakespearean Myth: William Shakespeare and Circumstantial Evidence', Morgan Appleton delves into the mysterious life of the renowned playwright, William Shakespeare. Through a meticulous examination of historical records and compelling circumstantial evidence, Appleton challenges the traditional narrative surrounding Shakespeare's identity and authorship. As the layers of the Shakespearean myth are peeled away, a captivating and thought-provoking exploration of the true origins of Shakespeare's works emerges. This book is a must-read for anyone intrigued by literary mysteries and the enigmatic figure behind some of the greatest plays in history.

Genres: Non-fiction, Literary Criticism, Biography

Themes: Authorship, Identity, Historical Interpretation
