Synopsis: In "Literary Friends and Acquaintance; a Personal Retrospect of American Authorship," William Dean Howells takes readers on a captivating journey through his encounters with some of the greatest American writers of his time. From Mark Twain to Henry James, Howells shares intimate and insightful anecdotes about these literary giants, offering a unique perspective on their lives and works. Through his personal reflections, Howells provides a fascinating glimpse into the world of American authorship, revealing the complexities and nuances of these celebrated writers.

Genres: Biography, Memoir, Literary Criticism

Themes: Friendship, Influence, Literary Legacy
