Synopsis: In 'The Grotesque in Church Art', T. Tindall Wildridge delves into the mysterious and often overlooked world of grotesque sculptures and carvings found in churches across Europe. From gargoyles to misshapen figures, the author explores the origins, symbolism, and hidden meanings behind these peculiar and enigmatic artworks. As he uncovers the secrets behind these grotesque creations, Wildridge takes readers on a captivating journey through history, art, and religion, revealing a fascinating and sometimes unsettling side of church art that will leave readers questioning the true purpose behind these intriguing sculptures.

Genres: Art History, Religious Studies, Cultural Studies

Themes: Symbolism, Religion, Aesthetics
