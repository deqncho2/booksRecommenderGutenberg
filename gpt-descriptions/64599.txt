Synopsis: In 'The Princess Casamassima: A Novel' by Henry James, a young man named Hyacinth Robinson finds himself torn between two worlds - the working class he was born into and the upper-class society he longs to be a part of. When he becomes involved with a group of political radicals, his loyalties and beliefs are put to the test. As he navigates the complexities of love, loyalty, and social class, Hyacinth is forced to confront the consequences of his choices and the true nature of his identity.

Genres: Fiction, Political Fiction, Psychological Fiction

Themes: Social class, Identity, Love and loyalty, Political activism
