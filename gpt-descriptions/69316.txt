Synopsis: 
'Letters of a Japanese schoolboy ("Hashimura Togo")' by Irwin Wallace is a captivating collection of letters written by a young Japanese boy named Hashimura Togo. Through his heartfelt correspondence, Togo shares his experiences, dreams, and challenges as he navigates the complexities of school, family, and society in Japan. From his friendships and first love to his aspirations and cultural observations, Togo's letters offer a unique and intimate glimpse into the life of a schoolboy in early 20th century Japan.

Genres: 
Epistolary fiction, Coming-of-age, Historical fiction

Themes: 
Identity, Cultural exploration, Adolescence, Education
