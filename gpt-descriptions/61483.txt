Synopsis: In 'Definitions in Political Economy,' Thomas Robert Malthus delves into the world of political economy, exploring the rules that should guide economists in defining and using their terms. He examines the deviations from these rules in the writings of his contemporaries, shedding light on the complexities and nuances of the field. Malthus's inquiry offers a thought-provoking analysis of the language and concepts used in political economy, inviting readers to question and reevaluate their understanding of this influential discipline.

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Philosophy

Themes: Language and terminology in political economy, Rules and guidelines in economic discourse, Critique of contemporary economic writings
