Synopsis: In "Darwinism (1889): An exposition of the theory of natural selection, with some of its applications," Alfred Russel Wallace delves into the groundbreaking theory of natural selection, exploring its implications and applications. Through a captivating exploration of the natural world, Wallace presents a compelling argument that challenges conventional beliefs and sheds light on the intricate mechanisms of evolution. With thought-provoking examples and insightful analysis, this work offers a fresh perspective on the origins of species and the forces that shape life on Earth.

Genres: Science, Non-fiction

Themes: Evolution, Natural selection, Biological diversity
