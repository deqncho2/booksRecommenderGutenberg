Synopsis: 

'Journal History of the Twenty-Ninth Ohio Veteran Volunteers, 1861-1865: Its Victories and its Reverses. And the campaigns and battles of Winchester, Port Republic, Cedar Mountain, Chancellorsville, Gettysburg, Lookout Mountain, Atlanta, the March to the Sea, and the campaign of the Carolinas, in which it bore an honorable part' is a gripping historical account that takes readers on a thrilling journey through the American Civil War. 

Through the eyes of the Twenty-Ninth Ohio Veteran Volunteers, the reader is transported to the battlefields of Winchester, Port Republic, Cedar Mountain, Chancellorsville, Gettysburg, Lookout Mountain, Atlanta, the March to the Sea, and the campaign of the Carolinas. The journal chronicles the victories and the heart-wrenching reverses experienced by the soldiers, providing a vivid and intimate portrayal of the horrors and heroism of war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: Courage and sacrifice, The impact of war on individuals and society, The resilience of the human spirit
