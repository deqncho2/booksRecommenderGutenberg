Synopsis: 'The Invention of Printing: A Collection of Facts and Opinions' delves into the fascinating history of early prints and playing cards, exploring the enigmatic block-books of the fifteenth century. The book also uncovers the legend of Lourens Janszoon Coster, a figure from Haarlem, and delves into the groundbreaking work of John Gutenberg and his associates. Through a collection of facts and opinions, Theodore Low De Vinne takes readers on a captivating journey through the origins and development of printing.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Art history, Biography

Themes: Innovation, Cultural transformation, Historical significance
