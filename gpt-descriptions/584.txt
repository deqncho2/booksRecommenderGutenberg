Synopsis:
'Our Nig; Or, Sketches from the Life of a Free Black, in a Two-story White House, North: Showing That Slavery's Shadows Fall Even There' is a captivating novel that delves into the life of a free black woman named Frado, who faces numerous challenges and hardships in a society still deeply affected by the shadows of slavery. Set in the North, the story follows Frado's journey as she navigates through a world that is far from the promised land of freedom and equality. From her struggles with poverty and discrimination to her search for identity and belonging, Frado's story is a powerful exploration of resilience and the enduring impact of slavery.

Genres:
Historical Fiction, Bildungsroman, Social Commentary

Themes:
Racial Identity, Social Inequality, Resilience, Discrimination
