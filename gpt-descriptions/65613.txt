Synopsis: In 'Ye Olde Mint', Frank H. Stewart takes readers on a captivating journey back to the year 1792, when the first U.S. Mint was established in Philadelphia. Through vivid descriptions and historical anecdotes, Stewart paints a fascinating picture of the Mint's location at Seventh Street and Sugar Alley (now Filbert Street). From the bustling streets of Philadelphia to the intricate processes of coin production, this book offers a glimpse into the birth of American currency and the challenges faced by the Mint in its early years. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, American history, numismatics

Themes: American identity, economic development, technological advancements
