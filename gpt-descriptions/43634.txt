Synopsis: In 'By-Ways of War: The Story of the Filibusters', James Jeffrey Roche takes readers on a thrilling journey through the forgotten world of filibustering, a daring and often controversial form of warfare. Set in the mid-19th century, this captivating historical account follows a group of adventurers who embark on audacious expeditions to overthrow governments and establish their own rule in foreign lands. Filled with tales of intrigue, danger, and political maneuvering, this book sheds light on a little-known aspect of history that will leave readers both fascinated and astounded.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, adventure, political

Themes: Exploration, rebellion, power dynamics
