Synopsis: 

In 'Ireland under the Stuarts and During the Interregnum, Vol. 3 (of 3), 1660-1690', Richard Bagwell takes readers on a captivating journey through a tumultuous period in Irish history. As the Stuarts regain power and the interregnum comes to an end, Ireland finds itself at a crossroads, torn between political factions and religious tensions. Bagwell delves into the intricate web of power struggles, rebellions, and alliances that shaped the destiny of the Irish people during this critical era. From the rise of the Protestant Ascendancy to the challenges faced by the Catholic majority, this volume offers a comprehensive exploration of the complex dynamics that defined Ireland under the Stuarts.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power struggles, Religious tensions, Political factions
