Synopsis: 

In "Isabel of Castile and the making of the Spanish nation, 1451-1504," Ierne L. Plunket takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of one of history's most influential queens. This meticulously researched biography delves into the remarkable story of Isabel of Castile, a woman who defied societal norms and played a pivotal role in shaping the destiny of Spain.

From her early years as a determined princess to her ascension to the throne, Isabel's unwavering determination and political acumen are brought to life in vivid detail. Plunket explores the challenges she faced, including the struggle for power, religious conflicts, and the quest for unity in a divided nation. Through her strategic alliances, military campaigns, and bold decisions, Isabel leaves an indelible mark on Spanish history.

As readers delve into the pages of this captivating biography, they will witness Isabel's unwavering commitment to her people, her unwavering faith, and her unwavering pursuit of a united Spain. Plunket's narrative skillfully weaves together historical facts, personal anecdotes, and political intrigue, painting a rich and nuanced portrait of a queen who defied expectations and left an enduring legacy.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and Politics, Gender Roles, Religion and Faith, Nation-building
