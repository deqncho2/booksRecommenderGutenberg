Synopsis: 

'Against Home Rule: The Case for the Union' takes readers on a captivating journey through the political landscape of early 20th century Ireland. Set against the backdrop of the heated debate surrounding Irish home rule, this thought-provoking work delves into the arguments and counterarguments that shaped the destiny of a nation. As the tension rises and alliances are tested, the reader is drawn into a world of political intrigue, passionate speeches, and the struggle for power. With its compelling narrative and rich historical context, this book offers a fascinating exploration of a pivotal moment in Irish history.

Genres: Historical, Political, Non-fiction

Themes: Nationalism, Identity, Power struggle, Political ideologies
