Synopsis: In 'The History of David Grieve' by Ward, Humphry, Mrs., readers are introduced to David Grieve, a young man with a passion for art and a desire to break free from the constraints of his working-class background. As he navigates the challenges of love, ambition, and societal expectations, David's journey takes him through the vibrant art scene of 19th-century Paris and the complexities of relationships back in his hometown. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this novel explores the transformative power of art and the pursuit of personal fulfillment.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Bildungsroman, Romance

Themes: Art and Creativity, Social Class, Identity and Self-Discovery
