Synopsis: 

'History of Company K of the 140th Regiment Pennsylvania Volunteers (1862-'65)' is a captivating and immersive account of the experiences and sacrifices made by the soldiers of Company K during the American Civil War. Written by Benjamin F. Powelson and Alexander Sweeney, this historical narrative takes readers on a journey through the trials and triumphs of this courageous group of men. From their initial enlistment to their final days on the battlefield, the book vividly portrays the camaraderie, bravery, and resilience of Company K as they navigate the horrors of war and fight for their beliefs.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: Patriotism, Brotherhood, Sacrifice, Resilience
