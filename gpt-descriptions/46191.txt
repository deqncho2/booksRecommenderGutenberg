Synopsis: In "Fighting Without a War: An Account of Military Intervention in North Russia," author Ralph Albertson takes readers on a gripping journey through the little-known military intervention in North Russia during the aftermath of World War I. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Albertson unveils the untold story of the brave soldiers who found themselves embroiled in a complex and dangerous conflict far from home. As the narrative unfolds, readers are immersed in the harsh realities of war, the political intricacies of international intervention, and the personal sacrifices made by those on the front lines. With its blend of historical accuracy and compelling storytelling, this book offers a captivating exploration of a forgotten chapter in military history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War, Military History

Themes: International intervention, Forgotten history, Sacrifice, Political complexities
