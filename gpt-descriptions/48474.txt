Synopsis: In 'Scorn of Women: A Play In Three Acts' by Jack London, the lives of two women from different social classes intertwine in a gripping tale of love, betrayal, and societal expectations. As their paths cross, secrets are revealed, and the consequences of their choices become increasingly dire. Set against the backdrop of early 20th-century America, this play explores the complexities of human relationships and the power dynamics that shape them.

Genres: Drama, Romance, Social Commentary

Themes: Love and passion, Class and social hierarchy, Gender roles and expectations
