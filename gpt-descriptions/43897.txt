Synopsis: 
In "Antoine of Oregon: A Story of the Oregon Trail" by James Otis, readers are transported back to the mid-19th century, where they follow the thrilling journey of Antoine, a young French-Canadian boy, and his family as they embark on the treacherous Oregon Trail. Facing numerous challenges, including harsh weather, dangerous encounters, and personal loss, Antoine must summon his courage and resilience to navigate the unforgiving wilderness and fulfill his family's dreams of a better life in the Oregon Territory. Along the way, Antoine forms unexpected friendships, discovers the true meaning of family, and learns valuable lessons about perseverance and the indomitable human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Coming-of-Age

Themes: Perseverance, Family, Friendship, Resilience
