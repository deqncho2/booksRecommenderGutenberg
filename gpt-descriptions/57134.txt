Synopsis: In 'A Manual of Bird Study: A Description of Twenty-Five Local Birds with Study Options', William H. Carr takes readers on an enchanting journey into the world of birds. Through vivid descriptions and captivating anecdotes, Carr introduces us to twenty-five local bird species, each with its own unique characteristics and behaviors. From the majestic Bald Eagle to the elusive Ruby-throated Hummingbird, readers will discover the fascinating lives of these feathered creatures. Alongside the descriptions, Carr provides study options, encouraging readers to observe and learn more about the birds in their own surroundings. This manual is a delightful resource for bird enthusiasts and nature lovers alike, offering a deeper understanding and appreciation for the avian world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Field Guide

Themes: Birdwatching, Wildlife Conservation, Environmental Education
