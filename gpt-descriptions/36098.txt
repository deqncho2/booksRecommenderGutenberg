Synopsis: In 'The Flowers of Evil', Charles Baudelaire takes readers on a mesmerizing journey through the dark and forbidden corners of the human psyche. Through a collection of poems, he explores the complexities of love, desire, beauty, and the inherent duality of human nature. With vivid and evocative imagery, Baudelaire delves into the depths of human emotions, exposing the raw and often unsettling truths that lie beneath the surface. This poetic masterpiece challenges societal norms and confronts the reader with the haunting beauty of the forbidden.

Genres: Poetry, Symbolism, Romanticism

Themes: Beauty and ugliness, Love and desire, Morality and sin, Alienation and isolation
