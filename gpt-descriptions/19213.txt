Synopsis: In this captivating volume, David Hume delves into the tumultuous period of English history from the reign of Henry VII to that of Mary. With meticulous research and a keen eye for detail, Hume explores the political intrigues, religious conflicts, and power struggles that shaped this era. From the rise of the Tudor dynasty to the challenges faced by the first female monarch, this volume offers a fascinating account of the events and personalities that defined England during this pivotal time.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Politics, Religion, Power dynamics
