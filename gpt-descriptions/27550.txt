Synopsis: 
In 'The Mother' by Norman Duncan, a young woman named Mary finds herself torn between societal expectations and her own desires when she becomes a mother unexpectedly. As she navigates the challenges of motherhood, Mary must confront her own fears, confrontations with her family, and the sacrifices she must make for her child. With vivid descriptions and emotional depth, this novel explores the complexities of motherhood and the choices we make for love.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Coming-of-age

Themes: Motherhood, Identity, Sacrifice
