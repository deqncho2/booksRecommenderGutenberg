Synopsis: In 'Memoir of Old Elizabeth, A Coloured Woman', we are transported to the early 19th century, where we follow the extraordinary life of Elizabeth, a woman of African descent who defied societal expectations and overcame numerous challenges. From her humble beginnings as a slave to her eventual freedom, Elizabeth's journey is one of resilience, courage, and the pursuit of justice. Through her captivating narrative, we witness the harsh realities of slavery, the complexities of racial identity, and the power of hope in the face of adversity. As Elizabeth's story unfolds, we are left inspired by her unwavering spirit and the indelible mark she leaves on history.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Fiction, Biography

Themes: Slavery, Identity, Resilience, Freedom, Social Justice
