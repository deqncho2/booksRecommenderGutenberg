Synopsis: 
In "Brother Jacob" by George Eliot, we follow the journey of David Faux, a young man with a cunning plan to escape his humble origins and rise in society. However, his plan takes an unexpected turn when he encounters his long-lost brother, Jacob, who is unlike anyone he has ever known. As David navigates the complexities of family, identity, and ambition, he is forced to confront the consequences of his actions and the true meaning of success.

Genres: 
Novella, Realism, Satire

Themes: 
Identity, Ambition, Family dynamics
