Synopsis: 

"The Founder of New France: A Chronicle of Champlain" by Charles W. Colby is a captivating historical biography that delves into the life and adventures of Samuel de Champlain, the visionary explorer and founder of New France. Set in the early 17th century, the book takes readers on a thrilling journey across the Atlantic as Champlain navigates uncharted waters, encounters indigenous peoples, and establishes the first permanent European settlement in Canada. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Colby paints a vivid portrait of Champlain's determination, courage, and unwavering commitment to his mission, making this a compelling read for history enthusiasts and adventure lovers alike.

Genres: Historical biography, Adventure, Exploration

Themes: Exploration and discovery, Colonialism, Indigenous relations, Leadership
