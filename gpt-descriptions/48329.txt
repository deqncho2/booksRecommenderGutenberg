Synopsis: 

In 'History of Sanitation', J. J. Cosgrove takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the fascinating evolution of sanitation practices. From ancient civilizations to modern societies, this book uncovers the surprising and often overlooked role that sanitation has played in shaping human history. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Cosgrove reveals the ingenious methods and innovative technologies that have been employed to combat disease, improve public health, and create more livable environments. From the sewers of ancient Rome to the sanitation revolution of the 19th century, this book offers a fresh perspective on the often taken-for-granted aspect of human civilization.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Science

Themes: Public health, Disease prevention, Technological advancements, Environmental impact
