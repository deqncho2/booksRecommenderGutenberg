Synopsis: In 'A Popular Account of Thugs and Dacoits, the Hereditary Garotters and Gang-Robbers of India', James Hutton takes readers on a captivating journey through the dark underbelly of India's criminal world. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Hutton unveils the hidden lives of the notorious thugs and dacoits who terrorized the country during the 19th century. From their secretive initiation rituals to their cunning methods of robbery and murder, this book offers a gripping exploration of the criminal minds that plagued India for generations. With thrilling anecdotes and historical context, Hutton sheds light on the complex social and cultural factors that gave rise to these criminal organizations, leaving readers both fascinated and horrified by the tales of these infamous outlaws.

Genres: Non-fiction, True Crime, History

Themes: Criminality, Social History, Cultural Anthropology
