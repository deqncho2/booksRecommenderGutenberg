Synopsis: 

'Pathology of Lying, Accusation, and Swindling: A Study in Forensic Psychology' delves into the intricate web of deception, manipulation, and deceit that exists within the human psyche. Written by renowned forensic psychologists Mary Tenney Healy and William Healy, this captivating study explores the dark underbelly of human behavior, examining the motivations behind lying, the consequences of false accusations, and the art of swindling. Through a series of compelling case studies and insightful analysis, the authors shed light on the complex psychological factors that drive individuals to engage in these deceptive practices. 

Genres: Psychology, True Crime, Non-fiction

Themes: Deception, Manipulation, Human Behavior, Forensic Psychology
