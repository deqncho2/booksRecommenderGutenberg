Synopsis: In "The Unlit Lamp" by Radclyffe Hall, we are introduced to Joan Ogden, a young woman with a burning desire for independence and self-discovery. Set in early 20th century England, Joan navigates the societal expectations placed upon her as she strives to find her own path in life. As she encounters various challenges and relationships, Joan must confront her own desires and make difficult choices that will shape her future. With richly drawn characters and a captivating narrative, this novel explores the complexities of identity, love, and the pursuit of personal fulfillment.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Historical

Themes: Independence, Self-discovery, Societal expectations
