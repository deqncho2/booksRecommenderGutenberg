Synopsis: In "The Ballad of Reading Gaol," Oscar Wilde takes readers on a haunting journey through the dark corridors of a prison, where he himself was once confined. Through vivid and poignant verses, he explores the lives of the inmates, their struggles, and the profound impact of their imprisonment. With a powerful narrative and lyrical beauty, Wilde delves into the depths of human suffering, redemption, and the universal longing for freedom.

Genres: Poetry, Ballad, Prison Literature

Themes: Crime and Punishment, Human Suffering, Redemption, Freedom
