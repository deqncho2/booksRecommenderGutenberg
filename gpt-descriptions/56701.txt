Synopsis: In "The Story of the Woman's Party," Inez Haynes Gillmore takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of the women's suffrage movement in the United States. Through the eyes of the courageous and determined women who fought for their right to vote, Gillmore unveils the challenges, triumphs, and sacrifices that shaped this pivotal moment in American history. From the suffrage parades to the relentless lobbying efforts, this book offers a compelling narrative that sheds light on the untold stories of the women who paved the way for gender equality.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Women's Studies

Themes: Women's rights, Activism, Political empowerment, Social change
