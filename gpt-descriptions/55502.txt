Synopsis: 
In the World by Maksim Gorky is a captivating novel that takes readers on a journey through the lives of a group of individuals living on the fringes of society. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the struggles, dreams, and aspirations of these characters as they navigate the harsh realities of poverty, oppression, and societal injustice. Through vivid descriptions and compelling storytelling, Gorky paints a vivid picture of the human condition, exploring themes of resilience, hope, and the pursuit of a better life. As the characters face various challenges and confront their own inner demons, readers are left pondering the complexities of the world and the indomitable spirit of the human soul.

Genres: Fiction, Social Realism, Naturalism

Themes: Poverty, Social Injustice, Resilience, Hope
