Synopsis: 

'Political Women, Vol. 2' by Menzies, Sutherland, active 1840-1883 is a captivating and thought-provoking historical novel that delves into the lives of influential women who shaped the political landscape of their time. Set against the backdrop of a rapidly changing society, this volume takes readers on a journey through the lives of remarkable women who defied societal norms and fought for their beliefs. From courageous suffragettes to trailblazing politicians, the book uncovers the untold stories of these extraordinary women, their triumphs, struggles, and the sacrifices they made to pave the way for future generations. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Women's Literature
Themes: Feminism, Power and Politics, Social Change
