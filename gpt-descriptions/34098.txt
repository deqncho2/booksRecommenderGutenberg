Synopsis: 

In "Science of Trapping," Elmer Harry Kreps delves into the fascinating world of fur-bearing animals, providing a comprehensive exploration of their nature, habits, and distribution. With a focus on practical methods for capturing these elusive creatures, Kreps offers invaluable insights into the art of trapping. From the cunning fox to the elusive mink, readers will be captivated by the intricate details of each animal's behavior and the strategies employed to outsmart them. As the author shares his wealth of knowledge and experience, readers will find themselves drawn into a world where patience, skill, and understanding of nature are essential. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature, Outdoor Skills

Themes: Animal behavior, Trapping techniques, Wildlife conservation
