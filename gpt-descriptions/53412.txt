Synopsis: In the final volume of "The Witchcraft Delusion in New England," authors Cotton Mather and Robert Calef delve into the gripping conclusion of the infamous witch trials that plagued the region. As the truth behind the accusations begins to unravel, the authors uncover shocking revelations and explore the complex web of fear, superstition, and power that led to the hysteria. With meticulous research and compelling narratives, Mather and Calef shed light on the dark chapter in American history, leaving readers captivated by the dramatic events that unfolded during the witchcraft delusion.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, True crime, American history

Themes: Hysteria, Power dynamics, Superstition, Justice and injustice
