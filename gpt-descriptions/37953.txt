Synopsis: 

'Italian Letters of a Diplomat's Wife: January-May, 1880; February-April, 1904' is a captivating collection of personal letters written by Mary King Waddington, the wife of a diplomat, during her time in Italy. Through her vivid and intimate correspondence, Waddington offers a unique glimpse into the political and social landscape of Italy during two distinct periods in history. From the bustling streets of Rome to the picturesque countryside, she shares her observations, encounters, and experiences, painting a rich tapestry of Italian culture and society. As the reader delves into Waddington's letters, they become immersed in her world, filled with intrigue, romance, and the complexities of diplomatic life.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Travelogue

Themes: Diplomacy, Cultural Exploration, Women's Perspectives, Historical Events
