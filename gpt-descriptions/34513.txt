Synopsis: 
'A Biographical Dictionary of Freethinkers of All Ages and Nations' by Wheeler, J. M. is a captivating exploration of the lives and ideas of remarkable individuals who dared to challenge conventional beliefs and embrace freethinking. From ancient philosophers to modern intellectuals, this comprehensive dictionary offers a fascinating glimpse into the lives of those who have shaped the course of human thought and challenged the status quo. Each entry provides a concise yet illuminating account of the individual's life, their contributions to freethought, and the impact they had on society. Whether it's the rebellious spirit of a Renaissance artist or the revolutionary ideas of a 19th-century philosopher, this book celebrates the courage and intellectual curiosity of those who have dared to question the prevailing norms of their time.

Genres: Biography, History, Philosophy

Themes: Freethought, Intellectual rebellion, Challenging societal norms
