Synopsis: In 'A Surgeon in Belgium', Dr. Henry Sessions Souttar takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences he faced as a surgeon during World War I. From the front lines to the operating table, Souttar's vivid and poignant accounts of the wounded soldiers he treated in Belgium provide a unique perspective on the devastating effects of war. As he navigates the chaos and horrors of the battlefield, Souttar's unwavering dedication to saving lives shines through, making this memoir a captivating and emotional read.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: War, Humanitarianism, Resilience, Sacrifice
