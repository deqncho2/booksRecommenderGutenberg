Synopsis: In 'Is Life Worth Living?' by W.H. Mallock, a mysterious stranger arrives in a small English village, challenging the residents to question the very essence of their existence. As he engages in thought-provoking conversations with the villagers, their beliefs and values are put to the test, leading to unexpected revelations and profound introspection. With its intriguing blend of philosophy, wit, and social commentary, this novel takes readers on a captivating journey that explores the meaning of life and the choices we make.

Genres: Philosophical fiction, Satire, Social commentary

Themes: Existentialism, Morality, Individualism, Society and its conventions
