Synopsis: 

In 'The American Indian in the United States, Period 1850-1914: ... The Present Condition of the American Indian; His Political History and Other Topics; A Plea for Justice,' Warren King Moorehead delves into the complex and often overlooked history of the American Indian during a critical period of American expansion. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Moorehead sheds light on the struggles, triumphs, and injustices faced by Native American tribes as they navigated the rapidly changing landscape of the United States.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Native American history, Cultural assimilation, Social justice, Government policies, Indigenous rights
