Synopsis: 

In 'Ancient Egyptian and Greek Looms', H. Ling Roth takes us on a captivating journey through time, unraveling the secrets of two ancient civilizations and their remarkable weaving techniques. From the intricate patterns of Egyptian linen to the vibrant tapestries of Greek mythology, this book delves into the fascinating world of looms and the artistry behind them. Through detailed descriptions and stunning illustrations, Roth paints a vivid picture of the loom's significance in both cultures, revealing surprising connections and shedding light on the ingenuity of these ancient civilizations. 

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Textile art, Cultural heritage
