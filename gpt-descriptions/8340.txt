Synopsis: 

In the Book of Habacuc, the prophet Habacuc questions God's justice and wonders why evil seems to go unpunished. As he seeks answers, he receives a divine revelation that challenges his understanding of the world and his role in it. Through vivid imagery and poetic language, Habacuc's journey unfolds, leading him to a deeper understanding of God's plan and the importance of faith in the face of adversity.

Genres: Religious literature, Poetry, Prophetic literature

Themes: Faith and doubt, Divine justice, Theodicy, Trust in God
