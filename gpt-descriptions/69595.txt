Synopsis: 
"The Life of John Metcalf, commonly called Blind Jack of Knaresborough" is a captivating biography that delves into the extraordinary life of John Metcalf, a blind man who defied all odds and became a legendary figure in 18th-century England. From his daring exploits in hunting and card-playing to his involvement in the expedition against the rebels in 1745, this book uncovers the remarkable adventures of Blind Jack. Moreover, it provides a fascinating account of his numerous contracts for constructing roads and bridges across Yorkshire, Lancashire, Derbyshire, and Cheshire. 

Genres: Biography, Adventure, Historical

Themes: Overcoming adversity, Perseverance, Ingenuity
