Synopsis: In 'Bygone Punishments' by Andrews, William, a renowned historian delves into the dark and intriguing world of historical punishments. From the chilling tales of medieval torture chambers to the bizarre methods of public humiliation, this captivating book uncovers the forgotten practices that were once used to maintain order and exact justice. As the author uncovers the secrets behind these punishments, readers are taken on a thrilling journey through time, exploring the psychological and societal implications of these brutal acts. Prepare to be both fascinated and horrified as you discover the shocking truth behind the bygone punishments that shaped our history.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, True Crime

Themes: Justice, Power, Societal Norms
