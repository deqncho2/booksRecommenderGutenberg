Synopsis: In 'Organization: How Armies are Formed for War' by Foster, Hubert, embark on a captivating exploration of the intricate process behind the formation of armies for war. Delve into the strategic planning, logistical challenges, and psychological dynamics that shape the creation of military forces. From the ancient battlefields to modern warfare, this book unveils the hidden complexities and fascinating stories behind the assembly of armies, shedding light on the crucial factors that determine their success or failure.

Genres: Military history, Non-fiction, War studies

Themes: Military strategy, Leadership, Logistics, Psychology of warfare
