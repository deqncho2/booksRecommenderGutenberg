Synopsis: 
In 'Some Forerunners of Italian Opera', Henderson takes us on a captivating journey through the early origins of Italian opera, unearthing the fascinating stories of the composers who paved the way for this beloved art form. From the enchanting melodies of Jacopo Peri to the dramatic compositions of Claudio Monteverdi, Henderson delves into the lives and works of these musical pioneers, revealing the struggles, triumphs, and innovations that shaped the birth of Italian opera. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, this book offers a captivating exploration of the rich history and evolution of this timeless genre.

Genres: Music History, Biography, Cultural Studies

Themes: Artistic Innovation, Musical Evolution, Historical Context
