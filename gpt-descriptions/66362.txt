Synopsis: 

'Gloucestershire Friends: Poems From a German Prison Camp' by F. W. Harvey takes readers on a poignant journey through the experiences of a group of friends held captive in a German prison camp during World War I. Through a collection of heartfelt poems, Harvey captures the camaraderie, resilience, and longing for freedom that these men endure in the face of adversity. From the mundane routines of camp life to the profound moments of hope and despair, the poems offer a glimpse into the human spirit's ability to find solace and beauty even in the darkest of circumstances. 

Genres: Poetry, War Literature, Historical Fiction

Themes: Friendship, Resilience, Captivity, Human Spirit, Hope
