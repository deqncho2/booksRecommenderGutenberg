Synopsis: 
In 'The Young Captives: A Story of Judah and Babylon' by Erasmus W. Jones, readers are transported to the ancient city of Babylon, where the lives of two young captives from Judah, Daniel and his friends, take an unexpected turn. As they navigate the treacherous world of the Babylonian court, they must confront their own beliefs, face moral dilemmas, and find the strength to stay true to their faith. Amidst political intrigue, cultural clashes, and personal challenges, the young captives embark on a journey that will test their resilience and shape their destinies.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Religious Fiction

Themes: Faith and Belief, Cultural Identity, Moral Dilemmas
