Synopsis:
In "The New Nation," Paxson takes readers on a captivating journey through the early years of the United States, exploring the challenges and triumphs that shaped the nation. From the aftermath of the Revolutionary War to the establishment of a new government, Paxson delves into the lives of key figures such as George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, and Alexander Hamilton, revealing the intricate web of political, social, and economic forces that shaped the birth of a new nation. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Paxson paints a vivid picture of the struggles and aspirations of the American people during this pivotal period in history.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Biography

Themes:
Nation-building, Political struggles, Founding Fathers, American Revolution, Early American history
