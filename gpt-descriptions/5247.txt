Synopsis: 
'The Old Wives' Tale' by Arnold Bennett is a captivating novel that follows the lives of two sisters, Constance and Sophia, as they navigate the challenges and triumphs of their individual journeys. Set in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, the story takes us from the quaint town of Bursley to the bustling streets of Paris. As the sisters face love, loss, and the passage of time, their paths diverge in unexpected ways, leading them to discover the true essence of their own identities. With richly drawn characters and a vivid portrayal of the changing times, 'The Old Wives' Tale' is a timeless tale of resilience, self-discovery, and the enduring bond of sisterhood.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction

Themes: Identity, Sisterhood, Time and Change
