Synopsis: In "An Economic Interpretation of the Constitution of the United States," Charles A. Beard delves into the hidden motivations and economic interests that shaped the drafting of the American Constitution. Through meticulous research and analysis, Beard uncovers a fascinating web of economic factors that influenced the framers' decisions, challenging traditional notions of the Constitution's purely ideological origins. As he unravels the intricate connections between wealth, power, and political decision-making, Beard sheds light on the complex interplay between economic interests and the formation of a nation's governing principles.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Economic determinism, Political power, Class struggle, Constitutional interpretation
