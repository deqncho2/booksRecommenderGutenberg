Synopsis: 
In "The Public and Its Problems," John Dewey delves into the complexities of democracy and the challenges faced by modern societies. Through a thought-provoking exploration of the relationship between the public and its government, Dewey offers insightful perspectives on the nature of public opinion, the role of education, and the importance of active citizen participation. As he navigates through the intricacies of public life, Dewey presents a compelling argument for the need to redefine and revitalize democracy in order to address the pressing issues of our time.

Genres: Philosophy, Political Science, Sociology

Themes: Democracy, Public Opinion, Education, Citizen Participation
