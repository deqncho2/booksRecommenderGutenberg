Synopsis: 
In 'Music and Its Masters' by Boise, O. B., embark on a captivating journey through the world of music as the author delves into the lives and legacies of some of the greatest composers and musicians in history. From the enigmatic Beethoven to the virtuosic Mozart, and from the passionate Tchaikovsky to the revolutionary Stravinsky, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the lives, struggles, and triumphs of these musical geniuses. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, 'Music and Its Masters' unveils the secrets behind their timeless compositions, shedding light on the profound impact they had on the world of music and the human spirit.

Genres: Biography, Music History, Non-fiction

Themes: Artistic genius, Musical innovation, Human emotion and expression
