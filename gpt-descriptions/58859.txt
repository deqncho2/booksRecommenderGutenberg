Synopsis: 
In 'Medical Inquiries and Observations, Vol. 1: The Second Edition, Revised and Enlarged by the Author', Benjamin Rush, a renowned physician and founding father of the United States, delves into a captivating exploration of various medical topics. From the causes and treatments of diseases to the effects of climate on health, Rush presents a wealth of knowledge and insights gained from his extensive experience in the field. With a blend of scientific rigor and engaging storytelling, this volume offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of medicine during the late 18th century.

Genres: Non-fiction, Medical literature, Historical literature

Themes: Medicine and healthcare, Disease and treatment, Scientific inquiry
