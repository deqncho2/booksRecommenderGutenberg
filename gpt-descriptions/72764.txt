Synopsis: In 'The Mongols in Russia' by Curtin, Jeremiah, embark on a captivating journey through the tumultuous era of the Mongol Empire's invasion of Russia. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous historical research, the author unveils the gripping tale of the clash between two mighty civilizations, exploring the profound impact it had on the Russian people and their struggle for survival. From epic battles to political intrigue, this enthralling narrative sheds light on a pivotal moment in history that forever shaped the destiny of Russia.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Cultural studies

Themes: Conquest and colonization, Cultural assimilation, Resistance and resilience
