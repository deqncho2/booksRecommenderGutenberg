Synopsis: In 'The Young People's Wesley', McDonald takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of John Wesley, the founder of Methodism. Through vivid storytelling and engaging anecdotes, the author explores Wesley's early years, his struggles and triumphs, and his unwavering dedication to his faith. From his transformative experiences at Oxford University to his groundbreaking preaching tours, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of a man who shaped the course of Christianity. With its compelling narrative and inspiring message, 'The Young People's Wesley' is a must-read for anyone seeking to understand the life and legacy of this influential figure.

Genres: Biography, Historical Fiction, Religious Literature

Themes: Faith, Perseverance, Spiritual Awakening
