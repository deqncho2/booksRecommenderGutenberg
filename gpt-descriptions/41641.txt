Synopsis: 
In 'Just Sixteen' by Susan Coolidge, we follow the story of a young girl named Rose Red, who finds herself facing unexpected challenges as she navigates the complexities of adolescence. Set in the late 19th century, Rose Red's journey takes her from the comforts of her family home to the bustling streets of Boston, where she encounters new friendships, heartaches, and the pursuit of her dreams. As Rose Red grapples with the pressures of society and the expectations placed upon her, she must learn to find her own voice and embrace the power of self-discovery.

Genres: Coming-of-age, Historical Fiction, Romance

Themes: Identity, Independence, Love and Relationships, Societal Expectations
