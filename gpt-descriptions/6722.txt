Synopsis: In "The Seven Who Were Hanged," Andreyev takes us on a gripping journey through the lives of seven individuals who find themselves on death row. As each character awaits their impending execution, their stories intertwine, revealing the depths of their humanity, the injustices they have faced, and the profound impact their impending deaths have on their own lives and the lives of those around them. With haunting prose and a relentless exploration of the human condition, Andreyev delves into the complexities of guilt, redemption, and the fragility of life itself.

Genres: Fiction, Drama, Psychological Thriller

Themes: Death and mortality, Justice and injustice, Human nature and morality
