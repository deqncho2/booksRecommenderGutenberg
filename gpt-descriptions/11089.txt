Synopsis: In "The Education of the Negro Prior to 1861," Carter G. Woodson delves into the often overlooked history of the education of African Americans in the United States before the Civil War. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Woodson uncovers the struggles, triumphs, and resilience of the colored people in their pursuit of knowledge and intellectual freedom. From the early days of slavery to the eve of the Civil War, this book sheds light on the educational experiences of African Americans and the profound impact it had on their lives and the nation as a whole.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, African American studies, Education

Themes: Racial inequality, Education as a tool for liberation, Historical resilience and perseverance
