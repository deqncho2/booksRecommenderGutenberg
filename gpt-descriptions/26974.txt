Synopsis: 

'Prisoners Their Own Warders: A Record of the Convict Prison at Singapore in the Straits Settlements Established 1825' takes readers on a captivating journey into the depths of a convict prison in Singapore during the 19th century. Through the eyes of the authors, Bayliss and McNair, the reader is immersed in the daily lives, struggles, and surprising resilience of the prisoners who become their own warders. This gripping account sheds light on the unique dynamics and unexpected transformations that occur within the prison walls, challenging preconceived notions and leaving readers eager to uncover the secrets hidden within.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Crime

Themes: Incarceration, Rehabilitation, Human resilience
