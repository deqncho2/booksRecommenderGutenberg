Synopsis: In 'Telepathy, Genuine and Fraudulent', W. W. Baggally delves into the mysterious world of telepathy, exploring both the genuine and fraudulent aspects of this extraordinary phenomenon. Through captivating anecdotes and meticulous investigations, Baggally takes readers on a thrilling journey, unraveling the secrets behind telepathic communication. From mind-reading experiments to encounters with renowned mediums, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the boundaries between science and the supernatural.

Genres: Non-fiction, Paranormal, Psychology

Themes: Telepathy, Psychic Phenomena, Skepticism, Scientific Inquiry
