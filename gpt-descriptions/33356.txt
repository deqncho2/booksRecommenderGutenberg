Synopsis: In 'The Vagabond in Literature' by Compton-Rickett, Arthur, embark on a captivating journey through the pages of literature as the author explores the enigmatic figure of the vagabond. From the wandering troubadours of medieval times to the modern-day nomads, this book delves into the rich tapestry of stories that have immortalized these elusive wanderers. Discover the allure of the open road, the complexities of human nature, and the timeless quest for freedom that resonates within the vagabond's soul.

Genres: Literary criticism, cultural studies, travel literature

Themes: Freedom, wanderlust, societal norms, human connection
