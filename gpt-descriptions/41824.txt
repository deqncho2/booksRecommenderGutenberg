Synopsis:
In 'Hogarth' by C. Lewis Hind, a mysterious and enigmatic artist named Hogarth emerges in the art world, captivating everyone with his unique and unconventional style. As his fame grows, so does the intrigue surrounding his true identity and the inspiration behind his mesmerizing creations. Set against the backdrop of the vibrant art scene of early 20th century London, this gripping tale delves into the complexities of art, identity, and the blurred lines between reality and imagination.

Genres:
Mystery, Historical Fiction, Art

Themes:
Identity, Artistic Expression, Perception of Reality
