Synopsis: In 'Personal Recollections and Civil War Diary, 1864', Lemuel Abijah Abbott takes us on a gripping journey through his experiences as a soldier during the American Civil War. From the front lines of battle to the intimate moments of camaraderie and despair, Abbott's vivid and heartfelt recollections provide a unique perspective on the horrors and triumphs of war. As he navigates the challenges of military life and grapples with the moral complexities of conflict, Abbott's diary becomes a testament to the resilience of the human spirit in the face of unimaginable adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: War and its impact on individuals, Brotherhood and camaraderie, Morality and ethics in times of conflict
