Synopsis: 

In "Selected Records of Reptiles and Amphibians from Kansas," Smith and Breukelman take readers on a captivating journey through the diverse and often mysterious world of reptiles and amphibians found in the state of Kansas. From the secretive salamanders hiding in the depths of the state's rivers to the vibrant and colorful snakes slithering through its grasslands, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the unique and often overlooked creatures that call Kansas home. Through detailed descriptions, stunning photographs, and insightful anecdotes, the authors shed light on the behavior, habitats, and conservation efforts surrounding these captivating creatures. Whether you are a seasoned herpetologist or simply curious about the natural world, this book is sure to ignite your curiosity and leave you with a newfound appreciation for the reptiles and amphibians that inhabit the heartland of America.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Wildlife

Themes: Biodiversity, Conservation, Ecological Interactions
