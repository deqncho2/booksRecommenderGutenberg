Synopsis: 
"The Secrets of the Great City: A Work Descriptive of the Virtues and the Vices, the Mysteries, Miseries and Crimes of New York City" takes readers on a captivating journey through the underbelly of one of the world's most iconic cities. Written by James Dabney McCabe, this work delves into the hidden secrets, scandals, and dark corners of New York City, exposing the vices, mysteries, miseries, and crimes that lurk beneath its glamorous surface. From the opulent mansions of the elite to the squalid tenements of the poor, McCabe uncovers the shocking truths that lie behind the city's glittering facade. With vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, this book offers a gripping exploration of the complex and multifaceted nature of New York City.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, True Crime

Themes: Urban life, Social inequality, Corruption, Morality
