Synopsis: In the second book of the Chronicles of England, Scotland and Ireland, we delve into the reign of Edward II, the son of Edward I. This captivating historical account takes us through the tumultuous events that shaped Edward II's rule, including his struggles with the Scottish rebellion led by Robert the Bruce and the internal conflicts within his own kingdom. As Edward II faces numerous challenges and makes fateful decisions, the reader is drawn into a world of political intrigue, power struggles, and the consequences of failed leadership. 

Genres: Historical fiction, Biography, Political drama

Themes: Power and leadership, Loyalty and betrayal, Conflict and rebellion
