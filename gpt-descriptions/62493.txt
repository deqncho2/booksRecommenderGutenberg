Synopsis: In "The Close of the Middle Ages, 1272-1494, 3rd Ed." by Richard Lodge, Sir, embark on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period that marked the end of the Middle Ages. From the rise of powerful monarchs to the clash of empires, this book delves into the political, social, and cultural transformations that shaped Europe during this pivotal era. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Lodge paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs that defined the closing chapter of the Middle Ages.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Power and monarchy, Cultural and social change, Conflict and empire
