Synopsis: In "Cleopatra: A Study" by Henry Houssaye, readers are transported to ancient Egypt, where the captivating and enigmatic Queen Cleopatra reigns. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, Houssaye delves into the life of this legendary figure, exploring her rise to power, her passionate relationships, and her ultimate downfall. As the narrative unfolds, readers are immersed in a world of political intrigue, love affairs, and the clash of empires, all against the backdrop of a civilization on the brink of change. With rich historical detail and a compelling narrative, "Cleopatra: A Study" offers a captivating exploration of one of history's most fascinating women.

Genres: Historical fiction, Biography, Historical analysis

Themes: Power and politics, Love and passion, Gender and identity
