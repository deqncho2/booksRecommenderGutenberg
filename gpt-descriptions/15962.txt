Synopsis: In 'Essays on Political Economy', Frédéric Bastiat takes readers on a thought-provoking journey through the intricacies of economic systems and their impact on society. Through a series of engaging essays, Bastiat challenges conventional wisdom and offers fresh perspectives on topics such as taxation, protectionism, and the role of government in the economy. With his signature wit and clarity, Bastiat presents compelling arguments that will leave readers questioning their own assumptions and reevaluating their understanding of political economy.

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Political Science

Themes: Free trade, Government intervention, Taxation, Protectionism, Economic liberty
