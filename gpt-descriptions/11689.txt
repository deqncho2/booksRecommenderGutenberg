Synopsis: 
'Are Women People? A Book of Rhymes for Suffrage Times' by Alice Duer Miller is a collection of witty and thought-provoking rhymes that shed light on the struggles and inequalities faced by women during the suffrage movement. Through clever verses and humorous anecdotes, Miller challenges societal norms and advocates for women's rights, urging readers to question the status quo and recognize the importance of gender equality. This collection serves as a powerful reminder of the ongoing fight for women's rights and the need for social change.

Genres: Poetry, Feminist Literature, Historical

Themes: Women's Rights, Gender Equality, Social Change
