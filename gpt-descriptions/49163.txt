Synopsis: 
'Journal of a Soldier of the Seventy-First, or Glasgow Regiment, Highland Light Infantry, from 1806-1815' takes readers on an extraordinary journey through the eyes of an anonymous soldier. From the battlefields of the Napoleonic Wars to the hardships of military life, this gripping journal offers a firsthand account of the soldier's experiences, emotions, and the camaraderie forged in the face of adversity. As the soldier navigates the horrors of war and the complexities of loyalty, readers are transported to a tumultuous era that shaped the destiny of nations.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Memoir, War

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, Loyalty and camaraderie in the face of adversity, The human experience in times of conflict
