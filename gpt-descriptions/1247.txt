Synopsis: 
In 'Second April', Edna St. Vincent Millay takes readers on a captivating journey through the complexities of love, loss, and the pursuit of self-discovery. Through a collection of poignant and evocative poems, Millay explores the depths of human emotions, delving into themes of passion, desire, and the bittersweet nature of existence. With her lyrical and introspective writing style, she invites readers to reflect on the beauty and fragility of life, leaving them yearning for more.

Genres: Poetry, Romance, Literary

Themes: Love, Loss, Self-discovery, Passion, Existence
