Synopsis: In 'Mr. Clutterbuck's Election' by Belloc, Hilaire, the small town of Clapham is thrown into chaos when the eccentric Mr. Clutterbuck decides to run for Parliament. As the campaign unfolds, the townspeople find themselves caught up in a whirlwind of outrageous promises, scandalous secrets, and unexpected alliances. With humor and wit, Belloc satirizes the world of politics, exposing the absurdities and follies that often accompany elections.

Genres: Satire, Comedy, Political Fiction

Themes: Politics, Power, Corruption, Social Commentary
