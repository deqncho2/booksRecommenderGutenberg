Synopsis: In 'A History of the Peninsular War, Vol. 5, Oct. 1811-Aug. 31, 1812: Valencia, Ciudad Rodrigo, Badajoz, Salamanca, Madrid', Charles Oman takes us on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the Peninsular War. From the strategic battles in Valencia and the sieges of Ciudad Rodrigo and Badajoz, to the decisive clash in Salamanca and the eventual capture of Madrid, Oman paints a vivid picture of the intense conflicts that shaped this pivotal period in European history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, War narrative

Themes: War and strategy, Leadership and tactics, Political and social upheaval
