Synopsis: In "Stone's River: The Turning-Point of the Civil War," Wilson J. Vance takes readers on a gripping journey through one of the most crucial battles of the American Civil War. Set against the backdrop of the brutal conflict, this meticulously researched historical account delves into the strategic maneuvers, the personal sacrifices, and the harrowing experiences of soldiers on both sides. As the battle unfolds, tensions rise, alliances are tested, and the fate of the nation hangs in the balance. With vivid storytelling and a keen eye for detail, Vance brings to life the pivotal moments that shaped the outcome of the war, leaving readers on the edge of their seats until the very last page.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American history

Themes: War and its consequences, Leadership and decision-making, Sacrifice and bravery
