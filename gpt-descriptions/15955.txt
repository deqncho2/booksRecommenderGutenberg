Synopsis: 
In 'A Short History of Scotland', Andrew Lang takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich and tumultuous history of Scotland. From the ancient Picts and Romans to the fierce battles of the clans and the rise of the Scottish Enlightenment, Lang weaves together a tapestry of stories, legends, and historical events that shaped the nation. With his engaging narrative style, he explores the struggles for independence, the influence of religion, and the enduring spirit of the Scottish people. From the mystical landscapes of the Highlands to the bustling streets of Edinburgh, this concise history offers a fascinating glimpse into the heart and soul of Scotland.

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: Scottish history, Independence, Cultural identity
