Synopsis: In 'Aleph, the Chaldean; or, the Messiah as Seen from Alexandria', Enoch Fitch Burr takes us on a captivating journey through ancient Alexandria, where a mysterious Chaldean scholar named Aleph unravels the secrets of the universe. As Aleph delves into the mystical teachings of the Chaldeans, he becomes entangled in a web of political intrigue, forbidden love, and divine revelations. With each revelation, Aleph's understanding of the Messiah deepens, leading him to question his own beliefs and the fate of humanity. This thought-provoking and enchanting tale will transport readers to a world where faith, knowledge, and destiny collide.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Religious Fiction

Themes: Faith and Doubt, Knowledge and Enlightenment, Love and Sacrifice
