Synopsis: In 'The Syrian Christ' by Abraham Mitrie Rihbany, a young Syrian man named Khalil embarks on a journey of self-discovery and spiritual awakening. Leaving behind his homeland and family, he travels to America, where he encounters a society vastly different from his own. As he navigates the challenges of assimilation and cultural clashes, Khalil grapples with questions of identity, faith, and the meaning of life. Through his experiences, he learns valuable lessons about love, compassion, and the power of forgiveness. 

Genres: Autobiography, Fiction, Cultural

Themes: Identity, Faith, Cultural Clash, Self-discovery, Love, Forgiveness
