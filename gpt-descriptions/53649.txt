Synopsis: In "Horace Walpole: A memoir: With an appendix of books printed at the Strawberry Hill Press," Austin Dobson delves into the fascinating life of Horace Walpole, an influential figure of the 18th century. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Dobson uncovers the intricate details of Walpole's life, from his aristocratic upbringing to his literary achievements and political endeavors. This memoir offers a captivating glimpse into the world of a man who was not only a prominent writer but also a collector, politician, and socialite. With an appendix showcasing the books printed at Walpole's renowned Strawberry Hill Press, this work provides a comprehensive exploration of his multifaceted legacy.

Genres: Biography, History, Literary Criticism

Themes: Biography, Literature, Politics, Socialite Life
