Synopsis: In 'The Irish War', author David Burr takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous period of Ireland's struggle for independence. Set against the backdrop of the early 20th century, this historical account delves into the lives of key figures, the political landscape, and the intense conflicts that shaped the nation's destiny. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Burr paints a vivid picture of the Irish War, capturing the bravery, sacrifice, and resilience of those who fought for their homeland's freedom.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War, Biography

Themes: Independence, Nationalism, Political Struggle, Identity
