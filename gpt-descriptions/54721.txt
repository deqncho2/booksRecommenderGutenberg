Synopsis: In 'The Food Question: Health and Economy', a renowned economist and a leading nutritionist join forces to explore the intricate relationship between food, health, and the economy. Through a captivating blend of research, case studies, and personal anecdotes, they delve into the hidden costs of our modern food system and the profound impact it has on our well-being and financial stability. As they uncover startling truths and propose innovative solutions, readers are taken on a thought-provoking journey that challenges conventional wisdom and offers a fresh perspective on the food we consume. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Health

Themes: Food systems, Public health, Economic impact, Sustainability
