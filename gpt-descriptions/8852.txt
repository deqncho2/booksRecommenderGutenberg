Synopsis: In 'Weymouth New Testament in Modern Speech, 3 John', Richard Francis Weymouth presents a captivating and modernized version of the third epistle of John. This short but powerful letter follows the struggles and triumphs of a faithful servant of God named Gaius, who faces opposition and persecution as he seeks to spread the message of love and truth. As Gaius navigates the complexities of his mission, he encounters unexpected allies and adversaries, leading to a climactic confrontation that will test his resolve and faith. Weymouth's translation brings this ancient text to life, immersing readers in the timeless themes of faith, perseverance, and the power of love.

Genres: Religious literature, Epistle, Christian fiction

Themes: Faith, Perseverance, Love, Spiritual warfare
