Synopsis: 
In the Royal Naval Air Service: Being the war letters of the late Harold Rosher to his family takes readers on a captivating journey through the eyes of Harold Rosher, a young man serving in the Royal Naval Air Service during World War I. Through a series of heartfelt and vividly written letters, Rosher shares his experiences, emotions, and the challenges he faces as he navigates the perils of war. From the exhilaration of flying high above the clouds to the heart-wrenching moments of loss and the bonds formed with fellow servicemen, this collection of letters offers a poignant and intimate glimpse into the life of a brave airman during a tumultuous time in history.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Literature, Epistolary

Themes: War and its impact on individuals, camaraderie and friendship, the human spirit in the face of adversity
