Synopsis: In "The Women of the Mayflower and Women of Plymouth Colony," Ethel J. R. C. Noyes delves into the untold stories of the courageous women who played a pivotal role in the founding of Plymouth Colony. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Noyes brings to life the struggles, triumphs, and resilience of these remarkable women. From their perilous journey aboard the Mayflower to their efforts in building a new community in the harsh wilderness, this book sheds light on the often overlooked contributions of these unsung heroines. Discover the extraordinary tales of strength, sacrifice, and determination that shaped the early days of America.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography

Themes: Women's History, Colonial America, Pioneering Spirit
