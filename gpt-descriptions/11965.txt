Synopsis: In 'Sex and Common-Sense' by Royden, A. Maude, a young woman named Emily finds herself torn between societal expectations and her own desires. As she navigates the complexities of love, marriage, and personal fulfillment, Emily must confront the limitations imposed on women in the early 20th century. With a cast of intriguing characters and unexpected twists, this thought-provoking novel explores the delicate balance between passion and practicality.

Genres: Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-age

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, self-discovery
