Synopsis: In 'Diddie, Dumps & Tot; or, Plantation child-life', Louise Clarke Pyrnelle takes readers on a captivating journey into the lives of three young siblings growing up on a Southern plantation. Through their adventures and misadventures, the children navigate the complexities of race, family, and friendship, while discovering the power of resilience and the importance of love and understanding.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Family Drama

Themes: Racial dynamics, Childhood innocence, Resilience, Family bonds
