Synopsis: 

In "Harlow Niles Higinbotham: A memoir with brief autobiography and extracts from speeches and letters," Harriet Monroe delves into the extraordinary life of Harlow Niles Higinbotham, a man whose impact on society and dedication to justice shaped the course of history. Through a combination of personal anecdotes, insightful letters, and powerful speeches, Monroe paints a vivid portrait of a man who defied societal norms and fought tirelessly for equality and progress. From his humble beginnings to his rise as a prominent figure in the legal and political spheres, Higinbotham's journey is one of resilience, passion, and unwavering commitment to his ideals. As readers delve into this captivating memoir, they will be inspired by Higinbotham's unwavering determination and moved by the profound impact he had on the world.

Genres: Biography, Memoir, History

Themes: Justice, Equality, Social Change
