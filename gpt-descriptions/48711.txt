Synopsis: In 'Hints on Mountain-Lion Trapping', Young, Stanley Paul takes readers on an exhilarating journey into the treacherous world of mountain-lion trapping. With vivid descriptions and heart-pounding encounters, this gripping narrative follows the author's quest to outsmart and capture these elusive predators. From the rugged landscapes of the wilderness to the adrenaline-fueled moments of danger, this book offers a thrilling exploration of the art and science of trapping mountain lions.

Genres: Non-fiction, Adventure, Nature

Themes: Wildlife conservation, Survival skills, Man vs. nature
