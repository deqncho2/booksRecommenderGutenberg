Synopsis: 
"Through the Iron Bars: Two Years of German Occupation in Belgium" by Emile Cammaerts is a gripping firsthand account of the author's experiences during the German occupation of Belgium during World War I. Cammaerts vividly describes the daily struggles, hardships, and acts of resistance that he and his fellow countrymen endured under the oppressive regime. From the initial shock of invasion to the resilience and determination of the Belgian people, this powerful narrative offers a unique perspective on the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, War literature

Themes: Occupation and resistance, Human resilience, Effects of war on civilians
