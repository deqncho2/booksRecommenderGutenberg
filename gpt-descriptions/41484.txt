Synopsis: In the fifth volume of "History of the Reformation in the Sixteenth Century," Merle d'Aubigné delves into the captivating story of the Reformation in England. From the tumultuous reign of Henry VIII to the rise of Queen Elizabeth I, this book uncovers the political and religious struggles that shaped the destiny of a nation. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, d'Aubigné explores the key figures, conflicts, and events that transformed England's religious landscape forever.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Religious history

Themes: Religious reform, Political intrigue, Cultural transformation
