Synopsis: 

In 'Representative Plays by American Dramatists: 1856-1911:: Introduction and Bibliography', Montrose Jonas Moses takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of American theater during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. This comprehensive collection introduces readers to a diverse range of plays that reflect the social, cultural, and political landscape of the time. From the works of renowned playwrights such as Eugene O'Neill and Susan Glaspell to lesser-known gems, Moses provides a fascinating exploration of the American dramatic tradition.

Genres: Drama, Historical, Literary Criticism

Themes: Social change, Identity, Morality, Gender roles
