Synopsis: In the bustling city of Chicago during the late 19th century, a diverse group of individuals find themselves intertwined in a web of ambition, love, and social change. From the wealthy elite to the struggling working class, their lives intersect in unexpected ways, revealing the stark contrasts and hidden complexities of urban life. As they navigate the challenges of a rapidly changing society, they must confront their own desires, aspirations, and the consequences of their choices. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, 'The Cliff-Dwellers: A Novel' offers a captivating glimpse into the vibrant tapestry of city life and the human spirit.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Social Realism, Urban Fiction

Themes: Social class, Ambition, Love and relationships, Urbanization, Identity
