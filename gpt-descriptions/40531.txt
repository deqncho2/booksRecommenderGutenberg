Synopsis: 

'A Century in the Comptroller's Office, State of New York, 1797 to 1897' takes readers on a captivating journey through the inner workings of one of the most influential government offices in American history. Written by James A. Roberts, this meticulously researched book unveils the untold stories, scandals, and triumphs that shaped the financial landscape of New York State over the course of a century. From political power struggles to economic crises, the Comptroller's Office becomes a stage for both heroes and villains, as they navigate the complex world of finance and governance. As the narrative unfolds, readers will be enthralled by the secrets and intrigues that lie behind the seemingly mundane world of accounting and auditing. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Financial history

Themes: Government corruption, Financial accountability, Political power struggles, Economic development
