Synopsis: In "Father Damien: An Open Letter to the Reverend Dr. Hyde of Honolulu," Robert Louis Stevenson pens a powerful and thought-provoking letter addressing the controversial views of Reverend Dr. Hyde towards Father Damien, a selfless missionary who dedicated his life to serving a leper colony in Hawaii. Through eloquent prose and heartfelt arguments, Stevenson challenges the Reverend's criticisms and sheds light on the true nature of Father Damien's heroic actions. This open letter serves as a testament to the unwavering compassion and sacrifice of a remarkable man.

Genres: Non-fiction, Epistolary

Themes: Selflessness, Compassion, Religious Duty
