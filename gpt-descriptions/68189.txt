Synopsis: In "Independence: Rectorial address delivered at St. Andrews October 10, 1923," Rudyard Kipling delivers a powerful and thought-provoking speech that challenges conventional wisdom and explores the concept of independence. Through his eloquent words, Kipling delves into the complexities of individuality, self-reliance, and the importance of embracing one's own path. This address captivates readers with its profound insights and encourages them to question societal norms and embrace their own unique journey.

Genres: Speech, Essay, Non-fiction

Themes: Independence, Individuality, Self-reliance, Challenging societal norms
