Synopsis: 

'Oppressions of the Sixteenth Century in the Islands of Orkney and Zetland: From Original Documents' takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of the sixteenth century in the remote islands of Orkney and Zetland. Drawing from a wealth of original documents, this historical work unveils a hidden world of political intrigue, power struggles, and social upheaval. From the struggles of the local inhabitants against oppressive rulers to the clash of cultures between the islanders and the mainland, this book sheds light on a fascinating and often overlooked chapter in history. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Cultural studies

Themes: Oppression, Power dynamics, Cultural clashes, Resistance, Historical documentation
