Synopsis: In 'The History of Currency, 1252 to 1896' by Shaw, William Arthur, embark on a captivating journey through time as the author unravels the fascinating evolution of money. From the humble beginnings of bartering to the rise of complex financial systems, this meticulously researched book delves into the intricate web of economic, political, and cultural factors that shaped the currencies we use today. Discover the stories behind ancient coins, explore the impact of global trade on monetary systems, and witness the birth of modern banking. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Shaw brings to life the captivating history of currency, offering a fresh perspective on the world of money.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Economics

Themes: Evolution of money, Economic systems, Global trade, Cultural influences
