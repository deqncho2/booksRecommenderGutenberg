Synopsis: 

In 'Medical Investigation in Seventeenth Century England: Papers Read at a Clark Library Seminar, October 14, 1967', Bodemer and King delve into the fascinating world of medical practices during the 17th century in England. Through a collection of papers presented at a seminar, they explore the various methods, theories, and challenges faced by physicians and surgeons of the time. From the study of anatomy to the treatment of diseases, this work offers a captivating glimpse into the medical landscape of this era.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medical History

Themes: Medical practices, Scientific advancements, Historical context
