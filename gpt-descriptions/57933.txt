Synopsis: In "Angels of the Battlefield: A History of the Labors of the Catholic Sisterhoods in the Late Civil War," George Barton delves into the untold stories of the courageous Catholic sisterhoods who played a vital role in the American Civil War. From the battlefields to the hospitals, these selfless women risked their lives to provide care and comfort to wounded soldiers, defying societal norms and challenging the boundaries of gender roles. Through meticulous research and captivating narratives, Barton sheds light on the remarkable contributions of these unsung heroes, revealing a hidden chapter of history that is both inspiring and heart-wrenching.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Women's Studies

Themes: Heroism, Gender roles, Sacrifice, Compassion, War
