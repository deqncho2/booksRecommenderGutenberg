Synopsis: In 'The Children's Story of the War Volume 4: The Story of the Year 1915', author Edward Parrott takes young readers on a captivating journey through the events of the year 1915 during World War I. Through vivid storytelling and engaging illustrations, the book explores the experiences of children and families affected by the war, highlighting their resilience, courage, and the power of hope amidst the chaos. As the war intensifies, readers will witness the impact of new technologies, the struggles of soldiers on the front lines, and the sacrifices made by ordinary people. This volume offers a unique perspective on history, allowing young readers to connect with the past and gain a deeper understanding of the human experience during wartime.

Genres: Historical fiction, Children's literature

Themes: Resilience, Courage, Hope, Sacrifice
