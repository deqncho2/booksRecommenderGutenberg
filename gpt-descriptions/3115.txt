Synopsis: In 'The Indeterminate Sentence: What Shall Be Done with the Criminal Class?' Charles Dudley Warner delves into the complex issue of how society should deal with criminals. Through a series of thought-provoking essays, Warner explores the flaws of the current penal system and proposes alternative methods of rehabilitation and punishment. With a keen eye for social commentary and a compelling writing style, Warner challenges readers to question their preconceived notions about crime and punishment, ultimately offering a fresh perspective on the criminal class.

Genres: Non-fiction, Social commentary, Criminal justice

Themes: Criminal rehabilitation, Penal system reform, Social responsibility
