Synopsis: 

In 'British Flags: Their Early History, and Their Development at Sea: With an Account of the Origin of the Flag as a National Device', William Gordon Perrin takes readers on a captivating journey through the fascinating history of British flags. From their humble beginnings to their evolution as symbols of national identity, this book delves into the rich tapestry of stories behind these iconic emblems. Perrin explores the significance of flags at sea, shedding light on their role in naval warfare and communication. With meticulous research and engaging storytelling, he uncovers the origins of the flag as a national device, unraveling the mysteries and legends that have shaped British history. 

Genres: History, Non-fiction

Themes: National identity, Symbolism, Naval warfare, Communication
