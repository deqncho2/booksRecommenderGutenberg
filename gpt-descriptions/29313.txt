Synopsis: 

'War from the Inside: The Story of the 132nd Regiment Pennsylvania Volunteer Infantry in the War for the Suppression of the Rebellion, 1862-1863' takes readers on a gripping journey through the American Civil War, as experienced by the brave soldiers of the 132nd Regiment Pennsylvania Volunteer Infantry. Through the eyes of Frederick L. Hitchcock, a member of the regiment, we witness the horrors and triumphs of war, the bonds formed between comrades, and the sacrifices made for a cause greater than themselves. From the battlefields to the trenches, this firsthand account provides a vivid and intimate portrayal of the realities of war.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Memoir

Themes: War and its impact on individuals and society, Brotherhood and camaraderie in the face of adversity, Sacrifice and duty
