Synopsis: In "The Home: Its Work and Influence," Charlotte Perkins Gilman delves into the intricate dynamics of the home, exploring its impact on individuals and society as a whole. Through thought-provoking analysis and personal anecdotes, Gilman challenges traditional notions of domesticity and offers a compelling vision for a more equitable and fulfilling home life. This enlightening work invites readers to question the status quo and consider the transformative power of reimagining the home.

Genres: Non-fiction, Sociology, Feminism

Themes: Gender roles, Domesticity, Social reform
