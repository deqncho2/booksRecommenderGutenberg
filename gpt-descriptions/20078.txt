Synopsis: 
In "Merely Mary Ann" by Israel Zangwill, we are introduced to Mary Ann, a young and innocent girl who finds herself caught between the allure of the city and the simplicity of her rural upbringing. As she navigates the bustling streets of London, Mary Ann encounters a cast of colorful characters, each with their own secrets and desires. Through her journey, she discovers the complexities of love, the power of self-discovery, and the importance of staying true to oneself. Will Mary Ann be able to find her place in this vibrant and ever-changing world?

Genres: Fiction, Romance, Coming-of-age

Themes: Love, Identity, Social class, Self-discovery
