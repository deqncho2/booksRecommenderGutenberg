Synopsis: In 'That Lass O' Lowrie's: 1877' by Frances Hodgson Burnett, we are transported to the gritty industrial town of Lowrie's, where we meet a young girl named Lass. Living in poverty and surrounded by harsh conditions, Lass finds solace in her vivid imagination and dreams of a better life. As she navigates the challenges of her environment, Lass's resilience and determination are put to the test, leading her on a journey of self-discovery and unexpected opportunities. This captivating tale explores the power of hope, the strength of the human spirit, and the transformative nature of dreams.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Coming-of-Age, Drama

Themes: Poverty, Imagination, Resilience, Social Inequality
