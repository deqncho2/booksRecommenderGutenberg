Synopsis: 

'A Review of the Resources and Industries of the State of Washington, 1909' by Howell, Ithamar M. takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse landscapes and thriving industries of Washington State in the early 20th century. From the majestic peaks of the Cascade Range to the bustling cities and fertile valleys, this comprehensive review explores the state's abundant natural resources, including timber, minerals, and fisheries. Delving into the various industries that shaped the state's economy, the author provides a fascinating glimpse into the growth and development of Washington, highlighting the innovative technologies and entrepreneurial spirit that propelled the region forward. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, this book offers a captivating portrait of a state on the brink of transformation.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Geography

Themes: Economic development, Natural resources, Industrialization
