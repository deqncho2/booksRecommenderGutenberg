Synopsis: 

In 'Climate and Time in Their Geological Relations: A Theory of Secular Changes of the Earth's Climate', James Croll takes readers on a captivating journey through the intricate relationship between climate and time. Drawing on extensive research and scientific observations, Croll presents a groundbreaking theory that sheds light on the long-term changes in Earth's climate. From the influence of astronomical factors to the impact of geological processes, this thought-provoking work explores the fascinating interplay between the Earth's climate and the passage of time.

Genres: Science, Geology, Climate Studies

Themes: Climate change, Geological processes, Astronomical influences
