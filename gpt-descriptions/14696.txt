Synopsis: 
In 'The Wheel of Life' by Ellen Anderson Gholson Glasgow, we are introduced to a young woman named Laura Wilde who finds herself trapped in a stifling marriage in the late 19th century American South. As she navigates the complexities of societal expectations and the limitations placed upon women, Laura embarks on a journey of self-discovery and liberation. Through her encounters with various characters and her own introspection, she begins to question the roles assigned to her and the possibilities that lie beyond. Set against the backdrop of a changing society, this novel explores the struggles and triumphs of a woman seeking her own path in a world that seeks to confine her.

Genres: Fiction, Historical Fiction, Southern Gothic

Themes: Gender roles, societal expectations, self-discovery, liberation, societal change
