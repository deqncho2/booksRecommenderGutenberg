Synopsis: 

'Philip Winwood: A Sketch of the Domestic History of an American Captain in the War of Independence; Embracing Events that Occurred between and during the Years 1763 and 1786, in New York and London: written by His Enemy in War, Herbert Russell, Lieutenant in the Loyalist Forces' is a captivating historical novel that delves into the life of Philip Winwood, an American captain during the War of Independence. Written by his former enemy, Herbert Russell, the story takes us through the tumultuous years between 1763 and 1786, exploring the events that shaped Winwood's life in both New York and London. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Biographical Fiction

Themes: War and Conflict, Loyalty and Betrayal, Identity and Self-discovery
