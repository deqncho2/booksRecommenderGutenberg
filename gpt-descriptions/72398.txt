Synopsis: In "The Right Hand: Left-handedness," Wilson, Daniel, Sir takes readers on a captivating journey exploring the intriguing world of left-handedness. Through a combination of historical anecdotes, scientific research, and personal experiences, Wilson delves into the mysteries and misconceptions surrounding this unique trait. From famous left-handed individuals throughout history to the impact of left-handedness on brain function, this book offers a fascinating exploration of a lesser-known aspect of human diversity. Prepare to be enlightened and entertained as Wilson uncovers the secrets of left-handedness, challenging conventional wisdom and shedding light on the power of being different.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, History

Themes: Human diversity, Brain function, Cultural perceptions
