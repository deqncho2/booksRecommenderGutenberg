Synopsis: In "Crimes of Preachers in the United States and Canada," author M. E. Billings delves into a shocking and captivating exploration of the dark side of religious leaders. Unveiling a series of scandalous crimes committed by preachers, this book takes readers on a gripping journey through the hidden world of corruption, deceit, and abuse within the clergy. From embezzlement and fraud to sexual misconduct and even murder, the author exposes the shocking truth behind these seemingly pious figures. With each chapter revealing a new and disturbing case, readers will be left questioning their trust in those who claim to be spiritual guides. 

Genres: True Crime, Non-fiction

Themes: Corruption, Deception, Abuse of Power, Religion and Morality
