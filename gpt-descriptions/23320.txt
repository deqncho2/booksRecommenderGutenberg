Synopsis: In "The Deaf: Their Position in Society and the Provision for Their Education in the United States," Harry Best delves into the often overlooked world of the deaf community in America. Through meticulous research and personal anecdotes, Best explores the challenges faced by the deaf in society and the educational opportunities available to them. From the struggles of communication to the fight for equal rights, this eye-opening book sheds light on the experiences and resilience of the deaf population. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Education, Social Sciences

Themes: Deaf culture, Communication, Education, Equality
