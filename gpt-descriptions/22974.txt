Synopsis: In 'The City Bride (1696): Or, The Merry Cuckold' by Joseph Harris, readers are transported to the vibrant streets of London in the late 17th century. The story revolves around the misadventures of a young bride who finds herself entangled in a web of deceit, mistaken identities, and scandalous affairs. As she navigates the treacherous world of love and marriage, the city's bustling atmosphere serves as a backdrop for a series of hilarious and unexpected events. With its witty dialogue, lively characters, and surprising twists, this comedic tale promises to keep readers entertained from beginning to end.

Genres: Comedy, Farce, Satire

Themes: Love and marriage, Deception and mistaken identities, Social satire
