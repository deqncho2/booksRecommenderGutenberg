Synopsis: In "The Milkmaid of Montfermeil," Paul de Kock takes us on a captivating journey through the streets of Montfermeil, a small village on the outskirts of Paris. The story revolves around the life of a young milkmaid named Louise, who finds herself entangled in a web of love, betrayal, and scandal. As she navigates the complexities of her relationships with the men in her life, including a charming artist and a wealthy nobleman, Louise must confront the harsh realities of her social status and the consequences of her choices. Set against the backdrop of 19th-century France, this novel offers a vivid portrayal of the struggles and desires of ordinary people in a rapidly changing society.

Genres: Historical fiction, Romance, Drama

Themes: Social class, Love and relationships, Morality, Society and its expectations
