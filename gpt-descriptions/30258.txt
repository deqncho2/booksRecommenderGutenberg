Synopsis: 

In 'The Fighting Governor: A Chronicle of Frontenac', Charles W. Colby takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Louis de Buade, Comte de Frontenac, a charismatic and fearless governor of New France in the late 17th century. From his arrival in the colony to his relentless efforts to defend it against rival European powers and indigenous nations, Frontenac's indomitable spirit and strategic brilliance shine through. As he navigates the treacherous political landscape and faces numerous challenges, including conflicts with the English and Iroquois, readers are immersed in a thrilling tale of power, ambition, and the pursuit of glory in the New World.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Biography, Adventure

Themes: Leadership, Colonialism, Conflict, Ambition
