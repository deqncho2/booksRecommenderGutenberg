Synopsis: In 'American Hero-Myths: A Study in the Native Religions of the Western Continent', Daniel G. Brinton delves into the captivating world of Native American mythology and spirituality. Through extensive research and analysis, Brinton uncovers the rich tapestry of hero-myths that have shaped the beliefs and traditions of the indigenous peoples of the Western Continent. From the powerful legends of gods and goddesses to the awe-inspiring tales of heroic figures, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the profound spiritual heritage of Native American cultures.

Genres: Non-fiction, Mythology, Anthropology

Themes: Native American spirituality, Heroism, Mythology, Cultural heritage
