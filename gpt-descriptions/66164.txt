Synopsis:
'Foods and Their Adulteration' takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of food products, uncovering the hidden truths behind their origin, manufacture, and composition. Harvey Washington Wiley delves into the dark underbelly of the food industry, exposing the common adulterations that plague our plates. From the deceptive practices of manufacturers to the loopholes in food standards and regulations, this book sheds light on the alarming reality of what we consume. With meticulous research and compelling anecdotes, Wiley presents a comprehensive exploration of the complex web of food adulteration, leaving readers both shocked and enlightened.

Genres:
Non-fiction, Food Science, History

Themes:
Food safety, Consumer awareness, Government regulations
