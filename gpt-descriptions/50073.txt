Synopsis: In 'The Age of Stonehenge', Edward Duke takes readers on a captivating journey through time, unraveling the mysteries surrounding one of the world's most enigmatic ancient structures. From the origins of Stonehenge to the rituals and beliefs of its builders, Duke delves into the fascinating history and significance of this iconic monument. As he explores the archaeological evidence and theories surrounding Stonehenge, the author paints a vivid picture of the people who lived during this era, their customs, and the profound impact this ancient site had on their lives. With a blend of meticulous research and engaging storytelling, 'The Age of Stonehenge' offers a captivating exploration of a timeless enigma.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Archaeology, Ancient History

Themes: Ancient civilizations, Rituals and beliefs, Archaeological mysteries
