Synopsis: 

'Changing China' takes readers on a captivating journey through the heart of a rapidly transforming nation. Set against the backdrop of early 20th century China, this enthralling work follows the experiences of Lady Cecil and William Gascoyne-Cecil as they navigate the complexities of a country in flux. From the bustling streets of Shanghai to the serene landscapes of the countryside, the authors provide a vivid and intimate portrayal of a nation on the brink of change. As they encounter a diverse cast of characters and witness the clash between tradition and modernity, readers are drawn into a world of intrigue, adventure, and profound cultural shifts. 

Genres: Historical fiction, travelogue, memoir

Themes: Cultural transformation, exploration, identity, societal change
