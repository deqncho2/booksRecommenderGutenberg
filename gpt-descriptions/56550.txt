Synopsis: In "The Popol Vuh: The Mythic and Heroic Sagas of the Kichés of Central America," Lewis Spence takes readers on a captivating journey into the ancient mythology and heroic sagas of the Kiché people of Central America. Through a collection of enchanting tales, Spence unveils the creation of the world, the adventures of gods and heroes, and the struggles between good and evil. From the birth of the universe to the epic battles fought by legendary figures, this book offers a mesmerizing glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Kiché civilization.

Genres: Mythology, Folklore, Anthropology

Themes: Creation, Heroism, Good vs. Evil, Cultural Heritage
