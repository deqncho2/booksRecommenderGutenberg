Synopsis: 

'From Isolation to Leadership, Revised: A Review of American Foreign Policy' by Latané, John Holladay takes readers on a captivating journey through the evolution of American foreign policy. This revised edition delves into the intricate web of decisions, conflicts, and ideologies that have shaped the nation's role on the global stage. From the early days of isolationism to the emergence of a superpower, the book explores the pivotal moments and key figures that have influenced America's approach to international affairs. With insightful analysis and thought-provoking anecdotes, Latané paints a vivid picture of the challenges and triumphs that have defined American foreign policy.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: American exceptionalism, Diplomacy, Power dynamics, International relations
