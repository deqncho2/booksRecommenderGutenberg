Synopsis: 

In 'The New York Times Current History of the European War, Vol. 1, January 9, 1915: What Americans Say to Europe', readers are transported back to the tumultuous period of World War I. Through a collection of articles and essays, this volume presents a captivating glimpse into the thoughts, opinions, and concerns of Americans during this critical time. From discussions on the role of the United States in the conflict to reflections on the impact of war on society, this book offers a unique perspective on the European war from across the Atlantic. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, journalism, war literature

Themes: World War I, American perspectives, international relations, societal impact
