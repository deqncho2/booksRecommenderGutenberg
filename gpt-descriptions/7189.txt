Synopsis: 

In "A Short History of the Great War," A. F. Pollard takes readers on a captivating journey through one of the most tumultuous periods in human history. From the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand to the signing of the Treaty of Versailles, this concise yet comprehensive account delves into the political, social, and military aspects of World War I. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, Pollard brings to life the key events, battles, and personalities that shaped the war, providing a gripping narrative that keeps readers on the edge of their seats.

Genres: History, Military History, Non-fiction

Themes: War and Conflict, Diplomacy, Nationalism, Human Resilience
