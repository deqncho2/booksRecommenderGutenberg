Synopsis: 
In 'Phaedo', Plato takes us on a philosophical journey as Socrates faces his impending execution. Set in ancient Athens, the dialogue explores the nature of the soul, the existence of an afterlife, and the pursuit of knowledge. As Socrates engages in profound discussions with his disciples, the reader is invited to ponder the mysteries of life and death, and to question the limits of human understanding.

Genres: Philosophy, Dialogue, Classical Literature

Themes: Immortality of the soul, Existence of an afterlife, Pursuit of knowledge, Philosophy of death
