Synopsis: 

In 'History of the Negro Race in America from 1619 to 1880. Vol 1: Negroes as Slaves, as Soldiers, and as Citizens', George Washington Williams takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of African Americans in America. From their arrival as slaves in 1619 to their struggles for freedom and citizenship, Williams delves into the untold stories of the Negro race, shedding light on their experiences as slaves, their contributions as soldiers, and their fight for recognition as citizens. This volume serves as a comprehensive exploration of the complex and often overlooked history of African Americans, offering a fresh perspective on their resilience, bravery, and determination in the face of adversity.

Genres: History, Biography, African American Studies

Themes: Slavery, Civil Rights, Identity, Resilience
