Synopsis: In "China, Japan and the U.S.A.: Present-Day Conditions in the Far East and Their Bearing on the Washington Conference," John Dewey delves into the complex dynamics between China, Japan, and the United States during a pivotal time in history. With a keen eye for detail and a deep understanding of the region, Dewey explores the political, economic, and cultural factors that shape the relationships between these nations. As tensions rise and negotiations unfold, the reader is taken on a thought-provoking journey through the intricacies of international diplomacy and the potential consequences of the Washington Conference.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: International relations, Diplomacy, Power dynamics, Cultural exchange
