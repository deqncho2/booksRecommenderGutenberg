Synopsis: In "Laughter: An Essay on the Meaning of the Comic," Henri Bergson takes us on a thought-provoking exploration of the nature and purpose of humor. Through a combination of philosophical analysis and witty observations, Bergson delves into the mechanisms behind laughter, dissecting its various forms and uncovering the underlying truths it reveals about human behavior. From the absurd to the satirical, from slapstick to wordplay, this essay offers a captivating journey into the essence of comedy, challenging our preconceptions and inviting us to reflect on the profound significance of laughter in our lives.

Genres: Philosophy, Literary Criticism, Humor

Themes: Comedy, Human Nature, Social Critique
