Synopsis: 
In 'Letters of a Dakota Divorcee' by Jane Burr, we are introduced to Sarah, a woman who finds herself in the midst of a tumultuous divorce in the late 19th century. As she navigates the challenges of her new life in the Dakota Territory, Sarah turns to writing letters to her estranged husband, pouring out her heart and soul onto the pages. Through her correspondence, we witness her journey of self-discovery, resilience, and the pursuit of happiness in a world that often seems determined to keep her down. With each letter, Sarah's voice grows stronger, her spirit more defiant, and her determination to find her own path becomes unwavering. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Women's Fiction, Epistolary Novel

Themes: Divorce, Independence, Resilience, Self-discovery
