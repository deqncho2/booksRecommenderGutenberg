Synopsis: In 'A Treatise on the Plague and Yellow Fever', Tytler delves into the chilling histories of devastating plagues that have plagued humanity throughout the ages. From the plague in Athens during the Peloponnesian War, to the outbreaks in Constantinople, London, and Marseilles, the author uncovers the harrowing details of these epidemics. With an appendix containing in-depth accounts of each event, this book offers a captivating exploration of the impact and consequences of these deadly diseases.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Medical

Themes: Epidemics, Disease, Historical Impact
