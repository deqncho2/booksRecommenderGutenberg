Synopsis: 

In "Ireland Under Coercion: The Diary of an American (1 of 2): (2nd ed.) (1888)" by William Henry Hurlbert, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the tumultuous landscape of Ireland during a period of intense political and social unrest. Through the eyes of an American observer, the diary offers a unique perspective on the struggles faced by the Irish people under British rule. As the author delves into the heart of the Irish countryside, he encounters a world filled with secret societies, agrarian violence, and the constant tension between the Irish nationalists and the British authorities. With each entry, the diary unveils a gripping narrative that explores the complexities of Irish history and the resilience of its people.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travelogue, Political commentary

Themes: Irish nationalism, British colonialism, Social unrest, Cultural identity
