Synopsis: In 'The Book of Susan: A Novel' by Dodd, Lee Wilson, a mysterious book is discovered, containing the captivating story of Susan, a woman who defies societal norms and embarks on a journey of self-discovery. As the pages unfold, readers are transported to a world filled with secrets, forbidden desires, and unexpected twists. With each chapter, the lines between reality and fiction blur, leaving readers questioning the true nature of Susan's existence. This gripping novel explores the power of identity, the complexities of human relationships, and the profound impact of personal choices.

Genres: Mystery, Psychological Thriller, Literary Fiction

Themes: Identity, Self-discovery, Societal Norms, Secrets, Relationships
