Synopsis: 

'Five Acres Too Much' is a captivating and enlightening book that delves into the allure of country living and the potential pitfalls of amateur farming. Written by Robert Barnwell Roosevelt, this work offers valuable advice and instruction to those considering purchasing rural properties, whether large or small. Through a truthful elucidation of the attractions of the countryside and a careful consideration of the question of profit and loss, the author explores the challenges and rewards of embarking on a farming venture. With an engaging narrative and insightful observations, 'Five Acres Too Much' provides a thought-provoking exploration of the rural lifestyle and the complexities of agricultural pursuits.

Genres: Non-fiction, Memoir, Agricultural Literature

Themes: Rural living, Farming, Profit and loss, Property ownership, Challenges and rewards
