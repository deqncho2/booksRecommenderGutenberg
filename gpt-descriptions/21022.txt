Synopsis: In 'Lessons on Soil' by Edward J. Russell, a renowned soil scientist embarks on a captivating journey to uncover the hidden secrets beneath our feet. Through vivid storytelling and scientific exploration, the author takes readers on a fascinating adventure, revealing the intricate world of soil and its profound impact on our lives. From ancient civilizations to modern agricultural practices, this book unveils the surprising connections between soil health, environmental sustainability, and human well-being. As the author delves into the depths of this often overlooked subject, readers will gain a newfound appreciation for the vital role soil plays in shaping our world.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Environmental

Themes: Soil science, Environmental sustainability, Human impact on the environment
