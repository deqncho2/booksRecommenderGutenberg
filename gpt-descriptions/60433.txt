Synopsis: 
'Letters of John Keats to Fanny Brawne: Written in the years MDCCCXIX and MDCCCXX and now given from the original manuscripts' is a collection of intimate and passionate letters exchanged between the renowned poet John Keats and his beloved Fanny Brawne. Through these heartfelt correspondences, Keats unveils his deepest emotions, desires, and struggles, painting a vivid picture of their intense and tumultuous relationship. As the letters unfold, the reader is drawn into a world of poetic brilliance, unrequited love, and the profound impact of art on the human soul.

Genres: 
Epistolary, Romance, Biography

Themes: 
Love and Desire, Art and Creativity, Longing and Loss
