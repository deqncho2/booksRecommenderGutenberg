Synopsis: In "Hanover; Or The Persecution of the Lowly: A Story of the Wilmington Massacre," Thorne, Jack takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the Wilmington Massacre. Set in the late 19th century, the story follows the life of Hanover, a young African American man who becomes entangled in a web of racial tension, political corruption, and violence. As the city of Wilmington becomes a battleground for power and prejudice, Hanover must navigate through the chaos, facing unimaginable challenges and fighting for justice. With its rich historical backdrop and compelling characters, this novel offers a thought-provoking exploration of the human spirit and the resilience of those who dare to stand against oppression.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Thriller, Social Justice

Themes: Racial Discrimination, Political Corruption, Resilience, Social Injustice
