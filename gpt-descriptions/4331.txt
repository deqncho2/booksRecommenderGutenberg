Synopsis: 
'Sun-Up, and Other Poems' by Lola Ridge is a collection of powerful and evocative poems that explore the human condition and the struggles of marginalized individuals in early 20th century America. Through vivid imagery and poignant language, Ridge delves into themes of social injustice, poverty, love, and the resilience of the human spirit. From the bustling streets of New York City to the vast landscapes of the American West, these poems offer a raw and unflinching portrayal of life's complexities and the pursuit of hope in the face of adversity.

Genres: Poetry, Social Realism, Modernism

Themes: Social injustice, Poverty, Love, Resilience, Human condition
