Synopsis: 

In 'Sniping in France: With notes on the scientific training of scouts, observers, and snipers', Hesketh-Prichard takes us on a thrilling journey through the dangerous and secretive world of snipers during World War I. Drawing from his own experiences as a sniper, he provides a captivating account of the tactics, techniques, and challenges faced by these highly skilled soldiers. From the intense training required to the adrenaline-fueled moments on the battlefield, Hesketh-Prichard offers a unique perspective on the art of sniping, shedding light on the often overlooked role they played in the war.

Genres: Non-fiction, Military History, Memoir

Themes: Warfare, Skill and Training, Psychological Impact of War
