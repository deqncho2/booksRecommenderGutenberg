Synopsis: In 'The Miller in Eighteenth-Century Virginia', Thomas K. Ford takes us on a fascinating journey into the world of mills and the craft of milling in colonial Virginia. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, Ford not only provides a comprehensive account of the various types of mills and their significance in the region but also delves into the intriguing history of the windmill near the Palace in Williamsburg. From the technical aspects of milling to the social and economic impact of mills on the community, this book offers a captivating exploration of an essential industry in eighteenth-century Virginia.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural history

Themes: Industrialization, Colonial life, Technological advancements
