Synopsis: In 'A Chilhowee Lily: 1911', Charles Egbert Craddock takes readers on a captivating journey through the picturesque landscapes of the Great Smoky Mountains. Set in the early 20th century, the story follows the life of Lily, a young woman with a mysterious past, as she navigates the challenges of love, family, and societal expectations. As Lily's world intertwines with the lives of the mountain folk, secrets are unveiled, and the true nature of human resilience and the power of forgiveness are explored. With vivid descriptions and compelling characters, this novel immerses readers in a world of beauty, hardship, and the enduring spirit of the Appalachian people.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Romance, Southern Literature

Themes: Identity, Love and Sacrifice, Resilience, Family Dynamics
