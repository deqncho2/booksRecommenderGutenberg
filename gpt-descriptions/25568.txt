Synopsis: In 'Domesticated animals: Their relation to man and to his advancement in civilization', Nathaniel Southgate Shaler delves into the fascinating and often overlooked connection between humans and the animals they have domesticated. Through captivating anecdotes and insightful analysis, Shaler explores how our relationship with domesticated animals has shaped our civilization and propelled us forward. From the earliest days of domestication to the present, this thought-provoking work sheds light on the profound impact these animals have had on our lives, and the reciprocal influence we have had on theirs.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Anthropology

Themes: Human-animal relationships, Civilization, Evolution
