Synopsis: In "George Du Maurier, the Satirist of the Victorians," T. Martin Wood delves into the life and works of the renowned artist and writer, George Du Maurier. From his early days as a cartoonist to his influential novels, Wood explores the fascinating journey of this talented satirist. With a keen eye for social commentary, Du Maurier's works offer a unique perspective on the Victorian era, captivating readers with his wit and insightful observations. Through a careful examination of his art and writings, Wood uncovers the hidden depths of Du Maurier's satirical genius, shedding light on the man behind the pen.

Genres: Biography, Art, Literary Criticism

Themes: Satire, Social Commentary, Victorian Society
