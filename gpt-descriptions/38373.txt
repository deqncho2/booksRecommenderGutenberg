Synopsis: In 'Liberty in the Nineteenth Century' by Frederic May Holland, embark on a captivating journey through the tumultuous era of the 1800s, where the struggle for freedom and individual rights takes center stage. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous historical research, Holland delves into the lives of remarkable individuals who fought against oppression, challenged societal norms, and paved the way for a more liberated future. From the suffragettes demanding women's rights to the abolitionists fighting against slavery, this book unveils the untold stories of those who dared to defy the status quo and championed the cause of liberty. Prepare to be inspired by the courage, resilience, and unwavering determination of these extraordinary individuals who shaped the course of history.

Genres: Historical Non-fiction, Biography, Social History

Themes: Freedom, Individual Rights, Social Justice, Activism
