Synopsis: In 'Begumbagh: A Tale of the Indian Mutiny', George Manville Fenn takes readers on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the Indian Mutiny. Set against the backdrop of the 1857 uprising, the story follows the lives of a diverse group of characters who find themselves trapped within the walls of Begumbagh, a fortified residency in Lucknow. As the rebellion rages outside, the inhabitants must navigate treacherous alliances, face harrowing challenges, and confront their own fears and desires. Amidst the chaos, love, loyalty, and sacrifice emerge as powerful forces that shape the destiny of those within Begumbagh.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, War

Themes: Rebellion, Survival, Loyalty, Love, Sacrifice
