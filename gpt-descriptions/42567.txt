Synopsis: 
In "William Penn" by Rupert Sargent Holland, embark on a captivating journey through the life of one of America's most influential figures. This meticulously researched biography delves into the fascinating story of William Penn, a man who defied societal norms and fought for religious freedom, ultimately leaving an indelible mark on American history. From his privileged upbringing to his transformative experiences in prison, Penn's unwavering commitment to his beliefs and his tireless efforts to establish a utopian society will keep readers engrossed from beginning to end. As the narrative unfolds, Holland skillfully paints a vivid portrait of a man driven by his convictions, navigating the complexities of politics, religion, and personal relationships. 

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Religious freedom, Utopian ideals, Political activism
