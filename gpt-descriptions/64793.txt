Synopsis: 

In "H. G. Hawker, airman: his life and work," Muriel Hawker takes us on a captivating journey through the extraordinary life of her husband, Harry George Hawker. From his humble beginnings as a mechanic to becoming one of the most renowned aviators of his time, this biography unveils the thrilling adventures and groundbreaking achievements of a true aviation pioneer. 

As we delve into the pages of this book, we witness Hawker's relentless determination to push the boundaries of flight, his daring escapades in record-breaking attempts, and his unwavering passion for aviation. From his involvement in the development of the Sopwith Camel, one of the most iconic fighter planes of World War I, to his daring attempts to cross the Atlantic, Muriel Hawker paints a vivid portrait of a man who lived and breathed aviation.

Through personal anecdotes, historical accounts, and intimate insights, "H. G. Hawker, airman: his life and work" offers a captivating glimpse into the life of a true aviation legend. This biography not only celebrates Hawker's remarkable achievements but also explores the challenges he faced, the risks he took, and the impact he had on the world of aviation.

Genres: Biography, History, Adventure

Themes: Aviation, Determination, Innovation
