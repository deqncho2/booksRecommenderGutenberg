Synopsis: In "Why Authors Go Wrong, and Other Explanations," Grant M. Overton delves into the fascinating world of literature, exploring the reasons behind the missteps and failures of renowned authors. Through insightful analysis and captivating anecdotes, Overton uncovers the hidden pitfalls that can lead even the most talented writers astray. From the pressures of fame to personal demons and creative struggles, this book offers a unique perspective on the challenges faced by authors throughout history. Prepare to be intrigued and enlightened as Overton unravels the mysteries behind why authors sometimes stumble on their path to literary greatness.

Genres: Literary criticism, non-fiction, biography

Themes: Creative process, fame and its consequences, the human condition
