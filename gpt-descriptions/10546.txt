Synopsis: In "So Runs the World" by Henryk Sienkiewicz, a young nobleman finds himself entangled in a web of political intrigue and forbidden love during the tumultuous times of the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth. As he navigates the treacherous world of power and deception, he must make difficult choices that will shape his destiny and the fate of his beloved country. With vivid descriptions and captivating characters, this historical novel takes readers on a thrilling journey through a society on the brink of collapse.

Genres: Historical fiction, romance, political thriller

Themes: Love and sacrifice, political corruption, societal upheaval
