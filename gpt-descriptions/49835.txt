Synopsis: In 'The Letters of Gracchus on the East India Question' by William Augustus Miles, readers are taken on a captivating journey through a series of thought-provoking letters. Gracchus, the protagonist, delves into the complex and controversial topic of the East India Company, shedding light on its impact on trade, governance, and the lives of countless individuals. Through his eloquent and passionate writing, Gracchus challenges conventional wisdom, exposes hidden truths, and offers a unique perspective on the East India Question. As the letters unfold, readers will find themselves engrossed in a compelling narrative that explores the intricate web of power, corruption, and the struggle for justice.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Political Commentary, Epistolary

Themes: Colonialism, Power Dynamics, Social Justice
