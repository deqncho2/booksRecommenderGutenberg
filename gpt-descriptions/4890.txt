Synopsis: In "Life and Death of John of Barneveld, Advocate of Holland," John Lothrop Motley takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous period of the Thirty Years' War. Set in the years 1609-14, this historical work delves into the life of John of Barneveld, a prominent figure in Holland's political landscape. Motley skillfully weaves together the primary causes and movements of the war, providing a gripping account of the events that shaped Europe during this time. As readers follow Barneveld's journey, they will be drawn into a world of political intrigue, religious conflicts, and power struggles that ultimately lead to his tragic fate. 

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Political history

Themes: Power and politics, Religious conflicts, War and its consequences
