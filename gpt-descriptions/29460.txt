Synopsis: In 'The Electoral Votes of 1876: Who Should Count Them, What Should Be Counted, and the Remedy for a Wrong Count', David Dudley Field delves into the controversial presidential election of 1876, exploring the intricate details of the electoral process and the consequences of a flawed count. With gripping narratives and meticulous analysis, Field uncovers the complex web of political maneuvering and legal battles that ensued, leaving readers on the edge of their seats as they witness the high-stakes struggle for power unfold.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Political Science

Themes: Electoral process, Political power, Legal battles, Democracy
