Synopsis: 

'Jelly-Fish, Star-Fish, and Sea-Urchins: Being a Research on Primitive Nervous Systems' takes readers on a captivating journey into the mysterious world of marine creatures. In this groundbreaking work, George John Romanes delves into the intricate workings of the nervous systems of jellyfish, starfish, and sea urchins, unraveling the secrets of their primitive yet fascinating biology. Through meticulous research and vivid descriptions, Romanes unveils the astonishing capabilities and behaviors of these enigmatic creatures, shedding light on their unique adaptations and evolutionary significance. As readers delve deeper into the pages, they will be enthralled by the intricate connections between these creatures and the natural world, leaving them with a newfound appreciation for the wonders of marine life.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Biology

Themes: Evolution, Adaptation, Marine Biology
