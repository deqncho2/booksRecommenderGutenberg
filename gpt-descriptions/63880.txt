Synopsis:
In "George Borrow, the Man and His Work," R. A. J. Walling delves into the fascinating life and literary contributions of the enigmatic figure, George Borrow. From his adventurous travels across Europe to his encounters with various cultures and languages, Borrow's life is a captivating tale of exploration and self-discovery. Walling skillfully weaves together anecdotes, letters, and analysis to paint a vivid portrait of this complex and enigmatic writer. As readers delve into Borrow's works, they will be transported to a world of mystery, adventure, and the exploration of human nature. 

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Travel Literature

Themes: Exploration, Identity, Language and Culture
