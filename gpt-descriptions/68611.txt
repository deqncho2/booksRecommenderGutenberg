Synopsis: In "Science and the Modern World," Alfred North Whitehead takes readers on a captivating journey through the history of science, exploring its profound impact on the modern world. From the ancient Greeks to the scientific revolution and beyond, Whitehead delves into the fascinating stories of scientific discoveries and their transformative effects on society. With his insightful analysis and thought-provoking ideas, he challenges conventional notions about the relationship between science, philosophy, and human progress. This book is a captivating exploration of the intricate interplay between scientific advancements and the evolution of civilization.

Genres: Non-fiction, Philosophy, History

Themes: Science and society, Philosophy of science, Progress and civilization
