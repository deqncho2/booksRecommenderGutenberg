Synopsis: 

In "Raphael; Or, Pages of the Book of Life at Twenty" by Alphonse de Lamartine, readers are introduced to the captivating story of Raphael, a young man on the cusp of adulthood, as he navigates the complexities of life, love, and self-discovery. Set against the backdrop of 19th-century France, this coming-of-age tale takes readers on a journey filled with passion, ambition, and the pursuit of one's true purpose. As Raphael encounters various characters and experiences, he grapples with the profound questions of existence, the meaning of love, and the pursuit of happiness. With poetic prose and vivid imagery, Lamartine weaves a tale that will leave readers captivated and eager to uncover the secrets of Raphael's journey.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Romanticism

Themes: Self-discovery, Love, Ambition, Existentialism
