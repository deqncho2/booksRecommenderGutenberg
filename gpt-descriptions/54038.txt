Synopsis: In "The Life and Times of Cleopatra, Queen of Egypt: A Study in the Origin of the Roman Empire," Arthur E. P. Brome Weigall takes readers on a captivating journey through the remarkable life of one of history's most enigmatic figures. From her rise to power as the last pharaoh of Egypt to her complex relationships with Julius Caesar and Mark Antony, Cleopatra's story unfolds with intrigue, passion, and political maneuvering. Weigall delves into the historical context, shedding light on the cultural clashes between Egypt and Rome, and explores the impact of Cleopatra's reign on the formation of the Roman Empire. This meticulously researched and engaging study offers a fresh perspective on Cleopatra's life, revealing the fascinating interplay between power, love, and ambition.

Genres: Biography, History, Non-fiction

Themes: Power and politics, Love and relationships, Cultural clashes
