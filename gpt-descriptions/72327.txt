Synopsis: Step back in time and immerse yourself in the captivating world of colonial New York as Alice Morse Earle takes you on a journey through the lives of its inhabitants. From the bustling streets of Manhattan to the quaint villages along the Hudson River, 'Colonial Days in Old New York' paints a vivid picture of the daily struggles, triumphs, and traditions of the early settlers. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, Earle brings to life the fascinating stories of ordinary people who shaped the history of this vibrant city. Discover the secrets of colonial fashion, explore the customs of courtship and marriage, and delve into the intriguing world of colonial cuisine. With each turn of the page, you will be transported to a bygone era, where the spirit of adventure and the pursuit of freedom were at the heart of every New Yorker.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural history, Biography

Themes: Colonial life, Everyday experiences, Social customs, Cultural heritage
