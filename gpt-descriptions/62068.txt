Synopsis: In "The Face of the Earth as Seen from the Air: A Study in the Application of Airplane Photography to Geography," Willis T. Lee takes readers on a captivating journey through the skies, exploring the world from a unique perspective. Through the lens of airplane photography, Lee unveils breathtaking landscapes, hidden patterns, and surprising connections that can only be seen from above. As he delves into the intricate relationship between geography and aerial imagery, the author invites readers to see the Earth in a whole new light, sparking curiosity and wonder about the world we inhabit.

Genres: Non-fiction, Geography, Photography

Themes: Aerial exploration, Geography, Perspective, Human impact on the environment
