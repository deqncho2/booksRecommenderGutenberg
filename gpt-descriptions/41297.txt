Synopsis: In 'Local Color' by Irvin S. Cobb, a young artist named John is sent to a small town in Kentucky to capture the essence of its unique charm and character. As he immerses himself in the local community, he encounters a colorful cast of characters, each with their own quirks and stories to tell. From the eccentric old man who claims to have seen a ghost, to the feisty widow who runs the local boarding house, John's journey becomes a delightful exploration of the human spirit and the beauty of everyday life.

Genres: Fiction, Humor, Regional Literature

Themes: Community, Identity, Appreciation of the Ordinary
