Synopsis: 

In "Acid Rain and Our Nation's Capital: A Guide to Effects on Buildings and Monuments," author McGee, E. S. takes readers on a captivating journey through the hidden consequences of acid rain on the iconic structures of Washington, D.C. From the majestic Lincoln Memorial to the grandeur of the Capitol Building, this guide unveils the shocking impact of this environmental phenomenon on the city's architectural treasures. Through vivid descriptions and compelling anecdotes, readers will discover the surprising ways in which acid rain has silently eroded the very foundations of these national symbols. As the author delves into the historical significance of each structure, readers will gain a deeper understanding of the delicate balance between nature and human creation. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Environmental Science, Architecture

Themes: Environmental degradation, Historical preservation, Human impact on nature
