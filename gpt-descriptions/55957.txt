Synopsis: In "A Study of Army Camp Life during American Revolution," Snuff, Mary Hazel takes readers on a captivating journey through the often overlooked aspects of the American Revolution. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the author delves into the daily lives of soldiers, exploring the challenges they faced, the camaraderie they built, and the sacrifices they made. From the harsh conditions of camp life to the unexpected moments of joy and resilience, this book offers a unique perspective on a pivotal period in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Social history

Themes: War and its impact on individuals, Resilience and camaraderie in the face of adversity, Everyday life during times of conflict
