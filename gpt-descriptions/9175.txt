Synopsis: In 'The Art of Poetry: an Epistle to the Pisos', the renowned Roman poet Horace imparts his wisdom and advice on the art of poetry to the Pisos, a prominent family of Roman nobles. Through a series of insightful and thought-provoking letters, Horace explores the various elements that make a poem successful, delving into topics such as structure, language, and the importance of pleasing both the reader and the gods. With his characteristic wit and charm, Horace offers invaluable guidance to aspiring poets, while also reflecting on the role of poetry in society and the responsibilities of the poet. 

Genres: Epistolary, Literary Criticism

Themes: The art of poetry, Aesthetics, Poetic structure, Language and style, The role of the poet in society
