Synopsis: In the second volume of "The Life of John Marshall," Albert J. Beveridge delves into the political career of one of America's most influential figures. From his early days as a lawyer to his appointment as Secretary of State, Marshall's journey is filled with intrigue, challenges, and pivotal moments that shaped the nation's history. As Marshall navigates the complex world of politics, diplomacy, and statesmanship during the crucial years of 1789-1801, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the birth of the American republic and the struggles faced by its leaders.

Genres: Biography, History, Political Science

Themes: Leadership, American Revolution, Constitutional Law, Diplomacy
