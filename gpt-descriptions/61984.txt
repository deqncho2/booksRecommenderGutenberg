Synopsis: In "Sylvia: A Novel," Sinclair, Upton takes readers on a captivating journey through the life of Sylvia Castleman, a young woman with a mysterious past. Set in the early 20th century, the story unfolds as Sylvia navigates the complexities of love, ambition, and societal expectations. As she encounters a cast of intriguing characters and faces unexpected challenges, Sylvia's determination and resilience are put to the test. With secrets lurking beneath the surface, the novel delves into the depths of human nature, exploring the power of self-discovery and the consequences of hidden truths.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mystery, Romance

Themes: Identity, Love and Relationships, Social Expectations
