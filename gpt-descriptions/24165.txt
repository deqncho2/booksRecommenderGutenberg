Synopsis: In 'The Development of Embroidery in America', Candace Wheeler takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history of embroidery in America. From its humble beginnings to its rise as a cherished art form, Wheeler explores the intricate techniques, vibrant designs, and the remarkable women who played a pivotal role in shaping this craft. Through vivid storytelling and stunning visuals, this book unveils the untold stories behind the stitches, revealing the profound impact embroidery has had on American culture and identity.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Art

Themes: Artistic expression, Women's history, Cultural heritage
