Synopsis: 

'Vermont riflemen in the war for the union, 1861 to 1865: A history of Company F, First United States sharp shooters' by Ripley, William Young Warren is a gripping historical account that delves into the experiences of Company F, a group of Vermont riflemen who fought in the American Civil War. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the author brings to life the harrowing battles, personal sacrifices, and unwavering camaraderie of these brave soldiers. From their initial enlistment to their final victory, this book offers a captivating narrative that sheds light on the untold stories of these unsung heroes.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, Biography

Themes: Patriotism, Sacrifice, Brotherhood, Resilience
