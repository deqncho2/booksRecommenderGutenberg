Synopsis: 

In '1914', John Denton Pinkstone, Earl of Ypres, takes us on a gripping journey through the tumultuous events of the First World War. Through his vivid and personal accounts, we witness the horrors and heroism of the soldiers on the front lines, the strategic decisions made by military leaders, and the profound impact of the war on society as a whole. From the trenches of Flanders to the diplomatic negotiations behind closed doors, this book offers a comprehensive and intimate portrayal of a pivotal year in history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War literature, Memoir

Themes: War and its consequences, Leadership and decision-making, Human resilience and sacrifice
