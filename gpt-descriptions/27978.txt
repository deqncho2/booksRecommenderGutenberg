Synopsis: In 'Commentary on Genesis, Vol. 2: Luther on Sin and the Flood', Martin Luther delves into the biblical book of Genesis, exploring the profound themes of sin and the catastrophic event of the Great Flood. With his characteristic passion and theological insight, Luther offers a thought-provoking analysis of these pivotal moments in human history, shedding light on the consequences of sin and the divine justice that unfolds. Through his commentary, Luther invites readers to reflect on the timeless questions of human nature, redemption, and the role of God's mercy in the face of human frailty.

Genres: Religious commentary, Theology, Biblical studies

Themes: Sin, Divine justice, Human nature, Redemption, God's mercy
