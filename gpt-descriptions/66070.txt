Synopsis: In "The Story of Greece: Told to Boys and Girls," Mary Macgregor takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich history and mythology of ancient Greece. From the legendary tales of gods and heroes to the rise and fall of powerful city-states, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the birthplace of Western civilization. Through engaging storytelling, Macgregor introduces young readers to the great philosophers, artists, and warriors who shaped Greece's cultural legacy. From the epic battles of the Trojan War to the founding of democracy in Athens, this book brings to life the captivating stories that have captivated generations. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, Mythology, Children's Literature

Themes: History, Mythology, Heroism, Cultural Legacy
