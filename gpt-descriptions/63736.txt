Synopsis: In "A Letter to the Rev. C. N. Wodehouse, Canon of Norwich," Charles Green pens a thought-provoking response to Wodehouse's controversial pamphlet, "Subscription the Disgrace of the English Church." Green's letter delves into the heart of the debate surrounding the necessity of subscription within the Church, challenging Wodehouse's arguments and offering a compelling alternative perspective. Through a series of well-reasoned arguments and insightful observations, Green seeks to engage readers in a critical examination of the role of subscription in the English Church.

Genres: Non-fiction, Religious discourse, Theological debate

Themes: Church reform, Religious freedom, Intellectual inquiry
