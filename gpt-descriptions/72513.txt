Synopsis: In "The Slave Trade: Slavery and Color," Theodore D. Jervey delves into the complex and often overlooked aspects of the slave trade, exploring the intricate relationship between slavery and the concept of color. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, Jervey uncovers the profound impact that color had on the lives of enslaved individuals, from the horrors of the Middle Passage to the struggles for freedom and identity. This thought-provoking work sheds light on the intersection of race, power, and human exploitation, offering a fresh perspective on a dark chapter in history.

Genres: History, Sociology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Slavery, Racism, Identity, Power Dynamics
