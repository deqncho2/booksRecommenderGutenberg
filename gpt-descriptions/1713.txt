Synopsis: In "Lincoln; An Account of his Personal Life, Especially of its Springs of Action as Revealed and Deepened by the Ordeal of War," Nathaniel W. Stephenson delves into the enigmatic life of Abraham Lincoln, exploring the intricate web of personal experiences and motivations that shaped the iconic leader. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, Stephenson uncovers the hidden depths of Lincoln's character, shedding light on the profound impact that the tumultuous period of the Civil War had on his psyche. From his humble beginnings to his rise to the presidency, this captivating account offers a fresh perspective on one of America's most revered figures.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Leadership, Personal growth, Psychological resilience
