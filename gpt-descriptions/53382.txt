Synopsis: 

'From Farm Boy to Senator: Being the History of the Boyhood and Manhood of Daniel Webster' by Horatio Alger, Jr. is a captivating biographical novel that follows the remarkable journey of Daniel Webster, from his humble beginnings as a farm boy to his rise as a prominent senator. 

The story delves into the challenges and hardships faced by young Daniel as he navigates through poverty, loss, and adversity. With determination and a thirst for knowledge, he overcomes these obstacles, displaying unwavering resilience and an unyielding spirit. As he grows older, Daniel's intelligence and eloquence become evident, propelling him towards a future filled with promise and success.

Through vivid storytelling and compelling characters, Alger paints a vivid picture of the early life of one of America's most influential figures. This inspiring tale of perseverance, ambition, and the pursuit of one's dreams will captivate readers of all ages.

Genres: Biographical Fiction, Historical Fiction

Themes: Perseverance, Ambition, Social Mobility
