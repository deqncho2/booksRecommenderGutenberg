Synopsis: 

'Round the Red Lamp: Being Facts and Fancies of Medical Life' by Arthur Conan Doyle takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of medicine, where the author skillfully weaves together a collection of intriguing stories set in the realm of doctors and patients. From the bustling streets of London to the remote corners of the countryside, these tales delve into the lives of medical professionals and the challenges they face, ranging from the mundane to the extraordinary. With each story, Doyle offers a unique glimpse into the human condition, exploring the complexities of life, love, and mortality through the lens of the medical profession.

Genres: Fiction, Medical Fiction, Short Stories

Themes: Medicine, Human Nature, Life and Death
