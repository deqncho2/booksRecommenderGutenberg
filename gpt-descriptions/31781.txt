Synopsis: In "The Bibliography of Walt Whitman" by Frank Shay, readers are taken on a captivating journey through the life and works of one of America's most influential poets. This meticulously researched and beautifully written book delves into the depths of Whitman's literary career, exploring the evolution of his writing style, the controversies surrounding his works, and the profound impact he had on American literature. From his early days as a struggling journalist to his rise as a celebrated poet, Shay's biography offers a fascinating glimpse into the mind and soul of Walt Whitman, shedding light on the man behind the iconic verses.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Identity, Individualism, Literary Legacy
