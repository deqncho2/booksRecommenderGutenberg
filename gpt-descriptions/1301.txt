Synopsis: In "The French Revolution: A History," Carlyle takes readers on a captivating journey through one of the most tumultuous periods in European history. With vivid storytelling and meticulous research, he delves into the causes, events, and aftermath of the French Revolution, painting a vivid picture of the political, social, and cultural upheaval that shook the foundations of an entire nation. From the storming of the Bastille to the rise and fall of Robespierre, Carlyle's narrative brings to life the key figures and moments that shaped this revolutionary era, leaving readers enthralled by the drama, passion, and complexity of this pivotal historical event.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Narrative history, Biography

Themes: Revolution, Power and authority, Social inequality, Ideology, Leadership
