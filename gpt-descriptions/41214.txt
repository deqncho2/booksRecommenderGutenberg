Synopsis: In 'Totem and Taboo: Resemblances Between the Psychic Lives of Savages and Neurotics', Sigmund Freud delves into the fascinating parallels between the minds of primitive societies and modern individuals plagued by neurotic tendencies. Through a captivating exploration of ancient rituals, myths, and cultural practices, Freud uncovers surprising connections that shed light on the mysteries of human behavior. This thought-provoking work offers a unique perspective on the origins of civilization and the intricate workings of the human psyche.

Genres: Psychology, Anthropology, Cultural Studies

Themes: Psychoanalysis, Collective Unconscious, Cultural Evolution
