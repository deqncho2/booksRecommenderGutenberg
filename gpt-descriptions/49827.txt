Synopsis: In 'Pygmalion and the Image' by Morris, William, a talented sculptor named Pygmalion becomes infatuated with his own creation, a beautiful statue named Galatea. As Pygmalion's obsession grows, he begins to wish for his statue to come to life. However, the consequences of his desire may be more than he bargained for. This captivating tale explores the boundaries between art and reality, and the power of human imagination.

Genres: Fantasy, Mythology, Romance

Themes: Art and creativity, Love and desire
