Synopsis: In 'King Eric and the Outlaws, Vol. 2: or, the Throne, the Church, and the People in the Thirteenth Century,' the captivating sequel to the first volume, the story continues to unfold in the tumultuous thirteenth century. King Eric faces new challenges as he navigates the complex dynamics between the throne, the church, and the people. As power struggles intensify, a group of outlaws emerges, determined to fight for justice and equality. Amidst political intrigue, religious fervor, and the plight of the common folk, the fate of the kingdom hangs in the balance.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, Political Drama

Themes: Power and Authority, Social Justice, Religious Conflict
