Synopsis: In the second volume of "Andersonville: A Story of Rebel Military Prisons," author John McElroy continues his gripping account of the infamous Confederate prison during the American Civil War. As the prisoners of Andersonville face unimaginable hardships and struggle to survive in the harsh conditions, McElroy delves deeper into the complex dynamics among the inmates and the prison authorities. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, he explores the resilience of the human spirit and the lengths people will go to in order to maintain their dignity and hope in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War, Biography

Themes: Survival, Resilience, Human Spirit, Adversity
