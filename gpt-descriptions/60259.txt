Synopsis: In "Reminiscences of Colored People of Princeton, N. J.: 1800-1900," Anna Bustill Smith takes us on a captivating journey through the lives of African Americans in Princeton during the 19th century. Through a collection of personal stories and historical accounts, Smith unveils the struggles, triumphs, and untold narratives of a community often overlooked by history. From the challenges of slavery and discrimination to the pursuit of education and the fight for equality, this book sheds light on the resilience and resilience of the colored people of Princeton.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, African American literature

Themes: Identity, Racism and discrimination, Community, Resilience, Education
