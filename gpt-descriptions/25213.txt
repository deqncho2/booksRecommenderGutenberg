Synopsis: 
In 'The Masters and Their Music', W. S. B. Mathews takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives and works of some of the greatest composers in history. Through a series of illustrative programs, Mathews provides biographical details, esthetical insights, and critical annotations that bring these musical geniuses to life. From the passionate melodies of Beethoven to the intricate harmonies of Bach, this book delves into the minds of the masters, exploring their inspirations, struggles, and the profound impact they had on the world of music. With vivid descriptions and engaging narratives, Mathews invites readers to immerse themselves in the enchanting world of classical music and discover the timeless beauty that lies within each composition.

Genres: Music, Biography, Criticism

Themes: Music appreciation, Artistic inspiration, Historical context
