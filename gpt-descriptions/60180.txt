Synopsis: 

In 'Education of the Negroes Since 1860', J.L.M. Curry delves into the transformative journey of African Americans in their pursuit of education following the Civil War. Through a meticulous examination of historical events and personal narratives, Curry uncovers the challenges, triumphs, and resilience of a community striving for knowledge and equality. From the establishment of schools to the struggles faced by educators and students alike, this book offers a compelling account of the African American educational experience during a critical period in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Education, African American Studies

Themes: Racial equality, Education as empowerment, Resilience and perseverance
