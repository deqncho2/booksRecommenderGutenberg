Synopsis: 
'Trees of Indiana: First Revised Edition' takes readers on a captivating journey through the diverse and enchanting world of Indiana's trees. Written by Charles Clemon Deam, a renowned botanist and conservationist, this comprehensive guide provides a wealth of information about the various tree species found in the state. From the towering oaks to the delicate dogwoods, Deam's meticulous descriptions and vivid illustrations bring these majestic trees to life, offering readers a deeper understanding and appreciation for the natural beauty that surrounds them. Whether you are an avid nature enthusiast or simply curious about the flora of Indiana, this book is an essential companion for exploring and discovering the remarkable trees that grace the Hoosier State.

Genres: Non-fiction, Nature Guide, Botany

Themes: Conservation, Biodiversity, Natural Beauty
