Synopsis: 
'From Bapaume to Passchendaele, 1917' by Philip Gibbs takes readers on a gripping journey through the harrowing events of World War I. Through vivid and poignant storytelling, Gibbs transports us to the battlefields of France and Belgium, where soldiers face unimaginable horrors and struggle to maintain their humanity amidst the chaos. As the war rages on, the narrative follows the lives of several individuals, each with their own unique experiences and perspectives, as they navigate the relentless brutality of the conflict. With its powerful portrayal of the human spirit and the devastating impact of war, this book offers a compelling and unforgettable account of one of history's darkest chapters.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Drama

Themes: War and its consequences, Human resilience, Loss and sacrifice
