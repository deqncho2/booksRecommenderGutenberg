Synopsis: 

In 'Searchlights on Health: The Science of Eugenics', authors Jefferis and Nichols delve into the fascinating world of eugenics, exploring the science behind it and its potential implications for society. Through a comprehensive examination of various health topics, they shed light on the importance of heredity, the impact of environment on physical and mental well-being, and the potential for improving the human race through selective breeding. This thought-provoking book challenges readers to consider the ethical and moral dilemmas surrounding eugenics, while providing valuable insights into the science behind it.

Genres: Non-fiction, Science, Health

Themes: Eugenics, Heredity, Environment, Selective Breeding, Ethics
