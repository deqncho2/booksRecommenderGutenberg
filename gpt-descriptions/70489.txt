Synopsis: In "A true & exact history of the island of Barbados," Richard Ligon takes readers on a captivating journey to the vibrant and mysterious island of Barbados. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Ligon explores the island's natural beauty, its people, and their customs. From encounters with indigenous tribes to the arrival of European settlers, Ligon's account offers a unique perspective on the island's history and its transformation over time. As he delves into the complexities of colonialism and the clash of cultures, Ligon's narrative unfolds with intrigue and fascination, leaving readers eager to uncover the secrets and untold stories of Barbados.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Travelogue, Anthropology

Themes: Colonialism, Cultural exchange, Indigenous cultures, Transformation
