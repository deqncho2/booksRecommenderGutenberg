Synopsis: 

In the Prison City, Brussels, 1914-1918: A Personal Narrative takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous years of World War I, as seen through the eyes of Julia Helen Watts. As a British woman living in Brussels during the war, Julia finds herself caught in the midst of a city transformed into a prison, where fear and uncertainty reign. Through her personal experiences and encounters with both allies and enemies, Julia's narrative offers a unique perspective on the human resilience and the power of hope in the face of adversity.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Memoir

Themes: Resilience, Survival, Human Connection, Hope, War and its Impact
