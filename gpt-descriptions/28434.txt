Synopsis: In 'The Astronomy of Milton's 'Paradise Lost',' Thomas Nathaniel Orchard delves into the celestial world that Milton weaves throughout his epic poem. Exploring the intricate connections between astronomy and theology, Orchard uncovers the hidden layers of meaning behind Milton's celestial imagery. From the cosmic battles between angels and demons to the celestial hierarchy of heaven, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the astronomical elements that enrich the narrative of 'Paradise Lost.'

Genres: Literary Criticism, Poetry, Religious Studies

Themes: Astronomy, Theology, Symbolism
