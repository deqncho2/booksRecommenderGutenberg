Synopsis: 
In "Partakers of Plenty: A Study of the First Thanksgiving," authors Jay Anderson and James Deetz delve into the historical origins of the iconic American holiday. Through meticulous research and engaging storytelling, they uncover the true story behind the first Thanksgiving, shedding light on the complex interactions between the Pilgrims and the Native Americans. As they unravel the events leading up to that fateful feast, the authors provide a fresh perspective on the cultural exchange, survival, and cooperation that took place in the early years of the Plymouth Colony. This captivating exploration of history invites readers to reexamine their understanding of Thanksgiving and the diverse narratives that shaped the foundation of America.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Cultural studies, Anthropology

Themes: Cultural exchange, Survival, Cooperation, Historical narratives
