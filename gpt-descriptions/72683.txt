Synopsis: 

'Martyrdom in Missouri, Volume 1' is a captivating historical account that delves into the religious persecution and suppression of ministers during the Civil War in the state of Missouri. Focusing on the seizure of churches and the implementation of the controversial "Test Oath" under the new Constitution, this book uncovers the untold stories of those who faced adversity and fought for their faith in a tumultuous time. Through meticulous research and compelling narratives, the author sheds light on the struggles and sacrifices endured by religious communities, offering a unique perspective on a lesser-known aspect of the Civil War era.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious history, Civil War history

Themes: Religious persecution, Freedom of religion, Civil liberties, Resilience, Faith
