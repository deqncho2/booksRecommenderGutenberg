Synopsis: In 'William Blake, the Man', Charles Gardner delves into the enigmatic life of the visionary poet and artist, William Blake. Through meticulous research and captivating storytelling, Gardner uncovers the hidden depths of Blake's imagination, exploring his mystical visions, his struggles with societal norms, and the profound impact of his art on the world. From his early years as a struggling artist to his later years as a revered figure, this biography offers a fascinating glimpse into the mind of a true creative genius.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Art History

Themes: Imagination, Spirituality, Nonconformity
