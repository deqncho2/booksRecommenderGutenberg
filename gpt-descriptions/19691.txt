Synopsis: In 'Dead Man's Plack and an Old Thorn', W. H. Hudson takes us on a captivating journey through the English countryside, where a mysterious monument and an ancient thorn tree hold secrets that have been lost to time. As the protagonist delves into the history and folklore surrounding these enigmatic landmarks, he becomes entangled in a web of intrigue and danger, uncovering a hidden world that threatens to consume him. With vivid descriptions and a sense of eerie atmosphere, Hudson masterfully weaves together elements of mystery, history, and the supernatural, keeping readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

Genres: Mystery, Historical Fiction, Supernatural

Themes: History and its impact on the present, the power of folklore and legends, the thin line between reality and the supernatural
