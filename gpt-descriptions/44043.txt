Synopsis: In "The Truth about Opium," William H. Brereton challenges the prevailing beliefs of the Anti-Opium Society and presents a compelling defense of the Indo-China Opium Trade. Through meticulous research and persuasive arguments, Brereton aims to debunk the fallacies surrounding opium and shed light on its true nature. As he delves into the history, economics, and social impact of opium, the author uncovers surprising truths that challenge conventional wisdom. This thought-provoking work invites readers to question their preconceived notions about opium and consider the complexities of its trade.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Social Commentary

Themes: Drug trade, Colonialism, Public perception, Economic impact
