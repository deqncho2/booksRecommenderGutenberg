Synopsis: In 'Women and War Work' by Helen Miller Moyes, a group of courageous women find themselves thrust into the heart of World War I, taking on roles traditionally reserved for men. As they navigate the challenges and dangers of their new responsibilities, they discover strength, resilience, and a sense of purpose they never knew they possessed. With the war raging on, these women must confront not only the external battles but also the internal conflicts that arise from their newfound independence and the sacrifices they must make. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Fiction, Women's Fiction

Themes: Gender roles, Empowerment, Sacrifice, Resilience
