Synopsis: 

In "Sicily in Shadow and in Sun: The Earthquake and the American Relief Work," Maud Howe Elliott takes readers on a captivating journey to the aftermath of a devastating earthquake in Sicily. Through vivid descriptions and personal anecdotes, Elliott paints a vivid picture of the destruction and chaos that ensued, as well as the heroic efforts of American relief workers who rushed to aid the survivors. As the story unfolds, the reader becomes immersed in the resilience and strength of the Sicilian people, their rich cultural heritage, and the transformative power of compassion and solidarity in the face of tragedy.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Memoir, Humanitarian literature

Themes: Natural disasters, Humanitarianism, Cultural resilience
