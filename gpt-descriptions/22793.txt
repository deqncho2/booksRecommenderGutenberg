Synopsis: 
In 'The Standard Oratorios: Their Stories, Their Music, And Their Composers', George P. Upton takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of oratorios. From Handel's majestic 'Messiah' to Mendelssohn's stirring 'Elijah', Upton delves into the stories behind these timeless musical masterpieces. With vivid descriptions and insightful analysis, he explores the lives of the composers and the historical context in which these oratorios were created. Whether you are a music enthusiast or simply curious about the power of choral music, this book will transport you to a world of soaring melodies and profound emotions.

Genres: Music, Biography, History
Themes: Music appreciation, Historical context, Composers' lives
