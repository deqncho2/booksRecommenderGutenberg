Synopsis: 

'Red-Tape and Pigeon-Hole Generals: As Seen From the Ranks During a Campaign in the Army of the Potomac' takes readers on a captivating journey through the eyes of soldiers serving in the Army of the Potomac during a campaign. Written by Armstrong, William H., Frick, Jacob G., and Morford, Henry, this book offers a unique perspective on the inner workings of the military hierarchy and the challenges faced by soldiers on the front lines. As the story unfolds, readers will be drawn into the gripping narrative that sheds light on the bureaucratic obstacles and the impact they have on the soldiers' experiences.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War, Military

Themes: Bureaucracy, Leadership, Soldier's Perspective
