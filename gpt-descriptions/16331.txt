Synopsis: 

In 'New York Times Current History: The European War from the Beginning to March 1915, Vol 1, No. 2: Who Began the War, and Why?', delve into the heart of one of the most significant conflicts in history. This captivating volume takes readers on a journey through the early stages of the European War, exploring the complex web of events and motivations that led to its outbreak. With meticulous research and compelling narratives, the book sheds light on the question that has haunted generations: Who truly started the war, and what were the underlying reasons?

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War history, Political analysis

Themes: Origins of war, International relations, Historical causation
