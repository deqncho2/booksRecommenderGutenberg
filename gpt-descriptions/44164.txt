Synopsis: 

In 'Criminal Types' by V. M. Masten, a renowned criminologist delves into the dark and twisted minds of some of history's most notorious criminals. Through meticulous research and analysis, Masten uncovers the hidden motivations and psychological complexities that drive these individuals to commit heinous acts. From calculating serial killers to cunning con artists, this gripping exploration of criminal types will keep readers on the edge of their seats, questioning the boundaries of human nature and the thin line between good and evil.

Genres: True Crime, Psychology, Non-fiction

Themes: Criminal psychology, Motivation, Morality
