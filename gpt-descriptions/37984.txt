Synopsis: In the 11th edition of the renowned Encyclopaedia Britannica, embark on a fascinating journey from the captivating life of Lord George Gordon to the intricate world of grasses. This volume delves into the political and social impact of Lord George Gordon, a controversial figure who left an indelible mark on history. As the pages turn, readers are transported to the realm of botany, exploring the diverse and often overlooked world of grasses. From their ecological significance to their role in agriculture and beyond, this volume unveils the hidden wonders of these seemingly ordinary plants. 

Genres: Reference, History, Botany

Themes: Politics, Social Impact, Botanical Diversity, Ecological Significance
