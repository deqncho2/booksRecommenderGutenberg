Synopsis: In "The Teaching and Cultivation of the French Language in England during Tudor and Stuart Times," Kathleen Rebillon delves into the fascinating history of how the French language was taught and embraced in England during the Tudor and Stuart eras. With an introductory chapter exploring the preceding period, Rebillon uncovers the methods, challenges, and cultural significance of learning French during this transformative time in English history. From the influence of French tutors to the impact of political and religious changes, this book offers a captivating exploration of the linguistic and cultural exchange between England and France.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Language studies, Cultural history

Themes: Language acquisition, Cultural exchange, Education, Historical linguistics
