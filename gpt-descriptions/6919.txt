Synopsis: 

In 'Darwiniana: Essays — Volume 02', Thomas Henry Huxley delves into the fascinating world of evolutionary biology and its implications for society. Through a collection of thought-provoking essays, Huxley explores various aspects of Charles Darwin's groundbreaking theories, examining their impact on religion, ethics, and the natural world. From dissecting the intricate relationship between science and faith to pondering the origins of human consciousness, Huxley's insightful writings challenge conventional wisdom and invite readers to question their own beliefs. With eloquence and intellectual rigor, 'Darwiniana: Essays — Volume 02' offers a captivating exploration of the profound implications of Darwin's evolutionary ideas.

Genres: Science, Philosophy, Essay collection

Themes: Evolution, Religion and Science, Ethics, Human Consciousness
