Synopsis: 
Ulysses by James Joyce is a modernist masterpiece that takes place over the course of a single day in Dublin, following the lives of three main characters: Stephen Dedalus, Leopold Bloom, and Molly Bloom. Through a stream-of-consciousness narrative style, Joyce delves into the inner thoughts, desires, and experiences of these characters as they navigate the mundane and extraordinary aspects of their lives. As the day unfolds, the reader is taken on a journey through various encounters, conversations, and events that reveal the complexities of human existence, exploring themes of identity, sexuality, religion, nationalism, and the search for meaning in a fragmented world.

Genres: Modernist literature, Stream-of-consciousness, Experimental fiction

Themes: Identity, Sexuality, Religion, Nationalism, Meaning of life
