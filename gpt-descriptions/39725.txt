Synopsis: In "William Morris: Poet, Craftsman, Socialist," Elisabeth Luther Cary delves into the fascinating life of William Morris, a multi-talented individual who defied societal norms and left an indelible mark on the arts and politics. From his early days as a poet and his influential role in the Arts and Crafts Movement to his passionate advocacy for socialism, Morris's journey is one of creativity, activism, and a relentless pursuit of a more just society. Cary's vivid portrayal of Morris's life and ideals will captivate readers, offering a glimpse into the mind of a visionary who sought to bridge the gap between art and social change.

Genres: Biography, History, Art

Themes: Artistic expression, Socialism, Activism
