Synopsis: 
In "Jeanne D'Arc: Her Life And Death," Mrs. Oliphant delves into the captivating story of Joan of Arc, the legendary French heroine. Through meticulous research and vivid storytelling, the author brings to life the extraordinary journey of a young peasant girl who defied societal norms and led armies to fight for her country's freedom. From her humble beginnings to her pivotal role in the Hundred Years' War, this biography explores the trials and triumphs of a remarkable woman who became a symbol of courage and inspiration.

Genres: Biography, Historical Fiction, Non-fiction

Themes: Heroism, Faith, Gender Roles, National Identity
