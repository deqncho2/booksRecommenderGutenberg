Synopsis: 

In 'Ireland Since Parnell', Daniel Desmond Sheehan takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous history of Ireland, exploring the country's political, social, and cultural developments since the era of Charles Stewart Parnell. From the rise of the Irish nationalist movement to the struggles for independence and the subsequent challenges faced by the newly formed Irish Free State, Sheehan delves into the complex dynamics that have shaped Ireland's modern identity. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, the author sheds light on the key figures, events, and ideologies that have shaped Ireland's path, offering a comprehensive and engaging account of the nation's history.

Genres: History, Politics, Cultural Studies

Themes: Irish nationalism, Independence, Political movements, Cultural identity
