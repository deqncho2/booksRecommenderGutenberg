Synopsis: In "Oliver Wendell Holmes," William Dean Howells takes readers on a captivating journey through the life and mind of the renowned American poet, essayist, and physician. From Holmes' early years as a Harvard student to his influential role in the literary and intellectual circles of 19th-century Boston, Howells paints a vivid portrait of a man whose wit, wisdom, and unique perspective on life left an indelible mark on American literature. Through anecdotes, conversations, and personal reflections, Howells delves into the complexities of Holmes' character, revealing the struggles, triumphs, and profound insights that shaped his remarkable career.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, Memoir

Themes: Intellectualism, Identity, Legacy, Literary Influence
