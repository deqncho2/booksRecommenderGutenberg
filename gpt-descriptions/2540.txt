Synopsis: 
In "Father and Son: A Study of Two Temperaments," Edmund Gosse takes us on a captivating journey through his complex relationship with his father. Set in the late 19th century, this memoir delves into the contrasting personalities of the two men, as they navigate the realms of religion, science, and art. As Gosse grapples with his own desires for intellectual freedom and self-discovery, he must confront the deeply ingrained beliefs and expectations of his devoutly religious father. Through vivid anecdotes and introspective reflections, Gosse paints a poignant portrait of the struggles and conflicts that arise when two individuals with divergent temperaments collide. 

Genres: Memoir, Biography, Autobiography

Themes: Father-son relationships, Religion vs. Science, Individuality and self-discovery
