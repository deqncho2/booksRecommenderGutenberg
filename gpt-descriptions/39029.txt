Synopsis: 

In the 11th edition of the renowned Encyclopaedia Britannica, Volume 13, Slice 7 takes readers on an intriguing journey from the fascinating world of horticulture to the vast and enigmatic Hudson Bay. This volume delves into the art and science of cultivating plants, exploring various techniques, tools, and practices that have shaped the development of gardens and landscapes throughout history. From the meticulous care of ornamental plants to the cultivation of fruits and vegetables, this edition provides a comprehensive and captivating exploration of horticulture. Moving on, the volume then transports readers to the vast wilderness of Hudson Bay, unveiling its rich history, diverse wildlife, and the remarkable stories of exploration and trade that have unfolded in this remote region. 

Genres: Reference, Non-fiction

Themes: Horticulture, Botany, Exploration, History, Wildlife
