Synopsis: 
'Paradoxes and Problemes: With two characters and an essay of valour. Now for the first time reprinted from the editions of 1633 and 1652 with one additional probleme.' by John Donne is a collection of thought-provoking paradoxes and problems that challenge conventional wisdom and explore the complexities of human nature. Through a series of dialogues and essays, Donne delves into various topics, including love, religion, and the nature of courage. The addition of one new problem adds an extra layer of intrigue to this reprinted edition.

Genres: 
Philosophical literature, Essay collection, Dialogue

Themes: 
Paradoxes, Human nature, Love, Religion, Courage
