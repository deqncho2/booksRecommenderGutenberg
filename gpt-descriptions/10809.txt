Synopsis: 

In the second edition of 'Why We Are at War', the esteemed University of Oxford's Faculty of Modern History delves into the complex web of conflicts that have shaped our world. This revised edition offers a captivating exploration of the underlying reasons behind humanity's seemingly perpetual state of war. From ancient civilizations to modern-day conflicts, the book uncovers the intricate tapestry of motivations, ideologies, and power struggles that have driven nations to engage in warfare throughout history. Through meticulous research and insightful analysis, the authors shed light on the multifaceted nature of war, challenging conventional narratives and providing a fresh perspective on the age-old question of why we fight.

Genres: History, Political Science, Military Studies

Themes: Causes of War, Power and Politics, Human Nature
