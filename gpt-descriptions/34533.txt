Synopsis: 

In the third volume of the renowned Encyclopaedia Britannica, 11th Edition, we delve into a fascinating exploration of topics ranging from the historical madness of "Bedlam" to the influential life and works of the enigmatic George Benson. This captivating slice of knowledge takes readers on a journey through the depths of human psychology, the evolution of mental health institutions, and the profound impact of an artist's vision on society. From the haunting corridors of Bedlam to the artistic genius of George Benson, this volume offers a captivating blend of history, psychology, and artistry that will leave readers eager to uncover the secrets within.

Genres: Reference, History, Biography

Themes: Mental health, Artistic expression, Historical institutions
