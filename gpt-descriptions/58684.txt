Synopsis: 

'Finding Themselves: The Letters of an American Army Chief Nurse in the British Hospital in France' is a captivating collection of letters written by Julia C. Stimson, an American nurse serving in a British hospital during World War I. Through her heartfelt and vivid correspondence, Stimson takes readers on a remarkable journey, sharing her experiences, challenges, and triumphs as she navigates the complexities of war and discovers her true self amidst the chaos and devastation. From the front lines to the intimate moments of connection with patients and fellow nurses, Stimson's letters offer a unique and poignant perspective on the human spirit and the power of compassion in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: Identity, Resilience, Compassion, War, Self-discovery
