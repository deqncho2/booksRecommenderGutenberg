Synopsis: 

In 'International Copyright: Considered in some of its Relations to Ethics and Political Economy', George Haven Putnam delves into the complex world of copyright laws and their impact on ethics and political economy. Through a thought-provoking exploration, Putnam examines the challenges faced by authors, publishers, and society as a whole in an increasingly globalized world. With insightful analysis and compelling arguments, he raises crucial questions about the balance between intellectual property rights and the dissemination of knowledge. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Political Science, Economics

Themes: Copyright, Ethics, Globalization, Intellectual Property, Publishing Industry
