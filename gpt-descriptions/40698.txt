Synopsis: 

'Address to the People of the United States, together with the Proceedings and Resolutions of the Pro-Slavery Convention of Missouri, Held at Lexington, July 1855' takes readers back to a pivotal moment in American history. This powerful and controversial work sheds light on the intense debates surrounding slavery in the mid-19th century. Through a collection of speeches, resolutions, and discussions from the Pro-Slavery Convention of Missouri, the book offers a unique perspective on the complex and deeply divisive issue that tore the nation apart. 

Genres: Historical, Political, Non-fiction

Themes: Slavery, Civil Rights, Political Ideologies
