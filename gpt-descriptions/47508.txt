Synopsis: 

In 'Wasps, Social and Solitary', Elizabeth Gifford Peckham and George Williams Peckham delve into the fascinating world of wasps, uncovering the intricate dynamics of their social structures and the intriguing behaviors of both social and solitary species. Through their meticulous observations and scientific analysis, the authors shed light on the remarkable intelligence, communication, and cooperation exhibited by these often misunderstood creatures. From the elaborate nests of social wasps to the solitary wasps' solitary hunting strategies, this book offers a captivating exploration of the diverse lives of these buzzing insects.

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Science

Themes: Animal behavior, Social structures, Insect intelligence
