Synopsis: In "The Falling Flag: Evacuation of Richmond, Retreat and Surrender at Appomattox," Edward M. Boykin takes readers on a gripping journey through the final days of the American Civil War. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Boykin paints a vivid picture of the chaos, desperation, and ultimately, the surrender that marked the end of the Confederacy. From the evacuation of Richmond, the capital of the Confederacy, to the dramatic events leading up to the historic surrender at Appomattox Court House, this book offers a compelling narrative that explores the human drama and the profound impact of war on both soldiers and civilians. As the Confederate forces face defeat, readers will be captivated by the struggles, sacrifices, and moments of heroism that unfold in this pivotal moment in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American history

Themes: War and its consequences, Leadership and decision-making, Loyalty and sacrifice
