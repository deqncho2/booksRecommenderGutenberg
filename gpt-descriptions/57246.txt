Synopsis: 

In "Rifles and Riflemen at the Battle of Kings Mountain," the United States National Park Service takes readers on a captivating journey back to the pivotal battle that changed the course of the American Revolution. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, this book unveils the untold stories of the brave riflemen who fought against all odds to secure victory on the rugged slopes of Kings Mountain. From the intense battles to the personal sacrifices made by these unsung heroes, this gripping account sheds light on a crucial turning point in American history.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Military history, American Revolution

Themes: Courage and sacrifice, Tactical warfare, Importance of individual marksmanship, Impact of local militias in the American Revolution
