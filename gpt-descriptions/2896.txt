Synopsis: In 'Story of the Session of the California Legislature of 1909', Hichborn takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous world of politics and power. Set against the backdrop of the California Legislature, this gripping narrative follows the lives of various politicians, lobbyists, and activists as they navigate the treacherous waters of state governance. With secrets, scandals, and high-stakes negotiations at every turn, the fate of California hangs in the balance. Will the legislators rise above their personal ambitions and work for the greater good, or will corruption and self-interest prevail?

Genres: Political fiction, Historical fiction, Drama

Themes: Power and corruption, Idealism vs. pragmatism, Ethics in politics
