Synopsis: 

In "Lights and Shadows in Confederate Prisons: A Personal Experience, 1864-5," Homer B. Sprague takes readers on a gripping journey through his own harrowing experiences as a prisoner of war during the American Civil War. From the moment he is captured by Confederate forces to his eventual release, Sprague vividly recounts the daily struggles, moments of hope, and unimaginable horrors he and his fellow prisoners endured within the confines of Confederate prisons. Through his personal narrative, the reader is transported into a world of darkness and despair, where the flickering lights of resilience and the haunting shadows of suffering intertwine.

Genres: Memoir, History, War

Themes: Survival, Resilience, Human Spirit, Prisoner of War Experience
