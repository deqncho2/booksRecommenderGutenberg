Synopsis: 

In "Vanished Arizona: Recollections of the Army Life by a New England Woman," Martha Summerhayes takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed landscapes of the American Southwest during the late 1800s. Leaving behind her comfortable life in New England, Martha finds herself thrust into the harsh realities of army life as she accompanies her husband, an officer in the U.S. Army, to the Arizona Territory. From encounters with Native American tribes to the challenges of living in remote military outposts, Martha's vivid recollections paint a vivid picture of the hardships and adventures she faced in this rugged and unpredictable land. As she navigates the complexities of frontier life, Martha's story becomes a testament to resilience, courage, and the indomitable spirit of a woman determined to adapt and thrive in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, Adventure

Themes: Frontier life, Women's experiences, Cultural encounters, Resilience
