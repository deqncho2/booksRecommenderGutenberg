Synopsis: In 'Calamities and Quarrels of Authors', Disraeli takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous lives of famous authors, revealing the hidden struggles, rivalries, and scandals that shaped their literary legacies. From Shakespeare to Byron, Dickens to Shelley, this book uncovers the untold stories behind their works, shedding light on the dark underbelly of the literary world. With wit and insight, Disraeli delves into the personal lives and creative battles of these iconic figures, offering a fascinating glimpse into the price of literary genius.

Genres: Biography, Literary Criticism, History

Themes: Creative rivalry, Personal struggles, Literary legacy
