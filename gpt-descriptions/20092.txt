Synopsis: In "Saint Bartholomew's Eve: A Tale of the Huguenot Wars," George Alfred Henty takes readers on a thrilling journey through the tumultuous period of religious conflict in 16th century France. The story follows the adventures of young Philip Fletcher, an Englishman who finds himself caught up in the violent clashes between the Huguenots and the Catholics. As Philip navigates treacherous political landscapes, dangerous battles, and unexpected alliances, he must rely on his courage, wit, and determination to survive. With vivid historical detail and gripping action, Henty paints a vivid picture of a time when faith, loyalty, and love were put to the ultimate test.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Adventure, War

Themes: Religious Conflict, Loyalty, Courage, Survival
