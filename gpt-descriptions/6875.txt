Synopsis: In "France and England in North America, Part V: Count Frontenac, New France, Louis XIV" by Francis Parkman, readers are transported to the tumultuous era of the late 17th century, where the fate of two great nations hangs in the balance. Amidst the backdrop of the New World, the charismatic and cunning Count Frontenac emerges as a central figure, navigating the treacherous waters of colonial rivalry, political intrigue, and war. As the French and English powers clash, alliances are forged and broken, battles are fought, and the destiny of North America is forever altered. With vivid storytelling and meticulous historical research, Parkman brings to life the larger-than-life characters and events that shaped the continent during this pivotal period.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Biography, Adventure

Themes: Colonial rivalry, Political intrigue, War, Leadership, Exploration
