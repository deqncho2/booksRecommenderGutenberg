Synopsis: In "Sons and Fathers" by Harry Stillwell Edwards, a young man named John finds himself torn between his loyalty to his father and his desire for independence. As he navigates the complexities of family dynamics and societal expectations, John must confront the consequences of his choices and the impact they have on his relationships. Set against the backdrop of the American South, this captivating novel explores the timeless themes of love, duty, and the struggle for personal identity.

Genres: Fiction, Coming-of-age, Southern literature

Themes: Family, Independence, Identity, Duty
