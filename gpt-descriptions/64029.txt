Synopsis: 

'Life at the Zoo: Notes and Traditions of the Regent's Park Gardens' takes readers on a captivating journey into the hidden world of one of the world's most renowned zoos. Through the eyes of author C. J. Cornish, we are introduced to a diverse cast of animal characters, each with their own unique personalities and stories. From mischievous monkeys to majestic lions, from playful penguins to wise old elephants, this book offers a fascinating glimpse into the daily lives and interactions of the zoo's inhabitants. As we delve deeper into the pages, we uncover surprising secrets, heartwarming anecdotes, and thought-provoking observations about the delicate balance between humans and animals in this extraordinary setting. 

Genres: Non-fiction, Natural History, Memoir

Themes: Animal behavior, Conservation, Human-animal relationships
