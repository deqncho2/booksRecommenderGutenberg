Synopsis: In "Mozart: The story of a little boy and his sister who gave concerts," Tapper, Thomas takes us on a captivating journey into the life of a young musical prodigy. Set in the 18th century, this enchanting tale follows the extraordinary adventures of a little boy named Mozart and his talented sister as they embark on a series of awe-inspiring concerts. From their humble beginnings to their rise to fame, the siblings' musical prowess and unwavering determination will leave readers spellbound. With each performance, they captivate audiences and defy societal expectations, proving that age is no barrier to greatness. This heartwarming story celebrates the power of music, the bond between siblings, and the indomitable spirit of a young genius.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Children's Literature, Biography

Themes: Music, Talent, Family, Perseverance
