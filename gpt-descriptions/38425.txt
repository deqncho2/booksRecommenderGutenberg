Synopsis: 

In 'Geographic Variation in the North American Cyprinid Fish, Hybopsis gracilis', Cross and Olund delve into the fascinating world of the Hybopsis gracilis, a cyprinid fish species found in North America. Through meticulous research and analysis, they uncover the intriguing patterns of geographic variation within this species, shedding light on the factors that contribute to its diverse characteristics across different regions. From examining the fish's physical traits to studying its genetic makeup, the authors provide a comprehensive exploration of the Hybopsis gracilis and its remarkable adaptability to various environments. 

Genres: Scientific research, Zoology, Ecology

Themes: Biodiversity, Adaptation, Geographic variation
