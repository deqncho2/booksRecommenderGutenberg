Synopsis: In "The Cross and the Shamrock: Or, How To Defend The Faith," author Hugh Quigley takes readers on a captivating journey through the trials and triumphs of Irish-American Catholics in the United States. Set in the great republic of Washington, the story follows the lives of the children of St. Patrick as they navigate the temptations, sufferings, and trials that come with their faith. This tale of real-life experiences offers both entertainment and special instructions for Catholic male and female servants in the United States, providing a unique perspective on the challenges faced by this community.

Genres: Historical Fiction, Religious Fiction, Cultural Fiction

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Immigration and assimilation, Identity and belonging
