Synopsis: In 'The Crest-Wave of Evolution: A Course of Lectures in History', Kenneth Morris takes readers on a captivating journey through time, exploring the fascinating tapestry of human history. Through a series of thought-provoking lectures given to graduates at the Raja-Yoga College, Morris delves into the depths of civilization, uncovering the hidden patterns and forces that have shaped our world. From ancient civilizations to modern societies, he weaves together a narrative that reveals the crest-waves of evolution, the rise and fall of empires, and the profound impact of human consciousness on the course of history. This enlightening work invites readers to ponder the mysteries of our past and consider the implications for our future.

Genres: Non-fiction, History, Philosophy

Themes: Evolution, Human consciousness, Civilization, Patterns of history
