Synopsis: 
'The Jesuit Missions: A Chronicle of the Cross in the Wilderness' by Thomas Guthrie Marquis takes readers on a captivating journey through the untamed wilderness of North America, where Jesuit missionaries risked their lives to spread their faith. This chronicle delves into the trials and triumphs of these brave individuals as they navigate treacherous landscapes, encounter indigenous cultures, and face the challenges of establishing missions in unfamiliar territories. With vivid descriptions and compelling narratives, Marquis paints a vivid picture of the Jesuit missions and the profound impact they had on both the missionaries and the native populations they sought to convert.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious history, Adventure

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Cultural exchange, Exploration and discovery
