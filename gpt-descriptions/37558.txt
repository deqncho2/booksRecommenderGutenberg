Synopsis: 
In 'A Catalogue of Sculpture in the Department of Greek and Roman Antiquities, British Museum, Volume 1 (of 2)', Arthur Hamilton Smith takes readers on a captivating journey through the rich collection of ancient sculptures housed in the British Museum. From the intricate details of Greek masterpieces to the awe-inspiring grandeur of Roman statues, Smith's meticulous descriptions and insightful analysis bring these ancient works of art to life. As readers delve into the pages of this catalogue, they will uncover the stories behind each sculpture, unraveling the mysteries of their creation, purpose, and historical significance. With stunning photographs accompanying the text, this volume offers a tantalizing glimpse into the world of ancient art, inviting readers to explore the beauty and wonder of these timeless masterpieces.

Genres: Art History, Archaeology, Catalogue

Themes: Ancient Sculpture, Greek and Roman Antiquities, Historical Significance
