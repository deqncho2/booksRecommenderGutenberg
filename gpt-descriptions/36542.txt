Synopsis: In "The American Occupation of the Philippines 1898-1912," James H. Blount delves into the complex and often overlooked period of American history when the United States took control of the Philippines after the Spanish-American War. Through meticulous research and compelling storytelling, Blount uncovers the political, social, and cultural dynamics that shaped this tumultuous era. From the clash of ideologies to the struggles for independence, this book offers a captivating exploration of a pivotal chapter in American imperialism.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Political history, Cultural studies

Themes: Imperialism, Colonialism, Nationalism, Identity, Power dynamics
