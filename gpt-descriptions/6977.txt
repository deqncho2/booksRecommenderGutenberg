Synopsis: 

In "Flying for France: With the American Escadrille at Verdun," James R. McConnell takes readers on a thrilling journey through the skies of World War I. As a young American pilot, McConnell joins the French Escadrille Lafayette and finds himself in the midst of the intense battle at Verdun. With vivid descriptions and heart-pounding aerial combat scenes, McConnell shares his experiences, the camaraderie among the pilots, and the harrowing dangers they face daily. From the exhilaration of flying to the devastating realities of war, this memoir offers a captivating glimpse into the life of a fighter pilot during one of history's most tumultuous periods.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War

Themes: Courage, Patriotism, Comradeship, Sacrifice
