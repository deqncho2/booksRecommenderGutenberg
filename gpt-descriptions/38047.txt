Synopsis: In 'Social Value: A Study in Economic Theory, Critical and Constructive', Benjamin M. Anderson delves into the intricate relationship between economics and society, unraveling the hidden forces that shape our understanding of value. Through a thought-provoking exploration of economic theory, Anderson challenges conventional wisdom and offers a fresh perspective on the true nature of social value. As he navigates through the complexities of economic systems, Anderson uncovers surprising insights that will leave readers questioning their preconceived notions and reevaluating the way they perceive the world around them.

Genres: Non-fiction, Economics, Philosophy

Themes: Economic theory, Social value, Critique of conventional wisdom
