Synopsis:
'The Early Christians in Rome' takes readers on a captivating journey through the ancient city of Rome, exploring the lives and struggles of the early Christian community. Set against the backdrop of a city steeped in history and pagan traditions, this book delves into the challenges faced by the early Christians as they navigate a society that often viewed them with suspicion and hostility. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Spence-Jones brings to life the vibrant characters and events that shaped the early Christian movement in Rome, offering a fascinating glimpse into a pivotal period of religious and cultural transformation.

Genres:
Historical non-fiction, Religious studies, Biography

Themes:
Religious persecution, Cultural clash, Faith and resilience
