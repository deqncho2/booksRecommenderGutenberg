Synopsis: In the second volume of "Personal Recollections of Joan of Arc," Mark Twain continues his captivating retelling of the extraordinary life of Joan of Arc. This installment delves deeper into Joan's journey as she faces new challenges, encounters unexpected allies, and navigates the treacherous political landscape of medieval France. Twain's vivid storytelling brings to life the indomitable spirit of this legendary figure, capturing the essence of her unwavering faith, courage, and determination. As Joan's story unfolds, readers are transported to a tumultuous era filled with intrigue, betrayal, and the timeless struggle between good and evil.

Genres: Historical fiction, Biographical fiction, Adventure

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Heroism, Gender roles, Political intrigue
