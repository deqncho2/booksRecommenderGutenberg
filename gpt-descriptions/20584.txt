Synopsis: In "You Should Worry Says John Henry" by George V. Hobart, readers are introduced to John Henry, a mysterious and enigmatic character who seems to have an uncanny ability to predict the future. As the story unfolds, John Henry's predictions become increasingly accurate, leaving those around him in awe and wonder. However, his gift comes with a warning - worry. John Henry believes that worry is not only necessary but also beneficial, and he sets out to convince others of its importance. Through a series of thought-provoking encounters and unexpected events, the story explores the power of worry and its impact on human lives.

Genres: Fiction, Mystery, Psychological Thriller

Themes: Fate and destiny, Human psychology, Importance of worry
