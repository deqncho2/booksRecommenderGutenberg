Synopsis: In the second volume of "Reminiscences of Sixty Years in Public Affairs," George S. Boutwell, a prominent figure in American politics, continues to share his firsthand experiences and insights into the tumultuous events that shaped the nation. From his involvement in the impeachment trial of President Andrew Johnson to his role in the Reconstruction era and the fight for civil rights, Boutwell offers a captivating account of his time in public service. With vivid anecdotes and astute observations, he delves into the challenges and triumphs of a nation grappling with issues of power, justice, and equality. This volume is a compelling journey through a pivotal period in American history, as seen through the eyes of a seasoned statesman.

Genres: Autobiography, History, Political Memoir

Themes: American politics, Civil rights, Reconstruction era, Power dynamics, Justice
