Synopsis:
In 'War and the Future: Italy, France and Britain at War', H. G. Wells takes readers on a captivating journey through the tumultuous times of World War I. Through his insightful observations and analysis, Wells explores the impact of war on the nations of Italy, France, and Britain, delving into the political, social, and technological aspects that shaped their experiences. From the trenches to the home front, Wells paints a vivid picture of the challenges faced by these countries and the profound changes brought about by the war. With his characteristic blend of historical detail and visionary foresight, Wells offers a thought-provoking exploration of the consequences of war and the potential paths to a better future.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, War literature, Political analysis

Themes: War and its effects, National identity, Technological advancements, Sociopolitical change
