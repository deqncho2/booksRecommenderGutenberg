Synopsis: 
In 'Ten Days in a Mad-House', Nellie Bly, a courageous and determined journalist, embarks on a daring undercover mission to expose the horrifying conditions within Blackwell's Island Asylum. Posing as a mentally ill patient, she endures the dehumanizing treatment and uncovers the shocking truth behind the mistreatment of the inmates. Through her harrowing experiences, Bly sheds light on the dark underbelly of the asylum system, revealing the desperate need for reform.

Genres: Non-fiction, Investigative journalism, Memoir

Themes: Mental health, Institutional abuse, Advocacy for social change
