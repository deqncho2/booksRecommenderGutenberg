Synopsis: 
In 'John Thorndyke's Cases: related by Christopher Jervis and edited by R. Austin Freeman', readers are introduced to the brilliant forensic scientist, Dr. John Thorndyke, as he unravels a series of perplexing mysteries. From a baffling case of a missing diamond to a suspicious death in a locked room, Thorndyke's keen intellect and scientific approach are put to the test. As the cases unfold, the reader is taken on a thrilling journey through intricate investigations, unexpected twists, and the meticulous unraveling of clues. With each case, the stakes rise higher, leaving readers on the edge of their seats, eager to discover the truth behind each enigma.

Genres: Mystery, Crime, Detective Fiction

Themes: Forensic science, Deductive reasoning, Justice, Intrigue
