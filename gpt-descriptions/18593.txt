Synopsis: In the third volume of "The Life of George Washington," author John Marshall delves into the pivotal role that Washington played as the Commander in Chief of the American forces during the War of Independence. This captivating biography explores the challenges and triumphs that Washington faced as he led his country to establish its independence. From the strategic battles to the personal sacrifices, Marshall paints a vivid portrait of Washington's unwavering determination and leadership. As the first President of the United States, Washington's legacy is further examined, shedding light on the early years of the nation's history.

Genres: Biography, History

Themes: Leadership, Independence, Patriotism
