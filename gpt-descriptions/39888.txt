Synopsis: 
In 'Thirty Days in Lithuania in 1919', Peter Saurusaitis takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous events of a nation in the midst of war. Through his vivid and personal account, we witness the struggles, triumphs, and heartbreaks of the Lithuanian people during a pivotal month in their history. From the chaos of battlefields to the intimate moments of human connection, Saurusaitis paints a poignant picture of a nation fighting for its independence and identity. As the days unfold, secrets are revealed, alliances are tested, and the true cost of freedom becomes clear. 

Genres: Historical Fiction, War, Memoir

Themes: National identity, Independence, Sacrifice
