Synopsis: In "Dante and the early astronomers," Mary Acworth Orr takes readers on a captivating journey through the celestial wonders of the Middle Ages. Through the lens of Dante's Divine Comedy, Orr explores the fascinating intersection of astronomy, philosophy, and literature. From the ancient Greeks to the Islamic scholars and the Renaissance astronomers, the book unveils the profound impact of celestial knowledge on Dante's poetic vision. As readers delve into the cosmic realms, they will discover the hidden connections between the stars and the human condition, ultimately leading to a deeper understanding of Dante's timeless masterpiece.

Genres: Literary criticism, History, Astronomy

Themes: Astronomy and literature, Influence of ancient knowledge, Symbolism in Dante's Divine Comedy
