Synopsis: 

In "Diary Kept by Rifleman B. C. Stubbs of the Second Draft Sent to the Queen Victoria Rifles in France," Bernard C. Stubbs takes us on a gripping journey through the harrowing experiences of a young rifleman during World War I. Through his diary entries, Stubbs provides a raw and intimate account of the daily struggles, camaraderie, and horrors faced by soldiers on the front lines. From the trenches to the battlefields, from moments of despair to fleeting moments of hope, Stubbs' diary offers a poignant and unfiltered glimpse into the realities of war.

Genres: Historical Fiction, War Literature, Memoir

Themes: War, Sacrifice, Comradeship, Survival, Loss
