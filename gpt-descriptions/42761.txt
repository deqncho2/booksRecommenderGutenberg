Synopsis: In 'Fables and Fabulists: Ancient and Modern', Thomas Newbigging takes readers on a captivating journey through the world of fables, exploring both ancient and modern tales. From Aesop's timeless fables to contemporary works, Newbigging delves into the rich tapestry of storytelling, examining the enduring power of these narratives and their impact on our lives. With a blend of analysis, anecdotes, and thought-provoking insights, this book offers a fresh perspective on the art of fable-telling, inviting readers to rediscover the magic and wisdom hidden within these enchanting tales.

Genres: Literary Criticism, Folklore, Comparative Literature

Themes: The power of storytelling, Morality and ethics, Cultural transmission
