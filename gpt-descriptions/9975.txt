Synopsis: In 'Paris War Days: Diary of an American', Charles Inman Barnard takes us on a captivating journey through the tumultuous streets of Paris during World War I. Through his vivid and intimate diary entries, we witness the city's transformation from a vibrant cultural hub to a war-torn battleground. As Barnard navigates the chaos and danger, he encounters a cast of intriguing characters, experiences unexpected friendships, and grapples with the profound impact of war on both individuals and society. This gripping memoir offers a unique perspective on the human spirit's resilience in the face of adversity.

Genres: Memoir, Historical Non-fiction, War Literature

Themes: War and its effects, Resilience, Cultural identity
