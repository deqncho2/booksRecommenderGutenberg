Synopsis: In "On the supply of printed books from the library to the reading room of the British Museum," Sir Anthony Panizzi explores the challenges and innovations in providing access to printed books within the prestigious British Museum. Through a detailed examination of the library's operations, Panizzi sheds light on the intricate system of cataloging, retrieval, and delivery that ensures readers can access the vast collection efficiently. This work offers a fascinating glimpse into the inner workings of one of the world's most renowned libraries and the efforts made to facilitate scholarly research.

Genres: Non-fiction, Library Science, History

Themes: Access to knowledge, Library management, Scholarly research
