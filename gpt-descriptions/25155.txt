Synopsis: In 'How to Draw a Straight Line: A Lecture on Linkages', Alfred Bray Kempe takes readers on a captivating journey into the world of mechanical linkages, revealing the fascinating secrets behind the seemingly simple task of drawing a straight line. Through a combination of historical anecdotes, mathematical principles, and ingenious inventions, Kempe explores the intricate mechanisms that enable straight lines to be drawn with precision. As he delves into the complexities of linkages, Kempe unveils a world of hidden connections and unexpected possibilities, challenging readers to rethink their understanding of geometry and mechanical design. With clarity and enthusiasm, Kempe's lecture captivates both the curious layperson and the seasoned mathematician, offering a fresh perspective on the beauty and elegance of mechanical systems.

Genres: Non-fiction, Mathematics, Engineering

Themes: Geometry, Mechanical Design, Historical Development of Linkages
