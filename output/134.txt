Synopsis: 
In 'Maria; Or, The Wrongs of Woman', Mary Wollstonecraft takes us on a gripping journey through the life of Maria, a young woman trapped in a world of societal constraints and oppressive relationships. As Maria navigates the complexities of love, marriage, and motherhood, she finds herself questioning the very foundations of the society she lives in. With her indomitable spirit and unwavering determination, Maria fights against the injustices she faces, seeking to reclaim her autonomy and challenge the status quo. This thought-provoking tale explores the limits placed on women in the 18th century and the urgent need for societal change.

Genres: Fiction, Feminist Literature, Social Commentary

Themes: Gender inequality, Women's rights, Freedom, Individualism, Social reform
