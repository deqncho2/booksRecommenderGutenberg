Synopsis: 
In 'Zen and the Art of the Internet', Brendan P. Kehoe takes readers on a captivating journey through the early days of the internet, exploring its vast potential and the profound impact it has on society. Through personal anecdotes and insightful observations, Kehoe delves into the world of online communities, hacking, and the philosophy behind the internet's development. As he navigates the complexities of this digital landscape, Kehoe invites readers to question their own relationship with technology and consider the transformative power of the internet.

Genres: Non-fiction, Technology, Memoir

Themes: Internet culture, Community, Philosophy of technology
