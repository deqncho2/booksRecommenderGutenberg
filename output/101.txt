Synopsis: In 'The Hacker Crackdown: Law and Disorder on the Electronic Frontier', Bruce Sterling takes readers on a thrilling journey into the world of hackers, law enforcement, and the battle for control on the electronic frontier. Set in the early 1990s, this non-fiction work explores the rise of computer hacking and the subsequent crackdown by authorities. With vivid storytelling and real-life accounts, Sterling delves into the cat-and-mouse game between hackers and those seeking to bring them to justice. As the lines between right and wrong blur, readers are left questioning the nature of freedom, privacy, and the power of technology.

Genres: Non-fiction, True Crime, Technology

Themes: Cybersecurity, Civil Liberties, Technological Advancement
