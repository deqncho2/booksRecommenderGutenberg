Synopsis: 
In John Milton's epic poem, 'Paradise Lost', a grand tale unfolds as the reader is transported to the depths of Hell, the splendor of Heaven, and the forbidden Garden of Eden. Through vivid descriptions and captivating characters, Milton explores the fall of mankind, the battle between good and evil, and the consequences of human ambition. As the story unfolds, the reader is left pondering the nature of free will, the power of temptation, and the complexities of divine justice.

Genres: Epic poetry, Religious literature, Fantasy

Themes: The fall of mankind, Good vs. evil, Free will, Temptation, Divine justice
